# Calendar and Timekeeping

The people of this world track time using various calendar systems depending on culture, region, and purpose. Two primary systems dominate: the practical **Accord Reckoning** used for trade and governance, and the ancient **Spirit Moons** calendar maintained by traditionalists and nature-focused communities.

---

## The Accord Reckoning (Primary Calendar)

**Origin:** Standardized approximately 400 years ago when the Heartland Accord formed  
**Adoption:** Widely used across the continent for trade, governance, agriculture, and official records  
**Year Zero:** The fall of the third Hollostar and creation of the Endless Eye (700 years ago)

### Why This System

When the Heartland Accord formed ~400 years ago (300 AR), the member cities needed a unified calendar for coordinating trade, military movements, and agricultural cycles. They chose a relatively recent, well-documented event that affected multiple regions: the third Hollostar's impact creating the Endless Eye.

This event was:
- Within recorded history (reliable dating)
- Transformative for multiple regions (created rivers that changed Shardfall Basin, Salt Shore, Silverbelt Coast)
- Dramatic enough to serve as clear epoch marker
- Recent enough that most nations/cities could easily convert their existing calendars

**Current Year:** 700 AR (Accord Reckoning)

### Structure

**12 months, ~30 days each, plus festival days**

The year contains 360 regular days divided into 12 months of 30 days each, plus 5-6 festival days that fall outside the regular calendar:

1. **Firstmonth** (30 days) - Winter, coldest month
2. **Thawmonth** (30 days) - Late winter, ice begins melting
3. **Seedmonth** (30 days) - Early spring, planting begins
4. **Bloommonth** (30 days) - Spring, flowers and growth
5. **Greenmonth** (30 days) - Late spring, abundant growth
6. **Sunpeak** (30 days) - Early summer, longest days
7. **Harvestmonth** (30 days) - Mid-summer, early harvests
8. **Heatmonth** (30 days) - Late summer, hottest period
9. **Goldmonth** (30 days) - Early autumn, main harvest
10. **Fallmonth** (30 days) - Autumn, leaves falling
11. **Darkmonth** (30 days) - Late autumn, days shortening
12. **Frostmonth** (30 days) - Early winter, first snows

**Festival Days (outside regular calendar):**
- **Midwinter's Rest** (between Firstmonth and Thawmonth) - 1 day
- **Spring's Awakening** (between Seedmonth and Bloommonth) - 1 day
- **Summer's Height** (between Sunpeak and Harvestmonth) - 1 day
- **Harvest's End** (between Goldmonth and Fallmonth) - 1 day
- **Year's Turn** (between Frostmonth and Firstmonth) - 1-2 days
  - 2 days every 4th year (leap year adjustment)

**Total:** 365 days most years, 366 every 4th year

### How Dates Are Written

**Formal:** Day of Month, Month Name, Year AR
- Example: "15th of Bloommonth, 700 AR"

**Informal:** Day/Month/Year
- Example: "15/4/700" (15th day, 4th month, year 700)

**Very informal:** Just month and year for approximate dates
- Example: "Harvest 700" or "Late Darkmonth"

### Regional Variations

While the Accord Reckoning is widely adopted, some regions maintain parallel counting:

**Lumencrest** internally tracks "Lumencrest Era" (LE) dating from their founding ~1,200 years ago. Official documents use AR, but scholars and old families reference LE.
- Current: 1,200 LE (approximately)

**Kingsrest** maintains royal chronicles dating from King Aldric Thornhart's coronation (82 years ago). Used for royal records and ceremonies, AR for everything else.
- Current: "82nd Year of the Thornhart Dynasty"

**Dwarven holds** (Ironhold, Deepforge, etc.) keep Deep Reckoning counting from mythical "First Delving." Used internally, AR for surface dealings.
- Current: Unknown thousands of years DR

## The Spirit Moons (Alternative Calendar)

**Origin:** Ancient, pre-dating any known civilization  
**Users:** Path of Spirits followers, Green Covenant, traditional communities, rural areas  
**Purpose:** Spiritual observance, nature cycles, agricultural timing

### Why This System

The Spirit Moons calendar existed long before the Accord Reckoning — some claim it dates from before the first Hollostar fell 10,000 years ago. It tracks natural cycles: lunar phases, seasonal changes, animal migrations, plant growth patterns.

Path of Spirits followers and the Green Covenant maintain this calendar because:
- It honors natural rhythms over human convenience
- Lunar cycles are spiritually significant
- Predates the Hollowstars (doesn't use cosmic catastrophe as epoch)
- Connects practitioners to ancestors who used same calendar

### Structure

**13 months, ~28 days each, following lunar cycles**

The year contains 13 lunar months of 28 days each (364 days), plus 1-2 intercalary days:

1. **Deep Cold** - Midwinter
2. **Stirring Below** - Late winter, seeds awakening underground
3. **Thaw Water** - Ice melting, rivers flowing
4. **First Green** - First shoots appear
5. **Birdsong** - Birds return, nesting begins
6. **Young Growth** - Rapid spring growth
7. **Long Light** - Summer solstice period
8. **Fruit Swell** - Early summer abundance
9. **High Sun** - Peak summer heat
10. **Harvest Plenty** - Main harvest
11. **Leaf Fall** - Autumn colors
12. **Dark Gathering** - Preparing for winter
13. **Spirit Watch** - Winter solstice, veil between worlds thin

**Intercalary Days:**
- **Between Deep Cold and Stirring Below:** "The Longest Night" (1 day)
- **Between Long Light and Fruit Swell:** "The Shortest Night" (1 day, every 3rd year a second day is added)

**Total:** 365-366 days (similar to AR but distributed differently)

### How It's Used

Unlike Accord Reckoning's linear year counting (700 AR, 701 AR, etc.), Spirit Moons practitioners view time as cyclical. Years aren't numbered sequentially.

**Dating:** Described by natural events and relationships
- "This is the third spring since the great fire"
- "Born in Deep Cold during the year of the white deer"
- "Last Harvest Plenty when the river ran low"

**For external communication:** Practitioners convert to AR when needed, but within their communities, the cyclical understanding prevails.

**Green Covenant usage:** The Archcovenant and Wardens track both calendars. Spirit Moons for internal ceremonies and natural event timing, AR for diplomatic communication and settlement relations.

## Other Calendar Systems

### Katari Oral Calendar

Katari traders and communities maintain complex oral calendars tracking lineages, trade routes, and historical events through memorized generational chains. No written form exists — the calendar lives in memory and recitation.

**Dating:** "In the seventh generation before my grandmother's grandmother, when the eastern road was opened..."

**Accuracy:** Remarkably precise despite oral transmission. Katari can date events hundreds of years ago accurately by cross-referencing family lines and historical markers.

### Somnarch Star Calendar

The Somnarchs track time by Hollostar falls and cosmic cycles. Their calendar is not widely understood outside the order.

**Known markers:**
- First Hollostar: ~10,000 years ago
- Second Hollostar: ~1,200 years ago (Lumencrest's Heart)
- Third Hollostar: 700 years ago (Endless Eye)
- Fourth Hollostar: Predicted within 2-5 years

The Somnarchs claim to track longer cycles — repetitions spanning tens of thousands of years. Most outsiders consider this theoretical at best.

### Dwarven Deep Reckoning

Dwarven holds maintain Deep Reckoning (DR) dating from mythical "First Delving" — the moment the first dwarf carved the first hall beneath the mountains. No surface-dwelling people know what year it is in Deep Reckoning.

Dwarves use AR for surface trade and diplomacy. DR is reserved for internal record-keeping, forge-histories, and stone-carved chronicles that stretch back millennia.

## Practical Usage

**Most common:** Accord Reckoning for everyday use, trade, governance, treaties, official records

**Spiritual/Traditional:** Spirit Moons for Path of Spirits ceremonies, Green Covenant operations, rural agricultural timing, traditional communities

**Specialized:** Katari oral calendar (internal), Somnarch Star Calendar (esoteric), Dwarven Deep Reckoning (internal)

**Current date as of campaign start:**
- **Accord Reckoning:** 700 AR, Late Goldmonth (autumn, harvest season)
- **Spirit Moons:** Harvest Plenty moon (autumn)
