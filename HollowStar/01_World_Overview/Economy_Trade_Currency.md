# Economy, Trade & Currency

The continent has no unified economy and no central bank — but it does have a shared currency, shared trade routes, and enough mutual dependency that economic shocks in one region ripple outward to others. The Heartland Accord formalized much of this 400 years ago. The current crisis is testing how much of that formalization holds under pressure.

---

## CURRENCY

### The Standard Coinage (Accord Minting)

The Heartland Accord standardized coinage 400 years ago alongside the calendar. Four denominations are recognized across all Accord member cities and most trading partners:

| Coin | Material | Common Name | Value |
|------|----------|-------------|-------|
| **Platinum Crown** | Platinum | "Crown" | Highest — rarely used in daily commerce |
| **Gold Dragon** | Gold | "Dragon" or "Gold" | Standard for large transactions |
| **Silver Stag** | Silver | "Stag" or "Silver" | Common everyday commerce |
| **Copper Penny** | Copper | "Penny" or "Copper" | Small purchases, tips, children's money |

**Rough conversions:** 10 Copper = 1 Silver. 10 Silver = 1 Gold. 10 Gold = 1 Platinum. (Exact exchange rates vary slightly by city and merchant.)

**Minting:** Each Accord city mints its own coins to the same standard weight and purity. Coins from different cities are theoretically equivalent but in practice Lumencrest-minted coins carry slight prestige premium due to their precise manufacture.

### Regional Currencies

Outside the Accord's direct influence, other currency systems exist:

**L'Empire Verdienne** — The maritime empire uses its own coinage (the Verdien Sou and Livre), accepted readily in Verdant Crescent ports and throughout the empire's territory. Converts at roughly 3 Sou to 1 Silver Stag in practice.

**Starfall Basin** — The Merchant Circle issues trade chits alongside coin — standardized promissory notes backed by goods stored at the Endless Eye. Widely accepted within the Basin, viewed with suspicion outside it.

**Barter** — Common in frontier regions (Northern Expanse, Frostwell Tundra, deep Verdant Crescent). Furs, preserved food, timber, and worked metal serve as de facto currency in areas where coin rarely circulates.

**Starsilver** — The Rivermark Sovereignty's rare magical metal commands its own informal value — a thumb-weight of Starsilver is worth more than its weight in gold to enchanters and artificers.

## MAJOR TRADE GOODS

### High-Value Exports by Region

**Central Heartlands**
- Manufactured goods (Lumencrest — tools, machinery, magical devices)
- Agricultural surplus (grain, preserved foods)
- Iron and worked metal (Ironhold)
- Hollostar-enhanced goods (limited, controlled, expensive)

**Rivermark Sovereignty**
- Starsilver (unique to eastern Ironspine — commands premium from enchanters)
- Tin and zinc alloys (alchemical use)
- Faun medicinal plants and rare herbs
- River valley hardwoods (furniture, musical instruments)

**Verdant Crescent / L'Empire Verdienne**
- Timber (old-growth hardwood, closely regulated by Green Covenant)
- Pearls (Les Perles archipelago)
- Simiah-crafted goods (canopy woodwork, rope, specialist tools)
- Medicinal plants (forest-specific species)

**Desert of Glass**
- Ashglass (volcanic glass harder than steel, holds enchantment exceptionally well — unique to Ashenveil)
- Specialized metalwork requiring extreme heat
- Geothermal energy products
- Raw minerals from volcanic deposits

**Starfall Basin**
- Fresh water (the Endless Eye — increasingly scarce and valuable)
- Desert-adapted goods (heat-resistant materials, preserved foods)
- Information (the Merchant Circle's trade network is also an intelligence network)

**Salt Shore / Silverbelt Coast**
- Fish and salt (processed, preserved, exported inland)
- Ships (Keeper's Bay builds for anyone who pays)
- Naval expertise and mercenary escort

**Glittering Marshes**
- Bioluminescent fungi (medicinal, alchemical, decorative)
- Marsh-specific cultivated plants
- Shard fragments (extremely rare, heavily restricted, commands enormous prices)

**Frostwell Tundra**
- Frostsilver (rare ore that loses properties if heated above freezing during extraction — unique to extreme cold mining)
- Ice gems (permafrost crystals)
- Furs and preserved arctic protein
- *Currently: mostly outflow as refugees carry everything they can south*

## TRADE ROUTES

### The King's Highway
The primary overland road connecting the major Heartlands cities — Lumencrest, Kingsrest, Ironhold, Steelwatch. Maintained by the Accord, patrolled by Steelwatch guards. The backbone of continental overland commerce.

### The Iron Road
Connects Ironhold's mines to Lumencrest's manufacturing. Heavy cargo route — ore, coal, finished metal. Heavily trafficked, not fast.

### The Northern Way
Runs from the Heartlands north through the Northern Expanse toward the Frostwell border. Currently congested with southbound refugee traffic from Frostwell.

### The Riverways (Silverthread System)
River transport through the Central Heartlands — faster than road for bulk goods, weather-dependent. The Silverfork extension connects through the Rivermark Sovereignty to the Starfall Basin trade routes.

### The Coastal Routes
Two distinct coastal shipping lanes: the western route (Silverbelt Coast to Salt Shore and south) and the eastern route (Salt Shore islands to Starfall Basin coast). The Tide King's network controls security on the central coastal route; the Four Houses control Silverbelt shipping.

### The Desert Crossing
Katari-guided routes through the Starfall Basin connecting the eastern seaboard to the Rivermark Sovereignty and Central Heartlands. The most reliable crossing requires Katari route-knowledge — attempting it without is dangerous.

### The Forest Roads
L'Empire Verdienne's internal road network through the Verdant Crescent. Maintained under strict Green Covenant agreements — no logging operations within sight of the road, no permanent camps in the deep forest.

## ECONOMIC POWER HIERARCHY

**Lumencrest** has historically dominated continental economics through technological advantage — magical manufacturing produces goods no one else can replicate. This dominance is declining as the Heart dims and production drops.

**The Merchant Circle** controls all trade entering or leaving the Starfall Basin. Their choke-point on desert water and desert-crossing routes gives them leverage far beyond their geographic size.

**L'Empire Verdienne** controls the Verdant Crescent's timber and the Les Perles pearl trade — two resources with no substitutes. The empire is wealthy but geographically distant from the Accord's political center.

**The Four Houses** control Silverbelt Coast shipping. Anyone moving goods by sea along the western coast deals with them eventually.

**The Tide King** is building toward a similar choke-point on the central coast — still in progress, still contested, but the trajectory is clear.

**The Rivermark Sovereignty** controls the only known Starsilver deposits and the eastern trade route between the Heartlands and the Basin. Their economic leverage significantly exceeds their military or political power — which is why everyone wants the Sixth Seat settled.

## THE CURRENT ECONOMIC CRISIS

The world's economy is under pressure from multiple directions simultaneously:

**Lumencrest's manufacturing decline** — 15-20% drop in production as the Heart dims. Goods that only Lumencrest could make are becoming scarce. Prices rising. Substitutes being sought.

**The Endless Eye's water shortage** — Desert communities dependent on the Eye's water are beginning to pay premium prices for alternatives. Agricultural output in the Basin is threatened. The Merchant Circle's control of scarce water is both their greatest current leverage and their greatest long-term risk.

**Frostwell disruption** — The Frostwell Tundra's unique exports (Frostsilver, ice gems, arctic goods) have nearly stopped as evacuation continues. Buyers are sitting on contracts they can't fulfill.

**Refugee economics** — 60% of Frostwell's population is now in the Northern Expanse, which was not economically prepared to absorb them. Food prices in Northern Expanse river towns have risen sharply. Lumencrest has sent limited aid; Kingsrest has sent none.

**Shard fragment prices** — As the known Hollowstars fail, demand for fragments to study has skyrocketed. The Glittering Marshes shards have never been for sale — but the pressure to put a price on them has never been greater. Black market prices for fragments are at historic highs.

**The Veilmarkets opportunity** — Every economic disruption creates a black market opportunity. The Veilmarkets are having an exceptionally profitable year.

## TRADE LANGUAGE & COMMUNICATION

**Common tongue** is the shared trade language across the continent — no formal name, just "Common." It evolved from an older language and absorbed vocabulary from every major ancestry. Literacy in Common varies: high in trade cities, low in frontier regions.

**Regional languages** persist in isolated communities:
- Elvish (formal and colloquial variants — Northern Expanse elves speak both)
- Dwarven (Deep Speech for mining/forge context, surface Dwarven for trade)
- Simiah forest languages (multiple, not mutually intelligible)
- Galapa (preserved carefully as cultural identity marker)
- Drakona tongue (different dialects between Frostwell and Desert populations)
- Faun seasonal-speech (communicates time and agricultural context untranslatable in Common)

**The Katari oral tradition** is not a language barrier so much as a communication philosophy — Katari will speak Common fluently but their internal record-keeping and history exists only in memorized chains that no outsider can access.
