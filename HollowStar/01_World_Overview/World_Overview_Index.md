## The World at a Glance

A single continent — unnamed in common tongue, called different things by different peoples — surrounded by oceans that have never been fully mapped. Civilization clusters in the temperate middle. The frozen north and volcanic south push habitation toward the center. The coasts are well-settled; the deep interior of the continent is mostly known.

**Current year:** 700 AR (Accord Reckoning) — Late Goldmonth, harvest season

**The defining fact of this age:** Something is wrong with the stars. The two intact Hollowstars that power civilization are both failing simultaneously for the first time in recorded history. A fourth star is approaching from the north. Ancient sites are waking up. The world is changing and nobody knows where it ends.

## Quick World Facts

- **Dominant governments:** City-states, monarchies, tribal councils — no single continental empire
- **Most powerful single city:** Lumencrest (~90,000), currently in crisis
- **Primary religion:** Six-Fold Faith (polytheistic), with First Flame (monotheistic) growing fast
- **Technology level:** Renaissance-adjacent, with Lumencrest as the sole exception (magical electricity, constructs)
- **Magic:** Real, present, not ubiquitous — Hollostar fragments are the primary source of magical enhancement
- **Literacy:** Variable — high in Lumencrest and trade cities, low in frontier regions
- **Primary trade language:** Common tongue (no formal name, just "Common"), with regional dialects
- **Continental travel:** Weeks to months by road or ship; faster by river

## The Three Things Every Traveler Should Know

**1. Hollowstars matter more than gods.**
The stars that fell from the sky shaped civilization more concretely than any deity. Cities were built around them. Religions formed to explain them. Wars were fought over fragments. Two intact stars remain — both are failing. Everyone has an opinion about what this means.

**2. The Heartland Accord is the closest thing to a continental authority.**
Four city-states in the Central Heartlands coordinate trade, defense, and law through a 400-year-old agreement. It has no army of its own, no single ruler, and no enforcement mechanism beyond collective pressure. It works well enough that most of the continent trades in its calendar and currency. It is currently under severe internal strain.

**3. The map is mostly settled, but the edges are wild.**
The coasts are known. The major rivers are charted. The mountains have names. But the deep ocean has never been crossed, the Frostwell Tundra is about to be transformed by cosmic impact, and the pre-Hollostar ruins scattered across the world suggest the map has hidden depths that no one has fully explored.
