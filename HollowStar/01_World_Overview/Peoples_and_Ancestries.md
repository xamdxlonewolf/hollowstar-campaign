# Peoples & Ancestries

The continent is home to a wide variety of peoples. All ancestries listed here are fully playable in Daggerheart. Most are present in multiple regions; a few are strongly associated with specific places. None are monolithic — culture varies enormously within each ancestry based on where they live and how long they've been there.

---

## ANCESTRIES OF THE WORLD

### Humans
The most widely distributed ancestry on the continent. Found everywhere in significant numbers — no region is without them. Humans tend toward adaptability, shorter lifespans driving urgency and ambition, and strong community bonds within generations. They built Kingsrest, dominate the river towns, fish the Salt Shore, run the merchant guilds, and make up the majority of the Heartland Accord's common population. No single human culture — they absorb and reflect wherever they are.

---

### Elves
Long-lived (up to ~400 years under normal circumstances), patient, with deep memories and sometimes an uncomfortable relationship with urgency. Concentrated in the Northern Expanse (where King Elowen Arathas has ruled for centuries) and the Rivermark Sovereignty (elven majority, founding culture). Smaller communities exist throughout the Verdant Crescent and in older Heartlands cities. The longevity gap between elves and shorter-lived peoples creates persistent low-level friction — elves who remember things three generations back, humans who experience that same span as ancient history.

*Note: One known elf — the 800-year-old elder of the Northern Expanse court — lives far beyond the natural span, apparently extended by proximity to the third Hollostar's fall. This is considered extraordinary and unrepeated.*

---

### Dwarves
Compact, sturdy, long-memoried (not as long as elves, but generational). Two distinct cultural branches exist on the continent: **mountain dwarves** (Ironhold, Barad-Khelek, Cinderhome — miners, smiths, holders of deep tradition) and **surface dwarves** (scattered through Heartlands cities and trade routes, more integrated with mixed cultures). The dwarven Forge-Father Faith survives almost exclusively in Cinderhome; most dwarves elsewhere have adopted Six-Fold Faith or First Flame. Dwarves of Barad-Khelek in Frostwell are uniquely cold-adapted compared to their southern kin.

---

### Halflings
Warm, community-rooted, practical. Found throughout the agricultural regions — particularly the river valleys of the Northern Expanse and Rivermark Sovereignty, the Heartlands farming communities, and Salt Shore fishing towns. Halflings are often the backbone of food production in regions that don't think of them as remarkable. Multi-generational families with deep local roots. Politically quiet, economically essential, often underestimated.

---

### Drakona
Reptilian, scaled, distinctly non-mammalian in physiology. The Drakona have diverged into two distinct populations over centuries of geographic separation:

**Desert/Skyspine Drakona** — Heat-resistant. Fully at home in volcanic environments, extreme temperatures, and the Desert of Glass. Found in Ashenveil, Lavaspire, Cinderhome's surroundings, and the Skyspine volcanic region. Many believe Cindrath the Ancient is their ancestor or creator; she doesn't clarify. Their pilgrimage destination is Ember Cradle in Frostwell — a journey that puts them in their cousins' cold homeland.

**Frostwell Drakona** — Cold-resistant. Fully biological adaptation over generations of living in the frozen north. Dark-scaled, at home in temperatures that kill other peoples. Found at Ember Cradle, where they guard ancient ruins they consider their sacred origin site. Their pilgrimage destination is Cindrath in Ashenveil — a journey into volcanic heat their desert cousins find comfortable. Both groups regard each other as distant cousins — different but respectful, no bitterness about the divergence.

---

### Infernis
Fire-resistant humanoids with a connection to something older and hotter than most people are comfortable acknowledging. Found primarily in the Desert of Glass — Lavaspire especially. Thrive in volcanic environments. Some Infernis believe their fire-resistance hints at demonic heritage; others consider this superstition. A radical faction within the Desert of Glass seeks to "awaken the demon within" all Infernis, which the majority regards with anything from skepticism to alarm.

---

### Faun
Goat-legged, horned, deeply attuned to seasonal cycles and natural rhythms. Concentrated in the Rivermark Sovereignty, where Faun agricultural wisdom forms half of the kingdom's cultural identity alongside elven patience. Also present in rural Heartlands communities and the Verdant Crescent edges. Faun culture runs on seasons — their sense of time, ceremony, and appropriate action is tied to the agricultural calendar in ways that can feel alien to urban ancestries. The Sovereignty's Confluence Council includes rotating Seasonal Speakers who represent Faun perspectives.

---

### Simiah
Anthropomorphic simians ranging from great-ape builds to small marmoset-like frames, with prehensile feet and instinctive comfort with vertical space. Among the oldest peoples of the Verdant Crescent — some Simiah communities predate every other settlement in the forest. Found almost exclusively in the Verdant Crescent and Les Perles islands. Their relationship with the forest is cultural bedrock — the canopy is not wilderness to them but architecture. Two major groups: **Great-folk** (large, rare, deeply respected), **Common-folk** (most numerous), and **Small-folk** (compact, fastest, highest canopy dwellers).

---

### Ribbet
Amphibious frog-folk, comfortable in water and on land, with a community culture built around pools, rivers, and shared space. Concentrated in the Starfall Basin (where they co-govern the Merchant Circle with Fairies) and the Glittering Marshes (where their relationship with the shard-lit water is spiritual as much as practical). Also found in Salt Shore fishing communities. Ribbet culture prizes practical wisdom, trade relationships, and the quiet knowledge of what lives beneath still water.

---

### Fairy
Small-statured, winged, with an acute sensitivity to the world's magical currents. Found across multiple regions but concentrated in the Starfall Basin (Merchant Circle co-governors) and Verdant Crescent. Fairies tend toward networks — information, trade, relationships. Their small size is rarely a limitation and sometimes a meaningful advantage. The Merchant Circle's Fairy leadership skews toward traditionalism in the current Eye crisis; the Ribbet leadership skews pragmatic.

---

### Fungril
Fungal humanoids — they are, in the most literal sense, ambulatory mycelium colonies that have achieved consciousness and mobility. Found primarily in the Glittering Marshes (where the shard-lit ecosystem has produced the most potent Fungril on the continent) and the Desert of Glass (geothermal cave communities). Their knowledge of fungal cultivation, bioluminescence, and mycelial networks is encyclopedic and largely untranslatable to other ancestries. The Deep Bloom colony in the Glittering Marshes has integrated actual Hollostar fragments into its mycelium — the only known organism to do so.

---

### Katari
Desert-adapted traders and route-keepers, with an oral tradition so precise it serves as calendar, law, and history simultaneously. No written records — the calendar lives in memorized generational chains. Found along trade routes throughout the continent, with concentrations in the Starfall Basin, Salt Shore, and Northern Expanse. Katari route-knowledge is their primary cultural currency and their primary trade leverage — they know paths others have forgotten. Politically neutral by tradition; neutrality is good for business.

---

### Giant
Several distinct subtypes exist (Storm Giants, Hill Giants, Firbolg, and others), but all share significant physical scale and often a different relationship to time and community than smaller ancestries. Firbolg — the forest-attuned Giant subtype — are found in the Verdant Crescent and Northern Expanse edges. True Giants have significant presence in the Northern Expanse frontier towns, where physical capability is genuinely valued. High Flame Kaida of the First Flame is a Giant, the first of her ancestry to hold that position.

---

### Galapa
Turtle-folk — shelled, slow-moving by reputation (not always in practice), with an extraordinary cultural memory for loss. Their homeland sank beneath the sea in living memory. Every Galapa alive is either a refugee or the child of one. Concentrated on the Isle of the Broken Sphere (~8,000) and in the Les Perles islands (~1,800, the "old families" who left before the sinking). Galapa culture is defined by the tension between preserving what was and building what comes next. They are not uniformly sad — they are pragmatic survivors. But the grief is always there.

---

### Clanks
Sentient constructs — built, not born. Created by Lumencrest to serve as labor. Some achieved full self-awareness, concluded servitude was wrong, and escaped. The Ember Refuge community (~600) in the Desert of Glass is the only openly free Clank settlement on the continent. Lumencrest officially considers them stolen property. Unofficially, the distance and the terrain make enforcement impractical. Clanks run on geothermal or magical power, do not eat, do not sleep in the biological sense, and have an intimate relationship with the question of what personhood means. Mechanically diverse — no two Clanks are identical.
