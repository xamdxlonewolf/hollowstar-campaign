# Geography & Continent Overview

The known world is a single large continent surrounded by ocean on all sides. No expedition has ever returned from crossing the deep ocean — if other landmasses exist, no one living knows it.

---

## THE CONTINENT

**No universal name.** Different peoples call it different things:
- The Heartland Accord simply refers to "the continent" or "the known world"
- Path of Spirits traditions call it "the Living Land" or "the Body"
- The Somnarchs' records use an ancient pre-Hollostar name that translates roughly as "the Marked Place" — marked, presumably, by the stars that fell here
- Most common people don't think of it as having a name at all — it's just the world

**Shape and scale:** A large irregular landmass, wider in the middle, narrowing toward both the frozen north and the volcanic south. Roughly 2,000 miles north to south at its widest point; perhaps 1,500 miles east to west. Travel across the full continent by road takes several months. By fast ship along the coast, six to eight weeks.

---

## CLIMATE ZONES

**The Frozen North (Frostwell Tundra)**
Permafrost, brutal winters eight months long, brief cool summers. Barely habitable without significant preparation. Currently the projected impact zone for the approaching Hollostar. Population has been evacuating south.

**The Northern Temperate Band (Northern Expanse)**
Cool to cold, four distinct seasons, extensive grassland and forest. The Nendalië River runs west to east through the center. Sparse population relative to land area — frontier culture, no dominant power. Currently absorbing Frostwell refugees.

**The Central Temperate Zone (Central Heartlands, Rivermark Sovereignty)**
The continent's agricultural and political heart. Rolling plains, river valleys, moderate seasons. The Ironspine Mountains run north to south through this zone, dividing it into the western Accord cities and the eastern Rivermark Sovereignty. Most densely populated region on the continent.

**The Western Forests (Verdant Crescent)**
Ancient old-growth forests covering the northwestern peninsula and surrounding islands. Mild, wet climate. Home to some of the oldest living things on the continent — trees that predate recorded history.

**The Glittering Marshes**
Southwestern wetlands. Warm, humid, bioluminescent at night from Hollostar shard concentration. Not inhospitable — just wet, strange, and inaccessible to those who don't know it.

**The Coasts (Salt Shore, Silverbelt Coast)**
Two distinct coastal cultures along the western and central coasts. The Silverbelt Coast is temperate and noble-house dominated. The Salt Shore is rockier, with active fishing communities and the emerging Tide King maritime power.

**The Eastern Desert (Starfall Basin)**
Hot, arid desert east of the Central Heartlands. The Endless Eye — a freshwater lake created by the third Hollostar — is the only reason significant population exists here at all. The lake is currently shrinking.

**The Volcanic South (Desert of Glass)**
Skyspine Mountains and surrounding volcanic plains. Extreme heat, active volcanism, glass deserts formed from ancient eruptions. Barely habitable for most ancestries. Home to Drakona, Infernis, Fungril, Clanks, and the dwarves of Cinderhome. Cindrath the Ancient makes her home in the Furnace volcano here.

**The Tropical Islands**
- **Isle of the Broken Sphere:** Volcanic tropical island, home to Galapa refugees. Something ancient is waking beneath it.
- **Les Perles (Verdant Crescent):** Pearl-diving archipelago, Galapa and Simiah communities, part of L'Empire Verdienne.
- **Salt Shore Islands:** Rocky fishing islands along the coast, under Tide King influence.

## MAJOR GEOGRAPHICAL FEATURES

### Mountain Ranges

**The Ironspine Mountains** — Run north to south through the Central Heartlands, dividing the continent's most populated region. Western slopes controlled by Ironhold (dwarven mining). Eastern slopes controlled by the Rivermark Sovereignty (Starsilver deposits). Passable at several points; no impassable barrier, but a meaningful political and cultural divide.

**The Skyspine Mountains / Northern Range** — The volcanic mountain chain of the Desert of Glass. The Skyspine continues north as the border range between the Northern Expanse and the Verdant Crescent. Dangerous, mineral-rich, home to extreme-environment communities.

**The Southern Frostwell Mountains** — Separating the Frostwell Tundra from the Northern Expanse. Not fully impassable — Silver Pass/Celebrant provides the primary crossing, currently seeing unprecedented refugee traffic.

### Rivers

**The Silverthread System (Central Heartlands)** — The primary river network of the Central Heartlands, feeding agricultural land and powering trade. The rivers flow generally westward toward the Silverbelt Coast.

**The Silverfork (Rivermark Sovereignty)** — Formed by the Crystalriver and Goldwater confluence at Twinwater, flowing east toward Starfall Basin. The Goldwater shimmers gold from Hollostar shard influence in its riverbed.

**The Nendalië (Northern Expanse)** — Runs west to east across the central Northern Expanse, the main population corridor of the region. Fed by the Coldrun from the north (current refugee route from Frostwell).

**The Arathandë/Skyspine River** — Runs north to south along the western border of the Northern Expanse, dividing it from the Verdant Crescent. Continues south through the Central Heartlands to the Salt Shore coast.

### Bodies of Water

**The Endless Eye** — Freshwater lake in the Starfall Basin desert, created by the third Hollostar impact 700 years ago. Impossible geography — water that should not exist in a desert, but does. Currently shrinking for the first time ever. The economic and spiritual heart of the Basin.

**The Glittering Marshes** — Not a lake but an extensive wetland system containing the highest concentration of Hollostar fragments on the continent. Glows at night. The shards in the marsh floor cannot be easily removed without damaging the ecosystem.

**The surrounding ocean** — Called different things in different regions ("the Salt Sea" to the west, "the Deep Green" to the east). Never fully charted. No expedition has successfully crossed it. The ocean is a hard boundary on the known world.

## WHAT LIES BEYOND

**The deep ocean:** No one knows. Expeditions have been mounted — none returned. The Somnarchs' oldest records suggest the pre-Hollostar civilization may have known what lies beyond the sea, but those records are fragmentary and largely indecipherable.

**The unmapped north:** Beyond Frostwell, there is land — Somnarch star observations suggest the continent continues further than anyone has explored. Whatever is there is inaccessible through permafrost and the incoming star's projected impact zone.

**Underground:** The dwarven Deep Reckoning dates from a "First Delving" thousands of years ago. What they have found in the deepest levels, they do not discuss with surface peoples.
