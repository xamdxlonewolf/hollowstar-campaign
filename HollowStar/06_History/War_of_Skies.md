# The War of Skies — History & Legacy

**Period:** Approximately 1,100–1,200 years ago (~400–500 BR, Before Reckoning)  
**Belligerents:** The Frostwell Drakona (Dragon Riders) vs. the proto-Northern Expanse peoples (Great Eagle Riders)  
**Outcome:** Decisive Northern Expanse victory. Drakona withdrew. The conflict shaped both cultures permanently — and left a dormant bloodline that neither side has fully reckoned with.

---

## THE WAR

### What Caused It

The continent's northern third was less settled then. The Frostwell Tundra and the Northern Expanse were not yet distinct political entities — they were contested territories, rich in timber, fur, fish, and early mineral deposits that both expanding peoples needed.

The Frostwell Drakona had, at that time, something they would later lose: **an active relationship with the ice-drakes of the deep tundra** — not the great fire-breathing dragons of legend, but something related and genuine. Cold-blooded, white-scaled, built for the arctic sky. The Drakona who carried the bloodline could bond with them from birth. They called this gift *Edrathar* — loosely, "the cold calling."

The Northern Expanse peoples had their own aerial tradition: the **Great Eagles** of the Arathandë peaks — enormous raptors, intelligent in their own way, who had been bonding with certain human and elvish families for generations. These bonds were earned, not inherited. An eagle chose its rider; the rider spent years proving worthy. The resulting partnership was not a weapon of war — until the war made it one.

Territorial disputes escalated over roughly thirty years. Raids became battles. The Drakona's ice-drakes gave them early aerial dominance — faster, heavier, devastating in concentrated strikes. The Northern Expanse peoples were losing.

### The Turning Point

The war turned on a single innovation: the **Arathandë formations** — a coordinated aerial tactics doctrine developed by an elvish war-commander whose name survives only in fragmentary records as *Caranwë* ("Red Voice"). Rather than matching the ice-drakes head-on, the Eagle Riders flew in rotating formations that exhausted drake riders through pursuit while protecting their own people on the ground.

Over the following two decades, the Eagles' superior endurance, higher altitude ceiling, and the discipline of their riders ground down the Drakona's advantages. The ice-drakes were never defeated outright — they were simply outlasted.

### The End

The war ended not with a dramatic final battle but with the collapse of Drakona territorial ambition. The Drake Riders' losses mounted. Bonded drakes that died took their riders' bloodline connection with them in some cases — or left riders too grief-broken to fight effectively. The Drakona withdrew north and east, into the deep tundra, and over the following centuries the political boundary that became Frostwell solidified.

No formal treaty. No monument. The Northern Expanse simply expanded into the territory and built.

**What the Drakona left behind:** The ice-drakes retreated with them, eventually dying out over centuries from cold, diminished territory, and the loss of Drakona riders to bond with. The last confirmed ice-drake sighting is approximately 600 years old. Whether any survive in the deepest tundra — in unmapped ice crevasses, in the mountains beyond Frostwell's settled territory — is unknown.

**What the Drakona took with them:** The *Edrathar* bloodline. Dormant, not destroyed.

## THE GREAT EAGLE RIDERS — LEGACY IN THE NORTHERN EXPANSE

### What They Are Now

The Great Eagle Riders are the Northern Expanse's most celebrated military tradition and one of its oldest institutions. Over a thousand years of practice have made them something genuinely formidable — and genuinely rare.

**The eagles:** The Great Eagles of the Arathandë peaks are not domesticated animals. They are not trained. They are bonded — a relationship that begins when an eagle chooses a candidate, typically between ages 14 and 22, and cannot be forced or predicted. Eagles have refused the children of celebrated Rider families. Eagles have chosen farmers' daughters and dockworkers' sons with no military background. The bond, when it forms, is for life.

**Size:** Great Eagles stand roughly 5 feet at the shoulder with a wingspan between 18 and 24 feet. Large enough to carry a single rider plus light equipment. Not large enough for heavy armor or two passengers — this shapes their entire tactical role.

**Intelligence:** Noticeably beyond normal raptors. They understand complex commands, navigate tactically, and have been observed making independent decisions in battle that saved their riders' lives. Whether they have genuine consciousness is a philosophical question the Rider tradition debates quietly and inconclusively.

**Lifespan:** 80–120 years. When an eagle dies, the rider does not automatically bond with another — some do, some never do. The loss of a bonded eagle is treated in Northern Expanse culture with the same gravity as the loss of a spouse.

### The Riders

**Active Riders:** Approximately **30–40** at any given time, spread across the Northern Expanse. This number has been roughly stable for centuries — the eagles' choosiness limits growth. There were more during the War of Skies era; scholars estimate 80–100 active Riders at the war's peak.

**Training:** Once a bond forms, the Rider tradition takes over — years of flight discipline, formation tactics, aerial combat, and the Arathandë formation doctrine that won the war. New Riders train under veterans. The tradition is self-perpetuating and deeply proud.

**Social position:** Rider families carry significant status, but the eagle's choice can come from anywhere. The tradition has a cultural commitment to honoring this — refusing a chosen rider on grounds of birth or ancestry is considered a serious transgression. It has happened. It has always ended badly.

**The Aerie at Elarindë:** The Rider tradition's formal home is a complex of platforms, nests, and training grounds built into the Arathandë cliffs above the city — accessible from Elarindë by a carved switchback path. The Aerie is the only place in the Northern Expanse that feels genuinely military; the rest of the Rider tradition is scattered across family holdings and peak roosts.

**Current status:** In peacetime, the Riders function as scouts, messengers, and emergency response — wildfire spotting, mountain rescue, refugee crisis aerial survey. Their military role has been dormant for centuries, which makes the approaching Hollostar crisis the first genuine deployment question in living memory.

### In the Current Crisis

The approaching star has created the first serious strategic conversation about the Riders in generations. King Elowen has been in private consultation with the Rider Commander (**Sera Windmane**, human, 58, 22-year bonded Rider) about aerial survey of the projected impact zone and monitoring of Frostwell's evacuation routes.

**The Riders' concern:** At 30–40 active Riders, they cannot cover the entire Frostwell evacuation — but they can identify where it's failing and relay information faster than any ground courier. They are already doing this informally. Windmane wants formal authorization and resourcing. Elowen has been "considering" this for four months.

**Adventure significance:** A Great Eagle Rider as a contact, ally, or party member represents access to aerial intelligence no other faction has. Getting Rider support for a mission is a significant diplomatic achievement — they take their autonomy seriously and answer ultimately to the eagle, not the crown.

## THE DRAKONA DRAGONRIDER BLOODLINE

*→ See also: [[02_Regions/Frostwell_Tundra/Locations/Landmarks/Dwarves_and_Drakona|Drakona of Frostwell]] and [[01_World_Overview/Peoples_and_Ancestries|Drakona Ancestry]]*

### What Was Lost

The *Edrathar* — the cold calling — was never destroyed. It was buried.

After the war, the surviving Drake Riders were a people who had just lost a prolonged conflict. The drakes were retreating. The bonds that remained were grieved rather than celebrated. Over two or three generations, the cultural emphasis shifted from "we are the people who ride drakes" to "we are the people who endure." The Nine Hearths culture absorbed some of this. The Drakona developed their spiritual identity around the Ember Cradle. The drakes retreated deeper into the tundra.

By 400 AR — 700 years after the war — there was no living Drake Rider. The knowledge of how the bond worked had become ceremony rather than practice, and then not even ceremony. The *Edrathar* became a word in old ritual texts that most Drakona couldn't translate.

**What remained:** The bloodline. Recessive, generational, unpredictable. Most Frostwell Drakona alive today carry no meaningful expression of it. But in roughly one in every several hundred Drakona births, something different persists — a child who runs warmer than their cold-adapted kin, who has an inexplicable pull toward large predatory animals, who sometimes has dreams of cold sky and wind that they can't explain.

These individuals exist. They don't know what they are.

### How the Bloodline Manifests

*For GMs — this is a PC option for Drakona players, not public world knowledge.*

**Physical signs (subtle, easy to miss or rationalize):**
- Slightly warmer body temperature than typical Frostwell Drakona — not heat-resistant like Desert cousins, but not as cold-adapted as kin
- Scale coloration often has faint iridescence that catches light differently from typical frost-dark scales
- Unusually strong spatial sense — instinctive awareness of altitude, wind direction, and three-dimensional position
- Animals — particularly large predatory birds and reptiles — react to them differently. Not fear. Something more like attention.

**The pull:** Drake bloodline carriers often feel an inexplicable draw to high places — cliff edges, rooftops, mountain peaks. Not suicidal. More like homesickness for somewhere they've never been.

**What triggers recognition:** The bloodline does nothing on its own without a drake (or drake-adjacent creature) to activate it. There are no living ice-drakes confirmed in Frostwell. But the approaching Hollostar crisis is doing something unusual to the ancient tundra sites — and there are things in the deep unmapped ice that haven't been seen in centuries.

### Discovery Paths for PC Drakona

**Path 1 — You always knew something was different.**  
The player establishes at character creation that their Drakona has always felt subtly off from their kin. Warmer. Drawn to heights. Animals respond strangely. They've never had an explanation. The war is a thousand years ago — most Frostwell Drakona have no cultural memory of it as anything other than a historical event. The bloodline has no name anymore.

**Path 2 — You find out during play.**  
The player's Drakona shows no unusual signs at character creation. Somewhere in the campaign — encountering certain ruins, reaching certain altitudes in the tundra, being near something ancient and cold and large — the recognition happens. GM determines when and how.

**Path 3 — Someone else recognizes it first.**  
Cindrath recognizes it. She knew the original Drake Riders. She hasn't seen the bloodline in a long time. Her reaction — quiet, careful interest — tells the player something is happening before they understand what. Alternatively: Thessara Windroot, 800 years old, was alive during the late war era. She has seen this before.

### What It Could Mean

A PC Drakona who discovers and develops the *Edrathar* bloodline is walking a path nobody has walked for 700 years. What that means in practice is yours to develop with your players, but some framings:

**The ice-drakes aren't gone.** The deep tundra is unmapped. The Hollostar's approach is waking ancient things at every major site. If something is waking in the ice — something large, cold-blooded, and very old — a Drakona with the bloodline is the only person on the continent equipped to approach it.

**The Drakona spiritual mission at Ember Cradle.** Flame-Keeper Sorith and the Drakona community are waiting for transformation. The bloodline's reawakening — especially near the Ember Cradle's warming ruins — could be part of what they've been waiting for. Or a complication of it. Sorith would have complicated feelings either way.

**The Great Eagle Riders.** If a PC Drakona is walking around with an ancient war bloodline that once fought the Eagle Rider tradition's ancestors, that history is still present in Rider culture — in songs, in training doctrine, in the tactics that won the war. Whether that history creates tension, curiosity, or unexpected alliance with a Rider NPC is genuinely interesting territory.

**The Frostwell Drakona community.** Most of them have no idea this bloodline still exists. A PC who discovers it will be faced with a choice: tell the community, or carry it alone. Both have weight.

## CONNECTING THE TWO TRADITIONS

The War of Skies created two legacies that still exist in the world — one celebrated and active, one dormant and forgotten. Both are connected to the Hollostar crisis in ways neither side has yet recognized:

- The Great Eagle Riders are the Northern Expanse's aerial intelligence arm at precisely the moment the continent most needs aerial intelligence
- The Drakona Drake Rider bloodline is reawakening at precisely the moment the deep tundra may be producing something that bloodline was built to respond to

Whether these two traditions ever meet again — a thousand years after the war that defined them — is a campaign question worth holding.
