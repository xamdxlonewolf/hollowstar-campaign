# The Age Before

**Period:** ~10,000 years ago and earlier  
**Status:** Almost entirely lost — what survives is architectural and fragmentary

---

## What the World Believes

A civilization existed before the first Hollostar fell. The evidence is in the ruins: structures in impossible locations, construction methods no current culture can replicate, an architecture unlike anything built in the last ten thousand years. No written records survive intact. Myths across every region refer to "the time before stars fell" as a lost golden age, an era of greater knowledge, a people who understood things the current world has forgotten.

Most scholars believe this civilization was destroyed by the first Hollostar's impact approximately 10,000 years ago. The Somnarchs' position is that the impact killed them. The Hexarchs treat the age before as a spiritual precursor era. The Path of Spirits holds that the ancestors of this civilization are still present in the world's living systems.

All of these positions are partially correct and fundamentally incomplete.

---

## What Actually Happened

*GM knowledge — do not share with players until they have earned it through play*

The pre-Hollostar civilization was not destroyed by the first fall. They caused it.

They were a civilization that had achieved deep understanding of this world's nature — not technologically in the way Lumencrest understands technology, but in the way of a people who had spent thousands of years in genuine conversation with the world's living systems. Their understanding of what the world was made of, how it moved, what inhabited it in ways current peoples can't perceive — all of it was embedded in what they built. The Resonance Network. The Sleeping Gate. The Frostvault. The sealed chamber beneath the Ember Cradle. These are not ruins. They are infrastructure — built to a specific purpose that has not yet been fulfilled.

That purpose: to receive themselves.

Facing a collapse that would consume their essential nature entirely — what it was is not recorded, because the recording was compressed into the escape — they made the only choice available. They converted themselves into a form of matter-energy that doesn't exist naturally in this world, compressed that form into the structures we call Hollowstars, and launched themselves into the void. Not randomly. Toward this world — toward the infrastructure they had already built to receive them.

They built the landing pads before they jumped.

The travel time from wherever they launched to here is approximately 10,000 years — the interval between the first fall and the fourth. They did not all leave at once. They left in waves, the way a civilization evacuates under pressure: some first, some later, the last ones at the final possible moment.

## What the Ruins Are

Every significant pre-Hollostar structure is part of the receiving infrastructure:

**The Frostwell Ruins and the Resonance Network:** The central reception architecture. Built to open a channel between what arrives inside a falling star and what exists outside it — not to absorb the impact energy but to make contact with what the impact releases. The Resonance Network requires four nodes functioning simultaneously: the Ember Cradle (anchor), the Frostvault (processor), the Sleeping Gate (aperture), and the Warmstone (catalyst). The Warmstone was removed at some point in the last ten thousand years. Its current location is unknown. This is not an accident.

**The Sleeping Gate:** An aperture — a door. Its carved symbols are not a language in the conventional sense. They describe a process: what to do when something arrives that does not fit any existing category. How to receive it. How to make room for it. The symbols change near Hollostar sites because the Gate is responding to proximity — registering that the infrastructure is relevant again.

**The Ember Cradle (sealed chamber):** The anchor point. What is in the sealed chamber is part of the Resonance Network's anchor mechanism. It has been warming for thirty years because the fourth fall's approach is activating it. Deep-Keeper Varra's records describe its function without describing its contents.

**Structures across the continent:** Every ancient ruin that feels strangely present — the carved coast arches in the Salt Shore, the Ironhold deep mines' lower levels, the formations under the Glittering Marshes — is a node in a continental network of awareness. Not all of them are active. All of them are listening.

## What the Civilization Became

The pre-Hollostar civilization survived — in compressed, transformed form — inside the Hollowstars. The escaping changed them. What arrived is not what left. The giving of electricity from the Heart and water from the Endless Eye is not a resource being extracted from an inert object. It is the civilization's transformed substance doing the only thing it still can: converting itself into what the land around it most needs.

But contact with the first arrival — when the Resonance Network was last active, when the civilization that built it was still here to use it — changed the builders in ways that ended them as a discrete civilization. They did not die. They distributed. The world's capacity to feel presence, to maintain spiritual connection, to sustain living networks like the Veleth and the marsh's bioluminescent consciousness — this is the inheritance of that contact. The pre-Hollostar civilization became the world's sensitivity.

Which means the current world's cultures are not the inheritors of the pre-Hollostar civilization. They *are* that civilization, dispersed and slow and no longer aware of itself as unified.

When the fourth fall comes and the Resonance Network activates, it will not be strangers making contact with something alien. It will be one part of a very old civilization — the part that became the world — hearing from the part that went into the stars.

| NPC | What They Know |

| Cindrath | The full truth — she watched the first fall as a young dragon and understood what she saw. Has told no one. Will tell players who ask the right question. |
