## Core Theology

### The Fundamental Belief

Six gods in **three hierarchical tiers** govern all existence. Each tier represents a different level of divine engagement with the mortal world:

- **Tier 1 (The Primarchs):** Cosmic forces, distant creators
- **Tier 2 (The Mediators):** Active intermediaries between cosmic and mortal
- **Tier 3 (The Intimates):** Close to mortal concerns, most commonly worshiped

### The Divine Hierarchy

The gods are not equal in power, but **equal in necessity**. Like a body needs both heart and hands, existence requires all six working in concert—even when they conflict.

**Key Theological Points:**
- The gods are in **eternal tension**, not perfect harmony
- This tension creates existence itself—stasis would mean death of the world
- Mortals are caught between divine forces, shaped by their conflicts
- Prayer doesn't change the gods; it aligns mortals with divine currents

## TIER 1: The Primarchs

*The Supreme Pair - Cosmic Creators*

### The Architect (The Builder, The Lawgiver, The First Foundation)

**Domains:** Order, Structure, Civilization, Law, Mathematics, Time, Permanence

**Sacred Symbol:** Compass and square, often with a perfect circle or geometric patterns

**Holy Colors:** Silver, white, deep blue

**Sacred Number:** Four (the foundation, the square, the cardinal directions)

**Portrayed As:**
- Masculine-coded in art and language
- Often depicted as an elderly man with a white beard, holding drafting tools
- Sometimes shown as pure geometric light
- Distant but watchful—a stern grandfather watching over creation
- Patient, methodical, exacting

**Epithets:**
- The Foundation Stone
- He Who Measures All
- The Eternal Planner
- Lord of Hours

**What Followers Believe:**

The Architect **designed existence** with mathematical precision. Every natural law, every physical constant, every pattern in nature reflects His grand design. Civilization is humanity's highest calling—bringing order to chaos, building permanence from transience.

**"To build is to pray. To plan is to honor. To follow law is to walk in His footsteps."**

**Followers Include:**
- Lawmakers and judges
- Architects and engineers  
- Scholars and mathematicians
- City administrators
- Those seeking stability and order
- [[Lumencrest]] technocrats (heavily Architect-aligned)

**Daily Practices:**
- Morning prayers facing east (sun's predictable rise)
- Keeping detailed records and journals
- Honoring contracts and promises
- Creating or maintaining something permanent
- Studying mathematics, history, or law

**Temples:**
- Geometrically perfect buildings
- Stone construction, built to last centuries
- Intricate mathematical decorations
- Libraries and archives attached
- Everything symmetrical, measured, planned

**The Architect's Perspective on [[Hollowstars]]:**

**Orthodox View:** The stars are **divine instruments**—tools the Architect placed in the heavens for specific purposes mortals don't yet understand. Their falling is part of His design.

**Crisis Interpretation:** The dimming suggests the design is **completing its cycle**. All things, even divine plans, have intended endpoints. Mortals must adapt or build anew.

**Radical Sect:** The Architect **IS** mathematical law itself. Hollowstars are cosmic equations made manifest. Study them to understand divinity.

---

### The Weaver (The Wild One, The Changer, Mother of Becoming)

**Domains:** Chaos, Change, Wilderness, Transformation, Growth, Storms, Freedom, Creativity

**Sacred Symbol:** Tangled threads, wild vines, spiral patterns, never the same twice

**Holy Colors:** Green, deep red, gold, constantly shifting patterns

**Sacred Number:** Five (the hand, the unpredictable, the living)

**Portrayed As:**
- Feminine-coded in art and language
- Depicted as a young woman with wild hair, bare feet, often in motion
- Sometimes shown as pure flowing energy or living vines
- Ever-present in every natural thing
- Mercurial, passionate, vital, untamable

**Epithets:**
- The Untamed Heart
- She Who Becomes
- The Storm-Bringer
- Lady of the Turning Year

**What Followers Believe:**

The Weaver **breathes life** into the Architect's static design. Without Her, the universe would be a perfect, dead machine. She is change itself—the seasons turning, seeds sprouting, rivers flowing, storms breaking, hearts beating. Wilderness is sacred; civilization must bend to nature, not dominate it.

**"To change is to live. To flow is to thrive. To resist the Weaver is to become stone."**

**Followers Include:**
- Travelers and explorers
- Artists and musicians
- Rangers and druids (though some prefer [[Path of Spirits]])
- Those embracing change
- Revolutionaries and reformers
- Farmers (who depend on seasons)
- [[The Endless Eye]] residents (who see the lake as Her gift)

**Daily Practices:**
- Dawn prayers outdoors
- Never eating the same meal twice in a row
- Planting or tending something living
- Creating art or music
- Embracing unexpected changes
- Spending time in nature

**Temples:**
- Living buildings—grown from trees or built around natural features
- No two temples look alike
- Gardens and wild spaces integral
- Organic, flowing architecture
- Often open-air or with minimal walls

**The Weaver's Perspective on [[Hollowstars]]:**

**Orthodox View:** The stars are **seeds of transformation**—the Weaver sows them across the cosmos. Where they land, change blossoms. This is natural and good.

**Crisis Interpretation:** The stars dimming/changing is **evolution**, not death. The world transforms; mortals must transform with it or be left behind.

**Radical Sect:** The Weaver **weaves the stars themselves**—each fall is Her hand reaching down to reshape reality. Oppose Her at your peril.

---

### The Divine Tension (Architect vs. Weaver)

**The Central Theological Question:** Are they enemies or partners?

**Answer:** **BOTH.** Their conflict creates existence.

- The Architect builds; the Weaver erodes
- The Architect plans; the Weaver improvises
- The Architect preserves; the Weaver transforms
- Without the Architect: pure chaos, nothing lasts, no meaning
- Without the Weaver: stagnation, death, sterile perfection

**How This Plays Out:**
- Cities (Architect) need farms and forests (Weaver)
- Laws (Architect) must adapt to circumstances (Weaver)
- Tradition (Architect) balanced with innovation (Weaver)
- Structure (Architect) given life by creativity (Weaver)

**In the Current Crisis:**
- Architect-worshipers want to **control** Hollowstars (study, harness, stabilize)
- Weaver-worshipers want to **flow with** Hollowstars (adapt, accept change)
- Both believe they're right
- Both are partially correct

## TIER 2: The Mediators

*The Middle Pair - Active Intermediaries*

Born from or serving the Primarchs, more directly involved with mortal affairs.

### The Sentinel (The Guardian, The Shield, The Unyielding)

**Domains:** Protection, Honor, Justice, Warfare, Courage, Duty, Sacrifice, Oaths

**Sacred Symbol:** Shield with a burning flame at its center

**Holy Colors:** Red and gold, iron gray

**Sacred Number:** Three (the triangle, strongest structure, three-fold oath)

**Portrayed As:**
- Gender-neutral or both masculine and feminine in different cultures
- Armored figure with shield, never showing face (vigilance is universal)
- Standing at a threshold, gate, or border
- Eternally watchful, stern but fair
- Neither cruel nor gentle—simply **present**

**Epithets:**
- The Eternal Watch
- Keeper of Oaths
- The Just Blade
- Guardian at the Gates

**What Followers Believe:**

The Sentinel stands **between** the cosmic and the mortal, **protecting** creation from forces too vast for mortals to comprehend. Warriors are sacred; so are guards, judges, and any who protect others. Justice must be served, oaths must be honored, the innocent must be defended.

**"Stand firm. Keep watch. Let no harm pass your shield."**

**Connection to Primarchs:**
- Enforces the Architect's **law** with the Weaver's **adaptability**
- Justice requires both structure (law) and flexibility (mercy)
- Protects civilization while honoring the wild places

**Followers Include:**
- Warriors and soldiers
- City guards and sentinels
- Judges and magistrates
- Bodyguards and protectors
- Those who've sworn oaths
- Paladins and knights
- [[Council of Lumens]] Conservators (protect the city from itself)

**Daily Practices:**
- Morning weapons drill or meditation
- Checking defenses (literal or metaphorical)
- Honoring oaths and promises
- Standing vigil or guard duty
- Meditating on justice and duty
- Helping those who cannot defend themselves

**Temples:**
- Fortress-like structures
- Training yards and armories attached
- Eternal flames burning (never extinguished)
- Halls of remembrance for fallen guardians
- Often at city gates or borders

**The Sentinel's Perspective on [[Hollowstars]]:**

**Orthodox View:** The stars are **wards** placed by the Primarchs to protect or test the world. Mortals must **guard** them responsibly.

**Crisis Interpretation:** The failing stars mean mortals **failed their duty**. Lumencrest **exploited** rather than protected its gift. Honor demands making amends.

**Question for Followers:** If a new star falls, do you **guard it from exploitation**, or **guard those who would use it** from attackers? Both are forms of protection.

---

### The Wanderer (The Pathfinder, The Laughing God, The One Who Knows)

**Domains:** Freedom, Trade, Secrets, Travel, Curiosity, Cleverness, Communication, Boundaries (and their crossing)

**Sacred Symbol:** A road winding into stars, sometimes with footprints leading both directions

**Holy Colors:** Silver and shadow, twilight purple, changeable

**Sacred Number:** Seven (the lucky, the unexpected, the journey's days)

**Portrayed As:**
- Gender-fluid—appears as whatever the viewer needs
- Cloaked figure, face half-hidden by hood or shadow
- Often smiling or laughing
- Shown at crossroads, doorways, thresholds
- Impossible to pin down or predict

**Epithets:**
- The Walking Road
- Master of the In-Between
- The Keeper of Keys
- The Cosmic Merchant

**What Followers Believe:**

The Wanderer moves **between** all things—between order and chaos, civilization and wilderness, life and death, truth and lies. They are the **threshold**, the journey, the space between destinations. Trade is sacred (exchange creates connection), secrets are power, and freedom is the highest good.

**"Every path leads somewhere. Every door opens both ways. Walk freely."**

**Connection to Primarchs:**
- Bridges the Architect's **structure** with the Weaver's **chaos**
- Roads are ordered (Architect) but enable change and travel (Weaver)
- Neither servant nor rebel to the Primarchs—the Wanderer **connects** them

**Followers Include:**
- Merchants and traders (especially [[The Endless Eye]] merchants)
- Travelers and explorers
- Spies and information brokers
- [[Veilmarkets]] members (unorthodox worship)
- Diplomats and messengers
- Those seeking freedom
- Scholars of hidden knowledge
- [[Starfall Seekers]] (some, who seek cosmic secrets)

**Daily Practices:**
- Greeting strangers with hospitality
- Learning one new thing each day
- Conducting fair trades
- Traveling, even if just a different route home
- Keeping some secrets, sharing others
- Laughing at life's absurdities
- Respecting thresholds and boundaries (then crossing them)

**Temples:**
- Located at crossroads, ports, city gates
- Open structures—easy to enter and leave
- Markets often attached
- No doors lock from the inside
- Travel shrines along major roads
- "All roads are temples to the Wanderer"

**The Wanderer's Perspective on [[Hollowstars]]:**

**Orthodox View:** The stars are **bridges** between the cosmic and mortal realms. Their falling creates **connections** mortals can explore.

**Crisis Interpretation:** The dimming stars are **closing doors**. New doors (the approaching star) are opening. Change is neither good nor bad—it's **opportunity**.

**Secret Teaching:** The Wanderer **knows** what the stars truly are but won't tell. Discovering the truth is the journey that matters, not the destination.

---

### The Dynamic Tension (Sentinel vs. Wanderer)

**Are they allies or rivals?** **BOTH.**

- The Sentinel protects boundaries; the Wanderer crosses them
- The Sentinel values duty; the Wanderer values freedom
- The Sentinel keeps oaths; the Wanderer knows when to break them
- The Sentinel is rigid; the Wanderer is flexible

**But:**
- Guardians need to know what they're guarding (secrets)
- Travelers need safe roads (protection)
- Justice requires understanding context (wisdom)
- Freedom requires someone to fight for it (courage)

**In the Current Crisis:**
- Sentinel-worshipers want to **guard** Hollowstars and **punish** exploiters
- Wanderer-worshipers want to **explore** Hollowstars and **trade** their secrets
- Both might be necessary

## TIER 3: The Intimates

*The Mortal-Touched Pair - Closest to Daily Life*

Most commonly worshiped in daily prayers. Less cosmic, more personal.

### The Hearthkeeper (The Mother, The Tender, The Warm Hand)

**Domains:** Home, Family, Healing, Community, Love, Nourishment, Childbirth, Comfort

**Sacred Symbol:** Hearth flame, often with a cooking pot or bread

**Holy Colors:** Warm orange, golden yellow, soft brown

**Sacred Number:** Two (the pair, the partnership, the parent and child)

**Portrayed As:**
- Feminine-coded universally
- Depicted as a middle-aged woman with kind eyes, often holding a child or bread
- Warm, present, comforting
- The smell of baking bread accompanies Her presence
- Gentle but fiercely protective (mother defending child)

**Epithets:**
- The Hearth That Never Dims
- She Who Mends
- The Bread-Giver
- Mother of All

**What Followers Believe:**

The Hearthkeeper tends the **human flame**—the warmth that makes life worth living. She is in every act of care: cooking a meal, healing a wound, rocking a child to sleep, welcoming a stranger. Community is sacred; family (chosen or blood) is holy; home is the true temple.

**"Tend the flame. Feed the hungry. Heal the hurt. Home is where love dwells."**

**Connection to Higher Tiers:**
- Brings the Primarchs' cosmic forces down to **daily life**
- Makes the Mediators' abstractions **tangible** (protection = caring for family)
- Most accessible, most human of all the gods

**Followers Include:**
- Parents and caregivers
- Healers and midwives
- Farmers and cooks
- Innkeepers and hospitality workers
- Community organizers
- Anyone who builds or maintains home
- Nearly everyone prays to Her at some point

**Daily Practices:**
- Lighting the hearth or candle with morning prayer
- Cooking or sharing food
- Caring for family or community
- Healing or comforting others
- Making home welcoming
- Quiet acts of love and service

**Temples:**
- Warm, inviting spaces
- Always have kitchens and healing rooms
- Free meals for those in need
- Community gathering spaces
- Fires always burning
- "Every home is Her temple"

**The Hearthkeeper's Perspective on [[Hollowstars]]:**

**Orthodox View:** The stars are **gifts** meant to help mortals **care for each other**. Lumencrest's light helped families; the lake sustains communities.

**Crisis Interpretation:** The failing stars are asking: **"What truly sustains you?"** Not cosmic power—each other. [[The Darkness]] will test whether communities stand together or fracture.

**Practical Response:** Hearthkeeper followers organize mutual aid, food distribution, community resilience planning.

---

### The Gravewalker (The Guide, The Silent One, The Final Friend)

**Domains:** Death, Memory, Transition, Ancestors, Endings, Grief, Acceptance, Legacy

**Sacred Symbol:** Lantern in darkness, sometimes with a path leading into shadow

**Holy Colors:** Deep black, soft gray, pale blue (dawn light)

**Sacred Number:** One (the solitary journey, the single candle, the individual soul)

**Portrayed As:**
- Masculine-coded in most cultures
- Depicted as a quiet figure with a lantern, face calm and ageless
- Neither frightening nor comforting—simply **present**
- Walks beside the dying, guides the dead
- Patient, knowing, inevitable

**Epithets:**
- The Final Companion
- He Who Remembers
- The Lantern-Bearer
- Lord of the Threshold

**What Followers Believe:**

The Gravewalker **guides souls** from life to what comes after (which the Six-Fold Faith leaves deliberately vague). He is **not death itself**—He is the compassionate presence that makes death bearable. Memory is sacred; honoring the dead keeps them alive in spirit. Every ending enables a new beginning.

**"All paths end. All flames dim. But nothing is truly lost if remembered."**

**Connection to Higher Tiers:**
- Represents the Primarchs' truth that **all things change** (nothing is permanent)
- Partners with Sentinel (guards the threshold of death) and Wanderer (guides the journey)
- The Hearthkeeper's opposite and complement—She tends life; He honors endings

**Followers Include:**
- Morticians and gravekeepers
- Historians and archivists
- The elderly and dying
- Those in grief
- Memorialists and storytellers
- Anyone making peace with endings

**Daily Practices:**
- Evening prayers as sun sets
- Speaking names of the dead
- Recording history and memories
- Tending graves or memorials
- Helping others through grief
- Accepting losses with grace
- Lighting lanterns for the departed

**Temples:**
- Quiet, contemplative spaces
- Often adjacent to cemeteries
- Libraries of the dead (records of every burial)
- Lanterns lit for each soul remembered
- Gardens of remembrance
- "Graveyards are His holiest ground"

**The Gravewalker's Perspective on [[Hollowstars]]:**

**Orthodox View:** The stars, like all things, were never meant to be **eternal**. Their dimming is natural—everything that exists must eventually end.

**Crisis Interpretation:** [[The Darkness]] is **transition**, not destruction. The age of Hollowstars is ending. A new age is beginning. **Accept it.**

**Comfort Offered:** The Gravewalker's followers remind others—even if the stars die completely, the world will remember what they gave. Legacy matters more than immortality.

---

### The Intimate Tension (Hearthkeeper vs. Gravewalker)

**Are they opposites?** **Yes, but inseparable.**

- She brings life into the world; He guides it out
- She lights the hearth; He lights the lantern for the dead
- She celebrates beginnings; He honors endings
- She clings; He releases

**But:**
- Both are **present** at every transition
- Both offer **comfort** in hardship
- Both understand **love** (She loves the living; He loves the dead)
- Life without death is meaningless; death without life is nothing

**In the Current Crisis:**
- Hearthkeeper-worshipers **fight** [[The Darkness]] (protect what we have)
- Gravewalker-worshipers **accept** it (all things end, make peace)
- Communities need both responses to survive

## Worship Practices

### The Hexarchies (Grand Temples)

**Architecture:**
- Six shrines arranged around a central nave (sacred center)
- Each shrine dedicated to one god, facing outward
- Central space for communal worship and festivals
- Geometric perfection (Architect) with organic decoration (Weaver)

**Daily Services:**
- Dawn: The Weaver and Hearthkeeper (beginning of life/day)
- Noon: The Architect and Sentinel (order and protection)
- Dusk: The Wanderer and Gravewalker (endings and journeys)

**The Six Bells:**
Each temple has six bells, one per god, rung at appropriate times:
- Architect: Deep, resonant, precise timing
- Weaver: Chiming, irregular, musical
- Sentinel: Strong, clear, martial
- Wanderer: Traveling, sometimes distant, unpredictable
- Hearthkeeper: Warm, welcoming, comforting
- Gravewalker: Soft, solemn, final

### Life Ceremonies

**Birth:** Hearthkeeper (blessing) + Weaver (welcome change) + Architect (name in the records)

**Coming of Age:** All six gods blessed, youth chooses a patron

**Marriage:** Hearthkeeper (home) + Architect (contract/oath) + Sentinel (protection)

**Death:** Gravewalker (primary) + All six honor the life lived

**Contracts/Oaths:** Architect (witness) + Sentinel (enforcement)

**Departure on Journey:** Wanderer (guidance) + Sentinel (protection)

### Prayer Practices

**Formal Prayers:**
- Scripted liturgies in temples
- Hexarchs lead on holy days
- All six gods honored in sequence

**Personal Prayers:**
- Pray to whichever god suits the need
- Most common: Hearthkeeper (daily life), Sentinel (protection), Wanderer (travel)
- Informal, personal, constant

**The Six-Fold Blessing:**
> *"By the Architect's plan,*
> *By the Weaver's hand,*
> *By the Sentinel's shield,*
> *By the Wanderer's road,*
> *By the Hearthkeeper's flame,*
> *By the Gravewalker's lamp,*
> *May all find their path."*

### Holy Days & Festivals

**The Turning of the Year (Seasonal Festivals):**
- **Spring Weaving** (Spring Equinox): Celebrate the Weaver, plant seeds, embrace change
- **Summer's Shield** (Summer Solstice): Honor the Sentinel, martial games, oath-taking
- **Autumn's Hearthfire** (Autumn Equinox): Thank the Hearthkeeper, harvest feasts, family gatherings
- **Winter's Rest** (Winter Solstice): Remember with the Gravewalker, honor the dead, quiet reflection

**The Architect's Day:**
- New Year (varies by culture, often winter)
- Make plans, set foundations, renew structures
- Contracts signed, laws reviewed, buildings begun

**The Wanderer's Week:**
- Week-long celebration mid-summer
- Markets, festivals, travel encouraged
- No business as usual—try something new each day
- Secrets revealed and trades made

## Theological Debates & Heresies

### Current Debates Within the Faith

#### 1. The Star-Born Heresy

**Claim:** The [[Hollowstars]] **ARE** the Six Gods, not their creations.

**Evidence They Cite:**
- Six confirmed Hollostar falls in ancient texts (six gods?)
- Stars bring both order (power grids) and chaos (transformations)
- They grant blessings (abilities) like gods do

**Orthodox Response:** Blasphemy. The gods created the stars as tools. Mortals confuse the gift for the giver.

**Growing Support:** Especially among [[Starfall Seekers]] and desperate citizens in [[Lumencrest]]

**Danger:** If the stars ARE gods and they're dying...

---

#### 2. The Seventh God Controversy

**Claim:** There is a **seventh god** never spoken of—the god of [[The Darkness]] itself.

**Evidence:**
- Six is incomplete (why not seven, the Wanderer's sacred number?)
- Ancient texts have lacunae (blank spaces) where a seventh might have been
- Something opposes the Six—what if it's divine too?

**Orthodox Response:** Dangerous nonsense. Six is the perfect balance. Seven would break the symmetry.

**Secret Whisper:** Some Hexarchs quietly wonder if this might be true.

---

#### 3. The Primarch Schism

**Question:** Are the Architect and Weaver **actually fighting**?

**Architect-Supremacists:** The Architect is supreme; the Weaver is chaos that must be controlled. Order is holiness.

**Weaver-Supremacists:** The Weaver is supreme; the Architect is stagnation to be overcome. Change is holiness.

**Orthodox Position:** BOTH are necessary. The tension creates existence.

**In Practice:** Cities lean Architect; rural areas lean Weaver. [[Lumencrest]] vs. [[The Endless Eye]] reflects this divide.

---

#### 4. The Empty Tier Theory

**Claim:** Tier 2 (Mediators) don't actually exist—they're metaphors for mortal virtues.

**Reasoning:** 
- Sentinel and Wanderer never speak directly in myths
- They're always described doing mortal-like things
- Perhaps they represent what mortals should aspire to be

**Orthodox Response:** They exist. They're simply more subtle than the others.

**Who Believes This:** Philosophers, skeptics, those who've never felt divine presence

---

### Ancient Heresies (Mostly Extinct)

- **The All-One Heresy:** All six are facets of one supreme god
  - **Status:** NOT extinct - evolved into [[The First Flame Faith]]
  - ~300 years ago, this "heresy" became an organized religion
  - Now a legitimate competitor to Hextheism in some regions
  - The theological debate continues
- **The Mortal Ascension:** The six were once mortal, ascended through great deeds
- **The Clockwork Universe:** The gods wound up creation like a machine and left

## The Six-Fold Faith and [[Hollowstars]]

### Official Hexarch Position

**Joint Statement from the Council (Recent):**

*"The falling stars are divine in origin, whether message, test, or tool. Their dimming is cause for concern, not despair. We call upon the faithful to prayer, charity, and careful study. The gods have not abandoned us. We must not abandon each other."*

**Translation:** They have no idea what's happening, but don't want panic.

### Individual Hexarch Positions

Each of the six high priests interprets differently:

**Hexarch of the Architect:**
- Advocates for rigorous study and controlled experiments
- "Understand the design to fix what's broken"
- Supports [[Council of Lumens]] Illuminists (cautiously)

**Hexarch of the Weaver:**
- Calls for acceptance and adaptation
- "The world changes. We must change with it."
- Sympathetic to [[The Endless Eye]] traditionalists

**Hexarch of the Sentinel:**
- Demands protection of Hollowstars from exploitation
- "We failed as guardians. We must make amends."
- Supports Conservators and opposes [[Green Covenant]] extremism (violence isn't protection)

**Hexarch of the Wanderer:**
- Encourages exploration and discovery
- "Every crisis is a threshold. Walk through it and see."
- Quietly funds [[Starfall Seekers]] expeditions

**Hexarch of the Hearthkeeper:**
- Focuses on community resilience
- "Stars may dim, but we have each other"
- Organizes aid for communities affected by power/water shortages

**Hexarch of the Gravewalker:**
- Preaches acceptance of endings
- "If the age of stars is ending, honor what they gave"
- Prepares for worst-case scenarios (mass deaths, collapse)

**The Tension:** They can't agree on a unified response. The gods' tension reflects in their servants.

## Gameplay Integration

### For Players

**Clerics of the Six-Fold Faith:**
- Choose a patron god from the six
- Access to specific domains/powers themed to that god
- Tension with followers of other gods in the pantheon
- Can pray to any of the six, but patron is primary

**Character Backgrounds:**
- Raised in Hexarchy temple (which god?)
- Hexarch's apprentice or acolyte
- Crisis of faith over Hollowstar situation
- Torn between two gods' philosophies

### For GMs

**Use Religious Tension:**
- NPCs argue theology
- Different temples offer different aid/interpretations
- Hexarchs can be patrons, rivals, or obstacles
- Religious debates reflect player moral choices

**The Gods Don't Answer Directly:**
- Prayers grant power (Daggerheart mechanics)
- But no divine voice says "do this"
- Mortals interpret signs, omens, feelings
- Ambiguity is intentional—faith requires uncertainty
