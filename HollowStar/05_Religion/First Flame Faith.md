## Core Theology

### The Eternal Flame - The One God

**Primary Name:** **The Eternal Flame** (also called The First Flame, The Divine Flame, The Radiant Unity)

**Nature:**
- **One divine being** with six aspects/faces, not six separate gods
- **Gender:** Neither/both - appears as needed by culture and individual
- **Personality:** All things the divine must be:
  - Wrathful judge when justice is needed
  - Loving parent when comfort is needed  
  - Distant watchmaker when mortals must learn
  - Present companion when guidance is needed
- **Intervention:** Acts through blessings (good outcomes) and trials (hardships that teach)

**The Six-in-One Truth:**

The First Flame faith accepts the same cosmology as the [[Six-Fold Faith]] but rejects the idea of six separate divine beings. Instead:

- **The Architect** = The Eternal Flame's aspect of ORDER
- **The Weaver** = The Eternal Flame's aspect of CHANGE  
- **The Sentinel** = The Eternal Flame's aspect of PROTECTION
- **The Wanderer** = The Eternal Flame's aspect of FREEDOM
- **The Hearthkeeper** = The Eternal Flame's aspect of LOVE
- **The Gravewalker** = The Eternal Flame's aspect of TRANSITION

**"The Hextheists see six gods in conflict. We see one god in perfect, dynamic unity."**

### The Flame Metaphor

**Fire as Sacred:**
- Eternal flames burn in all temples - some lit for hundreds of years
- Braziers that never go out (tended constantly by devoted followers)
- Fire represents: truth, knowledge, enlightenment, purification, unity
- "The light of truth and knowledge illuminates the darkness of ignorance"

**Every Soul is a Flame:**
- Each person has an "eternal flame" within - their soul, goodness, worthiness
- You **tend your flame** through good works and righteous living
- **Bright flame** = good person, worthy of return
- **Dim flame** = person who failed to live well, fades into nothing after death
- The brightest flames are **rekindled** - reincarnated when the Eternal Flame has need

## Creation & Cosmology

### The Beginning

**Simple Truth:** The Eternal Flame created everything from nothing, speaking reality into being through six great acts of creation (corresponding to the six aspects). This is essentially the same creation story as the [[Six-Fold Faith]], just attributed to one unified god rather than six.

### Relationship to the Six-Fold Faith

**First Flame Position on Hextheism:**

- The Six Gods of the Hextheists **are real** - they are the six aspects of the Eternal Flame
- Hextheists are **not enemies** - they're misguided siblings on a similar path
- They worship the same divine reality, just **misunderstand its nature**
- "They see the fingers of one hand and call each a separate being. We see the whole."

**Hextheist Position on First Flame:**

- Most view them as heretics spreading the ancient "All-One Heresy"
- Some (especially moderates) see them as **harmless but wrong**
- A few secretly wonder if they might be onto something
- The theological debate continues (see [[Six-Fold Faith]] for details)

### On [[Hollowstars]]

**This is where denominations differ most dramatically.** See denominations below for specific interpretations.

**General Agreement:**
- Hollowstars are somehow connected to the divine
- Their use is not inherently sinful (unlike transformed creatures)
- The current crisis is significant and requires response
- Non-believers can use them, but outcomes may not be as blessed

## The Divine Leader: High Flame Kaida

### Background

**Name:** Kaida  
**Title:** High Flame (supreme spiritual authority)  
**Ancestry:** Giant (Firbolg-descended)  
**Origin:** Frostwell Tundra - the frozen north  
**Age:** Elderly (exact age unknown, but visibly ancient)  
**Time in Power:** 50 years  
**Chosen:** By divine sign - during meditation, an eternal flame that had burned for 200 years suddenly flared, illuminating only her

### Her Legendary Journey

Before becoming High Flame, Kaida undertook the most difficult pilgrimage in First Flame history:

**The Route:**
1. **Frostwell Tundra** (starting point - frozen wastes)
2. **Over the Skyspine Mountains** (treacherous peaks, near-impossible for a Giant's size)
3. **Through the volcanic plains** (the region that became [[Desert of Glass]])
4. **Into the Central Heartlands** (where the central temple stands)

**Significance:**
- All Divine Leaders claim to have made difficult journeys
- Kaida's is the most documented and verified
- She was the first Giant to hold the position
- Her journey took three years
- She nearly died twice (once from cold, once from heat)
- Unites all denominations - even those who don't require pilgrimage respect her achievement
- **Symbol of unity:** She bridged the frozen north and the burning south

### Her Character

**Personality:**
- Calm and diplomatic
- Speaks slowly and deliberately (thinks before every word)
- Warm but reserved
- Physically imposing (she's a Giant) but gentle in demeanor
- True believer - absolutely convinced of the Eternal Flame's unity
- **Not dogmatic** - respects all denominations equally

**Leadership Style:**
- Refuses to favor any denomination over others
- Unifying force - keeps the six denominations working together
- Seeks consensus through prayer, study, and dialogue
- **Her crisis response:** "We must study, pray, and stand united. Division will destroy us faster than any darkness."

**Current Focus:**

On the [[Hollowstars]] crisis:
- Convened councils with all denomination leaders
- Encourages **study** (supports Scholarly Flame research)
- Demands **prayer** (supports Guiding Light contemplation)
- Insists on **unity** (prevents denominations from fracturing)
- Privately troubled - the Eternal Flame has not given her clear guidance
- Refuses to declare any denomination's interpretation as "correct"

### Selection of the Divine Leader

**How They're Chosen:**

Not elected, not hereditary, not appointed - **chosen by divine sign**.

**The Process:**
1. When a High Flame dies, the Flamekeepers pray and meditate
2. The Eternal Flame reveals its choice through unmistakable signs:
   - Visions experienced by multiple people simultaneously
   - Eternal flames behaving impossibly (pointing, flaring, speaking)
   - The chosen feeling overwhelming divine presence
3. The sign must be **witnessed** and **verified** by at least half the Flamekeepers
4. The chosen can refuse (rare), at which point the Flame chooses another

**Lifetime Appointment:**
- Once chosen, you serve until death
- No abdication (though a few have tried to resign and been "convinced" otherwise)
- Average tenure: 15-25 years
- Kaida's 50 years is unusually long

**Kaida's Selection:**

She was meditating in a temple in the [[Central Heartlands]] (visiting as a traveling priestess of the Radiant Path denomination). The eternal flame - which had burned steadily for 200 years - suddenly **exploded** in height, filling the chamber with light that touched only her. Witnesses said her shadow showed six forms simultaneously (the six aspects). She heard the voice of the Eternal Flame speaking her name in six harmonies.

She tried to refuse (feeling unworthy). The flame refused to return to normal until she accepted. She's served ever since.

## The Flamekeepers

### Structure

**Title:** Flamekeeper  
**Number:** Minimum 7, maximum 13 at any time  
**Current Count:** 9  
**Selection:** Appointed by the High Flame with approval from existing Flamekeepers  
**Role:** Advisors, administrators, keepers of sacred flames

**Why Not "Cardinals"?**

First Flame theology emphasizes **tending** over **ruling**. Flamekeepers don't govern regions - they maintain the faith's spiritual health, keep eternal flames burning, and counsel the High Flame.

### Their Duties

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- **Tend the eternal flames** in major temples
- **Advise the High Flame** on theological and practical matters
- **Mediate between denominations** when conflicts arise
- **Oversee major rituals** and holy days
- **Preserve sacred texts** and traditions
- **Train priests** and maintain doctrinal consistency
- **Remain neutral** - cannot publicly favor one denomination

**Cannot Do:**
- Take sides in denominational disputes
- Claim authority over the High Flame
- Make unilateral theological declarations
- Rule territories or wield political power

### Functional Roles

Flamekeepers have specializations:

**Example Roles:**
- **Keeper of the Book** - Preserves and interprets *The Book of the Flame*
- **Keeper of Rites** - Oversees ceremonies and holy days
- **Keeper of Flames** - Manages temple eternal flames
- **Keeper of Unity** - Mediates denominational conflicts
- **Keeper of Pilgrims** - Oversees those undertaking journeys
- **Keeper of the Lost** - Ministers to apostates and wayward faithful
- **Keeper of Study** - Coordinates with scholars (especially Scholarly Flame)

### Historical Notes

**Maximum Ever:** 13 Flamekeepers during a theological crisis 400 years ago (extra mediators needed)

**Minimum Ever:** 5, during a plague that killed several

**The Rivalry Flamekeepers:**

Two Flamekeepers in the past became rivals, each claiming different interpretations of the Eternal Flame's will. Their conflict split the church and led to **The Unbound** schism (see below). Since then, Flamekeepers who show ambition or divisiveness are quietly asked to step down.

## The Six Denominations

All six denominations are **equal under the High Flame**. They differ in emphasis, not core theology. Members may change denominations throughout life.

### 1. The Purifying Flame

**Core Identity:** Orthodox, traditional, fire-and-brimstone (but not violent)

**On [[Hollowstars]]:**
- Stars are **divine tests of faith**
- Using them is acceptable **if done righteously** for holy purposes
- **Crisis interpretation:** The dimming is divine punishment for unbelievers corrupting the gifts
- **Solution:** Reclaim and purify Hollowstars from unworthy hands (through conversion, not violence)

**Character:**
- Strict but passionate
- Evangelical (most active in converting others)
- Emphasis on sin, repentance, purification
- Fire as metaphor for burning away impurity
- "The Flame separates gold from dross"

**Practices:**
- Confession rituals (speak sins into flame, watch them burn)
- Fasting and self-denial during holy periods
- Vigorous preaching and public testimony
- Bright, dramatic religious services

**Where Strong:** [[Desert of Glass]] - where volcanic fire and heat define survival

**Relationship with Others:**
- **Tension with Scholarly Flame** (too friendly with unbelievers)
- **Tension with Unified Ember** (too compromising)
- **Respect Radiant Path** (share ascetic values)
- **Tolerate Guiding Light** (mysterious but sincere)
- **Work with Kindling** (both evangelical)

**Notable Features:**
- Temples often built near volcanic vents or hot springs
- Priests wear red and orange robes
- Services include fire-walking and flame-handling by the devoted

---

### 2. The Guiding Light

**Core Identity:** Mystical, contemplative, meditative

**On [[Hollowstars]]:**
- Stars are **divine mysteries** mortals shouldn't fully understand
- Reverence and study are good; exploitation is dangerous
- **Crisis interpretation:** The Eternal Flame is teaching us to rely on **inner light**, not external cosmic power
- **Solution:** Turn inward, meditate, seek enlightenment through contemplation

**Character:**
- Quiet, thoughtful, introspective
- Emphasis on personal spiritual experience
- Fire as metaphor for inner illumination
- "The Flame within shines brighter than any star"

**Practices:**
- Long meditation sessions before eternal flames
- Silent retreats and contemplative prayer
- Mystical experiences encouraged (visions, divine communion)
- Minimal dogma - emphasis on personal journey
- Soft, peaceful religious services

**Where Strong:** [[The Endless Eye]] - fits the region's non-judgmental, thoughtful culture

**Relationship with Others:**
- **Appreciated by Scholarly Flame** (both value understanding)
- **Tolerated by Purifying Flame** (too passive)
- **Admired by Unified Ember** (peaceful coexistence)
- **Respected by Radiant Path** (different approaches, same devotion)
- **Supported by Kindling** (provide spiritual depth)

**Notable Features:**
- Temples designed for acoustics and silence
- Underwater meditation chambers at The Endless Eye
- Priests wear white and soft blue
- Known for healing and counseling services

---

### 3. The Kindling

**Core Identity:** Evangelical, missionary-focused, growth-oriented

**On [[Hollowstars]]:**
- Stars are **tools the Eternal Flame provides** to spread faith and help people
- Using them to heal, protect, and convert honors the divine gift
- **Crisis interpretation:** This is our moment! Crisis creates opportunity to bring people to truth
- **Solution:** Use the fear and uncertainty to demonstrate the Eternal Flame's love and power

**Character:**
- Warm, passionate, outgoing
- Emphasis on spreading the faith through word and deed
- Fire as metaphor for spark that spreads from person to person
- "One flame lights a thousand candles"

**Practices:**
- Active missionary work (peaceful, through hospitality and aid)
- Community service and charity
- Vibrant, musical services with lots of participation
- Storytelling and testimony
- Establishing new temples and congregations

**Where Strong:** Coastal regions - spread via trade routes and port cities

**Relationship with Others:**
- **Partner with Scholarly Flame** (use research to support missionary claims)
- **Collaborate with Purifying Flame** (both evangelical, different methods)
- **Work with Unified Ember** (both focus on practical good works)
- **Respect Guiding Light** (provide the mystical depth Kindling lacks)
- **Tolerate Radiant Path** (too harsh for Kindling's warm approach)

**Notable Features:**
- Temples often have large communal kitchens and guest houses
- "Flame-bearer" missionaries travel trade routes
- Priests wear bright yellow and gold
- Known for excellent hospitality

---

### 4. The Radiant Path

**Core Identity:** Ascetic, demanding, pilgrimage-focused

**On [[Hollowstars]]:**
- Stars **test our worthiness** - true believers need no cosmic crutch
- Relying on them shows weakness of faith
- **Crisis interpretation:** This proves dependence on stars was folly from the start
- **Solution:** Survive through faith alone; demonstrate the Eternal Flame sustains without need for fallen stars

**Character:**
- Austere, disciplined, challenging
- Emphasis on self-denial and spiritual purity
- Fire as metaphor for refining through heat and hardship
- "The Flame demands sacrifice"

**Practices:**
- **The Great Pilgrimage:** Travel from one's home to [[Frostwell Tundra]] and back
  - Proves devotion and worthiness
  - Traditional path for those seeking to become Flamekeepers
  - High Flame Kaida's journey is their ultimate example
- Fasting, vigils, physical hardship as spiritual exercise
- Minimal services - individual devotion over communal worship
- Reject comfort and luxury

**Where Strong:** Scattered - their difficulty attracts fewer but intensely devoted followers

**Relationship with Others:**
- **Tension with Scholarly Flame** (head knowledge vs. heart faith)
- **Respect Purifying Flame** (share strict values)
- **Clash with Kindling** (too warm and easy)
- **Admire Guiding Light** (discipline in meditation)
- **Dismissive of Unified Ember** (too compromising)

**Notable Features:**
- Small, simple temples (often just a room with an eternal flame)
- Priests often show marks of their pilgrimages (frostbite scars, etc.)
- Wear rough gray and brown robes
- The smallest denomination numerically, but highly respected

---

### 5. The Unified Ember

**Core Identity:** Moderate, diplomatic, bridge-builders

**On [[Hollowstars]]:**
- Stars are **part of the Eternal Flame's design** - neither inherently good nor bad
- What matters is **how we use them** - for selfish gain or common good
- **Crisis interpretation:** Work together (even with Hextheists and unbelievers) to understand and solve this
- **Solution:** Collaboration, diplomacy, finding common ground across faiths

**Character:**
- Balanced, pragmatic, inclusive
- Emphasis on unity and cooperation
- Fire as metaphor for warmth that brings people together
- "The Flame warms all who gather near"

**Practices:**
- Interfaith dialogue and cooperation
- Community organizing and mutual aid
- Moderate services - blend of traditions from other denominations
- Focus on ethical living and practical good works
- Welcoming to seekers and questioners

**Where Strong:** [[Lumencrest]] - where they must coexist with majority Hextheists

**Relationship with Others:**
- **Partner with Scholarly Flame** (both diplomatic)
- **Work with Kindling** (shared focus on service)
- **Appreciate Guiding Light** (peaceful approach)
- **Frustrated by Purifying Flame** (too rigid)
- **Respect Radiant Path** (disagree on methods)

**Notable Features:**
- Temples designed to welcome all (comfortable, accessible)
- Often host interfaith councils and community meetings
- Priests wear earth tones and gentle flame colors
- Most popular among converts from other faiths

---

### 6. The Scholarly Flame

**Core Identity:** Academic, research-focused, intellectual

**On [[Hollowstars]]:**
- Understanding stars through **rigorous study** honors the Eternal Flame's gift of knowledge
- The divine gave mortals minds - **use them**
- **Crisis interpretation:** Ignorance and superstition caused this; only systematic scholarship can solve it
- **Solution:** Research, document, test hypotheses, collaborate across faiths and borders

**Character:**
- Intellectual, methodical, evidence-based
- Emphasis on learning and education
- Fire as metaphor for enlightenment and illumination of truth
- "The Flame lights the way to understanding"

**Practices:**
- Maintain vast libraries alongside temples
- Study as prayer - research is worship
- Encourage literacy and education for all faithful
- Document everything meticulously
- Share findings openly (knowledge spreads like flame)
- Collaborate with non-believers on scholarly work

**Where Strong:** [[Lumencrest]], universities, centers of learning

**Relationship with Others:**
- **Respected by most** for expertise and usefulness
- **Viewed skeptically by Purifying Flame** (too cozy with unbelievers)
- **Appreciated by Unified Ember** (shared diplomatic approach)
- **Admired by Guiding Light** (both value deep understanding)
- **Tolerated by Radiant Path** (head vs. heart tension)
- **Partners with Kindling** (provide intellectual backing)

**Notable Features:**
- Temples indistinguishable from libraries (books everywhere)
- **Why Lumencrest tolerates First Flame presence** - Scholarly Flame provides valuable research
- Collaborate with [[Council of Lumens]] Illuminists on Hollostar studies
- Priests wear simple scholarly robes, often ink-stained
- Awkward position: theology says Lumencrest's exploitation is wrong, but they value the research

**Current Crisis Role:**

Most active in studying the approaching [[Hollowstars|Hollostar]]. They:
- Chart its course with Lumencrest astronomers
- Analyze dimming patterns in existing stars
- Study transformed creatures (despite theological prohibition on eating them)
- Collaborate with [[Starfall Seekers]] (the scholarly ones)
- Walk a tightrope between faith and science

## The Unbound (Schism)

### Origin

**Founded:** ~80 years ago  
**Cause:** Rejection of Divine Leader authority  
**Leaders:** Two ambitious Flamekeepers who believed no mortal should claim divine appointment

**The Split:**

Two Flamekeepers - Serath and Velyn - publicly challenged the then-High Flame's divine selection, claiming:
- "No mortal voice speaks for the Eternal Flame alone"
- "We are all equal before the divine"
- "Authority should come from community consensus, not claimed visions"

They were excommunicated. They founded **The Unbound** - First Flame theology without a centralized divine authority.

### Current Status

**Size:** Small but growing  
**Location:** Same regions as main First Flame church, concentrated on **coastal areas** (where news and ideas spread fast)  
**Relationship with Main Church:** Separate, with growing hostility from the Eternal Flame toward them

**Why Growing Hostility?**

- Initially tolerated as wayward siblings
- But The Unbound are **effective missionaries** - their lack of hierarchy appeals to some
- They claim moral high ground: "We don't elevate mortals to godhood"
- The main church sees them as **threatening** - if they grow too much, it undermines the High Flame's authority
- Recent incidents of Unbound congregations "stealing" members from Eternal Flame temples

### Theology & Practice

**Core Difference:** No Divine Leader, no Flamekeepers

**Structure:**
- Individual congregations govern themselves
- Regional councils coordinate (no one supreme authority)
- Decisions made by community consensus

**Otherwise Identical:**
- Same six-in-one theology
- Same Book of the Flame (though their edition includes additional commentaries)
- Same sacred fire practices
- Same denominations (loosely - they don't formalize it)

**Sacred Text:** *The Testament of Unity* (their version of The Book of the Flame with added texts emphasizing communal authority)

**Greeting:** 
- Main Church: "The Flame burns eternal" / "And lights the way"
- **The Unbound: "In the light of the One" / "We are unified"**

**Appeal:**
- Attractive to those suspicious of hierarchical authority
- Popular among merchants and traders (coastal concentration)
- Some former Hextheists prefer it (Hextheism also has no single supreme authority)

## Salvation & the Afterlife

### The Eternal Flame Within

**Core Belief:** Every person has an "eternal flame" - their soul, essence, goodness.

**During Life:**
- You **tend your flame** through:
  - Good works (emphasized over faith alone)
  - Righteous living
  - Service to others
  - Learning and growth
  - Hospitality and kindness
- **Bright flame** = person living well
- **Dim flame** = person who neglects their spiritual life

### After Death

**The Judgment:**

When you die, the Eternal Flame measures your inner flame:

**Option 1: Bright Flame (The Worthy)**
- Your flame **does not extinguish**
- You enter a state of peaceful rest (exact nature deliberately vague)
- **Most importantly:** You may be **called back**

**Option 2: Dim Flame (The Unworthy)**
- Your flame **flickers out**
- You cease to exist (no hellish punishment, just... nothing)
- No second chances, no return

### The Rekindling - Reincarnation

**When the Eternal Flame Has Need:**

Occasionally, the divine needs a soul returned to the world:
- Great crisis requiring a hero
- Specific wisdom from a past life needed
- Balance requiring restoration

**The Process:**
- Your eternal flame is **rekindled** in a new body
- **Literal reincarnation** - new birth, new life
- **Usually no memory** of past lives (extremely rare exceptions)

**Evidence:**

- Only a handful of people have publicly claimed to remember past lives
- The church neither confirms nor denies such claims
- Some believe it's metaphorical (your legacy lives on through influence)
- Most take it literally but acknowledge mystery

**Who Gets Rekindled?**

Unknown. The Eternal Flame's choice. Could be heroes, could be ordinary people whose specific traits are needed. This is pure divine will.

## Sacred Practices

### Daily Observances

**The Morning Kindling:**

Every faithful First Flame follower performs this ritual at dawn:

1. **Light a flame** (candle, lamp, or approach an eternal flame)
2. **The Six-to-One Gesture:**
   - Touch forehead (Architect - mind/order)
   - Touch heart (Weaver - emotion/change)
   - Touch right shoulder (Sentinel - protection)
   - Touch left shoulder (Wanderer - freedom)
   - Touch stomach (Hearthkeeper - life/sustenance)
   - Touch throat (Gravewalker - final breath)
   - Then press both palms together over heart (Unity - all are One)
3. **Silent prayer** - personal communion with the Eternal Flame

**Takes about 2-3 minutes. Skipping it doesn't condemn you, but feels wrong.**

### Weekly Services

**In Temples:**

- Gather weekly (specific day varies by region)
- **Communal prayer** - congregation speaks together
- **Silence before the flame** - meditative period
- **On holy days: Sermons** about the day's significance
- Chanting and drumming (except in Guiding Light temples - they prefer silence)

**Structure:**
- Opening: Priest lights ceremonial flame from eternal flame
- Teaching or meditation
- Communal prayer
- Closing: Six-to-One gesture performed together

### Holy Days

**Shared with Hextheists:**

The First Flame observes the same seasonal festivals as the [[Six-Fold Faith]], but interprets them as aspects of one god:

- **Spring Weaving** - Celebrate the Change aspect
- **Summer's Shield** - Celebrate the Protection aspect
- **Autumn's Hearthfire** - Celebrate the Love aspect
- **Winter's Rest** - Celebrate the Transition aspect
- **Architect's Day** - Celebrate the Order aspect
- **Wanderer's Week** - Celebrate the Freedom aspect

**Unique First Flame Holy Days:**

1. **The Unification** (anniversary of the faith's founding, 300 years ago)
   - Celebrates the revelation that the Six are One
   - Major festival, week-long in some regions
   - Emphasis on unity and reconciliation

2. **The Kindling Day** (date varies by region)
   - Honors the moment a local eternal flame was first lit
   - Local celebration, not universal
   - Renewal of temple and community commitments

3. **The Pilgrims' Return** (late spring)
   - Honors those returning from journeys (especially Radiant Path pilgrims)
   - Welcoming celebrations, feasts
   - Stories of journeys shared

### The Sacred Restrictions

**All Faithful Must:**

1. **The Hospitality Law**
   - Must offer food and water to strangers who ask
   - Includes enemies (unless actively in combat)
   - Refusal is grave sin
   - "The Flame warms all who come near"

2. **The Purity Law**
   - **Cannot eat Hollostar-transformed creatures**
   - Reason: They give a "buzz" or altered state (like intoxication)
   - Considered impure, corrupted by cosmic forces
   - Eating them dims your eternal flame

**Minor Observances (encouraged but not required):**

- Let candles burn out naturally when possible (wasteful extinguishing disrespects the flame)
- Share meals when able (community-building)
- Never use fire to destroy sacred texts (any faith's texts)

## Sacred Texts

### The Book of the Flame

**Primary Scripture:**

- ~500 pages
- Structure: Six major sections (one per aspect of the Eternal Flame)
- Contains: Creation story, moral teachings, prophecies, prayers, rituals
- **Shared foundation** with Hextheist texts (early sections nearly identical)
- **Key difference:** Attributes everything to one unified god

**The Six Sections:**

1. **Book of Order** (The Architect aspect) - laws, structure, civilization
2. **Book of Change** (The Weaver aspect) - growth, transformation, nature
3. **Book of Protection** (The Sentinel aspect) - justice, courage, duty
4. **Book of Freedom** (The Wanderer aspect) - journeys, trade, discovery
5. **Book of Love** (The Hearthkeeper aspect) - family, community, healing
6. **Book of Transition** (The Gravewalker aspect) - death, memory, acceptance

**The Unification Chapter:**

Final chapter unique to First Flame (not in Hextheist texts):
- Reveals the Six-in-One truth
- Account of the original revelation
- Theological arguments for unity

### Other Sacred Texts

**Accepted as Holy (but secondary):**

- **The Testament of Unity** (Unbound version with additional commentaries)
- **The Pilgrim's Path** (accounts of great journeys, especially important to Radiant Path)
- **The Flame-Letters** (collected writings of early church leaders)
- **The Book of Rekindlings** (accounts of those who claimed past-life memories)

**Some denominations add:**
- Scholarly Flame: Academic treatises on theology
- Purifying Flame: Texts on sin and redemption
- Guiding Light: Mystical visions and meditative practices

## Temples & Sacred Spaces

### Grand Cathedrals

**Architecture:**
- Soaring structures (where resources allow)
- Central eternal flame in main chamber
- Six alcoves or chapels (one per aspect)
- Designed for acoustics (chanting, drumming echoes)
- Large gathering spaces for congregations

**The Eternal Flame:**
- Never allowed to extinguish
- Tended day and night by devoted attendants
- Some flames have burned for 200+ years
- Lighting a new eternal flame is a major ceremony

**Sensory Elements:**
- **Sight:** Fire light, stained glass showing six aspects unified
- **Sound:** Drums, chanting, echoes
- **Smell:** Specific incenses (vary by region and denomination)
  - Desert of Glass: Sharp, resinous scents
  - Endless Eye: Water lily and lotus
  - Coastal: Sea salt and herbs
  - Lumencrest: Old parchment and ink (Scholarly Flame influence)

### Variation by Denomination

**Purifying Flame:**
- Dramatic, with fire pits and volcanic imagery
- Red and orange color schemes
- Built near natural fire sources when possible

**Guiding Light:**
- Serene, designed for silence and meditation
- Soft blues and whites
- Water features alongside fire (The Endless Eye specialty)

**Kindling:**
- Warm, welcoming, communal spaces prominent
- Bright yellows and golds
- Large kitchens and guest quarters attached

**Radiant Path:**
- Simple, austere, minimal decoration
- Small, just a room with eternal flame
- Gray and brown tones

**Unified Ember:**
- Comfortable, accessible, blending styles
- Earth tones
- Multi-purpose spaces (meeting rooms, classrooms)

**Scholarly Flame:**
- Indistinguishable from libraries
- Books everywhere, reading tables

## Current Crisis Response

### High Flame Kaida's Approach

**Public Stance:**

"The Eternal Flame presents us with a trial. We will face it through **study, prayer, and unity**."

**Actual Strategy:**

1. **Convene councils** - bring denomination leaders together regularly
2. **Encourage research** - support Scholarly Flame's collaboration with Lumencrest
3. **Maintain unity** - prevent denominations from fracturing
4. **Prepare practically** - stockpile resources, organize mutual aid
5. **Seek divine guidance** - constant prayer and meditation
6. **Refuse to panic** - project calm authority

**Private Concerns:**

- The Eternal Flame **hasn't given her clear guidance** (troubling for someone chosen by divine sign)
- She fears the approaching star will fracture the church (denominations will interpret differently)
- She's elderly; if she dies during the crisis, choosing a new Divine Leader during chaos could split the faith
- The Unbound are growing; crisis makes their "no human authority" message appealing

### Denominational Responses

**Purifying Flame:**
- Most active in **blaming unbelievers**
- "This is divine punishment for Lumencrest's arrogance and Hextheist error"
- Increased evangelical efforts
- Some calling for "purification" of Hollowstars (reclaim them from unworthy hands)

**Guiding Light:**
- Increased meditation and contemplative practice
- Offering spiritual counseling to frightened people
- Mostly passive - "The Flame teaches through silence"
- Focus on inner peace during outer chaos

**Kindling:**
- **Massive missionary push**
- Using crisis fear to convert: "Join the Eternal Flame and find peace"
- Providing aid and hospitality to refugees and displaced
- Growing fastest of all denominations

**Radiant Path:**
- "We told you so" attitude (dependence on stars was always wrong)
- Increased asceticism and self-denial
- Calling faithful to **reject all Hollostar use**
- Pilgrimages continuing despite danger (proves faith)

**Unified Ember:**
- Organizing **interfaith cooperation**
- Working with Hextheists and secular authorities
- Practical mutual aid (food stores, shelter networks)
- Bridge-building between denominations

**Scholarly Flame:**
- **Most active in actual research**
- Collaborating with Lumencrest scientists and Starfall Seekers
- Documenting everything
- Torn between theology (dimming is divine will) and science (dimming is measurable phenomenon)
- Some questioning whether faith and evidence can be reconciled

## Relationship with Other Faiths

### With the [[Six-Fold Faith]]

**Official Position:**

"We worship the same divine reality. They see six gods; we see six aspects of one. We are siblings in faith, not enemies."

**In Practice:**

- **Varies wildly by region and individual**
- Generally peaceful coexistence
- Theological debates common (sometimes friendly, sometimes heated)
- Some Hextheists view First Flame as heretics
- Some First Flame view Hextheists as nearly-enlightened

**Areas of Cooperation:**
- Share holy days (interpreted differently)
- Sometimes share sacred texts
- Unified Ember and moderate Hexarchs work together frequently
- Scholarly Flame collaborates with Hextheist scholars

**Areas of Conflict:**
- Competing for converts
- Theological arguments over Hollowstars
- First Flame's growing numbers worry some Hexarchs
- Purifying Flame's evangelical zeal annoys some Hextheists

### With [[Path of Spirits]]

**Official Position:**

"Harmless but incorrect. They revere spirits and nature, not the divine source."

**In Practice:**

- Generally dismissive rather than hostile
- Some overlap (especially Guiding Light appreciates nature reverence)
- First Flame rarely tries to convert spirit-worshipers (too different)
- Peaceful coexistence in most regions

### With The Unbound (Internal)

**Official Position (from High Flame Kaida):**

"Wayward siblings who will return when ready. We pray for their reunion with the true church."

**In Practice:**

- **Growing hostility** from local priests and congregations
- The Unbound are seen as **threatening** - they grow by converting Eternal Flame members
- Some denominations more hostile than others:
  - Purifying Flame: Actively condemn them as apostates
  - Radiant Path: View them as weak (can't accept divine authority)
  - Scholarly Flame: Intellectually curious about the split
  - Unified Ember: Most sympathetic, seek reconciliation
- Recent incidents of violence (rare but increasing)

**The Unbound's Position:**

"We follow the same Eternal Flame, just don't elevate mortals to speak for it. We're more faithful than they are."

## Gameplay Integration

### For Players

**Clerics of the First Flame:**

- Choose a denomination (determines flavor and approach)
- All mechanically equivalent - difference is roleplay
- Can change denominations in-game (character growth)

**Character Backgrounds:**
- Raised in First Flame temple
- Convert from Hextheism (why?)
- Unbound member seeking reconciliation (or fleeing persecution)
- Priest seeking answers in the crisis
- Torn between two denominations

### For GMs

**Use Religious Tension:**
- NPCs argue theology
- Different temples offer different aid/interpretations
- Flamekeepers can be patrons, rivals, or obstacles
- Religious debates reflect player moral choices

**Plot Hooks:**

- Mediate between First Flame denominations on brink of schism
- Protect Unbound congregation from Purifying Flame zealots
- Help Scholarly Flame research in Lumencrest
- Escort Radiant Path pilgrims to Frostwell Tundra
- Investigate why an eternal flame extinguished (omen? sabotage?)
- Retrieve stolen sacred texts
- Witness a divine sign choosing new Flamekeeper (or Divine Leader if Kaida dies)
