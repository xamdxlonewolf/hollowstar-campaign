# Path of Spirits

> *"The spirits walk beside us, within us, through us. They are not distant gods in gilded halls—they are the breath of wind, the memory of ancestors, the pulse of the ancient tree. Listen."*
> — Shaman's teaching, oral tradition

---

**Founded:** Ancient - predates both [[Six-Fold Faith]] and [[The First Flame Faith]]  
**Nature:** Unified spiritual tradition with regional variations  
**Adherents:** Declining but still strong in rural and northern regions  
**Authority:** Elder shamans, druidic circles (decentralized, networked)  
**Sacred Texts:** Secret shaman writings + vast oral tradition  
**Tone:** Primal, shamanic, wise, druidic, mystical, practical (varies by region)

## Core Beliefs

### The Spirit World

**The Fundamental Truth:**

Everything has spirit. The world is not divided into sacred and mundane—**all is alive, all is aware, all is connected** through the web of spirits.

**Three Realms of Spirit:**

1. **The Mortal Realm** - Where we live, physical and tangible
2. **The Spirit Realm** - Separate plane, timeless and dreamlike, **very dangerous**
3. **The Threshold** - Thin places where realms touch (sacred groves, spirit wells, ancient trees)

**"We do not worship distant gods. We walk with spirits. We speak with them. They answer."**

### The Layered Cosmology

Spirits exist in hierarchy, not of worth but of **power and scope:**

#### Small Spirits (The Countless)
- **Individual spirits:** Every tree, stone, stream, creature
- **Household spirits:** Attached to families, homes, lineages
- **Personal spirits:** Guide individuals, especially shamans
- **Fleeting spirits:** Born from moments (a child's first laugh, a warrior's dying breath)

Most interactions are with small spirits. They are **personal, immediate, transactional**.

#### Great Spirits (The Named)
- **Place spirits:** The Forest, The Mountain, The River (capital letters—they are entities)
- **Ancient animal spirits:** The Great Bear, The Wise Crow, The Sacred Stag
- **Clan ancestors:** Founders, heroes, legendary shamans who grew powerful in the spirit realm
- **Seasonal spirits:** Spring's Awakening, Winter's Sleep

Great spirits are **powerful, territorial, demanding respect**. Offending one can curse an entire community.

#### Ancient Spirits (The Timeless)
- **The First Ancestors:** Those who walked the world when it was young
- **The Bound Spirits:** Tied to the oldest places (the great tree in [[Verdant Crescent]], spirit wells that never run dry)
- **The Forgotten:** Spirits so old their names are lost, but their power remains

Ancient spirits are **rarely contacted**. Most shamans never speak with one. Those who do are changed forever.

### Ancestor Spirits - The Beloved Dead

**When you die, you become a spirit.**

- **Lesser spirit** (if you lived ordinarily)
- **Greater spirit** (if you were blessed or marked in life—shamans, heroes, the deeply wise)

**Your ancestors watch over your family.** They:
- Guide descendants through dreams (some cultures believe this)
- Protect the bloodline
- Can be consulted through shamans
- May return if called (requires huge sacrifice, extremely rare)

**The Spirit Realm is not peaceful rest—it's an eternal trial.** Your spirit must:
- Navigate timeless, dangerous landscapes
- Avoid getting lost or trapped
- Earn the right to return (reincarnation)
- Merge with nature if you choose (tree grows in family's yard, river flows stronger)

**Most who return have no memory of past lives.** Those who DO remember are **always Path of Spirits followers**—proof, to believers, that their way is truth.

### Nature Spirits - The Living World

**Every natural thing has spirit:**

- **Trees:** Oldest trees are wisest, some spirits ancient beyond reckoning
- **Waters:** Rivers are travelers, lakes are keepers, springs are birthers
- **Stones:** Mountains are eternal watchers, caves are doorways, sacred stones are anchors
- **Weather:** Storms are angry, gentle rains are blessings, winds carry messages
- **Earth:** The ground itself is alive—fields remember what was planted, battlefields remember blood

**Respect is mandatory.** You:
- Ask permission before cutting a tree (even for firewood)
- Thank a river before drinking
- Apologize to a deer you hunt
- Honor the earth before building

**Reciprocity:** Spirits give (food, water, shelter) and take (offerings, respect, sometimes blood). Imbalance invites disaster.

### Animal Spirits - The Sacred Beasts

**Animals have two spirits:**

1. **Individual spirit** (the deer you hunt)
2. **Eternal spirit** (The Deer—the essence of all deer, timeless)

**Sacred animals** are those whose eternal spirits are **most powerful:**
- **The Great Bear** (strength, protection, ferocity)
- **The Wise Crow** (knowledge, trickery, prophecy)
- **The Sacred Stag** (grace, nobility, the hunt)
- **The Serpent-of-the-Deep** (mystery, danger, transformation)
- **The Wolf** (pack, loyalty, survival)

**Their bones are holy.** Jewelry made from sacred animal bones:
- Connects wearer to the animal's spirit
- Often given to shamans and warriors
- Must be earned or inherited, never bought

**Taboo:** **Slaying defenseless animals** is grave sin. Hunt fairly or not at all. Spirits remember cruelty.

## Hollowstars & The Cosmic

### The Falling Stars

**Path of Spirits View:**

[[Hollowstars]] are **another source of spirit energy**, gifts given to mortals to aid them.

**NOT alien.** NOT wrong. NOT to be worshiped. They are **forces, like storms**—powerful, dangerous, useful if understood.

**"Learn their patterns. Respect their power. Do not bow to them."**

### Do Hollowstars Have Spirits?

**Yes.**

But not spirits like trees or ancestors. Hollostar spirits are:
- **Vast and strange** (like great spirits, but from beyond the sky)
- **Not easily communicated with** (shamans can try, rarely succeed)
- **Generous but indifferent** (they give power, don't care how it's used)

**Using Hollostar power:**
- **Allowed** if done with proper respect and ritual
- **Dangerous** (like all spirit work—can harm you if careless)
- **Each practitioner decides** based on their relationship with spirits

**Some shamans use Hollostar shards in rituals.** Others refuse, finding the energy "too loud, too bright."

### Transformed Creatures

**Spirits trying to warn mortals.**

When creatures are warped by Hollostar energy, Path of Spirits followers believe:
- **The spirits are communicating** through mutation
- **They show what happens** when cosmic power is misused
- **They are messengers, not monsters** (though still dangerous)

**NOT:**
- New spirits born from contamination
- Proof Hollowstars are wrong
- Demons or abominations (that's [[The First Flame Faith|Eternal Flame]] thinking)

**Response:** Study them, learn the message, **then kill them mercifully**. They suffer. Release them.

## The Current Crisis

### What Spirit-Worshipers Believe

**The dimming stars, the shrinking lake, the approaching Hollostar:**

**Natural cycle?** Some say yes—the world breathes in and out, stars come and go, this is simply change.

**Spirits angry?** Many believe this—as people abandoned the Path for newer faiths, **offerings dwindled, rituals were forgotten**. The spirits grow restless, distant, harder to reach.

**"We warned them. They didn't listen. Now we all pay the price."**

### What They're Doing

**Consulting spirits for guidance:**
- More spirit journeys (dangerous—shamans pushing limits)
- Offerings increased (some communities sacrificing precious livestock)
- Seeking visions from ancient spirits (many shamans coming back changed, haunted)

**Performing protective rituals:**
- Warding villages with knotwork symbols
- Blessing fields and herds
- Strengthening sacred groves (druids working overtime)

**Warning others:**
- **Being ignored.** Non-believers dismiss them as superstitious, primitive, "full of it"
- Frustration and anger building in some communities
- [[Green Covenant]] extremists point to this as proof: "See? We must FORCE them to listen."

**NOT retreating to sacred places:**
- That would be backing down, abandoning the world
- Spirits are everywhere—there's nowhere to hide
- Stand and face what comes

### The Spirits Themselves

**Are they:**

- **Silent?** No—but communication is harder
- **Agitated?** Slightly more than usual (spirits agitated easily anyway if bothered too much)
- **Clearer?** If a spirit cares enough, it spends more energy to communicate clearly
- **Dying or fleeing?** No—but the **veil is thickening**, making contact difficult
- **Warning?** **Yes.** Those who listen hear the same message: *"Change comes. Prepare. Balance is breaking."*

**The veil between realms:**

Usually thin enough for shamans to cross easily. Now it's **harder to reach**, requiring more preparation, more risk, stronger rituals. Younger shamans struggle. Elders push themselves dangerously.

**Some spirits warn specifically about the approaching Hollostar:**
- It will bring great change (good and bad)
- Where it lands will transform
- Those who control it will shape the future
- Balance will shift—be ready to adapt

**Others are silent,** watching, waiting, **judging**.

## Practitioners of the Path

### Shamans - The Spirit-Walkers

**How They're Chosen:**

**Born with the mark.** At birth, a shaman-to-be has a **distinctive mark on their back**—no two marks are identical. Shape varies:
- Spiral patterns
- Knotwork
- Animal shapes
- Abstract swirls

**The spirits choose.** Parents see the mark, know their child's destiny. The community celebrates and mourns—shamans are honored but bear heavy burdens.

**Training begins young:**
- Apprenticed to elder shaman (often not a parent—avoid favoritism)
- Learn secret **shaman writing** (script known only to shamans, guards ancient knowledge)
- Serve as assistants (tend sacred sites, prepare rituals, gather herbs)
- First spirit journey attempted around age 16 (under strict supervision)

**What Shamans Can Do:**

1. **Guided Spirit Journeys**
   - Lead others into the spirit realm safely
   - Navigate the timeless, dreamlike landscape
   - Find specific spirits (ancestors, guides, great spirits)
   - **Critical:** Bring people BACK—without a shaman, travelers get lost forever

2. **Healing**
   - Physical wounds (herbalism + spirit blessing)
   - Spiritual ailments (curse removal, spirit possession)
   - Sickness caused by angered spirits

3. **Divination**
   - Read omens (bones, smoke, water, dreams)
   - Consult spirits about future events
   - Interpret signs in nature
   - **NOT fortune-telling**—spirits show possibilities, not certainties

**What Shamans Cannot Do:**
- Raise the dead (only spirits can choose to return, and it requires massive sacrifice)
- Control spirits (they bargain, request, honor—never command)
- Guarantee outcomes (spirits are unpredictable)

**The Shaman's Burden:**

- Walk between worlds (never fully in either)
- Carry community's spiritual weight
- See things others don't (visions, spirits, the future's shadows)
- Often isolated (people fear and revere them equally)
- Some go mad (the spirit realm is dangerous to mortal minds)

**Shaman Hierarchy:**

No formal ranks, but respect is given by:
- **Age and experience** (elders are wisest)
- **Power of their spirit journeys** (how deep they can go, what they've seen)
- **Wisdom of their guidance** (communities remember good advice)

**Elder shamans gather periodically**—no set schedule, but when great need arises (crisis, major decision, resolution of disputes). These gatherings are sacred, secret, and **rare**.

### Druids - The Grove-Keepers

**Different from shamans** (though lines blur in some cultures):

**Druids are:**
- **Protectors of balance** (nature's equilibrium)
- **Keepers of sacred groves** (maintain, guard, tend)
- **Teachers of natural law** (how to live in harmony)
- **Judges in disputes** (especially about land, resources, nature)

**How They're Chosen:**

NOT by birth mark. Druids are:
- Called by the land itself (feel deep connection to specific place)
- Trained by existing druids (apprenticeship, like shamans but different focus)
- Tested by nature (survive alone in wilderness, prove understanding)

**What Druids Do:**

1. **Maintain Sacred Sites**
   - Tend groves, spirit wells, ancient trees
   - Perform seasonal rituals (keep balance through the year)
   - Guard against desecration

2. **Mediate with Nature Spirits**
   - Speak for the forest, the river, the mountain
   - Negotiate between human needs and nature's limits
   - Prevent ecological disasters

3. **Teach and Guide**
   - Instruct communities in proper offerings
   - Lead seasonal festivals
   - Advise on sustainable living

**Druidic Circles:**

In larger settlements, druids form **circles**—organized groups with hierarchy:
- **Archdruid** (eldest, wisest, final authority)
- **Circle druids** (experienced, specialized—grove-tender, water-keeper, etc.)
- **Initiates** (learning, probationary)

**In rural/nomadic areas:** Druids are often solitary, visiting communities as needed.

**Druids vs. Shamans:**

| **Shamans** | **Druids** |
|-------------|------------|
| Walk between worlds | Root in this world |
| Commune with all spirits | Focus on nature spirits |
| Born marked | Called by land |
| Guides to spirit realm | Guardians of physical realm |
| Visionaries | Practitioners |

**But:** Some individuals are both. In some cultures, the distinction barely exists. In Frostwell Tundra, for instance, "shaman" covers both roles.

### Common Practitioners

**Anyone can try to commune with spirits.**

You don't need to be shaman or druid to:
- Make offerings
- Perform daily rituals
- Speak to household spirits
- Feel nature's presence

**But shamans guarantee connection.** Without training:
- You might be ignored
- You might anger spirits (improper offerings, wrong words)
- You might attract malicious entities (spirit realm has dangers)

**Common Folk Practices:**

**Daily:**
- Offerings to household spirits (food at hearth, flowers at doorway)
- Morning acknowledgment (greet the day's spirits)
- Evening thanks (thank spirits for the day's blessings)

**Before Important Events:**
- **Hunting:** Ask animal spirits for permission, promise clean kill
- **Planting:** Bless seeds, thank earth spirits
- **Building:** Apologize to trees, ask land spirits for space
- **Travel:** Request safe passage from path spirits

**Strict Rules (NOT Suggestions):**

1. **Never slay defenseless animals** (hunting is sacred, cruelty is sin)
2. **Always make offerings** before taking from nature
3. **Respect sacred sites** (never defile groves, wells, ancient trees)
4. **Honor the dead** (celebrate death days, remember ancestors)
5. **Balance give and take** (if you take much, give much)

**Breaking these rules invites:**
- Sickness (spirits punish through illness)
- Bad fortune (crops fail, hunts fail, accidents increase)
- Spiritual corruption (darkness enters the soul)
- Community exile (serious violations result in banishment)

## Sacred Practices & Rituals

### Daily & Common Observances

**NOT prayer-focused.** Prayer implies distance—gods far away, hearing petitions.

Path of Spirits is about **communion:** direct contact, conversation, transaction.

**Offerings:**
- **Food:** First fruits, portion of meals, milk, honey
- **Flowers:** Seasonal blooms, placed at thresholds and sacred stones
- **Crafts:** Woven grass figures, carved wood, pottery left at shrines
- **Blood:** In serious cases (illness, crisis, major undertaking)—animal blood, never human (officially)

**Rituals Before Important Actions:**

**Hunting:**
1. Fast the night before
2. Offer to animal spirits at dawn
3. Ask permission ("Grant me your child, that my family may live")
4. Promise clean kill and use of all parts
5. After kill: Thank the animal's spirit, mark forehead with its blood

**Planting:**
1. Bless seeds with smoke and song
2. Dig first furrow with ritual knife
3. Plant offering (small carving or stone) in field center
4. Thank earth spirits, promise care of land

**Building:**
1. Survey land, find where spirits are thinnest (avoid disturbing powerful places)
2. Offer to displaced spirits (especially trees)
3. Bury foundation offering (coins, grain, carved figures)
4. Shaman blesses site if available

### Coming of Age Rituals

**Three Major Transitions:**

#### Age 6 - First Marking
- **Ceremony:** Child receives first tattoo or ritual scar
- **Meaning:** Acknowledged by spirits as aware, accountable
- **Participation:** Entire community witnesses
- **Shaman role:** Reads child's spirit, gives **Spirit Name** (often different from birth name)

**Path of Spirits Naming System:**

Path followers accumulate names throughout life, creating a **three-part full name:**

**[Birth Name] [Spirit Name] [Path Name]**

- **Birth Name:** Given by parents at birth (common mortal name)
- **Spirit Name (Age 6):** Given by shaman after reading the child's spirit during First Marking
- **Path Name (Age 16):** Earned through coming-of-age trial, reflects achievement or vision

**Full Name Examples:**

1. **Kael Shadowroot Stormwalker**
   - *Birth:* Kael (simple, common)
   - *Spirit:* Shadowroot (shaman saw deep, hidden strength—nature-based)
   - *Path:* Stormwalker (survived trial in terrible storm—action-based)
   - *Region:* Verdant Crescent

2. **Mira Crowsight Swiftspear**
   - *Birth:* Mira
   - *Spirit:* Crowsight (shaman saw trickster spirit, keen eyes—animal-based)
   - *Path:* Swiftspear (proved herself in hunt, fastest throw—action-based)
   - *Region:* Northern Expanse

3. **Torin Deepthought Veilcrosser**
   - *Birth:* Torin
   - *Spirit:* Deepthought (contemplative child, old soul—abstract quality)
   - *Path:* Veilcrosser (had powerful vision during spirit journey—vision-based)
   - *Region:* Glittering Marshes (unusual for region, but shamans read what they see)

4. **Eira Frostborn Icefang**
   - *Birth:* Eira
   - *Spirit:* Frostborn (born during harshest winter, spirit of endurance—nature-based)
   - *Path:* Icefang (killed ice bear during trial, claimed its tooth—achievement-based)
   - *Region:* Frostwell Tundra (harsher names common here)

5. **Rhen Reedwhisper Mistwalker**
   - *Birth:* Rhen
   - *Spirit:* Reedwhisper (quiet child, spirit moved like wind through reeds—nature-based)
   - *Path:* Mistwalker (navigated marshes in deep fog during trial—action-based)
   - *Region:* Glittering Marshes (water-focused names common)

**Usage:**
- **Formal occasions:** Full three-part name spoken
- **Daily life:** Usually just Path Name (final name) used
- **Family/close friends:** May use Birth Name or Spirit Name (intimacy)
- **Shamans addressing spirits:** Always use full name (spirits need complete identity)

**Regional Variations:**

**Frostwell Tundra:** Harsher, survival-focused
- Spirit: Coldheart, Icevein, Snowstalker, Winterborn, Frostbite
- Path: Bearslayer, Longnight, Flamekeeper, Icebreaker, Survivedthevoid

**Glittering Marshes:** Water and earth blended
- Spirit: Reedborn, Mudfoot, Watervoice, Bogwatcher, Mistborn
- Path: Deepdiver, Marshwalker, Serpentfriend, Tidespeaker, Rootfinder

**Verdant Crescent:** Forest and growth
- Spirit: Oakhand, Leafwhisper, Vineborn, Mossfoot, Barktouch
- Path: Treetender, Grovekeeper, Forestsoul, Greenstrider, Branchwalker

**Northern Expanse:** Sky and plains
- Spirit: Windrider, Grassrunner, Skyborn, Plainsfeet, Sunwatcher
- Path: Swiftrunner, Horizonseeker, Thundervoice, Cloudchaser, Openroad

**Common Spirit Name Types:**
- **Nature-based:** Elements, plants, natural phenomena
- **Animal-based:** Sacred animals, behaviors, characteristics
- **Abstract qualities:** Personality traits, spiritual essence

**Common Path Name Types:**
- **Action-based (most common):** What you DID during trial
- **Achievement-based:** What you PROVED
- **Vision-based:** What you SAW in spirit realm
- **Quality-based:** What you BECAME

#### Age 16 - Adulthood
- **Ceremony:** Varies by culture
  - **Nomadic tribes:** Solo survival journey (days alone in wilderness)
  - **Settled communities:** Vision quest (fasting, meditation, seeking spirit guide)
  - **Coastal/marsh cultures:** Underwater trial (breath-holding, diving for sacred object)
- **Meaning:** Transition from child to adult, spirit-recognized
- **Shaman role:** Guides preparation, interprets results, **bestows Path Name**
- **Success:** Honored with **Path Name** (final part of three-part name), adult responsibilities, full community participation
- **Failure:** Rare, but shameful—must wait a year and try again (keeps Spirit Name until successful)

**Path Name:** Reflects what was achieved, seen, or proven during the trial. From this point forward, the individual is known by their full three-part name: **[Birth] [Spirit] [Path]**

#### Age 26 - Independence
- **Ceremony:** Formal separation from parents/family
- **Meaning:** "You must stand alone, though you can visit family"
- **Practical:** Expected to have own dwelling, livelihood, possibly family
- **Participation:** Smaller—just family and close community
- **Symbolic:** Severance ritual (cutting cord, burning childhood possessions)
- **Spirit contact:** Individual offering to ancestors ("I am now my own keeper")

**If you die before Age 26:** Spirit is considered incomplete, needs family's help in spirit realm. Extra offerings made.

### Death Rites - The Journey Begins

**Death is not ending—it's transition.**

**Immediate Actions (within hours):**
1. **Shaman summoned** (if available; if not, eldest family member acts)
2. **Body washed** with sacred water (from spirit well if possible)
3. **Symbols painted** on skin (knotwork, spirals—guide for the journey)
4. **Offerings placed** around body (food for the road, gifts for spirits)

**The Vigil (1-3 days):**
- Body kept in community space
- Continuous watch (spirits must see the person honored)
- Stories told (recounting life, good deeds, memorable moments)
- Songs sung (mourning songs, journey songs)
- Drums and rattles (rhythm helps spirit detach from body)

**The Shaman's Role:**
- **Guides the spirit** in its first steps into the spirit realm
- Enters trance, walks beside the deceased briefly
- Speaks for the dead (if they have messages)
- Ensures spirit doesn't linger or get lost immediately

**Burial or Cremation (culture-dependent):**

**Nomadic:** 
- Burn body (spirit freed to wander)
- Scatter ashes in meaningful place
- Keep small bone token (connection to ancestor)

**Settled:**
- Bury near home (ancestor stays close)
- Plant tree or flowers over grave
- Sacred ground maintained by community

**Marsh/water cultures:**
- Water burial (body given to river or lake)
- Spirits of water guide the dead
- Bones may be retrieved later, cleaned, kept in shrine

**After Burial:**

- **Death day celebrated annually** (like birthday)
- Offerings made (food, drink, crafted items)
- Stories retold (keep memory alive)
- Shaman may contact spirit on anniversaries (especially early years)

**Goal:** Keep ancestor spirit **strong and close**. Well-honored dead protect the living. Forgotten dead fade to nothing.

### Seasonal Festivals

**Path of Spirits observes the same seasonal festivals as [[Six-Fold Faith]] and [[The First Flame Faith]], but interprets them differently:**

#### Spring Festival (Awakening)
- **Hextheists:** Celebrate The Weaver (change/growth)
- **First Flame:** Celebrate Change aspect of Eternal Flame
- **Path of Spirits:** **Awaken the land spirits** after winter sleep
  - Plant offerings in thawing ground
  - Call to dormant spirits (trees, rivers, earth)
  - Bless seeds and tools
  - Young animals honored (first lambs, calves, foals)

#### Summer Festival (Peak)
- **Hextheists:** Celebrate The Sentinel (protection/warfare)
- **First Flame:** Celebrate Protection aspect
- **Path of Spirits:** **Honor the Great Spirits at height of power**
  - Pilgrimages to sacred sites
  - Major spirit journeys (veil thinnest in summer)
  - Warrior initiations
  - Feasting and storytelling (life is abundant, celebrate)

#### Autumn Festival (Harvest)
- **Hextheists:** Celebrate The Hearthkeeper (home/community)
- **First Flame:** Celebrate Love aspect
- **Path of Spirits:** **Thank nature spirits for bounty**
  - First harvest portions offered (grain, fruit, meat)
  - Preservation rituals (bless stored food for winter)
  - Ancestor remembrance (they helped bring harvest)
  - Preparation for winter hardship

#### Winter Festival (Rest)
- **Hextheists:** Celebrate The Gravewalker (death/memory)
- **First Flame:** Celebrate Transition aspect
- **Path of Spirits:** **Commune with the dead**
  - Veil thinnest in deep winter (death's season)
  - Major ancestor rituals (shaman-led)
  - Death day remembrances concentrated
  - Stories of the dead told around fires
  - Shamans read omens for coming year

**Additional Path-Specific Observances:**

**The Long Night (winter solstice):**
- Shamans enter deepest trances
- Entire community maintains vigil
- Fires kept burning all night (guide spirits home)
- Visions received are prophecy for the year

**First Thaw (early spring):**
- Children touch flowing water (blessed by awakening)
- Ice-breaking ceremonies (free trapped spirits)
- Renewal of household wards

### Crisis Rituals

**When disaster strikes—drought, plague, war, famine:**

#### Minor Crisis (Local)
- **Shaman consultation:** What angered spirits?
- **Community offering:** Everyone contributes (food, crafts, time)
- **Atonement ritual:** Fix what was broken (replant, rebuild, apologize)

#### Major Crisis (Regional)
- **Multiple shamans gather** (rare, significant)
- **Animal sacrifice:** Livestock offered (valuable, serious)
  - Blood given to earth/water
  - Meat shared in ritual feast (spirits fed first)
- **Spirit journey:** Shamans seek answers in spirit realm
- **Public ceremony:** Entire community participates (unity matters)

#### Severe Crisis (Existential)
- **Elder shamans convene** (across regions if possible)
- **Ancient spirits contacted** (desperate measure, dangerous)
- **Massive offerings:** Entire harvests, herds, treasures
- **Rumors of human sacrifice:** Officially stopped, but "what happens in the north stays in the north"
  - Most believe it's myth now
  - Outsiders exaggerate northern "barbarism"
  - Truth: Unknown, possibly occasional in most desperate times

**Current Crisis (Hollowstars dimming):**
- **All of the above happening** in different degrees
- Shamans exhausting themselves with spirit journeys
- Communities offering more than they can afford
- Fear and desperation rising

## Sacred Sites & Geography

### The Great Tree - Ael'Thoras (The Heart Tree)

**Most sacred site in the entire Path of Spirits tradition.**

**Names:**
- **Shaman name:** **Ael'Thoras** (AYL-THOR-ahs) - known only to shamans, spoken in secret language
- **Common name:** **The Heart Tree** (what most people call it)
- **Meaning:** In the ancient shaman tongue, "Ael'Thoras" translates directly to "The Heart Tree"—the heart of the forest, the heart of the faith, where all spirits' hearts converge

**Pronunciation Guide:**
- **Ael** - like "ale" with a soft L (AYL)
- **'** - slight pause, glottal stop (moment of silence)
- **Tho** - like "Thor" (THOR)
- **ras** - like "ross" but with "ah" sound (ahs)
- **Emphasis:** Second syllable (ael-THOR-ahs)

**Description:**
- **Immense beyond measure** (trunk wider than buildings, canopy visible for miles)
- **Ancient** (predates recorded history, possibly thousands of years old)
- **The Deep Forest's Heart** (center of Verdant Crescent's largest forest)

**Significance:**
- **Guarded by spirits in the afterlife** (ancestor spirits watch over it)
- **Gateway to spirit realm** (easiest place to cross the veil)
- **Living record** (bark carved with names, symbols, prayers across generations)

**What Happens There:**
- **Pilgrimages:** Shamans travel from across the world to visit once in life
- **Initiations:** Some druids complete their training here
- **Crisis gatherings:** Elder shamans convene beneath its branches
- **Spirit journeys:** Most powerful journeys launched from its roots

**Guardians:**
- **Druidic circle** (permanent residents, protect the tree)
- **Forest spirits** (ancient, powerful, territorial)
- **Ancestors** (the dead who choose to remain, watching)

**Access:**
- Open to true believers
- Outsiders allowed with shaman/druid escort (and **deep respect**)
- Desecration means death (spirits themselves enforce this)

### Spirit Wells

**Natural springs where the spirit realm touches the mortal world.**

**Characteristics:**
- Water never runs dry (fed by spirit energy, not groundwater)
- Tastes different (sweet, metallic, sometimes glowing faintly)
- Healing properties (not guaranteed, but common)
- Visions when gazed into (especially by shamans)

**Uses:**
- **Sacred water for rituals** (births, deaths, blessings)
- **Healing pilgrimages** (sick travel to drink/bathe)
- **Shaman meditation** (easiest to contact water spirits here)
- **Offerings dropped into depths** (valuables given to spirits)

**Locations:**
- Scattered across all regions
- Most in [[Verdant Crescent]] and [[Glittering Marshes]] (water-rich areas)
- Some in [[Frostwell Tundra]] (hot springs, never freeze)
- Rare in [[Desert of Glass]] (but intensely sacred when found)

**Guardians:**
- Local druids or shamans
- Communities nearby (consider it their sacred trust)

---

### Sacred Groves

**Forests or clusters of trees designated as spirit sanctuaries.**

**What Makes a Grove Sacred:**
- **Ancient trees** (centuries old, spirit-touched)
- **Shaman/druid consecration** (ritual claiming for spirits)
- **Natural power** (unusual growth, persistent life, strange phenomena)
- **Ancestor presence** (burial grounds within or nearby)

**Activities in Groves:**
- Meditation and prayer
- Offerings to tree/nature spirits
- Coming-of-age trials
- Druid rituals (seasonal, crisis)
- **NOT:** Hunting, logging, building (absolute taboos)

**Protection:**
- Communities defend groves fiercely
- [[Green Covenant]] most militant here (attacks on groves = attacks on faith)
- Spirits themselves punish violators (sickness, madness, death)

**Largest Groves:**
- **Verdant Crescent:** Dozens, including the one with the Great Tree
- **Glittering Marshes:** Mangrove groves (water and wood together)
- **Northern Expanse:** Small but fiercely protected (rare trees in grassland)

---

### Stone Circles & Standing Stones

**Ancient constructions, purpose partially forgotten.**

**What They Are:**
- Rings of standing stones (some small, some massive)
- Single menhirs (tall stones, inscribed or plain)
- Aligned to stars, seasons, or unknown patterns

**Beliefs:**
- **Built by ancestors** (First Ancestors, the Timeless Ones)
- **Markers for spirit paths** (spirits travel along these lines)
- **Astronomical calendars** (track seasons, predict events)
- **Amplifiers** (rituals performed here are more powerful)

**Uses:**
- Seasonal festival gathering points
- Shaman initiations (spend night within circle)
- Divination (read stars through the stones)
- **Dangerous:** Some circles are cursed or trap unwary visitors

**Maintenance:**
- Generally none (too old, too sacred to alter)
- Offerings placed at bases
- Druids keep them clear of overgrowth

---

### Ancestor Burial Grounds

**NOT universal—depends on culture:**

**Nomadic Peoples:**
- Burn dead, carry ashes or bone tokens
- No fixed burial sites
- Ancestors "travel with us always"

**Settled Communities:**
- Burial grounds near village/town
- Sacred but not always formalized
- Families tend their own plots

**Special Cases:**
- **Heroes/shamans:** Sometimes buried in sacred groves or near spirit wells
- **Dishonored dead:** Buried far from community (or not at all—left for scavengers)

**Maintenance:**
- Families responsible
- Shamans bless grounds periodically
- Future shamans (in training) help tend as part of learning

## Organization & Knowledge

### Authority Structure

**No centralized church.** No single supreme leader like other faiths.

**Instead:**

#### Elder Shamans
- **Informal authority** based on age, wisdom, power
- **Gather periodically** when needed (no set schedule, no formal summons)
- **Consensus-based decisions** (argue, debate, reach agreement)
- **Respected, not obeyed** (communities choose to follow advice or not)

**Topics they address:**
- Theological disputes (is this practice acceptable?)
- Regional crises (drought affecting multiple areas)
- Threats to the faith (persecution, land seizure)
- Passing of ancient knowledge (before elders die)

#### Druidic Circles
- **Hierarchical** in larger settlements
  - Archdruid (eldest, wisest)
  - Circle druids (specialists)
  - Initiates (learning)
- **Independent** in rural areas (solitary druids)
- **Communication network** (druids visit each other, share knowledge)

**Functions:**
- Maintain sacred sites
- Judge disputes (especially land/nature conflicts)
- Teach proper practices
- Coordinate seasonal festivals

#### Independent Shamans/Groves
- **Autonomous** (each shaman/druid governs their area)
- **Networked** (communication happens, just not formal)
- **Cooperation when needed** (call for help, others answer—usually)

**Balance:**
- Decentralized enough to avoid corruption
- Connected enough to maintain unity
- Flexible enough to adapt to regions

---

### Knowledge Transmission

#### Oral Tradition (Primary)

**The vast majority of knowledge is spoken, not written.**

**Why:**
- **Spirit knowledge is alive** (changes, grows, can't be trapped on page)
- **Personal relationship** (you learn from your teacher, not a book)
- **Sacred secrecy** (outsiders can't steal what isn't written)

**What's Transmitted Orally:**
- Stories (creation, ancestors, heroes)
- Songs (ritual, seasonal, mourning)
- Practices (how to make offerings, conduct rituals)
- Spirit names and natures (individual spirits, how to contact them)

**Methods:**
- **Repetition** (apprentices memorize through constant retelling)
- **Song and rhythm** (easier to remember, harder to forget)
- **Practical demonstration** (watch elder, then do it yourself)

**Risk:** Knowledge dies with teachers. If a lineage breaks (shaman dies without apprentice), wisdom is lost.

#### Secret Shaman Writing

**Only shamans know this script.**

**What It Looks Like:**
- **Knotwork-based** (flowing, interconnected lines)
- **Abstract symbols** (not pictures, not letters—concepts)
- **Personal variations** (each shaman's hand is unique)

**What They Write:**
- **Personal visions** (spirit journeys, prophecies)
- **Dangerous knowledge** (rituals too risky for oral transmission)
- **Maps of spirit realm** (paths, landmarks, safe routes)
- **Lineage records** (who taught whom, back generations)

**Where It's Kept:**
- Hidden (sacred caves, hollow trees, buried)
- Guarded (spirits and traps protect these caches)
- Limited (only most important knowledge written)

**Learning the Script:**
- Taught to marked shamans during apprenticeship
- Takes years to master
- **Never taught to non-shamans** (absolute taboo)

**If a non-shaman tries to read it:**
- Won't understand (symbols are meaningless without training)
- Might attract spirits (writings are spiritually active)
- Could go mad (some texts are dangerous even to read)

#### Spirit-Taught Knowledge

**Shamans learn directly from spirits during journeys.**

**What They Learn:**
- **New rituals** (spirits show methods mortals didn't know)
- **Hidden truths** (nature's secrets, cosmic patterns)
- **Prophecies** (possible futures, warnings)
- **Personal power** (unique abilities granted by specific spirits)

**NOT generational transmission—each shaman discovers anew.**

**This keeps the tradition alive:** Even if oral/written knowledge is lost, spirits still teach.

### Regional Variations

**All Path of Spirits practitioners share core beliefs, but regional cultures add flavor:**

#### Frostwell Tundra
- **Harsh, survival-focused**
- **Ancestor spirits most important** (lineage determines survival in frozen waste)
- **Animal spirits revered** (Great Bear, Arctic Wolf)
- **Shamans and druids overlap** (one person does both roles)
- **Rumors of human sacrifice** (outsiders believe this; truth unclear)
- **Ice spirits unique to region** (cold given spiritual form)

#### Northern Expanse
- **Nomadic traditions dominant**
- **Horse spirits sacred** (The Eternal Herd, individual steeds)
- **Sky spirits emphasized** (wind, storm, stars)
- **Portable shrines** (travel with the tribes)
- **Warrior culture** (spirit journeys often battle-focused)

#### Verdant Crescent
- **Forest spirits paramount**
- **The Great Tree as pilgrimage site**
- **Druidic circles most formalized here**
- **Ancient lineages** (shamans trace back centuries)
- **Deep lore** (oldest written shaman texts hidden here)
- **Green Covenant strongest** (protect the forest at all costs)

#### Glittering Marshes
- **Water and earth spirits blended**
- **Amphibious traditions** (underwater rituals, breathing exercises)
- **Decay and growth celebrated equally** (marshes are both)
- **Serpent and frog spirits** (Serpent-of-the-Deep most powerful)
- **Unique burial practices** (water burial, bone shrines)

#### Rural Settlements (All Regions)
- **Farmers emphasize earth spirits**
- **Seasonal cycles critical** (planting, harvest rituals)
- **Practical, less mystical** (spirits help crops, that's what matters)
- **Village elders often shamans** (spiritual and political authority combined)

**Despite variations, all recognize each other as Path of Spirits.** A tundra shaman and a marsh druid may practice differently, but both honor the same fundamental truths.

### Competing Traditions & Ancient Lineages

**"Schools" exist, but informal:**

#### The Old Way
- **Strictest adherents** (no compromise, ancient practices only)
- **Reject all Hollostar use** (cosmic power is foreign, dangerous)
- **Hermit shamans** (live alone, rarely engage with communities)
- **Dying out** (fewer each generation, young people find them too rigid)

#### The Adaptive Path
- **Pragmatic** (use what works, including Hollostar shards if necessary)
- **Engage with other faiths** (some cooperation with Hextheists, First Flame)
- **Most common** (especially in settled areas)
- **Criticized by Old Way** ("Compromisers, diluting the truth")

#### The Green Extreme
- **[[Green Covenant]] represents this**
- **Militant, uncompromising**
- **Violence justified** (protect nature by any means)
- **Small but loud** (draw attention disproportionate to numbers)

#### Ancient Lineages
- **Certain families/clans claim unbroken lines** back to First Ancestors
- **Secret knowledge** (passed only within lineage)
- **Respected but not universally trusted** (elitism breeds resentment)
- **Guarded texts** (oldest shaman writings kept by these families)

## Relationship with Other Faiths

### With the [[Six-Fold Faith]]

**"They worship distant cosmic forces. We honor close earthly spirits."**

**Path of Spirits View:**

**Generally tolerant but dismissive:**
- "Hextheists have lost touch with what matters"
- "They see six gods in the sky; we see countless spirits in every tree"
- "Their prayers go up; our communion happens HERE"

**Some common ground:**
- Both recognize divine multiplicity (many vs. one is the debate)
- Seasonal festivals align (different interpretations)
- Respect for nature exists in some Hextheist sects (especially Weaver-followers)

**Some friction:**
- Hextheists see spirit-worshipers as primitive, superstitious
- Spirit-worshipers see Hextheists as arrogant, disconnected from land
- Competition for followers (especially in rural areas)

**Theories on the Six Gods:**

**"They're just very powerful spirits, misunderstood:"**
- The Architect = Great Spirit of Order
- The Weaver = Great Spirit of Change
- Etc.
- Hextheists mistake great spirits for gods

**"Different paths, both valid:"**
- Some spirits, some gods—maybe both exist
- Less common view, mostly held by Adaptive Path practitioners

**In Practice:**
- Peaceful coexistence (mostly)
- Occasional theological debates (friendly to heated)
- [[Green Covenant]] sometimes targets Hextheist institutions (if they harm nature)

---

### With [[The First Flame Faith]]

**"Monotheism doesn't make sense to our worldview."**

**Path of Spirits View:**

**More alien than Hextheism:**
- "One god for everything? Absurd. Look around—spirits are everywhere, each unique."
- "They reduce the infinite to the singular. How can one flame speak to river AND mountain AND ancestor?"

**Philosophical incompatibility:**
- First Flame: Unity, convergence, all-in-one
- Path of Spirits: Diversity, multiplicity, each-is-itself

**Relationship is distant:**
- Less interaction (First Flame centers in cities, Path strongest in wilderness)
- Less debate (too different to argue—talking past each other)
- Some mutual respect (both recognize spirits/aspects exist, just interpret differently)

**Rare Syncretism:**

**The Emberglade (Path of Spirits name) / The Wild Ember (First Flame name)**

One small offshoot attempts to blend the two faiths:

- **Location:** Coastal area in [[Verdant Crescent]]
- **Belief:** The Eternal Flame is the Great Spirit of Unity; other spirits are its children/emanations
- **Practice:** Blend First Flame theology (six-in-one) with spirit offerings and shamanic journeys
- **Names:**
  - **Path of Spirits calls them:** "The Emberglade" (dismissive—trying to mix fire and forest, unnatural)
  - **First Flame calls them:** "The Wild Ember" (too wild, not proper Eternal Flame worshipers)
  - **They call themselves:** "The Kindred Path" (claiming kinship with both traditions)
- **Size:** Tiny, perhaps 200-500 adherents total
- **Status:** Fringe, distrusted by both mainstream faiths
- **NOT the norm** (most Path of Spirits followers reject this entirely, seeing it as corruption)

**Why It Exists:**
- Coastal location means exposure to First Flame missionaries
- Some spirit-worshipers attracted to Eternal Flame's warmth and structure
- Some First Flame converts miss their ancestral spirit traditions
- Genuine belief that both paths reveal different aspects of truth

**Why It's Rejected:**

By **Path of Spirits:**
- "Spirits are not aspects of one god—they are themselves, unique, countless"
- "The Eternal Flame's fire blinds them to individual spirits' voices"
- "They've lost the old ways, trying to dress up new faith in spirit-garb"

By **First Flame:**
- "They worship fragments when they should worship the whole"
- "Spirits are lesser emanations, not equals to the divine"
- "Too wild, too undisciplined—the Eternal Flame demands order"

**The Emberglade's Defense:**
- "We honor both truths. The Flame unifies; the spirits individuate. Both are sacred."
- "Why must we choose? Cannot fire and forest coexist?"

**In Practice:**
- Mostly separate (different geographic/cultural bases)
- Polite but uncomprehending when they meet
- [[Green Covenant]] more hostile (First Flame cities exploit nature)

---

### With [[Green Covenant]]

**Internal relationship—all Green Covenant MUST follow Path of Spirits.**

**But:**

**Not all Path of Spirits are Green Covenant** (in fact, most aren't).

**The Debate:**

**Are Green Covenant extremists perverting teachings?**
- **Some say yes:** "Violence betrays the spirits. Balance means harmony, not war."
- **Others say no:** "They do what must be done. The rest of us are too soft."

**Are they the TRUE followers?**
- **Some say yes:** "Only they act with full commitment. We compromise too much."
- **Others say no:** "Zealotry is not wisdom. Spirits teach patience, not fury."

**Most Common View:**
- **"They're necessary evil:"** Someone must protect sacred places. Better them than no one. But their methods are troubling.

**Tension Within the Faith:**

- **Mainstream shamans/druids** often condemn Green Covenant violence publicly
- **Privately**, some support their goals if not methods
- **Young practitioners** attracted to Green Covenant's passion and action
- **Elders** worried the extremism will draw persecution to all spirit-worshipers

**Green Covenant's Perspective:**

"We are the only ones who ACT. The spirits warned us. We listened. Now we fight."

## The Path of Spirits in the World

### Geographic Presence & Influence

**Where Path of Spirits is MAJORITY:**

- **Frostwell Tundra** (nearly universal)
- **Parts of Northern Expanse** (especially nomadic tribes)

**Where Path of Spirits is STRONG:**

- **Verdant Crescent** (dominant in forest regions, contested in towns)
- **Glittering Marshes** (majority in marsh communities)
- **Rural areas everywhere** (farmers, hunters, small villages)

**Where Path of Spirits is MINORITY:**

- **Lumencrest** (tolerated but not welcome—don't flaunt it)
- **Coastal regions** (First Flame spread via trade routes)
- **[[The Endless Eye]]** (diverse—all faiths accepted, no majority)
- **Urban centers generally** (cities lean toward organized religions)

---

### Political Power

**Deliberately avoids world politics** (mortal kingdoms rise and fall; spirits endure).

**But:**

#### Local Influence
- **Village elders are often shamans** (spiritual + political authority combined)
- **Druid councils govern some settlements** (especially in Verdant Crescent)
- **Land disputes judged by druids** (especially sacred land)

#### Hidden Influence
- **Spirits guide those who listen** (leaders consult shamans, often secretly)
- **Omens shape decisions** (if shaman says "spirits warn against war," leaders heed it—sometimes)
- **Nature itself enforces** (anger spirits, suffer consequences—leaders learn this)

#### No Regional Rule
- **No Path of Spirits theocracy** exists (they don't seek that power)
- **Some regions heavily influenced** (Verdant Crescent communities defer to druid circles)
- **But formal government?** No.

**Why Avoid Politics:**

"Mortals govern mortals. Spirits govern nature. Mixing them corrupts both."

**Exception:** When political decisions threaten nature/sacred sites, spirit-worshipers ACT (sometimes violently—see [[Green Covenant]]).

---

### Social Standing

**Varies wildly by region:**

**In rural/northern areas:**
- **Respected, honored** (shamans are pillars of community)
- **Normal** (everyone follows the Path, no stigma)
- **Powerful** (cross a shaman at your peril—spirits backing them)

**In cities/coastal areas:**
- **Primitive** (seen as backwards, superstitious)
- **Tolerated** (freedom of religion, but second-class)
- **Exotic** (urbanites find them mysterious, hire for fortune-telling)

**In Lumencrest specifically:**
- **Keep it quiet** (worship in private, don't advertise)
- **Marginalized** (Hextheism dominant, First Flame growing, spirits ignored)
- **Useful idiots** (some hire shamans for "local color" or hedge bets)

**In The Endless Eye:**
- **Fully accepted** (all faiths welcome, no judgment)
- **One of many** (Hextheists, First Flame, Path—all coexist)
- **Merchant-friendly** (shamans bless caravans, druids consult on trade routes)

---

### Declining Faith

**Path of Spirits is losing adherents.**

**Why:**

1. **Urbanization:** People move to cities, adopt city religions
2. **Organized faiths appeal:** Hextheism and First Flame have structure, texts, grand temples
3. **Younger generation:** Find spirits "old-fashioned," prefer modern faiths
4. **Persecution:** In some areas, spirit-worship is discouraged or banned
5. **Practical:** Cities have no sacred groves, spirit wells—hard to practice
6. **Success of other faiths:** First Flame's growth (especially) pulls converts

**Evidence:**

- Fewer young shamans (not enough marked children? or parents hide them?)
- Sacred sites neglected (druids spread thin, can't maintain all)
- Oral traditions fragmenting (lineages dying out)
- Written knowledge lost (caches forgotten, hidden texts decay)

**Response:**

- **Elders lament** but don't know how to stop it
- **Adaptive Path tries** to modernize (blend with other faiths—mostly fails)
- **Green Covenant fights** (literally—attack symbols of "civilization")
- **Some accept:** "Cycles. Faiths rise and fall. If spirits will it, so be it."

**Hope:**

- **The current crisis** (Hollowstars dimming) is bringing people BACK
- "The spirits warned us. We were right. Listen NOW."
- Whether this reverses decline... remains to be seen.

## Visual & Sensory Identity

### What Practitioners Look Like

**Clothing:**
- **Natural materials:** Leather, wool, linen, furs (no synthetic)
- **Earth tones:** Browns, greens, grays (blend with nature)
- **Regional variation:** Tundra furs, marsh woven reeds, forest leaf-patterns
- **Often indistinguishable** from non-believers (no required uniform)

**Shamans Specifically:**
- **Marked:** Tattoos, scars, body paint more common
- **Symbols visible:** Knotwork, spirals, animal designs on skin
- **Jewelry:** Bone, wood, stone (especially sacred animal bones)
- **Practical:** Carry ritual tools (drums, rattles, herb pouches)

**Druids Specifically:**
- **Green and brown** preferred (forest colors)
- **Living adornments:** Flowers in hair, vines woven in braids
- **Simplicity:** Less ornamentation than shamans (focus on grove, not self)

**No Totems:**
- Despite animal spirit reverence, **not into totem poles or carved figures**
- Spirits are not things to be captured in wood

### Sacred Symbols

**Knotwork:**
- **Primary visual motif**
- Endless loops (life's cycles, interconnection)
- Carved, painted, tattooed everywhere
- Each knot pattern has meaning (only shamans know all)

**Animal Representations:**
- **Abstract, not literal:** Stylized bears, crows, stags (not realistic drawings)
- Show **essence** of animal, not appearance
- Used in jewelry, carvings, tattoos

**Spirals:**
- **Journey inward and outward** (spirit journeys, cycles)
- Painted on ritual drums
- Carved into sacred stones

**NOT elemental symbols:**
- No specific earth/air/fire/water iconography
- Spirits are too diverse to categorize that way

### Sounds

**Drums:**
- **Primary ritual instrument**
- Heartbeat rhythm (connects to earth's pulse)
- Guides shamans into trance
- Calls spirits during ceremonies

**Chanting:**
- **Repetitive, rhythmic** (not melodic songs)
- Often in old languages (some words' meanings lost)
- Entire communities chant together in festivals

**Rattles:**
- **Made from gourds, bones, seeds**
- Sharper sound than drums (cuts through)
- Used to ward off malicious spirits

**Natural sounds:**
- **NOT ritualized** (wind, water, birdsong are spirits speaking—you listen, don't replicate)

**Silence:**
- **NOT emphasized** (that's more [[The First Flame Faith|Guiding Light]] style)
- Spirit communion is active, vocal, embodied

### Scents

**Burning sage and herbs:**
- **Cleansing smoke** (purifies space, people, objects)
- Specific herbs for specific purposes:
  - Sage: Protection
  - Cedar: Calling ancestors
  - Sweetgrass: Blessing
  - Mugwort: Vision enhancement

**Smoke offerings:**
- Herbs, resins, sometimes food burned
- Smoke carries offerings to spirits (physical becomes spiritual)

**NOT:**
- Incense (too refined, too "civilized")
- Perfumes (artificial scents mask nature)
- Blood scent (only in serious sacrifices, not daily)

## Gameplay Integration

### For Players

**Characters Following Path of Spirits:**

**Class Options:**
- **Druids:** Natural fit (literally the class)
- **Rangers:** Spirit-guided hunters
- **Clerics:** Shamans (domain: Nature, or custom Spirit domain)
- **Barbarians:** Rage as spirit possession (especially northern tribes)
- **Any class** can follow the Path (fighters with spirit-blessed weapons, rogues who honor trickster spirits, etc.)

**Character Backgrounds:**
- **Shaman-marked:** Born with the mark, trained, now adventuring (why leave your community?)
- **Druid initiate:** Protecting a sacred grove, now threatened
- **Exile:** Broke taboo, seeking redemption through deeds
- **Crisis-driven:** Spirits sent visions, commanded you to act
- **Lost faith:** Followed Path, now doubting (can spirits save us?)

**Mechanical Benefits (suggestions):**
- **Spirit Communion:** Once per day, contact a spirit for guidance (like Augury)
- **Ancestor's Luck:** Reroll one failed save per long rest (ancestors watching)
- **Nature's Ally:** Advantage on Animal Handling and Survival in natural environments

**Restrictions/Flavor:**
- **Cannot slay defenseless animals** (hurts roleplaying if ignored)
- **Must make offerings** before major actions (costs a bit of gold/time, adds flavor)
- **Spirit journey mechanics:** Long rest in sacred places grants visions/bonuses

---

### For GMs

**NPCs:**

**Shaman Archetypes:**
- **Wise Elder:** Cryptic, helpful, knows more than they say
- **Young Zealot:** Passionate, inexperienced, makes mistakes
- **Hermit:** Lives alone, powerful but eccentric
- **Fraud:** Claims to be shaman, actually charlatan (how do players know?)

**Druid Archetypes:**
- **Grove Guardian:** Protective, territorial, suspicious of outsiders
- **Wanderer:** Travels, shares knowledge, bridge between communities
- **Radical:** [[Green Covenant]] member, willing to kill for nature

**Spirit NPCs:**
- **Household spirit:** Mischievous, helpful if honored, troublesome if ignored
- **Great spirit:** Powerful, demanding, offers quests or threats
- **Ancestor:** Player character's dead relative, offers guidance or judgment

---

**Plot Hooks:**

- **Sacred site threatened:** Logging company, military outpost, Hollostar mining—stop them
- **Shaman needs escort:** Elderly shaman making pilgrimage to The Great Tree, dangerous journey
- **Spirit journey gone wrong:** Someone trapped in spirit realm, shamans need help retrieving them
- **Transformed creature:** Study it, learn its message, kill it mercifully
- **Taboo broken:** Someone slaughtered sacred animals, spirits demand justice
- **Green Covenant attack:** Stop them or help them, depending on party alignment
- **The Great Tree:** Pilgrimage, revelation, meeting with ancient spirits
- **Crisis ritual:** Participate in massive offering, perhaps including human sacrifice debate

---

**Using Spirits in Play:**

**Spirits are NPCs:**
- Give them personalities (trickster, stern, gentle, hungry)
- They want things (offerings, respect, tasks completed)
- They remember (PCs who honor them gain allies, those who don't make enemies)

**Spirits are consequences:**
- Ignore warnings → suffer disasters (crop failure, sickness, bad luck)
- Honor properly → gain benefits (easy hunts, good weather, visions)

**Spirits are mysteries:**
- Don't explain everything (spirits are unknowable in full)
- Contradictory messages (spirits disagree with each other)
- Players must interpret (shamans give guidance, not answers)
