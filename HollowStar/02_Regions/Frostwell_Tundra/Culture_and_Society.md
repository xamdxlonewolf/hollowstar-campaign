# Culture and Society - Frostwell Tundra

---

## NINE HEARTHS CULTURE

### Core Values

**The hearth is sacred.** Fire is life. Maintaining it is not optional. Letting a fire go out in winter is the worst social failure — metaphorically and literally.

**You carry your share.** No one survives the tundra alone. Contribution is expected, not praised. Praise is for extraordinary contribution.

**Memory is survival.** Which routes are safe. Which weather signs mean danger. Which plants under the snow are edible. This knowledge is passed down as seriously as any written tradition.

**Grief happens in spring.** Winter is for surviving. You mourn your dead when the ground thaws. This sounds cold. It isn't — it's the culture understanding that you can't fall apart in winter.

### Giants in the Nine Hearths

Giants (large humans — similar to Goliaths in build and nature) are fully integrated into the confederation. Not a separate culture — just larger members of the same communities.

**Physical role:**
- Breaking trail through deep snow (invaluable)
- Heavy lifting, construction, ice fishing through thick ice
- Some serve as living windbreaks for smaller community members in blizzards (this is considered an honor, not a burden)

**Social role:**
- No special status from size — contribution is contribution
- Some Giant families have lived in specific villages for generations (Coldfast has the highest Giant population)
- Cultural joke: Giants always get the coldest sleeping spot (farthest from hearth) because they radiate more heat. Giants: not wrong, still annoying.

### Daily Life

**Winter (8 months):**
- Survival mode — every action serves the community's winter endurance
- Long dark periods (aurora the main light)
- Communal halls (sleeping together for warmth is normal)
- Storytelling season (oral history, legends, practical knowledge passed down)

**Summer (4 months):**
- Explosion of activity — everything that can't be done in winter happens now
- Hunting, fishing, foraging, construction, travel between villages
- Trade season (outsiders visit during this window)
- Children born in summer have significantly better survival rates (culturally noted)

### The Stay/Flee Cultural Fracture

The star crisis has split something that was never meant to split.

**Stayer identity:** "We are Nine Hearths people. Nine Hearths is here. Leaving means we cease to be who we are."

**Leaver identity:** "Nine Hearths is the people, not the place. We carry it with us."

Both are sincere. Both are irreconcilable. Families are divided. Friendships ended. People who shared hearths for decades no longer eat together.

## RELIGION

### Path of Spirits (Dominant)
- Tundra spirits deeply integrated into daily life
- Storm spirits (respected, appeased)
- Ice spirits (ancient, territorial)
- Animal spirits (hunted animals honored, waste forbidden)
- Not formal organized religion — personal relationship with spirits

### Six-Fold Faith (Minor)
- Present, not dominant
- The Wanderer (travel, survival) most relevant aspect
- Small shrines in some villages

### The Ember Cradle Reverence (Drakona-specific, some Nine Hearths)
- Some Emberveil villagers have developed adjacent beliefs
- Not Drakona tradition exactly — more like: "something important is there and we should respect it"
- Path of Spirits pilgrims have complicated this (outsiders claiming the ruins for their faith)

### Outsider Faiths (Arriving)
- Path of Spirits pilgrims coming to witness the impact
- First Flame (some Lumencrest researchers)
- Tension: Locals feel their spiritual landscape is being colonized by outsiders' interpretations

## SURVIVAL PRACTICES

### Cold Survival
- **Layering:** Three layers minimum outside (outer wind layer, middle insulating, inner moisture-wicking)
- **Buddy system:** Never travel alone more than an hour from settlement
- **Snow caves:** Every Nine Hearths person can build one in under 20 minutes
- **Frostbite protocol:** Immediate warming, no rubbing, specific herbal treatments (local knowledge)

### Navigation
- Star navigation (reliable — clear nights frequent)
- Aurora reading (direction sense — locals can read subtle aurora patterns)
- Snow formation reading (wind patterns, terrain ahead)
- Cairn systems between villages (maintained communally)

### Food in Winter
- Preserved fish (primary protein — ice fishing year-round)
- Smoked and dried meat (summer hunting feeds winter)
- Underground root storage (warm spring areas have limited root vegetables)
- Emergency: Fat rendering, bone broth, emergency ration caches along major routes
