# Ancient Ruins - Frostwell Tundra

Pre-Hollostar structures scattered across Frostwell — evidence of civilizations that existed before the first star fell 10,000 years ago.

---

## THE SHATTERED SPIRE

**Location:** Northwestern tundra  
**Identity:** Tower fragment, pre-Hollostar architecture, partial excavation

A tower fragment jutting from the permafrost — only the top 50 feet visible, unknown depth below. The architecture is unlike anything built since Hollowstars arrived. The stone doesn't weather. The angles aren't quite right. Looking at it too long causes headaches.

**What it was:** Unknown. The visible portion suggests a tower or spire that once stood hundreds of feet tall. Whether it collapsed or was deliberately broken is unclear.

**Current status:** Partially excavated by Starfall Seekers. The permafrost makes deep excavation nearly impossible. What they've found: empty chambers, no artifacts, walls covered in symbols that don't match any known language.

**Local reaction:** Frostwell residents avoid it. The Spire feels wrong. Animals avoid the area. Those who camp nearby report disturbing dreams.

**The headache phenomenon:** Prolonged observation (more than an hour) causes severe headaches, nausea, occasionally nosebleeds. Starfall Seekers work in shifts to avoid prolonged exposure.

---

## FROSTVAULT

**Location:** Central tundra, buried complex  
**Identity:** Underground ruins, partially excavated, largest known pre-Hollostar site

Frostvault is a buried complex discovered 80 years ago when tundra erosion exposed an entrance. Excavation has revealed a structure extending deep underground — at least six levels explored, more suspected below.

**Architecture:** Massive stone construction, preservation perfect despite 10,000 years. The lowest explored level contains machinery of unknown purpose that still functions. Nobody knows what it does.

**The Starfall Seeker expedition:** A permanent Seeker outpost operates at Frostvault. 12 researchers rotating in two-month shifts. They've been excavating and documenting for decades.

**What they've found:**
- Intact chambers showing no weathering
- Mechanisms that still move when activated
- A chamber with a stone map showing stars in positions that don't match current sky
- Writing in three different unknown languages
- No bodies, no organic remains, no hint who built it

**The machine problem:** The lowest accessible level contains a large circular mechanism. When Seekers accidentally activated it three years ago, it ran for 20 minutes, the walls hummed, and then it stopped. It hasn't run since. Nobody knows what it did or whether it should have.

**Local position:** Coldfast residents consider Frostvault bad luck but tolerate the Seekers. The dwarves of Barad-Khelek find it fascinating and have offered mining expertise to excavate deeper. The Seekers declined (they don't trust dwarf methods around ancient mechanisms).

## THE SLEEPING GATE

**Location:** Near projected impact zone  
**Identity:** Massive arch, unknown material, ominous

A massive arch standing alone on the tundra — 200 feet tall, 150 feet wide, made of material that resembles stone but isn't. It has survived 10,000 years of Frostwell weather without weathering, without cracks, without any sign of degradation.

**What it does:** Nothing. It stands. It doesn't activate, react, or change. Just stands.

**Why it's called the Sleeping Gate:** Because it looks like a gate that's closed. Or hasn't opened yet. The name came from Frostwell folklore — locals have always said it's "sleeping" rather than dead or inert. They don't know why they say this.

**The symbols:** The arch is covered in carved symbols. Unlike Frostvault's writing, these symbols occasionally change. Not dramatically — a line here, a curve there. Starfall Seekers have been documenting this for 40 years. The changes are real. Nobody knows what triggers them.

**Local taboo:** Frostwell residents don't go near the Sleeping Gate. Not because it's dangerous (it isn't, as far as anyone knows) but because it's wrong. The animals won't approach. Birds don't fly through the arch. It just stands there, sleeping, waiting for something.

**Impact zone proximity:** The Sleeping Gate sits in the projected impact zone. If the Hollostar lands where calculations suggest, the Gate will be destroyed. Or will it? It's survived 10,000 years. Can an impact break what weather cannot?

**The Seeker outpost:** A small outpost (6 researchers) observes the Gate year-round. They're documenting symbol changes, monitoring for activation, and will remain until evacuation becomes absolutely necessary.

**The question:** What happens if the Hollostar lands here? Does the Gate wake up? Is that what it's for?

---

## WHY THEY MATTER

These ruins prove civilization existed before the first Hollostar. They prove the Somnarchs are right — the stars fall in patterns, cycles. Civilizations rise, stars fall, civilizations die. It's happened before.

**The Starfall Seekers' mission:** Document everything. If Frostwell is destroyed, the knowledge must survive. These ruins are evidence of what was lost 10,000 years ago. They may contain clues about survival.

**The Somnarch position:** The ruins are warnings. They survived the last cycle to warn the next. We should listen.

**Most people's position:** The ruins are creepy and best avoided. Let the scholars obsess. We have survival to worry about.

---

## CURRENT ACTIVITY

**Shattered Spire:** Minimal. Too dangerous for large-scale excavation. Small Seeker team documents visible portions.

**Frostvault:** Active. 12-person rotating expedition. Permanent outpost. Ongoing excavation of lower levels.

**Sleeping Gate:** Observation only. 6-person outpost documenting symbol changes. Will evacuate last.

**Evacuation plans:** Frostvault outpost will evacuate to Northern Expanse if impact becomes imminent. They're packing the most important documentation and leaving excavation equipment behind. Sleeping Gate outpost refuses to commit to evacuation timeline — they want to observe what happens when the star approaches.
