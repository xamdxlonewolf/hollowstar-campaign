# Dwarves and Drakona of Frostwell

**Region:** [[02_Regions/Frostwell_Tundra/Frostwell_Tundra_Index|Frostwell Tundra]]  
**Populations:** Barad-Khelek (dwarves, ~2,000) and Ember Cradle (Drakona, ~300)  
**Relationship:** Separate, respectful distance, occasional cooperation

---

## OVERVIEW

Two distinct populations live in Frostwell apart from the Nine Hearths human/Giant villages:

**The Dwarves of Barad-Khelek:** Mountain miners living in carved holds, extracting rare minerals, maintaining traditional isolation while trading with Nine Hearths.

**The Drakona of Ember Cradle:** Cold-resistant reptilian people guarding ancient ruins they claim as sacred origin site, preparing for prophesied transformation. Centuries of living in Frostwell has changed them — where their southern cousins in the Desert of Glass are heat-resistant, these Drakona have adapted fully to the frozen north.

They occupy same region but different territories, interact occasionally, respect boundaries mostly.

---

## BARAD-KHELEK (THE DWARVEN HOLD)

### Location & Structure

**Carved into:** Northwestern mountain range, sheltered valley position

**The name:** "Barad-Khelek" in Dwarven means "Ice-Tower-Hold" — named for prominent frozen waterfall that appears like tower next to entrance.

**Population:** ~2,000 dwarves (stable for centuries)

**Architecture:** Traditional dwarven construction:
- External gates (massive stone doors, defensible)
- Main hall (communal gathering space, carved decorations)
- Residential halls (family quarters, multi-generational)
- Mine tunnels (extensive network into mountains)
- Forge district (utilizing geothermal vents, not as hot as volcanic regions but sufficient)

### The Mines

**What they extract:**
- **Frostsilver:** Rare ore found only in extreme cold (loses properties if heated above freezing during extraction)
- **Deep iron:** High-quality iron from ancient rock layers
- **Ice gems:** Crystals formed in permafrost under specific pressure conditions
- **Permafrost coal:** Preserved ancient forests, valuable fuel

**The challenge:** Mining in permafrost is dangerous. Tunnels can collapse when ice melts from body heat/lamps. Dwarves developed techniques for cold-mining (minimal heat, quick work, constant monitoring).

**Export:** Quarterly caravans to Nine Hearths settlements (trade minerals for food, supplies). Occasional long-distance trade with southern settlements (Frostsilver commands premium prices).

### Culture

**Extreme isolation:** Even by dwarven standards, Barad-Khelek is insular. Outsiders rarely welcomed. They maintain trade relationships but minimal social contact.

**Why so isolated:** After collapse of their southern kingdom centuries ago, these dwarves fled north seeking place nobody else wanted. They found it. They intend to keep it.

**Traditional values:** Strongly maintain old dwarven ways:
- Deep Reckoning calendar (not Accord Reckoning)
- Clan structure (five clans, hereditary leadership)
- Ancestor veneration (not Forge-Father Faith, older traditions)
- Craft excellence (every item made properly or not at all)

**Cold adaptation:** Barad-Khelek dwarves are uniquely adapted:
- Thicker natural cold resistance than southern dwarves
- Cultural knowledge of extreme cold survival
- Specialized equipment for sub-zero mining
- "Cold-beards" — they let beards ice up deliberately (acts as insulation)

### Leadership

**The Stone-Thane:** **Thorald Ice-Beard** (dwarf, 412, male) — leader of Barad-Khelek, seventh generation since founding, conservative, cautious, deeply concerned about Hollostar.

**The Council of Five:** Representatives from each clan advise Stone-Thane. Decisions require his approval plus clan consensus.

**Current clans:**
- **Ice-Beard** (Thorald's clan, miners, largest)
- **Frost-Hammer** (smiths, forge-masters)
- **Stone-Delver** (deep miners, specialists)
- **Silver-Hand** (merchants, external trade)
- **Deep-Song** (record-keepers, historians)

### Relationship to Others

**Nine Hearths humans:** Trading partners, respectful distance. Dwarves provide minerals, receive food/supplies. No deep friendship but reliable business relationship.

**Ember Cradle Drakona:** Minimal contact. Dwarves find Drakona odd (cold-adapted lizard-people who clearly belong here and yet still seem like strangers). Drakona find dwarves irrelevant to their spiritual mission. Both sides maintain boundaries.

**Starfall Seekers:** Dwarves fascinated by ancient ruins but don't trust Seekers' methods. They've offered mining expertise for deeper excavation, been refused. Some resentment but not serious conflict.

**Southern dwarves (Cinderhome, Ironhold):** Aware of existence but minimal contact. Centuries of isolation created cultural divergence. Barad-Khelek sees southern dwarves as soft/compromised. Southern dwarves see Barad-Khelek as backwards/isolated.

### The Hollostar Crisis

**Dwarven concern:** Impact will devastate surface Frostwell. But deep halls might survive. Stone protects.

**The plan:** If impact imminent, seal gates, retreat to deepest levels, shelter until aftermath passes. Food stores for 2+ years. Water from underground sources. They could survive.

**The debate:** Should they offer shelter to Nine Hearths survivors? Some dwarves say yes (moral duty). Others say no (insufficient resources, maintain security). Thorald hasn't decided.

**Current status:** Preparing for worst. Expanding deep halls, increasing food stores, reinforcing structural supports. If star hits, they'll survive. What they emerge to is unknown.

## EMBER CRADLE (THE DRAKONA SETTLEMENT)

### Location & Structure

**Near ancient ruins:** Eastern tundra, built around/near the Ember Cradle ruins they guard.

**Population:** ~300 Drakona (200 permanent, 100 pilgrims fluctuating)

**Architecture:** Temporary/semi-permanent structures:
- Hide tents (insulated with multiple layers)
- Stone shelters (using ruins' materials, simple construction)
- Communal warmth buildings (large insulated structures where Drakona gather to conserve body heat against the cold)
- Pilgrimage hospice (basic shelter for visitors)

**Why temporary:** Despite their cold resistance, Frostwell Drakona don't consider the settlement permanent — not because it's inhospitable, but because their purpose here is spiritual, not civic. Buildings reflect this — functional and communal, not built to last generations.

### The Edrathar — The Lost Drake Rider Bloodline

*This section is GM knowledge. Most Drakona — including those at Ember Cradle — have no awareness of this history.*

Over 1,100 years ago, Frostwell Drakona who carried a specific bloodline could bond with the **ice-drakes** of the deep tundra — cold-blooded, white-scaled cousins to the great fire-dragons, built for the arctic sky. This bond was called *Edrathar* ("the cold calling"). At the war's peak, these Drake Riders were formidable aerial fighters who contested the Northern Expanse's northern territory with the Great Eagle Riders in a prolonged conflict that lasted generations.

The Drakona lost. The war ended not with a single defeat but with attrition — drake deaths, rider grief, territorial collapse. The surviving Drake Riders withdrew. Over the following centuries the ice-drakes retreated deeper into unmapped tundra and eventually disappeared from confirmed sightings (~600 years ago). The *Edrathar* became a word in ritual texts that most Drakona could no longer translate.

**What remains:** The bloodline. Recessive and unpredictable, it persists in roughly one in several hundred Frostwell Drakona. Carriers typically show subtle signs — slightly warmer than cold-adapted kin, unusual spatial awareness, inexplicable draws to high places, animals responding to them differently. Most rationalize these or never notice them at all.

The bloodline does nothing without a drake to activate it. There have been no living ice-drakes for centuries. But the approaching Hollostar's energy is waking ancient things across the continent's deep sites — and the unmapped tundra beyond Frostwell's settled territory has never been fully surveyed.

**→ Full details on the war, the bloodline, and PC options:** [[06_History/War_of_Skies|The War of Skies]]

### Two Peoples, One Ancestry

**The great divergence:** Drakona were not always split. Centuries ago — how many is disputed — a population migrated north and never returned. Over generations, living in permafrost and frozen tundra reshaped them biologically. What began as cultural adaptation (gear, technique, ritual cold-hardening) became something deeper: their scales darkened to absorb heat, their blood runs cooler, their bodies no longer suffer in temperatures that would kill their southern kin.

**Frostwell Drakona physiology:** Cold-resistant by nature — ice and extreme cold cause them no harm and they are fully at home in Frostwell's brutal winters. Heat, however, is deeply uncomfortable. Prolonged exposure to volcanic or desert temperatures drains them, slows their thinking, leaves them weakened. The Desert of Glass would be genuinely dangerous for them without preparation.

**Desert of Glass Drakona physiology:** The reverse — heat-resistant, thriving in volcanic environments, deeply uncomfortable in sustained cold. Ashenveil and the Skyspine Mountains are their natural home. Frostwell Tundra would be punishing without significant preparation.

**How they see each other:** Distant cousins — different but respectful. There is no bitterness about the divergence. Both groups understand that their ancestors walked different roads and the roads changed them. When they meet (at Ember Cradle pilgrimages, or when Frostwell Drakona journey south to Cindrath), there is curiosity, some cultural friction around customs and temperature preferences, and an underlying sense of shared origin that neither side dismisses. They are kin who became strangers without ever meaning to.

### The Pilgrimages

**Desert Drakona to Ember Cradle:** Southern Drakona who feel the spiritual call to witness the warming ruins must travel north into cold that their bodies were not built for. This is part of the pilgrimage's weight — arriving at a sacred site where your cousins are at home and you are vulnerable. Many travel with cold-weather gear, guides, and fire-magic. Some arrive in genuine danger. The difficulty is considered meaningful, not a problem to be solved.

**Frostwell Drakona to Cindrath:** The reciprocal journey — Frostwell Drakona making the long trek south into volcanic heat to seek audience with the Ancient in Ashenveil. For them, the Desert of Glass is the alien environment their southern cousins navigate without thinking. They sweat, they slow, they wrap themselves in cooling cloths. Cindrath receives them with the same mild fondness she shows all Drakona — and is said to find the sight of frost-scaled Drakona wilting in her heat privately amusing, though she would never say so.

### The Cold Endurance Settlement

**How Frostwell Drakona live in the tundra:** Comfortably, by and large. The cold that kills Nine Hearths travelers in hours is simply weather to them. What they still need:

### Leadership

**Flame-Keeper Sorith:** (Drakona, 67, female) — spiritual leader, village elder, guardian of ruins, interpreter of signs.

**Her role:**
- Spiritual authority (guides ceremonies, interprets prophecy, maintains traditions)
- Practical leadership (manages resources, coordinates pilgrims, negotiates with outsiders)
- Guardian of secrets (knows more about ruins/warming than she tells)

**Her burden:** She's responsible for 300 lives in hostile environment. Every decision balances spiritual mission against survival. She fears making wrong choice when crisis comes.

**The Council of Flame-Speakers:** Six senior Drakona advise Sorith. Represent different functions (security, resources, ceremonies, pilgrimage coordination, health, scouts).

### The Spiritual Mission

**Why they're here:** Ember Cradle is their holy site. The warming is prophesied sign. They must witness whatever comes.

**The belief:** When ruins fully wake, something transformative happens. Drakona will be part of it — witnesses, participants, or transformed themselves.

**Prophecy variants:** Different Drakona interpret differently:
- **Birthers:** Believe new Drakona will be born/created
- **Transcenders:** Believe existing Drakona will transform (evolve? Ascend? Change?)
- **Witnesses:** Believe their role is observation and recording
- **Warriors:** Believe they must defend ruins/event from outsiders

Sorith hasn't declared official interpretation. Intentional ambiguity keeps all factions united.

### Daily Life

**Daily routines:**
- Morning: Tend fires (for light and cooking, not warmth), check on visiting southern pilgrims, begin ceremonies
- Midday: Hunting, gathering, maintenance, spiritual observances at the ruins
- Evening: Communal gathering, storytelling, sharing news from southern cousins
- Night: Sleep under open sky if the mood takes them, or in shelters — either is fine

**Food:** Hunted locally (tundra elk, arctic birds, ice fish from frozen rivers) with supplements traded from Nine Hearths. Frostwell Drakona are capable hunters who range freely in conditions that would kill other peoples.

**Health:** The permanent residents are in their element and generally healthy. The health challenge is managing visiting Desert Drakona pilgrims who arrive from volcanic climates into bitter cold — the settlement maintains a dedicated warming room for southern cousins who underestimate the journey.

### Relationship to Others

**Starfall Seekers:** Complicated. Seekers want ruins access. Drakona grant limited permission but restrict sacred areas. Ongoing negotiation between Sorith and Seeker team leader.

**Nine Hearths:** Minimal interaction. Drakona trade sporadically for supplies. Nine Hearths find Drakona presence strange but unthreatening. Neither side seeks close relationship.

**Barad-Khelek dwarves:** Practically no contact. Different territories, different concerns, different cultures. They're aware of each other, nothing more.

**Incoming pilgrims:** Constant influx. Sorith struggles managing numbers. Every Drakona who feels the spiritual call wants to be here. She can't turn them away (spiritually wrong) but can't support all of them (practically impossible).

### The Hollostar Reality

**When impact comes:** Ember Cradle is in projected impact zone. Everyone here will die unless they evacuate.

**Drakona position:** They won't evacuate. This is prophesied moment. They must be present.

**Sorith's private fear:** She believes in prophecy but also understands physics. If they stay and impact is as destructive as predicted, everyone dies. Is faith worth mass death? She hasn't told anyone she's questioning.

**The secret evacuation plan:** Sorith has hidden contingency — if evidence becomes overwhelming that staying means certain pointless death, she'll order evacuation. Most Drakona will refuse. She'll have failed both spiritually and practically.

## DWARVES vs. DRAKONA: CONTRAST

**Survival approach:**
- Dwarves: Preparation, isolation, defense, survive underground
- Drakona: Faith, sacrifice, witness, accept death if necessary

**Relationship to impact:**
- Dwarves: Pragmatic threat requiring practical response
- Drakona: Prophetic event requiring spiritual presence

**Cultural values:**
- Dwarves: Tradition, craft, clan, ancestry
- Drakona: Transformation, cold endurance, prophecy, collective experience

**Outsider relations:**
- Dwarves: Minimal, controlled, transactional
- Drakona: Complicated, spiritually significant, negotiated

They're very different peoples pursuing very different goals in same harsh region.

## CURRENT TENSIONS

### The Dwarven Shelter Debate

**Question:** Should Barad-Khelek offer shelter to surface survivors when impact comes?

**Arguments for:** Moral duty, save lives, strengthen future relationships, prove dwarven worth

**Arguments against:** Insufficient resources, security risk, can't save everyone (who decides who lives?), compromises dwarven survival

**Thorald's position:** Undecided, paralyzed by weight of decision

**Timeline:** Must decide before impact. If they wait too long, preparation impossible.

### The Drakona Evacuation Question

**Sorith's dilemma:** Order evacuation (save lives, abandon spiritual mission) or stay (fulfill prophecy, likely die)?

**Complications:** Most Drakona won't evacuate voluntarily. She'd have to force them (impossible) or abandon them (unconscionable).

**The sealed information:** She knows warming rate is accelerating faster than she's told Seekers. Something is definitely happening. Impact might not be needed to trigger event.

**Her choice:** Stay and witness (faithful to mission, potentially fatal) or leave and survive (preserve Drakona community, spiritual failure)?

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Dwarven Refuge:** Party caught in Frostwell when evacuation orders given. Barad-Khelek offers shelter — for price. Navigate dwarven politics, earn place in deep halls, or find alternative.

**The Forced Evacuation:** Authorities hire party to evacuate Drakona from Ember Cradle before impact. Drakona refuse. Party must convince/force them to leave or help them stay (moral complexity).

**The Cultural Exchange:** Party mediates between dwarves and Drakona. Minimal past contact, but impact crisis requires cooperation. Can two very different cultures find common ground?

**The Pilgrimage Protection:** Escort a large group of Desert Drakona pilgrims north across frozen tundra to Ember Cradle. They're heat-resistant by nature and genuinely unprepared for sustained cold — some are stoic about it, some dangerously overconfident, all are determined. Party must keep them alive through an environment their Frostwell cousins call home but they find punishing.

**The Deep Halls Expedition:** Barad-Khelek discovers something strange in deepest mine levels. Need outsiders to investigate (dwarves are superstitious about it). Party explores, discovers: [ancient mechanism? Sealed chamber? Something sleeping?].

**The Warming Investigation:** Party hired by both groups independently. Dwarves want to know if ruins threaten their territory. Drakona want to understand spiritual significance. Party must satisfy both clients with very different priorities.
