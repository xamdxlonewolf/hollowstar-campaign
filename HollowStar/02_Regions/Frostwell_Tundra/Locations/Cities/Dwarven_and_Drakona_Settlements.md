# Dwarven Holds & Drakona Settlements - Frostwell Tundra

The dwarven mountain holds and Drakona communities of Frostwell.

---

## DWARVEN HOLDS (Southern Mountains)

All three holds voted to stay. The mountains will protect them.

### Barad-Khelek / Ironpeak (~2,500)

**Dwarven name:** Barad-Khelek ("Ice-Fortress")  
**Common name:** Ironpeak  
**Location:** Central southern mountains  
**Identity:** Major hold, extensive mining, fortified deep

Barad-Khelek is carved deep into the mountain's heart. Seven levels descending, with the deepest reaching ancient stone untouched since the world formed. The hold has survived ten thousand years of Frostwell winter — a Hollostar impact on the surface is survivable.

**Thane Grom Icehammer** (dwarf, 287, male) leads five clans (Icehammer, Frostbeard, Stonedelve, Ironforge, Shieldbreaker). His position is simple: the hold stays, the gates open to refugees if impact approaches, survival underground is possible for decades if necessary.

**Mining operations:** Iron, copper, coal, gemstones. The hold is self-sufficient for metalworking and fuel. Trade with Nine Hearths has been minimal but functional.

**Current preparation:** Stockpiling food, expanding deep storage caverns, reinforcing upper levels against potential quakes. If the star lands close, the mountain will shake. The deep holds will survive.

**Refugee offer:** Barad-Khelek has offered shelter to surface communities if evacuation south becomes impossible. Few have accepted — humans don't want to live underground for years. The dwarves find this baffling but don't insist.

---

### Khazad-Helek / Stonehearth (~1,800)

**Dwarven name:** Khazad-Helek ("Frost-Hall")  
**Common name:** Stonehearth  
**Location:** Southern mountains, deeper interior  
**Identity:** Traditional stronghold, conservative, isolationist

Khazad-Helek is older and more traditional than Barad-Khelek. The hold has maintained dwarven ways with minimal surface contact. Their trade with Nine Hearths was limited to necessity.

**Elder Council** (five clan elders, ages 300-450) leads by consensus. They speak less than Barad-Khelek but mean more. Their position: we have survived here since before the first Hollostar fell, we will survive the fourth.

**Character:** Deep tradition, minimal change, suspicious of surface dwellers. Not hostile — just uninterested. The coming star is surface problem. Mountains endure.

**Resources:** Less mining than Barad-Khelek, more focused on deep farming (fungi cultivation, underground streams for fish), completely self-sufficient for basic survival.

---

### Fundinul-Gabil / Frostforge (~1,200)

**Dwarven name:** Fundinul-Gabil ("Fire-Deep")  
**Common name:** Frostforge  
**Location:** Eastern mountains  
**Identity:** Geothermal forges, volcanic vents, exceptional metalwork

Fundinul-Gabil was founded on geothermal vents — volcanic heat rising through the mountains. The hold channels this heat to forges that burn hotter than surface fuel can achieve. This allows metalworking other holds cannot match.

**Forge-Master Thora Flameheart** (dwarf, 198, female) leads with focus on craft over tradition. Her position: the forges survive because the mountain survives. Surface catastrophe is irrelevant to deep fires.

**Products:** Exceptional weapons, armor, tools requiring extreme heat. Fundinul-Gabil trades finished metalwork for food and materials they can't produce underground.

**Relationship to surface:** Most open of three holds. Frostforge dwarves interact with surface communities more frequently. They've actually liked some of the humans. They've offered shelter more directly than the other holds.

## DRAKONA SETTLEMENTS (Eastern Territories)

Drakona are more mobile than humans or dwarves. Most evacuated early and fast.

### Scalecrest (~5,500, 80% Drakona)

**Location:** Eastern interior tundra  
**Identity:** Major Drakona city, cultural hub, staying despite impact threat

Scalecrest is THE Drakona cultural center — largest Drakona settlement on the continent. When impact projections were confirmed, the community debated evacuation extensively. Unlike human hearths, the Drakona made a collective cultural decision: stay and preserve their heritage.

**Wing-Elder Council** (five elders, ages 120-200) leads. Their position: Drakona have survived by adapting, not fleeing. They believe their architecture, flight capability, and cultural knowledge are worth protecting in place. If impact becomes truly imminent, they can evacuate by air in days.

**Architecture:** Built for Drakona scale and flight — tall structures, landing platforms, wide spaces. The city is vertical where human settlements are horizontal.

**Cultural preservation:** Scalecrest maintains Drakona history, art, oral traditions, flight techniques. Libraries, performance spaces, training grounds for young Drakona learning to fly.

**Current status:** Fully functional city. ~4,400 Drakona staying (80% of population), ~1,100 others (mostly humans and Giants who've integrated). The city operates normally while preparing evacuation plans if needed.

**Wing-Captain Zara Skyborn** (Drakona, 67, female) coordinates defense and evacuation planning. Her scouts monitor impact signs constantly. If the star's trajectory becomes certain, the city can evacuate by air in 3-4 days.

**The Drakona decision:** This is about cultural survival. If they abandon Scalecrest, they lose their largest repository of Drakona knowledge and tradition. They're betting they can evacuate fast enough when necessary. They might be right.

---

### Windspire (~900, completely evacuated)

**Location:** Eastern coastal  
**Identity:** Coastal Drakona community, evacuated completely

Windspire was built on coastal cliffs where wind conditions are ideal for Drakona flight. The entire community evacuated in one coordinated wave four months ago.

**Status:** Abandoned but maintained. Some scouts from Scalecrest visit periodically to check structures.

---

### Cliffhaven (~600 staying)

**Location:** Eastern cliffs overlooking approaches  
**Identity:** Scout outpost, observation position

Cliffhaven was always more outpost than settlement — a position for watching eastern approaches. The entire community is scouts and observers. They voted unanimously to stay.

**Scout-Leader Kael Windwatch** (Drakona, 89, male) coordinates observation operations. His scouts watch the sky (for the star), the ground (for signs of imminent impact), and the ancient ruins (for unexplained activity).

**Purpose:** Early warning. If something changes — the star's trajectory becomes certain, ruins activate, ground shows pre-impact signs — Cliffhaven scouts can fly to Northern Expanse in two days to warn everyone.

## DWARVEN-DRAKONA RELATIONSHIPS

Minimal but respectful. Dwarves live deep, Drakona live high. They rarely interact but when they do it's professional.

The dwarves think Drakona abandoning the region is premature panic. The Drakona think dwarves staying underground for potentially years is madness. Neither judges the other's choice directly — just mutually bewildered.

---

## REFUGEE OFFERS

**Dwarven:** All three holds have offered underground shelter if surface evacuation becomes impossible. Few surface dwellers accept — living underground for years is psychologically difficult for non-dwarves.

**Drakona:** Scalecrest scouts have offered to evacuate surface dwellers by air if evacuation routes get cut off. Limited capacity (can't carry supplies, just people), but faster than overland travel.
