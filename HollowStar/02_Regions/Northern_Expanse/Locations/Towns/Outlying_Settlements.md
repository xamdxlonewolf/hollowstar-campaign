# Outlying Settlements - Northern Expanse

The communities beyond the Skyspine River corridor — the settlements that make the Northern Expanse's vast territory feel inhabited. These are the dwarven holds in the northern mountains, the frozen harbor on the North Sea coast, the Giant villages in the far north, and the scattered communities filling the enormous space between the western and eastern borders.

---

## THE NORTHERN COAST (North Sea)

### Frostwharf / Kelgrim
**Population:** ~2,800  
**Location:** Northern coast of Northern Expanse, on the North Sea — approximately 150 miles north of the Skyspine River headwaters  
**Map Position:** Northern coastline, roughly center of Northern Expanse's northern border where it meets the sea  
**Names:** Frostwharf (common), Kelgrim (dwarven - "Ice harbor")  
**Identity:** The frozen harbor — hardy people making life work where most wouldn't survive

Frostwharf sits on a natural deep-water bay that freezes for 4-5 months per year. The town exists because the harbor provides North Sea access during the navigable season and because the people here are too stubborn to leave during winter.

**The population:** Mixed — humans (~40%), dwarves (~35%), Giants (~15%), others (~10%). You don't live in Frostwharf unless you can handle the cold and don't mind isolation. The population self-selects for hardiness.

**The economy:** 
- **Summer fishing** (North Sea fishing fleet, ~40 boats, operates 6-7 months)
- **Whale hunting** (dangerous, profitable, 3-4 crews)
- **Ice harvesting** (winter work — cut, store, ship south when harbor opens)
- **Trade hub** (summer only — ships from Frostwell coast and beyond stop here)

**Winter survival:** The town is built for it. Every structure has double walls, every family has 8+ months of stored food, and the community operates under winter protocols that have kept people alive for generations. When the harbor freezes, Frostwharf becomes isolated. They planned for it.

**The Thaw Festival:** When the harbor ice breaks (usually early spring), the entire town celebrates for three days. Ships arrive, trade resumes, isolation ends. The festival is partly celebration, partly relief that everyone survived another winter.

**Current role in refugee crisis:** Frostwharf receives almost no refugees (too remote, too harsh, refugees need immediate settlement not seasonal isolation). The town has sent food and supplies south when ships could carry them. Not heroes, just neighbors helping where they can.

**Notable figures:**
- **Harbor-Captain Thom Frostbeard** (dwarf, 156) — manages the harbor, coordinates the summer fleet, makes the calls about when ice is safe
- **Fleet-Master Sera Coldwater** (human, 52) — leads the fishing fleet, lost two boats to North Sea storms last season
- **Winter-Keeper Bren** (Giant, 67) — manages winter food stores, tracks consumption, ensures nobody starves during isolation

## NORTHWEST MOUNTAINS (Verdant Crescent Border)

### Ironpeak Hold
**Population:** ~1,600  
**Location:** Northwestern mountains of Northern Expanse, high peaks bordering Verdant Crescent  
**Map Position:** Western mountain range, approximately 100 miles west of Skyspine River and 120 miles north of Elarindë/Lakemere  
**Identity:** Dwarven mining hold, iron and silver, traditional clan structure

Ironpeak is carved into the mountain itself — three levels deep, extensive mine networks, forges that have been operating for 400 years. The hold produces iron, silver, and occasionally gems. Trade with both Northern Expanse (south and east) and Verdant Crescent (west through mountain passes).

**Clan structure:** Three major clans share the hold — Ironforge (miners), Silverhand (smiths), and Stonekeeper (hold administration/defense). The clans cooperate because the hold requires it. Disputes are settled by the Council of Three (one representative per clan).

**The mines:** Iron is primary export. The silver vein discovered 80 years ago made the hold wealthy. Gems are occasional bonus. The dwarves know exactly how much ore remains (centuries worth) and mine sustainably.

**Trade routes:** 
- **East to Skyspine River** towns (Farhold, Coldwater) — iron for grain/goods
- **West through passes to Verdant Crescent** — limited trade with forest edge settlements
- **South to capital** Elarindë/Lakemere — silver and quality metalwork

**Current situation:** The hold is stable, prosperous, and watching the refugee crisis with dwarven pragmatism. They've sent metal and tools south (payment for future trade credit). They're not taking refugees (the hold is full and dwarven clans don't integrate outsiders easily).

**Notable figures:**
- **Thane Brom Ironforge** (dwarf, 201) — Ironforge clan leader, speaks for miners
- **Master-Smith Kara Silverhand** (dwarf, 178) — Silverhand clan leader, best smith in three holds
- **Hold-Captain Drek Stonekeeper** (dwarf, 189) — Stonekeeper clan leader, manages hold defense

---

### Stormwatch
**Population:** ~400  
**Location:** Northwestern foothills below Ironpeak, on the approach from Verdant Crescent  
**Map Position:** Western foothills, approximately 80 miles west of Skyspine River, northwest of Elarindë  
**Identity:** Mixed human/dwarf trading post, gateway between regions

Stormwatch exists because Ironpeak needs a surface trading post and the mountain passes from Verdant Crescent need a waystation. The town is half human traders and half dwarven families connected to Ironpeak.

**Function:** Receives trade goods from both regions, coordinates deliveries to Ironpeak, manages the pass traffic. Small but essential for regional commerce.

**Notable figures:**
- **Factor Tess Broadacre** (human, 48) — runs the trading post, coordinates between human/dwarf communities

## NORTHEAST MOUNTAINS (Toward Central Heartlands)

### Deepdelve
**Population:** ~1,800  
**Location:** Northeastern mountains of Northern Expanse, high peaks toward Central Heartlands border  
**Map Position:** Eastern mountain range, approximately 130 miles northeast of Elarindë/Lakemere, 100 miles east of Skyspine River  
**Identity:** Dwarven mining hold, copper and coal, more isolated than Ironpeak

Deepdelve is deeper than Ironpeak — five levels carved into the mountain, older excavations, more conservative clan culture. The hold mines copper (primary export) and coal (secondary). Less wealthy than Ironpeak but more traditional.

**Clan structure:** Two major clans — Deepdelve (miners/administrators, founded the hold) and Coalkeeper (specialized in coal mining/processing). Single clan dominance makes politics simpler and more rigid.

**Trade routes:**
- **Southwest to Skyspine River** towns — copper for food/goods
- **South to capital** — copper and coal
- **East toward Central Heartlands** — limited trade through difficult passes

**Current situation:** Even more isolated than Ironpeak. The refugee crisis barely touches them. They're sending some copper south (discounted, considered their contribution) but otherwise focused on hold business.

**Notable figures:**
- **High Thane Morek Deepdelve** (dwarf, 243) — holds founder's line, traditional leadership
- **Mine-Master Thom Coalkeeper** (dwarf, 167) — manages coal operations, younger generation, slightly more open to outside contact

---

### Highstone
**Population:** ~600  
**Location:** Northeastern mountains, lower elevation than Deepdelve  
**Map Position:** Eastern mountains, approximately 110 miles northeast of Elarindë, 80 miles east of Skyspine River  
**Identity:** Human/Giant mountain community, lumber and stone

Highstone is the rare mountain community that isn't dwarven. Founded by humans and Giants three generations ago around lumber operations (high-altitude hardwood) and stone quarrying. The community trades with both Deepdelve and river towns.

**Mixed population:** Humans (~60%), Giants (~30%), scattered dwarves (~10%). The Giants provide the physical strength for heavy lumber/stone work. Humans manage trade. Dwarves maintain tools and equipment.

**Current role:** Has absorbed ~80 refugees (mostly human) into lumber operations. The work is hard but available. Integration is working because the community is practical and the labor shortage was real.

**Notable figures:**
- **Headman Bren Highstone** (Giant, 41) — community leader, coordinates lumber operations
- **Trade-Factor Mara Cole** (human, 52) — manages trade with river towns and Deepdelve

## FAR NORTHERN PLAINS (Between Mountains and Forest)

### Longstride
**Population:** ~1,400  
**Location:** Northern plains of Northern Expanse, between northern mountains and northern edge of forest zone  
**Map Position:** Central-north region, approximately 100 miles north of Elarindë/Lakemere, 60 miles north of northern forest edge  
**Identity:** Giant-majority community, herding and hunting

Longstride is a Giant village — one of several scattered across the northern plains. The population is ~70% Giant, ~20% human, ~10% other. The community is semi-nomadic (seasonal movement following herds) but maintains a permanent settlement core.

**The economy:**
- **Herding** (large cattle-equivalent herds, move seasonally)
- **Hunting** (northern plains game)
- **Trade** (hides, meat, bone to southern towns in exchange for grain, metal, goods they don't produce)

**Community structure:** Loosely organized around extended family groups. The community Elder speaks for the village in external matters but internally governance is family-based.

**Current role:** Longstride has absorbed ~100 refugees (mostly Giants from Frostwell). Giant-to-Giant integration has been smoother than expected — shared ancestry, practical skills match, and the herding economy can expand to accommodate more workers.

**Notable figures:**
- **Elder Kess Longstride** (Giant, 81) — village elder, speaks for community
- **Herd-Master Thom Broadhorn** (Giant, 45) — manages the largest herd, employs ~40 people including refugees

---

### Northwatch
**Population:** ~800  
**Location:** Northern plains, slightly east of Longstride  
**Map Position:** North-central plains, approximately 95 miles north of Elarindë, 70 miles east of Skyspine River  
**Identity:** Mixed human/Giant farming community

Northwatch sits at the northern edge of viable farming territory. The growing season is short but sufficient. The community produces hardy crops (root vegetables, short-season grains) and trades with Longstride (crops for meat/hides).

**Population split:** Humans (~55%), Giants (~35%), others (~10%). The humans farm, the Giants provide labor for heavy work, the community functions through mutual necessity.

**Current role:** Limited refugee absorption (~40 people). The farming is marginal and labor-intensive but can't expand easily. The community is helping but at capacity.

**Notable figures:**
- **Council-Head Mara Northwatch** (human, 58) — community leader, manages inter-species cooperation

---

### The Wandering Camps
**Various locations across northern plains**  
**Population:** ~600 total across multiple groups  
**Identity:** Nomadic Giant family groups, fully mobile

Not all northern Giants settled in permanent villages. Several family groups maintain traditional nomadic patterns — following herds, hunting, never staying in one place more than a season. They trade with settled communities but remain independent.

**Current role:** Some nomadic groups have taken in refugee families (Giants can travel with them, learn the herding life). It's working for small numbers. The groups remain mobile and independent.

## WESTERN SETTLEMENTS (Toward Verdant Crescent)

### Westmarch
**Population:** ~1,200  
**Location:** Western Northern Expanse, between Skyspine River and western mountains  
**Map Position:** Western plains, approximately 50 miles west of Skyspine River, 80 miles northwest of Elarindë  
**Identity:** Farming community, cultural bridge to Verdant Crescent

Westmarch sits in the western grassland approaching the mountains and forests of Verdant Crescent. The community is primarily human with some elvish families (connections to Verdant Crescent elves). Mixed culture — Northern Expanse governance, Verdant Crescent trade and cultural influence.

**The economy:** Farming (grain, livestock), trade with both Verdant Crescent edge settlements and Skyspine River towns. The location makes them natural intermediaries.

**Current role:** Moderate refugee absorption (~120 people). The community has space and farming labor needs. Integration is slower than Millhaven but working.

**Notable figures:**
- **Mayor Tess Westwood** (human, 51) — manages community and the cultural balancing act
- **Elder Soren Greenleaf** (elf, 198) — represents elvish families, maintains Verdant Crescent connections

---

### Thornhedge
**Population:** ~600  
**Location:** Western edge of Northern Expanse, approaching forest transition to Verdant Crescent  
**Map Position:** Far western region, approximately 90 miles west of Skyspine River, near Verdant Crescent border  
**Identity:** Mixed human/elf logging community

Thornhedge operates at the forest edge where Northern Expanse grassland meets Verdant Crescent forest influence. The community logs the transition forests (not deep Verdant Crescent — Green Covenant wouldn't allow it) and trades timber to both regions.

**Population:** Humans (~50%), Elves (~40%), others (~10%). The mixed population is essential — elves understand forest sustainability, humans understand markets.

**Current role:** Very limited refugee absorption (~30 people). The community is small and specialized. They're sending timber south (building material for refugee housing) instead.

**Notable figures:**
- **Timber-Master Kael Thornhedge** (human, 48) — manages logging operations
- **Forest-Keeper Sera Greenwood** (elf, 176) — ensures sustainable practices, maintains relationship with Verdant Crescent

---

## EASTERN SETTLEMENTS (Toward Central Heartlands)

### Eastmarch
**Population:** ~1,100  
**Location:** Eastern Northern Expanse, between Skyspine River and eastern mountains  
**Map Position:** Eastern plains, approximately 60 miles east of Skyspine River, 70 miles northeast of Elarindë  
**Identity:** Mixed farming/herding community, trade gateway to Central Heartlands

Eastmarch sits on the eastern grassland approaching the mountains and eventual passes toward Central Heartlands. The community farms, herds, and trades with both Skyspine River towns and (via difficult passes) some Central Heartlands northern settlements.

**The economy:** Farming, herding, trade coordination. The eastern passes are less traveled than southern routes but Eastmarch makes them work.

**Current role:** Moderate refugee absorption (~90 people). The community has work available and space for expansion. Integration is functional.

**Notable figures:**
- **Headman Bren Eastwood** (human, 54) — community leader, manages trade routes
- **Pass-Guide Venn** (human, 38) — knows the eastern passes, coordinates with Central Heartlands

---

### Riverbend
**Population:** ~900  
**Location:** Eastern Northern Expanse, on a tributary that flows into Skyspine River  
**Map Position:** East-central region, approximately 45 miles east of Skyspine River, 85 miles north of Elarindë  
**Identity:** Fishing and farming village on tributary river

Riverbend sits on a tributary of the Skyspine — not the main river but a significant waterway. The community fishes the tributary, farms the surrounding land, and trades with Skyspine River towns via water transport.

**Current role:** Limited refugee absorption (~50 people). Small community, limited expansion capacity. They're contributing what they can.

**Notable figures:**
- **Village Elder Mara Riverbend** (halfling, 69) — community leader, practical governance

---

## SCATTERED HOMESTEADS & SMALL COMMUNITIES

Throughout the Northern Expanse's vast territory, hundreds of small homesteads, family farms, and tiny communities (50-200 people) exist independently. These account for an additional ~3,000 people scattered across the region.

**Characteristics:**
- Self-sufficient farming/herding
- Minimal contact with larger towns except for trade
- Generally human or Giant families
- Some absorbing 1-5 refugees (family scale)
- Make the region feel inhabited even in remote areas
