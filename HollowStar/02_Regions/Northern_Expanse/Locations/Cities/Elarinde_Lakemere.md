# Elarindë / Lakemere

**Population:** ~14,000 | **Region:** [[02_Regions/Northern_Expanse/Northern_Expanse_Index|Northern Expanse]]  
**Elven name:** Elarindë — "Mirror of still water" | **Common name:** Lakemere  
**Government:** Monarchy — King Elowen Arathas

---

## THE CITY

Built at the forest's edge where it meets Caranmírë / Redwater Lake — half in the trees, half on the water. Three centuries of construction have layered elvish architecture (grown, curved, integrated with living wood) beneath and around practical human additions. The result is a city that looks like it grew rather than was built.

**Districts:**

**The Silverwood Palace:** King Elowen's residence — grown from living trees over centuries. Not fortified (deliberate — the king is not afraid). Gardens more extensive than the building itself.

**The Leafward Quarter:** Elvish residential, oldest part of the city. Buildings grown from living trees. Quiet, unhurried. Outsiders sometimes feel unwelcome — not hostility, just a different pace.

**The Common Bank:** Human, Giant, Halfling residential. Practical timber construction. Markets, inns, craftworks. The Refugee Processing Hall — recently established, currently very busy.

**The Lakeshore:** Docks, fishing boats, market. Most diverse district. Where common and elvish names are heard together most often.

**The Aerie:** Built into the Arathandë cliffs above the city — a complex of platforms, nests, and training grounds accessible by carved switchback path from Elarindë. Home of the Great Eagle Rider tradition. The only place in the city that feels genuinely military. Currently under increased activity as Rider Commander Sera Windmane pushes for formal authorization to support the Frostwell evacuation.

---

## THE LAKE

Caranmírë / Redwater Lake — named for the red-gold reflection at dawn when the forest canopy filters the light. Deep, clear, cold. The Nendalië flows into it from the north (bringing goods and refugees) and exits south as the Arathandë/Skyspine. Fishing boats, trading barges, and the king's ceremonial vessels share it.

---

## KING ELOWEN ARATHAS

**Age:** 134 (elven) | **Reign:** 12 years

Elowen inherited the throne young by elven standards when his mother died unexpectedly. He is thoughtful, careful, deeply aware that his decisions matter to lives he will never meet. The refugee crisis is testing him.

**The Warden vacancy:** He has left the Warden of the North position vacant for two years while he decides between three candidates. This is careful deliberation to some, paralysis to others.

**His struggle:** Every decision has a human cost. Opening the passes fully means overwhelming towns that can't absorb more. Closing them means people die in the mountains. Managing the flow carefully means bureaucracy while people suffer. There is no good answer and he knows it.

---

## THE REFUGEE SITUATION

60-80 new arrivals per week, increasing. Crown-funded resettlement. Mostly working in southern towns — Aranthem/Millhaven's farm labor shortage has absorbed many. Strain beginning in Lakemere itself: housing shortage, wage competition in the Common Bank district.

**Processing:** The Refugee Processing Hall coordinates skills assessment, temporary housing, and placement. Understaffed, overwhelmed, functional but barely.

---

## ELDER VALANDIL

An 800-year-old elf living in the Deepwood who has seen a Hollostar fall before. He has been quietly preparing an ancient cavern network beneath the Deepwood to shelter thousands for up to 40 years. He has not told anyone — not the king, not the Council, not even other elders. He is waiting to see if it becomes necessary.

**The caverns:** Old, deep, forgotten by most. Valandil has been stocking them with preserved food, water systems, growing chambers. Could shelter 3,000-5,000 for decades if needed.

**His calculation:** Tell people now and cause panic, or wait and risk being too late? He has chosen waiting. He might be wrong.

---

## THE THREE WARDEN CANDIDATES

**Commander Yara Stonefield** — Frontier-born, would close the passes. Practical. Popular in frontier towns. Feared in Lakemere.

**Advisor Theron Mael** — Elf, court-aligned, would enforce open borders as policy. Popular in Lakemere. Hated in frontier towns.

**Captain Brek Coldmarch** — Giant, Nine Hearths refugee. Walked through Silver Pass four months ago with nothing. Trusted by both refugees and locals. Doesn't want the job. Is the right person for it.

**Elowen's delay:** He knows Coldmarch is right. He also knows forcing the position on someone who doesn't want it could break them. He's still deciding.

---

## CURRENT TENSIONS

**Capital vs. Frontier:** Lakemere sees the crisis as manageable with proper organization. Frontier towns see it as overwhelming their capacity. Both are correct from their perspectives.

**Economic stress:** Housing costs rising, wages under pressure, tensions between refugee labor and local workers. Not crisis yet, but trending wrong.

**The king's reputation:** Some see Elowen as thoughtful and careful. Others see paralysis. The vacant Warden position has become symbol of this division.
