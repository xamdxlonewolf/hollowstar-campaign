# Geography - The Salt Shore

**Climate:** Coastal maritime, moderate temperatures, storms frequent, salt air constant  
**Terrain:** Rocky mainland coast, island chains (Kept Islands, Drift Chain), natural harbors, rough seas  
**Population:** ~60,000 (mainland coast and islands)  
**Identity:** Fishing, maritime trade, contested authority under Tide King Voss

---

## HISTORICAL NOTE

**Before the Third Hollostar (700+ years ago):** The Salt Shore was scattered fishing villages on barren coast backed by vast desert. The Shardfall Basin interior was true desert - uninhabitable, waterless, dead.

**The Transformation (700 years ago):** The third Hollostar fell, creating the Endless Eye. Over decades, rivers flowed from the impact lake toward the coast. The desert transformed. River valleys became fertile. Population exploded as previously uninhabitable lands became prosperous.

**The Modern Shore (last 300 years):** What was barren coast backed by desert became thriving maritime region with river trade connecting interior to sea. The Salt Shore exists in its current form because the Hollostar impact created the water systems that made it viable.

---

## THE COASTLINE

The Salt Shore runs along the northern and western edges of the Shardfall Basin - rocky coast dotted with natural harbors, fishing towns, and the occasional larger port.

**Character:** Working maritime culture. Fishing dominates economy. Salt extraction operations at salt flats. Trade flowing through natural harbors connects basin interior to coastal shipping routes.

**Gulf of Tides:** Northern waters around Keeper's Bay - protected waters, excellent fishing, major shipping lanes. The gulf's name comes from dramatic tidal variations that expose vast flats at low tide.

---

## THE ISLANDS

**Kept Islands:** Inner island chain where Lantern's Rest (Nine-Lanterns headquarters) sits. Called "kept" because they're kept by whoever's strong enough - currently the pirates.

**Drift Chain:** Outer island chain including Wrecker's Rest and other fishing islands. Called "drift" because currents drift wreckage onto island shores - salvage operations common.

**Character:** Island communities operate semi-independently. Fishing, salvage, and in Lantern's Rest's case, piracy. Mainland authority (Voss) extends to islands in theory, contested in practice.

---

## MAJOR SETTLEMENTS

**Keeper's Bay** (~35,000) - Northern coast, shipbuilding capital, largest city, operates independently despite Voss's claims

**Brinehaven** (~11,000) - Central coast, Tide King Voss's seat, merchant hub, political center

**Island communities:** Lantern's Rest (pirate haven), Wrecker's Rest (salvagers), numerous fishing villages

**Coastal towns:** Tidewrack, Driftmere, Saltholm, Tidepost, Gullhaven, Shallowmere, Reaper's Bay, Aldric's Landing - fishing and trade towns of varying Voss loyalty

---

## THE POLITICAL SITUATION

**Tide King Voss** claims authority over entire Salt Shore but only truly controls central coast around Brinehaven. Northern towns (especially Keeper's Bay) operate independently. Southern towns are divided - some loyal, some resistant, some bought by competing interests.

**The consolidation:** Voss is attempting to unify Salt Shore under his authority. Success is mixed. He uses combination of force, economic pressure, and strategic alliances.

**The Nine-Lanterns:** Criminal fleet based in Lantern's Rest. Voss tolerates them because they pay him percentage and don't prey on his towns. Mutually beneficial arrangement nobody discusses openly.

---

## ECONOMY

**Fishing:** Primary industry. Rich coastal waters provide abundant catches. Every town operates fishing fleet of some size.

**Salt extraction:** Salt flats north of Brinehaven produce high-quality sea salt extracted through evaporation. Tidepost coordinates operations.

**Shipbuilding:** Keeper's Bay dominates - second only to L'Empire Verdienne's operations. Builds merchant vessels, fishing boats, occasional military ships.

**Maritime trade:** Natural harbors connect basin interior (Starfall Basin settlements) to coastal shipping. Goods flow through Salt Shore ports to destinations across the continent.

**Salvage:** Wrecker's Rest and other outer islands profit from storm-driven shipwrecks. Line between rescue and opportunistic scavenging is blurry.

---

## WATERS & WEATHER

**The Gulf of Tides:** Northern waters, dramatic tidal variations, protected from worst storms, excellent fishing

**Outer waters:** Rougher seas beyond island chains, dangerous in storms, primary shipping lanes

**Storms:** Frequent, especially autumn and winter. Coastal communities build for weather. Islanders know when storms approach - they survive by attention to signs.

**Currents:** Complex coastal currents make navigation tricky. Local knowledge essential. Pilots who know these waters are valuable.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO REGIONS

**Starfall Basin interior:** Salt Shore ports connect basin settlements to coastal trade. Goods from Endless Eye flow through here.

**Silverbelt Coast:** Southern neighbor, technically separate region but waters connect. Contested boundary around Shallowmere area.

**Central Heartlands:** Eastern neighbor via Keeper's Bay river connections. Skyspine River provides trade route inland.

---

## CURRENT TENSIONS

**Voss's consolidation:** Towns are choosing sides or trying to stay neutral. Economic pressure, political maneuvering, occasional violence. The region is fragmenting rather than unifying.

**House Dorian (Saltmere):** Competing power from Silverbelt trying to buy Salt Shore towns with economic deals. Some towns have taken offers. Voss is not pleased.

**Keeper's Bay independence:** The shipbuilding capital operates independently despite Voss's claims. Too large and economically important to force into submission.

**Nine-Lanterns tolerance:** How long will Voss tolerate pirates based in his claimed territory? As long as they pay him. But the arrangement could break if either side changes calculation.

## THE CARVED COAST

The Salt Shore's most distinctive geographic feature is not its harbors or its islands but its ancient stone formations: sea arches, isolated stacks, and cliff faces along the central and southern coast that carry carvings nobody made.

### The Formations

Dozens of arches and sea stacks rise from the water along the central Salt Shore coast, carved by millennia of wave action into shapes that are unusual enough to stop outsiders cold. What makes them remarkable is not the shapes but what is on them.

Every major formation carries carved symbols. Not weathered decoration or navigational marks — deep, precise, geometrically consistent carvings cut into stone that has otherwise been worn smooth by ten thousand years of salt water. The carvings should not still be readable. They are.

**The symbols** are almost — but not quite — the same as those found in the Frostwell ruins. Similar enough that a scholar who had seen both would feel the connection immediately. Different enough that they are clearly not the same hand, the same language, or the same era. They suggest a shared origin, something both symbol systems are descended from, that predates either. Nobody has connected this yet. The Somnarchs, who have catalogued the Frostwell carvings extensively, have no record of the Salt Shore formations.

**The largest formation** — a double arch locals call the Saltgate — sits three miles south of Brinehaven, visible from the harbor on clear days. Its inner face is covered almost entirely in carvings. Fishermen pass it daily. They don't think about it much. It has always been there.

### The Older Sea

People who arrive at the Salt Shore from elsewhere — from the Central Heartlands, the Silverbelt, the Starfall Basin — often remark on something they cannot name. The sea here feels different. Not in weather or color or temperature. In quality. Something about the water at the Salt Shore gives the impression of great age, of depth beyond depth, of a sea that was here before everything else and will be here after.

Salt Shore locals find this observation puzzling. The sea is the sea. It has always felt like this. They grew up with it. They are faintly proud of it, the way people are proud of things they didn't earn and don't fully understand.

The feeling is strongest near the carved formations and on still nights when the tide is going out.

**Nobody knows what causes it.** Path of Spirits practitioners who have visited believe the carvings are still doing something — that whatever they were made to do is ongoing, slow, and too old to have a name. Starfall Seekers who have documented the formations note that the symbols show no weathering whatsoever, which should be impossible. The Somnarchs, if they knew about the connection to Frostwell, would have a great deal to say. They don't know.

### What Voss Knows

The Saltgate arch is why Voss made Brinehaven his seat rather than the larger, more prosperous Keeper's Bay.

He found the inner carvings twenty-two years ago, diving off a boat he'd borrowed after a storm. He had been in the water before but never that deep, never that close to the arch's inner face. What he found was not just carvings. At the arch's base, half-buried in sand and kept dry by the arch's overhang, was a small chamber — not natural, deliberately cut — containing objects he has never described to anyone.

He does not know what the objects are. He knows that one of them belonged to a woman named Cala, who died in these waters seven years before he found the chamber, and whose death is the reason he discarded his family name and came to the sea in the first place. How her belongings ended up in a chamber that predates any civilization he knows of is a question he has spent twenty years not answering.

He has never told anyone about the chamber. Not Sela. Not The Ledger. Not his most trusted fleet captain. He visits it alone, twice a year, at low tide, when the Saltgate is briefly accessible from below.

He protects the formations from exploitation, excavation, and outside attention not because he understands what they are but because Cala died here and this is the only memorial that exists for her. If the formations are ever destroyed or exposed to scholarly extraction, he will respond in the way the Tide King responds to things that threaten what matters to him. Quietly. Completely.

**What the chamber contains** (GM layer): Objects that match the Frostwell ruins in construction and material. A map that doesn't match current coastlines but is clearly the Salt Shore. And something that responds, very faintly, to proximity — warmth, or a sound below hearing, or something with no good name. It has been responding more strongly in the past two years. Voss has noticed. He doesn't know what it means.
