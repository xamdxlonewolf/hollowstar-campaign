# Culture and Society - The Salt Shore

---

## CORE VALUES

**Competence over credentials**
Nobody cares where you're from or who your family is. Can you sail? Can you fish? Can you fix a net at 3am in the rain? That's what matters.

**The cut is sacred**
Paying Voss's cut isn't just compliance — it's the social contract. People who don't pay aren't just cheating Voss. They're cheating the whole system that keeps the Shore working. More social pressure around the cut than around actual laws.

**The sea gives and takes**
Fatalism that isn't resignation. Bad things happen at sea. Good things happen too. Neither is deserved. You prepare as well as you can and accept what comes. Complaining is considered weak — not because feelings don't matter, but because complaining to the sea doesn't help.

**Loyalty to people, not abstractions**
Salt Shore folk don't get passionate about politics, nations, or movements. They get passionate about their crew, their town, their family. Asking someone to betray their captain for a principle is asking the wrong thing entirely.

## DAILY LIFE

**Before dawn:** Fishing fleets depart — biggest social moment of the day (everyone sees each other off)

**Morning:** Processing catch, boat maintenance, market

**Afternoon:** Rest (heat), repairs, children's work (everyone works)

**Evening:** The real social time — communal meals, The Anchor equivalent in every town, stories

**Night:** Watch rotations, late arrivals, stars for navigation study

**Weather rhythms:**
- Storm season (late summer): Everyone tightens up, prepares, helps neighbors
- Calm season (spring): Expansion, long voyages, island exploration
- The dead weeks (deep winter): Quiet, internal, community bonding time

## ISLAND CULTURE VS MAINLAND CULTURE

**Mainland towns:**
- More stable community identity
- Know their neighbors for generations
- Voss's system well-understood, mostly comfortable
- Suspicious of outsiders initially, warm once trust is built

**Islands:**
- Transient population = no assumptions about anyone
- Everyone treated as capable until proven otherwise (can't afford to assume otherwise)
- Information moves fast (small populations, nothing stays secret)
- More religiously diverse (every faith washes up, none dominates)
- Self-reliant by necessity (can't always wait for mainland help)

## ANCESTRIES & HOW THEY FIT

**Humans:** Majority on mainland, present everywhere

**Katari:** Strong presence in Lowport and islands — the desert-coast transition makes them natural Salt Shore settlers. Their route-knowledge translates from desert to sea surprisingly well.

**Halflings:** Concentrated in Tidewrack and Greenholm — farming, fishing, generational community builders

**Ribbet:** Dock workers, harbor pilots, fish market specialists. Not as numerous as near [[02_Regions/Starfall_Basin/Locations/Cities/The_Endless_Eye|The Endless Eye]] but well-integrated.

**Goblins:** Repair yards (Twin Teeth, Salthook) — practical engineering culture fits maritime needs perfectly

**Faun and Firbolg:** Less common, usually island-dwellers. Drawn to the wilder outer islands.

**Mixed everything:** Especially on The Scatter — Salt Shore is one of the few places where mixed-ancestry families face minimal social friction. Too practical for prejudice.

## RELIGION

**No dominant faith** — typical for maritime cultures

**[[Six-Fold Faith]]:** Present, the Wanderer aspect popular (travel, change)

**[[Path of Spirits]]:** Significant — sea spirits, storm spirits, island spirits. Salt Shore fishermen leave small offerings before long voyages. Not formal religion — just practical respect.

**[[The First Flame]]:** Minority, growing. The Guiding Light denomination (contemplative) suits long sea voyages — sailors have time to think.

**The Tide King's blessing:** Not a religion, but Voss's token-necklace worn as lucky charm by some fishermen. He hates this and has said so publicly multiple times. It has not stopped.

## RELATIONSHIP WITH SALTMERE

**Complicated and getting more so.**

Saltmere offers real things: trade legitimacy, The Block's auction access, legal protection in other regions. Merchants who deal with Saltmere get better prices on some goods.

But the Salt Shore's independence — including Voss's cut system — is what makes it functional. Saltmere's Covenant taking a cut on top of Voss's cut, plus formalizing trade in ways that favor The Block, would squeeze ordinary fishermen significantly.

**The generational divide:**
- Older residents: Built something here, protective of it
- Younger merchants: See Saltmere's reach as opportunity
- Fishermen (all ages): Don't care about politics, care about whether they can afford their boats

**The Driftmere situation** is being watched closely. If Driftmere formally joins the Saltmere trade network and does *well*, other towns will reconsider.

## LAWS & JUSTICE

**Voss's rules** (see [[04_Factions/Salt_Shore/The Tide King|The Tide King]]) function as regional law

**Town-level:**
- Each town handles minor disputes internally
- Serious disputes escalate to Voss (via The Anchor or his representatives)
- No formal courts, no written code — precedent and reputation

**What counts as serious:**
- Violence between residents
- Disputed salvage (Ledger handles these)
- Accusations of cheating the cut

**Punishment:**
- Minor: Fines (to injured party, not to Voss)
- Serious: Exile from Salt Shore
- The three absolute rule violations: Death or permanent exile (Voss decides which)
