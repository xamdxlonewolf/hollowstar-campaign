# Brinehaven

**Population:** ~11,000  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Salt_Shore/Salt_Shore_Index|The Salt Shore]]  
**Location:** Central Salt Shore coast, natural harbor  
**Government:** Tide King Voss (informal authority, operates through reputation and economic power)  
**Economy:** Fishing, merchant trade, salt extraction coordination, smuggling (unofficial)

---

## OVERVIEW

Brinehaven is the Tide King's seat — the port city where Voss built his power and maintains his headquarters. It's not a capital in the traditional sense (Voss has no formal government), but it's where his protection system is strongest, where his flagship The Greywater moors, and where the Shore Eyes network coordinates.

**Character:** Working port city, pragmatic, salt-weathered. People here know Voss personally. They saw him rise from fishing boat captain to regional power. They respect him because his system works — their boats come home, their catches aren't stolen, and pirates know better than to raid here.

**The reality:** Brinehaven is only city on Salt Shore where Voss's authority is complete and uncontested. Move 20 miles in any direction and that authority becomes negotiable.

---

## HISTORY

### Before Voss (Pre-20 Years Ago)

Brinehaven was decent-sized fishing port — larger than most Salt Shore settlements but not exceptional. Population ~8,000, economy based on fishing and local trade. Pirates were constant problem. No unified law, just local watch trying their best.

**The name:** "Brine" (salt water) + "haven" (safe harbor). Ironic, since harbor wasn't particularly safe until Voss made it so.

### Voss's Rise (20-10 Years Ago)

Voss started his protection system here. First just his own fishing boats, then neighboring crews, eventually most of Brinehaven's fleet. Within 5 years, the port was under his protection.

**What changed:** Pirate raids stopped. Fishing boats returned with their catches intact. Merchants could operate without being robbed. The percentage system seemed fair — 5% of catch for actual security.

**Population growth:** As Voss's reputation spread, more fishermen migrated to Brinehaven. Safer harbor meant better business. Population climbed from 8,000 to 11,000 over 15 years.

### Consolidation Era (10 Years Ago to Present)

As Voss expanded beyond Brinehaven, the city became his base of operations. The Greywater moors here. Shore Eyes network coordinates from the harbor. Key allies live here. It's the center of his growing maritime power.

**Current status:** Most loyal, most secure, most prosperous Salt Shore city. Also most watched — enemies know attacking Brinehaven means direct confrontation with Voss.

## GEOGRAPHY & LAYOUT

**Natural harbor:** Deep, protected by rocky breakwater. Can handle 30-40 vessels simultaneously. The harbor is Brinehaven's defining feature — everything else grew around it.

**The city structure:** Built up hillside from harbor in three terraced levels:
- **Harborside** (lower): Docks, warehouses, fish markets, working waterfront
- **Merchant Quarter** (middle): Shops, homes, businesses, town center
- **Highside** (upper): Better homes, Voss's house, harbormaster's office, overlooks harbor

---

## DISTRICTS

### Harborside (Lower City, ~4,000 residents/workers)

**Character:** Salt-weathered, work-focused, constantly moving

The working waterfront. Docks extend into harbor, warehouses line shore, fish markets operate dawn to dusk. This is where Brinehaven's economy actually happens.

**Key locations:**
- **The Docks** - 8 major piers, dozens of moorings, constant boat traffic
- **Fish Market** - Daily auction, catches sold at dawn, processing through afternoon
- **The Net Menders' Quarter** - Where fishermen live, repair equipment, prepare for next voyage
- **Warehouse Row** - Storage facilities for catches, trade goods, supplies

**The Inner Harbor:** Where The Greywater moors when Voss is in port. Secure section with limited access. Voss's patrol boats dock here too.

**Culture:** Hard-working, practical, early hours. Fishermen wake before dawn, markets operate by sunrise, work continues until catch is processed. By evening, waterfront is drinking, eating, resting for next day.

**Power dynamics:** This is Voss's stronghold. Everyone here knows him. Most have benefited from his protection directly. Loyalty is genuine, not enforced.

### Merchant Quarter (Middle City, ~5,000 residents)

**Character:** Commercial, busy, where land meets sea

The transition zone between working waterfront and residential areas. Shops, businesses, inns, taverns, homes of merchants and craftspeople.

**Key locations:**
- **Market Square** - Central commerce hub, non-fish goods traded
- **The Crossroads Tavern** - Where merchants, captains, and Voss's people meet "casually"
- **Harbor Street** - Main thoroughfare from docks to upper city
- **Guild Houses** - Fishermen's Guild, Merchants' Association (Shore Eyes fronts)

**The Harbormaster's Office:** Technically manages port operations. Actually, Harbormaster Liss coordinates Shore Eyes network, tracks payments, identifies problems, and runs day-to-day administration while Voss handles strategy.

**Harbormaster Liss** (Ribbet, 44, female):
- Manages port officially, coordinates Shore Eyes unofficially
- The person who actually runs things day-to-day
- Voss trusts her completely (she's earned it over 12 years)
- Currently troubled by Shore Eyes leak — blames herself for missing Saltmere infiltration

**Culture:** More diverse than Harborside. Merchants from across Salt Shore and beyond. River traders bringing inland goods. Visiting captains. The Quarter is where outsiders interface with Brinehaven's economy.

**Notable businesses:**
- **The Saltwright's Supply** - Ship provisions, fishing equipment (Shore Eyes front, gathers intelligence from customer conversations)
- **Coral & Compass Trading** - Import/export company (legitimate, but owner reports to Liss)
- **The Mended Net** - Tavern/inn frequented by Voss's patrol captains

### Highside (Upper City, ~2,000 residents)

**Character:** Quieter, residential, overlooks harbor

The better neighborhoods. Not wealthy by Keeper's Bay standards, but comfortable. People who prospered from Brinehaven's security live here.

**Voss's House:** Large, practical building with good harbor view. Not palace, not fortress — just well-built house with meeting rooms, private quarters, and space for his inner circle to gather. The house is deliberately unpretentious. Voss lives like successful captain, not king.

**Architecture:** Stone and timber construction, weathered but well-maintained. Most homes have small gardens, harbor views. Streets are cleaner, quieter than lower city.

**The Watch House:** Small garrison for town watch. They handle local crime (rare), disputes (common), and drunk fishermen (frequent). Serious threats are Voss's patrol boats' responsibility.

**Culture:** Families of successful merchants, master fishermen, craftspeople. Children play in streets. Gardens grow vegetables. Life is comfortable but not ostentatious.

## ECONOMY

**Fishing:** Foundation of everything. Brinehaven's fleet (~200 boats) brings in daily catches. The harbor facilitates processing, preserving, distribution.

**Trade:** Merchants use Brinehaven as hub. Coastal goods flow through here. Inland traders from Saltmere and basin interior come here for fish, salt, maritime products.

**Salt coordination:** While actual salt extraction happens at flats near Tidepost, Brinehaven coordinates distribution. Salt merchants operate from here.

**The percentage system:** Every economic activity pays Voss's percentage. Fishing boats (5% of catch), merchants (percentage of goods), even smugglers (they pay too, though nobody admits it). This funding flows through Harbormaster Liss's office, pays for patrol boats, Shore Eyes, Voss's operations.

**Smuggling (unofficial but real):** Brinehaven facilitates discreet cargo movement. Not competing with legitimate trade — complementing it. Voss takes cut, maintains plausible deniability, everyone prospers.

## CULTURE & DAILY LIFE

### Values

**Practical loyalty:** People support Voss because his system works, not because of ideology or fear.

**Self-reliance:** Fishing culture — you solve your own problems, help neighbors when they need it, don't expect others to do your work.

**Sea respect:** Ocean provides livelihood and kills people. Treat it with respect, prepare properly, accept that some boats don't come home.

**Community bonds:** Small enough that everyone knows everyone. Reputation matters. Breaking trust means social exile.

### Daily Rhythm

**Dawn:** Fishermen launch boats, fish markets prepare, harbor comes alive

**Morning:** Catches return, auction happens, processing begins

**Afternoon:** Markets operate, merchants trade, maintenance work on boats and equipment

**Evening:** Work ends, taverns fill, families gather, stories shared

**Night:** Harbor quiets (except for night fishing operations), patrol boats visible with lanterns

### Food & Drink

**Seafood:** Obviously. Fresh fish, shellfish, seaweed dishes. Prepared dozens of ways but always featuring day's catch.

**Salt-preserved goods:** Traditional preservation for long-term storage. Salt fish, salt pork, pickled vegetables.

**Local brew:** Brinehaven Dark — bitter ale brewed with seaweed (acquired taste, locals love it, visitors tolerate it).

**The Crossroads Tavern specialty:** "Captain's Stew" — whatever the cook got cheap that day thrown in pot with salt, herbs, served with hard bread. Actually good despite questionable description.

### Festivals

**Tide Turning (spring):** Celebrate fishing season start, bless boats, honor those lost to sea previous year.

**Salt Moon (summer):** Celebrate salt harvest, traditional dancing, excessive drinking.

**Storm's End (autumn):** Celebrate surviving hurricane season, final major festival before winter.

## VOSS'S PRESENCE

**When he's in port:** The Greywater is visible in inner harbor. Voss walks docks, talks to fishermen, meets with captains. He's accessible but busy. People respect his time but know they can approach him if needed.

**When he's away:** System runs without him. Liss handles administration, patrol boats maintain coverage, Shore Eyes continue operations. His absence is normal — he's often at sea or visiting other ports.

**The house:** Voss uses it for private meetings, strategy sessions with inner circle, occasional hosting of important visitors (like merchant princes or foreign diplomats). Not throne room — just place where decisions happen.

**His reputation here:** Respected, trusted, not feared. People remember when he was just fishing boat captain. They watched him build this. They benefit from it. Support is genuine.

## CURRENT TENSIONS

### The Shore Eyes Leak

Someone exposed Shore Eyes operation in Driftmere (~11 months ago). Saltmere agents infiltrated the network. Liss is investigating but hasn't found culprit yet. This is personal failure in her mind — she coordinates the network, missed the breach.

**Impact:** More cautious Shore Eyes operations, harder to gather intelligence, Voss's expansion efforts complicated.

### Growth Pressure

Brinehaven is approaching capacity. Harbor can't handle many more boats. City is densely built up available land. If Voss continues consolidating Salt Shore, Brinehaven becomes bottleneck.

**Options being discussed:**
- Harbor expansion (expensive, disruptive)
- Secondary base in another port (politically complicated)
- Accept current size as limit (restricts growth)

**Current status:** Voss hasn't decided. Harbor expansion seems likely but timing uncertain.

### Keeper's Bay Competition

Keeper's Bay is larger, wealthier, better positioned. Some Brinehaven merchants wonder if they're backing wrong power center. Voss has current momentum, but if Keeper's Bay decisively rejects him, where does that leave Brinehaven?

**Voss's response:** Continue building. Make Brinehaven so prosperous, so secure, that Keeper's Bay eventually sees value in cooperation.

### External Interest

Saltmere wants access to Brinehaven harbor for their trade expansion. House Dorian (Silverbelt merchant house) is offering to fund harbor improvements in exchange for preferential trade terms. L'Empire Verdienne merchants are interested in northern trade routes.

**The problem:** Each deal brings external influence. Too much external involvement and Brinehaven becomes dependent on outsiders. Voss is negotiating carefully.

## MILITARY PRESENCE

**Patrol boats:** Voss's fleet operates from inner harbor. ~12 boats active at any time, rotating crews, constant patrols of local waters.

**Town watch:** ~30 members, handle local security, mostly deterrent to petty crime.

**The Greywater:** When moored, serves as floating headquarters. Impressive symbol of Voss's power. Crew of ~40 lives aboard when in port.

**Defense philosophy:** Don't need walls or garrison. Patrol boats provide mobile defense. Attack Brinehaven by sea, boats intercept. Attack by land, boats can evacuate population or bring reinforcements. Flexible response beats static fortification.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER SETTLEMENTS

**Keeper's Bay:** Respectful distance. Brinehaven acknowledges Keeper's Bay is larger. Keeper's Bay acknowledges Brinehaven is Voss's seat. Trade happens, politics stay separate.

**Tidewrack, Tidepost, Gullhaven:** Smaller towns that integrated into Voss's system. They look to Brinehaven as center of network, coordinate through Liss's office.

**Saltmere:** Complicated. Trade partners, political rivals, source of Shore Eyes leak. Brinehaven merchants do business with Saltmere merchants while both governments maneuver.

**River towns (Rivermeet, Saltmouth):** Brinehaven serves as coastal outlet for river trade. Goods from basin interior flow through here to sea routes.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Shore Eyes Leak:** Party hired to find who compromised network. Investigation leads through Brinehaven's underworld, Saltmere connections, political intrigue.

**Harbor Expansion:** Voss wants harbor expanded. Engineering challenges, political opposition, suspicious accidents. Party investigates whether difficulties are natural or sabotage.

**The Missing Boat:** Fishing boat returned to Brinehaven unmanned — crew missing, everything intact. Party investigates. Maritime mystery with implications.

**Saltmere Negotiation:** Saltmere trade delegation in Brinehaven seeking harbor access. Some people want deal, others oppose. Party caught between factions.

**Medical Emergency:** Disease outbreak in Net Menders' Quarter. Physician needs rare medical supplies from Saltmere. Party must acquire them before people die. Time pressure, competing interests, medical drama.

**The Rival Captain:** One of Voss's patrol boat captains is undermining his authority, building personal power base. Voss needs this handled quietly before it spreads. Delicate internal politics.
