# Keeper's Bay

**Population:** ~35,000 | **Region:** [[02_Regions/Salt_Shore/Salt_Shore_Index|Salt Shore]]  
**Location:** Northern Salt Shore coast, at mouth of Skyspine River  
**Government:** Harbor Council (merchant oligarchy)  
**Identity:** Shipbuilding capital, northern trade hub, maritime power

---

## THE CITY

Keeper's Bay is the Salt Shore's largest city and shipbuilding capital, built at the mouth of the Skyspine River where it empties into the Gulf of Tides. The city's prosperity comes from ships - building them, repairing them, supplying them, and sending them out.

**The harbor:** One of the finest natural harbors on the continent - deep water, protected from storms, access to both river and sea. The harbor can hold 200+ vessels simultaneously.

**The shipyards:** Five major shipbuilding operations employing thousands. Keeper's Bay builds merchant vessels, fishing boats, and some military ships (though not for any single power - the city sells to whoever pays).

**Economy:** Shipbuilding (dominant), maritime trade, fishing, rope/sail production, timber processing

**Population:** Human majority (~60%), Ribbet (~15%), half-elf (~12%), others (~13%)

---

## THE HARBOR COUNCIL

Keeper's Bay is governed by the Harbor Council - seven merchant houses that control the city's major industries.

**Council seats:**
- House Keelwright (shipbuilding)
- House Saltwind (fishing fleet)
- House Tideborne (maritime trade)
- House Ropier (naval supplies)
- House Clearwater (timber import)
- House Ironhull (ship repair)
- House Wayfinder (navigation/charts)

**How it works:** Each house holds one seat. Decisions require four votes. The council rotates chair annually. Currently chaired by Madam Essa Keelwright.

---

## SHIPBUILDING EXPERTISE

Keeper's Bay builds the best ships on the eastern coast. The city's reputation for quality draws orders from across the continent.

**What they build:** Merchant vessels (cargo ships, trade ships), fishing boats (all sizes), luxury yachts, military vessels (to order, no questions about buyer)

**The expertise:** Generations of shipwright families, apprenticeship systems, trade secrets passed down, innovation combined with tradition

**Current demand:** High. Continental tensions mean everyone wants ships. Keeper's Bay yards are booked 18 months out. Prices rising.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO SALT SHORE

Keeper's Bay is technically part of the Salt Shore but operates independently. The Tide King (Voss) in Brinehaven has no authority here. The Harbor Council acknowledges him as regional leader while ignoring him in practice.

**The arrangement:** Keeper's Bay doesn't challenge Voss's authority over smaller towns. Voss doesn't interfere in Keeper's Bay's self-governance. Both sides benefit from appearance of unified Salt Shore.

**Current tension:** Voss wants Keeper's Bay to support his consolidation efforts. The Harbor Council wants to stay neutral and profitable. Pressure increasing.
