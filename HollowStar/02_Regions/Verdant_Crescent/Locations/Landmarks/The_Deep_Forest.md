# The Deep Forest

The ancient forest interior — Simiah canopy communities, elvish root-groves, Faun trails, Goblin warrens, and Druid settlements. All under the Green Covenant's stewardship.

---

## THE HEARTGROVE

**Location:** Center of the Deep Forest  
**Identity:** Green Covenant headquarters, sacred center

The Heartgrove is where the ancient forest is oldest — trees so massive their canopy blocks the sun, roots so deep they tap the world's bones. The Green Covenant is based here.

**Archcovenant Maren Stillwater** leads from the Heartgrove. The living vine around her left forearm appeared when she was selected — nobody told her this would happen, nobody can explain it.

**The secret:** The Covenant's 2,000-year records documenting two previous ancient wakings are sealed in the Heartgrove's archives. Only Archcovenant knows the full truth.

## MOSSHAVEN (~8,000)

**Location:** Deep Forest, Green Covenant coordination center  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (elf ~40%, Simiah ~25%, human ~15%, Faun ~10%, others ~10%)  
**Identity:** Forest governance, Covenant operations, largest Deep Forest settlement

Mosshaven is where the Green Covenant coordinates forest stewardship. Not the Heartgrove itself — that's sacred center — but the working administrative hub.

**Economy:** Forest stewardship, Covenant operations, sustainable resource coordination, Path of Spirits support

**Character:** Functional governance without empire's bureaucracy. Decisions made by consensus, enforced by shared understanding that forest's health matters to everyone.

**The Wardens:** Covenant's working administrators operate from Mosshaven:
- **Warden Syl of the Edge** manages forest-settlement border
- **Warden Chit of Memory** keeps the historical archives
- **Warden of Waters** manages forest-marsh boundary

**Relationship to ancient waking:** Mosshaven knows something is happening. The settlement has quietly prepared stockpiles and emergency protocols without announcing why.

---

## BRIGHTHOLLOW (~2,400)

**Location:** Deep Forest interior  
**Ancestry:** Elf majority (~85%), others (~15%)  
**Identity:** Elvish forest community, ancient culture, minimal outside contact

Brighthollow is where Deep Forest elves maintain traditional ways. Not isolated — the settlement trades with other communities — but deliberately separate from empire and even Covenant governance.

**Economy:** Forest gathering, traditional crafts, sacred site maintenance

**Character:** Old ways practiced without performance. These elves know forest lore that predates the Green Covenant. They share it carefully.

**Relationship to Lumencrest:** None. Brighthollow has no interest in outside politics. The timber pressure on edge settlements might as well be happening on another continent.

---

## ROOTWATCH (~1,800)

**Location:** Deep Forest, monitoring position toward forest edge  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (human ~45%, elf ~30%, Simiah ~15%, others ~10%)  
**Identity:** Forest edge monitoring, early warning, Covenant observation post

Rootwatch sits where Deep Forest transitions toward edge settlements. The community monitors what's happening at the boundary — Lumencrest pressure, edge settlement compliance with agreements, forest health.

**Economy:** Monitoring operations, Covenant coordination, sustainable edge resource management

**Character:** Practical forest guardians. Not mystical — working.

**Reports to:** Warden Syl of the Edge (based in Mosshaven)

---

## CANOPY (~1,600)

**Location:** Deep Forest upper levels  
**Ancestry:** Simiah majority (~90%), others (~10%)  
**Identity:** Treehouse community, canopy living, vertical forest culture

Canopy is built in the trees — platforms, rope bridges, structures integrated with branches. The settlement exists 100-200 feet above forest floor. Simiah live here naturally. Others adapt or leave.

**Economy:** Canopy resource gathering, aerial platform construction, high-forest expertise

**Character:** Vertical culture. The forest floor is "down there" the way sea is "down there" for ship-dwellers. Simiah who live in Canopy rarely descend.

**Unique position:** Canopy residents noticed the ancient waking first — bioluminescence changes visible from tree-top level years before ground observers saw anything.

---

## FERNREST (~1,200)

**Location:** Deep Forest clearing  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (human ~40%, Faun ~30%, elf ~20%, others ~10%)  
**Identity:** Forest clearing community, mixed ancestries, Faun gathering point

Fernrest sits in a natural forest clearing — one of few places in Deep Forest where sunlight reaches ground. The clearing attracts Faun who follow seasonal patterns, making Fernrest a meeting point.

**Economy:** Clearing agriculture (limited), Faun trade coordination, seasonal gathering services

**Character:** Seasonal rhythm. Population fluctuates as Faun communities arrive and depart. Permanent residents know this and plan around it.

---

## STILLWATER GLADE (~900)

**Location:** Deep Forest, sacred pool location  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (druid majority ~60%, others ~40%)  
**Identity:** Sacred site, Path of Spirits center, druid community

Stillwater Glade is built around a sacred pool — perfectly still water reflecting forest canopy, Path of Spirits site that predates the Green Covenant by millennia.

**Economy:** Sacred site maintenance, spiritual guidance, Path of Spirits practice

**Character:** Quiet reverence. The glade is not silent — birds, wind, forest sounds — but human/elven conversation happens softly. The pool matters.

**The druids:** Multi-ancestry druid community maintains the glade. Some are Covenant members, many aren't. All respect the site.

---

## UNDERMOSS (~1,100)

**Location:** Deep Forest floor  
**Ancestry:** Goblin majority (~90%), others (~10%)  
**Identity:** Largest Goblin settlement, forest floor city

Undermoss is built into the forest floor's root systems and shallow caves — Goblin architecture integrated with living roots. To surface dwellers it looks like scattered clearings. To Goblins it's a proper city.

**Economy:** Forest floor resources, mushroom cultivation, root system expertise

**Character:** Goblin culture operating at Goblin scale. Surface-dwellers welcome but everything is Goblin-sized.

---

## ROOTSHADE (~600)

**Location:** Deep Forest floor, secondary Goblin settlement  
**Ancestry:** Goblin majority (~85%), others (~15%)  
**Identity:** Smaller Goblin community, forest floor operations

Secondary Goblin settlement, similar character to Undermoss but smaller, more isolated.

---

## IRONBARK HOLD (~800)

**Location:** Northern forest  
**Ancestry:** Orc majority (~90%), others (~10%)  
**Identity:** Traditional Orc stronghold, northern forest position

Orc community maintaining traditional ways in northern forest. Relationship with empire complicated (history of conflict before current peace) but functional.

## SCATTERED COMMUNITIES

**Simiah canopy settlements:** Dozens of small platforms throughout upper forest (50-200 residents each)

**Faun seasonal camps:** Mobile communities following forest rhythms (100-300 per group)

**Druid groves:** Small multi-ancestry druid communities at sacred sites (20-100 per site)

**Goblin warrens:** Small Goblin settlements throughout forest floor (30-100 each)

---

## THE ANCIENT WAKING

Three Sacred Groves showing unprecedented bioluminescence. They form perfect triangle. The waking site is at center.

**Who knows:** Simiah elders (8 years), Green Covenant leadership (restricted knowledge), some druid communities (observing but not understanding)

**Who doesn't know:** Empire, most edge settlements, most forest communities

**Current status:** Accelerating. The Covenant is preparing without announcing. Archcovenant Maren carries the weight alone.
