# Edge Settlements - Verdant Crescent

The human and mixed-ancestry towns at the forest's eastern edge where Verdant Crescent meets Central Heartlands. Operating under Green Covenant-observed agreements while feeling Lumencrest's pressure for timber.

---

## Timberwatch (~2,800)

**Location:** Northern forest edge  
**Ancestry:** Human majority (~60%), elf (~20%), others (~20%)  
**Identity:** Logging operations, Lumencrest pressure point, northern edge position

Timberwatch operates sustainable logging under Covenant agreements. The town cuts timber, processes it, and ships it east. The Covenant watches closely — sustainable means sustainable, not "as much as the market demands."

**Economy:** Sustainable logging (Covenant-regulated), timber processing, limited trade with Central Heartlands

**The Lumencrest problem:** Timber buyers from Lumencrest offer premium prices for increased volume. The math is tempting. The Covenant's position is clear: exceed sustainable limits and lose permission to log at all.

**Town-Master Kael Forestborn** (human, 54) manages this tension daily. He's turned down Lumencrest offers three times. They keep coming back with higher numbers.

**Current tension:** Some residents want to take Lumencrest money. Others know breaking Covenant trust would destroy their entire operation. Town is divided.

## Greengate (~1,400)

**Location:** Central forest edge, main forest-Heartlands crossing  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (human ~50%, Katari ~25%, elf ~15%, others ~10%)  
**Identity:** Main crossing point, Katari trade hub, cultural mixing

Greengate sits where the main road from Central Heartlands enters the forest. The town is both waystation and cultural mixing point — Katari traders pass through regularly, forest goods meet Heartlands commerce.

**Economy:** Waystation services, Katari trade coordination, forest-Heartlands commerce, crossing facilitation

**The Katari presence:** Significant Katari community uses Greengate as route-point. Their oral navigation tradition and memory culture meshes well with forest values. Relationship is respectful.

**Gate-Keeper Senna Pathwise** (human, 48) manages crossing traffic and coordinates with Covenant observers who monitor what enters/exits forest.

**Character:** Cultural bridge. Forest culture and Heartlands culture meet here without conflict. Trade happens, goods flow, agreements hold.

## Mosshaven (~800)

**Location:** Southern forest edge, near Glittering Marshes border  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (human ~45%, Ribbet ~20%, elf ~20%, others ~15%)  
**Identity:** Herbal medicine, forest-marsh border, specialized gathering

Mosshaven (different from Deep Forest's Mosshaven — confusing but both names stuck) operates at the forest-marsh transition. The town specializes in medicinal plants that grow where forest meets wetland.

**Economy:** Medicinal plant gathering and processing, herbal trade, forest-marsh expertise

**The herbalists:** Mosshaven's gatherers know which plants grow where, when to harvest, how to process. This knowledge is valuable — healers across the continent buy Mosshaven products.

**Herbalist-Master Mira Greenleaf** (elf, 234) coordinates gathering operations and maintains relationships with both Covenant and marsh communities.

**Relationship to Covenant:** Excellent. Mosshaven's sustainable gathering practices are model the Covenant points to. No Lumencrest pressure here — they want timber, not herbs.

## Thornvale (~3,200)

**Location:** Northern forest edge toward Northern Expanse  
**Ancestry:** Human majority (~55%), elf (~25%), others (~20%)  
**Identity:** Forest edge border town, mixed human/elf culture

Thornvale is larger than typical edge settlement, operating at transition between forest, empire influence to west, and Northern Expanse to north. Mixed culture reflecting multiple spheres of influence.

**Economy:** Mixed agriculture and forest goods, border trade, waystation services

**Character:** Cultural transition. Not quite empire, not quite deep forest, not quite Northern Expanse. Benefits from being at intersection.

---

## Greenmark (~2,800)

**Location:** Eastern edge toward Central Heartlands  
**Ancestry:** Human majority (~65%), mixed others (~35%)  
**Identity:** Heartlands-facing trade, eastern border position

Greenmark faces Central Heartlands more directly than other edge settlements. The town handles trade between forest communities and Heartlands markets.

**Economy:** Forest goods trade, Heartlands commerce, market coordination

**Lumencrest pressure:** Present but less intense than Timberwatch. Greenmark trades finished goods more than raw timber.

## Oakheart (~1,400)

**Location:** Western edge toward L'Empire Verdienne peninsula  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (human ~55%, elf ~25%, others (~20%)  
**Identity:** Western forest edge, empire-forest interface

Western edge settlement at the transition between Deep Forest and empire's sphere. Manages relationship between Covenant authority and imperial presence.

**Economy:** Forest goods for empire trade, interface services, cultural mediation

**Imperial relationship:** Respectful distance. The empire doesn't govern here but commerce flows freely. Oakheart benefits from position between two powers.

## THE LUMENCREST PROBLEM

**Timber demand:** Lumencrest needs timber. The Council's infrastructure requires wood — buildings, bridges, docks, fuel. The Heart's decline means imports are expensive.

**Edge settlement pressure:** Lumencrest buyers offer premium prices for increased logging volume. The offers are generous. The implications are clear — exceed Covenant limits and lose everything.

**Town responses vary:**
- **Timberwatch:** Heavily pressured, town divided, holding line but strained
- **Greengate:** Less pressure (not timber-focused), stable
- **Mosshaven:** No pressure (herbs not timber), comfortable

**Covenant position:** Sustainable limits are not negotiable. Exceed them and lose logging rights permanently. The Covenant has enforced this before. Towns know it's not bluff.

**Current status:** Edge settlements holding agreements, but Lumencrest pressure increasing as Heart decline accelerates. The question is whether towns can resist when numbers get high enough.

---

## COVENANT MONITORING

**Warden Syl of the Edge** manages forest-settlement relationships from Mosshaven (Deep Forest). She knows exactly which towns are holding firm and which are wavering.

**Her list:** Syl maintains a list of edge settlement contacts who've taken Lumencrest money or are considering it. People on the list have bad days in the forest — not violence, just consistent difficulty. The forest notices things.

**The message:** Covenant authority isn't enforced through laws. It's enforced through relationship with the forest. Break that relationship and the forest stops cooperating.
