# Les Perles (The Pearls)

The island chain south of Montauseau, extending into the Great Depths. Imperial territory with distinct maritime culture and Galapa presence.

---

## THE ISLAND CHAIN

**Name:** Les Perles (The Pearls) - named for how they appear from Montauseau's heights: white beaches catching sunlight like pearls on dark water

**Geography:** Five islands total, three inhabited at population levels worth noting. The westernmost islands are inhabited by small Galapa communities and other water-focused ancestries not counted in imperial census.

**Imperial status:** Full imperial territory, governed from Montauseau with local prefects, but maintaining distinct maritime culture

**Galapa presence:** The Perles are home to established Galapa populations who've lived here for generations - long before the empire, continuing after. These are distinct from the Broken Sphere Galapa - different migration history, different culture.

---

## Vinnoît (~9,000)

**Location:** First island, closest to mainland (south of Montauseau)  
**Identity:** Largest Perles settlement, pearl diving, maritime trade  
**Prefect:** Océane Marépulse, human, age 47

Vinnoît is Les Perles' de facto capital - the largest settlement, the most connected to mainland empire, the center of the islands' pearl diving industry.

**The pearl diving:** Vinnoît's economy runs on pearls harvested from the Great Depths waters surrounding Les Perles. Human divers, Galapa divers (superior skill), Ribbet divers (specialized techniques) - the industry employs hundreds and produces pearls exported across the empire and to Vossomar.

**Economy:** Pearl diving and trade (dominant), fishing, maritime services, connection point to mainland

**Population:** Human majority (~65%), Galapa (~20%), Ribbet (~10%), others (~5%)

**Character:** Maritime expertise, diving culture, wealth from pearls, comfortable relationship between human imperial authority and Galapa diving mastery

**The diving culture:** Respect transcends ancestry. The best diver gets respect regardless of being human, Galapa, or Ribbet. Depth records are maintained religiously. Deaths happen - diving is dangerous - but deaths are honored not avoided through fear.

**Current prosperity:** Pearl demand high - Vossomar traders pay premium prices. Vinnoît is wealthy by Les Perles standards, comfortable by mainland standards.

**Galapa community:** ~1,800 Galapa living in Vinnoît, primarily in the harbor quarter. Fully integrated into diving economy, maintain distinct cultural practices, relationship with mainland Galapa complicated (these are "old families" vs. "newcomers" like Broken Sphere refugees).

**Notable figures:**
- Prefect Océane Marépulse - Imperial governor, former merchant, understands diving economy
- Diving-Master Cascade (Galapa, age unknown, male) - Best diver in Les Perles, legendary depth records
- Pearl-Factor Theron Brightwater - Coordinates pearl export to mainland

## Périlet (~6,000)

**Location:** Third island (second inhabited island, skipping the smaller second island)  
**Identity:** Fishing, boat-building, island independence  
**Prefect:** Prefect-Captain Bastien Salvent, human, age 52

Périlet is smaller, more isolated, more independently-minded than Vinnoît. The island builds boats, catches fish, and maintains a cultural distance from mainland empire while staying technically loyal.

**Economy:** Fishing (primary), boat-building (specialty), maritime supplies

**Population:** Human majority (~70%), Galapa (~15%), others (~15%)

**Character:** Independent streak, island pride, "we take care of our own" mentality, suspicious of mainland interference

**Boat-building expertise:** Périlet's boatwrights build excellent small craft - fishing boats, diving boats, personal vessels. Not warships - small, fast, seaworthy. Périlet boats are sought across Les Perles and on the mainland.

**The independence question:** Périlet pays imperial taxes, follows imperial law (mostly), acknowledges Montauseau's authority. But the island governs itself in practice. Prefect Salvent is local, elected by island community, then approved by Montauseau - not appointed from outside.

**Current tension:** Mainland empire is tightening authority everywhere. Périlet resists quietly. No rebellion - just slow bureaucratic resistance, convenient misunderstandings, paperwork getting lost.

**Notable figures:**
- Prefect-Captain Bastien Salvent - Local governor, islander himself, manages mainland carefully
- Master Boatwright Senna Keelwise - Builds best boats in Les Perles
- Fisher-King Corin Netmaster - Leads fishing fleet, community elder

## Narçon (~3,000)

**Location:** Fourth island, eastern end of settled chain  
**Identity:** Galapa majority, frontier character, distant outpost  
**Prefect:** None (administered from Vinnoît), informal leadership by Elder Tidescale

Narçon is different. The island has Galapa majority (~60%), significant other water-ancestry minorities (Ribbet ~20%, human ~15%, others ~5%), and operates semi-autonomously despite imperial territory status.

**The Galapa community:** These Galapa have lived on Narçon for centuries - longer than the empire has existed. They pre-date imperial expansion and continue after it. The empire's approach: acknowledge their presence, don't interfere too much, collect minimal taxes, call it imperial territory on maps.

**Economy:** Fishing (Galapa methods), deep-water navigation, maritime knowledge, some trade with Vinnoît

**Character:** Frontier, isolated, self-sufficient, maintains pre-imperial culture while nominally accepting empire

**The arrangement:** Narçon pays token taxes to Vinnoît (which passes portion to Montauseau). In exchange, empire leaves them alone. Galapa Elder Tidescale is informal leader - the empire doesn't appoint prefect because they'd just ignore them anyway.

**Deep-water expertise:** Narçon Galapa know the Great Depths approaches better than anyone. They navigate waters others avoid, catch fish from depths others can't reach, survive storms that would kill mainland sailors.

**Current status:** The empire's military build-up hasn't touched Narçon. The island noticed and appreciates being left alone. If war comes, Narçon's loyalty is uncertain - not hostile, but not guaranteed either.

**Notable figures:**
- Elder Tidescale (Galapa, age unknown but old, male) - Informal leader, respected across island
- Navigator Deepcurrent (Galapa, ~80, non-binary) - Best deep-water navigator, teaches young
- Fisher Waverunner (Ribbet, 34, female) - Connects Ribbet and Galapa communities

## THE WESTERN ISLANDS

The westernmost two islands in Les Perles chain are inhabited but not at population levels the empire counts formally:

**Small Galapa communities:** Probably 400-600 total across both islands, living traditionally, minimal contact with empire

**Other water ancestries:** Ribbet families, possibly Sea Elves (rumors), isolated homesteads

**Imperial approach:** These islands are on maps as imperial territory. In practice, empire has no presence. The inhabitants neither acknowledge nor reject imperial authority - they just exist.

---

## LES PERLES CULTURE

**Maritime mastery:** Everyone in Les Perles knows boats, knows water, knows weather. Swimming is universal. Drowning is common cause of death - the sea takes people.

**Ancestry mixing:** Les Perles has more functional integration between humans and Galapa than anywhere on mainland. Shared expertise in dangerous work creates respect that transcends ancestry.

**Island identity:** Les Perles residents identify as islanders first, imperial citizens second (if at all). Vinnoît less so, Périlet more so, Narçon entirely so.

**The pearl trade:** Wealth flows from pearls but doesn't pool - diving is dangerous, profitable, but not rich-making. Comfortable rather than wealthy.

**Mainland relationship:** Connected but distinct. Les Perles needs mainland (trade, supplies, connection). Mainland needs Les Perles less than the islands need it - this shapes the power dynamic.
