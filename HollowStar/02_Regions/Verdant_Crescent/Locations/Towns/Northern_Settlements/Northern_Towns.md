# Northern Settlements - Verdant Crescent Towns

Smaller northern settlements beyond imperial territory, scattered across forest edges and rugged terrain.

---

## Harkenbell (~7,000)

**Location:** Southeast of Swallownook, southern coast, south of Rootwatch  
**Identity:** Functional port, Deep Forest goods gateway, forest-coast connection  

Harkenbell is a small but functional port town bringing goods from the Deep Forest interior to coastal trade routes. The town sits where forest meets coast, serving as transfer point between forest economies and maritime commerce.

**Economy:** Port operations, Deep Forest goods handling, warehousing, coastal trade coordination

**Character:** Working port, practical focus, connects two economic worlds (forest and maritime)

**Population:** Mixed (human ~50%, elf ~25%, others ~25%)

---

## Swallownook (~6,000)

**Location:** West of Deep Forest southern edge, river from mountains, separated from empire by mountains  
**Identity:** River settlement, mountain border, independent trade  

Swallownook sits on a river flowing from the mountains, separated from the empire by the mountain range itself. The town operates independently, trading with forest communities to the east and maintaining distance from imperial authority.

**Economy:** River trade, mountain goods, independent commerce, mixed farming

**Character:** Independence-minded, mountain buffer creates separation from empire, river provides trade route

**Population:** Mixed (human ~60%, others ~40%)

**The border question:** Mountains form physical barrier. Some say river is border between empire and independent forest region. Most say mountains are border. The distinction matters for trade authority. Swallownook benefits from ambiguity.

---

## Crystalcrest (~5,000)

**Location:** South of Onyxveil, warmer waters, across from Perpilles  
**Identity:** Coastal trade, empire connection, northern maritime settlement  

Crystalcrest sits on warmer northern coast, positioned to trade with empire settlements across the water (especially Perpilles). The town is more connected to empire than most northern settlements without being imperial territory.

**Economy:** Coastal trade with empire, fishing, maritime services, cross-water commerce

**Character:** Connected but independent, benefits from empire trade without imperial authority

**Population:** Human majority (~70%), mixed others (~30%)

---

## Onyxveil (~4,000)

**Location:** Far northwest, frigid waters, isolated coastal  
**Identity:** Remote frontier, cold-water resources, isolation culture  

Onyxveil is the northern coast's most remote settlement - frigid waters, harsh climate, isolated position. The town exists for cold-water resources (certain fish, seal products, materials found only in cold) and serves those who prefer distance from civilization.

**Economy:** Cold-water fishing, seal hunting, unique resource gathering, frontier supplies

**Character:** Hard life, isolation valued, self-sufficient community, suspicious of outsiders

**Population:** Human majority (~75%), others (~25%) - those adapted to harsh conditions

---

## Swiftwind (~3,000)

**Location:** East of Skyspine Mountains, northern edge of Glittering Marshes approach  
**Identity:** Remote waystation, stopping point, crossing location  

Swiftwind is isolated - not much happens here, but the town is essential waystation for travel between regions. Going to/from Glittering Marshes, Central Heartlands, or western forest areas means stopping at Swiftwind.

**Economy:** Waystation services (lodging, supplies, guides), minimal other activity

**Character:** Exists because geography demands it, residents accept isolation as trade-off

**Population:** Mixed (~60% human, ~40% others) - transient population higher than permanent

**Geographic importance:** Nearest settlements are Harkenbell (west), Shadowpine (east near marshes), Greenmark (east toward Heartlands) - all distant. Swiftwind is the connection point.

---

## Castingbridge (~2,000)

**Location:** Near river from northern mountains to Arathandë River, mixed area  
**Identity:** River crossing, bridge town, Fairy presence  

Castingbridge sits where a northern mountain river meets the Arathandë. The town operates the bridge crossing and has unusual feature - small Fairy population living nearby in forest glades.

**Economy:** Bridge tolls, river crossing services, trade facilitation

**Character:** Bridge town culture plus Fairy influence creates unique feel

**Population:** Mixed (human ~45%, elf ~25%, Fairy ~15%, others ~15%)

**The Fairy presence:** Small Fairy community in nearby glades maintains careful relationship with town. They're not integrated - Fairies maintain independence - but relationship is respectful. Occasional trade, no conflict.
