# Peninsula Cities - L'Empire Verdienne

Additional imperial cities along the peninsula, each with distinct character and economic identity.

---

## Porteclaire (~22,000)

**Location:** Western coast, between Montauseau and Calvaire  
**Identity:** Western maritime gateway, merchant hub, second-largest port  
**Governor:** Factor-Duchess Éloise Marmont, human, age 49

Porteclaire is the empire's western trade powerhouse - the second-largest port after Montauseau, handling significant maritime commerce with destinations south and west along the coast. The city's prosperity comes from being perfectly positioned between the capital and Calvaire's naval operations.

**Economy:** Maritime trade (primary), shipbuilding (merchant vessels), warehousing, luxury goods import/export, fish processing

**Character:** Wealthy merchant families, competitive trade houses, cosmopolitan population, less military than Calvaire but more commercially aggressive than Montauseau

**The merchant quarter:** Porteclaire's wealth is visible - three-story stone townhouses, paved streets, the Merchant's Exchange where contracts worth kingdoms change hands daily

**Current activity:** Benefiting from increased Vossomar trade flowing through Montauseau - Porteclaire handles overflow and specializes in luxury goods that don't need capital oversight

**Notable figures:**
- Factor-Duchess Éloise Marmont - Governor, former merchant herself, understands trade from inside
- Merchant-Lord Bastien Cœurdelune - Wealthiest trader, Vossomar luxury specialist
- Harbor-Master Seren Tidewell - Half-elf, manages second-busiest imperial port

## Draguisart (~23,000)

**Location:** Northern coastal strip, between Sainteloise and Perpilles  
**Identity:** Northern fishing capital, salt production, coastal trade hub  
**Governor:** Captain-Prefect Margaux Salvent, human, age 52

Draguisart is where the northern coast's fishing industry is headquartered. The city processes more fish than anywhere in the empire except Calvaire, and dominates the northern salt trade - extensive salt flats south of the city produce high-quality sea salt exported across the empire.

**Economy:** Fishing (massive scale), salt production and trade, fish preservation, coastal shipping

**Character:** Working-class prosperity - fishers who own their boats, salt workers who share profits, a city that smells of brine and honest labor

**The salt flats:** South of the city, geometric pools where seawater evaporates leaving salt crystals. The flats employ 800+ workers in season. The white expanse visible for miles.

**The fishing fleet:** 200+ vessels from small coastal boats to deep-water trawlers. Draguisart's fleet is second only to Calvaire's. During peak season the harbor is packed solid with boats.

**Current tension:** Northern Expanse refugees arriving via Sainteloise are reaching Draguisart. The city can absorb workers (fishing always needs hands) but housing is tight. Governor Salvent is managing integration better than some, not as well as others.

**Notable figures:**
- Captain-Prefect Margaux Salvent - Governor, former fishing captain, understands the industry
- Fleet-Master Corin Netwise - Commands fishing fleet coordination
- Salt-Master Theron Whitehand - Oversees salt flat operations

## Maugueux (~17,000)

**Location:** Western coast, north of Porteclaire  
**Identity:** Textile manufacturing, coastal agriculture, middle-class prosperity  
**Governor:** Prefect Davan Threadwell, human, age 61

Maugueux makes the empire's textiles. The city's workshops produce linen, wool cloth, canvas for sails, and fine fabrics for imperial fashion. Surrounding agricultural land grows flax and raises sheep - raw materials processed locally then shipped to markets across the empire.

**Economy:** Textile manufacturing (dominant), agriculture (flax/sheep), dyeing operations, finished cloth export

**Character:** Middle-class artisan city - weavers, dyers, fullers, spinners. Not wealthy like Porteclaire's merchants, not working-class like Draguisart's fishers. Comfortable skilled labor.

**The workshops:** Hundreds of small operations plus three large manufactories employing 100+ workers each. The sound of looms is the city's constant background.

**The dye quarter:** Where cloth is colored using local and imported materials. The river running through this district runs different colors depending on what's being processed. Locals joke you can tell the season by the river's hue.

**Current focus:** Increased military orders from Montauseau - canvas for naval sails, uniform cloth for soldiers. The empire is quietly building supplies. Maugueux benefits economically but workers notice the shift.

**Notable figures:**
- Prefect Davan Threadwell - Governor, administrator, manages guild relationships carefully
- Guild-Master Senna Brightthread - Weavers' Guild leader, influential
- Dye-Master Kael Colorfast - Chemical expertise, guards trade secrets

## Aurigneux (~15,000)

**Location:** Eastern coast, southeast of Belacroix  
**Identity:** Eastern maritime trade, forest goods export, gateway to forest edge  
**Governor:** Warden-Prefect Élise Forestier, half-elf, age 87

Aurigneux is the empire's eastern maritime presence - where forest goods from the interior (timber, medicinal plants, crafted items) meet coastal trade routes. The city connects the empire's interior production to seaborne commerce.

**Economy:** Forest goods export (primary), maritime trade, warehousing, timber shipment, medicinal plant processing

**Character:** Mixed population (human majority, significant elf/half-elf minority), closer to forest culture than most imperial cities, mediates between empire and forest-adjacent communities

**The lumber yards:** Massive storage and processing facilities for timber floated down from interior forests. Aurigneux ships processed lumber coastwise to other imperial cities and beyond.

**The herbalist quarter:** Where medicinal plants from forest edge settlements are processed, preserved, and prepared for export. The quarter smells of a hundred different herbs and draws healers seeking quality supplies.

**Current tension:** Aurigneux's position near forest edge means the city feels Lumencrest pressure indirectly - Lumencrest wants timber, some comes through here, the empire carefully controls how much. Governor Forestier walks a careful line between commerce and strategic denial.

**Notable figures:**
- Warden-Prefect Élise Forestier - Governor, half-elf, understands both imperial and forest perspectives
- Timber-Factor Bastien Oakwise - Coordinates lumber operations
- Herbalist-Master Seren Greenleaf - Elf, medicinal plant expert, Green Covenant connections

## Marticourt (~14,000)

**Location:** Southern peninsula, between Calvaire and Vierteaux  
**Identity:** Naval support, shipwright community, rope and sail production  
**Governor:** Captain-Prefect Roland Keelwright, human, age 56

Marticourt exists to support Calvaire's naval operations - the city produces ropes, sails, naval supplies, and trains shipwrights. Less glamorous than building warships, essential to keeping them operational.

**Economy:** Rope-making (massive scale), sail production, shipwright training, naval supply manufacturing

**Character:** Technical expertise, pride in craft, understands that empires run on logistics not just battles

**The ropeworks:** Enormous facilities where hemp is processed into rope of every size from thread to anchor cables. The ropeworks employ 1,000+ workers and produce rope for both military and civilian use.

**The shipwright school:** Imperial naval shipwrights train here under master craftsmen before going to Calvaire's yards. Graduation from Marticourt's program is prestigious.

**Current activity:** Increased production for naval expansion - Calvaire's building more ships, Marticourt supplies more rope, more sails, more trained workers. The city is prosperous but stretched.

**Notable figures:**
- Captain-Prefect Roland Keelwright - Governor, former shipwright himself
- Master Ropier Theron Strongtwist - Oversees ropeworks, technical genius
- Sail-Master Élise Canvaswind - Manages sail production

## Canau (~12,000)

**Location:** Southern peninsula tip, southwest of Montauseau  
**Identity:** Fishing, whaling, southern maritime culture  
**Governor:** Harbor-Prefect Maren Deepwater, human, age 44

Canau is the southern tip's fishing community - smaller than Calvaire but with its own character. The city specializes in whaling and deep-water fishing in the southern approaches to the Great Depths.

**Economy:** Whaling (specialty), deep-water fishing, whale product processing, fishing supplies

**Character:** Hardy, isolated, proud of difficult work, separate identity from larger northern cities

**The whaling culture:** Canau's fleet pursues whales in deep waters where few others go. Dangerous work, profitable returns. The city processes whale oil, meat, bone - every part used.

**Current character:** Remote enough to maintain distinct culture even within the empire. Canau sees itself as frontier even though it's imperial territory. Governor Deepwater doesn't fight this - she knows forcing conformity would break what works.

**Notable figures:**
- Harbor-Prefect Maren Deepwater - Governor, former whaling captain
- Whaling-Master Corin Harpoonwise - Fleet coordinator, legendary harpooner
- Processor-Chief Senna Bonecarver - Whale product processing expert

## Côte-d'Or (~11,000)

**Location:** Eastern coast, south of Aurigneux  
**Identity:** Golden coast resort, wealthy retreat, fishing and leisure  
**Governor:** Prefect-Regent Bastien Soleilmer, human, age 39

Côte-d'Or is the empire's luxury retreat - golden beaches, warm waters, where wealthy imperials vacation and maintain estates. The city's economy runs on leisure and luxury service, unusual in the practical empire.

**Economy:** Luxury hospitality, estate management, high-end fishing (sport), wealthy tourism

**Character:** Beautiful, expensive, slightly detached from empire's working reality. Not decadent - the empire doesn't permit that - but definitely comfortable.

**The golden beaches:** The coast here has unusual sand that glints gold in sunlight (mineral content). The effect is striking and gave the city its name.

**The estates:** Wealthy merchants, successful governors, military officers - those who've earned imperial favor maintain vacation properties here. The estates are comfortable not ostentatious (Carmine's empire has standards).

**Current tension:** The contrast between Côte-d'Or's leisure and empire's military preparation is jarring. Some suggest the city's existence sends wrong message. Governor Soleilmer argues morale matters and imperial leaders need rest too. Carmine hasn't commented.

**Notable figures:**
- Prefect-Regent Bastien Soleilmer - Governor, young, wealthy family, managing carefully
- Estate-Master Éloise Goldshore - Coordinates estate services
- Harbor-Master Seren Clearwater - Manages sport fishing operations
