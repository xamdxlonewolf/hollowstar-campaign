# Peninsula & Coastal Cities — Verdant Crescent

The subordinate cities of [[04_Factions/Verdant_Crescent/L'Empire Verdienne|L'Empire Verdienne]], arranged along the western peninsula and northern coastal strip. Each has its own character and economic identity while operating within imperial governance structures. All are meaningfully larger than the forest edge settlements to the east and exist in a different cultural register — coastal, imperial, outward-facing — while still existing in the shadow of the Verdant Crescent's great forest.

---

## PENINSULA CITIES

### Calvaire
**Population:** ~28,000  
**Location:** Southern tip of the peninsula, facing open ocean  
**Identity:** The empire's naval stronghold and deep-water fishing capital  
**Governor:** Countess Elara Sevenne, human, age 55 — practical, weather-beaten, has captained ships herself

Calvaire is where the empire's serious sailors come from. The southern tip of the peninsula catches weather off the Great Depths before anywhere else does — Calvaire sailors know ocean moods the way Millhaven farmers know soil. The city's military harbor houses the empire's deep-water naval fleet: the ships that escort Vossomar trade vessels, patrol the western approaches, and would form the backbone of any naval campaign.

The civilian fishing industry operates at scale — deep-ocean fishing that brings in species found nowhere else. Calvaire exports preserved fish, whale products, and navigational expertise across the empire and beyond.

**The naval culture:** Military and civilian life are deeply intertwined. Most families have both naval service members and fishers. The distinction matters to outsiders more than it does to Calvaire residents, who consider the ocean the organizing fact of existence regardless of what you do on it.

**Current tension:** Commander-General Aumont has been requesting additional deep-water vessels for two years. The imperial budget has accommodated most of these requests. Governor Sevenne has been pushing for the naval yards to be expanded to match the increased orders — the current yards are at capacity and behind schedule. She and Aumont have a productive disagreement about this that Carmine mediates occasionally.

**Hook:** A Rouçon trading vessel that should have arrived three weeks ago has not. Calvaire's harbor watch has noted it missing. The route it would have taken passes through waters where two other ships have disappeared in the past year. Nobody has connected these incidents officially. Yet.

### Belacroix
**Population:** ~19,000  
**Location:** Mid-peninsula, sheltered eastern bay — the peninsula's agricultural and craft center  
**Identity:** Lumber processing, agriculture, the peninsula's internal supply chain  
**Governor:** Prefect Orun Cassell, half-elf, age 78 — administrator's administrator, deeply uninterested in glory

Belacroix does the work that makes everything else possible. The agricultural districts surrounding the city feed the peninsula's population. The lumber operations — run under imperial sustainability agreements that are enforced more strictly than the Green Covenant's own rules at the forest edge — supply timber to Montauseau's shipyards and Calvaire's naval construction. The craft quarters produce the goods that fill the trade ships.

The city itself is comfortable rather than impressive. Efficient infrastructure, reliable food supply, a population that takes quiet satisfaction in being the part of the empire that actually works. Prefect Cassell has governed here for twenty-two years and his primary achievement is that the city rarely requires his direct attention.

**The lumber relationship:** Belacroix's logging operations run along the peninsula's interior forests — not the Verdant Crescent's great forest, which is off limits under the non-interference agreement, but the peninsula's own stands of timber. The Green Covenant monitors these operations without formally governing them. The arrangement works because Belacroix follows the rules without being told to on each occasion. This is not virtue — it is the calculation that a good relationship with the Covenant is worth more than the timber they might extract by cheating.

**Current tension:** The Lumencrest demand for timber has reached Belacroix through intermediaries offering above-market prices for wood they can't get through normal channels. Prefect Cassell has refused three times. The offers keep coming, now with a desperation that tells him how bad things are in Lumencrest. He has reported this to the imperial council. It is being treated as intelligence.

**Hook:** A logging crew has found something in the peninsula's interior forest — not the Verdant Crescent proper, but close to the border. Something that shouldn't be there. The crew chief has told Cassell. Cassell has told no one else and is trying to figure out whether this requires informing the Covenant before the Covenant finds out he didn't tell them immediately.

### Varemonde
**Population:** ~14,000  
**Location:** Northern peninsula, where the peninsula joins the coastal strip — crossroads city  
**Identity:** The gateway between peninsula and coast, overland trade hub, diverse population  
**Governor:** Warden-Prefect Solen Marchais, orc, age 44 — first Orc governor in imperial history, appointed by Carmine four years ago

Varemonde sits at the junction where the peninsula's road network meets the northern coastal strip. Everything moving between Montauseau and the northern settlements passes through here. Everything moving between the coast and the interior passes through here. The city has developed the character of crossroads places everywhere — diverse, transactional, less invested in any single identity than cities with more singular economic purposes.

**Warden-Prefect Marchais:** His appointment was deliberate signal. Carmine chose him from a list of three qualified candidates specifically because his appointment would mean something to the northern Orc and Goblin communities whose relationship with the empire is still being repaired. He was the most qualified candidate regardless. She made sure of that before making the appointment political.

He governs carefully and competently and is aware that he is watched more closely than other governors for any sign of failure. He has not failed. He is tired of being watched but understands why.

**The mixed population:** Varemonde has the most diverse population of any imperial city outside Montauseau itself — humans, elves, half-elves, orcs, half-orcs, a Goblin quarter, Katari traders, and occasionally Simiah passing through from the forest edge. The mixing is functional rather than harmonious in the idealized sense. People coexist because the crossroads requires cooperation. This is enough, most of the time.

**Current tension:** A series of robberies targeting Goblin-owned businesses in the market quarter. Not obviously motivated by anything beyond opportunism, but the pattern is there. Marchais is investigating personally. The Goblin community is watching whether the investigation produces results or gets quietly shelved. So is Carmine.

**Hook:** A message passed through a Katari trader's network has reached Varemonde addressed to someone who doesn't exist — a name that nobody in the city recognizes but that matches a known Lumencrest intelligence operative designation. Marchais has the message. He doesn't know what to do with it. He needs someone outside official channels to help figure out who it was actually meant for.

## NORTHERN COASTAL CITIES

### Sainteloise
**Population:** ~11,000  
**Location:** Northern coastal strip, where river mouth meets the sea  
**Identity:** River trade, northern goods, the empire's interface with the Northern Expanse's trade flows  
**Governor:** Factor-Prefect Calla Dourne, human, age 51

The northernmost significant imperial settlement — the last city before the Verdant Crescent's territory gives way to Northern Expanse influence. Sainteloise sits at the mouth of a river that runs north into the Northern Expanse, making it a natural collection point for northern trade goods flowing south and a natural distribution point for imperial goods flowing north.

The city is smaller and quieter than the peninsula cities. It does important logistical work without drama. The population is heavily human with a significant Elven minority — the Northern Expanse's Elven culture bleeds south here, and Sainteloise has a different cultural texture than the more imperial-feeling cities to the south.

**The northern relationship:** Sainteloise exists in a complicated position between the empire's authority and the Northern Expanse's crown. Technically imperial. Practically a border community that trades with both and fully belongs to neither. Governor Dourne manages this skillfully — she has good relationships with Northern Expanse trade officials and has never pushed the question of where imperial authority formally ends.

**Current tension:** The Frostwell refugee flow has reached Sainteloise's doorstep. Northern Expanse refugees traveling south are arriving at the city's gates. The imperial asylum system applies — service requirement for residency — but the volume is exceeding the system's designed capacity. Dourne has sent three requests for additional processing resources to Montauseau. The first two received acknowledgment. The third has not been answered.

### Riveaulx
**Population:** ~7,000  
**Location:** Northern coastal strip, cliff-top position overlooking sheltered cove  
**Identity:** Fishing, small-scale trade, the empire's quiet northern outpost  
**Governor:** Captain-Prefect Hen Arbel, half-orc, age 38

The smallest of the named imperial settlements — more large town than city by most measures, but formally incorporated and governed. Riveaulx exists on a dramatic coastal cliff above a natural cove that provides some of the best sheltered anchorage on the northern coast. It is a stopping point rather than a destination — ships moving between Sainteloise and points south rest here, resupply, and continue.

Arbel has been governor for three years — young for the role, appointed because nobody else wanted it and he was genuinely interested. He is learning the job in real time and making mostly good decisions. The city's small size means mistakes are catchable before they become crises.

**The local character:** Riveaulx's population is tight-knit in the way of isolated communities that depend on each other. The fishing families have been here for four generations. The inn district serves passing sailors. The two communities have an established if slightly wary coexistence. Outsiders are noticed.

**Hook:** Something has been washing up on the cove below Riveaulx — objects from a ship, but not any ship Arbel's harbor registry shows as missing. The wreckage is old. The objects are not. Someone knows what ship this is and they have not reported it.

## VILLAGES AND SMALLER SETTLEMENTS

The peninsula and coastal strip contain numerous smaller communities not formally governed as imperial cities but operating within the empire's legal and economic structure.

**The Peninsula Villages** — fishing communities, farming hamlets, lumber camps. Each has a headman or council that reports to the nearest city governor. Mostly human and half-elf, deeply embedded in the peninsula's working economy.

**The Northern Orc Villages** — the cluster of communities in the northern coastal strip and transition zone toward the Northern Expanse. Five main villages ranging from 400 to 1,200 residents. The largest, **Grevhal**, has a respected council of elders whose authority the empire formally recognizes. Relations with Montauseau have improved significantly under Carmine's reign — the appointment of Warden-Prefect Marchais in Varemonde was understood in Grevhal as a genuine gesture rather than a political performance, which is a distinction the community is experienced in making.

**The Goblin Settlements** — smaller, more scattered, two main clusters. Their self-sufficiency is real — they trade with the empire on their own terms, provide services nobody else offers as well, and maintain their communities with minimal outside involvement. The understanding that their treatment is improving is correct. The understanding that history matters and improvement is not the same as trust is also correct.

**Forest Edge Villages** — the handful of very small settlements where imperial territory transitions to Covenant-observed forest edge. These are the empire's most unusual communities — imperial citizens who have absorbed enough forest culture to make the Green Covenant comfortable with their presence near the boundary. They exist in a genuinely dual world and are valued by both sides for it.
