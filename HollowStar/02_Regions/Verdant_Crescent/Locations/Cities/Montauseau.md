# Montauseau

**Population:** ~95,000 (city proper) + ~40,000 in surrounding districts  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Verdant_Crescent/Verdant_Crescent_Index|Verdant Crescent]] — western peninsula  
**Location:** At the peninsula's widest point, where the natural harbor opens to the Great Depths  
**Identity:** Capital of [[04_Factions/Verdant_Crescent/L'Empire Verdienne|L'Empire Verdienne]] — the continent's great western power, trade gateway to Vossomar, seat of the Montauseau dynasty

---

## What Montauseau Is

Montauseau does not look like a city that has been waiting sixty years to fight a war. It looks like a city that has been busy becoming worth fighting for.

The harbor district is the most active commercial port on the western continent — Rouçon trading vessels from Vossomar anchor here, their goods unloaded into warehouses that then distribute them across the continent. The residential quarters are clean and maintained to imperial standards. The market districts are organized, taxed, and regulated without stifling them. The military quarter is large, visible, and deliberately so — the empire does not hide its strength.

The city was founded by Carmine's great-grandfather on a natural harbor site that the peninsula's scattered fishing communities had been using for generations. He did not displace them — he incorporated them, built around what was already working, and added the institutions that turned a trade post into a capital. Three generations later the result is a city that feels organic rather than imposed, which is rarer in imperial capitals than it should be.

**The smell of it:** Salt, timber, cooking, the sharp metallic edge of the smithing districts. Montauseau smells like work — not unpleasant, just present.

## The City Districts

**The Harbor Quarter**  
The economic engine. Rouçon vessels, coastal traders, fishing fleets, imperial naval ships — the harbor is never quiet. The dockworkers' guilds are the most powerful labor organizations in the empire; the Empress has maintained a careful working relationship with them. When ships arrive from Vossomar the entire district changes tempo — crowds gathering to see what the western traders have brought this time.

The harbormaster's office is the real power in this district day-to-day: **Harbormaster Delcot Bruyne**, a heavyset human in his fifties who has run the harbor for fifteen years and knows every ship captain, dock boss, and merchant factor by name. He is the kind of official who makes complicated things run smoothly and asks for very little in return except that people do not waste his time.

**The Imperial Quarter**  
The administrative and ceremonial heart of the city. The Empress's residence — the Maison Verdienne — sits here, a building that is large without being ostentatious, deliberately designed to project competence rather than magnificence. The Imperial Council chambers. The main court. The archive building where the court archivist maintains the empire's institutional memory.

The Imperial Quarter is not closed to ordinary citizens. The Empress walks here without heavy escort by preference. Court sessions for major disputes are public. This openness is both genuine and calculated — it maintains the trust that is the empire's most important asset.

**The Military Quarter**  
Barracks, training grounds, armories, the military command building. The standing army's Montauseau garrison is approximately 8,000 soldiers. This is not the full army — significant forces are stationed in each subordinate city and along the northern border — but 8,000 professional soldiers in the capital is a number that visiting dignitaries notice and are meant to notice.

The naval yards are adjacent — the shipbuilding operations that maintain the imperial fleet and the Vossomar trade vessels that operate under imperial charter.

**The Service Quarter**  
Where asylum seekers serve their mandatory period before citizenship. Clean, organized, not luxurious — housing, communal meals, assignment to labor or military duties, access to language instruction and imperial civic education. The service quarter has its own internal culture built by decades of new arrivals from every part of the continent and occasionally from Vossomar.

The Clank community is centered here, though those who have completed service have dispersed into other districts. A small permanent Clank presence remains — those who have completed service but feel most comfortable among others who came the same way. They run a repair workshop that has become the best mechanical services operation in the city.

**The Craft Districts**  
Smithing, weaving, shipbuilding components, woodwork, glasswork. The craft districts are organized by guild, regulated by the council, and proud of it. Montauseau craft work — particularly metalwork and shipbuilding — is renowned across the continent. The standards are enforced and the enforcement is why the standards mean something.

**The Northern Settlements**  
The outermost ring before the city proper gives way to surrounding farmland and eventually forest edge. This is where the Orc and half-Orc communities are concentrated — several villages that predate the empire's formal consolidation, incorporated rather than displaced when Montauseau expanded. They are imperial citizens, they serve in the military in notable numbers, and they have a complicated relationship with how the empire's history has treated them that is better now than it was and not yet fully resolved.

The Goblin community is smaller and keeps more to itself — generations of poor treatment across the continent have produced a community that has learned self-sufficiency and does not readily extend trust. The empire's formal policy treats them as full citizens. The informal reality varies by district and depends on individual experience. The Empress has visited twice. The visits helped. They were not sufficient.

## People

**Empress Carmine Montauseau**  
See [[04_Factions/Verdant_Crescent/L'Empire Verdienne|L'Empire Verdienne]] for full detail. Present in the city more than most rulers — she governs from here, not from a retreat. Visible. Accessible by design. Not soft.

**First Advisor Seret Callande**  
**Ancestry:** Elf-Human, age 62 | **Role:** Head of the Imperial Council, the Empress's most trusted advisor

Callande has been on the council since Carmine's father's last years and was the one advisor who supported Carmine's consolidation moves without reservation when she moved against the nobility. He is not a yes-man — he has told her she was wrong three times in ten years on specific decisions. She revised two of those decisions. On the third she was right and he acknowledged it. This is why she trusts him.

His current position on the Lumencrest question: not yet. The picture is not clear enough. He wants three more months of intelligence before advising a decision. Carmine has given him those months.

**Commander-General Varis Aumont**  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 48 | **Role:** Commander of the imperial military

The man who would run the war if there is one. Professional, precise, has led two significant military operations during Carmine's reign — one against a pirate network in the western approaches, one suppressing a provincial rebellion that never became serious because Aumont moved fast enough. He believes the moment to act against Lumencrest is approaching but has not yet arrived. He is preparing regardless.

**Trade Envoy Thessaly Renne**  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 39 | **Role:** Manager of the Rouçon relationship, western trade

Renne has crossed the Great Depths seven times. She speaks Rouçon's trading dialect fluently and understands their culture well enough to negotiate with them effectively. She is the empire's most valuable trade asset and is aware of this without being insufferable about it. Her current concern: the Rouçon delegation that arrived three months ago is asking questions about the continent's political situation that go beyond normal trade interest. Something is happening on Vossomar. She has not reported this to the council yet — she is still trying to understand what she's seeing.

## The Rouçon Connection

Montauseau's western harbor hosts a permanent Rouçon trading post — a compound of six buildings where the eastern kingdom's trade representatives live and conduct business year-round. The current senior representative, **Factor Erem Douval**, has been in Montauseau for four years and has developed genuine relationships in the city.

The trade is substantial: Vossomar produces materials not found on this continent — certain metals, plant compounds, textile techniques, navigational instruments of a sophistication that slightly embarrasses imperial engineers. In exchange the empire sends the things Vossomar does not have: Hollostar-adjacent goods, forest products, agricultural variety.

Rouçon traders find this continent fascinating and do not fully understand it. The concept of Hollowstars — objects that fall from somewhere and power cities — has no equivalent in their experience. They treat the entire subject with the polite wariness of people who suspect the locals are doing something dangerous that they don't want to get caught near.

## Adventure Hooks

**"The Factor's Question"** — Factor Erem Douval approaches the party quietly. Something is happening in Rouçon — he has received messages he cannot explain and instructions that don't make sense. He needs people who are not imperial officials to investigate what's happening on the Vossomar connection. Trade Envoy Renne is already suspicious of him. The party is caught between an imperial investigation and whatever is actually wrong.

**"Service Records"** — A Clank who completed imperial service three years ago and is now a free citizen has been trying to report something about Lumencrest's research programs for six months through official channels. The channels haven't responded. They find the party. What they know is specific, verifiable, and would change how several factions understand what Lumencrest has been doing.

**"The Northern Villages"** — A dispute in the northern Orc settlements has escalated beyond what local resolution can handle. The Empress has sent a formal mediator. The mediator has gone quiet. The party is asked to find out what happened — and discovers that what looks like a community dispute is actually something with roots in old imperial decisions that were never fully resolved.

**"The Timing Debate"** — The party, working within the empire on another matter, is present when Callande and Aumont have a disagreement that escalates into the council chamber and accidentally reveals the full scope of what the empire is planning regarding Lumencrest. Now they know. What do they do with that?
