# Geography - The Verdant Crescent

**Climate:** Temperate to warm, wet; heavy canopy cover in forest interior; coastal winds and salt air on peninsula and northern strip  
**Terrain:** Dense ancient forest interior, western coastal peninsula, northern coastal strip, river valleys, forest edge settlements  
**Population:** ~350,000 (L'Empire Verdienne ~280,000; Deep Forest & Northern ~70,000)

---

## THE THREE ZONES

The Verdant Crescent is not one landscape — it is three distinct zones that share a regional identity while operating under very different conditions.

---

### The Western Peninsula & Northern Coastal Strip

**L'Empire Verdienne territory** — the long peninsula jutting southwest into the Great Depths plus the northern coastal strip running north to the river boundary.

**Montauseau** sits at the peninsula's center — the capital, the harbor, the empire's beating heart. South of it, **Calvaire** commands the deep-water approaches. **Porteclaire** and **Draguisart** dominate western and northern maritime trade. Peninsula interior cities (**Belacroix**, **Marticourt**, **Maugueux**) handle agriculture, shipbuilding, and textiles.

**The peninsula's interior** is forested — not the Verdant Crescent's ancient forest, but dense stands of timber that the empire harvests under strict sustainability agreements. The forest becomes *the forest* the closer you travel east and north — gradual, then sudden, like a wall you didn't see coming until you're against it.

**Northern coastal strip** runs from **Varemonde** north to **Sainteloise** at the river mouth. **Covenwind** across the waters serves northern shipping. Strip narrows as it goes north — at some points the road between sea and tree line is barely wide enough for two wagons.

**Les Perles islands** — island chain south of Montauseau extending into Great Depths. **Vinnoît** (~9k, pearl diving), **Périlet** (~6k, boat-building), **Narçon** (~3k, Galapa majority). Established Galapa communities pre-dating the empire.

---

### The Deep Forest Interior

The great western forest — old, deep, governed by the Green Covenant. The canopy is so complete in the interior that the forest floor exists in permanent green twilight. At night, bioluminescent fungi and organisms make the undergrowth glow.

**The Heartgrove** sits at the center — where the Green Covenant is based. This is not imperial territory. The Green Covenant's non-interference agreement with the empire is clear: where the forest begins, the empire's authority ends.

**Deep Forest communities:**
- Simiah canopy settlements in the upper levels
- Elvish root-groves integrated with ancient trees
- Faun trails connecting sacred sites
- Goblin warrens on the forest floor
- Druid communities at various depths

**Major settlements:**
- **Mosshaven** (~8,000) - Green Covenant coordination center
- **Brighthollow** (~2,400) - Elf majority settlement
- **Rootwatch** (~1,800) - Forest edge monitoring
- **Canopy** (~1,600) - Treehouse community
- **Fernrest** (~1,200) - Forest clearing settlement
- **Stillwater Glade** (~900) - Sacred pool location

**The Ancient Waking:** Something in the deep forest is activating. Three Sacred Groves showing unprecedented bioluminescence. Animal behavior shifting. The Simiah elders noticed 8 years ago. The Green Covenant knows. Most settlements don't.

---

### The Forest Edge

The eastern border where forest meets the Central Heartlands. Human settlements operate here under Covenant-observed agreements — sustainable logging, regulated hunting, trade that benefits both sides.

**Edge settlements:** **Timberwatch** (~2,800), **Greengate** (~1,400), **Mosshaven** (~800) manage the transition between forest and open country. This is where Lumencrest pressure is most felt — the Council wants timber and the edge communities feel the squeeze.

**Northern settlements:** Beyond imperial authority but not quite deep forest. **Thornvale** (~3,200), **Greenmark** (~2,800), **Shadowpine** (~2,100), **Oakheart** (~1,400) mark the gradual transition from empire influence to forest autonomy.

**Orc communities:** **Ironbark Hold** (~800), **Grevhal** (~1,200), scattered family clans throughout northern forest

**Goblin communities:** **Undermoss** (~1,100), **Rootshade** (~600), scattered warrens throughout forest

---

## THE NORTHERN TERRITORIES

Beyond L'Empire Verdienne's formal authority:

**Emberfire** (~13,000) - Dwarven volcanic forge city in Skyspine Mountains near **Mount Ashcrown** (active volcano), ~80-90 miles from Fernrest. Geothermal forges producing exceptional metalwork.

**Swallowfield** (~10,000) - Mixed-ancestry trade hub beside **Mirrorwater Lake**, north of Deep Forest. The Circle government (seven ancestry representatives) coordinates northern trade.

**Covenwind** (~10,000) - Northern shipping center across waters from Riveaulx, handling rugged northern goods export.

**Smaller northern settlements:** Harkenbell (~7k), Swallownook (~6k), Crystalcrest (~5k), Onyxveil (~4k frigid), Swiftwind (~3k waystation), Castingbridge (~2k with Fairy presence)

---

## THE ANCIENT WAKING

Three Sacred Groves in the Deep Forest showing unprecedented activity. They form a perfect triangle on any map. The ancient waking site is at the triangle's center.

The empire has no direct knowledge of this. The Green Covenant has not shared it. The non-interference agreement means the empire does not ask questions about what happens inside the forest. This blind spot may matter.

---

## MAJOR WATERWAYS

**Internal rivers and streams** cross the forest. The Green Covenant's Warden of Waters manages the forest-marsh boundary where these drain toward the Glittering Marshes.

**Skyspine Mountains** form eastern boundary with Central Heartlands — source of rivers flowing both directions.

---

## REGIONAL RELATIONSHIPS

- **L'Empire Verdienne:** The governing power of coast and peninsula
- **The Green Covenant:** The forest's governing order, non-interference agreement with empire
- **Central Heartlands:** Eastern neighbor — edge settlements trade; Lumencrest timber pressure; empire-Lumencrest cold war
- **Northern Expanse:** Northern neighbor — Sainteloise transition point; elvish connections; refugee flows
- **Glittering Marshes:** Southern neighbor — forest meets wetland; Covenant Warden of Waters operates border
- **Vossomar (Rouçon):** Across Great Depths — empire's western trade partner via Montauseau

## THE STILLNESS

Once a year, the Deep Forest goes silent.

Not quiet — silent. No birdsong. No wind through leaves. No insect sound. No small animals moving in undergrowth. No creak of wood or rustle of branch. For exactly one day, the Verdant Crescent's Deep Forest produces no sound whatsoever, and the silence has the particular quality of something that is choosing not to speak rather than something that has nothing to say.

### What Happens

The Stillness, as Verdant Crescent residents call it, arrives in late autumn — always within a week of the same date, though the specific day varies slightly year to year. Locals know it is coming by a series of preceding signs: birds that normally winter in the Deep Forest begin moving to the forest edges two or three days before. The mycelial network that the Fungril and Simiah track goes quiet in a specific pattern, like a conversation winding down. Certain trees — the oldest ones — shift their leaves inward, a posture that has no botanical explanation but that experienced foresters recognize immediately.

On the morning of the Stillness, the silence begins at dawn and ends precisely at the following dawn. Weather does not interrupt it — storms move around the Deep Forest during the Stillness in a pattern that Seekers have documented but cannot explain. Rain falls at the forest edge. The interior stays still and dry.

### What It Does to People

Outsiders who experience the Stillness for the first time are uniformly affected. The most common description is not frightening — it is clarifying. The absence of sound creates an unusual perceptual state: attention sharpens, peripheral awareness expands, thoughts slow and become more distinct. Many people report that problems they had been unable to think through became suddenly solvable during the Stillness. Some report hearing things they cannot explain — not sounds, exactly, but impressions that function like hearing.

A smaller number of outsiders find the Stillness genuinely distressing. The silence registers as wrong to people with strong noise-orientation, and the day can produce anxiety that lingers for weeks afterward.

**Locals are not dramatically affected.** Verdant Crescent residents treat the Stillness as they treat any known annual event — with respect, appropriate preparation, and the mild impatience of people who have experienced something many times and understand its patterns. The appropriate behavior during the Stillness is to stay in the forest, move slowly, and not try to speak unnecessarily. Locals who have left the Verdant Crescent and returned often say that experiencing the Stillness again after time away is the most reliable sign they are home.

### What It Is

The Verdant Crescent's scholarly community has three positions:

**The Green Covenant's position:** The forest is alive in a way that goes beyond ecological complexity. The Stillness is the forest's equivalent of sleep, or meditation, or a state that has no good equivalent in biological terms. The forest is doing something. What it is doing is its business. The Covenant's role is to protect the conditions that allow it to continue.

**The Path of Spirits position:** The spirits of the Deep Forest gather once a year for something that could be called a council, though the word is inadequate. The Stillness is the silence that falls when an enormous number of very old presences are paying attention to something else entirely. Humans and other mortals experience the residue of that attention — the sharpened clarity — without being part of whatever is actually happening.

**The Seeker position:** Unknown. No Seeker expedition has been permitted to conduct monitoring equipment inside the Deep Forest during the Stillness. Archcovenant Mosswood has declined every request. He has not explained why.

**The truth (GM layer):** The Stillness is connected to the forest's ancient waking. It has been occurring since before any current resident can remember, but its character has been changing — the clarity effect is stronger than it was a generation ago, the preceding signs are more pronounced, and in the past three years the Simiah elders who track the mycelial network have noticed that the Stillness leaves a residue: for several days after, the network carries information it didn't carry before. Something is being processed during the Stillness. The processing is becoming more active as the forest's waking accelerates.

### The Stillness and the Current Crisis

The most recent Stillness, two months before campaign start, was the longest anyone has recorded — not by much, an hour past the expected dawn. The hour was noticed. The Archcovenant held an emergency session with the senior Wardens the following day. The session's conclusions were not shared with edge settlements or outside parties.

Pilgrim-Speaker Maret (Path of Spirits) happened to be in the Verdant Crescent for the Stillness and has been unusually quiet about what she experienced during that extra hour. She left for the Frostwell Tundra shortly after.
