# Geography and Environment - Desert of Glass

> *"The earth bleeds fire. The sky rains ash. The ground is knives. This is home."*

---

## PHYSICAL LANDSCAPE

### The Skyspine Mountains (Northern/Western Zone)

**Formation:** Ancient volcanic range, still active

**Scale:** Medium mountain range (~150 miles north-south, ~80 miles east-west)

**Key Features:**

**Active Volcanoes (4 major, 12 minor):**
- **Mount Ashfall** (tallest, most active, constant smoke plume)
- **The Twins** (two peaks, alternating eruptions every few decades)
- **Scorchpeak** (dormant for centuries, locals worry it'll wake)
- **The Furnace** (low peak, rivers of lava flow from caldera)

**Volcanic Activity Level:**
- **Non-threatening** but constant
- Minor eruptions monthly (ash, small lava flows)
- Major eruptions rare (decades between)
- Ground tremors daily (locals don't notice anymore)

**Livable Zones:**
- **Valley settlements** between peaks (cooler, sheltered)
- **Eastern slopes** (away from prevailing ash winds)
- **Geothermal caves** (warm but not deadly, Fungril thrive here)

**Resources:**
- Obsidian cliffs
- Rare mineral veins (cinnabar, sulfur, volcanic gems)
- Geothermal vents (power source for Clanks)
- Lava tubes (travel routes, defensible homes)

---

### The Transitional Zone (Central)

**Where mountains meet desert:**

**Lava Pools:**
- Massive pools of cooling magma (some miles wide)
- Surfaces crusted, molten beneath
- Geysers erupt unpredictably
- **Deadly:** Step on thin crust = death

**The Obsidian Fields:**
- Square miles of black volcanic glass
- Jutting shards (10-30 feet tall)
- Labyrinthine (easy to get lost)
- Highly reflective (sunlight blinds travelers)

**Unstable Ground:**
- Sinkholes into lava tubes
- Sudden venting (steam/gas explosions)
- Shifting terrain (cooling contracts rock)

**Settlements:**
- Very few (too dangerous)
- Nomadic Drakona camps (move frequently)
- Prospector outposts (temporary, risky)

---

### The Glass Flats (Southern/Eastern Zone)

**Formation:** Ancient mega-eruption created this

**Scale:** Vast (~200 miles east-west, ~120 miles north-south)

**Appearance:**
- Black volcanic glass plains
- Smooth in places, shattered in others
- Reflective surface (mirror-like when clean)
- Ash dunes accumulated over centuries

**Key Features:**

**The Mirror Sea:**
- Flattest section (perfectly smooth glass, 50 miles across)
- Reflection disorients travelers (sky and ground identical)
- Sacred to some Drakona tribes (they see visions in reflections)

**The Shardfields:**
- Glass shattered into blade-like fragments
- Impassable without protective footwear
- Cuts through leather, cloth, even thin metal
- Produces haunting sounds when wind blows through

**Scattered Lava Pools:**
- Cooling but still molten
- Miles apart (oases of danger)
- Some creatures live near them (heat-dependent life)

**Ash Dunes:**
- Accumulated volcanic ash
- Black "sand" dunes (but sharper, heavier than sand)
- Shift in windstorms
- Bury ruins, roads, unwary travelers

## CLIMATE & WEATHER

### Temperature

**Skyspine Mountains:**
- **Cooler valleys:** 80-100°F (livable for most ancestries)
- **Near lava:** 120-150°F (only fire-adapted survive)
- **Night:** Drops to 60-70°F (rapid cooling)

**Glass Flats:**
- **Day:** 110-130°F average, 140-160°F in summer
- **Night:** 70-90°F (glass retains heat)
- **Record:** 180°F recorded in Mirror Sea (lethal to most)

**Survival:**
- Drakona and Infernis are naturally heat-resistant — both thrive in high-temperature environments that would harm most other ancestries
- Fungril adapted (fire-resistant strain)
- Clanks immune (mechanical)
- Others require water, shade, magic, or die

### Precipitation

**Rare:**
- Rain almost never (maybe once every few years)
- When it does rain, glass steams violently (dangerous)

**Moisture Sources:**
- Geothermal condensation (in caves)
- Imported water (caravans, expensive)
- Fungril cultivation (they create moisture)

### Wind

**Ash Winds:**
- Prevailing west-to-east (from volcanoes toward desert)
- Carry volcanic ash constantly
- Breathing without protection = lung damage

**Glass Storms:**
- Rare but deadly
- High winds lift glass shards (become airborne razors)
- Shred exposed flesh, destroy tents, kill livestock
- Warning signs: Sudden stillness, metallic taste in air, vibrations

### Ash Clouds

**From active volcanoes:**
- Blanket region periodically
- Reduce visibility to feet
- Accumulate on surfaces (collapse roofs)
- Toxic if inhaled long-term

## NATURAL RESOURCES

### Obsidian & Volcanic Glass

**Abundance:** Massive deposits

**Uses:**
- **Weapons:** Obsidian blades (sharper than steel, more brittle)
- **Tools:** Cutting implements, arrowheads
- **Art:** Sculptures, jewelry (beautiful when polished)
- **Magic:** Some mages claim obsidian amplifies spells
- **Construction:** Obsidian bricks (heat-resistant buildings)

**Quality Variations:**
- **Black obsidian:** Most common
- **Mahogany obsidian:** Red-brown, rare, premium
- **Rainbow obsidian:** Iridescent, extremely rare, valuable
- **Snowflake obsidian:** White patterns, decorative

**Export:**
- Major trade good
- Caravans carry obsidian to [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]], [[02_Regions/Verdant_Crescent/Verdant_Crescent_Index|Verdant Crescent]]
- Dwarven miners control best quarries

### Rare Minerals

**Cinnabar (Mercury Ore):**
- Found in volcanic veins
- Alchemical component (mercury extraction)
- Toxic (mining is dangerous)
- Valuable export

**Sulfur:**
- Deposits around fumaroles
- Used in alchemy, explosives, medicine
- Pungent smell (miners develop immunity)

**Volcanic Gems:**
- **Fire opals:** Red-orange, highly prized
- **Garnets:** Deep red, semi-precious
- **Peridot:** Olive-green, formed in lava
- Rare, sought by jewelers

**Rare Earth Minerals:**
- Unnamed ores (unique to region)
- Alchemists experiment with them
- Some have magical properties (unconfirmed)

### Geothermal Energy

**Natural Power Source:**
- **Vents and hot springs** produce steam
- **Clanks harness this** (mechanical turbines, experimental)
- **Limited technology** (Lumencrest has better, but Clanks independent)
- **Potential:** Vast untapped energy

### Hollostar Shards

**Scattered throughout:**
- Ancient atmospheric explosions rained shards
- Embedded in volcanic glass, obsidian, cooled lava
- **Rare but findable** (prospectors search constantly)

**Value:**
- Extremely high (see [[Hollowstars]])
- Black market thriving
- Clanks seek them (power sources for independence)

## FLORA & FAUNA

### Plants (Fire-Adapted)

**Ashroot:** Hardy plant in ash dunes; bitter but edible. Fungril cultivate it.

**Embergrass:** Red-tipped grass near lava; alchemical uses.

**Lavalichen:** Grows on cooling lava; bioluminescent; toxic if eaten.

**Scorchwood Trees:** Rare, geothermal valleys; black bark; wood doesn't burn easily.

**Fungril Gardens:** Fire-adapted mushrooms; glow-caps, heat-spore mushrooms.

### Animals (Extremophiles)

**Lava Beetles, Ashwings, Scorchscales, Emberhounds, Glass Serpents** — heat-adapted; some dangerous (glass serpents venomous).

### Monsters & Magical Creatures

**Fire Elementals** (near lava), **Magma Oozes** (lava tubes), **Ashwraiths** (during ash storms — legends say victims of ancient eruption).

---

## NATURAL HAZARDS

**Volcanic Eruptions** (minor monthly, major rare). **Glass Storms** (airborne razors; seek shelter). **Heat exhaustion** (constant; travel at night, water essential). **Lava pool accidents** (thin crusts, geysers). **Toxic gases** (fumaroles). **Sinkholes** (cooling lava creates voids).

---

## ENVIRONMENTAL BENEFITS

**Natural defenses;** freedom from authority; rare resources; geothermal energy; Hollostar shards; testing ground for Clank tech and alchemists.

## CINDRATH'S DREAMING

Cindrath the Ancient is not always awake.

She sleeps for long periods — months, sometimes years — in her lair deep in the Skyspine volcanic region. Her sleeping body fills a lava chamber that has shaped itself around her over millennia. When she sleeps, the Desert of Glass changes.

### The Volcanic Response

The Skyspine volcanoes are not independent geological systems. They are, in some way that no scholar has fully mapped, connected to Cindrath. When she is awake, they are relatively stable — active, but patterned, predictable in the way that experienced Desert of Glass residents understand. When she sleeps, the pattern changes.

**Eruption cycles shift.** Vents that are quiet during her waking periods become active during her sleep. Lava flows take different paths. The Glass Flats are, in the most literal sense, a record of where lava has flowed during thousands of years of Cindrath's sleeping cycles — the obsidian formations map her dreams the way a tide chart maps the moon.

**The air changes.** During her deep sleep, something comes off the volcanic activity that is not simply heat or sulfur — a trace element, a quality, something that settles into the lungs of people who spend time in the Desert of Glass during these periods. Long-term residents develop a faint amber tint to their irises. Newcomers don't notice it happening. After about six months in the desert during an active dreaming period, it's visible. It fades slowly if they leave. It doesn't fade entirely.

Locals call it the Ashmark. It is not harmful. It is considered, in Desert of Glass culture, a sign that the desert has accepted you.

### The Shared Dreams

People who sleep near the Skyspine during Cindrath's dreaming periods dream what she dreams.

Not completely — not the full experience of whatever a several-thousand-year-old dragon processes during sleep. But fragments. Images. Impressions of scale and heat and enormous spans of time. The feeling of flying over a landscape that doesn't match any current geography. Fire that isn't destructive but generative.

**Residents are accustomed to this.** Cinderhome dwarves, Drakona communities near the Skyspine, Infernis in Ashenveil — they have all grown up with Cindrath's dream-fragments in their sleep. The dreams are considered unremarkable, sometimes pleasant, occasionally informative. The cultural practice of dream-sharing (comparing what you saw, finding the common elements that confirm you were all in the same place) has developed specifically around Cindrath's dreaming periods.

**Outsiders find it profoundly disorienting.** Travelers who camp near the Skyspine and don't know what's happening often leave believing they've experienced something supernatural or prophetic. They're not entirely wrong.

**What the dreams contain** varies by how deeply Cindrath is sleeping. Surface sleep produces landscape images — the desert as it was, as it is, in fragments without narrative. Deep sleep produces something else: sequences that seem to show intention, decision, choice. The Drakona who interpret these sequences believe Cindrath is, in some fundamental sense, dreaming the desert into its future form. The more scholarly-minded believe she is simply processing accumulated memory. Both interpretations are consistent with the observable content.

### The Current Situation

Cindrath has been asleep for three years — an unusually long cycle. The volcanic activity has been consequently unusual: two vents that have been dormant for sixty years have become active, and the Glass Flats are seeing new obsidian formation at a rate that has filled in sections of previously passable desert.

**The dreams have been different during this cycle.** The common elements that Desert of Glass residents compare each morning have shifted: less landscape imagery, more sequences that feel like preparation. The Drakona interpreters say she is dreaming about arrival — something coming that she has been expecting for a very long time.

Three weeks ago, Cindrath moved in her sleep. She has never done this in any documented dreaming period. She shifted her position slightly, like someone adjusting in a chair. The volcanic activity spiked for about six hours afterward and then returned to its previous pattern.

Nobody has told the outside world about this. It is Desert of Glass business.

**When she wakes** — which will happen before the fourth fall, by most Desert of Glass residents' reckoning — the first thing she will do is look at the sky. She always does. She looks at the sky, she assesses whatever has changed while she was sleeping, and then she decides what, if anything, she intends to do about it. Nobody knows what she will decide. She doesn't announce her decisions in advance.
