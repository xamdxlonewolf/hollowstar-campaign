# Cinderhome (Karak-Vardum)

**Population:** ~12,000 (95% Dwarf, 5% others)  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Desert_of_Glass/Desert_of_Glass_Index|Desert of Glass]]  
**Location:** Skyspine Volcanic Region, Mount Ashfall eastern slopes  
**Original Name:** Karak-Vardum ("Fire-Heart-Hold" in Dwarven)  
**Economy:** Volcanic mining, rare mineral extraction, obsidian trade  
**Government:** Mine-Master Council (3 master miners elected by workers)  
**Religion:** Forge-Father Faith (ancient dwarven religion, nearly extinct elsewhere)

---

## OVERVIEW

Cinderhome is the last true dwarven city — a place where ancient traditions, old festivals, and the Forge-Father Faith are kept alive while the rest of the dwarven world modernizes and compromises. Built into Mount Ashfall's eastern slopes, these 12,000 dwarves mine volcanic riches while maintaining dwarven culture as their ancestors practiced it.

**The tragedy that defines them:** 400 years ago, the original settlement Karak-Vardum was destroyed by massive volcanic eruption. 2,000 dwarves died. The survivors rebuilt on safer ground but refused to use the old name — Karak-Vardum died with those who lived there. They call it "Cinderhome" now, acknowledging they live in the cinders of what was.

**The pride:** After losing everything, survivors dedicated themselves to preserving true dwarven soul. Other holds might compromise with surface ways, adopt foreign religions, modernize traditions. Not here. Cinderhome keeps the old ways alive. They are what dwarves should be.

---

## HISTORY

### Karak-Vardum Era (600-400 Years Ago)

The original settlement was higher on Mount Ashfall, closer to richer mineral veins. Founded by three clans seeking fortune in volcanic mountains, it prospered for 200 years. Population grew to ~2,000. They believed they understood the mountain, controlled the danger, mastered the fire.

They were wrong.

### The Catastrophe (400 Years Ago)

Without warning, Mount Ashfall erupted catastrophically. The entire settlement was buried under volcanic debris. Nearly everyone died — only those away on trading caravans or working outlying mines survived. ~200 dwarves out of 2,000.

**The trauma:** The mountain betrayed them. They'd lived there safely for generations, then sudden annihilation. Survivor guilt, rage at the mountain, despair at loss — these shaped everything that followed.

### The Rebuilding (400-300 Years Ago)

Survivors refused to abandon the mountain (valuable minerals, clan pride, nowhere else to go). They rebuilt on eastern slopes — farther from peak, better ash protection, more defensible position.

**The name change:** Using "Karak-Vardum" would dishonor the dead. That name died with them. "Cinderhome" was humble memorial — "we live in cinders of what was, never forgetting who fell."

**The cultural preservation:** Rebuilding, survivors decided: We lost the hold but we won't lose our soul. Old traditions, old religion, old clan ways — these must survive even if Karak-Vardum didn't.

### The Pride Era (300 Years Ago to Present)

As Cinderhome prospered again, cultural identity strengthened. Other dwarven holds adopted Six-Fold Faith, traded with surface folk, compromised traditions. Cinderhome held firm. They became keeper of old ways — the last true dwarves.

**Current status:** 12,000 population, thriving economy, proud culture. They survived catastrophe and stayed dwarven. This is their identity.

## THE FORGE-FATHER FAITH

**The ancient dwarven religion:** Predates Six-Fold Faith, First Flame, all modern religions. Centers on The Forge-Father — the first dwarf, teacher of metalworking, ancestor-deity who showed dwarves how to master stone and fire.

### Core Beliefs

**The Forge-Father:**
- First dwarf, emerged from living stone
- Discovered fire, learned to shape metal
- Taught these secrets to his children (all dwarves)
- Ascended by crafting perfect work, became divine
- Watches over dwarves through forge-fire

**Sacred principles:**
- Fire and stone are holy — combining them in forge is prayer
- Craft is worship — making something well honors Forge-Father
- Quality over quantity — shoddy work insults ancestors
- Clan is sacred — family bonds mirror Forge-Father's children
- Ancestors watch — the dead judge our work and character

**Worship:**
- Temples are working forges where priests craft while teaching
- Beginning major work, smiths invoke: "Forge-Father guide my hands"
- Masterwork pieces dedicated to Forge-Father
- Ancestor veneration integrated — all dwarves descended from Forge-Father

### Why Mostly Extinct Elsewhere

**Six-Fold Faith absorbed most dwarves:** Centuries ago, The Architect aspect appealed to dwarven values (order, craft, structure). Most holds converted or blended faiths. Forge-Father Faith faded to cultural memory.

**Modern pragmatism:** Dwarven holds trading with surface cities found Six-Fold Faith easier — shared religion meant better relations. Forge-Father Faith isolated them.

**Seen as old-fashioned:** By other dwarves, Forge-Father Faith represents past. Quaint, outdated, limiting.

### Why Alive in Cinderhome

**The tragedy cemented it:** Losing Karak-Vardum made survivors cling to dwarven identity. Forge-Father Faith was distinctly dwarven — rejecting it meant losing more than they already had.

**Pride in purity:** Cinderhome sees themselves as last true dwarves. Maintaining Forge-Father Faith proves this. Other holds compromised; they stayed pure.

**Volcanic environment feels appropriate:** Working forges while volcano burns overhead feels spiritually right. Fire and stone surround them constantly.

**Children raised in the faith:** Every generation taught old ways. Festival calendar, prayers, forge-blessings — these are normal here, exotic elsewhere.

### Forge-Father Festivals

**Firstfire Festival (midwinter):**
- Commemorates Forge-Father's discovery of fire
- All forges extinguished, then relit from single sacred flame
- Community feast, stories of Forge-Father's teachings
- Young dwarves take forge-oaths (commit to learning craft)

**Hammerstrike Day (spring):**
- Celebrates first metal ever shaped
- Communal projects — entire city works on shared creation
- Competition: Best piece crafted that day wins honor
- Winners' work dedicated to Forge-Father, displayed in temple

**Ancestor's Vigil (autumn):**
- Honor all dead, especially Karak-Vardum victims
- Names of 2,000 lost read aloud in temple (takes hours)
- Forges burn all night, vigil maintained
- Masterwork pieces created in ancestors' memory

**The Founding (anniversary of Karak-Vardum establishment):**
- Bittersweet — celebrates original founding, mourns its loss
- Tell stories of old hold, remember what was
- Recommit to preserving dwarven culture
- Youngest generation learns history from eldest

## GEOGRAPHY & LAYOUT

**Built into mountainside:** Not surface town — carved into eastern slopes of Mount Ashfall across three vertical levels descending into rock. Surface entrances protected by stone overhangs from ash fall.

**Why eastern slopes:** Prevailing winds blow ash west. Eastern side gets less ashfall, more livable. Still harsh but manageable.

**The defensive position:** After Karak-Vardum's destruction, survivors chose site they could evacuate quickly. Multiple exits, emergency tunnels, evacuation routes planned. Never again trapped.

## THE THREE HALLS

### Upper Hall (Surface Level, ~3,000 residents)

**Character:** Interface between Cinderhome and outside world

Where surface meets stone. Trading post, caravan facilities, outsider accommodations. Non-dwarves rarely go deeper than this.

**Key locations:**
- **The Cinder Gate** - Main entrance, massive stone doors (can seal against volcanic flow)
- **Trader's Plaza** - Where external merchants conduct business
- **The Molten Mug** - Tavern (popular with traders, serves dwarven dark beer)
- **Residential quarters** - Family halls carved into stone
- **The Temple of the Forge-Father** - Main temple, working forge where priests teach

**The temple:** Stone hall with central forge burning constantly. Priest-smiths work while congregation observes, learns, prays through craft. Masterwork pieces dedicated here displayed on walls.

**Outsider experience:** Surface folk tolerated in Upper Hall, provided with necessities, but subtle pressure to conclude business and leave. Dwarves are polite but make clear this is dwarven space.

### Middle Hall (Mid-Level, ~6,000 residents)

**Character:** Heart of daily dwarven life

Main residential district, workshops, communal spaces. This is where Cinderhome actually lives.

**Key locations:**
- **The Great Forge District** - Hundreds of workshops using geothermal heat
- **Clan Halls** - Each major clan maintains hall (meetings, records, ceremonies)
- **The Mushroom Caves** - Geothermal caverns growing food
- **Market Street** - Where dwarves trade among themselves
- **Guild Quarter** - Miners' Guild, Smiths' Guild, Stoneworkers' Guild halls

**The forges:** Utilize volcanic heat through geothermal vents. Constant temperature, no fuel needed, perfect for continuous work. Creates distinctive Cinderhome metalwork — volcanic-forged.

**Family life:** Extended families live in carved halls (multi-generational). Children learn clan trades from parents. Every family maintains small forge for daily work, teaching.

**Clan structure:** Three original survivor clans (Cinderforge, Ashdelver, Stonehand) dominate, but dozen smaller clans exist. Clan membership determines social position, work assignments, marriage prospects.

### Lower Hall (Deep Level, ~3,000 residents)

**Character:** Mining heart and deep refuge

Where miners live close to work, where ore processing happens, where deepest forges burn. Also serves as emergency shelter if surface becomes uninhabitable.

**Key locations:**
- **Mine access tunnels** - Leading to extensive volcanic mining network
- **Ore processing facilities** - Separate valuable minerals from volcanic rock
- **The Deep Forges** - Hottest forges, closest to magma (for special work)
- **Deep Residences** - Miners who prefer living underground
- **Emergency stores** - Food, water, supplies for long-term shelter

**The miners:** Toughest dwarves, even by Cinderhome standards. Work dangerous volcanic mines daily. High casualty rate (cave-ins, gas, heat) but high pay. Miners are respected, feared, slightly pitied.

**The deep culture:** Miners maintain own traditions — songs sung in mines, superstitions about volcanic spirits, rituals before descending. Part Forge-Father Faith, part miner pragmatism.

## GOVERNANCE

### Mine-Master Council

Three master miners elected by workforce for 10-year terms. Must have minimum 50 years mining experience to qualify.

**Current Masters:**

**Thorin Cinderforge (267, male) - Chief Mine-Master:**
- Third term, respected safety advocate
- Survived Karak-Vardum as child (memory shapes everything)
- Prioritizes preventing another catastrophe
- Conservative on expansion, aggressive on safety protocols
- Clan Cinderforge patriarch

**Greta Ashdelver (189, female) - Expansion Advocate:**
- First term, brilliant but aggressive
- Discovered three major mineral veins through risky prospecting
- Pushes for deeper mining, richer resources
- Argues over-caution costs prosperity
- Clan Ashdelver rising star

**Borin Stonehand (312, male) - Traditionalist:**
- Second term, keeper of old ways
- High Priest of Forge-Father Faith (dual role)
- Opposes risky ventures, foreign influence, cultural compromise
- Most conservative Council member
- Clan Stonehand elder

**Decision-making:**
- Unanimous required for major changes (new mines, external treaties, cultural policy)
- Majority (2 of 3) for routine operations
- Deadlock common (Thorin and Greta often oppose each other, Borin breaks ties)

**Philosophy:** What's good for mines is good for Cinderhome. Profit matters but safety comes first. Dead miners don't produce ore. Cultural preservation outweighs convenience.

## ECONOMY

### Mining Operations

**What they extract:**

**Rare minerals:**
- **Cinnabar** (mercury ore, valuable for alchemy and Lumencrest technology)
- **Volcanic gems** (fire opals, garnets, pyrope, unique heat-formed stones)
- **Sulfur** (industrial applications, alchemical uses)
- **Rare earths** (specific volcanic deposits found nowhere else)

**Obsidian:** Highest quality volcanic glass. Sharper than steel, holds edge, multiple applications. Ashenveil produces better but Cinderhome produces more.

**Volcanic gold:** Gold deposits formed by volcanic processes. High purity, distinctive coloration.

**Why valuable:** These minerals command premium prices across continent. Lumencrest needs cinnabar and rare earths for technology. Jewelers want volcanic gems. Alchemists need sulfur. Everyone wants gold.

### Export System

**The Caravan Route:**
- Quarterly caravans carry minerals to The Saddle (neutral trading hub)
- From Saddle, goods distribute across continent
- Return caravans bring food, timber, cloth, luxuries

**Trade partners:**
- **Lumencrest:** Biggest customer (cinnabar, rare earths essential for failing technology)
- **Saltmere:** Secondary market (gems, obsidian, gold)
- **L'Empire Verdienne:** Maritime trade (volcanic gems popular with nobility)
- **Ironhold:** Friendly but competitive (buy specific ores they don't mine)

**Trade balance:** Cinderhome exports high-value minerals, imports everything else. Food, timber, textiles — all imported. Mushroom cultivation provides some food but insufficient.

### Self-Sufficiency Limitations

**What they grow:** Extensive mushroom farms in geothermal caves. Provides ~30% of food needs.

**What they import:** Grains, vegetables, meat, fruit, timber, cloth, tools they don't forge, luxuries.

**The vulnerability:** If trade routes cut, Cinderhome struggles. They could survive on mushrooms and mining but life would be harsh. This creates incentive to maintain external relationships despite cultural isolationism.

## CULTURE & DAILY LIFE

### Dwarven Pride

**Core identity:** "We are what dwarves should be."

After Karak-Vardum fell, survivors made covenant: We'll preserve true dwarven culture. Other holds might compromise, adopt surface ways, forget ancestors. Not us. We remember.

**What this means:**
- Forge-Father Faith maintained as primary religion
- Old festivals celebrated as ancestors celebrated them
- Dwarven language preserved (many holds use Common more now)
- Clan laws maintained (inheritance, marriage, justice)
- Traditional crafting methods taught (hand-tools, not powered machinery)

**The judgment:** Cinderhome dwarves view other holds as compromised, softened, losing dwarven soul. They're polite but internally superior. "We stayed true. They weakened."

**Outsider perception:** Other dwarves see Cinderhome as stubborn, old-fashioned, excessively proud. Some admire them (keeping traditions alive). Others find them annoying (self-righteous, inflexible).

### Daily Life

**Work schedule:** Early wake (before dawn), forge work or mining until midday meal, afternoon work, evening rest. Six-day week, seventh day (Forgeday) for rest, family, worship.

**Forgeday observance:** No mining (mountain rests). Families attend temple, priests craft while teaching. Young dwarves practice skills. Communal meals. Stories of ancestors told.

**Heat adaptation:** Living on active volcano requires preparation. Ash masks (worn constantly outdoors), heat-resistant clothing, knowledge of evacuation routes, constant volcanic monitoring.

**Volcanic respect:** Mount Ashfall killed their ancestors. They never forget. Daily seismic readings, gas vent monitoring, lava flow tracking. When warning signs appear, they evacuate immediately (no debate, no hesitation).

### Social Structure

**Clan hierarchy:**
- **The Three** (Cinderforge, Ashdelver, Stonehand - original survivor clans) - Highest status
- **The Sworn** (clans that swore oaths to The Three after catastrophe) - Middle status
- **The Adopted** (non-dwarves or clanless granted provisional membership) - Lowest status

**Profession hierarchy:**
- **Master smiths** (highest - they create lasting works)
- **Master miners** (respected - they find the materials)
- **Priests** (honored - they maintain faith)
- **Skilled crafters** (valued - they serve community)
- **Laborers** (necessary - they support others)

**Respect earned through:** Quality of work, years of service, clan reputation, religious devotion, survival of hardships.

**Mobility:** Possible through excellence. Superb craftsman from lower clan can earn high respect. But clan membership still matters — Adopted dwarf can become master smith but still Adopted.

### Family & Children

**Extended families:** Three or four generations live together in carved family halls. Grandparents teach traditions, parents work, children learn.

**Children raised communal:** All adults in clan help raise all children. "It takes a clan to forge a dwarf."

**Education:** Begins age 10, continues until 30 (dwarven maturity). Learn clan trade, Forge-Father Faith, dwarven history, mining safety, volcano survival.

**Coming of age (30 years old):**
- **The Fire-Walk:** Cross designated volcanic flow (crusted, mostly safe, still dangerous)
- **The First Forge:** Create first independent work (judged by masters)
- **The Oath:** Swear clan loyalty and Forge-Father dedication publicly

**Marriage:** Traditionally arranged between clans (strengthens bonds, political alliances). Love matches accepted but less common. Marriages blessed by Forge-Father priests in temple ceremony.

### Food & Drink

**Mushroom-based diet:** Multiple varieties grown in geothermal caves. Mushroom bread, mushroom stew, dried mushrooms, pickled mushrooms. Dwarves here genuinely enjoy fungi (outsiders struggle).

**Imported foods:** Grains, dried meats, preserved vegetables arrive via caravan. Expensive, rationed, appreciated.

**Dwarven Dark Beer:** Brewed from imported grains and local fungi. Strong, bitter, beloved. Brewing is sacred craft (Forge-Father first brewer, legend says).

**The Molten Mug specialty:** "Cinderbrew" - dark beer heated with hot stone, served steaming. Warms body after cold mining shift.

**Feast days:** Festival days feature imported delicacies (meat, fresh vegetables, fruits). These are special occasions, demonstrating prosperity despite volcanic environment.

### Arts & Crafts

**Volcanic metalwork:** Distinctive style using geothermal forge heat. Produces metal with unique properties (heat-tempered, slightly more resilient).

**Obsidian carving:** Cinderhome produces excellent obsidian work (blades, tools, sculpture). Not Ashenveil quality but very good.

**Stone sculpture:** Carved directly from volcanic rock. Clan halls feature ancestor statues, historical reliefs, religious imagery.

**Jewelry:** Volcanic gems set in gold. Traditional dwarven geometric patterns. Each piece tells story (clan history, personal achievement, religious devotion).

**Smithing as art:** Every masterwork piece is art. Function is prerequisite; beauty is achievement. Forge-Father Faith teaches this: "A blade that works but isn't beautiful dishonors the maker."

## THE MINES

### The Volcanic Network

**Extensive tunnels:** Hundreds of miles of passages following mineral veins through Mount Ashfall. Some natural (lava tubes), most carved by dwarven labor over 400 years.

**Three depth levels:**
- **Upper Mines** (safest, depleting): Original veins, mostly exhausted, some small-scale work
- **Middle Mines** (active, productive): Current primary operations, richest active veins
- **Deep Mines** (dangerous, promising): Newest expansions, richest deposits, highest danger

**The expansion debate:** Greta Ashdelver pushes for aggressive deep mining (highest value). Thorin Cinderforge resists (too dangerous). Borin Stonehand supports Thorin (risking lives for greed dishonors ancestors). Council deadlocked.

### Mining Dangers

**Volcanic activity:** Unpredictable magma movement, sudden gas releases, extreme heat, lava intrusions.

**Structural collapse:** Volcanic rock is unstable. Cave-ins common. Mining requires constant shoring, careful assessment, quick evacuation skills.

**Toxic gases:** Sulfur dioxide, carbon monoxide, other volcanic gases accumulate in poorly ventilated areas. Miners carry detection equipment, evacuate when sensors trigger.

**Heat stress:** Deep mines exceed 120°F. Even dwarves struggle. Shifts limited to 4 hours, frequent breaks required.

**The casualty rate:** ~20 miners die annually (out of ~3,000 mining workforce). Families compensated, names added to memorial, but loss is constant reality.

### Mining Culture

**The songs:** Miners sing while working. Rhythm coordinates labor, sound warns of danger (echoes change when structure threatens collapse), songs honor ancestors.

**Superstitions:** Never mention cave-in directly (say "the mountain speaks"). Always leave offering at shaft entrance (small food, drink for "mountain spirits"). Whistle in tunnels invites disaster.

**Brotherhood:** Miners depend on each other for survival. Bonds formed underground transcend clan rivalries. "What happens in the deep stays in the deep" — miners keep each other's secrets.

**The First Descent:** New miners blessed by Forge-Father priest, given protective amulet, escorted by experienced miners. First shift is ceremonial — limited work, extensive teaching, building confidence.

## MILITARY & DEFENSE

**No standing army:** Cinderhome doesn't maintain military force. Volcanic environment is defense — attacking them means braving mountains, ash, heat, unpredictable eruptions.

**Civilian militia:** All adult dwarves trained in basic defense. In emergency, population mobilizes. ~8,000 potential fighters (though most are workers, not warriors).

**Defensive advantages:**
- **Location:** Attacking requires approaching through volcanic wasteland
- **Fortifications:** Stone walls, sealed gates, defensible tunnels
- **Knowledge:** Dwarves know evacuation routes, hidden passages, emergency protocols
- **Evacuation:** If overwhelmed, entire population can retreat into deep mines (sealed tunnel network impossible to follow without guides)

**Weapons:** Every dwarf owns axe or hammer (tool and weapon). Clan halls maintain armories (spears, shields, crossbows). Quality is excellent (dwarven-forged) but quantity is limited (they're miners, not soldiers).

**Historical conflicts:** Cinderhome has never been militarily attacked. Remote location, harsh environment, limited strategic value (minerals are valuable but not worth invasion cost) keeps them safe.

**Current threat level:** Low. Neighbors (The Saddle, other volcanic settlements) are traders not threats. Major powers (Lumencrest, Saltmere) are customers not enemies.

## CURRENT TENSIONS

### The Expansion Debate

**Greta Ashdelver's position:** Deep mines contain richest veins. Not mining them wastes opportunity. Modern techniques make risks manageable. Prosperity requires boldness.

**Thorin Cinderforge's counter:** Karak-Vardum died from overconfidence. Deep mines are barely stable. One catastrophic failure and we lose everything again. Safety over profit.

**Borin Stonehand's support:** Agrees with Thorin. Ancestors died because we pushed too hard. Greed killed them. Honoring their memory means restraint.

**Current status:** Council deadlocked 2-1 against expansion. Greta building support among younger miners (they want prosperity, upward mobility). Thorin supported by older generation (they remember loss). Vote coming within year.

**The stakes:** Richest deposits vs. existential risk. Wrong choice could destroy Cinderhome.

### Cultural Isolation vs. Trade Necessity

**The contradiction:** Cinderhome prides itself on cultural purity, minimal outside influence. But economy depends on external trade.

**Tension points:**
- Some younger dwarves want more contact with outside world (curiosity, ambition, romance)
- Elders resist (exposure to surface ways threatens traditions)
- Traders bring foreign ideas, fashions, religions into Upper Hall
- How much interaction is necessary vs. how much is compromise?

**Borin's solution:** Restrict foreign contact to Upper Hall, prohibit outsiders from Middle/Lower Halls, maintain cultural quarantine.

**Greta's counter:** Isolation weakens us. Learning from others doesn't mean abandoning traditions. We can trade ideas without losing soul.

**Current status:** Unofficial compromise — limited contact permitted but monitored. But pressure building.

### The Forge-Father Faith Question

**Other dwarves converting:** Some Cinderhome dwarves exposed to Six-Fold Faith find it appealing. The Architect aspect aligns with dwarven values. Modern religion might offer advantages.

**Borin's response:** Apostasy. Converting is betraying ancestors. Forge-Father Faith defines Cinderhome. Lose religion, lose identity.

**The converts' position:** We can be dwarven and worship The Architect. Faith evolves. Ancestors would understand adapting.

**Current status:** ~100 dwarves (less than 1%) practice Six-Fold Faith secretly. Borin knows but can't stop them without creating martyrs. He pressures socially (converts excluded from certain clan activities) but can't forbid it.

**The fear:** If conversion spreads, Cinderhome becomes just another hold. The thing that makes them special — last keepers of old ways — dies.

### Lumencrest Dependency

**The problem:** Lumencrest is largest customer. Their crisis increases demand (they need rare earths desperately for failing Heart). This creates leverage and vulnerability.

**Lumencrest's ask:** Increased production, preferential pricing, maybe allowing Lumencrest engineers to "assist" mining operations.

**Cinderhome's concern:** Too much dependence on one customer. If Lumencrest collapses, major market disappears. If they demand too much, what happens when Cinderhome refuses?

**Current status:** Thorin negotiating carefully. Increase production moderately, charge premium prices, refuse engineers access (no outsiders in mines). Balancing profit against independence.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Deep Mine Disaster:** Collapse in deep mines traps miners. Party hired to rescue survivors before air runs out or magma breaks through. Navigate unstable tunnels, evacuate wounded, discover what caused collapse (accident or sabotage?).

**The Apostate Crisis:** Prominent dwarf (clan leader's child?) converts to Six-Fold Faith publicly. Crisis threatens to fracture community. Party mediates between traditionalists and converts before violence erupts.

**The Lumencrest Deal:** Lumencrest offers massive payment for exclusive mineral rights and access to mines. Council divided — profit vs. independence. Party investigates: What's Lumencrest really after? Is it worth the price?

**The Forge-Father Relic:** Ancient artifact from original Karak-Vardum discovered in deep mines (survived volcanic burial). Multiple factions want it — temple (religious significance), clans (ancestral claim), collectors (monetary value). Party must decide who deserves it.

**The Expansion Vote:** Council voting on deep mine expansion. Party hired by faction (Greta wants evidence expansion is safe, Thorin wants evidence it's dangerous, Borin wants evidence either way). Investigation reveals mining secrets, clan politics, personal agendas.

**The Caravan Defense:** Cinderhome's quarterly caravan to The Saddle carrying year's mineral wealth. Bandits, rival powers, or desperate groups target it. Party must defend/escort/recover valuable cargo through volcanic wasteland.

**The Cultural Exchange:** Foreign scholar wants to study Forge-Father Faith (academic research, genuine interest). Borin suspicious (stealing secrets? Converting dwarves?). Party escorts scholar, ensures good intentions, perhaps learns something about faith's origins.

**The Volcanic Prophecy:** Seismic readings suggest major eruption coming (like one that destroyed Karak-Vardum). Party investigates — is warning accurate? Can disaster be prevented? Should Cinderhome evacuate or is it false alarm? Time pressure, incomplete information, lives hanging on decision.
