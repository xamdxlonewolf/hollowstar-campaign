# The Shardkeep

**Population:** ~200 (fluctuates with expeditions)  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Desert_of_Glass/Desert_of_Glass_Index|Desert of Glass]]  
**Location:** Edge of Glass Flats, fortified waystation  
**Economy:** Expedition support, glass harvesting, survival services  
**Government:** The Keeper (single administrator appointed by major settlements)  
**Unique:** Only permanent settlement on Glass Flats edge

---

## OVERVIEW

The Shardkeep is fortified waystation at Glass Flats edge. It exists for one purpose: keep people alive who venture onto deadly glass plains. Expeditions gather here, stock supplies, hire guides, hope they return.

**Why it exists:** Glass Flats are deadly (heat, dehydration, cutting glass, navigation hazards). Without waystation offering supplies and rescue coordination, casualty rate would be 100%.

**The name:** "Shard" (volcanic glass) + "keep" (fortified position, military term).

---

## THE LOCATION

**Edge position:** Built where volcanic mountains transition to Glass Flats. Last solid ground before glass begins.

**Fortified:** Stone walls (protection from sandstorms, occasional bandits), secured water supply, defensible position.

**The Flats view:** From walls, you see vast expanse of black glass extending to horizon. Beautiful, terrifying, deadly.

---

## THE KEEPER

**Single administrator:** Appointed by joint agreement between Cinderhome, Ashenveil, The Saddle. Must be competent, trustworthy, slightly mad (who else volunteers?).

**Current Keeper:** **Marcus Glasswalk** (human, 47, male) - Former expedition guide, lost hand to glass accident, now administers rather than explores. Knows Flats better than anyone alive.

**Authority:** Total within Shardkeep. Manages supplies, coordinates rescues, decides who goes onto Flats (can refuse obviously doomed expeditions).

**Staff:** ~15 permanent (guards, supply managers, medics, guides). Plus ~185 temporary residents (expeditions preparing, recovering, or waiting).

---

## SERVICES

**Expedition outfitting:** Specialized equipment (heat-resistant clothing, glass-proof boots, navigation tools, emergency supplies).

**Guide services:** Experienced guides who've survived multiple Flats crossings. Expensive but worth it (difference between life/death).

**Rescue coordination:** If expedition doesn't return, Keeper organizes rescue. Limited resources, not all rescues successful.

**Recovery facilities:** Medical care for survivors (treating dehydration, glass cuts, heat stroke, psychological trauma).

**Information:** The Keeper maintains records (safe routes, dangerous areas, recent casualties, discoveries). Sells information to expeditions.

---

## WHY PEOPLE GO ONTO GLASS FLATS

**Obsidian harvesting:** Specific glass types found only in Flats. Valuable for crafting, magical applications.

**The Mirror Sea:** Flat glass plains rumored to show visions (spiritual significance to some Drakona). Pilgrims seek it.

**Shortcuts:** Crossing Flats saves weeks versus going around. Desperate people risk it.

**Research:** Scholars study volcanic glass formation, heat effects, unique ecosystem of glass-adapted life.

**Treasure hunting:** Rumors of ancient sites buried in glass (probably false, but people try anyway).

**Stupidity:** Some people underestimate danger. Darwin handles these.

---

## THE RULES

**Rule 1:** Don't go alone (enforced by Keeper refusing solo expeditions).

**Rule 2:** Register with Keeper before departing (so rescue knows who's missing).

**Rule 3:** Carry minimum 5 days water (more than you think you need).

**Rule 4:** Return by stated deadline or presumed dead (rescue attempts depend on timeline).

**Rule 5:** Pay respects at the Memorial (honor those who didn't return).

---

## THE MEMORIAL

Stone monument listing names of everyone who died on Flats. ~800 names over decades. Sobering reminder.

Expeditions traditionally visit Memorial before departing (acknowledge danger, honor dead, steel nerves).

---

## CURRENT TENSIONS

**Rescue resource debate:** Keeper has limited resources. Recent expeditions demanded multiple rescue attempts (expensive, dangerous). Where's the line between reasonable rescue and wasting resources on foolish mistakes?

**The disappeared expedition:** Large, well-equipped group vanished three weeks ago. No trace found. Keeper suspects they're dead but families demanding continued search.

**Supply shortage:** Recent demand surge (more expeditions) depleting stocks. Keeper needs resupply from major settlements but caravans delayed.

---

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Rescue Mission:** Party hired for rescue attempt (last expedition before declaring victims lost). Race against time, navigate Flats, find survivors (if any).

**The Mirror Sea Pilgrimage:** Drakona pilgrims need escort to Mirror Sea. Party must guide them safely, deal with spiritual expectations vs. practical danger.

**The Supply Run:** Keeper desperately needs resupply. Party must escort caravan from The Saddle through dangerous territory to Shardkeep.

**The Disappeared:** Investigate what happened to lost expedition. Their equipment was excellent, guides experienced. What went wrong? Discovery might be disturbing.

**The Foolish Noble:** Wealthy person demands to cross Flats despite obvious incompetence. Keeper hires party to escort them (keep idiot alive against their best efforts).
