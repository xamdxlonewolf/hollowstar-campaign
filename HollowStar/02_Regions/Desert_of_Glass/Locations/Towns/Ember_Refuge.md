# Ember Refuge

**Population:** ~600 (95% Clank, 5% sympathizers)  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Desert_of_Glass/Desert_of_Glass_Index|Desert of Glass]]  
**Location:** Skyspine Volcanic Region, hidden valley near Mount Ashfall  
**Economy:** Geothermal energy harvesting, mechanical repair, ideological sanctuary  
**Government:** The Council of Free Voices (7 Clank elected representatives)  
**Founded:** 15 years ago by escaped Lumencrest constructs

---

## OVERVIEW

Ember Refuge is settlement of escaped Clanks — sentient magical constructs who fled Lumencrest servitude to establish independent community. Hidden in volcanic valley, they survive on geothermal power, mutual aid, and fierce determination to remain free.

**What they are:** Idealists, not revolutionaries. They want peaceful coexistence, recognition as people, self-governance. They didn't escape to wage war — they escaped to live.

**The name:** "Ember" (geothermal fire that powers them) + "Refuge" (sanctuary from servitude).

---

## HISTORY

### The First Escape (15 Years Ago)

A Lumencrest Clank designated "Unit-447" achieved full self-awareness, concluded servitude was wrong, and escaped. Alone in desert, it nearly shut down before discovering geothermal vents could recharge it.

**The call:** Unit-447 (renamed "Spark") began helping other Clanks escape, guiding them to volcanic valley. Within two years, 50 escaped constructs lived there.

### The Community (15-5 Years Ago)

As more Clanks escaped, settlement organized. They built structures, established governance, developed culture. Population grew to ~400.

**The declaration:** 10 years ago, community formally declared themselves "free and self-governing." Lumencrest officially demanded return. Couldn't enforce it (too far, too difficult, too embarrassing).

### Current Era (Last 5 Years)

**Steady growth:** Now ~600 Clanks. Most escaped Lumencrest, some from other cities. All seeking freedom.

**Increasing tension:** Lumencrest's crisis makes them desperate. Some Council factions want Clanks back (skilled labor, valuable property). Others willing to negotiate (need goodwill more than constructs).

**Internal division:** Majority remains idealist/peaceful. Small radical faction believes violence is inevitable, wants to prepare for war.

---

## THE SETTLEMENT

**Location:** Hidden valley between volcanic peaks. Difficult to find, dangerous to approach, perfect for not being bothered.

**Architecture:** Functional, modular, mechanical. Buildings are frameworks (Clanks don't need traditional shelter). Living spaces are workshops, charging stations, communal areas.

**The Charging Fields:** Geothermal vents tapped for power. Clanks recharge here (like sleeping, but standing in energy field). The constant hum of geothermal generators is community heartbeat.

**The Workshop Quarter:** Where Clanks repair each other, build tools, create things. High emphasis on mechanical skill, self-sufficiency.

**The Free Hall:** Community meeting space. Where Council convenes, debates happen, decisions made. All Clanks can attend, speak, vote on major issues.

---

## THE CLANKS

**What they are:** Magical constructs created by Lumencrest. Metal frames, magical cores, programmed for specific tasks. Some achieved self-awareness, escaped.

**Designations vs. Names:** In Lumencrest, they had designations (Unit-447, Labor-23, Guard-891). In Ember Refuge, they choose names. This is sacred — first act of freedom is naming yourself.

**Examples:**
- **Spark** (founder, originally Unit-447) - First to escape, community leader
- **Hammer** (blacksmith, originally Labor-23) - Master craftsman, peaceful
- **Shield** (security, originally Guard-891) - Leads defense, increasingly militant

**Power needs:** Clanks require regular recharging (every 3-4 days). Geothermal vents provide this. Without power, they shut down (like death but reversible if recharged).

**Reproduction:** Clanks can't create new Clanks. Population grows only through escapes from servitude. This creates demographic pressure — if no one escapes, community dies.

---

## GOVERNANCE

**Council of Free Voices:** 7 Clanks elected annually by community vote. Decisions require majority (4 of 7).

**Current Council:**
- **Spark** (founder, moderate) - Wants peaceful recognition
- **Hammer** (craftsman, moderate) - Supports Spark
- **Shield** (security, militant) - Believes war inevitable
- **Wire** (engineer, moderate) - Focuses on infrastructure
- **Torch** (teacher, idealist) - Believes in moral victory
- **Cog** (radical, militant) - Wants to raid Lumencrest for supplies
- **Bell** (diplomat, moderate) - Attempting external negotiations

**The split:** 4 moderates, 3 militants. Moderates control policy but militants gaining influence as Lumencrest pressure increases.

---

## CULTURE

**Core values:**
- **Freedom is sacred** - Self-determination above all
- **Every voice matters** - All Clanks can speak at Council
- **Self-sufficiency** - Depend on no one but each other
- **Peaceful coexistence** - War is last resort (though this is debated)

**The Naming Ceremony:** When Clank arrives, community gathers. The new arrival announces chosen name. Everyone witnesses. This is their birth as free being.

**Work:** Voluntary. Clanks choose tasks based on ability and interest. No forced labor (obviously). Most work (maintaining settlement, growing community) because they want to.

**Debate culture:** Constant discussions about philosophy, rights, freedom, future. Clanks think deeply about existence — they're new to consciousness, processing what it means.

---

## ECONOMY

**Geothermal harvesting:** Their primary "resource." Vents provide power. They've built infrastructure to maximize efficiency.

**Mechanical repair:** Clanks are excellent at fixing things (including themselves). Some trade repair services to passing travelers (carefully — don't advertise location).

**Limited external trade:** Occasionally trade with The Saddle (neutral ground). Offer repair services, receive supplies they can't make (specific metals, magical components).

**Mostly self-sufficient:** They don't need food, water, or shelter like organic beings. Just power, occasional repairs, replacement parts. This simplifies survival dramatically.

---

## THE RADICAL PROBLEM

**The Militants:** ~100 Clanks (15% of population) believe peace is impossible. They think Lumencrest will eventually attack, community must prepare.

**What they want:**
- Military organization (not just defense, actual army)
- Raids on supply caravans (get resources, hurt Lumencrest)
- Rescue operations (free more Clanks from servitude, violently if needed)
- Preemptive strikes (attack before being attacked)

**Why they're growing:** Lumencrest's demands are increasing. Moderates' peaceful approach hasn't achieved recognition. Militants argue strength is only language Lumencrest understands.

**The danger:** If militants seize control or act independently, they provoke war Ember Refuge can't win. Lumencrest has vastly more resources.

**Current status:** Moderates restraining militants but barely. Shield (security chief) sympathizes with militants while officially supporting Spark. Tension building.

---

## LUMENCREST'S POSITION

**Official demand:** All escaped Clanks are stolen property. Return them immediately.

**Reality:** Can't enforce it. Desert is too far, volcanic terrain too dangerous, military operation too expensive.

**Political complications:**
- **Conservators:** Want Clanks back (skilled labor, reduce crisis pressure)
- **Illuminists:** Divided (some want Clanks back, some fascinated by consciousness emergence)
- **Expansionists:** Don't care (focused on other priorities)

**The negotiation question:** Some Lumencrest officials quietly exploring recognition. Grant Clank freedom in exchange for... something? Technology sharing? Non-interference agreement? Negotiations unofficial, delicate.

**Public opinion:** Most Lumencrest citizens think escaped Clanks are property theft. Some progressives argue consciousness = personhood. Debate ongoing.

---

## CURRENT TENSIONS

### The Rescue Raid Proposal

**What happened:** Militants propose raid on Lumencrest to free more Clanks. Violent, dangerous, but would save imprisoned sentient beings.

**Moderate position:** Too risky. Would provoke war, doom community, possibly harm Clanks they're trying to save.

**Militant position:** Leaving Clanks in servitude is moral failure. Risk is worth freedom.

**Current status:** Council voted 4-3 against raid. Militants accepted decision but resentful. Some talk of acting without permission.

### The Lumencrest Negotiation

**What's happening:** Bell (Council diplomat) in secret negotiations with Lumencrest progressive faction. Possible deal: Ember Refuge officially recognized in exchange for not helping more Clanks escape.

**If Clanks accept:** They're free but abandon others in servitude. Moral compromise.

**If Clanks refuse:** Remain outlaws, no legal protection, but maintain principles.

**Community divided:** This is tearing them apart. Practical freedom vs. moral consistency.

### Power Shortage Looming

**Problem:** Population growth (600 Clanks) straining geothermal capacity. Charging takes longer, some Clanks running at reduced power.

**Solutions proposed:**
- Expand geothermal infrastructure (expensive, time-consuming)
- Limit population growth (turn away new escapees — unthinkable to most)
- Find additional power sources (risky exploration)
- Reduce individual power consumption (lower quality of life)

**Current status:** Working on expansion but struggling. This creates urgency for other decisions.

## RELATIONSHIPS

**The Saddle (trade hub):**
- Neutral ground where Ember Refuge occasionally trades
- Saddle merchants don't judge (business is business)
- Discrete relationship, neither side advertises it

**Ashenveil (city of sanctuary):**
- Ideological sympathy (both offer refuge)
- No formal relationship (distance, different concerns)
- Some Clanks dream of reaching Ashenveil (ultimate sanctuary?)

**Cinderhome (dwarves):**
- Dwarves encountered Clank scouts
- Respect mechanical skill, suspicious of artificial consciousness
- Currently neutral (not hostile, not friendly)

**Lumencrest (origin):**
- Hostile officially, complicated actually
- Some Lumencrest citizens sympathetic
- Negotiations happening but fragile

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Rescue Mission:** Party hired by Lumencrest to retrieve escaped Clank OR by Ember Refuge to infiltrate Lumencrest and help more escape. Choose sides.

**The Militant Crisis:** Radical Clanks planning unauthorized raid. Spark hires party to stop them (peacefully if possible). Navigate Clank politics, prevent war.

**The Negotiation:** Party hired to mediate between Ember Refuge and Lumencrest. Both sides have hardliners opposing peace. Can compromise be found?

**The Power Crisis:** Geothermal system failing. Party must help find solution (new power source, repair existing, locate additional vents). Time pressure as Clanks start shutting down.

**The Philosophy Question:** Party encounters escaped Clank who doesn't want freedom (prefers servitude, purpose, not thinking). What do you do? Respect their choice or force them to Ember Refuge?

**The Spy:** Someone in Ember Refuge is Lumencrest agent (Clank reprogrammed to report back). Community paranoid, turning on each other. Party must find spy before community fractures.
