# Glass Flats

**Region:** [[02_Regions/Desert_of_Glass/Desert_of_Glass_Index|Desert of Glass]]  
**Type:** Vast volcanic glass plains  
**Size:** ~200 miles east-west, ~120 miles north-south  
**Danger Level:** Extreme (heat, dehydration, cutting glass, navigation hazards)

---

## OVERVIEW

The Glass Flats are the result of ancient mega-eruption that created vast plains of cooled volcanic glass. Black, reflective, deadly beautiful — this is landscape that kills unprepared travelers while mesmerizing them with impossible beauty.

**Formation:** ~5,000 years ago, massive volcanic eruption created lava lake covering hundreds of square miles. It cooled into solid glass. Millennia of weathering created current landscape — some areas smooth as mirrors, others shattered into blade-forests.

---

## THE REGIONS

### The Mirror Sea (Central, ~50 miles across)

**Character:** Perfectly flat, smooth black glass reflecting sky like mirror.

**The effect:** Walking across Mirror Sea is disorienting — sky reflects perfectly, creating sensation of walking between two skies. No horizon, no reference points, easy to lose direction.

**Spiritual significance:** Some Drakona believe Mirror Sea shows visions. They undertake pilgrimages here seeking spiritual insight. Whether visions are real or heat-induced hallucinations is debated.

**Danger:** Navigation is primary threat. People walk in circles, become lost, die of dehydration within sight of where they started.

**What makes it beautiful:** Sunset on Mirror Sea is reportedly most beautiful sight in known world. Sky and reflection create infinite space of color. Those who survive describe it as transcendent.

### The Shardfields (Eastern sections)

**Character:** Glass shattered into vertical blade-like fragments.

**The landscape:** Black glass shards jutting up 2-20 feet tall, creating maze-like passages. Some shards razor-sharp, others weathered smoother.

**The sound:** Wind blowing through shards creates haunting, musical sound — high-pitched ringing, chimes, echoes. Beautiful and eerie.

**Danger:** Physical cuts are constant. Glass edges slice through leather, cloth, thin metal. Protective boots and gloves essential. Lost blood from accumulated cuts kills people.

**Navigation:** Visibility limited to a few yards. Easy to get lost in identical-looking passages. Some passages dead-end, forcing backtracking.

**What lives here:** Surprisingly, some creatures adapted. Glass lizards (reflective scales matching surroundings), sharp-beaked birds nesting on shard-tops, predators that hunt by sound in the maze.

### Scattered Lava Pools (Throughout)

**Character:** Pockets where cooling isn't complete — still-molten lava beneath crusted surface.

**The danger:** Surface looks solid. Step on thin crust, break through, fall into lava. Death is instant and horrible.

**Identification:** Heat shimmer, sulfur smell, sometimes visible glow through cracks. Experienced guides know warning signs.

**Why they persist:** Geothermal activity keeps these pools molten. Some are hundreds of years old, slowly cooling but still deadly.

**Positive use:** Heat-resistant creatures (Infernis, some Drakona) rest near pools — the warmth is pleasant for them even if not strictly necessary.

### Ash Dunes (Southern edges)

**Character:** Where ash from continuing volcanic activity accumulates on glass.

**The landscape:** Black glass dunes covered in gray ash. Wind constantly reshaping them.

**Danger:** Ash obscures glass beneath. People think they're walking on sand, suddenly hit sharp glass hidden underneath.

**Limited vegetation:** A few heat-adapted, ash-resistant plants grow here. Desert shrubs, volcanic cacti, heat-loving fungi. This is only place on Glass Flats with any plant life.

## HAZARDS

### Heat

**Temperature:** 110-130°F during day (glass absorbs and radiates heat). 50-70°F at night (glass loses heat quickly).

**Effect:** Extreme dehydration. Most deaths on Flats are thirst-related. People underestimate water needs.

**Glass radiation:** Black glass absorbs sunlight, radiates heat upward. Walking across Flats is like being in oven.

### Reflection Blindness

**The problem:** Glass reflects sunlight intensely. Without eye protection, temporary or permanent blindness results.

**Goggles essential:** Dark glass goggles standard equipment. Without them, hours on Flats can cause lasting damage.

### Cutting Glass

**Sharp edges:** Weathered glass creates razor edges. Shardfields especially dangerous but even Mirror Sea has hidden cracks.

**Protection:** Thick leather boots, reinforced gloves, layered clothing. Still not perfect — cuts accumulate.

### Navigation

**Disorientation:** Mirror reflections, heat shimmer, lack of landmarks make navigation extremely difficult.

**Getting lost:** Primary killer. People circle, become confused, exhaust themselves, die within miles of safety.

**Solution:** Experienced guides, quality compasses, careful route marking.

### Storms

**Ash storms:** Volcanic ash carried by wind creates blinding, choking storms. Visibility zero, breathing difficult.

**Glass-wind:** Strong winds turn glass fragments into projectiles. Getting caught in open during glass-wind is deadly.

## WHY PEOPLE CROSS IT

**Resources:** Specific obsidian types found only here. Valuable for crafting, worth the risk to some.

**Shortcuts:** Crossing Flats saves weeks versus going around. Time vs. danger calculation.

**Pilgrimage:** Drakona spiritual seekers pursuing Mirror Sea visions.

**Research:** Scholars studying volcanic processes, glass formation, extreme ecosystem adaptation.

**Challenge:** Some do it for achievement. "I crossed Glass Flats" is major bragging rights.

**Desperation:** Fugitives, exiles, people with no other options risk Flats hoping to reach other side.

---

## SURVIVAL

**Minimum requirements:**
- Experienced guide (knows safe routes, warning signs, navigation)
- 5+ days water per person (more than seems necessary)
- Protective gear (boots, goggles, gloves, layered clothing)
- Navigation tools (compass, maps, route markers)
- Emergency supplies (medical, shelter, extra water)

**Safe crossing time:** 3-4 days minimum (straight line, experienced party, good conditions). Most take 5-7 days (safer route, rest stops, caution).

**Casualty rate:** ~30% of amateur expeditions have deaths. Professional guided expeditions: ~5%. Solo attempts: near 100%.

---

## DISCOVERIES

**Ancient sites (rumored):** Some believe pre-Hollostar structures are buried beneath glass. Occasionally, expeditions report seeing shapes under glass surface. Most believe these are optical illusions.

**Unique creatures:** Glass-adapted species found nowhere else. Biologists interested but dangerous to study.

**Volcanic glass varieties:** Flats contain dozens of obsidian types. Crafters seek specific types for specialized applications.

**The anomaly:** Some expeditions report compass malfunctions in specific Flats areas. Magnetic interference? Hollostar fragment influence? Unknown.

---

## THE LEGEND OF THE GLASS WALKER

**The story:** Decades ago, someone named "Glass Walker" supposedly lived on the Flats. Survived alone, knew every safe path, helped lost travelers.

**Reality:** Probably myth. No confirmed sightings in 20+ years. But stories persist — travelers report mysterious assistance, finding water caches they didn't leave, footprints in ash leading to safety.

**Believers say:** Glass Walker is still out there, ageless, protecting fools from their own stupidity.

**Skeptics say:** Heat-induced hallucinations, lucky survival, people not wanting to admit they got lost and found their own way.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Mirror Sea Pilgrimage:** Guide Drakona pilgrims to Mirror Sea. They seek visions. Party must keep them alive while respecting spiritual quest.

**The Lost Expedition:** Wealthy family hiring rescue party. Their expedition disappeared two weeks ago. Find them (alive or dead), discover what happened.

**The Obsidian Rush:** New valuable glass type discovered. Multiple groups racing to claim territory. Party involved in competition/conflict on deadly terrain.

**The Ancient Structure:** Expedition reports seeing ruins beneath glass. Party hired to investigate. Is it real or illusion? If real, how to access it?

**The Storm Survival:** Party caught in ash storm on Flats. Must find shelter, survive, navigate back with zero visibility and dwindling supplies.

**The Glass Walker:** Party encounters mysterious helper who disappears. Coincidence or is legendary Glass Walker real? Investigation reveals surprises.
