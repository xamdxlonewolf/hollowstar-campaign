# Marsh Settlements - Glittering Marshes

All settlements of the Glittering Marshes, organized by zone.

---

## EASTERN EDGE

### Reedgate (~2,400)

**Location:** Eastern marsh edge, closest to Central Heartlands  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (Ribbet ~40%, human ~35%, others ~25%)  
**Identity:** Guide trade hub, outside contact point, gateway settlement

Reedgate is where outsiders enter the marsh. The settlement operates guide services (Ribbet guides essential for deep marsh travel), trades marsh goods for outside supplies, and maintains the only reliable road connection to Central Heartlands.

**Economy:** Guide services, marsh goods trade, waystation hospitality

**Character:** Cultural mixing point. Outsiders who can't survive deep marsh operate here. Ribbet who prefer contact with outside world. Functional coexistence based on mutual need.

**Guide Guild:** Ribbet-operated organization coordinating marsh navigation services. Outsiders attempting deep marsh without guides usually die.

---

### Reed's Landing (~180)

**Location:** South of Reedgate, smaller edge settlement  
**Identity:** Secondary access point, fishing

Small edge settlement providing alternative marsh entry. Primarily fishing operations in marsh shallows.

---

## DEEP INTERIOR

### Deepsilt (~1,800)

**Location:** Central marsh, built over deepest water  
**Ancestry:** Ribbet majority (~95%), others (~5%)  
**Identity:** Largest stilt settlement, built over shard cluster

Deepsilt is built on stilts over the marsh's deepest water — platforms connected by walkways, entire community elevated above surface. Beneath the settlement sits the largest known Hollostar shard cluster on the continent.

**Economy:** Fishing, marsh resource gathering, guide services

**Character:** Ribbet mastery. The settlement exists where only Ribbet can comfortably operate. Non-Ribbet visitors are rare and uncomfortable.

**The shards below:** Somnarchs and Starfall Seekers obsess over this location. The Ribbet permit limited research access in exchange for trade goods. The shards glow faintly at night, visible through deep water.

---

### Deep Bloom (~2,100)

**Location:** Deep marsh interior, living fungal colony  
**Ancestry:** Fungril majority (~90%), others (~10%)  
**Identity:** Fungal colony settlement, three shard fragments integrated

Deep Bloom is not built — it grew. A massive fungal colony with Fungril living integrated within the mycelium network. Three Hollostar fragments are wrapped in fungal growth, partially absorbed into the colony's structure.

**Economy:** Fungal products, spore cultivation, shard research (limited access granted to Seekers)

**Character:** Unlike anywhere else. The settlement is alive. The Fungril and the colony are not separate — they're symbiotic. Non-Fungril visitors find it deeply unsettling.

**The integrated shards:** The fragments don't just sit within the colony — they're incorporated. The mycelium has grown around and through them. What effect this has is unknown. The Fungril say the colony is "learning" from the shards. Nobody knows what this means.

---

### Hush Hollow (~250)

**Location:** Deep marsh interior, isolation community  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (various ~100%)  
**Identity:** Those who want to be left alone

Hush Hollow is where people go to disappear. Not criminals (mostly) — just those who want isolation. The community maintains no contact with outside settlements beyond necessary trade.

**Economy:** Subsistence gathering, minimal trade

**Character:** Deliberate isolation. Questions about why you're here are unwelcome. People respect privacy or leave.

---

### Stilt Villages (~400 total across multiple sites)

**Location:** Scattered throughout deep marsh  
**Identity:** Small Ribbet fishing villages, semi-permanent

Multiple small stilt settlements (30-100 residents each) scattered across deep marsh. Ribbet fishing communities operating traditionally. Most maintain minimal contact even with other marsh settlements.

---

## DRY GROUND SETTLEMENTS

### The Dry Crossing (~400 permanent)

**Location:** Largest stable dry ground in marsh interior  
**Identity:** Council meeting site, neutral ground

The Dry Crossing is where the Marsh Council meets — representatives from major settlements gathering on neutral ground to coordinate on issues affecting all communities.

**Population:** ~400 permanent residents maintaining the site, population swells during council meetings

**Character:** Neutral territory. The Crossing belongs to no single settlement. Hospitality to council delegates is mandatory.

**The Council:** Meets quarterly. Five seats: Deepsilt (Ribbet), Deep Bloom (Fungril), Reedgate (edge settlements), Shellmound (archives), rotating fifth seat.

---

### Shellmound (~1,200)

**Location:** Southern dry hummocks  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (Galapa ~40%, Fungril ~35%, others ~25%)  
**Identity:** Archive location, shard documentation

Shellmound sits on southern dry ground where the marsh approaches the coast. The settlement houses the Marsh Archives — documentation of shard locations, fragment properties, and Hollostar research collected over generations.

**Economy:** Archive maintenance, shard research, trade with coastal settlements

**Character:** Scholarly without being formal. The archives are open to serious researchers. Casual curiosity is discouraged.

**The Archives:** Maintained by mixed-ancestry scholars. Contains maps of shard locations, fragment documentation, research on shard properties. Both Somnarchs and Starfall Seekers consult these archives regularly.

## NORTHERN BORDER

### Mistwatch (~600)

**Location:** Northern marsh near Verdant Crescent border  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (Ribbet ~35%, elf ~25%, human ~20%, others ~20%)  
**Identity:** Forest-marsh overlap, monitoring settlement

Mistwatch operates where marsh transitions to forest. The settlement monitors the boundary and maintains relationship with the Green Covenant's Warden of Waters.

**Economy:** Boundary monitoring, trade between marsh and forest communities, unique resource gathering from overlap zone

**Character:** Dual ecology. The forest-marsh overlap produces resources found nowhere else. Mistwatch harvests these sustainably under Covenant observation.

## SOUTHERN ISLANDS

### Tidemark (~400)

**Location:** Larger southern island where marsh meets open water  
**Ancestry:** Mixed (Ribbet ~50%, Galapa ~35%, others ~15%)  
**Identity:** Fishing community, pearl diving, island settlement

Tidemark sits on the larger southern island where marsh waters transition to open gulf. The community operates both marsh-style and ocean-style fishing, plus pearl diving in surrounding waters.

**Economy:** Fishing (both marsh and ocean), pearl diving, trade between marsh settlements and coastal communities

**Character:** Hybrid culture. Ribbet marsh traditions meet Galapa ocean expertise. The mixing works because both groups respect water.

**Pearl diving:** The waters around Tidemark produce modest pearl yields — not Les Perles quality but valuable. Galapa divers teach Ribbet techniques, Ribbet teach Galapa marsh navigation.

---

### Saltmere Isle (~250)

**Location:** Smaller southern island  
**Ancestry:** Ribbet majority (~75%), others (~25%)  
**Identity:** Salt water fishing, waystation between marsh and Salt Shore

Saltmere Isle is smaller and more remote. The community operates salt water fishing and serves as waystation for those traveling between Glittering Marshes and Salt Shore (rare but it happens).

**Economy:** Salt water fishing, waystation services, minimal trade

**Character:** Quiet isolation. The island is far enough from main marsh that residents rarely visit other settlements. They prefer it this way.

---

## COASTAL SETTLEMENTS

### Glowmere (~300)

**Location:** Southern marsh near coast  
**Identity:** Small coastal marsh settlement, bioluminescent phenomena

Small settlement where marsh meets coast, known for bioluminescent algae that makes the water glow at night. The effect is beautiful and attracts occasional visitors.

**Economy:** Fishing, gathering, limited tourism (rare scholars or artists seeking the glow)

---

### Canopy Run (~900)

**Location:** Eastern marsh edge, elevated forest transition  
**Ancestry:** Simiah Common-folk/marmoset majority (~75%), others (~25%)  
**Identity:** Tree settlement, smaller-scale Simiah community, marsh-forest overlap

Canopy Run sits where marsh meets scattered trees at the eastern edge. Unlike the massive Simiah of the Verdant Crescent, these are Common-folk Simiah (marmoset-scale) — smaller, lighter, perfectly adapted to thin branches and elevated living.

**Architecture:** Tree houses built at Simiah scale (small), rope bridges connecting platforms, structures integrated with marsh-edge trees. Everything elevated above marsh water.

**Economy:** Marsh-tree overlap resources (unique fruits, aerial fishing, canopy gathering), guide services for marsh-edge navigation

**Character:** Vertical living at smaller scale. Common-folk Simiah are comfortable in spaces that would be cramped for larger Simiah or impossible for humans.

**Relationship to Deep Forest Simiah:** Distant cousins. Common-folk Simiah are separate subspecies — smaller, different social structures, adapted to different environments.

**The Listeners:** Canopy Run's most distinctive feature is known only within the marsh. Certain individuals develop *Veleth* — a felt awareness of movement and disturbance through the marsh's shard-bioluminescent network. They are the quiet backbone of the guide system and the settlement's primary security. Senior Listener: **Mist-of-Branches** (Common-folk Simiah, age 84). The Listeners have noticed the shard pulse changing over the past year and have told no one outside the marsh.

## GEOGRAPHIC NOTES

**The marsh is vast** — travel between settlements takes days even with Ribbet guides

**Deep water is deadly** — without guides, outsiders drown, get lost, or encounter things best avoided

**The shards matter** — Densest fragment concentration means the marsh is strategically important despite being inhospitable

**Three powers watch** — Somnarchs (obsessively), Starfall Seekers (scientifically), Lumencrest (covetously)
