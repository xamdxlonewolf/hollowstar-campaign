# Settlements - Glittering Marshes

The communities of the Glittering Marshes — five major settlements arranged around the marsh's geography, each with distinct character shaped by ancestry, location, and relationship to the ancient shards beneath the water.

---

## THE ELDER COUNCIL

One elder per major settlement, meets quarterly at the Dry Crossing. Decisions require consensus. This is not symbolic — the marsh's decentralized governance only works because the five elders genuinely respect each other and understand that forcing a vote would break the system.

**Current council:**
- **Elder Mira Fen** (Reedgate, human)
- **Elder Pip** (Deepstilt, Ribbet)
- **Elder Broum Shell** (Shellmound, Galapa)
- **Tender Myss** (Deep Bloom, Fungril)
- **Elder Kael Mistborn** (Mistwatch, human)
- **The Keeper** (Dry Crossing, rotating position — currently held by a Ribbet named **Silt**)

**Current agenda:** Lumencrest factor house request at Reedgate (unanimous opposition, no formal vote yet); Deep Bloom's warning about the mycelium detecting "something wrong" in the deep marsh's eastern channels; new channel opened last season requiring guide map updates; Researcher Cassen's instruments getting too accurate.

## REEDGATE (Population: ~2,400)

**Location:** Eastern marsh edge, closest to Central Heartlands  
**Ancestry:** Human majority (~60%), Ribbet minority (~30%), scattered others  
**Identity:** The entry point — where outsiders arrive, guides are hired, and the marsh decides if you're welcome

Reedgate is the marsh's face to the outside world. The settlement has developed a particular culture around this role — professionally helpful, deliberately opaque about anything that matters, and extremely good at appearing simple while being strategically sophisticated.

**The architecture:** Built on the last solid ground before the marsh proper. Wooden structures on stone foundations, raised walkways connecting buildings, boat docks extending into the marsh channels. Everything is maintained obsessively — not for beauty, but because a rotting dock means someone dies.

**The guide trade:** The economic and cultural center of Reedgate. Licensed marsh guides are registered with the Elder Council and carry certification that means something to locals and nothing to outsiders who don't know what it represents. The guide families — several are now in their fourth or fifth generation — know the marsh as well as any Ribbet through accumulated practical knowledge rather than instinct.

**Current tension:** Lumencrest has made a formal request to establish a factor house in Reedgate. The request comes with money, trade benefits, and the promise of "mutual economic development." Elder Mira Fen has said no four times. The fifth request arrived last month with a larger offer and a Lumencrest representative who is not leaving until he gets an answer. The merchants in Reedgate are divided — half want the money, half understand why Elder Mira keeps refusing.

**The Guide Hall:** Where licensed guides register, report unusual findings, and maintain the quarterly-updated route maps. The hall's master map shows every safe channel, every seasonal variation, every known hazard. Unlicensed guides who are caught guiding professionally are brought here for a conversation that usually ends with them leaving the marsh permanently.

**Notable figures:**
- **Elder Mira Fen** (human, 56) — see NPC file; the marsh's diplomat to the outside world, firm about boundaries
- **Wren Mudpath** (human, 48) — senior licensed guide, knows the marsh better than anyone except Old Chorus
- **Merchant-Factor Dess Cole** (human, 41) — leads the pro-Lumencrest merchant faction, genuinely believes the trade deal would help Reedgate, doesn't understand what saying yes would mean for the marsh

## DEEPSTILT (Population: ~1,800)

**Location:** Central marsh, built on extensive stilt network over deep water  
**Ancestry:** Ribbet majority (~75%), mixed others  
**Identity:** The Ribbet heart — oldest continuous water-settlement in the marsh, built over the brightest glow

Deepstilt is a city on stilts 15–20 feet above water level in dry season, 5–10 feet in wet season. The stilt network extends for roughly a quarter mile in each direction — rope bridges, slide-poles, platforms at different heights. Ribbet move through it with total ease. Others manage with varying degrees of success. Falling into the water is not dangerous. Being embarrassed about falling is inevitable.

**The glow beneath:** At night, the water under Deepstilt glows bright enough to read by. The bioluminescence here is stronger than anywhere else in the marsh — not surprising, since Deepstilt sits directly above the densest concentration of ancient shard fragments. The original Ribbet settlers built here specifically because the glow was strongest.

**The deepest secret:** If you excavated the marsh floor beneath Deepstilt's oldest foundations — something nobody has done and Elder Pip will make sure nobody does — you would find the largest intact Hollostar shard fragment known to exist anywhere on the continent. Still glowing. Still warm to touch. Still producing low-level energy that has shaped the entire marsh ecosystem for millennia.

Elder Pip knows this. A handful of the oldest Ribbet families know this. They don't discuss it. When outsiders ask why the glow is so strong here, Ribbet shrug and say the marsh has always been bright. This is true. It is not an answer.

**Culture:** Deepstilt Ribbet have developed a relationship with the water and the light beneath it that is as much spiritual as practical. Ribbet children born here have their first water in shard-lit channels. Deepstilt dead are lowered into the glowing water. Major community decisions are made from the oldest platform, directly above the brightest glow. None of this is formalized religion. All of it matters.

**The fishing:** The best in the marsh. Bioluminescent organisms concentrate near shard-light, and fish follow the bioluminescence. Reading the glow patterns tells you where fish will be before they arrive. Deepstilt fishers can predict catches three days out by watching how the light moves beneath the water.

**Notable figures:**
- **Elder Pip** (Ribbet, 71) — see NPC file; cheerful gatekeeper to the marsh's biggest secret
- **Old Chorus** (Ribbet, 89) — retired guide, oral historian, tells stories that contain information if you listen correctly
- **Stillmaster Keff** (Ribbet, 52) — maintains the stilt network, knows which supports need replacing before they fail, quietly ensures nobody does maintenance near the oldest foundations

## SHELLMOUND (Population: ~1,200)

**Location:** Southern marsh, cluster of large dry hummocks  
**Ancestry:** Galapa majority (~50%), Fungril significant minority (~30%), mixed others  
**Identity:** The slow settlement — most self-sufficient, deepest roots, longest memories

Shellmound sits on raised ground that hasn't flooded in living memory. The settlement is built around the Archive — the structure that houses the marsh's oldest records, maintained by Elder Broum Shell and a rotating staff of scholar-scribes who take the work very seriously.

**The pace:** Everything in Shellmound moves at Galapa speed — deliberate, thorough, taking as long as it takes. Meetings happen when they need to happen. Decisions are made when there's sufficient information. Rush is not a concept that has purchase here.

**Galapa shell-craft:** The finest in the marsh and possibly on the continent. Galapa shell-craft here incorporates bioluminescent materials in ways unique to this region — containers that hold a faint glow for weeks after being filled with marsh water, tools that emit soft light when held, decorative objects that respond to temperature and humidity with shifting patterns. This isn't magic. It's centuries of understanding exactly which organisms bind to which shell compositions under what conditions.

**Fungril cultivation:** The Fungril community in Shellmound tends the dry hummocks' fungal colonies with encyclopedic knowledge. They know which species grow where, why, and how to encourage or limit growth. The medicinal and alchemical fungi exported from Shellmound are sought across the continent — expensive, potent, and cultivated by Fungril who don't explain why marsh-grown fungi work better than forest-grown variants. The answer connects to the shards. They don't share this.

**The Shellmound Archive:** Three buildings of records — written (mostly Galapa-maintained), oral (recorded by scribes interviewing elders), and material (samples, preserved specimens, objects from deep marsh excavations). The archive's prize holding is Elder Broum Shell's personal collection: accounts of "the first light" that predate all current settlements, including the most detailed description of an ancient Hollostar impact anywhere on the continent.

**Current mystery:** Six months ago, a section of the archive flooded — not from outside water, but from condensation that formed inside a sealed room overnight. The room contained the oldest physical specimens from deep marsh digs. Several samples were damaged. Elder Broum insists this was not natural condensation. The investigation is ongoing quietly.

**Notable figures:**
- **Elder Broum Shell** (Galapa, 203) — see NPC file; keeper of the oldest records, remembers things nobody else does
- **Archivist Melle Greenheart** (human, 68) — chief scribe, has spent forty years organizing records that predated her arrival
- **Tender Voss** (Fungril, 87) — leads Shellmound's Fungril cultivation community, knows the relationship between fungi and shards better than anyone, will not explain it

## DEEP BLOOM (Population: ~2,100)

**Location:** Deep marsh interior, built on and around a semi-sentient fungal superorganism  
**Ancestry:** Fungril majority (~85%), others who have learned to connect to the mycelium network  
**Identity:** The living settlement — not built, cultivated; not separate structures, one organism

Deep Bloom is not a town in the traditional sense. It is a single massive fungal colony that the Fungril have cultivated, shaped, and lived within for generations. The entire settlement is alive — growing, breathing, responding, and possibly thinking.

**The organism:** The colony extends roughly half a mile in diameter, with the visible above-water growth representing perhaps a third of its total mass. The mycelium network runs deep into the marsh floor where it has wrapped around and partially absorbed three large shard fragments. The shard energy doesn't just make the fungi glow. It makes them *aware*.

**The architecture:** There are no constructed buildings. The Fungril grow chambers, bridges, platforms, and structures by encouraging the colony to develop in specific directions. A chamber can take months to grow properly. Bridges form over weeks. The living architecture responds to need — if the colony senses a section is being used heavily, it reinforces that area. If a chamber isn't used, the growth gradually reclaims it.

**The three unique species:**

**Glowtongue Caps** — Bioluminescent mushrooms that respond to thought and emotion. Fungril can make them pulse specific patterns for communication. The patterns are a language — not words, but meaning. A quick bright pulse means urgency. A slow fade means peace. Complex patterns convey complex thoughts. This is Deep Bloom's visual communication system, visible across the settlement at night.

**Depthroot Stalks** — The structural framework. These mushrooms grow both above and below the waterline, creating platforms, bridges, and supports strong enough to hold multiple Fungril. The stalks are hollow and filled with air, making them buoyant. They flex with water movement rather than resisting it. A Depthroot structure has never collapsed in living memory.

**Memory Spore** — The most remarkable and least understood species. Memory Spore mushrooms produce spores that carry information. When Fungril breathe these spores (which they do constantly in Deep Bloom), they experience fragments of memory — not their own, but the colony's. The mycelium network stores everything that happens in Deep Bloom. Every conversation near the network, every strong emotion, every significant event — absorbed, stored, accessible. This is how Deep Bloom maintains institutional knowledge without written records. The colony remembers. The Fungril access those memories through the spores.

**The mycelium network:** Every Fungril in Deep Bloom is constantly in contact with the network — touching platforms, breathing spores, standing on growth. This creates a form of telepathic connection that's not quite hivemind. Individuals retain complete autonomy. But thoughts can be shared instantly if the Fungril chooses. Warnings propagate across the settlement in seconds. Emotional states ripple through the population. The experience of being connected is described by Fungril as "never being truly alone."

Non-Fungril can connect to the network but find it overwhelming. The constant low-level awareness of others' presence, the occasional flashes of memory that aren't yours, the sensation that the ground beneath you is thinking — most visitors leave Deep Bloom unsettled. The Fungril find this normal.

**The semi-sentience:** The colony is aware. Not in the way a person is aware — more like how a forest is aware. It responds to needs, provides for the community, and occasionally acts without being asked. Fungril wake to find new chambers grown overnight where they mentioned needing space. Dangerous channels develop warning growths. Deep Bloom protects its residents.

The Fungril don't worship the colony, but they respect it as a partner in survival. The relationship is symbiotic — the Fungril cultivate and tend the growth, the colony provides shelter and connection. Neither could exist here without the other.

**The shard connection:** The colony's semi-sentience comes from prolonged shard exposure. The mycelium has wrapped around three large fragments deep in the marsh floor. The energy has made the fungi think. The Fungril discovered this centuries ago and have been carefully managing the relationship ever since. They don't fully understand it. They know not to disturb it.

**What Deep Bloom knows:** The colony's memory extends back roughly 400 years — when the Fungril first started cultivating this particular growth into a settlement. Those memories are accessible to Tender Myss and a handful of the eldest Fungril. The colony remembers the marsh changing. It remembers when the shards were brighter. It remembers disturbances in the deep water that stopped decades ago and started again recently. It has been trying to communicate this to the Fungril for six months.

**Current situation:** The colony is agitated. Something in the eastern deep marsh channels is wrong — the mycelium can feel it through its deep roots. The disturbance is growing. Tender Myss has reported this to the Elder Council three times. Nobody has investigated yet because "the colony is concerned" is not data that translates well to non-Fungril elders.

**Notable figures:**
- **Tender Myss** (Fungril, 94) — Elder Council member, can access the colony's deep memory, has been arguing for investigation of the eastern disturbance
- **Bloom-Speaker Resh** (Fungril, 52) — interprets the colony's responses, maintains the cultivation balance, concerned about recent growth patterns
- **Memory-Keeper Vess** (Fungril, 67) — specializes in accessing and interpreting the colony's stored memories, has found fragments from 400 years ago that mention "lights going dark"

## THE DRY CROSSING (Population: ~400 permanent, ~800 during council season)

**Location:** Largest stable dry ground in marsh interior  
**Ancestry:** Mixed — population shifts based on season and council timing  
**Identity:** Neutral ground, council seat, the marsh's only permanent market

The Dry Crossing exists because geography demanded it. This is the only large stable ground in the marsh's interior, the point where five major channels meet, and therefore the natural meeting place for anyone who needs to be central without favoring any settlement.

**The Council Hall:** Built from marsh hardwood over a century ago. Five elder seats arranged in a circle with no head position. Bioluminescent moss growing on every surface provides light without fire. The hall smells like the marsh — not unpleasant, just present. Decisions made here require consensus. If consensus can't be reached, the matter is tabled until it can be. This has worked for six generations.

**The permanent market:** Small by city standards, essential by marsh standards. Operates year-round, expands significantly during council season. This is where marsh goods meet external traders who are willing to travel inland. Licensed guides bring merchant caravans here. The merchants buy, sell, trade, and leave. Nobody stays longer than necessary except the ~400 permanent residents who make the market function.

**The Keeper:** The Dry Crossing has no elder in the traditional sense because the population is too transient. Instead, the council seat is held by the Keeper — a rotating position held for five-year terms. The current Keeper is a Ribbet named **Silt** (age 61), in her third year. Her job is maintaining the hall, organizing council meetings, and ensuring the market stays neutral territory. She does this without apparent effort and with the understanding that showing favoritism to any settlement means losing the position.

**Current tension:** Lumencrest's representative has been here twice in the past six months — officially attending open council meetings as an observer, actually mapping every channel approach to the Dry Crossing. Keeper Silt has noted this. Has not acted on it yet. Is deciding whether this requires council attention or just careful watching.

## MISTWATCH (Population: ~600)

**Location:** Northern marsh, near Verdant Crescent border  
**Ancestry:** Human majority (~55%), Ribbet minority (~25%), Faun seasonal presence  
**Identity:** The border settlement — where marsh meets forest, where two ecosystems overlap

Mistwatch sits at the transition zone where the marsh's bioluminescence meets the forest's understory fungi. At night, the mixing creates visual effects found nowhere else — blue-green marsh glow bleeding into amber-gold forest glow, creating gradients and patterns that shift with wind and water movement.

**The dual culture:** Mistwatch residents operate in both worlds. They guide marsh routes. They trade with Verdant Crescent edge settlements. They host the Green Covenant's Warden of Waters when they visit. They navigate the Forest Covenant's expectations and the marsh's independence without picking sides. This requires diplomatic skill that most people don't notice until they try it themselves.

**The forest connection:** More connected to the Verdant Crescent than any other marsh settlement. Warden Sable of Waters visits quarterly, maintains relationships with both the Elder Council and the Green Covenant, and has been reporting to both groups about changes in the border ecology. The Faun come through seasonally — more frequently in the past two years, mentioning the ancient waking carefully, in the Faun way of telling you something important without stating it directly.

**The border ecology:** The overlap zone supports species found in neither marsh nor forest individually. Fungi that need both environments to fruit. Plants that grow in marsh water but require forest shade. The biodiversity here is remarkable and has attracted attention from researchers who don't understand why they're not welcome to study it.

**Current concern:** The bioluminescence patterns have been changing. Warden Sable noticed first — the forest's understory glow is extending further into the marsh than it should. The marsh's bioluminescence is responding, brightening in areas that were dim. The two ecosystems are interacting in ways that feel less like natural competition and more like communication. Warden Sable has reported this to both the Covenant and the Elder Council. Both are concerned. Neither knows what it means.

**Notable figures:**
- **Elder Kael Mistborn** (human, 63) — Elder Council member, spent forty years living at the forest-marsh border, can read the glow patterns like Wren reads water
- **Border-Guide Tess** (human, 38) — licensed for both marsh and forest edge, one of few guides who can navigate the overlap zone safely
- **Warden Sable of Waters** (elf, 140) — Green Covenant's representative, based at Mosshaven but visits Mistwatch regularly, increasingly concerned about the connection between forest waking and marsh changes

## SMALLER COMMUNITIES

**Glowmere** (~300) — Southern marsh near coast, fishing community, primarily Ribbet, known for deepwater marsh fishing that brings catches from the brackish transition zone

**Hush Hollow** (~250) — Deep marsh interior, mixed population, chosen the location specifically for its isolation, functions as a retreat for people who need to be away from settlements temporarily

**Reed's Landing** (~180) — Edge settlement south of Reedgate, human majority, smaller guide operation serving travelers who don't want to go through Reedgate's formal systems

**The Stilt Villages** (scattered, ~400 total across 5-6 small communities) — Ribbet fishing villages scattered throughout the deep marsh, semi-permanent structures that move seasonally with water levels
