# Peoples and Culture - Glittering Marshes

---

## ANCESTRIES

### Ribbet (Majority)
The dominant ancestry, concentrated in Deepstilt and throughout the water channels. Ribbet here are fully marsh-adapted — generations of living in and over water has produced communities with deep knowledge of current patterns, seasonal flooding, and the behavior of marsh creatures.

**Marsh Ribbet vs. [[02_Regions/Starfall_Basin/Starfall_Basin_Index|Starfall Basin]] Ribbet:**
Different communities, different adaptations. Starfall Basin Ribbet grew up around freshwater lake culture. Glittering Marshes Ribbet grew up in a complex brackish wetland. The cultures recognize kinship but are distinct — accent, tradition, and relationship with water all differ.

**Their relationship with the shards:**
Deepstilt Ribbet live directly above the densest shard concentration. For them, the glow beneath the water is as fundamental as the water itself. Ribbet children learn to read the glow — which channels run brightest (deepest shards), which are fading (shard deposits thinning). This knowledge overlaps entirely with the best fishing routes, since bioluminescent organisms concentrate near shard-light.

### Galapa
Shell-backed, deliberate, deeply rooted in Shellmound. Galapa culture in the marsh has developed distinct from Galapa elsewhere — the constant humidity has influenced shell texture (smoother, with a faint iridescence from bioluminescent mineral absorption over generations).

**The long view:** Galapa lifespan and their deliberate cultural pace makes them the marsh's institutional memory. Shellmound's elder is always Galapa — not by law, simply because Galapa elders have consistently been the ones who remember what was tried before.

**Craft:** Galapa shell-craft in the marsh incorporates bioluminescent materials in ways unique to this region — tools, containers, and decorative objects that hold a faint glow long after removal from the marsh.

### Fungril
Concentrated in Shellmound and Mistwatch, smaller presence elsewhere. The marsh is possibly the most natural environment for Fungril on the continent — warmth, moisture, organic richness, and the bioluminescent energy feeding their cultivation.

**The cultivation knowledge:** Fungril understanding of bioluminescent fungi is encyclopedic. They know which species grow where, why, and how to encourage or limit growth. This knowledge is the marsh's most significant export — the medical and alchemical properties of marsh fungi are sought across the continent.

**What they don't export:** The understanding of *why* the fungi here are more potent than elsewhere. The answer connects to the shards. The Fungril don't discuss it.

**The Veleth — Marsh Sight:**
Certain Canopy Run Simiah develop a capacity called *Veleth* ("the underneath listening") — a felt awareness of movement and disturbance through the marsh's bioluminescent shard-network. Not magic, not a spirit gift — something biological that developed over generations of living above shard-rich water and using subsonic tap-codes on branch systems rooted in the marsh mud. Those who develop it become the marsh's most valuable scouts and the quiet backbone of the guide system. Roughly one in fifteen to twenty Canopy Run Simiah develops meaningful sensitivity; profound practitioners are rare enough to be legendary within the settlement.

### Humans (Edge)
Primarily at Reedgate and scattered in smaller edge communities. Generations deep — not recent arrivals. Marsh-edge human culture is practical, guide-focused, and has thoroughly absorbed the shared understanding about the shards.

**The guide families:** Several human families in Reedgate have been licensed guides for four or five generations. They know the marsh as well as any Ribbet, through accumulated practical knowledge rather than instinct.

## THE GUIDE TRADITION

The licensed guide system is the marsh's primary interface with the outside world and its most important cultural institution.

**Licensing:** Granted by the elder council after demonstrated competency (routes, safety, creatures, seasonal changes). Reviewed annually. Can be revoked.

**What guides do:**
- Navigate outsiders safely through marsh routes
- Manage what outsiders see (certain areas simply aren't on the route)
- Translate marsh culture for people who don't understand it
- Report unusual outsider behavior to Reedgate's elder

**What guides don't do:**
- Take anyone to the deep shard concentrations
- Answer questions about the glow's source with anything other than "the marsh"
- Work without payment (guide fees fund the council's operating costs)

**The unspoken rule:** A guide who takes someone somewhere they shouldn't go loses their license permanently and the community's trust indefinitely. This has happened twice in living memory. Both people left the marsh and didn't return.

## CULTURE AND VALUES

**The marsh provides.** Not passively — the marsh provides to those who learn its rhythms. Work with it, it sustains you. Work against it, it doesn't.

**Patience.** The marsh operates on its own schedule. Flooding comes when it comes. Channels shift when they shift. Fighting this is exhausting and pointless. Marsh people wait, read signs, and move when the marsh permits.

**The glow is home.** Bioluminescence isn't remarkable to marsh residents — it's domestic, like firelight. Children grow up sleeping above glowing water. The darkness outsiders find in other places feels wrong here.

**Quiet hospitality.** Outsiders at Reedgate are welcomed, fed, and offered guides. Outsiders who push into the deep marsh uninvited find it unwelcoming in ways that are hard to attribute to specific cause.

## RELIGION

**Path of Spirits (universal):**
The marsh spirits are old and specific. The channel spirits, the fog spirit (called the White Breath — present every dawn), the creature spirits. Most residents maintain personal relationships with local spirits through small daily offerings — a bit of caught fish returned to the water, a piece of fungi left at a root system.

**The Shard-Light (informal, not a religion):**
No formal worship of the shards, but a reverent relationship. The light beneath the water is acknowledged at certain moments — births (a Ribbet infant's first water should be shard-lit water), deaths (Deepstilt dead are lowered into the glowing water), major decisions (elders meet above the brightest channel for important votes).
