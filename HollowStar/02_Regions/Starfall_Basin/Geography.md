# Geography and Environment - Starfall Basin

---

## THE BASIN DESERT

### Physical Landscape

**Scale:** ~400 miles east-west, ~300 miles north-south (one of the larger regions)

**Terrain Types:**

**Rocky Badlands (Northern & Western):**
- Eroded rock formations, mesas, canyon systems
- Dramatic but navigable
- Canyons provide shade (precious) and ambush points (dangerous)
- Ancient petroglyphs on canyon walls (who made them?)

**Salt Flats (Eastern):**
- Vast white expanses of dried salt lake beds
- Blinding in daylight (reflective like Glass Flats but white)
- Completely flat (navigation by landmarks impossible — use stars)
- Hidden sinkholes beneath salt crust (step through = buried)

**Central Depression:**
- Basin geology (water drains here naturally)
- The Endless Eye sits at lowest point
- Surrounding terrain slopes gently toward lake
- Ring of scrubland (sparse but present) around lake shores

**Canyon Systems:**
- **The Redwall Canyons** (north): Deep red sandstone, Katari tribes live here
- **The Whispering Cuts** (west): Narrow slot canyons, wind creates eerie sounds
- **The Bone Roads** (south): Ancient dried riverbeds used as travel routes

### Climate

**Temperature:**
- Summer day: 100-120°F (brutal but survivable with water)
- Summer night: 70-80°F (travel time for desert folk)
- Winter day: 70-85°F (pleasant by local standards)
- Winter night: 40-50°F (cold enough to kill unprepared travelers)

**Rain:**
- Rare (3-4 times per year, usually brief)
- When it rains: Celebration at The Endless Eye (festivals, rituals)
- Flash floods in canyons (deadly if caught in them)

**Wind:**
- Constant low-level wind (desiccating)
- Sandstorms (several per year, less deadly than Glass Storms but still dangerous)
- **The Breath of the Basin:** Hot thermal winds rising from valley floor — disorienting

**Comparison to Desert of Glass:**
- Less extreme than Glass Flats (survivable for most ancestries with preparation)
- More populated (water sources exist if you know where to look)
- Caravans cross it regularly (established routes, dangerous but not suicidal)

## THE ENDLESS EYE LAKE

### Physical Description

**Shape:** Roughly oval, ~8 miles across at widest point

**Depth:**
- **Shallow edges:** 5-20 feet (surface city docks, fishing)
- **Middle depths:** 50-200 feet (Ribbet residential areas)
- **The Deeps:** 400-600 feet (where Hollostar rests, restricted)

**Water Quality:**
- Freshwater (miraculous in desert context)
- Crystal clear to ~100 feet depth
- Below that: Increasingly dark, cold, pressurized
- **Faintly luminescent** (Hollostar energy diffuses through water — subtle blue-green glow at night)

**The Shoreline:**
- Western shore: Surface city (docks, markets, buildings)
- Eastern shore: Quieter, residential, Fairy communities
- Northern shore: Agricultural terraces (freshwater farming)
- Southern shore: Merchant warehouses, caravan staging

**Water Temperature:**
- Surface: Warm (desert climate)
- Mid-depth: Cool (Ribbet prefer this zone)
- The Deeps: Cold and pressurized

### The Glow

**At night:** Lake emits faint blue-green luminescence from depth

**Causes:**
- Hollostar energy diffusing upward
- Bioluminescent organisms (thrive on Hollostar-fed ecosystem)
- Ribbet have bioluminescent markings that pulse in response to it

**Cultural significance:**
- The glow has been consistent for 700 years
- **Recently:** Slightly dimmer (scholars noticed, most haven't)
- Traditionalists see glow as the star's heartbeat
- If glow fades noticeably — panic will follow

### The Deeps

**The Hollostar's Resting Place:**

**Access:**
- Swim down 400+ feet (requires water-breathing or magic)
- Ribbet regularly descend (natural ability)
- Surface-dwellers need assistance (potions, Ribbet guides)
- The Merchant Circle controls access permits

**What's Down There:**
- The Hollostar itself: Embedded in lake floor (half-buried in sediment)
- **Still active:** Faint light emanates, water temperature slightly warmer nearby
- **Dimming:** Output measurably lower than 50 years ago (Ribbet elders remember it brighter)
- Ancient sediment layers surround it (700 years of lake floor accumulation)
- Strange fish and creatures unique to The Deeps (Hollostar-adapted, never studied)

**Restricted Access:**
- Merchant Circle permits required
- Only authorized researchers, Ribbet elders, council members
- Strictly enforced (Traditionalists ensure this)
- Penalty for unauthorized access: Exile

## NATURAL RESOURCES

### Water (Primary)

**The Endless Eye provides:**
- Fresh drinking water (most valuable resource in desert)
- Irrigation (terraced farms on north shore)
- Fishing (lake ecosystem thrives)
- Trade good (water caravans export it at premium prices)

**Desert Water Sources (Outside Lake):**
- **Hidden springs:** Known to desert tribes, guarded carefully
- **Seeps:** Rocky canyon walls sometimes weep moisture
- **Dew collection:** Practiced by nomads (cloth traps overnight condensation)
- **Cacti and succulents:** Emergency water source

### Food

**Lake:**
- **Eyefish:** Unique to The Endless Eye (translucent, faintly glowing, delicious)
- **Deepsharks:** Larger predators (dangerous to fish, prized meat)
- **Starshells:** Mollusks with iridescent shells (food + valuable crafting material)
- **Pearl mussels:** Ribbet cultivate (luxury export)

**Desert:**
- **Cacti:** Fruit, pads (edible), water
- **Dates:** Rare palms near water sources
- **Desert hares, lizards:** Hunted by tribes
- **Scorpions:** Edible if prepared correctly (tribal delicacy)

### Trade Goods

**Unique to region:**
- **Desert pearls:** Ribbet-cultivated, highly prized
- **Eyefish:** Delicacy (exported preserved in salt)
- **Starshell:** Iridescent material for jewelry, decoration
- **Hollostar water:** Rumored that water from The Deeps has healing properties (black market)
- **Canyon herbs:** Medicinal plants unique to Redwall Canyons

### Hollostar-Adjacent Resources

**Glow-stones:** Small rocks from lake bed that absorbed Hollostar energy over centuries
- Faintly luminescent
- Sold as novelties, some believe magical properties
- Harvesting regulated (Merchant Circle taxes it)

## FLORA & FAUNA

### Desert Plants

**Ironwood Cactus:** Tall, broad, water-storing
- Primary water source for desert travelers
- Wood harder than most timber (valuable)
- Spines used as needles by tribes

**Nightbloom:** Flowers only at night (closed during day heat)
- Beautiful, fragrant
- Used in perfumes (exported)
- Guides travelers (blooms toward water sources, old belief — actually somewhat true)

**Dustsage:** Gray-green scrub
- Aromatic (burned for ceremonies)
- Medicinal (fever treatment)
- Grows everywhere near water

**Redwall Vine:** Grows in canyon cracks
- Edible berries (tart, nutritious)
- Roots accessible water deep in rock

### Desert Animals

**Basin Scorpions:** Large (hand-sized), venomous
- Territorial but avoidable
- Venom used medicinally by tribes
- Respected, not feared (part of life)

**Dune Serpents:** Long, sand-colored, fast
- Ambush predators
- Non-venomous (constrict)
- Tribal symbol of patience

**Dust Eagles:** Large raptors, thermal riders
- Spot water from miles away
- Following them = navigation technique
- Sacred to some tribes

**Canyon Foxes:** Small, large-eared, nocturnal
- Clever, sometimes tamed
- Desert tribes consider them good luck

### Lake Animals

**Eyefish:** (Already mentioned above)
- Unique to lake
- Translucent bodies (organs visible)
- Faintly glow near The Deeps

**Deepsharks:** Apex predators
- Adapted to darkness (large eyes, sensitive lateral lines)
- Rarely come to surface
- Ribbet communities have arrangements (don't hunt where sharks den)

**Starshell Mollusks:** Large, iridescent shells
- Filter feed on Hollostar-energized water
- Unique iridescence (no equivalent anywhere)
- Ribbet farm them on the lake floor

**Glimmerfish (Schools):** Tiny bioluminescent fish
- Create light shows in mid-depths
- Ribbet children swim with them (safe, beautiful)
- Tourism attraction (surface-dwellers rent diving equipment)

## NATURAL HAZARDS

### Desert

**Sandstorms:**
- Several per year
- Reduce visibility to zero
- Abrasive (damage exposed skin and equipment)
- Survival: Face away from wind, cover everything, wait it out

**Flash Floods:**
- Follow rare rain events
- Canyon systems become death traps instantly
- Rule: Never camp in dry riverbeds or canyon floors

**Salt Flat Sinkholes:**
- Thin salt crust over voids
- Unpredictable (no warning)
- Affected area: Eastern salt flats
- Caravan routes avoid worst areas (but shift over time)

**Heat Exhaustion:**
- Constant risk for non-adapted travelers
- Rule: 2 gallons water per day minimum, travel at night

### Lake Hazards

**The Deeps Pressure:**
- Below 200 feet: Dangerous for non-adapted beings
- Non-Ribbet need magic or equipment
- Current makes The Deeps navigation treacherous even for Ribbet

**Deepsharks:**
- Rare surface appearances (usually when something disturbs The Deeps)
- Warning signs: Water discoloration, Ribbet fleeing area

**The Lake Drop:**
- Shallow shelf drops suddenly to deep water
- Unwary swimmers can slip off edge
- Marked with buoys (maintained by Merchant Circle)
