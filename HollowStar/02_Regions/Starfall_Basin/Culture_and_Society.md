# Culture and Society - Starfall Basin

---

## THE ENDLESS EYE VALUES

### Commerce Above All
- Trade is the city's lifeblood and identity
- "What do you trade?" is the standard greeting
- Wealth is respected but **reputation matters more** (bad traders blacklisted)
- Contracts sacred (break one = social death)

### Hospitality as Business
- Travelers = income; treat them well or lose them
- Merchant Circle enforces hospitality standards
- But it's genuine too — cosmopolitan culture likes new people

### Coexistence
- All ancestries, all faiths, all backgrounds welcome
- Not idealistic — purely practical (diversity = more trade)
- Result: Genuinely tolerant culture
- Prejudice exists but is socially discouraged (bad for business)

### The Lake is Life
- No one forgets where the city came from
- Hollostar created this; destroying it destroys everything
- Underlies even the Profiteer faction (exploit it, yes — but not break it)

## SURFACE CULTURE

### Daily Life

**Morning:**
- Market opens at sunrise (merchants already setting up at dawn)
- Breakfast: Eyefish (common, cheap), flatbread, dates
- Water channels deliver fresh lake water to households

**Midday:**
- Quiet hours (heat — even here, midday brutal)
- Shuttered shops, siestas
- Markets reopen late afternoon

**Evening:**
- Most active time (cooler, social)
- Markets busy, taverns fuller
- Rooftop gatherings (neighbors, families)
- Festival lights (Fairy communities light decorative displays)

**Night:**
- Some commerce continues (night markets for desert-arrived caravans)
- Entertainment (music, storytelling)
- The lake glows below (visible from shore — beautiful)

### Food
- **Eyefish:** Staple (grilled, smoked, dried)
- **Starshell:** Delicacy (expensive, special occasions)
- **Desert dates and cactus fruits:** Sweet components
- **Imported grain:** From [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]] (expensive but necessary)
- **Preserved goods:** Caravans bring variety

### Fashion
- Light fabrics (cotton, linen — heat appropriate)
- Bright colors (contrast with desert muted tones)
- Pearl jewelry common (Ribbet trade makes it accessible)
- Fairy-influenced: Flower adornments, living accessories popular

### Entertainment
- **Water diving:** Sport — how deep can you go? (non-Ribbet compete)
- **Boat racing:** Lake surface races (festival events)
- **Desert storytelling:** Traveling performers bring tales from everywhere
- **The Glimmer Shows:** Ribbet guide glimmerfish schools into visible patterns (paid spectacle)

## UNDERWATER CULTURE (Ribbet)

### Slower Rhythm
- Water resistance changes movement; Ribbet move deliberately
- Contemplative mindset — less hurried than surface
- Long conversations, patience valued

### Bioluminescence as Language
- Ribbet skin markings pulse with emotion/intent
- Surface-dwellers can't read it (private communication layer)
- Polite to "dim" markings around surface guests (equivalent to lowered voice)

### Oral History
- Ribbet elders remember lake's founding (generation-to-generation)
- 700 years of detailed oral history (remarkable accuracy)
- Elders recite history at festivals (underwater, surface-dwellers watch through water in boats)

### The Pearl Tradition
- Each Ribbet cultivates one pearl over lifetime (from birth)
- Given to closest partner/friend at death
- "Their pearl" = their legacy
- Trade pearls are separate (farmed commercially)

### Festivals (Shared Surface/Underwater)
- **The Star's Birth** (anniversary of Hollostar fall): Biggest festival
  - Surface: Lights, music, feasting
  - Underwater: Ribbet gather at The Deeps, songs to the star
- **The Rain Festival** (whenever it rains): Spontaneous celebration
- **The Harvest Moon:** Pearl presentations, new trade agreements signed

## DESERT TRIBAL CULTURE

### Endurance Values
- Surviving the desert proves worth
- Complaining = weakness (same as Desert of Glass culture)
- Generosity with water (highest virtue — sharing scarce resource)

### Navigation Knowledge = Status
- Knowing safe routes = power and respect
- Tribal leaders often defined by route knowledge, not combat
- Shared selectively (knowledge is valuable)

### Water Etiquette
- **Never refuse water** to someone in need (capital offense in tribal law)
- **Ask before drinking** from unknown sources (might be claimed)
- **Thank the source** (religious/superstitious but universal)

### Night Culture
- Most activity at night (cooler)
- Stories told around fires
- Relationships formed in darkness (daytime too hot for socializing)
- Children learn by starlight (astronomy = navigation = survival)

## RELIGION

### All Faiths Welcome
- No state religion, no enforcement
- Economic logic: Diverse travelers → need diverse temples

### What's Present

**Six-Fold Faith:** ~30% (Wanderer most popular — travel, trade)

**The First Flame Faith:**
- ~35% (growing)
- **The Guiding Light** dominant denomination (contemplative, non-judgmental — perfect fit)
- Meditation chambers near lake shore (beautiful at night — glow visible)

**Path of Spirits:** ~20%
- Desert tribes especially
- Lake spirits honored by Ribbet (their own tradition, compatible)
- The star has a spirit (Path of Spirits says so; Ribbet believe something similar)

**Individual/Hybrid:** ~15%
- Personal beliefs mixing elements
- Common among longtime Endless Eye residents

### Unique Beliefs

**The Star's Breath:**
- The faint luminescence = star breathing
- When it glows, it lives; when it dims, it weakens
- Not affiliated with any formal faith — universal belief here

**Ribbet Deep-Faith:**
- Not formally Path of Spirits, but similar
- The star gave life; the lake is alive; the water connects all things
- The Deeps is sacred space (not just restricted — holy)

## LAWS & JUSTICE

### Merchant Circle Law

**Trade Laws (strictly enforced):**
- False weights and measures: Fine + reputation damage
- Contract breach: Debt enforcement + blacklisting
- Theft from traders: Exile (severe — worse than prison)

**Social Laws (moderately enforced):**
- Violence: Proportional response (fines or exile)
- Discrimination: Fines (rare prosecution but exists)

**The Deeps Laws (absolutely enforced):**
- Unauthorized access: Exile (no exceptions)
- Disturbing The Deeps area: Exile
- Attempting to harm/study star without permit: Exile

**Circle Guards:**
- Professional security force (~150 active)
- Mixed ancestry (deliberate — reflects city's diversity)
- Paid well (Merchant Circle invests in order)

### Desert Tribal Law
- Each tribe self-governing
- Universal: Never deny water to someone dying
- Inter-tribal disputes: Arbitrated at The Endless Eye (neutral ground)
