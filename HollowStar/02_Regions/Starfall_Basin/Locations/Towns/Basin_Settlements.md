# Starfall Basin - Interior Settlements

Settlements around the Endless Eye lake and desert interior.

---

## LAKESIDE SETTLEMENTS

### Stillwater (~800)

**Location:** Southern shore of Endless Eye  
**Identity:** Sheltered cove, fishing, boat operations  

Stillwater sits in a sheltered cove on the Endless Eye's southern shore. The town operates fishing boats and provides services for lake travelers.

**Economy:** Lake fishing, boat operations, waystation services

**Character:** Quiet, focused on lake rather than desert, benefits from freshwater proximity

**Population:** Mixed (human ~60%, Katari ~20%, others ~20%)

---

### Dusthaven (~600)

**Location:** Eastern shore of Endless Eye  
**Identity:** Caravanserai, main arrival point  

Dusthaven is the primary arrival point for caravans from Central Heartlands. The town operates the largest caravanserai in the basin.

**Economy:** Caravanserai operations, supplies, guide services, caravan coordination

**Character:** Transient population, always travelers, connection point between desert and outside world

**Population:** Permanent residents mixed, transient population high

---

### Redwall Post (~400)

**Location:** Northwestern shore of Endless Eye  
**Identity:** Katari interface, canyon trade  

Redwall Post sits near the canyon systems (the Redwalls) where Katari tribes operate. The post serves as interface between settled lake communities and nomadic canyon dwellers.

**Economy:** Trade facilitation, Katari goods, desert knowledge services

**Character:** Frontier outpost, respects Katari traditions, facilitates rather than controls

**Population:** Mixed, significant Katari presence

---

## NOMADIC TRIBES

### Redwall Katari (~1,200)

**Location:** Northwestern basin, canyon systems  
**Identity:** Canyon navigators, traditional Katari culture  

The Redwall Katari live in the red-stone canyon labyrinths northwest of the Endless Eye. They maintain traditional nomadic culture while trading with settled communities.

**Culture:** Traditional Katari navigation, oral history, canyon expertise, seasonal movement patterns

**Relationship to Endless Eye:** Trade partners, maintain independence, resist Merchant Circle authority

---

### Saltwalkers (~800)

**Location:** Southern basin, salt flats  
**Identity:** Salt flat experts, mineral gatherers  

The Saltwalkers move across the southern salt flats, gathering salt and minerals for trade. They know which areas are safe and which will kill.

**Culture:** Salt flat survival expertise, mineral knowledge, harsh conditions adaptation

**Trade:** High-quality salt, rare minerals, desert survival knowledge

---

### Windrunners (~500)

**Location:** Northern basin, open hardpan  
**Identity:** Speed specialists, northern territory  

The Windrunners move across the northern hardpan - flat, open ground where they can travel fast. The tribe values speed and endurance.

**Culture:** Endurance running, fast travel, open space living, minimalist lifestyle

**Trade:** Speed delivery services, northern route knowledge

---

### Starwatchers (~300)

**Location:** Western basin near Glass Flats border  
**Identity:** Sky observers, astronomical knowledge  

The Starwatchers maintain observation sites in the western basin near the Desert of Glass. They watch the stars and track celestial patterns.

**Culture:** Astronomical observation, pattern recognition, long-term record keeping

**Knowledge:** Star movements, celestial events, navigation by stars, time tracking

---

## WAYPOINTS & CAMPS

### Broken Stone (~100 permanent)

**Location:** Southern basin  
**Identity:** Salvage camp, route to Salt Shore  

Broken Stone is a salvage camp where ancient basin artifacts are found and traded. The camp sits on a route between the Endless Eye and Salt Shore.

**Economy:** Artifact salvage, waypoint services, small-scale trade

---

### Mournwatch (~150)

**Location:** Basin interior  
**Identity:** Small waystation  

Mournwatch is a small waystation providing services for desert travelers.

**Economy:** Waystation services, water sales, guide coordination

---

### Coppergate (~200)

**Location:** Basin interior  
**Identity:** Mining camp, small-scale operations  

Coppergate operates small-scale mining of desert minerals. The camp is semi-permanent, following mineral deposits.

**Economy:** Desert mining, mineral processing, small-scale trade

---

### Saltwind (~180)

**Location:** Desert interior  
**Identity:** Waypoint settlement  

Small waypoint providing basic services for travelers crossing the basin.

**Economy:** Water sales, basic supplies, direction guidance

---

### Calderford (~120)

**Location:** Desert interior  
**Identity:** Minimal settlement  

Minimal settlement, barely more than a permanent camp. Exists because travelers need stopping point.

**Economy:** Basic survival services

---

### Windrunners Camp (~variable)

**Location:** Northern basin, moves  
**Identity:** Nomadic tribe primary gathering site  

Not a permanent settlement - the Windrunners' primary gathering location. Population varies from 50 to entire tribe (~500).

---

## GEOGRAPHIC NOTES

**The Endless Eye:** Central freshwater lake in ancient impact basin - impossibility that exists anyway

**Desert zones:**
- Northern: Open hardpan (Windrunner territory)
- Northwestern: Red canyon systems (Redwall Katari)
- Southern: Salt flats (Saltwalker territory)  
- Western: Approaches Glass Flats border (Starwatcher territory)

**Trade routes:** Radiate from Endless Eye outward to Central Heartlands (east), Salt Shore (south), Desert of Glass (west), and northern approaches
