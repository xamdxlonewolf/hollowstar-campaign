# The Endless Eye

> *"Above the water: commerce and chaos. Below: peace and purpose. The lake holds both without contradiction."*

---

## OVERVIEW

**Population:** ~20,000 (15,000 surface, ~5,000 underwater)  
**Government:** The Merchant Circle  
**Founded:** ~700 years ago (Hollostar fall event)  
**Identity:** The Oasis City, The Trade Hub of the East, The Miracle

---

## THE SURFACE CITY

### Layout & Districts

**The Docklands (Western Shore):**
- Largest district, most chaotic
- Permanent docks (stone piers extending into lake)
- Caravan staging (wagons, animals, temporary camps)
- Warehouses stacked three stories high
- Smell: Fish, salt, exotic spice, animal
- Always busy (day and night shifts)

**The Market Quarter:**
- Central surface district
- Permanent market stalls (stone and timber)
- Goods from across continent converge here
- The Great Exchange: Covered market hall (permanent, impressive)
- Price boards updated daily (what's trading, what's scarce)

**The Hospitality Row:**
- Inns, taverns, bathhouses, entertainment
- Caters to every ancestry (specialized facilities)
- The Pearl & Fin: Finest inn (expensive, renowned)
- The Dustwalker's Rest: Budget option (caravan crowd)
- Bathhouses fed by lake water (luxury in desert context)

**The Residential Terraces (Eastern Shore):**
- Permanent residents (non-Ribbet)
- Quieter, more orderly
- Fairy communities concentrated here (aerial structures, hanging bridges)
- Gardens (freshwater irrigation, green in desert)

**The Agricultural Terraces (Northern Shore):**
- Farmed land fed by lake water
- Vegetables, herbs, date palms
- Community-managed (not private — Merchant Circle oversees)
- Produces ~30% of city's food needs

**The Scholar's Quarter:**
- Lumencrest-adjacent research station (tolerated, watched)
- Libraries, observation posts
- First Flame Scholarly Flame presence
- Guiding Light denomination temple (most popular faith here)
- [[04_Factions/Starfall Seekers|Starfall Seekers]] maintain office

### Architecture

**Surface Style:**
- Sandstone and lake-stone (pale yellow, cream)
- Flat roofs (heat dissipation, rooftop living)
- Interior courtyards (shade, privacy, small gardens)
- Water channels running through streets (constant supply, cooling effect)
- Bridges over channels (foot traffic everywhere)

**Fairy Additions:**
- Aerial walkways connecting rooftops
- Hanging gardens (vines cascading from high structures)
- Lightweight construction (bamboo, woven materials)
- Wind chimes (constant pleasant background sound)

**Height:**
- Mostly 2-3 stories (desert building tradition)
- Merchant Circle Hall: Tallest structure (5 stories, central location)
- The Beacon Tower: Lighthouse-like structure, lit at night (guides desert travelers)

### Life on the Surface

**Cosmopolitan:**
- Most diverse population outside [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Locations/Cities/Lumencrest|Lumencrest]]
- All faiths tolerated (no dominant religion enforced)
- All ancestries welcome (economics > prejudice)
- Language: Common tongue universal; dozens of others heard daily

**Commerce-focused:**
- Almost everyone involved in trade somehow
- Even residents not merchants still serve merchants (inns, food, repairs)
- "What do you trade?" common greeting

**Hospitable by necessity:**
- Travelers = money
- Reputation for hospitality carefully maintained
- Ripping off travelers = Merchant Circle discipline

## THE UNDERWATER COMMUNITY

### Who Lives Below

**Ribbet (primary residents):** ~3,500
- Natural water-breathers
- Built their city in the lake's middle depths (50-150 feet)
- Been here since the lake formed (700 years of continuous habitation)
- Consider the lake their ancestral home

**Ribbet-Fairy Mix (secondary residents):** ~800
- Offspring of Ribbet and Fairy unions (both present in region for centuries)
- Some can breathe underwater (Ribbet trait), some cannot
- Those who can: Live below; those who can't: Live on surface but visit family

**Other water-breathers (small numbers):** ~700
- Various ancestries with aquatic adaptation
- Some permanent residents, some seasonal

### The Underwater City - Lakehaven

**Location:** 50-150 feet depth, centered under The Endless Eye surface

**Construction:**
- Carved directly from lake floor bedrock
- Expanded over 700 years (constantly growing)
- Stone chambers, tunnels, open plazas (no roofs needed — water is "sky")
- Bioluminescent lighting (cultivated organisms + Hollostar glow from below)

**Districts:**
- **The Root Quarter:** Oldest section (700 years old, stone worn smooth)
- **The Pearl Gardens:** Starshell and pearl mussel farms
- **The Deep Market:** Trade with surface (goods lowered in waterproof containers)
- **The Elder Halls:** Ribbet council chambers, archives, sacred spaces
- **The Nursery Pools:** Shallower areas (warmer, safer) for young Ribbet

**Daily Life:**
- Ribbet live entirely underwater (surface visits occasional, social)
- Food: Lake fish, cultivated shellfish, seaweed (traded with surface for variety)
- Entertainment: Current-riding (sport), depth diving, bioluminescent art
- Communication with surface: Written messages in sealed containers, Ribbet couriers, some surface-dwellers learn to dive

**Culture:**
- Slower pace than surface (water resistance changes movement, mindset)
- Contemplative (Guiding Light denomination popular)
- Deep memory (Ribbet elders remember the lake's founding — oral history rich)
- Privacy (surface-dwellers rarely visit — respectful distance maintained)

### Surface-Underwater Relations

**Interdependent:**
- Surface provides: Manufactured goods, variety, outside information
- Underwater provides: Pearls, Eyefish, Starshells, Hollostar access, lake knowledge

**Mutual respect:**
- Ribbet have full council representation (not second-class citizens)
- Surface-dwellers learn basic waterproof communication
- Shared festivals (above and below water simultaneously)

**Tensions:**
- Surface growth (expanding docks) sometimes disturbs underwater areas
- Merchant Circle occasionally prioritizes surface commerce over Ribbet concerns
- Ribbet protective of The Deeps (surface-dwellers too curious)

## THE DEEPS

### Access & Control

**Location:** 400-600 feet below surface

**Getting There:**
- Only natural water-breathers can safely descend without assistance
- Depth pressure dangerous for most (even Ribbet need training)
- Merchant Circle issues permits (tightly controlled)
- Only Ribbet elders, authorized researchers, council members

**Physical Environment:**
- Cold (near-freezing at deepest points)
- Dark (Hollostar provides only illumination)
- Pressurized (non-adapted beings cannot function)
- Thick sediment floor (700 years of lake-bottom accumulation)

### The Hollostar

**Appearance:**
- Half-buried in sediment (only upper portion visible)
- Roughly 15 feet across (larger than most shards)
- Faint blue-white light emanates from cracks
- Surrounded by unique fauna (Deeps creatures found nowhere else)

**Activity:**
- Still active (lake exists because of it)
- **Dimming:** Output measurably lower than records show 50 years ago
- Water temperature around it: Slightly warmer (still generating heat)
- Ribbet elders: "It used to be brighter. We remember."

**The Crisis (Early Stages):**
- Lake level: Down ~6 inches from 10-year average (scholars' measurements)
- Surface residents: Haven't noticed yet
- Ribbet elders: Know something is wrong
- Merchant Circle scholars: Quietly alarmed, not yet public

**What Happens If It Fails:**
- Lake shrinks (gradually then catastrophically)
- Underwater city exposed (uninhabitable)
- Surface city loses water supply (desert without water = death)
- Trade routes collapse (no reason to come here)
- 700-year civilization ends

## THE MERCHANT CIRCLE

### Structure

**Council Composition (9 seats):**
- **3 Merchant seats:** Elected by registered traders (wealth-weighted voting)
- **2 Ribbet seats:** Elected by underwater community
- **2 Fairy seats:** Elected by surface Fairy community
- **1 Wanderer seat:** Rotating (represents desert tribes and passing traders)
- **1 Scholar seat:** Appointed by research institutions

**Current Council:**

**Merchant seats:**
- **Senior Merchant Voss Tanakar** (human, age 54) — pragmatist, pro-study
- **Merchant Liria Dawnpetal** (Fairy, age 203) — traditionalist, anti-exploitation
- **Merchant Brek** (Ribbet, age 31) — profiteer, controversial

**Ribbet seats:**
- **Elder Suu** (Ribbet, age 89) — oldest council member, traditionalist
- **Voice Kessa** (Ribbet, age 44) — pragmatist, advocates for underwater community

**Fairy seats:**
- **Wingspeaker Aelindra** (Fairy, age 156) — environmentalist, spiritual
- **Thornwing** (Fairy, age 78) — merchant-aligned, practical

**Wanderer seat (current):**
- **Dust-Chief Orah** (Katari, age 38) — desert tribe representative, skeptical of all factions

**Scholar seat:**
- **Researcher Pell Ashwick** (human, age 41) — desperate to study The Deeps, frustrated by restrictions

### How It Functions

**Meetings:** Weekly (formal), emergency sessions as needed

**Decisions:** Majority vote (5 of 9)

**Powers:**
- Trade regulation (tariffs, access, licensing)
- Lake access control (who goes to The Deeps)
- Law enforcement (Circle Guards)
- Dispute resolution

**Limitations:**
- Cannot override Ribbet rights to their underwater home
- Cannot sell or grant ownership of the lake/Hollostar
- Cannot expel ancestral residents

### The Three Factions

**Traditionalists (Elder Suu, Wingspeaker Aelindra, Liria Dawnpetal):**
- "The star gave us the lake. It will sustain the lake. Our job is gratitude, not interference."
- Oppose aggressive study
- Believe disturbing The Deeps will accelerate decline
- Support spiritual/[[Path of Spirits]] approach (commune with star's spirit)

**Pragmatists (Voss Tanakar, Voice Kessa, Pell Ashwick):**
- "The star is dimming. We have years at most. We must understand why."
- Favor careful, respectful research
- Want to expand access to The Deeps for study
- Currently dominant on council (5 votes with Dust-Chief Orah uncommitted)

**Profiteers (Merchant Brek, Thornwing):**
- "Access to The Deeps is valuable. We should monetize it before it's gone."
- Sell research permits to outside parties (especially [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Locations/Cities/Lumencrest|Lumencrest]])
- Most controversial (accused of selling out community)
- Minority but dangerous (Lumencrest funds them quietly)

## THE LAKE CRISIS (CURRENT STATE)

### What's Known (Scholars Only)

- Lake level: 6 inches below 10-year average
- Rate: Consistent (~0.5 inches per month)
- At current rate: Noticeable to public in ~12 months
- The Deeps Hollostar output: Down ~8% from records
- Projection: Critical in 10-15 years if trend continues

### What's NOT Known

- Whether decline is linear or exponential
- Whether it can be reversed
- What exactly causes it (star failing, or something drawing on it?)
- Whether [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Locations/Cities/Lumencrest|Lumencrest]]'s Hollostar dimming is related

### Response So Far

**Merchant Circle:** Classified the data (not public)

**Ribbet Elders:** Quietly preparing (what preparation looks like is unclear)

**Lumencrest Delegation:** Increased pressure to "collaborate" (they suspect something)

**[[04_Factions/Starfall Seekers|Starfall Seekers]]:** Sniffing around (their instruments detect something)

**[[Path of Spirits]] practitioners:** Warning that the star's spirit is distressed (being ignored)
