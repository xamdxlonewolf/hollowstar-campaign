# Tidewall

**Population:** ~18,000  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Silverbelt_Coast/Silverbelt_Coast_Index|Silverbelt Coast]]  
**Location:** Northern coast, deep harbor  
**Government:** Council of Banners (Four Houses coordinate here)  
**Founded:** ~800 years ago by House Vethara  
**Character:** Naval power first, commerce second

---

## OVERVIEW

Tidewall is the Silverbelt Coast's largest city and seat of the Council of Banners — where the Four Houses meet to coordinate regional decisions. Unlike most ports that grew from fishing villages, Tidewall was built deliberately as fortress-harbor by House Vethara ~800 years ago.

**The defining feature:** Massive sea walls (the "Tide Walls" that gave city its name) protect the harbor from storms and enemies. Naval power dominates. This is city built for war that became prosperous through peace — but never forgot its martial origins.

**Current reality:** Tidewall is largest, wealthiest, most powerful Silverbelt city. House Vethara controls it directly. The Council of Banners meets here, giving Vethara home-field advantage in regional politics. Other Houses resent this but can't challenge it effectively.

---

## HISTORY

### The Founding (800 Years Ago)

House Vethara, newly risen to power, needed secure northern base. They chose this location for:
- Deep natural harbor (can handle large vessels)
- Defensible headland position
- Northern approaches control
- Strategic river valley access

**The construction:** Vethara spent enormous resources building sea walls, fortifications, naval facilities. The Tide Walls took 30 years to complete. The investment paid off — city became unassailable.

### The Unification Wars (280-260 Years Ago)

When Four Houses fought for regional control, Tidewall was Vethara's stronghold. The city survived multiple sieges, its walls never breached. This defensive success contributed to the stalemate that forced Treaty of Four Banners.

**Why the treaty:** No House could take Tidewall by force. Vethara couldn't expand beyond it effectively. Mutual exhaustion led to negotiated settlement.

### The Council Era (260 Years Ago to Present)

Treaty established Council of Banners meeting in Tidewall. This was compromise — Vethara controlled city but all Houses had voice in chamber. The arrangement gave Vethara advantage while providing legitimate forum for coordination.

**Growth period:** Peace enabled commerce. Naval facilities attracted merchant shipping. Harbor fees provided revenue. City grew from ~8,000 (post-war) to ~18,000 (present).

**Harbor expansion (8 months ago):** Vethara funded major improvements. Capacity increased 30%, port fees rising accordingly. Other Houses suspicious of Vethara's growing economic dominance.

## GEOGRAPHY & LAYOUT

**The harbor:** Deep, protected by massive breakwaters and the Tide Walls. Can accommodate 60+ vessels simultaneously. Two harbors actually:
- **Naval Harbor** (restricted): Warship berths, military facilities, Four Houses fleet
- **Commercial Harbor** (open): Merchant shipping, trade facilities, civilian operations

**The Tide Walls:** 40-foot stone barriers protecting harbor from north and east (storm directions). Engineering marvel, still maintained 800 years later. Symbol of Vethara's power and city's martial character.

**City structure:** Built on headland and surrounding low hills. Districts arranged by function and status.

---

## DISTRICTS

### The Fortress Quarter (Central, ~2,000 residents)

**Character:** Military, formal, imposing

The original Vethara stronghold. Stone fortress, Council chambers, military headquarters, Vethara family estate.

**Key locations:**
- **The Keep** - Ancient fortress, Vethara family residence
- **Council Hall** - Where Four Houses meet for Banner Council
- **Naval Command** - Coordinates Four Houses fleet operations
- **The Barracks** - Housing for permanent garrison (~500 soldiers)

**Council of Banners:** Meeting hall seats representatives from all Four Houses. Vethara provides space, other Houses provide members. Quarterly meetings for major decisions, emergency sessions as needed.

**The power dynamics:** Vethara controls the city, other Houses have seats at table. This creates constant tension — they need to meet here but resent Vethara's home advantage.

### The Naval District (Harborside, ~4,000 residents/workers)

**Character:** Military precision, constant activity

Where Four Houses maintain their warships. Shipyards, drydocks, naval facilities, sailor housing. This is functional military infrastructure, not civilian port.

**Key locations:**
- **The Yards** - Ship construction and repair facilities
- **Naval Arsenal** - Weapons, supplies, equipment storage
- **Officers' Quarter** - Housing for ship captains and naval command
- **The Wharves** - 18 warship berths plus auxiliary vessel moorings

**The Fleet:** Combined Four Houses naval power:
- House Vethara: 18 warships (largest presence, home port)
- House Mourne: 6 warships (maintain presence, most ships elsewhere)
- House Aldric: 8 warships (training command based here)
- House Caldren: 4 warships (reduced presence during succession crisis)

**Security:** Heavily guarded. Civilian access restricted. The Houses don't advertise military capabilities.

### The Merchant Quarter (Commercial Harbor, ~7,000 residents)

**Character:** Prosperous, cosmopolitan, commercial

Where commerce happens. Merchant shipping uses this harbor. Trade companies operate here. Warehouses store goods. This is what makes Tidewall wealthy.

**Key locations:**
- **Commercial Docks** - 40+ berths for merchant vessels
- **Warehouse Row** - Storage facilities along harborfront
- **Traders' Square** - Central marketplace, goods exchange
- **The Harbor Exchange** - Where merchants negotiate contracts, arrange shipping

**The economy:** Tidewall charges port fees for all commercial traffic. These fees fund city operations and flow to House Vethara. Recent harbor expansion means increased capacity and higher fees.

**Notable businesses:**
- **Vethara & Sons Trading** - House-owned company dominates import/export
- **The Northern Star** - Shipping company (Mourne interests)
- **Caldren River Freight** - Was major operation, struggling since House crisis

**Foreign presence:** Merchants from Lumencrest, L'Empire Verdienne, Saltmere, even distant ports maintain offices here. Tidewall is regional trade hub.

### The Craftsmen's Quarter (~3,000 residents)

**Character:** Skilled work, guild organization, middle-class

Artisans, craftspeople, skilled workers who supply both naval and civilian needs. Shipwrights, ropemakers, sailmakers, metalworkers, provisioners.

**The Guilds:** Strong guild system controls quality and training. Guild membership required for most trades. This maintains standards and Vethara influence (guilds are loyal).

**Key locations:**
- **Guild Hall Row** - Professional associations maintain buildings
- **The Workshops** - Clustered by trade (metalworking district, textile district, etc.)
- **Market Street** - Where craftsmen sell finished goods

**Culture:** Pride in skill, traditional methods valued, apprenticeship system rigorous. These are people who prosper through competence.

### The Commons (~2,000 residents)

**Character:** Working-class, crowded, functional

Laborers, sailors, dock workers, servants. People who make city function but don't profit greatly from it.

**Living conditions:** Dense housing, shared facilities, basic amenities. Not slums — Vethara maintains standards — but clearly not prosperous districts.

**The tension:** Watching wealthy merchants and naval officers while doing hard labor creates resentment. So far, Vethara's firm but fair governance prevents serious unrest.

## THE COUNCIL OF BANNERS

**Purpose:** Coordinate Four Houses regional decisions without centralized authority. Each House maintains independence; Council handles shared interests.

**Meeting schedule:** 
- Quarterly regular sessions (mandatory)
- Emergency sessions (any House can call with good reason)
- Naval coordination meetings (monthly, fleet operations)

**Structure:** Each House sends representatives:
- House Vethara: Admiral-Lord Cassius (61) personally attends
- House Mourne: Lady-Admiral Sera (44) personally attends
- House Aldric: Lord-Admiral Brennan (57) sends senior captain (rarely attends personally)
- House Caldren: Currently Steward Orvyn Glast (succession crisis)

**Decisions require:** 3 of 4 Houses agreement for major actions (military deployment, treaty, major infrastructure). 4 of 4 for constitutional changes (altering Four Houses system).

**Current deadlock:** Caldren succession crisis means they effectively can't vote decisively. This leaves 3-House system that struggles to reach agreement.

### What Council Handles

**Shared military operations:** Piracy suppression, foreign threats, regional defense

**Trade coordination:** Avoid destructive competition, maintain regional prosperity

**River town disputes:** Mediate conflicts over territory, resources, authority

**External relations:** Coordinate response to Lumencrest, Tide King, foreign powers

**What it doesn't handle:** Internal House matters, territorial boundaries, succession disputes, economic competition

## ECONOMY

**Port fees:** Primary revenue. All commercial vessels pay for harbor access. Recent expansion increased capacity and fees.

**Naval contracts:** Shipbuilding, provisioning, repairs for all Four Houses generate significant business.

**Trade hub:** Goods flow through Tidewall en route to interior, other ports, distant markets.

**Manufacturing:** Skilled craftsmen produce high-value goods (ships, equipment, luxury items).

**The Vethara advantage:** House Vethara benefits disproportionately from Tidewall's prosperity. Port fees, trade dominance, manufacturing — all strengthen Vethara position relative to other Houses.

**Current concern:** If Vethara becomes too dominant economically, will other Houses accept it? Growing wealth gap creates political tension.

## CULTURE & DAILY LIFE

### Naval Tradition

**Service requirement:** Every Four Houses heir must complete minimum 1 year active naval service before inheriting. Many serve in Tidewall-based vessels or train at naval facilities here.

**The culture:** Military discipline pervades city. Even civilian districts maintain order, cleanliness, efficiency. This is city that never forgot martial origins.

**Respect for service:** Naval officers are social elite. Ship captains are celebrities. Veteran sailors are respected. Military culture shapes everything.

### Social Hierarchy

**Top:** Four Houses nobility (especially Vethara family)

**Upper:** Naval officers, successful merchants, guild masters

**Middle:** Craftsmen, guild members, ships' officers, skilled workers

**Lower:** Sailors, laborers, servants, dock workers

**Mobility:** Possible through naval service or guild excellence. Merit matters but connections help.

### Food & Drink

**Seafood:** Obviously abundant, but prepared with more sophistication than typical fishing port.

**The Officers' Club specialty:** "Admiral's Feast" — seven-course meal featuring local seafood prepared with imported spices. Expensive, ostentatious, delicious.

**Guild district:** Each guild maintains traditional recipes passed through generations. Metalworkers' Guild feast is legendary (held annually, jealously guarded invitation list).

**Common fare:** Hearty stews, fresh bread, fish (cheaper cuts than upper districts eat), local beer.

### Festivals

**Banner Day (spring):** Commemorates Treaty of Four Banners, all Houses display colors, naval parade, ceremonial Council meeting.

**Tide Walls Festival (summer):** Celebrates city's founding, honors House Vethara, competitive rowing races in harbor.

**Winter Watch (midwinter):** Naval tradition marking storm season, honors sailors lost at sea, solemn ceremony with all fleet.

## MILITARY PRESENCE

**Permanent garrison:** ~500 soldiers (House Vethara maintains them)

**Naval fleet:** ~36 warships total (varies as Houses deploy ships elsewhere)

**Town guard:** ~100 members (maintain civilian order, separate from military)

**Coastal defenses:** Artillery positions on Tide Walls, signal towers, patrol boats

**Capability:** Tidewall can defend against any realistic naval assault. The Tide Walls, fleet presence, and garrison make it essentially unassailable from sea. Land assault would require massive force, difficult logistics, and face formidable defenders.

## CURRENT TENSIONS

### House Caldren Crisis

Caldren succession deadlock means they can't participate effectively in Council. This creates 3-House system that struggles with decisions requiring 3 of 4 agreement. Currently everything deadlocks 2-1 or decisions require Caldren abstention (which creates legitimacy questions).

**Impact on Tidewall:** Council meetings are tense. Other Houses blame each other for crisis. Vethara wants it resolved. Mourne pressuring Aldric to support their candidate. Nothing moves forward.

### Vethara Economic Dominance

Recent harbor expansion, port fee increases, trade dominance — Vethara's wealth growing faster than other Houses. This creates resentment.

**Other Houses' concern:** Is Vethara using Council system to entrench dominance? Will they eventually be too powerful to challenge?

**Vethara's position:** We built this city. We maintain it. We deserve to prosper from it. Other Houses benefit too — stop complaining.

### The Grey Sails Mystery

Independent pirate fleet operating in Silverbelt waters. They never attack Vethara shipping. They do attack Aldric and Caldren vessels. Coincidence? Or is Vethara using privateers?

**Evidence:** Circumstantial but compelling. Grey Sails seem to know Vethara shipping schedules, avoid their patrols, target their rivals.

**Vethara denials:** Complete. Admiral-Lord Cassius insists he has no connection to Grey Sails. Nobody can prove otherwise.

**Impact:** Other Houses suspicious, trust eroding, Council cooperation harder.

**Grey Sails representative:** Embedded in Tidewall as intelligence contact (allegedly). Nobody knows who, but rumors persist.

### Lumencrest Pressure

Lumencrest wants increased timber from Verdant Crescent. They're offering Silverbelt Coast premium prices for helping pressure Green Covenant.

**Vethara's position:** Trade partner, maintain good relations, don't antagonize powerful continental city.

**Mourne's position:** Neutral on timber, uncomfortable with aggressive tactics.

**Aldric's position:** Oppose helping Lumencrest exploit forest.

**Result:** Council deadlocked, Lumencrest frustrated, relationship strained.

## RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER SETTLEMENTS

**Vetheris:** Second-largest Silverbelt city, House Mourne territory, cooperative but competitive with Tidewall.

**Harborpoint:** Southern port, growing rival, House Mourne-aligned, competing for commercial shipping.

**River towns:** Look to Tidewall for coordination, resent Vethara dominance but accept Four Houses system.

**Keeper's Bay (Salt Shore):** Mutual respect between large ports, minimal competition (different regions), occasional trade.

**Lumencrest:** Trade partner, political complication, relationship strained by timber pressure and Caldren crisis.
