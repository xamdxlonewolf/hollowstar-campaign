# Vetheris

**Population:** ~13,000  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Silverbelt_Coast/Silverbelt_Coast_Index|Silverbelt Coast]]  
**Location:** Northern coast, at the mouth of the Clearwater Run  
**Government:** House Vethara directly — Lord-Governor appointed by Admiral-Lord Cassius Vethara  
**Identity:** House Vethara's stronghold, the northern power, where Vethara money and influence are most visible

---

## What Vetheris Is

Vetheris does not pretend to be neutral. This is a Vethara city — House colors on the flags, House officers in the harbor authority, House interests shaping every major decision. The other three Houses maintain token presences here but everyone understands this is Vethara territory.

The city sits where the Clearwater Run empties into the Silver Belt Sea — excellent natural harbor, direct river access inland, strategic position on the northern coast. House Vethara has held this location for four generations and built it into the second-largest city on the Silverbelt Coast through a combination of aggressive trade development, naval investment, and making very clear that challenging their control here would be expensive.

The wealth is visible. Clean streets, maintained infrastructure, a merchant quarter that rivals anything in Tidewall. The naval yards are the finest on the coast outside Tidewall itself — three drydocks, a shipyard capable of building warships, and the supply chain to keep a significant fleet operational.

This is what House Vethara wants people to see when they think of Vethara power: competent, wealthy, unstoppable.

## The City Districts

**The Naval Quarter**  
The largest employer in the city. House Vethara's northern fleet is based here — approximately 20 warships and support vessels. The yards build and maintain Vethara's portion of the Council of Banners fleet plus merchant vessels contracted privately. The quality is excellent and the prices reflect it.

The naval quarter is organized, disciplined, and proud. Vethara officers maintain tight control. Workers are well-paid by coastal standards and know their employment depends on Vethara remaining dominant. This creates loyalty through pragmatism rather than sentiment.

**The River District**  
Where the Clearwater Run enters the city. River trade from inland territories arrives here — timber, agricultural goods, some mining products from the foothills further north. The district is mixed population, more diverse than the naval quarter, less tightly controlled by House authority.

Several Katari trading families have established permanent operations here, running the river-to-coast supply chains. Vethara tolerates this because the Katari make the trade work efficiently and don't involve themselves in House politics.

**The Merchant Quarter**  
Counting houses, factor offices, warehouses, the Guild Hall where the city's merchant association meets. This is where Vetheris's economic power is actually managed. The merchant families here are wealthy, well-connected, and almost all aligned with House Vethara through either family ties, business partnerships, or the calculation that opposing Vethara in Vethara's own city would be financial suicide.

The Guild Hall technically represents merchant interests independent of House authority. In practice it functions as an extension of Vethara's economic policy. The current Guild Master, **Theron Marks** (human, 58), is Admiral-Lord Cassius Vethara's cousin by marriage. This is not hidden information.

**The Heights**  
Residential district for Vetheris's elite — senior naval officers, wealthy merchants, the Lord-Governor's estate. Views of the harbor, the river, and the Silver Belt Sea. Architecture that demonstrates taste and expense without being ostentatious about either.

The Heights is where House Vethara's social power is maintained. Dinner invitations here matter. Being excluded from Heights social circles means being excluded from decision-making access. This is understood and ruthlessly exploited.

**The Lower City**  
Working-class residential. Naval yard workers, dock laborers, service trades. Dense, functional, maintained to a standard that keeps people housed and fed but not comfortable enough to forget who provides the housing and feeding.

The Lower City is loyal to Vethara in the way that employed populations are loyal to their employers when unemployment means leaving the city. It works. Vethara doesn't need love. Vethara needs a functioning workforce that shows up and does the work.

## Lord-Governor Petran Corvain

**Age:** 47 | **Ancestry:** Human | **Appointed:** 6 years ago

Corvain is Admiral-Lord Cassius Vethara's direct appointee and reports to Cassius on everything that matters. He is competent, administratively talented, and understands his role is to govern the city in ways that serve House interests without creating problems that require Cassius's personal attention.

He does this job well. The city runs smoothly. Trade flows efficiently. The naval yards stay busy. Problems are resolved before they escalate. Corvain is not beloved but he is effective, which is what Vethara values.

**His position:** Corvain understands he holds this position because Cassius trusts his competence, not his creativity. He does not make policy — he implements it. When situations arise that require judgment calls, he reports to Cassius and awaits instruction. This has kept him in position for six years while three previous governors lasted a combined four.

**His awareness:** Corvain knows House Vethara is maneuvering against House Caldren and preparing to absorb Caldren territory when the succession crisis resolves in Vethara's favor. He knows Captain Rynn Saltblood's connection to the Grey Sails exists even if he doesn't know the details. He knows Lumencrest representatives have been in Vetheris three times in the past year offering trade deals that would strengthen Vethara's position at the expense of House unity.

He implements what he's told to implement. He reports what he's told to report. He does not initiate.

## The Lumencrest Connection

Vetheris is the Silverbelt Coast's primary point of contact with Lumencrest's trade network. House Vethara has been cultivating this relationship deliberately — Lumencrest manufactures require coastal access, Vethara controls the best harbor north of Tidewall, mutual benefit follows.

**What this means practically:** Lumencrest goods move through Vetheris preferentially. Vethara merchants get favorable rates on Lumencrest-manufactured items. Information flows both directions — Vethara intelligence on Grey Sails activity and Silver Belt trade in exchange for Lumencrest intelligence on Central Heartlands developments.

**The political problem:** This relationship bypasses the Council of Banners. The other three Houses see Vethara's Lumencrest connection as a betrayal of Silverbelt independence. House Aldric in particular views it as the first step toward Lumencrest dominance of the coast. They're not entirely wrong.

**Admiral-Lord Cassius's position:** The Silverbelt Coast can either adapt to the reality that Lumencrest is the continent's dominant power or be bypassed by it. Vethara is adapting. If the other Houses choose pride over pragmatism, they will be left behind. This is business, not betrayal.

Lord Brennan Aldric considers this the most dangerous attitude on the coast and the thing most likely to destroy the Four Houses entirely.

## The Naval Yards

Three major facilities:

**Vethara Naval Works** — House-owned and operated. Builds warships exclusively for Vethara's fleet. Employs ~600 workers. The quality is exceptional and the designs prioritize speed and maneuverability — Vethara's fleet doctrine emphasizes hunting the Grey Sails rather than convoy defense.

**Clearwater Maritime** — Private yard, Vethara-aligned. Builds merchant vessels and accepts some contracts from the other Houses when they pay Vetheris rates. Employs ~400. Solid work, slightly faster turnaround than Tidewall's yards, slightly more expensive.

**The Compact Yard** — Smaller operation, established eight years ago by a consortium of Katari merchants. Specializes in river barges and shallow-draft vessels. Employs ~150. The only yard in Vetheris not directly controlled by Vethara interests, which is allowed to continue because the Compact Yard fills a niche the House yards don't care about.

## Current Tensions

**The Caldren Situation:** Vethara is actively positioning to absorb the most valuable pieces of Caldren territory once the succession crisis resolves. This is not subtle. Other Houses know. Vetheris is the staging point for this — where the resources are being assembled, where the legal frameworks are being prepared, where the military coordination would launch from if it becomes necessary.

Steward Orvyn Glast has sent three formal protests to Vetheris regarding Vethara vessels "accidentally" operating in Caldren-claimed waters. Lord-Governor Corvain has responded with three polite letters acknowledging the protests and doing nothing about them.

**House Mourne's Concern:** Lady-Admiral Sera Mourne has been watching Vethara's Lumencrest connection and Vetheris's expansion carefully. She views Vetheris as the physical manifestation of what happens when one House becomes too powerful — it stops needing the other Houses. She has been quietly strengthening her own positions and has considered approaching House Aldric about a defensive alliance.

She has not acted on this yet. Once she does, the Four Houses' political landscape changes significantly.

**The Grey Sails:** Vethara's aggressive anti-piracy doctrine has made Vetheris less attractive to Grey Sails operations than other coastal cities. This is deliberate. Vethara wants to be seen as the House that actually takes the pirate problem seriously, which makes the other Houses look either incompetent or compromised.

The Grey Sails have noticed this and adjusted operations accordingly. They still operate near Vetheris waters but with more caution. One Grey Sails captain was caught and hanged in Vetheris two years ago. The message was received.

## People

**Lord-Governor Petran Corvain** — see above. Effective administrator, Cassius's appointee, implements policy without initiating.

**Guild Master Theron Marks** — Human, 58, Cassius Vethara's cousin by marriage. Manages the Merchant Guild, coordinates Vetheris's economic policy with House interests. Competent and aware his position exists because family connection makes him trustworthy rather than because he's the most qualified merchant in the city.

**Yard-Master Kess Ironhull** — Dwarf, 73, runs the Vethara Naval Works. Built half the ships in Vethara's current fleet personally. Loyal to the House through professional pride — she builds the best ships on the coast and Vethara gives her the resources to keep doing it. That's the relationship.

**Harbor-Captain Lira Stone** — Human, 41, manages Vetheris harbor operations. Efficient, no-nonsense, treats every House equally which in Vetheris means giving Vethara priority while maintaining the appearance of neutrality. She's very good at this balance.

## Adventure Hooks

**"The Caldren Intercept"** — A Caldren merchant vessel carrying valuable cargo has been intercepted by Vethara patrol ships in disputed waters. The cargo is being held in Vetheris pending "investigation." Steward Glast hires the party to recover it through negotiation, theft, or leverage. What they discover is that the cargo contains something Vethara wants badly enough to risk open conflict.

**"The Lumencrest Contract"** — A Lumencrest trade delegation is arriving in Vetheris to finalize an agreement that would give Lumencrest preferential harbor access in exchange for military equipment. House Aldric wants this stopped. House Vethara wants it protected. The party is hired by one side — or both — and must navigate the situation without starting a war.

**"The Compact Yard Fire"** — A fire destroys part of the Compact Yard's facility. The Katari owners insist it was arson. The city investigation concludes it was accidental. The Katari hire the party to find out what actually happened. What they discover connects to Vethara's expansion plans and a group within House leadership who want the Compact Yard gone.

**"The Grey Sails Message"** — A Grey Sails representative approaches the party in Vetheris with a message for Admiral-Lord Cassius Vethara. The message is not a threat. It's an offer. The party must decide whether to deliver it, what delivering it means, and what happens when Cassius's response suggests he's more willing to negotiate with pirates than anyone expected.
