# Silverspan Reef - Silverbelt Coast

**Location:** Offshore Silverbelt Coast, underwater reef system  
**Identity:** The Coral homeland, sea people settlement, underwater city

---

## THE REEF

Silverspan Reef is a massive underwater reef system extending for miles along the Silverbelt Coast. This is where The Coral (sea people) live — an underwater civilization unknown to most surface dwellers.

**Architecture:** The Coral build with living coral, encouraging growth into structures. Their "buildings" are alive, constantly growing and adapting. The city is integrated with the reef itself.

**Depth:** The reef system ranges from 20 feet (shallow gardens) to 200+ feet (deep halls). The Coral navigate all depths comfortably.

---

## THE CORAL PEOPLE

An aquatic ancestry adapted for underwater life. Amphibious, capable of breathing water and air, but preferring underwater living.

**Population:** Estimated ~3,000-4,000 living in Silverspan Reef system (exact count unknown — they don't census themselves)

**Culture:** Communal, non-hierarchical, focused on reef health and community wellbeing. The Coral view themselves as reef-keepers, not reef-owners.

**Surface contact:** Minimal but not hostile. Some Coral trade with coastal communities (Tidewall, Vetheris). Most prefer staying underwater. Surface dwellers are welcome if respectful.

---

## REEF ZONES

**The Shallows:** Upper reef (20-50 feet), gardens, young Coral learning zones, surface contact area

**The Middle Depths:** (50-120 feet), main residential areas, communal halls, art spaces

**The Deep Halls:** (120-200+ feet), elder gatherings, sacred sites, deep coral forests

---

## ECONOMY

**Reef cultivation:** The Coral cultivate coral, kelp, and other reef organisms. They trade some harvests with surface communities.

**Pearl farming:** Limited pearl cultivation in protected reef areas. High-quality but rare.

**Fish herding:** The Coral maintain relationships with reef fish schools, protecting them from overfishing in exchange for sustainable harvest.

---

## SURFACE RELATIONSHIPS

**Tidewall:** Best surface relationship. The Four Houses respect Silverspan Reef boundaries. Occasional trade happens.

**Vetheris:** The Four Houses have negotiated fishing agreements that exclude Silverspan Reef areas. Relationship is professional.

**Threats:** Some surface fishing operations ignore reef boundaries. The Coral discourage this non-violently (damaged nets, herded fish, subtle hints). Persistent violators find fishing mysteriously unprofitable near the reef.
