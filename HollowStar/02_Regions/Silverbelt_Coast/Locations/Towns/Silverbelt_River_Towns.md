# Silverbelt River Towns & Coastal Settlements

The smaller cities and towns that fill the spaces between Tidewall and Vetheris — river communities where House authority meets inland trade, and coastal settlements caught in the perpetual low-grade conflict between Houses Aldric and Caldren.

---

## RIVER TOWNS

### Vethport
**Population:** ~4,500  
**Location:** Clearwater Run, ~15 miles inland from Vetheris  
**House:** Vethara  
**Identity:** The northern river gateway — where inland trade meets Vethara control

Vethport sits where the Clearwater Run narrows enough that bridging becomes practical. The town controls the crossing — one stone bridge, heavily maintained, toll-collected. Everything moving between the northern interior and the coast passes through here.

The town is prosperous in the way of strategic chokepoints. The bridge tolls fund infrastructure that keeps merchants happy enough not to route around. The House presence is light but real — a small garrison, a Vethara tax collector, and the understanding that this town's primary purpose is feeding trade toward Vetheris.

**Current mayor:** **Osten Clearmark** (human, 53) — appointed by House Vethara, competent administrator, runs the town to maximize trade flow. Not beloved but not resented. Understands his job is to make things easy for merchants while ensuring Vethara gets its cut.

**Tension:** The timber operations upriver have been expanding. More logs means more river traffic means more bridge tolls means more money. It also means more congestion, more disputes over right-of-way, and occasional accidents when log rafts don't navigate the bridge passage correctly. One serious accident in the past two years. Clearmark is trying to prevent a second.

### Mournwatch
**Population:** ~3,200  
**Location:** Silvermere River mouth  
**House:** Mourne  
**Identity:** The watch-town — where Lady-Admiral Sera Mourne keeps an eye on everything

Mournwatch sits at the Silvermere's mouth, where the river enters the Silver Belt Sea. Strategic position — visibility on coastal traffic in both directions, river access inland, natural harbor in the river delta.

The town is smaller than its position warrants, deliberately. House Mourne maintains Mournwatch as an observation post more than a trade hub. The garrison is larger than the population needs. The signal tower on the headland can be seen from Tidewall on clear days. This is a town built to watch and report, not to grow.

**Current commander:** **Captain Meric Ash** (human, 39) — House Mourne naval officer, stationed here for three years. Competent, disciplined, slightly bored by garrison duty after years at sea. Runs Mournwatch like a naval posting because that's what House Mourne wants it to be.

**The mixed population:** Mournwatch has a notable Coral presence — ~400 of the town's population. They work the delta fishing grounds and have a working relationship with the garrison that is professional if not warm. House Mourne's policy toward Coral has been more accommodating than the other Houses, partly because Lady-Admiral Sera Mourne's unacknowledged Coral ancestry makes her sympathetic even if she can't be public about it.

### Aldric's Landing
**Population:** ~2,800  
**Location:** Stoneflow River, inland ~20 miles  
**House:** Aldric  
**Identity:** The traditional town — where Aldric values are most visible

Aldric's Landing is exactly what House Aldric wants a Silverbelt town to be: orderly, traditional, self-sufficient, and resistant to outside influence. The town has been Aldric-held for six generations. The families here remember when House Aldric was the dominant coastal power and carry that memory as identity.

The architecture is older, well-maintained, deliberately traditional. The town square has a memorial to Aldric sailors lost in the pirate wars forty years ago. The local militia drills every third day. This is a town that takes House Aldric's values seriously because they genuinely believe in them.

**Current mayor:** **Thom Stonewater** (human, 61) — elected by the town council (Aldric territory maintains this tradition), served 12 years. Deeply conservative, deeply loyal to House Aldric, increasingly worried about what Commander Lysa Aldric's pragmatism means for towns like his.

**Current tension:** Aldric's Landing is inland, which means it depends on the coastal road for trade access. That coastal road passes through the disputed territory House Caldren also claims. In the past six months, three merchant caravans from Aldric's Landing have been stopped by Caldren militia claiming authority to inspect cargo. Each time was resolved without violence but the pattern is escalating. Mayor Stonewater has reported this to Lord-Admiral Brennan Aldric. The response has been to continue using the road and document every incident. Stonewater thinks this is insufficient. He has not said this to Brennan's face.

### Calderford
**Population:** ~2,100  
**Location:** Oldrun River, at a major ford crossing  
**House:** Caldren (in crisis)  
**Identity:** The town watching its House collapse and trying not to go down with it

Calderford sits where the Oldrun River can be forded at low tide — historically important crossing point, town built around it, House Caldren's administrative center for the river valley. The town was prosperous when House Caldren was stable. It is now watching that stability unravel in real time.

The succession crisis has not hit Calderford as hard as Caldren's coastal territories, but the effects are visible. Trade has declined as merchants route around Caldren-held areas. The House garrison has been reduced — soldiers reassigned to protect more valuable positions. The town's mayor left six months ago to "pursue other opportunities" and has not been replaced. The town council is running things on an interim basis and doing their best.

**Current council head:** **Elder Marna Riverstone** (halfling, 68) — merchant family, served on council for 20 years, never intended to run the town. Trying to keep Calderford functional while House Caldren falls apart. Has been in contact with Steward Orvyn Glast twice asking for guidance. Glast has sent sympathetic letters and no resources.

**The Mira Situation:** Mira Caldren-Ashvale, the distant heir Steward Glast is trying to convince to take the succession, comes from **Calmwater** — a small river community (~400 people) upriver from Calderford. Mira's community is pragmatic, mixed-ancestry, and has always operated with more independence than House Caldren's formal towns. Calderford is watching to see if Mira taking the succession means the riverfolk's practical approach replaces Caldren's traditional administration. Opinion is divided on whether this would be good or disastrous.

## THE DISPUTED COAST

### The Bounty Coast
**Location:** 30-mile stretch of coast between Aldric's Landing and Calderford territories  
**The Resource:** The richest fishing grounds on the Silverbelt Coast — deep shelf waters, seasonal migrations, year-round productivity  
**The Legal Problem:** Both Houses claim it, both have historical documentation supporting their claim, neither will back down

The Bounty Coast is not dramatic territory. No strategic harbors, no river mouths, no defensive positions of significance. What it has is fish. A lot of fish. The best fishing on the coast outside the Silverspan Reef Coral grounds.

Forty years ago this was uncontested Caldren territory. Thirty years ago House Aldric challenged the claim based on older documentation showing Aldric family holdings predating Caldren's formal incorporation. Twenty years ago both Houses were fishing the waters simultaneously under an informal truce. Ten years ago the truce broke down. Now it's a perpetual low-level conflict.

---

### What the Conflict Looks Like

**Not violence.** Neither House wants that. Both understand that actual combat would force the Council of Banners to take sides and nobody wants that political crisis.

**What happens instead:**

**Legal warfare:** Both Houses file competing claims with the Council of Banners. The Council hasn't ruled because ruling would require three votes and no coalition of Houses wants to set the precedent. The legal cases accumulate. Nothing resolves.

**"Accidental" interference:** Aldric fishing boats discover their nets have been cut. Caldren boats report Aldric vessels fishing too close to their operations, causing entanglement. Harbor records show Aldric boats entering waters Caldren claims are theirs. Patrol vessels from both Houses stop each other's fishing boats for "documentation checks." Nothing is prosecuted. Everything is documented.

**Border marker disputes:** Stone markers indicating territorial boundaries mysteriously move. New markers appear. Old markers disappear. Both Houses accuse the other of tampering. Nobody proves anything.

**Economic pressure:** Aldric offers better prices to fishermen who exclusively sell their catch through Aldric ports. Caldren offers subsidized boat repairs to vessels that recognize Caldren authority. The fishermen caught in the middle play both sides when they can and suffer when they can't.

**Escalation incidents:** Every six months something happens that could become serious. An Aldric patrol vessel rams a Caldren fishing boat (accidentally, both sides claim). A Caldren militia detachment arrests an Aldric fisher for "poaching" and holds them for three days before releasing them with apologies. A disputed catch leads to a harbor brawl that puts five people in the medical ward.

Each incident gets resolved. Each incident adds to the accumulated tension. Neither side backs down.

---

### Why the Other Houses Don't Intervene

**House Vethara's view:** Children fighting over fish. Vethara is building a Lumencrest partnership. The Bounty Coast dispute is beneath notice.

**House Mourne's view:** Annoying but not worth the political capital to resolve. If it escalates to actual violence Lady-Admiral Sera Mourne will intervene. Until then, let them exhaust themselves.

**The practical effect:** The two strongest Houses ignoring the conflict makes it unsolvable. Aldric and Caldren can't force resolution without one of them backing them. Neither Vethara nor Mourne will back either side. The conflict continues indefinitely.

---

### Current State

House Caldren is losing. Not dramatically — slowly, steadily, through attrition. The succession crisis means they have fewer resources to dedicate to the dispute. Fewer patrol vessels. Fewer funds for legal challenges. Fewer political allies willing to support their position.

House Aldric is not winning — they're just losing less slowly. Lord-Admiral Brennan Aldric views this as a matter of principle and family honor. He will not cede the coast. He also doesn't have the resources to force a resolution.

The fishermen working the Bounty Coast are tired. They pay fees to both Houses, follow rules from both Houses, and navigate a situation where being caught by the wrong patrol on the wrong day means losing the day's catch. Some have stopped fishing there. Others have no choice — it's the best grounds on the coast.

The conflict continues.

## SMALLER COASTAL SETTLEMENTS

### Coppergate
**Population:** ~600  
**Location:** Coast between Vetheris and Tidewall  
**Identity:** Goblin craftwork village — copper goods, river glass, small-scale trade

Coppergate is Silverbelt Coast's only Goblin-majority settlement. The community was founded 40 years ago by families who left the Central Heartlands looking for a place where they could practice their crafts without the historical suspicion that follows Goblins in older cities.

They found it here. The copper work produced in Coppergate — jewelry, tools, decorative pieces — is excellent. The village trades along the coast, maintains good relationships with passing merchants, and asks only to be left alone to work.

**Elder Finn** (goblin, 67) runs the community council. His goal is keeping Coppergate safe, employed, and out of House politics. So far it has worked. The Houses buy Goblin copper work and don't interfere. This is the arrangement Coppergate needs.

---

### Saltwind
**Population:** ~800  
**Location:** Southern coast, between Mournwatch and Tidewall  
**Identity:** Fishing village, mixed human/Ribbet, House Mourne territory

Standard coastal fishing village. Good grounds close to shore, sheltered harbor, community that has been here for generations. House Mourne collects taxes, protects the coast, and otherwise leaves Saltwind to fish in peace.

The village is prosperous by fishing village standards. The Ribbet population is larger than in most surface settlements — the work suits them and the community is welcoming. This is one of several Silverbelt towns where Ribbet integration works well because nobody makes it complicated.

---

### Highcrest
**Population:** ~400  
**Location:** Cliffs north of Vetheris, House Vethara territory  
**Identity:** Lighthouse community, signal station, very small population doing essential work

Highcrest exists because the cliffs north of Vetheris are the highest point on the northern coast and therefore the best location for a lighthouse. The community maintains the light, operates the signal station that coordinates with Vetheris's harbor, and lives isolated lives doing work everyone else depends on.

The population is small, transient, and mostly House Vethara employees or families who prefer isolation. Not a place people visit. Not a place that wants visitors. Essential infrastructure staffed by people who like being left alone.

## CALMWATER (Mira's Community)
**Population:** ~400  
**Location:** Oldrun River, upriver from Calderford  
**Identity:** River pilot community, mixed ancestry, practical independence

Calmwater is where Mira Caldren-Ashvale comes from — the community that produced the woman Steward Glast is trying to convince to save House Caldren.

The town is river-focused. River pilots, boat builders, fishing guides, the people who know every sandbar and current on the Oldrun. Mixed population — human, Katari, some Ribbet, families who married across lines generations ago and stopped tracking who was what.

The community's relationship with House Caldren has always been complicated. Technically Caldren territory. Practically independent — too far inland for Caldren to govern closely, too useful as river pilots to risk alienating. Calmwater paid taxes, followed the major rules, and otherwise did what it wanted.

Mira grew up here. This is where she learned rivers. This is why she has no formal training for House leadership and why Steward Glast thinks she might succeed anyway — she learned practical problem-solving in a community that couldn't afford theory.

The town is watching what happens next. If Mira becomes head of House Caldren, Calmwater becomes politically significant for the first time in its history. Opinion is mixed on whether this is an opportunity or a disaster.
