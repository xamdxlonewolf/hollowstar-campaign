# Geography - Isle of the Broken Sphere

**Climate:** Tropical, hot and humid year-round, monsoon season (3 months heavy rain)  
**Terrain:** Volcanic island, mountainous interior, coastal settlements, dense jungle  
**Population:** ~8,000 (concentrated in coastal settlements)  
**Identity:** Galapa refugee island, volcanic activity, isolated tropical environment

---

## THE ISLAND

The Isle of the Broken Sphere is a large tropical island far south in the Great Depths — mountainous interior rising to peaks, coastal plains where settlements cluster, surrounded by coral reefs.

**What everyone believes:** Volcanic origin. The "volcanic peaks" in the interior, the geothermal heat, the tremors — all attributed to an active volcanic system beneath the island.

**What is actually true:** *→ See [[02_Regions/Isle_Broken_Sphere/The_Dragon_Turtle|The Dragon Turtle]] — GM document only.*

**The name:** When the Broken Sphere (Galapa homeland) shattered and sank, refugee fleets scattered. This island became sanctuary for thousands of Galapa survivors. The name honors what was lost.

---

## TERRAIN

**Coastal plains:** Where settlements are built. Flatter ground, access to ocean, all major communities here.

**Interior:** Dense tropical forest covering mountainous interior — or what was once forest. The dead zone expanding from the central fault line is consuming it. Animals have been fleeing the interior for a decade.

**The "peaks":** Three high ridges dominating the interior. Smoke and heat rise from them. Long assumed to be volcanic. Up close, the material they are made of is unusual — smoother than volcanic rock, curved in ways that don't match any geological formation scholars can name.

**The fault line:** A crack running through the island's center that has been slowly widening for thirty years. Heat rises from it. The Spine Stones emerge along it. Its origin is not geological.

**Coral reefs:** Surrounding the island, providing fishing grounds, natural harbor protection, navigation hazards. The reef has been behaving unusually in recent years — certain sections dying, others growing rapidly, as if responding to changes in water temperature and current around the island's base.

---

## CLIMATE

**Year-round heat:** Tropical temperatures, hot and humid constantly. Galapa (amphibious) handle this well. Others find it oppressive.

**Monsoon season:** Three months of heavy rain. Infrastructure built for flooding. Settlements remain functional but movement difficult. This is survival season, not prosperity season.

**Dry season:** Nine months with less rain but still humid. Primary working season - fishing, building, trading. Communities prepare for monsoon during dry months.

---

## GALAPA SETTLEMENTS

**Tideshallow** (~4,200) - Largest settlement, refugee coordination center, where most survivors landed

**Coralbreak** (~2,100) - Secondary settlement, fishing village grown larger  

**Shallowrun** (~1,200) - Smallest major settlement, eastern coast

**Character:** Refugee communities building permanent homes. The settlements blend Broken Sphere culture (what survived) with adaptation to new environment.

---

## THE "VOLCANIC" THREAT

**What the Galapa believe:** The island's heat and tremors come from volcanic activity beneath. This is the working explanation for everything — the peaks, the fissures, the heat, the rhythmic tremors.

**What the evidence actually shows:** The tremors are rhythmic in a way that no volcanic process produces. The heat from the fissures is body-temperature warm, not the intense dry heat of magma venting. The "peaks" are made of material that resists every geological classification scholars have attempted. The fault line's walls, examined closely, have a texture that is not stone.

Every scientific expedition has concluded volcanic activity is the explanation because it is the only framework available. The data fits — imperfectly — if you don't look too closely.

**Why stay:** Because they have nowhere else. The Broken Sphere is gone. This island is what they have. They'll stay until forced to leave. And increasingly, "forced to leave" feels like it may be coming.

---

## ISOLATION

**Distance:** Far south in Great Depths, weeks of sailing from major continental ports. Difficult to reach, easy to ignore.

**Contact:** Limited trade with continental settlements. Some fishing boats visit. Occasional ships from L'Empire Verdienne. Mostly the island is alone.

**Self-sufficiency:** By necessity. Ships rarely come. The Galapa fish, grow tropical crops, harvest jungle resources. They survive without outside help because they must.

---

## RESOURCES

**Fishing:** Primary food source. Coral reef waters provide abundant catches. Galapa excel at this.

**Tropical crops:** Fruit, root vegetables, crops adapted to monsoon climate. Grown during dry season.

**Jungle harvesting:** Timber, medicinal plants, materials from interior. Dangerous work but necessary.

**Geothermal:** Hot springs provide heat for some applications. Galapa are experimenting with volcanic heat use.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO REGIONS

**L'Empire Verdienne:** Most contact is with the empire - occasional ships, limited trade, diplomatic recognition. The empire acknowledges the island exists and Galapa have claim to it.

**Verdant Crescent (Narçon):** Established Galapa in Les Perles are distant cousins. Different migration history, different culture. Relationship is respectful but distant.

**Continental powers:** Most ignore the island. Too far, too isolated, nothing valuable enough to claim. The Galapa prefer this.

---

## CURRENT STATUS

**Building permanence:** Refugee settlements becoming permanent communities. Galapa are building for long-term occupation, not temporary shelter.

**Cultural preservation:** Maintaining Broken Sphere traditions while adapting to island life. The culture is changing but surviving.

**The volcano question:** Everyone knows the island could erupt catastrophically. Nobody knows when. They build anyway because where else would they go?

**Population growth:** Births outnumber deaths. Population slowly increasing. If trend continues, the island will need expansion or some Galapa will need to leave.

---

## THE BROKEN SPHERE MEMORY

Every Galapa on the island remembers the Broken Sphere or is descended from those who do. The memory shapes everything:

- **Loss:** They lost their homeland. This island is second choice.
- **Survival:** They survived when thousands didn't. They'll survive the island's dangers too.
- **Vigilance:** The Sphere sank without warning. They watch the volcanoes constantly.
- **Adaptation:** They couldn't save the Sphere. They can adapt to this island.

The island is sanctuary born from catastrophe. The Galapa make it work because they have no alternative.
