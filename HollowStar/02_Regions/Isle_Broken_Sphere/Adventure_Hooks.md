# Adventure Hooks - Isle of the Broken Sphere

---

## THE WAKING (5)

### 1. "The Formations"
**Hook:** Someone touched a formation and hasn't been right since — not sick exactly, just... elsewhere
**Employer:** Their family (frightened, don't know what happened)
**Goal:** Understand what the formations are and whether the person can be helped
**Discovery:** The person absorbed something from the formation — impressions, not memories. Images of something vast looking upward from below.
**Level:** 5-7

### 2. "The Fault Line"
**Hook:** The fault line crack widened six inches overnight — it's been averaging one inch per week
**Employer:** Elder Tess (needs someone to go to the crack and observe — Galapa elders won't go, community members won't go)
**Goal:** Observe the fault line, document what's there
**What they find:** The crack goes down further than any light reaches. And something at the bottom is warm. And moving.
**Level:** 5-7

### 3. "The Twelfth Formation"
**Hook:** A thirteenth formation appeared overnight — inside Shellhaven's outer edge, not the interior
**Employer:** Darro (the community's logistics voice — this changes everything)
**Goal:** Assess whether evacuation is now necessary
**Complication:** Elder Tess doesn't want to call evacuation yet (Galapa can't easily relocate). The formation is 200 feet from the harbor. The Lumencrest ship arrives in two weeks.
**Level:** 5-7

### 4. "Elder Tess's Theory"
**Hook:** Elder Tess asks the party to carry a message to the Frostwell Tundra — she needs to know if the Ember Cradle's warming matches her timeline
**Goal:** Get to Frostwell, speak to Flame-Keeper Sorith, compare timelines
**Discovery:** The timelines match almost exactly. The Sleeper and the Ember Cradle began stirring within days of each other. Something triggered both simultaneously.
**Level:** 6-8

### 5. "What It Wants"
**Hook:** A Galapa elder who has spent weeks in meditation near the Spine Stones believes she is receiving something from the island's spirit — impressions, not words
**Employer:** Elder Tess (cautious, wants witnesses she trusts)
**Goal:** Participate in the ritual, document what the elder receives
**What they experience:** Something vast and old. The pull of deep water. A sense of movement — not threat, not anger, just direction. The island's spirit, if that's what it is, does not notice the people on it. It is oriented toward something else entirely.
**What this tells the GM:** The Dragon Turtle is beginning to orient. What it tells the players: something enormous and indifferent is involved, and it is not focused on them.
**Level:** 6-9

## LUMENCREST ARRIVAL (3)

### 6. "Before The Meridian Lands"
**Hook:** The community spots the Lumencrest ship two days out
**Employer:** Darro (needs to prepare — what does the community want Lumencrest to know?)
**Goal:** Help the community decide what to tell Lumencrest and how to manage the visit
**Complication:** The embedded Lumencrest agent will be working against whatever the community decides
**Level:** 4-6

### 7. "Researcher Dann's Problem"
**Hook:** Lumencrest researcher Orev Dann is genuinely brilliant and genuinely heading toward the formations with instruments
**Goal:** Either escort him safely (he'll find what he finds) or redirect him (he'll find something else)
**Moral complexity:** Dann's data, if it reached the wrong people, would have the Expansionists on the next ship. His data, if it reached the Seekers, might save the island.
**Level:** 5-7

### 8. "The Agent"
**Hook:** The embedded Lumencrest agent has been identified by Darro (he's suspicious of everyone, correctly)
**Employer:** Darro
**Goal:** Confirm the agent's identity and determine what they've already reported back
**Discovery:** The agent has already sent one message. The Expansionists know the energy readings are significant. A second ship is already being prepared.
**Level:** 5-7

## COMMUNITY STORIES (4)

### 9. "The Last Boats"
**Hook:** The harbor master's records show a pattern — boats are leaving, fewer arriving
**Employer:** Brint (Galapa harbor master, showing party the records privately)
**Goal:** Assess whether community evacuation is feasible and where people would go
**Real question:** What does it mean for the Galapa if they leave? This is the only place on the continent they've called home.
**Level:** 4-6

### 10. "Fresh Start, Wrong Time"
**Hook:** A boat arrives with a family seeking exactly what the Isle promises — a fresh start, no questions asked
**Goal:** The community must decide whether to tell them what's happening or let them settle
**The party's role:** Witness and potentially advisor — what do you tell people arriving into a crisis?
**Level:** Any (character/moral hook)

### 11. "Darro's Warehouse"
**Hook:** Darro asks party to help quietly move community valuables to the harbor warehouse — "just in case"
**Goal:** Complete the task, assess what Darro actually knows that he hasn't said publicly
**Discovery:** Darro has been planning for evacuation for six weeks. He knows more about the timeline than he's let on. Someone on the mainland has been sending him information.
**Level:** 4-6

### 12. "The Young Galapa"
**Hook:** A group of young Galapa want to leave — their elders won't give permission (cultural expectation to stay together)
**Goal:** Mediate between generations — the elders' commitment to the island vs. the young adults' survival instinct
**No easy answer:** Both sides are right. The Sleeper may subside. It may not. Leaving means abandoning the only home Galapa culture has fully expressed itself. Staying means staying.
**Level:** 3-5

---

**Levels:** 3-5 (community stories, Lumencrest management, moral choices) · 4-6 (evacuation planning, faction politics, Dann's investigation) · 5-7 (formations, fault line, Lumencrest operation) · 6-9 (Elder Tess's theory, what the Sleeper wants, continental connections)
