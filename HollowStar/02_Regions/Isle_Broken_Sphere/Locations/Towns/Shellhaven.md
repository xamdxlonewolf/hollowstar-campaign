# Shellhaven

**Population:** ~3,500 (and falling) | **Region:** [[02_Regions/Isle_Broken_Sphere/Isle_Broken_Sphere_Index|Isle of the Broken Sphere]]  
**Identity:** The only real settlement on the Isle — harbor, market, Galapa quarter, the place trying to maintain normalcy

---

## THE SETTLEMENT

Shellhaven is where the Isle's population concentrates — partly on the coast, partly working inward toward the drier, more stable ground. It was never a planned city. It is an accumulation of people who had reasons to stop here: the Galapa who came first, the escaped debtors, the fleeing families, the opportunists who stayed.

The deliberate normalcy is the settlement's most distinctive feature. The Spine Stones are visible from the market square on a clear day. The harbor master keeps meticulous records of departures. The Galapa elders meet regularly. And the market runs, the fishing boats go out, the inn serves meals. Shellhaven carries on. It is unclear how much longer this will remain possible.

---

## THE GALAPA QUARTER

The oldest part of Shellhaven. Galapa families whose ancestors arrived generations ago, shells worn by decades of island life. Some elder shells have a faint warmth to them — years of proximity to the Sleeper's energy. The Galapa Quarter has the highest density of people who intend to stay regardless of what happens.

**Elder Tess** (Galapa, 156) lives here — the de facto spiritual and strategic leader of the Isle. Shell worn with extraordinary age, dark and deeply ridged, with warmth that has nothing to do with sun exposure. The slowest voice in any room, not from age but from the Galapa understanding that fast decisions in uncertain situations are usually wrong.

*"The Sleeper has woken before. It has slept again. I am trying to determine whether this time is different before I tell people it is different."*

---

## THE HARBOR

The Isle's connection to the outside world. Fishing boats, trading vessels passing through, and now increasingly — boats leaving with more people than arrived, and fewer new arrivals.

**Harbor master Brint** (Galapa, 89): Has been keeping records for decades. The departure numbers are clear and he showed them to Elder Tess two months ago. Has not shown them to anyone else until now.

**The warehouse:** Darro has been quietly moving community valuables here — tools, records, food stores. Not officially sanctioned. Not secret. Nobody has asked him to explain it.

---

## THE MARKET AND INN

The Dry Corner Inn — the settlement's social center. Run by a family that has been here three generations and has no intention of leaving ("we've survived worse," which they haven't, but they believe it). Current clientele: a mix of long-term residents and an increasing number of people who arrived recently and are deciding whether to stay or go.

The market runs six days a week. Attendance is down 15% from last year. Nobody mentions this.

---

## THE THREE INTERIOR SETTLEMENTS (EVACUATED)

Three smaller communities in the island's interior were quietly relocated over the past four months. The evacuations happened without announcement — Galapa elders suggested the communities "visit" Shellhaven, the visits became permanent.

- **Stonehollow** — closest to the fault line. First to go. Nobody discusses where the buildings are now.
- **Ridgecamp** — mining community. The Spine Stone that emerged inside their perimeter triggered the relocation decision.
- **The Hollow** — a small farming collective. Left when the interior forest started dying around them.

The people from these communities are in Shellhaven now, adding to the housing strain and the general sense of crowding and dread.
