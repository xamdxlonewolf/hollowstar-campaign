# Thornwood

**Location:** Central-Western Heartlands, near [[Thornkeep|Thornkeep]]  
**Type:** Large managed forest  
**Size:** ~15,000 acres (substantial regional forest)

---

## OVERVIEW

Thornwood is large forest managed through sustainable logging practices. Unlike Verdant Crescent's ancient wild forests or small sacred Ember Grove, Thornwood is working forest—logged continuously for 150 years without depletion through careful management.

**The model:** Selective harvest, replanting, forest health maintenance, druid cooperation. This system sustains both forest and timber economy.

---

## CHARACTERISTICS

**Tree types:** Mixed hardwood—oak, ash, maple, beech. Quality timber for construction and furniture.

**Age distribution:** Managed properly, forest has trees of all ages. Old growth for timber, young growth for future, continuous cycle.

**Wildlife:** Abundant. Deer, boar, wolves, smaller animals. Healthy forest supports diverse ecosystem.

**Accessibility:** Network of logging roads and trails. Not wilderness—working forest with infrastructure.

---

## THE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM

**Thornkeep loggers:** Conduct harvest operations following druid-approved plans.

**The Thornwood Circle:** Druid group that marks harvest areas annually. They assess forest health, identify sustainable cuts, ensure balance maintained.

**The agreement:** Loggers cut only marked trees, follow replanting requirements, maintain forest roads, report any issues. Druids provide cooperation, ensure forest remains healthy.

**Why it works:** Mutual benefit. Loggers get sustainable livelihood. Druids protect forest long-term. Everyone prospers.

---

## ZONES

**Active Harvest Zone (Northern section):** Currently logged areas. Heavy activity, obvious human impact.

**Recovery Zone (Central section):** Previously logged areas now regrowing. 10-30 year old trees, being allowed to mature.

**Protected Zone (Southern section):** Old growth reserved for future harvest. Also serves as wildlife sanctuary, seed source for reforestation.

**The Druid Glade (Southeast corner):** Where Thornwood Circle lives. Off-limits to loggers, sacred space.

---

## CURRENT PRESSURE

**Lumencrest timber demand:** With Heart failing, Lumencrest wants increased Thornwood harvest. Offering premium prices.

**The temptation:** Thornkeep could accelerate logging, take short-term profit. This would damage forest, break agreement, end sustainable model.

**Druid position:** Current harvest rate is sustainable maximum. Increase and forest health suffers. Circle Keeper Elder Rowan has warned that violation means end of cooperation.

**Industry pressure:** Not just Lumencrest—other cities also want more timber. Thornwood is proven sustainable source. Everyone wants more than forest can provide.

---

## THE WILDLIFE

**Natural:** Deer, boar, wolves maintain healthy predator-prey balance.

**Unusual:** Some creatures seem unusually intelligent. Druids claim forest spirits inhabit animals. Loggers just know some animals watch them too closely.

**Dangerous:** Wolves and boars are real threats. Loggers carry weapons, work in groups. Fatal attacks are rare but possible.

**Protected:** Druids forbid hunting except for food or defense. Commercial hunting in Thornwood is prohibited. Break this rule, face expulsion.

---

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

- Hired to increase timber harvest (violate agreement for profit)
- Hired by druids to prevent over-logging
- Investigation: Someone logging illegally, threatening sustainable model
- Missing: Logging crew disappeared in forest. Search mission reveals complications.
- Discovery: Ancient structure found during logging. Historical significance vs. economic value.
- Conflict: Loggers and druids accusing each other of agreement violations. Mediate or investigate truth.
