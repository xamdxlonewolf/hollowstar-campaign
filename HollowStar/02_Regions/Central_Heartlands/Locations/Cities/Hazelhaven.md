# Hazelhaven

**Population:** ~38,000 (city proper) + ~60,000 in surrounding agricultural districts  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Location:** Central Heartlands interior, where the Hearthflow's western tributaries fan out across the great grain plains  
**Identity:** Agricultural capital of the Heartlands — grain production, food distribution, the region's breadbasket

---

## What Hazelhaven Is

Hazelhaven does not look like a powerful city. It lacks Lumencrest's gleaming infrastructure, Ironhold's dramatic mountain setting, Kingsrest's ancient walls, or Steelwatch's military bearing. It is flat. It is surrounded by fields. Its architecture is practical rather than impressive — wide streets for grain wagons, vast covered markets, warehouses that dwarf the civic buildings. The smell of grain and soil is constant.

It is also the reason everyone else eats.

The agricultural districts surrounding Hazelhaven produce the majority of the Central Heartlands' grain supply and a significant portion of what feeds the Northern Expanse and Salt Shore as well. The city itself is the collection and distribution point — grain comes in from hundreds of surrounding farms by cart and river barge, gets processed and stored in Hazelhaven's vast granary network, and goes back out as flour, preserved goods, and bulk grain to every major city in the region.

Remove Hazelhaven from the equation and every other city in the Heartlands starts rationing within a season. Everyone who sits at the Heartland Accord knows this. The Hazelhaven government knows they know it.

## The City

**The Great Market:** The physical and economic heart of Hazelhaven — a covered market complex that runs for nearly half a mile along the main canal. Grain trading, bulk food goods, agricultural equipment, seed stock. On market days it is one of the busiest commercial spaces in the Heartlands. Buyers come from every major city. Prices set here ripple outward across the region.

**The Granary Towers:** Hazelhaven's most distinctive skyline feature — eight large stone towers at the city's edge, each capable of holding months of grain reserves. The towers are managed by the Grainward, a semi-independent body that controls access and maintains the strategic reserve. The Grainward answers to the city government but has historically operated with significant autonomy. Knowing how full the towers are is considered sensitive information — it's the clearest measure of how much leverage Hazelhaven actually has at any given moment.

**The Irrigation Works:** The visible legacy of Lumencrest's tech assistance program — a network of shard-powered pumping stations and channel controls that pull water from the Hearthflow tributaries and distribute it across the surrounding farmland with a precision that natural flooding never achieved. The system dramatically increased yields when it was installed twelve years ago. It is now showing its age and degrading as Lumencrest's maintenance support has thinned. Pumping stations run less reliably. Channel controls stick. A full failure of any of the three main pumping hubs would be serious. Everyone in Hazelhaven's government is aware of how close two of them currently are to that threshold.

**The Harvest Quarter:** The residential and commercial district where the farming families come during market season — inns, traders, equipment shops, the offices of the agricultural guilds. More transient population than the permanent city, but during harvest months this quarter swells and the city feels twice its size.

## Government and Politics

Hazelhaven is governed by the **Grainmasters' Assembly** — a council of representatives from the major agricultural guilds, granary interests, and landed farming families. It is not a democracy in the way Lumencrest's Council is. It is the institutional expression of the people who own and control food production in the region.

The Assembly's external-facing arm is the **Accord delegation**, currently led by **Accord-Speaker Maret Dunfor**. Dunfor is the third generation of her family to hold this role — her grandmother established the aggressive negotiating posture that Hazelhaven's delegation has maintained ever since. The family philosophy is simple: you have something everyone needs, and the moment you start apologizing for that or accommodating people who should be accommodating you, you lose the only advantage you have.

This approach is effective. It is also exhausting for everyone who has to work with Hazelhaven at the Accord table.

**The internal tension:** The Assembly is not uniformly hardline. A significant minority of members — particularly the mid-size family farmers who make up the Assembly's second tier — believe that Hazelhaven's confrontational posture is creating enemies the city doesn't need and storing up future consequences. They haven't yet been able to move the Assembly's direction. Dunfor's faction is too entrenched and too successful in the narrow sense of winning concessions. But the dissent exists and is growing as the Lumencrest tech assistance situation deteriorates — because antagonizing your primary tech partner turns out to have consequences.

## The Tech Assistance Problem

Twelve years ago, Lumencrest offered Hazelhaven a technology partnership — Illuminist engineers would design and install shard-powered agricultural systems in exchange for favorable grain pricing and a seat at the Accord table that Hazelhaven had previously been denied. Hazelhaven accepted.

The systems worked. Yields increased significantly. Hazelhaven became more important and more confident. The city's government credit this success to their own hard negotiating rather than to the partnership, which is part of why the relationship has soured.

What's happening now:

**The degradation is real.** The irrigation pumping stations require shard components for maintenance that Lumencrest is no longer supplying on schedule. Two of the three main hubs are operating below capacity. The mechanized harvesting equipment — smaller individual units distributed across farming districts — is failing at roughly one unit per month with no replacement parts forthcoming.

**Lumencrest is not being malicious.** They simply don't have the bandwidth. The Heart crisis is consuming every resource and the Illuminists who designed the Hazelhaven systems are now working on other problems. The Hazelhaven account has been deprioritized. Nobody made a decision to abandon the partnership — it has just slipped, the way things slip when an institution is in crisis.

**Hazelhaven's government is furious about this distinction.** Whether it's malicious or negligent is irrelevant to them. The result is the same: their yields are falling, their infrastructure is degrading, and Lumencrest is not fixing it. Dunfor's ultimatum — full restoration of the tech assistance program or Hazelhaven withdraws from the mutual defense compact — is written and ready. She is waiting for the right Accord session to table it.

**The actual farmers are scared.** The third consecutive difficult yield is generating real anxiety in the farming districts. The political posturing of the Assembly feels disconnected from the ground-level reality of families watching their harvests decline. A few farming community leaders have begun making quiet inquiries — not to the Assembly, to anyone who will listen — about what happens if this continues.

## People

**Accord-Speaker Maret Dunfor**  
Hazelhaven's external face. Third-generation political operator, completely clear-eyed about what Hazelhaven is and what it has. Does not waste time on pleasantries. Will absolutely hold votes hostage. Is currently the most dangerous person at the Accord table because she has a concrete grievance, a concrete threat, and the leverage to back it up. She is not wrong that Lumencrest has failed to hold up their end of the partnership. She is possibly miscalculating how the other three seats will respond to Hazelhaven withdrawing from mutual defense.

**Grainmaster Tessul Aurin**  
Head of the Grainmasters' Assembly. Older, more cautious than Dunfor — not in terms of political goals but in terms of methods. He is the internal voice asking whether Hazelhaven's confrontational approach is serving the city's long-term interests or just its short-term satisfaction. Has not moved the Assembly yet. Is watching the tech assistance situation carefully and updating his calculations.

**Chief Engineer Polla Vayne**  
The Lumencrest-trained engineer who has been maintaining the irrigation works since the original installation. She knows exactly how close the main pumping hubs are to failure. She has submitted six maintenance reports to the Assembly in the past year. Three have resulted in partial funding. Three have been tabled for "budget review." She is not optimistic. She has begun quietly documenting everything so that when something fails, the record is clear.

## Surrounding Districts

The agricultural districts surrounding Hazelhaven are not administratively part of the city but are economically inseparable from it. Dozens of farming communities, ranging from large estates owned by Assembly members to small family plots, all feed into Hazelhaven's market.

**The Frostwell resettlement:** Several of these districts have received Frostwell refugee families through the Northern Expanse's resettlement program. The integration is uneven — the families are hardworking and the labor is genuinely needed, but wage pressure from the newcomers has created friction with established tenant farmers. The Assembly has taken no formal position on this, which the resettled families experience as indifference and the established tenant farmers experience as favoritism in the opposite direction.

**The eastern estates:** The largest landholdings, owned by Assembly members. Better maintained, more insulated from the tech assistance degradation because they had older backup systems the Lumencrest equipment was layered on top of. They are not experiencing the crisis the same way the smaller farms are. This is noticed.

## Adventure Hooks

**"The Third Report"** — Chief Engineer Vayne's most recent maintenance report contains a finding she hasn't shared with the Assembly yet: the northern pumping hub is not just degrading, it shows signs of active interference. Something is accelerating the failure. She needs people she can trust — not Assembly loyalists — to investigate before she decides what to do with the information.

**"The Ultimatum"** — The party intercepts or learns the contents of Dunfor's undelivered ultimatum before she tables it at the Accord. If Hazelhaven withdraws from mutual defense, the implications ripple everywhere. Does the party try to prevent it, broker a solution, or let it play out?

**"The Yield Numbers"** — The true agricultural assessment of the surrounding districts — conducted by an outside assessor at Grainmaster Aurin's private request — is being suppressed by someone in the Assembly. The actual yield decline is significantly worse than the official numbers suggest, and the full picture would change the political calculus for everyone. Finding the report is step one. Deciding what to do with it is harder.

**"The Resettlement Problem"** — A Frostwell farming family has been forced off their resettlement plot by an eastern estate owner claiming a prior land claim. The claim is legally dubious. The Assembly hasn't acted. The family is now camped at the edge of the Great Market attracting attention. The party gets involved — and discovers the land claim connects to something larger about who controls the eastern estates and why.
