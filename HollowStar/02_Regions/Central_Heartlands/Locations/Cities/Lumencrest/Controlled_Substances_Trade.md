# Lumencrest — Controlled Substances, the Trade, and the Unseen Guild

_GM reference only. This document covers all major substances circulating in Lumencrest, the street-level trade, the Unseen Guild's Lumencrest operation, and the darker machinery running beneath it. All conditions referenced here are defined in [[Lumencrest_Conditions_Reference]]. Cross-reference [[05_Shadowside_Expanded]] for primary above-ground trade locations and [[08_The_Underground_Expanded]] for tunnel-level supply chain detail._

## Lumencrest and the Drug Problem

Lumencrest has one of the worst substance dependency rates on the continent. The reasons are not difficult to understand.

Eighty thousand people live inside a slow emergency that the people in charge won't fully name. The Heart is failing. Power rationing gets worse every year. Manufactories workers watch their livelihoods become conditional on energy that flickers without warning. Shadowside has been promised electricity for six centuries and has stopped expecting it. The Clank community serves decades of labor with no guarantee the freedom at the end means what they were told. The Academic Quarter's researchers are solving a problem they may not be able to solve in time they may not have.

People in sustained pain find things that interrupt the pain. This is not a moral failure — it is a material consequence, and the material conditions of Lumencrest have been generating it at an accelerating rate for thirty years. Substance dependency has roughly doubled in the past fifteen years. The Council's response has been enforcement. Enforcement has not worked, because enforcement addresses supply rather than conditions, and the people supplying are considerably more motivated than the people enforcing.

Shadowside is where the trade is most visible. The Night Market's substance section, the Smoke Den, and several smaller Maze Quarter operations make Shadowside the retail face of a system that reaches through every district and out across the continent. The wealthy buy through the Quiet Market or private arrangements. The middle class buy from the Night Pharmacy or specific Night Market contacts. Manufactories workers buy from district distributors who source from Shadowside. The Academic Quarter has its own supply chain through Innovation Row. Everyone buys. The supply just looks different by district.

## The Unseen Guild — Lumencrest Operation

_The Unseen Guild is a continental organization headquartered in Sablemark. Full continental context is held in separate campaign documentation. What follows covers only the Lumencrest operation and its specific complications._

The Guild's Lumencrest presence runs two parallel operations that share infrastructure but have different command structures, different risk profiles, and — critically — different levels of exposure if something goes wrong.

**The Standard Operation:** Substance distribution, information brokerage, protection arrangements, contract services. Professionally run, deliberately low-profile, managed through strict layers of separation between continental logistics and street-level retail. The Guild does not produce anything in Lumencrest — it moves, stores, and distributes. Independent dealers operate under Guild tolerance arrangements, staying within Guild pricing and avoiding heat in exchange for protection and reliable supply.

**The Shard Division:** Something new. Not standard Guild business — this is a personal investment by Mira Voss and her patron Lyra Vael, running through shared infrastructure with a completely separate command chain. The Shard Division kidnaps vulnerable people — Shadowside residents the Council doesn't count, Frostwell refugees in transit through the Northern Expanse — and exposes them to Hollostar shard fragments under controlled conditions, documenting what happens. The goal: determine whether shard transformation can be directed, controlled, and weaponized into enhanced Guild operatives. The data so far is significant. The cost in lives is significant. These facts coexist without apparent tension for the people running the program.

_The players will not know the Shard Division exists at campaign start. They learn about it through the Shadowside disappearances — residents who have gone missing without the pattern that ordinary crime produces._

### Key Guild NPCs — Lumencrest

**Mira Voss** — Guild leader for Lumencrest and surrounding regional operations. Half-elf, 41. Brilliant, cold, and entirely transactional. She runs the operation with the precision of someone who understands that criminal enterprises fail when they become emotional or sloppy. She views the Shard Division as a business investment — the development costs are high in every sense, but the potential value of shard-enhanced operatives is a market the Guild currently cannot access and she intends to create. She has never been in the same room as a Shard Division subject. This is deliberate. Her relationship with Lyra Vael is the closest thing she has to a peer relationship — two people who have made the same internal calculation about the relationship between power and morality. She has identified that the players are investigating the Shadowside disappearances. She has not moved against them yet — they are not significant enough, and drawing attention is worse than their current level of interference. She has warned the Collector. The Lumencrest disappearances have paused. The Northern Expanse operation has not.

**Lyra Vael** — House Vael operative and Shard Division patron. Human, 25. Publicly: a junior House representative, charming, ambitious in ways the elite find admirable. Privately: the architect of the Guild arrangement and the Division's funding source. She views morality as a framework other people use to limit themselves. She has identified what the next source of power will be — not political, not economic, but physical — and is building toward it without her father's knowledge or permission. She and Mira understand each other. Neither one would hesitate.

**The Collector** — Shard Division operational lead. Ancestry deliberately obscured. The person who actually runs the kidnapping operation. Identity is a secret even within the Guild's Lumencrest structure — Mira knows; street-level distributors do not. Has been operating for two years without being identified. Knows Shadowside better than city watch does — at the level of specific passages, specific routines, specific people who will not be immediately missed. The Collector is not cruel. The operation is professional. In some ways this is worse.

**Seeker-Researcher Aldis Crane** — Corrupted Starfall Seeker, senior of three. Human, 52. Provides shard samples to the Guild in exchange for experimental data — the Seekers want to understand shard effects on living beings; the Guild is doing the experimentation they cannot officially perform. Crane genuinely believes the data justifies the method. He has been telling himself this for eighteen months and finding it harder each month. He will not stop on his own. He is, however, the weak point in the Shard Division's external relationships — a man who has not completed the internal calculus required to do what he is doing without consequence.

## How the Trade Reaches the Street

**Import:** Raw materials and finished product arrive via the Silverrun River's less-monitored barge sections, Pauper's Gate's deliberate lack of close inspection, and — for Hollostar Dust — the pre-city tunnel passages beneath the Maze Quarter that the Vault Guard has not fully mapped. A Halfling Quarter merchant house facilitates Silverrun transport believing they are moving Accord-legal herbal goods. They have not examined their cargo in six years. The Guild considers this arrangement stable.

**Warehousing:** Two locations. One beneath the Manufactories (Guild-funded, Clank-staffed under arrangements the Free Hand does not know about). One in a basement on Shadowside's eastern edge behind a legitimate cooperage operation.

**Distribution:** Regional distributors receive from Guild deputies and break to street level. Deputies do not meet dealers. The separation is operational security maintained rigorously. Independent dealers operate under tolerance arrangements.

**Enforcement buffer:** The Guild pays specific people within the city's administrative apparatus for information — gate staffing rotations, inspection schedules, Council enforcement priorities. Purchased rather than stolen, because purchased information is reliable. Specific recipients are not named here; GMs place them where they serve the campaign.

## The Seven Substances

_Presented in order of social acceptability — most tolerated to most restricted._

### 1. HOLLOW PIPE

_Street names: Greenblend, Ashsmoke, "the Pipe"_ _Risk tier: Low — socially tolerated, technically unclassified_ _Consumption method: Smoked in a pipe or hand-rolled paper_

**What it is:** A dried herb blend built on hollow reed grass — a semi-aquatic plant from the Silverrun marshes with mild psychoactive properties. Standard blends add lavender, marsh root, and ashen bark. Premium versions incorporate a small processed Fade compound (undisclosed) that deepens effects and creates a subtle repeat-purchase hook without the user's knowledge.

**Appearance:** Loose dried herb mix, pale grey-green to dark brown depending on quality. Distinctly herbal smell with a faint sweetness. Burns pale white-grey smoke. Any Lumencrest resident who has spent time in Shadowside recognizes the smell immediately.

**How people use it:** Packed into a short-stemmed pipe or rolled in thin paper, lit and inhaled slowly. Social in the way wine is social — shared after a shift, passed among friends, lit alone at the end of a hard day. There is no ritual around it. It is ordinary.

**Production:** Base components sold openly in the Night Market. Assembly requires no specialized equipment. The Fade-infused premium versions need a simple alchemical fixative step any experienced herbalist can perform. Three or four regular producers supply most of Lumencrest; the Guild does not control this market — margins don't justify it.

**The experience:** Warmth in the chest, mild anxiety reduction, ease. The city's background crisis becomes easier to carry. Duration 1-2 hours. No perceptual distortion. Fully functional throughout.

---

**GAME MECHANICS — HOLLOW PIPE** _All conditions: [[Lumencrest_Conditions_Reference]]_

**Boon (2 hours):** Clear 1 Stress. Advantage on Presence rolls for duration.

**Detriment (2 hours):** Disadvantage on Instinct rolls involving awareness or sharp attention.

**Addiction roll:** Standard blend — not required. Premium Fade-infused blend used daily for 2+ weeks: Presence (Composure) DC 12. Failure means Fade dependency is building without the character's knowledge — GM tracks privately, Fade mechanics apply going forward.

**Dependency (daily use, standard blend):**

- 2+ weeks: Burning Need Tier 1. Character prefers to use before social situations. No mechanical effect yet.
- 1+ month: Burning Need Tier 2. -1 to Presence in demanding social encounters without Pipe.
- Clears: 1 week abstinence. Character rolls Presence with Fear during that week, no other penalty.

**Permanent debuff threshold:** None for standard blend. Hollow Pipe does not ruin you if you stop. This is intentional and unique among this list.

**Cost:** 1-3 copper (standard). 8-12 copper (premium infused).

**Key sellers:** Renn Aldous (Smoke Den, house blend is infused without disclosure), Night Market herb vendors (two sell finished blends openly), Tessiel Wane (Night Pharmacy, plain version, no infusion).

### 2. VELVETONGUE

_Street names: Velvet, the Kiss, Silvertaste_ _Risk tier: Low-Moderate — illegal but rarely enforced for possession; strongly associated with wealthy and Academic users_ _Consumption method: Dissolved — thin pressed wafers placed under the tongue and held until fully dissolved (approximately 2 minutes)_

**What it is:** Lumencrest's "acceptable" illegal substance — the one that appears at Radiant Core dinner parties alongside imported wine and nobody calls the watch. Velvetongue is derived from the secretion of the _silvermouth mollusk_, a freshwater creature from the Accord's eastern rivers. Originally a legitimate surgical recovery pain tool; the recreational preparation strips the medicinal application and keeps the social and cognitive effects. Illegal under the Alchemical Safety Ordinance. Possession draws a fine — theoretically. The fine has never been levied against anyone with a Radiant Core address. This is widely known and widely resented in Shadowside.

**Appearance:** Thin pressed wafers, thumbnail-sized, translucent white with a slight silver shimmer. Packaged in small lacquered wooden boxes of 2-4 wafers — elegant enough to be mistaken for confectionery. Clean, faintly sweet smell. Unlike everything else on this list, Velvetongue is pleasant to be near. This is deliberate and part of its class appeal.

**How people use it:** One wafer placed under the tongue. Held until dissolved — about 2 minutes. Taste: brief intense sweetness, then a clean mineral note. No smoke, no smell, no visible preparation. Can be used in formal company without any sign. The complete discretion of this method is a significant feature for its primary market.

**Production:** The silvermouth mollusk does not live in Lumencrest's climate. Dried raw secretion is the Guild's most expensive eastern import. Processing into wafers requires a skilled alchemist; one independent producer operates in the Academic Quarter (Professor Dorin Mast, see NPCs) and one Guild-funded operation runs outside the city. The lacquered boxes come from a Halfling Quarter craftsman who believes he makes confectionery containers. He is not wrong in any way he is aware of.

**The experience:** Lucidity — thoughts connect more easily, conversation flows, social friction decreases. Not euphoria exactly; more the removal of background self-consciousness and hesitation. Mild enhancement of verbal fluency and social confidence. The effect is subtle enough that people who don't know someone has used it often simply think they're having an unusually good conversation. Duration: 3-4 hours. At double dose (2 wafers): the lucidity tips into light euphoria, colours slightly more vivid, social confidence approaching fearlessness.

---

**GAME MECHANICS — VELVETONGUE**

**Boon, single wafer (3-4 hours):** Advantage on Presence rolls. Once per scene, may reroll a failed Presence roll and take the better result. No resource cost — the clarity is genuine.

**Boon, double dose (2 wafers):** As above, plus clear 2 Stress and treat one Presence partial success as a full success once per scene. Apply the double detriment below.

**Detriment, single (3-4 hours):** Disadvantage on Knowledge rolls requiring careful analysis or precise memory recall. Better at talking; worse at remembering exactly what was said and to whom.

**Detriment, double dose:** Disadvantage on all Knowledge AND Instinct rolls. The fearlessness and the tunnel vision arrive together.

**Addiction roll:** Presence (Composure) DC 10. Low difficulty reflects mild physical dependency profile. Social dependency — reaching for it before any difficult conversation — builds narratively regardless of roll.

**Dependency:**

- 3+ weeks daily use: Burning Need Tier 1. Narratively present, not yet mechanical.
- 2+ months daily use: Burning Need Tier 2. -1 to Presence in situations the character would previously have used Velvetongue for. Not a capability loss — a return to actual baseline that now feels like a deficit.

**Permanent debuff threshold:** None physical. The permanent consequences of Velvetongue dependency are social and narrative — relationships built on a chemically-enhanced version of the character, conversations maintained without the tool that shaped them, the slow work of figuring out what is real. The GM tracks these; they do not have a mechanical ceiling.

**Cost:** 12 silver per box of 2 wafers. 20 silver (Academic Quarter source, marginally more refined). Available Quiet Market, Night Pharmacy, and Radiant Core social connections.

**Key sellers:** Tessiel Wane (Night Pharmacy, primary retail, established customers), Professor Dorin Mast (Academic Quarter, source — see NPCs), Quiet Market.

### 3. THORNBLOOM

_Street names: Bloom, Thorn, "the Itch" (derogatory, non-users)_ _Risk tier: Moderate — illegal, elven-specific, enhanced watch attention in Radiant Core_ _Consumption method: Skin absorption — dried flower held against inner wrist, back of neck, or inner elbow for 10-15 minutes. Requires stillness._ _Target population: Elves and half-elves only. No effect on other ancestries._

**What it is:** A dried flower preparation from the _thornbloom vine_ — a plant with no culinary or medicinal application that grows in the Accord's deep western forests. The vine's flowers interact specifically with elven and half-elven nervous system biology through skin absorption. The mechanism is not fully understood even by its producers; discovered empirically three generations ago by an elven herbalist who has been dead fifty years. Their notes have been circulating in the elven herbalist community since.

Thornbloom does nothing to humans, dwarves, halflings, gnomes, or any non-elven ancestry. This specificity concentrates its use in Lumencrest's elven population — the Radiant Core's elven estates and the Academic Quarter's elven faculty and students primarily. City watch enforcement in Shadowside treats Thornbloom possession as a minor matter. City watch enforcement in the Radiant Core treats elven Thornbloom possession as a social problem requiring careful handling. This means almost no enforcement at all.

**Appearance:** Small dried flowers, deep purple-black with an iridescent quality when dry. Packaged in small cloth sachets tied with a specific double-knot that regular users recognize. Mild woody smell that non-elves find pleasant; elves apparently find considerably more interesting. When pressed against skin for use, the flower warms noticeably — users describe this warmth as the first sign absorption is working.

**How people use it:** One or two dried flowers pressed against a pulse point — inner wrist most commonly, back of neck for faster absorption, inner elbow for slower and longer-lasting effect. Held in place for 10-15 minutes while sitting still. Cannot be used while doing anything active; the absorption requires stillness and the initial effects begin during contact. The flower is typically discarded after use, though some users keep spent flowers as a habit marker that has nothing to do with efficacy.

**Production:** The thornbloom vine does not grow near Lumencrest. Guild imports dried flowers through western trade routes — longer supply chain than most substances. Processing is minimal: dried to a specific moisture content, harvest timing critical to effect quality. Lumencrest's supply comes from a specific western forest commune who believe they supply a legitimate elven herbalist market. They are not entirely wrong.

**The experience:** The warmth at the pulse point spreads during contact. By the time the flower is removed, the effect is established: profound temporal expansion — time moves unhurriedly, with a depth and richness that elven users describe as close to a meditative state otherwise requiring days to achieve. Memory becomes vivid and textured without being intrusive. The present moment feels complete. Duration: 4-6 hours. No functional impairment — an elf under Thornbloom is not obviously altered. They are simply very present. At dependency, the mirror of this emerges: the ordinary present begins to feel thin, rushed, and insufficient. This is the trap.

---

**GAME MECHANICS — THORNBLOOM** _Elves and half-elves only. No effect on any other ancestry._

**Application time:** 15 minutes of stillness required. Cannot be used mid-scene. Planning is necessary.

**Boon (4-6 hours):** +2 to all Knowledge rolls. Once per scene, the character may ask the GM one question about a situation, person, or environment they are currently observing and receive an honest answer as if they had succeeded on a DC 14 Instinct roll. This is not a roll — it simply happens once. The temporal depth makes them an exceptional reader of situations.

**Detriment (4-6 hours):** Disadvantage on Agility rolls. The character is not slow — they are not hurried, even when they should be. Urgency does not land correctly.

**Addiction roll:** Instinct (Composure) DC 11 (elves). DC 13 (half-elves — partial biology makes the interaction slightly more potent in its hook).

**Dependency:**

- 3+ weeks daily: Burning Need Tier 1. The ordinary world feels slightly less rich.
- 1+ month daily: Burning Need Tier 2. -1 to Knowledge rolls without Thornbloom. The once-per-scene GM question is no longer available without Thornbloom — the character has been outsourcing that depth of observation and can no longer reach it unassisted.
- 2+ months daily: Burning Need Tier 3. -2 to Knowledge without Thornbloom. The Agility detriment is now present at all times as baseline temporal experience has shifted — even without the drug, slower to urgency. **Permanent debuff threshold.**

**Permanent debuff threshold:** 2 months daily use. After this point, even with full detox, the character permanently retains **-1 to Agility rolls** as the nervous system has reorganized around the altered temporal experience. The GM informs the player only when it becomes clear in play.

**Cost:** 8 silver per sachet (1-2 uses). Quiet Market primary source. Specific Night Market contacts serving the elven community. Private Radiant Core arrangements.

**Key sellers:** Quiet Market, **Wren Ashal** (Shadowside, see NPCs — primary street-level dealer), Radiant Core private arrangements facilitated but not directly operated by the Guild.

### 4. RED CRADLE

_Street names: The Red, Cradle, Voss Blood_ _Risk tier: Moderate-High — illegal, Alchemical Safety Ordinance, distribution carries mandatory arrest_ _Consumption method: Dissolved under tongue — a small amount of paste held against mucous membrane until absorbed_

**What it is:** A concentrated alchemical extract derived from the cradle flower of the Vossomar highlands. The legitimate medicinal version manages surgical pain. The street version is 4-6x concentrated via a refinement process developed in Lumencrest thirty years ago. Origin of the refinement formula: unknown, Academic Quarter suspected. Imported seed pods via the Guild's Vossomar supply chain — volumes needed far exceed what the legitimate herbal market explains.

**Appearance:** Dense dark red-burgundy paste, nearly black in shadow. Sold in small sealed clay pots — a single dose is roughly a quarter-teaspoon. Bitter-sweet smell that users find distinctive and non-users find unpleasant. **The paste stains the underside of the tongue faint reddish-brown for 6-8 hours.** Experienced city watch and Vault Guard know to check for this during inspections. Users are aware of the tell; some use tongue-blackening preparations from the Night Market to obscure it.

**How people use it:** A pea-sized amount pressed under the tongue and held there. Activates through mucous membrane contact. Warmth is immediate; full effect takes 3-5 minutes.

**The experience:** Profound physical comfort. Pain eliminated. Muscle tension fully released. Emotional pain joins physical pain in the background. Users feel capable, calm, unhurried. Duration 4-6 hours. The body adapts — doses increase, baseline drops. Regular users begin to feel worse than they did before they started, driving continued use to return to normal rather than to feel good. This is the trap. Vael at the Stitch treats Red dependency more than any other condition she sees.

---

**GAME MECHANICS — RED CRADLE**

**Boon (4-6 hours):** Clear 2 Stress. Remove one minor physical condition (pain, fatigue, physical discomfort). Advantage on Strength rolls — the physical ease is real and functional.

**Detriment (4-6 hours):** Roll Knowledge with Fear. The calm that removes pain removes urgency and precision.

**Addiction roll:** Strength (Fortitude) DC 13. On a fail by 5 or more (result 8 or lower on a DC 13 roll), GM notes immediate dependency onset privately — the character feels the flatness when the effect ends that same session.

**Dependency:**

- Weeks 1-2: Burning Need Tier 1. Preference, no mechanical effect.
- Week 3: Burning Need Tier 2. -1 to Strength rolls more than 24 hours without Red. Character may not connect this to the substance — it presents as ordinary physical decline.
- Week 4+: Burning Need Tier 3. -2 to Strength without Red. Character gains **Distracted** at the start of demanding scenes without Red. The Boon no longer clears Stress — it returns the character to baseline only. **This is the trap.**

**Withdrawal:** 1 week of daily Strength (Fortitude) DC 14. Failure: **Broken** condition for 24 hours. Critical failure: Major physical condition (severe cramping, cannot act normally) for 24 hours. Vael's support reduces DC to 11.

**Permanent debuff threshold:** 6 weeks at Burning Need Tier 3 without detox attempt. Strength baseline reduces by 1 permanently until Vael's full 6-week protocol is completed. Even after protocol, 3 additional weeks to full recovery.

**Cost:** 8 copper (Night Market, clean Guild supply). 12 copper (Night Pharmacy). 5 copper (bulk, contamination risk — contaminated batches apply a Major physical condition on first use).

**Key sellers:** Night Market Guild distributors (two), Maren Solt (Maze Quarter, independent, see NPCs), Renn Aldous (Smoke Den, clean Guild supply).

### 5. FADE

_Street names: Grey Fade, the Grey, Ghost_ _Risk tier: High — illegal, enhanced Alchemical Safety Ordinance penalties, 90-day mandatory detention for distribution_ _Consumption method: Dissolved — fine powder dissolved in water (tasteless, undetectable), or pressed directly to the upper gum with a moistened fingertip and held 30 seconds_

**What it is:** A crystallized alchemical preparation synthesized in Lumencrest approximately fifty years ago in a discontinued Luminarium experimental program. The synthesis notes were supposed to be sealed. They circulated in the Academic Quarter for a decade before reaching the Underground. An independent chemist spent three years making the formula producible without a full lab. That chemist is dead. The formula is not.

Fade targets fatigue signaling specifically — binding to nervous system receptors that handle exhaustion. Under Fade the body does not register tiredness correctly. Cognitive function genuinely sharpens. This is not illusion. It spread through the Academic Quarter and Manufactories management class first because it works, and because the demands of those populations created exactly the conditions for sustained use.

**Appearance:** Fine crystalline powder, white to very pale grey. Darker batches indicate lower quality and higher contamination risk. Sold in small folded paper packets — a dose fits on a fingertip. No distinctive smell. No smoke. Cannot be detected in a drink. **The tell for those who know:** users under Fade blink slightly less than normal. The eyes are very still. Alert. Experienced Vault Guard officers and city watch with specific training recognize it; most do not.

**How people use it:** Dissolved in water (discrete, slower onset) or pressed to the upper gum with a moistened finger (faster, stronger, slightly visible in the act of application). Either way: tasteless, odourless, producing no smoke. Takes effect within minutes.

**The experience:** Mental clarity. Fatigue drops away. Sustained concentration available for 6-10 hours. Lower doses: capable, rested, clear. Higher doses: a brittle euphoria with edges that feel like power and are the beginning of neurological fraying. The trap: the receptors that handle fatigue also handle rest and recovery. Sustained use bypasses actual rest needs. Regular users stop sleeping normally. Their bodies stop performing maintenance. After weeks, the clarity reverses — sharpness becomes brittleness. Long-term heavy users lose the ability to think clearly without Fade while also being unable to think clearly with it. This is the life-ruining trajectory.

---

**GAME MECHANICS — FADE**

**Boon (6-10 hours):** Remove Tired or Fatigued condition if present. Ignore sleep deprivation penalties for duration. Advantage on Knowledge and Instinct rolls. Once per session while under Fade, treat a partial success on a Knowledge or Instinct roll as a full success — the clarity occasionally produces results otherwise out of reach.

**Detriment (6-10 hours):** Roll Presence with Fear. Cannot sleep during the active period regardless of conditions — attempting rest produces wakefulness with zero rest benefit.

**Addiction roll:** Instinct (Composure) DC 14. Fast hook. On a failure, the character experiences a noticeable come-down when the effect ends — not withdrawal, just a flatness, a specific absence of the sharpness they had.

**Dependency:**

- Week 1: Burning Need Tier 1. Using feels normal. Not using feels slightly worse than before.
- Week 2: Burning Need Tier 2. -1 to Knowledge and Instinct more than 18 hours without Fade. May present as ordinary cognitive decline from overwork — plausible in Lumencrest.
- Week 3+: Burning Need Tier 3. -2 to Knowledge and Instinct without Fade. Cannot benefit from extended rest normally — needs 12 hours of sleep to gain what 8 hours used to provide. With Fade, the once-per-session bonus no longer functions — maintenance only, not enhancement.

**Severe dependency (1+ month regular use):** The character gains **Broken Rest** as a persistent condition — extended rests restore half normal benefit unless Vael's protocol is followed for 3 weeks.

**Permanent debuff threshold:** 6 weeks at Burning Need Tier 3. After this, -1 to Knowledge and Instinct permanently until Vael's 6-week protocol is completed. Even after protocol, one additional month for full recovery.

**Withdrawal:** Vael's protocol: 3-week taper with supportive alchemical preparation (4 silver per week + consultation). Without support: 2 weeks of daily Strength (Fortitude) DC 15. Failure: **Broken** condition for 48 hours. Critical failure: cannot form reliable short-term memories for 72 hours (Major cognitive condition).

**Cost:** 15 copper (Night Market). 20 copper (Quiet Market, cleaner). Bulk pricing available through Guild contacts — itself a dependency trap, as bulk access normalises daily use.

**Key sellers:** Tessiel Wane (Night Pharmacy, primary retail, established customers only — she keeps records), Pale (Maze Quarter, also the producer — accessible through Renn Aldous, see NPCs), Quiet Market (premium, Academic Quarter and management class primary market).

### 6. HOLLOSTAR DUST

_Street names: Star Dust, the Shine, Void Salt_ _Risk tier: Extreme — banned by Council emergency order, Vault Guard jurisdiction, mandatory arrest for any possession quantity_ _Consumption method: Inhaled — a fine line of powder tapped from the vial onto a smooth surface and inhaled sharply through one nostril_

**What it is:** Ground Hollostar shard material — the crystalline outer shell of a Hollostar fragment — refined to powder through physical grinding and an arcane activation step. Without the activation, inert powder. With it, the most restricted substance in Lumencrest. Shards come from Hollostar fragments shed during energy-output cycles; the Heart produces some, the Starfall Basin produces significantly more and is the primary continental source.

The Council banned Hollostar Dust eight years ago primarily for security reasons, not health ones. Users can perceive the Lumen Network directly — its energy flow, distribution, weak points. Several long-term users have provided accurate descriptions of the Heart's internal structure that matched classified documentation they had no access to. The Vault Guard does not want people perceiving things near the Heart. This is the real reason for the emergency order.

**The supply crisis:** Since the Shard Division began consuming shard material at volume, Hollostar Dust has been growing progressively harder to find. Dependent users who cannot access clean product are using whatever they can — Dust cut with grey salt, chalk, minor alchemical residue, and in two documented cases with something from the Shard Division's waste stream that produced effects far beyond recreational. Overdose risk is real. Death is now possible from cut product. Buying from unverified sources is genuinely dangerous, and the desperation driving people to unverified sources is increasing.

**Appearance:** Fine crystalline powder, pale silver-white in clean product. Murkier grey-white in cut product. **Clean product has a faint luminescence** — barely visible in daylight, real in darkness, not bright but real. Cut product smells slightly wrong — chemical and organic in a way that doesn't fit. Experienced users can tell. Desperate ones sometimes ignore it. Sold in sealed dark glass vials; light exposure degrades activated material over time.

**How people use it:** A small amount tapped from the vial onto a smooth dark surface — the back of the hand, a piece of dark paper. Inhaled in a single sharp breath through one nostril. Takes effect within 2 minutes. **At onset: an involuntary visual flash** — the user briefly sees the room's architecture in terms of its energy content before Network perception stabilises. This flash lasts 2 seconds and produces a momentary unfocused quality in the user's gaze that is visible to anyone watching closely. Significant if someone is paying attention.

**Production:** Three stages requiring specialised equipment at each. First: raw shard material imported through the pre-city tunnel passages. Second: physical grinding with specialised tools (standard grinding destroys the crystalline structure; the tools' presence is itself criminal evidence). Third: arcane activation by a capable practitioner — without this step the ground material is inert powder. Two such practitioners exist in Lumencrest currently. Both are deeply embedded in Guild protection. Both know their identities are the most critical secret in the Lumencrest operation.

---

**GAME MECHANICS — HOLLOSTAR DUST** _Conditions: Resonant, Signal Bleed, Grief, Transformed — see [[Lumencrest_Conditions_Reference]]_

**Boon (2-4 hours):** The character gains the **Resonant** condition for the duration (see [[Lumencrest_Conditions_Reference]] for full mechanical detail). Additionally, on first use and then again after each 3 uses within a single month, roll on the **Hollostar Dust Ability Table** below — a permanent transformation is occurring.

**Detriment:** In areas of high Network density (Radiant Core, Academic Quarter, near distribution nodes), the character gains **Signal Bleed** — Resonant's benefits become interference. During a brownout while Resonant: automatic **Shaken**. The character's eyes carry the Dust's luminescence while Resonant — detectable to anyone who knows what to look for (Vault Guard, trained city watch officers, experienced Night Market traders). This is a significant exposure risk.

**Addiction roll:** Presence (Composure) DC 16. The dependency is psychological before physical — what the drug shows is genuinely significant and the desire to return to that perception is profound.

**Dependency:**

- 3 uses within a week: **Grief Tier 1** applied. The ache begins.
- 2 weeks regular use: **Grief Tier 2**. -1 to Presence rolls in high-Network areas without Dust.
- 1 month regular use: **Grief Tier 3 — The Hollow**. Automatic **Shaken** during brownouts. -2 to Presence baseline. Cannot benefit from long rest in locations with active Network infrastructure unless under Dust.

**The Transformation:** On the roll trigger (first use, then each 3 uses within a month), roll on the Hollostar Dust Ability Table. The ability manifests gradually over the following week — the character notices it, cannot explain it. The ability is **permanent** and cannot be removed by detox.

**Dependency on the Transformation:**

- 1 month continued regular use after gaining ability: -1 to all rolls that would logically use the new ability (still functions, but noticeably less sharp).
- 2 months continued use: ability functions at half effectiveness without Dust (GM interpretation).
- 3 months continued use: **character cannot use the ability at all without Dust** until full detox is completed.

**Permanent impairment threshold:** 3 months regular use without detox attempt. Even after successful detox, the character's natural access to their Transformed ability is permanently reduced — activating it requires a DC 12 Instinct roll rather than being freely available. The ability does not disappear. It becomes effort rather than instinct.

**Cut product risk:** If using unverified supply (any source other than confirmed Guild or Quiet Market), before rolling addiction: roll Strength (Fortitude) DC 13.

- Failure: Major physical condition (severe neurological disruption — vomiting, temporary blindness, seizure-like episode)
- Critical failure: **Broken** condition immediately
- Fail by 8+: Death roll, DC 15. Vael can treat overdose with immediate attention. She cannot treat contamination-caused death.

**Withdrawal:** No established treatment protocol for the perceptual component. Vael addresses physical symptoms. Losing the Network perception: users describe it as going blind in a sense that does not map to ordinary blindness. 2 weeks Disadvantage on Instinct rolls as perception recalibrates. Grief Tier 1-2 clears after 3-week detox with support. Grief Tier 3 reduces to Tier 1 permanently — the Hollow becomes the Ache, and it stays.

**Cost:** 8 silver (verified Guild supply, deep Maze Quarter contacts — requires Shadowside reputation +2 or equivalent). 15 silver (Quiet Market, intermittent). 3-4 silver (unverified, dangerous).

**Key sellers:** "The Chandler" contact point (deep Maze Quarter, specific access requirements), Quiet Market (intermittent, supply dependent), nowhere else that can be trusted.

### 7. MIRRORPETAL

_Street names: The Still, Ghost Flower, Once_ _Risk tier: Unclassified — technically legal because no authority has fully understood what it is. The Vault Guard is aware of it. The Luminarium has three research files on it. No one has published conclusions._ _Consumption method: Skin contact — a single fresh petal held face-down against the palm of the hand with fingers loosely closed around it for 20 minutes. Stillness required._ _Dependency profile: None. This is not a trick._

**What it is:** The freshly harvested petal of the _mirrorpetal flower_ — a plant of uncertain origin growing in only one confirmed location: a specific chamber in the pre-city tunnels beneath the Maze Quarter, fed by reflected Hollostar energy from the Network above. Nobody planted it. It has been there longer than Lumencrest has been a city. The petals are translucent, membrane-thin, and dissolve against skin within the contact period. The plant produces at most a dozen petals per week from the full colony. This natural scarcity makes large-scale trade impossible and removes it entirely from the Guild's distribution model.

Mirrorpetal has no addiction profile. The Luminarium's three research files exist because what it does cannot be explained within current understanding of how drugs or arcane substances work. The effect is singular, non-repeating in content, and leaves no physical trace. Users sometimes feel altered for weeks afterward — not in ways that drive repeat use, but in ways that drive reflection.

**Appearance:** A single petal is roughly thumbnail-sized, translucent white with visible internal structure like pressed glass. It holds moisture from the growing environment and must be used within one hour of harvest or it dries completely and loses all effect. Dried mirrorpetal is inert. There is no way to preserve it, stockpile it, or process it for later use. Fresh only, within the hour. This makes standard distribution channels irrelevant.

**How people use it:** The petal is placed face-down on the palm and the hand closed gently around it. 20 minutes to dissolve against the skin's warmth. Most users sit quietly during this period — not strictly required, but the effect begins during contact and the onset is disorienting enough that stillness is practical advice. The petal leaves no residue when fully dissolved. No smell. No visible sign of use. No stained skin, no altered eyes, no tell of any kind.

**The experience:** Onset begins during the contact period as a growing sense of stillness — not calm exactly, more like the moment before a significant thing happens, suspended and present. When the petal finishes dissolving, the effect fully arrives: the character experiences a vision, fully sensory, of something true. Not a hallucination. Not a dream. Something the user needs to see — or needs to know exists. The content is always personal, always significant, and always accurate. Former users report seeing the death of a loved one before it happened; understanding the nature of a relationship they had misread for years; perceiving the structural reality of a choice they were avoiding. The Luminarium's researchers believe mirrorpetal interfaces with the user's memory and forward modeling — constructing from real information a clear picture of something the user has not allowed themselves to know. Others believe something older is involved. Neither camp can prove their position. Duration: 2-4 hours for the vision. Afterward, fully present and functional, but quiet. Many users describe the aftermath as the most important day of their recent life. Some describe it as the worst.

**Production / sourcing:** The colony produces what it produces and cannot be cultivated elsewhere. Transplant attempts have produced plants that do not flower. The people who know the colony exists: Harrow in the Dry Chambers knows and does not harvest from it. A tunnel researcher (now deceased) documented it twenty years ago and the documentation reached the Luminarium. And one current harvester: a person known only as **Sable** (see NPCs) who is not Sable Mors from Threadbare and whose presence in Shadowside predates that Sable Mors by at least eight years.

**GAME MECHANICS — MIRRORPETAL**

**Scarcity rule:** Mirrorpetal cannot be purchased. It can only be received — as a gift, an expression of earned trust, or through direct knowledge of the colony's location. Maximum one use per player character per campaign unless the GM determines otherwise. This limit is soft, not hard — the GM decides when a second encounter with Mirrorpetal is narratively appropriate, which will be rare.

**No addiction roll.** No dependency mechanics. No Burning Need. The reason most characters do not seek it repeatedly is that the vision is not comfortable, and the average person does not want to see clearly the things it shows. This is a roleplay constraint, not a mechanical one.

**Application:** 20 minutes of stillness. Cannot be used in any active scene. The character must have uninterrupted quiet time for this to work.

**Boon:** The character receives a vision controlled entirely by the GM, providing one of the following (GM choice, based on what is most narratively significant at this point in the campaign):

- Accurate knowledge of one fact about the current campaign arc the character could not have discovered through investigation
- A clear view of the consequence of a specific choice the character is currently facing or avoiding
- An honest perception of a relationship — with an NPC or another player character — the character has been misreading
- A fragment of the Heart's history: what it was, what happened to it, what it might become — experienced as memory, not information

The vision is always accurate. It is not always complete. It rarely provides comfort.

**Detriment:** The character is **Distracted** for 1 hour after the vision ends — not from impairment, from the weight of clarity. Additionally, the GM passes the player one private note: something the vision showed that the character now knows and cannot unknow, which may become mechanically relevant later. This is information, not a penalty. What the character does with it is theirs.

**Interaction with the Transformed condition:** If the character has the **Transformed** condition from Hollostar Dust use, Mirrorpetal interacts with the Transformation. The GM rolls 1d6 privately: on a 4-6, the vision extends — the character briefly perceives not just what they personally need to know but what the Network knows, including a fragment of the Heart's actual current condition that goes beyond anything Hollostar Dust normally provides. The GM should prepare this content before any Transformed character uses Mirrorpetal.

**Cost:** Not for sale. Access through Sable (see NPCs), through knowledge of the colony location (the pre-city tunnels, accessible from the Dry Chambers route — Harrow knows where it is and will not take anyone there without significant reason), or as a gift from someone who received a petal and chose to share it rather than use it. The last option has happened twice in the past three years. Both times, the person who gave their petal away was someone who had already seen what they needed to see.

## Substance NPCs — Introduced in This Document

_Full entries for NPCs appearing elsewhere (Renn Aldous, Tessiel Wane, Maren Solt, Pale) are in [[05_Shadowside_Expanded]] or [[08_The_Underground_Expanded]]._

**Professor Dorin Mast** — Academic Quarter, Innovation Row. Human, 58. The independent Velvetongue producer. A tenured Luminarium researcher who stumbled into the production process twenty years ago and has been running a quiet side operation since. He does not consider himself a criminal — he considers himself a practical person who identified an unmet need and has the chemistry to meet it. His product is the cleanest in the city. His prices are the highest. He supplies the Academic Quarter's Velvetongue market and, through two intermediaries, the Quiet Market's stock. He has never been investigated; his social position makes investigation socially awkward for the people who would have to conduct it.

**Wren Ashal** — Shadowside, Maze Quarter adjacent. Half-elf, 29. Lumencrest's primary street-level Thornbloom dealer. A regular user themselves — four years, currently Burning Need Tier 2, managed through careful self-dosing. The temporal effects of Thornbloom have given Wren a quality of presence that Shadowside residents find either striking or unsettling. They sell only to elves and half-elves — not from restriction but from practical knowledge that selling something with no effect on humans is a waste of product and a waste of relationship. They know the elven community in Lumencrest better than almost anyone and are a natural first contact for elven player characters. They will not sell to anyone visibly in crisis, which is a line they hold even when the financial pressure is real.

**"Sable" (the second one)** — Location undisclosed. Ancestry unknown. Age estimated 40s based on manner; physical appearance does not confirm or deny this. The sole harvester and distributor of Mirrorpetal from the pre-city tunnel colony. Has been doing this for an unknown period — the Dry Chambers community has been aware of their existence for at least eight years, though Harrow has never met them directly. Distributes petals by leaving them in specific locations for specific people who have been assessed as ready for what the petal will show. Has never been wrong about readiness. Does not explain how they assess it. Does not explain much. The name duplication with Sable Mors at Threadbare is mildly annoying to Sable Mors, who has been in Shadowside considerably longer and chose her name without reference to anyone else's.

## Integration and Campaign Notes

**The supply crisis as a living condition:** As the campaign progresses and the Shard Division's material consumption increases, Hollostar Dust scarcity should escalate. Track this as a world condition — prices rise, cut product becomes more prevalent, dependent NPCs the players have met begin to show signs of using dangerous supply. This pressure eventually becomes foreground when a player or significant NPC faces the contaminated product death roll. The crisis should feel like it's building before it breaks.

**The thread that connects everything:** Players who investigate the substance trade deeply enough will eventually reach the upper levels of the Dust supply chain — and those upper levels use the same shard material, the same tunnel passages, and some of the same Guild infrastructure as the Shard Division. The Collector moves through the same tunnels that Dust comes through. The players will find one thread first. Either path leads eventually to the same knot.

**Mirrorpetal as a campaign tool:** Deploy this at a dramatically appropriate moment — not as a reward or a power-up, but as a clarifying event. A character who receives a Mirrorpetal vision at the right moment sees something that reframes what they have been doing and why. Used well, it is the most powerful single narrative tool in this document. Save it.
