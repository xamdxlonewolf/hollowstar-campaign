# The Academic Quarter — Expanded District Guide

## Points of Interest, Vendors, and NPCs

_This document expands on the Academic Quarter as established in [[06_Academic_Quarter]], adding the locations, people, and daily texture needed to run the district at the table. The Academic Quarter is Lumencrest's smallest major district and its most intellectually concentrated — the place where the city's crisis is understood most completely and where the gap between understanding and being able to act on that understanding is most acutely felt. Cross-reference [[07_Vault_District]] for the Luminarium's relationship with the Heart's physical location, [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] for the Night Pharmacy's connection to the Quarter's substance use, and [[Controlled_Substances_Trade]] for the Fade dependency profile that runs through the research community._

---

## Running the Academic Quarter at the Table

The Academic Quarter runs on a different clock from the rest of Lumencrest. The city wakes at dawn and works through daylight; the Quarter works whenever the work requires, which is currently at all hours. The Luminarium's research facilities have been continuously occupied for nine months — day shifts running into evening shifts running into the people who don't go home because they've stopped being able to tell the difference between being here and being home.

**The light:** The Quarter runs at full brightness even at midnight, and this is visible from Lamplight Ward — a specific quality of light above the Quarter's roofline that is brighter and whiter than the amber of the Ward's street lamps. Something is being worked on urgently by people who can't afford to stop. Everyone in the adjacent districts has noticed. Nobody in the Quarter is explaining it in terms that reach the public.

**The atmosphere:** Tense in the productive way, which is different from the Manufactories' tense or Shadowside's tense. This tension has the quality of a held breath — people who are very smart doing work they believe matters under conditions that are increasingly strained. The social result is a community that is simultaneously more insular than usual (the work consumes everything) and more fragile than usual (people at the limit of their capacity are less socially stable than people operating normally). Arguments in the Quarter's cafes run sharper and longer than they would elsewhere. Friendships are more intense. The person who was reliable three months ago has been up for thirty hours twice this week.

**Class and access:** The Quarter has a sharper internal hierarchy than it presents publicly. The Luminarium's senior fellows — the people running the classified programs — have access, information, and institutional protection that the junior researchers and students don't. This hierarchy is not usually relevant because the senior fellows and junior researchers are working on the same things. In the current crisis it has become relevant, because the senior fellows' classification decisions determine what the junior researchers know about their own situation. Several junior researchers have concluded they know less than they should. The Student Assembly has made this the center of one of its three active campaigns.

**What players find here:** The Academic Quarter is the district where the city's intellectual reality lives — where the actual state of the Heart is understood (imperfectly, by specific people), where the history of Hollostar research exists (in the Archives), where the people who might be able to solve the city's problem are working themselves to pieces trying to. It is also a district where information that exists in this building and nowhere else in the world could change everything, if it could be accessed and understood and used. The friction between those two things — the information exists, and it is very difficult to reach — is what the Academic Quarter is for, at the table.

## The Luminarium Campus

_(Referenced in [[06_Academic_Quarter]] — expanded here as multiple connected locations)_

**The campus:** Multiple colleges arranged around the Great Courtyard — an open space large enough for the student body to assemble, which happens rarely and has meaning when it does. The architecture is impressive in the way it was built to be: stone that announces permanence, windows that announce light and knowledge, the Archives dome visible from any point in the courtyard. The public areas of the campus — the courtyard, the lecture halls, the ground-floor common spaces — are open to any city resident during operating hours. The research facilities, restricted labs, and upper archives require authorization that is not easily obtained.

---

### The Great Courtyard

**What it is:** The Luminarium's public face — an open plaza that serves as the campus's social center, assembly point, and the space where the distinction between the institution and the city is most porous. City residents come here to use the public reading room, attend lectures, eat at the courtyard's outdoor vendors during good weather. Students and researchers move through it between buildings. On the rare occasions when the Student Assembly calls a public gathering, this is where it happens.

**The outdoor vendors:** Three permanent vendors operate in the courtyard's covered section during daylight hours:

- _Aldric's Books_ — a secondhand book stall run by an elderly man who has been here for thirty years and whose organizational system is entirely mental. He knows where everything is. Nobody else does. He will find a requested text in under three minutes and cannot explain how.
- _The Bread Cart_ — a Halfling Quarter delivery partnership selling fresh bread and simple preparations from a cart that arrives each morning and sells out by early afternoon. The most reliable food in the Quarter at the most accessible price.
- _Instrument and Equipment Repairs_ — a gnome named **Tessa** (no relation to Tessiel Wane, though the similarity has been noted) who repairs precision instruments, measurement tools, and research equipment. Faster and cheaper than the Luminarium's internal maintenance service and willing to work on things that aren't technically in her scope, which makes her the first call for researchers whose equipment has problems they'd rather not report to facilities.

**What happens here for players:** The courtyard is the Quarter's most accessible entry point. The lecture schedule is posted at the courtyard's main entrance. The public reading room's registration desk is just inside the Archives' ground-floor entrance. Students moving between buildings are willing to talk, have opinions about everything currently happening, and know where things are. The courtyard vendors — particularly Tessa — are connected to the research community's practical layer in ways that the official staff aren't.

---

### The Luminarium Research Facilities — Classified Programs Overview

**What they are:** Seventeen concurrent research programs into the Heart's failure, representing the most concentrated scientific effort the city has mounted. They occupy three floors of the Luminarium's northern wing, behind authorization barriers that range from a locked door with a standard key to active arcane monitoring with Vault Guard consultation.

**What players need to know about access:**

- The northern wing's ground floor: accessible with a Luminarium visitor pass (obtainable from the Rector's administrative office with a stated research purpose — takes one day and involves a conversation with a senior administrator named **Wren** who is efficient and hard to mislead).
- The restricted labs on the second floor: requires Research Board authorization (3-5 days minimum, frequently denied for non-Luminarium affiliates) or an escort from a senior fellow with current clearance. Several senior fellows can be persuaded to provide escort under specific circumstances.
- The classified program labs on the third floor: Council Research Board authorization only. No exceptions in the official process. The unofficial exceptions are significant and relevant.

**The programs players will care about:** Six of the seventeen are classified. Of those six, two are relevant to what the players will eventually need to know. The relevant classification decisions were made by Rector Aldis Marke on the basis that the findings were incomplete and potentially alarming if misunderstood. The unofficial reason: one of the two programs has found something that would change the city's political situation immediately and fundamentally if it became public, and Marke is still deciding what to do with it.

---

### The Rector's Office

**Location:** First floor, main Luminarium building, directly accessible from the Great Courtyard. The door is usually open.

**What it is:** The administrative center of the Luminarium and the point of contact for anyone who wants formal access to the institution. The Rector's administrative staff — three people, led by the senior administrator Wren — handle visitor passes, research partnerships, access requests, and the volume of correspondence that comes with running the most significant academic institution on the continent.

**Getting to Aldis Marke:** The administrative staff will route a standard inquiry through the standard process, which takes days. Marke himself is accessible through two other channels: he attends the Ember Lecture Hall's public series when the topic intersects his interests (and his interests are wide), and he eats lunch in the Great Courtyard most days at the same bench, alone, reading, which he describes as the only hour of his day where he is not required to make decisions and which he is paradoxically more willing to interrupt for an interesting conversation than he is to interrupt for a formal meeting.

**The Rector:** **Aldis Marke** — see NPCs.

### The Archives

_(Referenced in [[06_Academic_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** The southern anchor of the campus, the domed building visible from the courtyard. The dome was built before the electrical era and lit by skylights; the Network has since added electric lighting throughout, but the dome's natural light during the day remains the building's best feature.

**The public reading rooms (ground floor):** Available to any city resident with a library card, free to obtain from the registration desk. The reading rooms contain non-classified academic texts, historical records, city administrative history going back four centuries, and the general collection. The ambient quality of the reading rooms — quiet, well-lit, with the specific smell of old paper and binding material that serious libraries accumulate — has made them one of the better thinking spaces in the city. Several people who don't have research business come here specifically to be in a room where serious thought is happening.

**What's in the public collection that matters:**

- The complete public history of Hollostar research — everything the Luminarium has published about Hollostars, Network architecture, and energy theory. Extensive. Accessible. Specifically: the published research stops approximately six years ago, which is when the classified programs began. The gap is visible and meaningful to anyone who knows to look.
- Historical city records including the original construction documentation for the Lumen Network's earliest sections — technical documents that describe the infrastructure beneath Shadowside and the Maze Quarter in more detail than any current city map does.
- The Starfall Seeker research that was published before the organization became more selective about publication — including work by Aldis Crane's previous, legitimate research period that predates his current arrangement with the Guild.

**The restricted sections (lower level, key-locked):** Technical documentation on the Network's current state, Heart output readings going back to the installation, and the classified research records from the current programs. **Oswin Fletch** (see NPCs) is the gatekeeper. The locked door requires either a Research Board key or an approach that Oswin finds convincing, which is not the same as a key but is available through different means.

**Getting into the restricted sections:**

- Official process: Research Board authorization. See above for timeline and difficulty.
- Oswin's discretion: He has, in 130 years, granted unofficial access to five people he concluded needed it badly enough and were trustworthy enough to have it. He would dispute this number and maintain that all five had adequate authorization of a form he considers valid. The means of convincing him is not a trick or a lock — it is genuine, demonstrable need combined with demonstrated trustworthiness over a period of time he sets and will not rush. Players who have been in the Quarter long enough to be known will find his threshold lower, not because he's worn down but because trust takes time to establish and he takes it seriously.
- Alternative access: The Archives' lower level has a maintenance passage that connects to the Luminarium's sub-basement, which connects to the older infrastructure beneath the campus that predates the building's current layout. This passage is on none of the current floor plans but is in the original construction documentation in the public collection. This is not a coincidence.

**The chief restricted-section librarian:** **Oswin Fletch** — see NPCs.

### The Ember Lecture Hall

_(Referenced in [[06_Academic_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** The Luminarium's eastern building, ground floor. The largest single room in the Quarter — 300 seats in tiered arrangement, a raised stage, and the Luminarium's best acoustic engineering because lectures are what the institution was built for.

**The public lecture series:** Free, twice weekly, any city resident may attend. The topics rotate through the Luminarium's faculties; the quality varies by speaker; the attendance varies accordingly. The lectures that draw standing-room crowds are: anything by Aldis Marke, anything touching the Heart situation presented in terms that don't require technical background, and occasional lectures by visiting speakers from other institutions who bring perspectives the Quarter doesn't have.

**The lecture series as a player resource:** Three specific uses:

1. **Finding academics:** Researchers who give public lectures are more accessible than researchers in the middle of classified work. Attending a relevant lecture and asking good questions afterward is the natural entry to a conversation that wouldn't be available through the Rector's office in under a week.
    
2. **Gathering intelligence:** The audience for lectures on the Heart's situation includes city watch officers doing soft monitoring, Vault Guard observers who attend in plain clothes (badly — they are detectable if players have any experience with the Quarter), and the specific Illuminist and Conservator faction members who understand enough to follow what's being said and enough to be anxious about it. The conversations in the Hall before and after lectures are unusually candid.
    
3. **Disseminating information:** The lecture series is one of the few public forums in Lumencrest where something can be said to 300 people at once without going through the Council's communication channels. A player character or allied NPC with something that needs to be publicly said might find that delivering it as a guest lecture (requiring Marke's approval, or the right faculty contact's invitation) is the most direct path to the public hearing it.
    

**The Hall's current booking:** The next three lectures are posted on the courtyard board. One is by **Professor Neva Strand** (see NPCs), covering theoretical Hollostar energy modeling. The title is academic; the content includes the first public presentation of a modeling result that the classified programs have been sitting on for four months. Strand has decided to present it. The Research Board was not informed of this decision. The lecture is in four days.

## Innovation Row

_(Referenced in [[06_Academic_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**What it is:** The Quarter's western edge — a commercial and experimental strip that exists in the productive space between institutional research and independent enterprise. Former students who graduated and couldn't leave, researchers testing hypotheses that the Luminarium's risk assessment wouldn't approve, small ventures that needed proximity to the Quarter's intellectual ecosystem without its institutional constraints. The buildings are smaller here, the signage less formal, the working hours less predictable.

Innovation Row is also the Quarter's informal information market: everyone here knows everyone else, the buildings are close enough that conversations carry between workshops, and the aggregate of those conversations is the most current real-time picture of what's happening in the Quarter's research world — including the things happening in the classified programs that the people working on them talk about over coffee with the people next door who work on something adjacent.

### The Four Constants

_(Referenced in [[06_Academic_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Innovation Row, halfway down the strip. Open 5am to 2am.

**What it is:** The Quarter's best café and the district's social center — the place where the research community gathers when it's not working, which in the current crisis means the place where they gather while working, because the work follows them. The name comes from a theoretical physics principle that the founding proprietor found satisfying; four constants are displayed in chalk on the wall behind the counter and have been there since opening.

**The physical space:** Larger than a café has reason to be, because the Quarter needed it to be. Fifteen tables, a long counter with eight seats, the corner booth near the east window that has the Quarter's best reputation as a thinking space. Bright — the Quarter's full lighting, which at midnight makes the Four Constants feel like noon somewhere sensible. The smell is coffee and the specific warm-paper smell of nearby books and the faint chemical smell that drifts in from the Innovation Row workshops when the windows are open.

**What's on offer:**

_Coffee:_

- The best in Lumencrest. This is not contested. The beans are sourced through the Halfling Quarter's supply network and the extraction method is the proprietor's specific obsession — she has opinions about it that she will share if given any opening and that are correct. 2 copper marks a cup; 3 copper marks for the double preparation that the senior fellows drink at midnight.
- Cold-brewed preparation available in warm months, served in a cup that is larger than it looks. 2 copper marks.

_Food:_

- A short rotating menu calibrated for people who need to eat without stopping thinking. Things that can be held in one hand. Things that are substantial enough to replace a proper meal. Things that are ready when ordered rather than cooked to order, because researchers on deadline don't wait. 2-4 copper marks.
- The exception: the afternoon food service, from mid-afternoon to early evening, when the kitchen produces a proper menu for people between shifts who need something that requires a fork. 5-8 copper marks. Excellent. The proprietor cooks this herself.
- Pastries from the Halfling Quarter delivery network — fresh each morning, gone by mid-morning. 1-2 copper marks.

**The corner table:** The east window booth has a reputation that the proprietor does not encourage because it would require her to manage people competing for the table. It sits empty approximately three hours per day. The rest of the time it's occupied by whoever got there first and is doing something they find important.

**What the Four Constants is for at the table:** This is where academics talk when they're not being careful. The research community's informal conversations happen here — the thing that was observed in the lab last night that doesn't fit the current model, the concern about the classified program on the third floor that can't be raised in a formal meeting, the argument about research ethics that started in the lecture hall and continued over coffee. Players who spend time here will hear things that aren't available through official channels. They will also be noticed and assessed by the community, because the Quarter is small enough that unfamiliar faces at the Four Constants are observed.

**The proprietor:** **Maren Solith** — see NPCs.

### The Workshop Row — Innovation Row Establishments

Six significant workshops operate on Innovation Row. Briefly:

**Crane's Precision Instruments** _(no relation to Aldis Crane)_ — a legitimate instrument-making workshop producing calibration tools for the Luminarium's research facilities. The proprietor, **Dov Crane** (dwarf, 50s), makes precision measurement devices that the Luminarium buys at contract price and that are sold at above-contract price to private buyers in other cities. He knows what his instruments are used to measure, which means he has a specific and technical picture of what the Quarter's research programs are measuring, inferrable from his order logs.

**Varith's Bindery** — bookbinding, document restoration, and archival preparation. **Varith** (gnome, 40s) does the physical work that the Archives requires — repairing deteriorating documents, preparing new acquisitions for shelving, and occasionally, for clients who come through the back of the shop rather than the front, preparing documents that need to exist without a clear origin. She is careful about this second service and selective about its clients.

**The Glass House** — an experimental glassblowing operation producing custom components for laboratory equipment. Run by two Halfling siblings who graduated from the Luminarium five years ago, decided that what the research community most needed was better glass equipment, and are correct. Their waiting list is eight weeks. The Luminarium's facilities team has been trying to get them on staff for three years and they keep declining because their waiting list is eight weeks and that means they're doing fine.

**Holloway's Alchemical Supply** — the Quarter's primary supplier of alchemical materials for research and private use. **Etta Holloway** (human, 60s) runs a fully licensed operation that is the legitimate face of what is also the Quarter's primary retail point for Fade, which she sells from a back shelf arrangement that the Luminarium's administration is aware of and has decided is not their problem because the alternative is researchers obtaining it from less reliable sources. Her supply is Guild-sourced through Tessiel Wane's network. She tracks her Fade customers as carefully as Tessiel does.

**The Reading Room** — a private study space, not a library, that charges 2 silver marks per half-day for the use of a quiet private table with no interruptions, good lighting, and coffee brought to the table without being asked. Fully booked two weeks ahead. The proprietor, a former Luminarium administrator named **Bess** (human, 50s), makes a comfortable living from the proposition that academics with access to perfectly good free study space will pay for the guarantee of uninterrupted quiet. She is correct.

**Shard Workshop** _(no public name — identifiable by a small amber-glass lamp above the door)_ — a research workshop operated by a former Luminarium fellow named **Tern Ashe** (see NPCs) who left the institution's classified program eighteen months ago and set up independently. What Tern is working on in this workshop is not publicly known. What is known: the equipment purchases from Dov Crane's instrument shop in the past year are consistent with work on arcane energy properties of specific crystalline materials. Tessa the repair gnome has been called in twice for equipment that failed. Both times Tessa was asked to sign a discretion agreement.

## Student Housing and Community

_(Referenced in [[06_Academic_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**The student housing block:** Three connected buildings on the campus's eastern edge — purpose-built residential accommodation for approximately 600 students, with common rooms on each floor and a large shared kitchen-and-common-space at each building's base. Managed by the Luminarium's residential administration, nominally. Practically managed by the students themselves, because residential administration is understaffed and the students figured this out three years ago.

**The Student Assembly:** Approximately forty students functioning as the Quarter's informal political voice. Three active campaigns:

_Research Ethics:_ The Assembly has been formally requesting that the Luminarium's Rector brief students on the classified programs' findings, arguing that students whose research is being affected by classification decisions they don't understand deserve to know the context. The Rector has met with the Assembly twice. Both meetings produced acknowledgment without information. The Assembly's current position is that the third meeting will be a public event rather than a private one. The Rector is aware of this.

_Clank Rights:_ The largest student political organization outside the Halfling Quarter, and the one with the most active street presence — they attend the Ironwright Collective's Speakers' Corner sessions, they have brought the issue to three Ember Lecture Hall public forums, and they have a specific and ongoing relationship with Pip Underwell that has produced joint-authored statements distributed at both the Quarter and the Manufactories. This organization is watched by the Council's intelligence operation, which several of its members know and none of them are deterred by.

_Power Rationing Effects:_ The most immediate grievance — the brownouts that hit the student housing block have disrupted research work and living conditions in ways that are daily rather than episodic. The Assembly has documented these effects in a formal report submitted to the Rector and the Council. The Council's response was a form letter. The Assembly's response was to submit the report again, with the form letter attached, to the Lamplight Ward's Evening Broadsheet.

**The student to know:** **Calla Voss** (see NPCs — no relation to Mira Voss of the Guild; the name overlap is unfortunate and occasionally confusing). The Assembly's most effective member.

## The Scholar's Rest

_(Referenced in [[06_Academic_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** A building on the Quarter's northern edge, at the border with Lamplight Ward. Close enough to the Ward to access its amenities; close enough to the Luminarium to walk to early lectures.

**What it is:** The Quarter's main inn — twelve rooms, faculty-visitor pricing (1.5 gold marks per night), a dining room that serves until midnight and is one of the better late-night food options in the district. The Scholar's Rest caters specifically to visiting academics, research partners from other institutions, and the family members of Luminarium staff who need a place to stay during visits. It is quiet in the way that institutions serving academic visitors are quiet: the guests are busy, they keep to themselves, they eat at odd hours, and the conversations in the dining room are genuinely interesting.

**The dining room:** Eight tables, a short menu that changes daily based on Halfling Quarter delivery, good wine at academic-visitor pricing (reasonable — the proprietor has concluded that visiting academics should be able to afford a decent bottle). Open to non-guests for dinner.

**The dining room's actual value at the table:** It is one of the few places in the Quarter where senior Luminarium researchers eat with visiting researchers from other institutions in an informal setting. The cross-institutional conversations here contain things that neither party would say in a formal meeting — the visiting researcher's perspective on how Lumencrest's situation looks from outside, the Luminarium researcher's unguarded response to that perspective. A player at the adjacent table eating quietly is effectively in the room for one of the most honest assessments of the Heart situation available anywhere in the city.

**The proprietor:** **Carris Mourne** — see NPCs. No relation to Senna Mourne at the Long Table; this has come up.

## Notable NPCs — The Academic Quarter

### Aldis Marke

**Role:** Rector of the Luminarium; the person formally responsible for the research programs; the most intellectually significant figure in the city **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 68 **Location:** The Luminarium, specifically his office and anywhere else in the building he needs to be. The Great Courtyard bench at midday. The Ember Lecture Hall when the lecture interests him.

**Appearance:** The specific appearance of a 68-year-old who has been thinking very hard for a very long time — rumpled in a way that is genuine rather than affected, with the beard and the quality of attention that the base file describes. His clothing is functional and has been the same style for thirty years. He has the specific physical quality of someone who forgets that he is in a body until the body reminds him, which it does regularly and which he finds mildly inconvenient.

**Personality:** A terrifying intellect in a package that does not announce itself. He will engage with an idea from any source if the idea is interesting, which means he is simultaneously one of the most accessible senior figures in the city (his courtyard bench is public) and one of the most demanding contacts to be useful to — not demanding in a difficult sense, but in the sense that he has no patience for anything that isn't actually good. Players who approach him with something real will find him more engaged than his position would suggest. Players who approach him with a performance of competence will find the performance assessed accurately and quickly.

**What he knows:** The most complete picture of the Heart's actual situation available to any non-Vault Guard civilian. The classified programs' findings — including the two that matter, one of which he has been sitting with for three months while deciding what to do. The political reality of the city's situation at the level of what is actually true versus what is officially maintained. He knows things that the players will eventually need to know, and he knows that he knows them, and he has not decided what the correct thing to do with them is.

**What he has not decided:** Whether the finding from the classified program that would change the city's political situation immediately constitutes information that should become public, and if so through what channel, and how the city reacts to it, and whether that reaction is survivable. He is not a villain for holding this decision. He is a person who understands consequences and is taking them seriously. Players who demonstrate that they can be trusted with consequences — not just that they want the information, but that they understand what having it means — will find his threshold moveable.

**The courtyard bench:** He is there from approximately noon to one o'clock on days when the work allows. He has a book. He will look up from it if approached. He will engage with the first genuinely interesting thing said to him. He will return to the book if the conversation stops being interesting. Players have roughly a paragraph to establish that they're worth talking to.

### Petra Vane

**Role:** Mid-level research fellow; operator of one of the six classified Heart-failure programs; the researcher who has found something she doesn't understand and is afraid to report **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 34 **Location:** Research lab, third floor, Luminarium northern wing. The Four Constants late at night. Occasionally the Ember Lecture Hall's back row for lectures she doesn't want to be seen attending.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has been too close to a significant problem for too long — not quite rested, with the quality of attention that people develop when they're very tired and very alert simultaneously. Her work clothing is functional and slightly chemical-stained. She has the habit of holding a pen she's not using when she's talking, which is something she does when thinking and doesn't notice.

**Personality:** Precise and private in a way that reads as reserved until she decides to trust someone, at which point the precision becomes directness and the reserve becomes something closer to urgency. She is not dramatic about her situation — she is a researcher, she found a result, the result is significant and she doesn't fully understand it and she knows she should report it and she hasn't yet and the time between now and when she will have to either report it or suppress it is getting shorter. She approaches this practically rather than emotionally. She is, however, scared. This is visible to careful observers.

**What she found:** In the classified program's analysis of the Heart's energy output patterns, she identified a secondary periodicity — a cycle-within-the-decline that the current models don't account for. The secondary periodicity's period is approximately 14 months. The Heart's output decline curve, if this periodicity is correctly factored in, produces a significantly different projection than the current official estimate. Specifically: the official estimate gives the city 3-5 years. The corrected projection gives approximately 14 months to a threshold failure. She has run the analysis seven times. The result is consistent.

**Why she hasn't reported:** She is not certain the periodicity is real and not an artifact of her measurement methodology. She has been trying to either confirm or eliminate the methodological artifact for three months. She cannot do it alone with her current equipment. She is beginning to suspect she needs someone with a different technical background than hers — specifically someone with expertise in arcane periodicity patterns, which is not her field and which the Luminarium has one specialist in who is on the third classified program and who she cannot consult without triggering a reporting requirement she's not ready to trigger.

**Working with her:** She will not discuss her research with strangers. She will discuss it with people who have demonstrated technical competence and, specifically, who are not institutionally connected in ways that create mandatory reporting chains. Players who are not Luminarium staff, not Council agents, and not Vault Guard — and who can demonstrate they understand what they're being told — are exactly the category of person she might be able to use. The conversation that gets to her research will require either an introduction through someone she trusts (Maren Solith at the Four Constants has known her for three years) or demonstrating specific technical knowledge that establishes credentials without institutional connection. Either path takes time. This is appropriate. What she's sitting on is significant.

### Oswin Fletch

**Role:** Chief restricted-section librarian, the Archives **Ancestry/Age:** Elf, 203 **Location:** The Archives, always. The restricted sections most of the day. The public reading rooms when he is assessing a researcher's genuine need.

**Appearance:** 203 years of careful work in a specific space have produced a person who is essentially part of the building — unhurried, precise, with the specific quality of stillness that comes from someone who has never needed to rush because they have always had time. His clothing is tidy and unchanged from decade to decade in style. He moves through the stacks with the absolute certainty of someone who has memorized the exact location of every item in a collection that has been growing for 130 years.

**Personality:** Formidable, fair, and entirely serious about the collection he protects. His apparent coldness toward people trying to access the restricted sections without authorization is not personal — it is the product of 130 years of watching what happens to knowledge that isn't properly protected, which includes knowing what happened to two sections of the original Archives that no longer exist because their protection was inadequate. He takes unauthorized access attempts as an insult to the people who created the knowledge, not to himself.

**What makes him moveable:** He has granted unofficial access to five people in 130 years. The common factor was not charm, social standing, or persistence — it was demonstrated genuine need combined with demonstrated trustworthiness established over time. He assessed each person by the quality of their questions in the public reading rooms, the care with which they handled public materials, and eventually direct conversation that he initiated when he decided a researcher's need was real. The process cannot be accelerated. Players who want restricted access and are in the Quarter for any length of time should be in the public reading rooms, asking real questions, treating the materials with appropriate care, and eventually drawing his assessment.

**What he knows:** The complete Archive collection, including the restricted sections, at a level of detail that is not catalogued anywhere. He knows what the gap in the Hollostar research publication record looks like from inside — he was here when the publications stopped and he knows why. He also knows the maintenance passage beneath the Archives that is documented in the original construction records, because he read those records 120 years ago. He has not used the passage himself. He is aware that someone who had read the construction records could find it.

### Professor Neva Strand

**Role:** Theoretical physics faculty, Luminarium; the researcher planning to publicly present the classified modeling result in four days **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 51 **Location:** Faculty wing, Luminarium. The Ember Lecture Hall (upcoming lecture). The Scholar's Rest dining room most evenings.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has made a decision they are at peace with — which contrasts with most of the Quarter's current population. She is calm in a way that is specific rather than general: not unaware of her situation, fully aware of it and satisfied with her choice. Her clothing is better maintained than most researchers' and she is one of the Quarter's few faculty members who reliably sleeps enough.

**Personality:** Principled in the specific way of someone who has thought carefully about what she believes and why, and who has concluded that the public deserves to know what the modeling shows because they are the people who will be affected by it. She is not naive about the consequences — she has assessed them and decided they are worth bearing. She is also genuinely warm and a very good teacher, which is why her public lectures consistently draw full rooms.

**The upcoming lecture:** In four days, at the Ember Lecture Hall's regular public series, Professor Strand will present a theoretical Hollostar energy modeling result that has been sitting in the classified program for four months. The result is the corrected projection that is consistent with Petra Vane's finding — she reached it from a different angle, through theoretical modeling rather than observational data, and the convergence of two independent approaches should significantly strengthen both findings' credibility. She does not know about Petra's finding.

**What happens at the lecture:** The Research Board will not have been informed. The Vault Guard observers in the audience will be there for routine monitoring. What they will see is a senior Luminarium faculty member presenting, in a public forum to 300 people, a model suggesting the Heart's failure timeline is significantly shorter than the official estimate. The consequences will unfold from that point in ways the GM determines. Players who know about the lecture in advance — Neva is visible in the courtyard and available for conversation — can be in the room when it happens.

**Working with her:** She is accessible and willing to discuss her work. She considers public scientific communication part of her professional responsibility and engages with it accordingly. Players with genuine technical interest will find her forthcoming. Players who have information that bears on her modeling — specifically Petra Vane's observational data, if they have access to it — will find the conversation shifts significantly when that information is shared.

### Calla Voss (Student)

**Role:** Student Assembly's most effective organizer; not related to Mira Voss **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 22 **Location:** Student housing, Luminarium eastern block. The Great Courtyard most days. Wherever the Assembly is currently meeting.

**Appearance:** The specific appearance of someone young who is doing something that matters and knows it — energized, precise in her communication, with the quality of someone who has thought carefully about every element of her approach. Practical clothing. Always carrying a satchel that is always full of something.

**Personality:** Sharper than most people expect from someone 22 years old, which she is aware of and does not perform or suppress. She has been organizing the Student Assembly for two years and has built it from a social club into a political organization with three active campaigns, a relationship with the Ironwright Collective, and a distribution network for its documents that reaches four districts. She is not interested in the performance of activism — she is interested in outcomes, and she calibrates everything she does against whether it produces them.

**What she wants:** The three Assembly campaigns to produce concrete results — specifically: a public briefing from the Rector on the classified programs, specific Labour Board policy changes on Clank assignment conditions, and power rationing relief for the student housing block. She has prioritized these in order and is working the first one because she believes the others depend on it.

**What she knows:** The Quarter's political landscape at the student level, which is more current than most — the Assembly's network includes people working in most of the Luminarium's departments, and their conversations produce an aggregate picture of what's happening in the institution that is more complete than any individual's. She also knows about Petra Vane's situation in approximate terms — she doesn't know the specific finding, but she knows that a mid-level researcher in the classified programs has found something that hasn't been reported, because the Assembly has been watching the classified programs as closely as it can.

**Working with her:** Direct and efficient, if players are genuinely working toward outcomes she believes in. She will tell them what the Assembly knows and what it needs. She will connect them with the Assembly's network in other districts if the purpose is clear. She is wary of being used as an intelligence source without reciprocal value — she has encountered this before and doesn't repeat the experience.

### Tern Ashe

**Role:** Independent researcher, Shard Workshop, Innovation Row; former Luminarium classified program fellow **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 39 **Location:** Shard Workshop, Innovation Row. The Four Constants at irregular hours.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who left an institutional situation under complicated circumstances and has been defining themselves differently since — less tidy than the Luminarium's research fellows, more deliberate about the difference. The workshop smell has settled into their clothing in the way that specific work does. They have the specific quality of someone who knows more than they're saying, which is different from someone with something to hide — it is someone who has assessed what information is safe to share and is careful about the assessment.

**Personality:** Careful and precise in a way that is post-institutional — they came through the Luminarium's classified program and left it, and the caution they carry is calibrated to that experience. They are not unfriendly; they are deliberate. They will engage genuinely with people they assess as trustworthy, and their assessment criteria are specific: they want to understand why someone wants to know what they seem to want to know before they say anything that matters.

**What they're working on:** The arcane energy properties of Hollostar shard material, specifically the activation mechanism that makes ground shard material — Hollostar Dust — perceptually significant rather than inert. They left the classified program eighteen months ago because of a disagreement about how the research was being used. The disagreement was with Rector Marke about publication; the research was theoretically relevant to understanding Hollostar Dust's perceptual effects, and Marke classified it on the basis that its implications for Network security were significant. Tern has been pursuing the same line of research independently since, without the institutional resources and with equipment that is less reliable.

**What they know:** The arcane activation mechanism for Hollostar Dust at a technical level no one else outside the classified program has. The specific interaction between the shard material's crystalline structure and the nervous system's arcane receptors — the mechanism that produces both the recreational perceptual effects and the Transformed abilities that long-term use generates. And, inferentially from their research: something about the Shard Division's experimental approach that they have not stated publicly, arrived at from the technical direction. They do not know about the Shard Division by name. They know that the shard material consumption pattern in Lumencrest's supply chain does not match recreational use alone.

**Working with them:** The Shard Workshop doesn't advertise and Tern doesn't receive walk-ins. Getting to them requires either Innovation Row social credit (the Four Constants, over time) or a specific introduction from Maren Solith, who knows them well enough to vouch. With access, they are a significant resource — their technical knowledge connects Hollostar Dust, the Transformed abilities, and the Shard Division's experimental program in ways that could change what players understand about the campaign's core conflict.

### Maren Solith

**Role:** Proprietor of the Four Constants; the Quarter's social center of gravity **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 48 **Location:** The Four Constants, Innovation Row. She lives in the building above it.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who runs a demanding operation with genuine pleasure in it — present, warm, with the quality of attention that good café proprietors develop when they care about their customers in a non-performative way. Her hair is usually in a practical arrangement that has partially escaped. She is almost always holding a coffee cup. It is almost always hers.

**Personality:** Warm and intellectually curious — she likes the work because she likes the people who do the work, and she has been serving the Quarter's research community for fifteen years in a space that functions as their third place in the sense that a third place is a place that is not home and not work and therefore more honest than either. She knows everyone. She knows things about everyone. She does not gossip. She introduces people who should meet each other, which is different.

**What she knows:** The Four Constants' ambient intelligence — fifteen years of the Quarter's research community talking in her café, which is more information than anyone has managed to compile deliberately. She knows who is working on what, who is struggling, who has found something they're not talking about, which collaborations are productive and which aren't. She will not share what she knows directly. She will, for people she has decided are trustworthy and whose purposes she endorses, make specific introductions that turn out to be exactly what was needed. The introductions to Petra Vane and Tern Ashe both run through her.

**What she wants:** The café to keep running and the Quarter to keep being what it is — a place where serious thought happens in a community that takes it seriously. She is, behind the warmth, concerned about what the classified programs' pressure and the research community's current state means for the Quarter's longer-term intellectual health. She has seen brilliance eroded by sustained crisis before and she is watching the early signs of it now.

## Faction Presence — Academic Quarter

**The Luminarium's Research Board** — the institution's governing body for research decisions, appointed by the Council but operating with significant practical independence. Currently under pressure from both the classified programs' findings and the Student Assembly's ethics campaign. Three members are Illuminist-aligned, two are Conservator-aligned, one is genuinely independent and is the swing vote on every significant decision.

**The Student Assembly** — treated as a faction at this scale; their three campaigns have real political weight in the Quarter and growing connections to the Ironwright Collective and the Halfling Quarter community.

**The Vault Guard** — present through quarterly inspections and soft monitoring of public lectures. Their authorized access to the third-floor classified labs creates a specific tension with the research community that both parties navigate carefully. The Guard's observers at public events are detectable with a DC 12 Instinct roll — they are city watch trained and the Quarter's researchers are more observant than city watch accounts for.

**The Unseen Guild (Shard Division connection)** — present through the corrupted Starfall Seekers, who have physical access to the Quarter's research community through Aldis Crane's academic relationships and who have been providing shard samples from the Luminarium-adjacent supply chains. This connection is not known to most of the Quarter and is the most significant hidden threat in the district.

**The Free Hand (indirect)** — present through the Student Assembly's Clank rights campaign and Pip Underwell's connections. Not operationally active in the Quarter but the Assembly has been distributing Free Hand-aligned materials.
