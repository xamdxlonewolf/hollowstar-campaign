# Lumencrest — Districts Overview

*This file is the map reference. Use it to orient players spatially and to describe the city from above or from elevated vantage points like cable cars or rooftops. Each district has a position, character summary, and map notes for Wonderdraft or AI generation.*

---

## City Layout — The Big Picture

Lumencrest is built on gently rising ground around the impact basin where the Heart of Lumencrest fell 3,000 years ago. The city spreads outward from the Radiant Core in a roughly concentric pattern — not perfect rings, because the terrain, the river, and 1,200 years of organic growth have pushed and pulled the shape — but the general logic is: wealth and power at the center, decreasing toward the edges, with Shadowside breaking the pattern entirely to the north.

The **Silverrun River** runs along the eastern edge of the city, feeding the water infrastructure and carrying goods in by barge. The **Ironspine Road** enters from the west (coming from Ironhold) and the **Kingroad** enters from the south (coming from Kingsrest). The eastern road follows the river toward Starfall Basin.

The **Lumen Network** — the underground infrastructure carrying electrical power — runs in a web from the Vault District outward. The closer to the center, the more power infrastructure exists, the more reliable the power, and the wealthier the neighborhood.

## The Districts in Brief

### Radiant Core
The seat of power. Built around the point where the Heart sits deepest underground. Council chambers, aristocratic estates, the Luminarium's administrative buildings, the Accord Chamber. The streets here are wider, cleaner, and lined with the city's most impressive electric streetlamps. Guards are permanent and visible. You need a reason to be here or you'll be asked for one politely, then less politely.

**Landmark for map:** The Accord Chamber — a domed building with four tall towers at the cardinal points, lit from within so the dome glows at night. Visible from much of the city.

### Lamplight Ward
The commercial heart of Lumencrest for everyone who isn't rich or poor. Three-and-four story stone buildings, shops at ground level, residences above. The main commercial streets are permanently busy. Street lamps here have the distinctive warm amber hue of slightly older Lumen Network infrastructure — beautiful, but one of the first things to dim when power dips.

**Landmark for map:** Merchant Square — an open plaza with a central fountain (electrically pumped, always running) surrounded by the city's highest-traffic shops and the main Guildhall complex.

### Halfling Quarter
Southeast of center, recognizable from a distance by the rooftop gardens, colorful window boxes, and lower building heights (Halfling-scaled doorways mean ground floors built for smaller frames, with human-height upper floors added later). The smells here are impossible to mistake — brewing, baking, roasting. The Common Green is the Quarter's social heart.

**Landmark for map:** The Common Green — an open park with a permanent market pavilion along one edge and the twin brewery towers of Copperkettle & Brum visible behind it.

### The Manufactories
West of center, identifiable by chimney stacks, factory buildings (large, rectangular, industrial), and the constant sound of machinery. Even at night the factories run — you can hear them and see the glow of furnace light in the high windows. The workers' housing fills the gaps between factory blocks: dense, practical, functional.

**Landmark for map:** The Great Press — the city's largest single factory, three stories and a full city block, producing powered printing and manufacturing equipment. Its chimney stack is the tallest structure in this district.

### Shadowside
The city's unlit district. North of center, denser than anything else, with streets that narrow into passages and buildings that lean toward each other. No street lamps. No cable car service. Residents use candles, oil lamps, and small coal fires. The streets feel different here — slower, more aware. Everyone is watching. The Night Market operates on the main thoroughfare after dark, openly, because city watch patrols here minimally.

**Landmark for map:** Pauper's Gate — the northern city gate, older and less maintained than the main gates, flanked by the densest part of Shadowside. Also mark the Maze Quarter — the tightest section of Shadowside, near impossible to navigate without local knowledge.

### Academic Quarter
East of center, adjacent to the Radiant Core. The Luminarium campus dominates — several connected buildings around a central courtyard, with the Archives building (recognizable by its wide low dome) beside it. Student housing fills the streets around campus. Innovation Row is a block of storefronts and workshops where researchers run private experiments and test ventures.

**Landmark for map:** The Luminarium — main building with the Archives dome visible to its south. A large electric clock tower on the main campus quad that has run accurately for 400 years (locals say it's never been wrong; this is almost true).

### Vault District
A restricted zone you can navigate around the outside of but not enter without authorization. High walls, additional fencing, guard stations at every approach. The Vault Gate itself — the entrance to the underground complex where the Heart is — is a massive reinforced door set into what looks like a low stone building but is actually just the entrance structure. Vault Guards patrol in pairs, visibly armed.

**Landmark for map:** The Vault Gate building — mark it as restricted. The wall surrounding the Vault District is lower than the city wall but more heavily monitored.

## Street-Level Descriptions — The Main Thoroughfares

**The Lumenway** — the city's central road, running north-south through the Radiant Core and Lamplight Ward. Twice as wide as normal streets, paved with fitted stone, street lamps on both sides every fifteen feet. This is the road visitors are steered toward and where formal processions happen.

**The Wire Road** — follows the first cable car line, east-west through the city. Named for the cable overhead. Merchants have clustered along it because of foot traffic from car stops.

**Factory Row** — the main east-west road through the Manufactories. Industrial traffic — carts, Clank workers moving in groups, deliveries at all hours.

**The Hollow Road** — the main road into Shadowside from Lamplight Ward. Feels like crossing a border. The street lamps end at an identifiable line and the road gets narrower on the other side.

**Brewers' Lane** — the main thoroughfare of the Halfling Quarter, running through the brewing and baking district. The smell is the landmark.

For any map of Lumencrest, the following should be labelled:

**Major Structures:**
- The Accord Chamber (Radiant Core)
- The Vault Gate (Vault District)
- The Luminarium Campus (Academic Quarter)
- The Archives (Academic Quarter)
- The Great Press Factory (Manufactories)
- Common Green & Brewery Towers (Halfling Quarter)
- Merchant Square & Guildhalls (Lamplight Ward)

**Infrastructure:**
- 4 cable car lines with station markers
- Silverrun River (eastern edge)
- Main city gates (south, west, north — Pauper's Gate)
- The Ironspine Road entrance (west gate)
- The Kingroad entrance (south gate)

**Districts:**
- All 7 districts clearly labelled with rough boundaries
- Note Shadowside boundary (where lamps end) as a visual feature
