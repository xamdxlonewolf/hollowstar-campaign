# The Academic Quarter

**Population:** ~5,000 (faculty, researchers, students, support staff)  
**Power Status:** Full and recently intensified. The Academic Quarter runs at full brightness even at midnight. Something is being worked on, urgently, by people who can't afford to stop.  
**Cable Car:** 1 station (Luminarium Stop)  
**Access:** Open streets and campus public areas. Research facilities, restricted labs, and the Archives' restricted sections require authorization.

---

## Overview

The Academic Quarter is the smallest major district and the most intellectually dense. Every building does something: research, teaching, experimentation, storage of knowledge, production of new knowledge. The Luminarium campus anchors the district, and the streets around it are occupied by student housing, private research workshops, specialist suppliers, and the kind of cafes and small restaurants that exist specifically to feed people who work too long to leave the building for a real meal.

The Quarter's current atmosphere is tense in a particular way — the productive tension of people who know they're racing against something and are not yet sure they can win. The Luminarium's seventeen concurrent research programs into the Heart's failure represent the most concentrated scientific effort the city has ever mounted. They're running experiments at midnight. They're running experiments in labs that should be closed for maintenance. They're running experiments that haven't been fully documented because documenting them would require stopping to write and stopping feels like losing time they don't have.

Some of these programs have produced things their researchers don't understand. At least one has produced something they're afraid to report. The Quarter knows this, in the way that a small community where everyone knows what time their neighbours' lights come on always knows more than it formally acknowledges.

## Key Locations

**The Luminarium** — the premier academic institution of the known world. Multiple colleges arranged around a central courtyard, with the Archives dome to the south, student housing to the east, and the Innovation Row workshops along the western edge. The Luminarium's architecture is deliberately impressive — it was built to announce that knowledge is power, and that Lumencrest has both.

The Luminarium's current Rector, **Aldis Marke** (human, 68, beard, rumpled, terrifying intellect), is the person formally responsible for the research programs. He is aware that several programs have exceeded their authorization. He is not stopping them because he believes the city needs a solution more than it needs compliance. He is also aware that this is an ethical position with a time limit — if something goes wrong, the authorization question becomes very important very fast.

**The Archives** — Lumencrest's primary knowledge repository. Vast, meticulously organized, containing copies of significant texts from across the continent going back 800 years. The public reading rooms are available to any city resident with a library card (free to obtain from the registration desk). The restricted sections contain technical documentation on the Lumen Network, the Heart's historical output readings, and the classified research records from the current programs. Accessing the restricted sections requires either authorization from the Council's Research Board or a method of access that the restricted sections' security was not designed to anticipate.

**Innovation Row** — the commercial and experimental strip along the Quarter's western edge. Workshops, prototype labs, small ventures, and specialist suppliers. A Artificer working on a private project, a scholar testing a hypothesis, a former student who graduated and couldn't bring herself to leave — this is where they operate. Innovation Row is also where the Quarter's informal information market lives: everyone here talks to everyone else, and the aggregate of those conversations is an extraordinary real-time picture of what's happening in Lumencrest's research world.

**The Ember Lecture Hall** — the largest teaching space in the Quarter, used for major public lectures and significant events. The Luminarium holds a free public lecture series here twice a week — on anything from theoretical physics to herbalism to the history of the Accord. These lectures are extremely well-attended. They are also a useful place to find academics willing to discuss what they know more frankly than they would in their offices.

## People Worth Knowing

**Petra Vane** (she/her, human, 34) — a mid-level research fellow running one of the Luminarium's six classified Heart-failure programs. She is the researcher who has produced something she doesn't understand and is afraid to report. She knows she should report it. She hasn't yet because she wants to understand it first, and she is beginning to suspect she can't understand it alone. She will respond well to someone who demonstrates competence without institutional attachment.

**Oswin Fletch** (he/him, elf, 203) — the Archives' chief restricted-section librarian. Has worked here for 130 years. Knows the collection better than anyone. Has a particular and very well-founded loathing of people who try to access restricted materials without authorization, developed over a century of exactly this. Is not unmoveable by genuine need and demonstrated trustworthiness. The process of demonstrating these things to him takes time and cannot be faked.

**The Student Assembly** — not a single person but an organized body of approximately forty students who function as the Quarter's informal political voice. Currently agitating around three issues: research ethics (the classified programs), Clank rights (the largest student organization outside the Halfling Quarter), and power rationing's effect on student facilities. They are loud, smart, organized, and being watched by the Council's intelligence operation with more attention than they realize.

## Where to Sleep and Eat

**The Scholar's Rest** — the Quarter's main inn, twelve rooms, priced for faculty visitors (1.5gm per night). The dining room serves until midnight. Good wine, acceptable food, excellent conversation.

**Student housing common rooms** — technically not available to non-students. In practice, a current student with an empty bed in their room and a need for income is not hard to find. 4 silver a night. You will be asked to not advertise this.

**The Four Constants** — the Quarter's best cafe, open from 5am to 2am because researchers work at those hours. Excellent coffee (the best in Lumencrest), adequate food, and the ambient noise of people solving interesting problems. The corner table near the east window has hosted more significant intellectual breakthroughs than anyone has bothered to count.
