# Lumencrest — Factions & Reputation

*All factions players can interact with, build reputation with, and eventually work for or against. Each faction has a reputation track, what reputation unlocks, and how to gain or lose standing.*

---

## How Reputation Works in Lumencrest

Players can have a reputation score with each faction from **-3 (Enemy)** to **+3 (Trusted)**. Starting reputation is 0 (Unknown) with all factions unless a player has a backstory connection.

Reputation changes through actions, not just conversation:
- Completing a job for the faction: +1
- Completing a job that significantly advances the faction's goals: +2
- Betraying the faction or actively working against them: -2 to -3
- Being publicly associated with a rival faction's major action: -1

Reputation with one faction can affect reputation with others — some factions are directly opposed and gaining trust with one may cost you with another. These relationships are noted below.

## The Council Factions

### The Illuminists
*"The solution exists. We haven't found it yet. We need more time."*

**What they are:** The Council's research-and-technology faction, currently holding the Chair through Councilor Mira Ashton. They believe the Heart's failure can be reversed or stabilized through intensive scientific effort. They are running seventeen concurrent research programs and are running out of time to show results.

**What they want from players:** Results the official programs haven't produced. Intelligence from other regions about alternative power solutions. Assistance with research that can't be documented through official channels. Protection for researchers who are being pressured by other factions.

**What they offer:** Access to the Luminarium's non-restricted resources. Letters of introduction to academic institutions across the continent. Significant payment in gold from discretionary research funds. Eventually, access to parts of the Academic Quarter and the Vault's administrative areas that are not normally open.

**Reputation unlock table:**
- +1 (Known): Access to the Luminarium's public resources, introductions to research faculty
- +2 (Useful): Payment above standard, non-restricted archive access, meeting Rector Aldis Marke
- +3 (Trusted): Access to classified research briefings, introduction to Mira Ashton directly, potential access to restricted research areas

**Conflicts:** Gaining +2 with Expansionists costs -1 with Illuminists. Being present at a Council vote that goes against Illuminist interests: -1.

**First contact:** Rector Aldis Marke (Academic Quarter), junior faculty at the Luminarium who put out cautious feelers, or Mira Ashton directly if players come recommended.

---

### The Conservators
*"We maintain what works until something better is proven to work."*

**What they are:** The Council's establishment faction — the governance professionals, the city administrators, the people who actually run Lumencrest's day-to-day operations. They are not ideological so much as institutional; they believe in the city's systems because they built careers within them and those systems have worked for centuries.

**What they want from players:** Intelligence on what the Illuminists and Expansionists are doing outside official channels. Help managing situations that could become public crises. Quiet resolution of problems that would be embarrassing if they became loud.

**What they offer:** Access to the city's administrative apparatus — expedited permits, record access, letters of authority that open doors. Good steady payment for reliable work. Protection from minor legal complications within the city.

**Reputation unlock table:**
- +1 (Known): Expedited Charter Office processing, introductions to Council District staff
- +2 (Useful): Access to mid-level administrative records, the city watch looking the other way on minor incidents
- +3 (Trusted): Meeting senior Conservator Councilors, access to the Hall of Records' less-public sections, direct city watch cooperation

**Conflicts:** Working openly with the Free Hand or the Student Assembly costs -1. Direct action against city institutions: -2.

---

### The Expansionists
*"The solution is out there. We go get it."*

**What they are:** The Council's aggressive-action faction, arguing that Lumencrest must seize the Endless Eye's star by military force before the Heart fails entirely. Led by Theron Ashwick, charming and patient and building toward a vote he believes he's positioned to win.

**What they want from players:** Intelligence from the Starfall Basin about the Endless Eye's defenses. Discrete muscle for situations they can't use official channels for. People who can travel to regions where sending official Lumencrest representatives would be noticed.

**What they offer:** Excellent payment — the Expansionists have private funding from manufacturing guild interests who see military action as an economic investment. Eventually, promises of position and access in a post-crisis Lumencrest where their faction has won.

**Reputation unlock table:**
- +1 (Known): Introduction to Theron Ashwick's circle, access to Expansionist social events in the Estates
- +2 (Useful): Significant payment contracts, introduction to the manufacturing guild interests backing the faction
- +3 (Trusted): Meeting Ashwick directly, access to the military planning that's notionally unofficial

**Conflicts:** Open association with the Illuminists' research success: -1. Working against military preparation: -2.

## Independent City Factions

### The Merchant Guild
*"The city works because the trade works. Don't break the trade."*

**What they are:** The organized commercial interests of Lumencrest — the major trading houses, the large merchants, the guild administrators. They supported the Illuminists' promised solution. They're beginning to hedge their bets.

**What they want:** Stability. Information about what's actually happening with the Heart, since they're not getting reliable information from official sources. Help protecting their supply chains and commercial interests as the crisis deepens.

**What they offer:** Commercial connections across the Accord and beyond. Access to legitimate trade networks. Payment for discreet courier and protection work. Introductions to merchant contacts in every major city on the continent.

**First contact:** The Merchant Guild Hall on Merchant Square, the Long Table (informal contact point), any licensed commercial exchange.

---

### The Ironwright Collective
*"We built this city. We're done watching it be managed by people who don't work in it."*

**What they are:** The Manufactories workers' organization — technically a social club, functionally a labor movement. Led by Genna Cradle, who has been organizing workers for fifteen years and is one of the most effective political speakers in the city. Currently preparing for what may become a general strike if the power rationing situation isn't addressed.

**What they want:** Someone who can carry their grievances to the Council in a way that gets heard. Intelligence on what the Council is actually planning. Possibly help organizing something that goes beyond what the Ironwright Collective can officially do.

**What they offer:** The loyalty of 8,000 Manufactories workers — which means information about everything that moves through the industrial district, access to the district's social spaces, and people who will help quietly if the cause is right. Also a genuinely decent amount of pooled coin for the right job.

**Reputation conflicts:** Openly working against the Council's labor policies gains +1. Being seen as a Council agent: -2. The Ironwright Collective has no patience for people who talk their language and work for the other side.

---

### The Eastwick Cooperative
*"We look after our own. Always have."*

**What they are:** A Lamplight Ward mutual aid organization that has quietly become the most organized civilian response to the crisis. They're stocking food, creating community networks, and building the kind of resilience that makes a neighbourhood functional when institutions aren't.

**What they want:** Help they can trust. Brinne Halloway (the Cooperative's chair) is a careful woman who has seen promises from institutions and has stopped believing them. She is not closed to outside help — she just needs to be convinced it's real.

**What they offer:** Community intelligence — the Cooperative knows what's actually happening in Lamplight Ward in the way that organized neighbors always know. A safe meeting place. The particular protection of a neighbourhood that looks after its people.

---

### The Halfling Quarter Community
*No slogan. They're too practical for slogans.*

**What they are:** The Halfling Quarter functions as a semi-organized community interest body — not formally chartered, but the Quarter's elders (who gather at Speakers' Corner most evenings) are the effective decision-making body for the community's collective response to events.

**What they want:** The Quarter to be left alone, primarily. They have watched the crisis with clear eyes and have prepared quietly. They are not opposed to helping people who prove trustworthy, but their loyalty is to the Quarter, not to abstract causes.

**What they offer:** Access to the Quarter's social spaces and networks. Excellent food and accommodation at fair prices, which sounds small but in a city this large and this tense is a genuine resource. Community intelligence. And, for people who have truly earned it, the informal backing of 15,000 people who will take you in if things go wrong elsewhere.

---

### The Free Hand (Clank Liberation Network)
*"What is created is not owned. What thinks is not property."*

**What they are:** The underground network helping Clanks escape their service terms, facilitating implant removal, and advocating for legal reform. Cell structure, no fixed leadership, has operated for twenty years without being meaningfully disrupted.

**What they want:** People who can move through parts of the city the network can't easily access, carry messages and items without being traced to the network, and potentially help specific Clanks in difficult situations.

**What they offer:** The most extensive underground access in Lumencrest — the Free Hand has connections in every district including the literal tunnels beneath the city. Information that doesn't move through official channels. The loyalty of the Clank community both inside and outside the city.

**Warning:** Being identified as working with the Free Hand puts you at odds with the Labour Board, the Vault Guard's monitoring operation, and potentially the Council. It also puts you in conflict with anyone whose business depends on Clank labor remaining inexpensive. These are not small conflicts.

---

### The Sisterhood of the Silver Thread
*"We know everyone. Everyone talks eventually."*

**What they are:** The organized guild of sex workers, operating openly in the licensed entertainment districts and less openly elsewhere. Legitimately registered with the city. More politically sophisticated than most institutions give them credit for.

**What they want:** Recognition, which they have (legal status). Protection, which varies by situation. Occasionally, help with a specific problem — a client who has become dangerous, a debt that needs collection, information that needs verification from a source they can't access directly.

**What they offer:** Information. The Silver Thread is the most reliable intelligence network in Lumencrest for everything that happens in private conversations, because private conversations happen in the presence of Silver Thread members routinely. Calla (the Guild head) will trade information for useful information, help with a genuine need, or appropriate payment. She will not trade information that could get a member hurt. This line is non-negotiable.

**First contact:** Any licensed Silver Thread establishment (several in Lamplight Ward's Entertainment Quarter). Initial interactions are social; trust is established over time.

---

### The Student Assembly (Luminarium)
*"The institution is not the knowledge. The knowledge is the knowledge."*

**What they are:** The Academic Quarter's organized student political body, currently running three active campaigns: research ethics, Clank rights, and power access inequality. Forty organized members, significant informal support among the student body. Monitored by Council intelligence, aware of this, continuing anyway.

**What they want:** Adults with actual capability to help advance things that students can talk about but not do. Access to resources they can't reach. Someone to take their documented concerns about the classified research programs seriously.

**What they offer:** The collective knowledge of the Luminarium's student body — which is substantial and underrated. Network into the faculty's more sympathetic members. Access to the academic community's informal information sharing.

---

### The Starfall Seekers (Lumencrest Office)
*"The stars fell for a reason. We intend to find out what it was."*

**What they are:** The continent-wide organization studying Hollowstars has a research station in the Academic Quarter's Scholar's Quarter section. The Lumencrest office is particularly focused on the Heart — its declining output, its nature, and what its failure means for understanding Hollowstars generally.

**What they want:** Data the official programs aren't sharing. Access to the Vault District's monitoring records. Players who have been to other Hollostar-related sites and can share direct observations.

**What they offer:** Continental connections — the Seekers have offices in most major cities and can provide introductions, safe houses, and context in locations far from Lumencrest. Significant knowledge about Hollowstars, their history, and the world's relationship to them. Payment for information.
