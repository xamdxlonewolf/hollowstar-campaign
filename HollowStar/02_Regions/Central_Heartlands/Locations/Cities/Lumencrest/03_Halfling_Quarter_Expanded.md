# The Halfling Quarter — Expanded District Guide

## Points of Interest, Vendors, and NPCs

_This document expands on the Halfling Quarter as established in [[03_Halfling_Quarter]], adding the locations, people, and daily texture needed to run the district at the table. The Halfling Quarter is the warmest district in Lumencrest and one of the most politically clear-eyed. It feeds the city, brews what the city drinks, and watches the crisis with the calm of people who built their infrastructure to not need what's failing. Cross-reference [[04_The_Manufactories]] for the Ironwright Collective's mutual aid relationship with the Quarter and [[Factions_and_Reputation]] for the Quarter elders' role in the city's social fabric._

---

## Running the Halfling Quarter at the Table

The Halfling Quarter arrives through the senses before it arrives as a place. Players entering from Lamplight Ward or from the Manufactories' northern edge will notice the smell first — yeast and bread and roasting and something green and herbal underneath all of it, the smell of a district that has been producing food and drink for three hundred years and whose streets have absorbed it. The sound changes too: lower, warmer, more human-scaled. The industrial percussion of the Manufactories drops away. What's left is voices, the creak of bakery doors, the specific hollow sound of barrels being moved on cobblestones, children.

**Scale:** The buildings are lower here. A taller player character will not duck through doorways — the Quarter's construction accounts for visitors — but they will feel the proportional difference. The streets are narrower in a way that feels intentional rather than crowded: built for the community that built them, which means built for people who prefer closeness to distance and designed their environment accordingly.

**The welcome:** The Quarter is the most genuinely welcoming district in Lumencrest, but welcoming is not the same as without social intelligence. Halfling hospitality is warm and real, and it comes attached to the sharpest community awareness in the city. The elders at Speakers' Corner notice everything. The family networks communicate faster than the cable car. A stranger in the Quarter is welcomed; what kind of stranger they are is assessed within the hour and the assessment circulates. Players who behave well will find this works strongly in their favor. Players who misstep will find the warmth develops a specific quality of politeness that is not quite the same as warmth.

**The independence:** The Quarter's backup brewing and baking infrastructure means brownouts hit here last and least. When the Network flickers and the rest of the city dims, the Quarter's lights stay on — candles and small coal-burning stoves and the backup systems that the breweries have been running for sixty years. During a full blackout, the Quarter becomes the place people in adjacent districts come to, because it is lit and warm and the bakeries are still operating. The elders have opinions about this that they express at Speakers' Corner with measured clarity.

**The politics:** The Quarter is not politically naïve. It does not have a faction in the Council's sense — it has the Halfling Quarter Community as its expression in the city's social landscape, informal and effective. The elders' evening gatherings at Speakers' Corner have been the most consistent source of honest public commentary on the city's crisis for three years. They say what the Council won't say. They say it politely and precisely. People come from other districts to listen.

## The Common Green

_(Referenced in [[03_Halfling_Quarter]] — expanded here as a full playable location)_

**What it is:** An open park in the Quarter's center — genuine grass, maintained by the community's shared labor with a pride that comes from choosing to maintain it rather than being required to. Old trees planted when the Quarter was young enough that several are now the width of a person's arm-span. The covered market pavilion on the southern edge runs daily with local vendors. Speakers' Corner, the raised stone platform on the Green's eastern side, operates most evenings from approximately two hours before dark until the speakers run out of things to say, which in Lumencrest's current climate is late.

**The daily rhythm:**

- _Morning:_ The Green is walkers and children and vendors setting up. The covered market opens. The smells from the Bakery District's night shift drift across.
- _Midday:_ The Green is lunch — workers from the breweries and bakeries eating outside when the weather allows, visitors from other districts who came for the Quarter's food and found the Green.
- _Evening:_ The Green is community. Speakers' Corner pulls whoever has something to say and everyone who wants to hear it. This is the Quarter's heartbeat.

**The covered market pavilion:** A permanent structure of old timber and good stone, open-sided, with vendor stalls on both interior sides and a central walkway. Open daily, runs from morning to early evening. What's available:

- _Produce:_ The Quarter's herb gardens and the community's supply relationships with the agricultural settlements outside the city combine to make this the best fresh produce market in Lumencrest. Not cheap, but real. Several Lamplight Ward households send staff here specifically.
- _Prepared food:_ Eight or nine vendors at any given time. Halfling prepared food at market stall scale — meat pies, roasted roots and greens, grain dishes with herb preparations, sweet pastries that the Academic Quarter's students make the walk for. 1-4 copper marks per item. Some of the best food available in Lumencrest at this price point.
- _Craft goods:_ Pottery, preserved foods, candles, small tools — the Quarter's craft production visible in market form.
- _The herb vendors:_ Three specific herb vendors who sell both culinary and medicinal preparations. Two of these are fully above-board. One — **Merry Thatch** (see NPCs) — sells a wider range than is visible from the front of the stall.

**Speakers' Corner:** The raised stone platform where community members address the Quarter. No official process for using it — anyone can speak, and the crowd's response determines how long they speak. In practice: short announcements cycle through first, then genuine community business, then the substantive political commentary that draws the wider audience.

What gets said at Speakers' Corner:

- The Quarter's elder **Bram Kettlemarch** (see NPCs) speaks most evenings. His topics rotate — community business, the city's power situation, observations about the Council's recent decisions. He is precise and he does not exaggerate and he is more widely listened to than most city officials are.
- Workers from the Manufactories come here to hear themselves represented clearly. Several have spoken themselves about the labor situation.
- The elders rotate — Bram is the most prominent but not the only voice. A Halfling woman named **Pip Underwell** (see NPCs) speaks on the Clank situation with a clarity that the Ironwright Collective has been citing in their documentation.
- Players can speak here. The community will listen and respond honestly, which is worth something and can also be uncomfortable.

## Brewers' Lane

_(Referenced in [[03_Halfling_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**What it is:** The Quarter's main commercial thoroughfare and the visual center of what the Quarter produces. The twin brewery towers of Copperkettle & Brum are visible at the northern end from most of the Lane's length — functional structures that have become the Quarter's skyline in the way that industrial architecture becomes symbol when it's been there long enough. The Lane below them is smaller craft breweries, a specialist hop-and-grain supply shop, a coopers' operation, and the taverns and shops that cluster wherever there is beer.

**The smell on Brewers' Lane** is specifically and distinctly yeast-forward — a warm, slightly sweet fermentation smell that experienced visitors describe as immediately comforting and first-time visitors describe as overwhelming. It normalizes within about ten minutes. Most people find they miss it when they leave.

---

### Copperkettle & Brum Brewery

**Location:** The northern end of Brewers' Lane, occupying two full buildings connected at the second story by a covered walkway. The copper-plated kettle above the main entrance — three feet across, purely decorative, installed 200 years ago — is the Quarter's most recognizable single landmark.

**What it is:** The most significant food and drink production operation in Lumencrest, and probably in the surrounding region. Copperkettle & Brum produces the _Copperkettle Amber_ (highest volume beer sold in the city), the _Brum Reserve_ (highest price per unit), twelve other named ales and lagers sold across the Accord, and three spirits — a wheat whiskey, a hop-derived gin that is one of the Quiet Market's most requested legitimately-produced products, and a dark liqueur made from a preparation that the family describes as "traditional" and does not break down further.

The brewery is genuinely old — the founding families' names are Copperkettle and Brum, married three generations ago, and the current operation is run by their grandchildren with the fourth generation already working in various capacities. The production is real and significant: sixteen workers on rotating shifts, three independent fermentation cellars beneath the building, a hop storage warehouse on the Lane's northern wall that is larger than it looks from outside.

**Tours:** Copperkettle & Brum offers paid brewery tours twice a week — 3 silver marks, includes a tasting flight of six pours, led by a family member rather than a hired guide. The tours are genuinely popular with visitors from other districts and have been running for twenty years. The tour does not cover the fermentation cellars' lower level, which is not a mystery — it's simply that the lower fermentation level is fully operational and visitors walking through would disrupt the process.

**What they sell retail:** At the brewery's front room — a small shop just inside the main entrance — visitors can purchase bottles of any Copperkettle & Brum product at brewery prices (10-15% below tavern pricing). Limited quantities of Brum Reserve available at the shop only; it is not sold through distributors.

**The family:** The current generation — **Marta Copperkettle-Brum** (see NPCs) and her two brothers — runs the operation with the efficiency of people who grew up inside it. Marta is the public face; her brother **Aldric** manages production; her brother **Tomas** manages the export business, which means he is frequently not in the Quarter and is, by the Quarter's standards, unusually worldly about continental affairs.

### The Copper Kettle Pub

_(Referenced in [[03_Halfling_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Brewers' Lane, approximately midway. A building that has been a pub for four generations and shows it — worn smooth in the right places, the kind of accumulated warmth that can't be installed.

**What it is:** The Quarter's central gathering pub and one of the best drinking establishments in Lumencrest by any reasonable measure. Not the fanciest — the Lamplight Ward's Entertainment Quarter has establishments with considerably more visual ambition. But the Copper Kettle is doing something different: it is a place where the community that built and runs it gathers because it belongs to them, and that quality is legible the moment you walk in.

The walls are covered in portraits — some painted, some sketched, some photographs taken in the early photographic era before Network dependence made the equipment unreliable. Four generations of people who have sat at these tables. Players who look at the portraits will notice that they are not organized or labeled — the Quarter residents who grew up here simply know who they're looking at, and explaining them to visitors is the work of an evening.

**What's on offer:**

_Drink:_

- Copperkettle Amber on tap — the correct choice for a first drink, always properly kept, 2 copper marks a pint
- Four rotating craft ales from smaller Brewers' Lane producers, selected by the current proprietor's preference. Quality varies; she picks well.
- Brum Reserve by the measure — the only pub in Lumencrest with guaranteed access, family arrangement. 8 copper marks a measure. Worth it.
- The Quarter's own house spirits — the hop gin, available here and almost nowhere else at pub pricing. 5 copper marks.
- A non-alcoholic cold-brewed grain drink served in summer that regulars swear by and that visitors are always slightly surprised to enjoy.

_Food:_

- The Copper Kettle's kitchen is the standard against which pub food in Lumencrest is measured. Full menu: roasts, stews, pies, the specific Halfling preparation of preserved-and-then-roasted root vegetables that is one of those things that sounds unimpressive and is extraordinary in practice. 3-6 copper marks. Portions are Halfling generous, which means large by any standard.
- The desserts are not optional — visitors who decline dessert are not turned away but are noted. The current cook's spiced pear preparation has been mentioned twice in Academic Quarter food writing.

**The atmosphere:** Loud in a warm way, particularly in the evenings when the Quarter's working population arrives after shift. Visitors are welcome and are treated with the particular generous curiosity of people who like meeting new faces in their own space. Players will be offered the seat that's available, which is always at a communal table, and will be in a conversation within ten minutes whether or not they sought one.

**The proprietor:** **Bessa Kettlemarch** — see NPCs. Distinct from Bessa Pipwick at the inn — this has caused confusion.

### The Craft Breweries — Brewers' Lane Smaller Operations

Four independent craft breweries operate on Brewers' Lane below Copperkettle & Brum. All are Halfling-run, all produce excellent beer, and all have their own character:

**Underwell's** — run by the Underwell family, specializing in dark ales with pronounced malt character. The Quarter's choice for winter drinking. Pip Underwell (see Speakers' Corner NPCs) is Henly Underwell's aunt, which is mentioned because it explains the Free Hand pamphlets visible on the pub wall inside.

**The Green Cap** — the Quarter's most experimental brewery, run by three young Halflings who apprenticed at Copperkettle & Brum and left to try things the main brewery won't. Seasonal and limited releases, consistently interesting. The hop gin variant they make with the Quarter's herb garden is technically not a beer but is the best-selling thing in their small front tap room.

**Barrelstone** — the oldest craft brewery after Copperkettle & Brum, specializing in lagers and lighter ales. Feeds most of the Quarter's daily drinking at ordinary prices. The proprietor, **Finn Barrelstone** (human, 50s, the Quarter's most prominent non-Halfling long-term resident), has been here for thirty years and is accepted by the community as more Quarter than not.

**Kettlefire** — the newest operation, five years old. Halfling Quarter Clank collaboration — the brewery was founded by a free Clank named **Brass** and a Halfling named **Lenny Roach** after Brass completed their service term. Brass handles production; Lenny handles the front of house and the business end. The beers are unusual and have a devoted following among the Academic Quarter's less conservative faculty. Brass is visible proof that a completed service term Clank integrating into a Lumencrest community is possible, which is part of why Pip Underwell mentions the brewery at Speakers' Corner.

## The Bakery District

_(Referenced in [[03_Halfling_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**What it is:** The Quarter's southern section, parallel to Brewers' Lane, where the bread that feeds Lumencrest is made. Open twenty-four hours — the night shift bakes through the dark so the morning deliveries can reach every district before the city wakes up. The Bakery District smells different from Brewers' Lane — yeast here is hotter, more bread-forward, with the specific warmth of functioning ovens that has never stopped.

**The scale:** Eight major bakeries employ a total of roughly 120 workers across their shifts. The output is significant enough that any disruption — strike, equipment failure, supply shortage — would be noticed citywide within 24 hours. The Quarter's elders are aware of this. They are not aggressive about it. They simply know.

**The supply arrangement:** The bakeries' grain supply comes through a combination of city supply contracts (the formal arrangement) and the Quarter's own long-standing relationships with farming settlements along the Silverrun River (the practical arrangement that ensures supply when the city contracts fall short, which has happened twice in the past three years). The Quarter does not publicize the independent supply arrangements. The city is aware of them and has concluded that the arrangements make the bakeries more reliable and that reliable bread supply is a higher priority than administrative tidiness.

### The Hearthstone — The Quarter's Largest Bakery and Café

**Location:** The Bakery District's main street, occupying a corner building that is longer than it looks from outside — the production space extends back nearly half a block behind the public café front.

**What it is:** The Bakery District's largest operation and also the most visitor-accessible — the Hearthstone has a café front that opens to the street from early morning until mid-afternoon, selling fresh bread, pastries, and a short menu of café food. The production behind it is industrial-scale for hand-craft baking: six wood-burning ovens, eight bakers on each of the three shifts, an output that feeds significant portions of the Lamplight Ward's mid-range restaurants and the Manufactories' worker canteens.

**What's available at the café:**

- Fresh bread in any quantity — sold by the loaf (4 copper marks for a standard white, 6 copper for seeded or grain) or by the slice with whatever is fresh from the most recent bake
- The morning pastry selection — available from opening until they're gone, which is usually about two hours after opening. Players who want the good pastries need to arrive early.
- A café menu running from morning to early afternoon: soft eggs with herb preparations, grain porridges with various toppings, the Hearthstone's signature open sandwiches with whatever the current seasonal ingredients are. 3-5 copper marks.
- Coffee — real coffee, imported via the Quarter's supply contacts, not the grain-substitute sold elsewhere in the city. 2 copper marks. Worth specifying this because it is rarer than it sounds.

**The owners:** **Willa and Cord Hearthstone** (Halflings, late 50s) — a married pair who bought the building thirty years ago and built the operation from a small family bakery to what it currently is. Willa manages the production and the bakers; Cord manages the café and the accounts. They are not dramatic people and their establishment reflects this — functional, warm, excellent, without performance.

**What they know:** The Quarter's food supply situation at the operational level — which grain suppliers are reliable, which supply contracts with the city are currently being honored and which are running short, what the realistic supply position is if the city's distribution system is further disrupted by the power crisis. This information is not secret, but it is accurate, and accurate information about the city's food supply chain is more valuable than it usually sounds.

### The Night Baker — Small Bakery and Late Trading

**Location:** A side street off the Bakery District's main thoroughfare, identifiable by the window light at 2am when everything else in the district has closed.

**What it is:** A single-person bakery operation run through the night hours by a Halfling named **Pessel Nook** (see NPCs). Pessel bakes the specialty items that the larger bakeries don't produce — small-batch, unusual preparations from regional traditions, items that require attention a production-scale operation can't give. Sold from a single counter through a window that opens directly onto the street.

**What's available (depending on the night):**

- Spiced seed rolls from a Vossomar recipe that Pessel's grandmother brought to Lumencrest sixty years ago. Available when Pessel has the right seeds, which is most of the time.
- A dense rye bread with specific sour culture that requires a three-day fermentation — available only when Pessel has decided it's ready, not to schedule. Dedicated customers check the window.
- Night pastries — heavier, richer than the day-shift versions, designed for workers coming off late shifts who need something substantial.
- Occasionally: a savory tart or pie that represents Pessel using up the day's surplus ingredients in whatever the most interesting available combination is. Never the same twice.

**The cost:** 1-3 copper marks for most items. Pessel does not price for the market — they price for the workers who buy from the window at 2am.

**Why it matters:** The Night Baker is where you go when you need to talk to someone in the Quarter at 2am and you need a reason to be in the Bakery District. It is also where you find out, from Pessel, who else has been there recently — not because Pessel gossips but because Pessel is awake when the rest of the district isn't, and they notice things.

## The Green Hearth Inn

_(Referenced in [[03_Halfling_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** The Common Green's western edge, with windows looking directly onto the Green itself. One of the better views from an inn room in Lumencrest.

**What it is:** The Quarter's only full-service inn, run as a cooperative business by seven Halfling families who rotate the management role in four-month shifts. The current managing family (the Wickhams) runs the inn with the particular warmth of people who will be neighbors with the previous guests long after those guests have left, and therefore have a stake in the guests' experience that a purely commercial establishment doesn't.

**The rooms:** Twelve rooms total. Eight standard rooms — slightly smaller than the Lamplight Ward equivalent, furnished with genuine care: real wood furniture, linens laundered twice weekly, windows that open fully and have working shutters. Four larger rooms with a sitting area, available at a premium. All rooms: 1 gold mark per night. The price includes dinner and breakfast in the inn's dining room, which is the more significant benefit.

**The dining room:** Open to non-guests for dinner — six tables, reservation strongly preferred. The menu rotates weekly based on what the Quarter's markets have available. Quality is what you'd expect from Halfling cooking at its most careful: not elaborate, not theatrical, exactly what it is supposed to be and better than most things that try to be more. 6-10 copper marks for a full dinner. The wine list is short and excellent and cheaper than it should be because the cooperative has a family relationship with a Lamplight Ward wine merchant who values the relationship.

**The common sitting room:** A ground-floor room that functions as the inn's social space — armchairs, a fire in cool weather, a selection of books and games belonging to the cooperative collectively, and a perpetual state of readiness for the kind of quiet evening conversation that inns exist for. The current manager on duty will join guests who want company and leave those who don't, accurately. This is a skill not all innkeepers have.

**The cooperative relationship:** The seven families rotate management, but they also collectively maintain the inn's reputation, which means any guest who behaves in a way that requires the managing family to do something about it will find the response comes from seven families' collective authority rather than one manager's discretion. This information is available to guests who ask, and some guests are informed of it proactively by the front-door welcome conversation, depending on what the door assessment suggests.

**Current managing family contact:** **Ros Wickham** — see NPCs.

## Pipwick's Rooms

_(Referenced in [[03_Halfling_Quarter]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** The Common Green's south side, above the Pipwick bakery. The bakery occupies the ground floor; the seven rooms occupy the two floors above.

**What it is:** A small, personal, exceptional boarding option. Bessa Pipwick has been running these rooms for decades and her approach to it has not changed: guests are treated as expected cousins, the breakfast included in the price is produced by the bakery one floor below, and the warmth is not performed — it is the natural expression of someone whose entire approach to life involves genuine care for people in her immediate vicinity.

**The rooms:** Seven rooms, all small, all simple, all extremely clean and furnished with the particular cozy density of Halfling interiors — things in the right places, nothing superfluous, a quality of considered arrangement that makes a small space feel complete rather than cramped. 7 silver per night. The price includes breakfast; the breakfast includes fresh bread from this morning's bake, whatever preserves are current, soft-boiled eggs, and the coffee that Bessa sources through the same Quarter contact as the Hearthstone.

**The guests Bessa prefers:** She will rent to anyone who is not actively hostile or obviously a risk to the building. Her preferred guests are people who treat the rooms with care, appreciate the breakfast without requiring that it be the same every day, and occasionally sit in the small common room on the second floor for an evening without feeling that they need to fill the silence. She is perceptive about who these people are and occasionally, when a guest doesn't meet the criteria, there happens to be no vacancy.

**The proprietor:** **Bessa Pipwick** — see NPCs. Distinct from Bessa Kettlemarch at the Copper Kettle pub. Both are Bessa; both are Halfling; both have been confusing visitors for fifteen years.

## The Quarter's Specialty Shops and Services

---

### Merry Thatch's Herb Stall and Back Room

**Location:** The covered market pavilion, Common Green. Third stall from the eastern entrance.

**What it is:** At the front: a herb vendor selling culinary and medicinal preparations — dried herbs, tinctures, preparations for common ailments, the kind of stock that any respectable market herbalist carries. Quality is genuinely excellent; Merry grows a significant portion of her stock in her own garden on the Quarter's eastern edge and sources the rest through a supplier relationship she's had for twenty years.

At the back — meaning, for customers who ask correctly — a wider range. Preparations that exist in gray legal territory, substances that are not on the controlled list but that a strict reading of the Alchemical Safety Ordinance might raise questions about, and several preparations that Merry describes as "traditional regional medicine" that the city has not gotten around to classifying. Nothing approaching the Night Market's harder offerings. Everything one step past what the front of the stall carries.

Merry is not a drug dealer. She is an herbalist with a very thorough knowledge of what plants do and a position that the city's classification system is behind its subject matter. Both things are true. She will say both things directly if asked.

**What she carries (front stall):**

- Fresh and dried culinary herbs at competitive prices
- Medicinal tinctures for common conditions: pain, fever, digestive complaints, sleep
- A specific preparation for Lumen Network-related headaches — the symptoms that come from extended time near Network nodes — that is uniquely available here and has a devoted following in the Academic Quarter among researchers who won't admit that's what they're treating

**What she carries (back room, correct approach required):**

- Extended pain management preparations for conditions where normal tinctures aren't adequate
- Anxiety and sleep preparations beyond the mild versions on the front stall
- Several Hollow Pipe blend components at better-than-Night-Market quality
- One preparation she calls _Greenveil_ that she will not describe in full, which produces a several-hour state of heightened sensory awareness without any impairment. She sells this rarely and to people she has assessed carefully. It is not on any controlled list. It is extremely potent.

**The proprietor:** **Merry Thatch** — see NPCs.

### The Mending Room — Healing Services

**Location:** A private building on the Common Green's northern edge, between two family compound gates. A small carved sign above the door: THE MENDING ROOM. The sign has been there for forty years.

**What it is:** The Quarter's healing practitioner — a Halfling healer named **Ora Finch** (see NPCs) who has been providing care to the Quarter's residents and, increasingly, to people from other districts who have found their way here. The Mending Room is not a clinic in the clinical sense; it is a practice that combines conventional healing, herbal medicine, and minor restorative magic that Ora has developed over thirty years of working with both.

**What's available:**

- Healing of injuries at a level appropriate to Ora's capability — minor to moderate wounds, illness treatment, the kind of care that Vael at the Stitch provides in the Underground and that the city's clinics theoretically provide everywhere but in practice do not
- Ongoing care for chronic conditions — several Manufactories workers travel here specifically because the Manufactories' medical resources are inadequate and Ora does not ask about the circumstances of injuries
- Restorative treatment — not the magical acceleration of a full healer, but the specific combination of minor restorative touch, herbal support, and rest that shortens recovery time meaningfully
- Mental health support, which Ora offers carefully — not a specialist, but experienced, and the Quarter has a lower rate of untreated anxiety and trauma than comparable populations in the city, which is not coincidental

**Cost:** Sliding scale. Ora charges what she assesses people can pay. Quarter residents pay less than Lamplight Ward visitors. Nobody is turned away for inability to pay, which the Quarter's community fund quietly subsidizes.

**The practitioner:** **Ora Finch** — see NPCs.

### The Wickham House — Quiet Information

**Location:** Not a formal establishment — a family compound near the Common Green that happens to be the residence of Lumencrest's most thorough private information network in the civilian sense. **Elder Tommas Wickham** (see NPCs) has spent forty years building a picture of Lumencrest's social and political landscape from the Quarter's particular vantage point.

**What it is:** Tommas Wickham is the Quarter's elder with the longest memory and the most carefully maintained network of informal contacts across the city. He is not an information broker in any commercial sense — he does not sell what he knows. He trades it, very carefully, for things that serve the Quarter's interests. Getting an audience with him requires introduction through someone he trusts, which requires being someone the Quarter trusts, which requires time.

**What he knows:** Forty years of the city's political and social movements from the perspective of a community that provides food and drink to all of it. His network includes: long-standing contacts in the Lamplight Ward through brewery and bakery supply relationships, specific Academic Quarter faculty who eat his family's bread and have been talking to the Quarter's delivery people for decades, contacts in the Halfling Quarter Community's formal interactions with the Council, and the specific quality of awareness that comes from providing a critical resource and watching how people behave when they think the person they're talking to is just a baker.

**Working with him:** He will not meet with players directly without a vouching introduction from someone the Quarter trusts (Bram Kettlemarch, Bessa Pipwick, Ora Finch, or Genna Cradle would each be sufficient). With introduction, he will assess the players in a single conversation that feels like pleasant dinner discussion and is not. If he decides the players' goals align with the Quarter's interests, or with the city's survival in a way that the Quarter has an interest in, he will provide specific and accurate context that is available nowhere else. He does not ask for payment. He asks for what the players know that he doesn't, which is usually more than they expect him to ask for.

### The Delivery Network — A System Worth Knowing

**What it is:** The Halfling Quarter's food delivery operation is the most extensive civilian logistics network in Lumencrest — sixteen delivery routes covering every district, running twice daily, operated by a rotating roster of Quarter community members and their adolescent children who earn their first independent income this way. The delivery network is not a faction or an information service. It is a bread delivery system.

It also happens to mean that Halfling Quarter delivery runners are present in every district of the city every morning and every evening, know the routes and schedules better than anyone, and are neither threatening nor officially interesting to city watch or the Vault Guard. They are invisible in the way that service workers are always invisible to people who benefit from their service.

Players who need a reliable carrier, a known presence in a specific district at a specific time, or someone who can confirm what is and isn't on a specific street on a specific morning would find it worthwhile to establish a relationship with the delivery network. The Quarter's young delivery runners are not intelligence operatives. They are community members doing their jobs. The distinction matters and should be respected. But they know the city.

**Contact:** Ros Wickham at the Green Hearth manages the delivery schedule coordination. She is the correct point of contact for anyone seeking to establish a relationship with the network.

## Notable NPCs — The Halfling Quarter

---

### Bram Kettlemarch

**Role:** Quarter elder, primary Speakers' Corner voice, informal community leader **Ancestry/Age:** Halfling, 74 **Location:** The Common Green most evenings. The Copper Kettle Pub most days. His family compound two streets from the Green.

**Appearance:** The particular quality of very old Halflings — more compact and more permanent-looking than younger people, as if the decades have settled them rather than worn them. White hair, worn longer than practical, a habit from youth that has become identity. Moves with the deliberate pace of someone who has learned that hurrying rarely improves outcomes. His hands are baker's hands — his generation built the Bakery District, and his hands remember it even though he hasn't baked commercially in twenty years.

**Personality:** Patient, precise, and possessed of the long perspective that is the most useful and most occasionally infuriating thing about people who have been watching events unfold for seventy-four years. He does not exaggerate at Speakers' Corner. He does not minimize either. He says what he observes, and what he observes is usually accurate, and he has the credibility of someone whose observations have been accurate for long enough that the record is public.

**What he wants:** The Quarter to continue being the Quarter — not preserved in amber, but sustained. He has watched three crises in his lifetime and the Quarter has come through each of them more intact than the districts that depended on whatever was failing. He is under no illusion that the current crisis is smaller than it appears. He is quietly making the same preparations the Quarter has made before.

**What he knows:** The full sweep of Lumencrest's recent political history from the Quarter's vantage point. The Council members' characters and histories, assessed over decades of watching how people behave when they want the Quarter's cooperation. The city's food supply vulnerabilities at a level of detail that would concern the Council if the Council asked him, which they haven't. What the city actually needs versus what the Council is currently planning to provide, which are different.

**Working with him:** He will talk freely to players who approach with genuine respect and some demonstrated connection to the city's wellbeing. He is not difficult — he is warm — but he is evaluating throughout the conversation and adjusting what he shares accordingly. Players who are honest about their situation and goals, including the parts that are complicated, will find him more forthcoming than players who present a simplified version of themselves. He has heard every simplified version and finds them less interesting than the reality.

### Marta Copperkettle-Brum

**Role:** Lead operator of Copperkettle & Brum Brewery, Quarter business community leader **Ancestry/Age:** Halfling, 48 **Location:** The brewery most days. The Copper Kettle Pub evenings. Wherever the Quarter's business community is currently meeting, which is often.

**Appearance:** The specific appearance of someone who has been running a large operation since her early twenties and whose face reflects that. Practically dressed always — she does not separate work presentation from social presentation because she does not separate work from the rest of her life in a way that would make that meaningful. Dark hair with early grey, cut short for function.

**Personality:** Businesslike without being cold, which requires a particular skill set. She is direct about what the brewery needs, direct about what the Quarter needs, and direct about what she thinks of people she's dealing with. The directness is warm — she is not blunt in the way of someone who doesn't care about the effect, but in the way of someone who has concluded that saying what she means serves everyone better than the alternative.

**What she wants:** The brewery to continue operating and the Quarter's supply chains to stay intact. Her brother Tomas, who manages export, has been providing her with information about the continental reception to Lumencrest's power situation that has made her more concerned than Bram's Speakers' Corner commentary suggests is public knowledge. She is making quiet export preparations — not abandoning the city, not planning for collapse, but building a version of the business that would survive significant civic disruption if it came to that. She does not discuss this publicly.

**What she knows:** The Quarter's export business relationships, which means she has a picture of how Lumencrest is perceived in other major Accord cities that most Lumencrest residents don't have access to. She also knows the city's food and drink supply situation at the operational level — the gap between what the Council says is the supply situation and what the actual supply situation is.

**Working with her:** She will be direct and fair in commercial dealings. Players who need something the brewery can provide — space, resources, legitimacy in certain contexts — will get a clear assessment of whether she can provide it and at what cost. She is interested in people who are actively working on the city's crisis in ways that might actually help, not as a patron but as a community member who would prefer the city to survive its current trajectory. Her export contacts are the most significant resource she could provide to players who need a continental connection.

### Bessa Pipwick

**Role:** Proprietor of Pipwick's Rooms **Ancestry/Age:** Halfling, 62 **Location:** Pipwick's Rooms and the bakery below, Common Green south side. She has not left the building for more than a few hours in any given week for twelve years.

**Appearance:** Round and warm and in perpetual motion between the bakery and the rooms above it, carrying something almost always — a tray, a basket of linens, a plate of something that needs to be delivered somewhere. Her hands are flour-marked at all times because she is in and out of the bakery continuously. The kind of person whose appearance immediately communicates welcome.

**Personality:** Genuine warmth without softness. She has been running a boarding house for decades and has an accurate read on people from years of letting them into her space. The welcome is real and the assessment is real and they happen simultaneously and without apparent effort. She treats guests as expected cousins and has a specific threshold for people who mistake this warmth for naivety, at which point they discover the other side of the welcome.

**What she knows:** Everyone who has stayed at Pipwick's Rooms in the past thirty years, filtered through the impressionistic knowledge of someone who was present but not necessarily paying attention to specifics. More relevantly: the Common Green's daily social texture — who gathers where, who has been speaking with whom, what the community's current mood is at the level of the everyday rather than the political.

**For players:** Pipwick's is the most comfortable and most socially connected place to stay in the Quarter. Bessa's network extends through the community in ways that don't have a single channel — she simply knows people, and her endorsement of a guest travels through the community in the natural way that endorsements travel in tight-knit communities. Players who are her guests and who treat the rooms well and the community with care will find doors opening in the Quarter that they didn't knock on.

### Bessa Kettlemarch

**Role:** Proprietor of the Copper Kettle Pub **Ancestry/Age:** Halfling, 55 **Location:** The Copper Kettle Pub, Brewers' Lane. She lives three buildings away.

**Appearance:** Stockier than Bessa Pipwick (their shared name has produced a local habit of distinguishing them by build, which both Bessas find mildly amusing), with close-cropped silver hair and the particular quality of someone who has been managing a very busy pub for twenty years without becoming indifferent to it. Moves through the Copper Kettle with the authority of ownership that doesn't require announcement.

**Personality:** Bessa Kettlemarch is the Copper Kettle's character as much as its manager — her warmth, her humor (dry, sudden, underestimated by people who haven't been around long enough to hear it), and her specific fierceness about the pub being what it is rather than what it could be made into commercially. She has turned down three offers to expand. She has turned down one offer to franchise the name. She finds the concept of franchising the Copper Kettle mildly offensive, which she expressed clearly and without drama to the person who offered it.

**What she knows:** The Brewers' Lane community's professional relationships and current tensions, the Quarter's social connections to the rest of the city through the relationships that form at her tables, and the specific political opinions of every regular in her pub, which is a significant slice of the Quarter's adults. She does not share individual opinions. She will share general community sentiment accurately and without embellishment.

**Working with her:** Players who treat the Copper Kettle with the respect of guests in someone else's space will find her genuinely generous. She will feed them well, introduce them to people she thinks they should meet, and eventually — if they have been coming long enough — treat them as regulars, which in the Quarter is a social classification with real meaning.

### Pip Underwell

**Role:** Speakers' Corner voice on Clank rights and labor; Underwell's brewery family **Ancestry/Age:** Halfling, 38 **Location:** Speakers' Corner most evenings when she has something to say, which is frequent. Underwell's Brewery, Brewers' Lane.

**Appearance:** Small even for a Halfling, which she uses effectively — she is underestimated regularly and has stopped correcting people who do, finding it more useful. Dark hair, quick hands, the specific energy of someone who is always thinking about the next thing while managing the current one.

**Personality:** Politically sharp and practically oriented in a way that distinguishes her from advocates who are better at naming problems than addressing them. She speaks at Speakers' Corner because she has something specific to say about specific situations, not for the act of speaking. She is impatient with performance — her own and others' — and will cut short a conversation that has become about itself rather than what it was supposed to be about.

**What she wants:** Concrete progress on Clank labor rights — not eventual reform, specific changes to specific Labour Board policies in specific timeframes with specific accountability. She has documented her position over three years of Speakers' Corner addresses and the documentation is publicly available. She will tell you this if you ask and will also tell you what she thinks needs to happen next.

**What she knows:** The Clank rights situation in the Manufactories at the community level — she has relationships with Sable-Seven and with Kael at the Free Clank Cooperative that give her accurate current information. She also knows the Kettlefire brewery's operation, which means she has a concrete example of successful Clank integration into civilian community life that she uses in her Speakers' Corner addresses. She knows the Free Hand's existence and supports its work without being operationally involved.

**Working with her:** Direct and easy, if players are genuinely interested in the issue rather than in her as an information source. She will give players everything she publicly knows without much persuasion if their goals align. The non-public layer — specifically the Sable-Seven connection and what she knows about Free Hand activity in the Barracks — requires players to have demonstrated that they're working toward the same outcome she is, not just gathering information.

### Ora Finch

**Role:** Healing practitioner, the Mending Room **Ancestry/Age:** Halfling, 67 **Location:** The Mending Room, Common Green northern edge. Present most days; closed on the seventh day of each week, which the Quarter respects.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has been caring for people for forty years and whose presence has been calibrated by those years — still, unhurried, with a quality of attention that makes the person she's looking at feel specifically seen. Her hands are small and precise. Her workroom smells of the herbs she uses and something warmer underneath that is harder to name.

**Personality:** Gentle without being soft. She has seen a great deal of pain in forty years — physical, mental, the kind that comes from the city's structural inequalities and the kind that comes from individual circumstances — and has developed a response to it that is neither detached nor overwhelmed. She cares about everyone who comes through her door and she maintains the capacity to do this by being very careful about the limits of what she can provide. She refers patients when they need more than she has. She is not embarrassed by her limits.

**What she wants:** Her patients to be well, in the full sense — not just physical recovery but the conditions for continued wellbeing, which in Lumencrest is often a political as much as a medical question. She does not prescribe politics, but she is clear about the connection between the city's material conditions and the health she sees in her consulting room.

**What she knows:** The Quarter's health situation in detail — what the community is experiencing medically and mentally, and what is causing it. Also the broader city's health situation in aggregated and anonymized form from the patients who come to her from other districts: the specific physical effects of the Manufactories' work conditions, the anxiety profile of the Academic Quarter's researchers, the dependency situations she's been referring to Vael at the Stitch. She does not share individual cases. She will, for players with legitimate concern, describe the patterns.

### Merry Thatch

**Role:** Herbalist, covered market pavilion **Ancestry/Age:** Halfling, 51 **Location:** Third stall from the eastern entrance of the covered market pavilion, Common Green. Present every market day.

**Appearance:** Herb-scented in a way that has stopped being a quality of her clothing and become a quality of her person — she smells of her garden and her workroom and this has been true for so long that she does not notice it. Practical clothing in earth tones, always with pockets, always with something in them.

**Personality:** Confident in her knowledge in the way of someone who has thirty years of practical results and stopped feeling the need to justify the confidence. She will tell you what a preparation does, what it won't do, what the correct use is, and what the incorrect use will produce. She does not moralize about how people use what she provides; she is specific about effects and leaves the decisions to the person buying.

**What she knows:** The medicinal and psychoactive properties of the plant-based pharmacopeia more thoroughly than anyone in the city except possibly specific Luminarium researchers — and her knowledge is practical where theirs is theoretical. She knows what Hollow Pipe does because she has been providing its components for twenty years. She knows what Network exposure does to the body because she's been treating it with herbal preparations that she developed herself. She has a specific and accurate understanding of Hollostar Dust's physiological effects, from treating patients in early withdrawal, that the Luminarium does not have access to because it didn't occur to them to ask a Halfling herbalist.

**Working with her:** She is accessible and direct in all her dealings. The back-room range requires the correct approach — not a password, just a clear and direct request that demonstrates the person knows what they're asking about. She does not sell to people she assesses are in a dependency crisis rather than a recreational context; she will redirect those people to Ora Finch or Vael at the Stitch. For players who need herbal preparations beyond the Night Market's range, or who need someone who knows the city's substance landscape from a harm-reduction perspective, Merry is a significant resource.

### Elder Tommas Wickham

**Role:** Quarter elder with the longest memory and deepest network; Ros's grandfather **Ancestry/Age:** Halfling, 81 **Location:** His family compound, two streets from the Common Green. Occasionally at Speakers' Corner when he decides something needs to be said that Bram hasn't said. Almost never seen elsewhere.

**Appearance:** The most settled-looking person in the Quarter, which is saying something. Eighty-one years of Halfling life in Lumencrest has produced a quality of rootedness — he moves slowly and looks permanent. Bright eyes that have lost nothing of their acuity. Hands that have done every kind of work the Quarter has and are now mostly still.

**Personality:** Patient beyond what most people can achieve or maintain. He has been watching Lumencrest for eighty-one years and has concluded that most situations benefit from observation before action. This is not passivity — he has acted when he decided action was correct, and those actions have been effective. He is simply more certain than most about when action is correct.

**What he knows:** The full living history of the Quarter and a substantial portion of the city's recent history from the Quarter's specific vantage point. Contacts in every significant district through relationships built over forty years of supply chain connections, political observation, and the particular social access that comes from being the person who helps feed a city. He has met every significant political figure in Lumencrest personally, at some point, in some context that most of them don't fully remember and that he does.

**Access:** Introduction required. Bram Kettlemarch or Ora Finch must vouch, or the players must have done something concrete that serves the Quarter's interests in a way that has reached Tommas's attention, which it will — he still hears everything. The conversation, when it occurs, will be one of the most information-dense players have in the city, delivered over what appears to be simply a pleasant afternoon discussion at a family compound table with good tea and better biscuits.

## Halfling Quarter Reputation Track

**Starting position:** +1 (Welcome). The Quarter starts players at one step ahead of every other district — the default is genuine welcome. Players must work to lose this, not to gain it.

**Gaining standing:**

- Regular presence at the Copper Kettle or Speakers' Corner over a week: +1
- Demonstrated care for the Quarter's residents in a specific situation: +1
- Helping the Quarter's preparations for crisis in any practical way: +1
- Being vouched for by Bram, Bessa Pipwick, or Ora Finch to someone they introduced: +1
- Action that visibly protects the Quarter's food supply or community infrastructure: +2

**Losing standing:**

- Treating the Quarter's welcome as extraction — taking information or resources without reciprocity: -1 (slow accumulation, hard to reverse)
- Exploiting a Quarter resident's trust or hospitality: -2
- Bringing dangerous official attention to a Quarter resident or establishment: -2
- Violence anywhere in the Quarter: -3 (near-permanent, requiring significant restorative action)

**Reputation unlocks:**

|Standing|What Opens Up|
|---|---|
|+1 (Welcome — default)|All public establishments treat players as genuine guests; Speakers' Corner information available; Bessa Pipwick gives rooms without reservations|
|+2 (Known)|Merry Thatch's back room accessible; delivery network available as couriers; Bram will have a real conversation|
|+3 (Trusted)|Marta's export contacts available; Tommas Wickham accessible; Pip Underwell shares Sable-Seven connection; Ora Finch refers to specific city-wide medical network|
|+4 (Community)|Family compound invitations extended; the Quarter's full cooperative resources available in a crisis; Tommas's full network accessible — the highest social trust available in Lumencrest outside of faction leadership|

## Faction Presence — Halfling Quarter

**The Halfling Quarter Community** — the Quarter is its own faction at the table. The elder network (Bram, Tommas, Pip, and three others not named here) functions as a collective decision-making body for community affairs. They have no formal alliance with any Council faction and this is deliberate — their power rests on being useful to all and obligated to none.

**The Ironwright Collective** — the mutual aid arrangement between the Collective and the Quarter (referenced in [[Factions_and_Reputation]]) is a living relationship: the Quarter provides food support for a work stoppage in exchange for the Collective's protection of the Bakery District's supply routes during any civic disruption. Genna Cradle and Bram Kettlemarch have met three times. The arrangement is real and recent.

**The Free Hand** — present through Pip Underwell's personal connections and through the Kettlefire brewery's visible example. Not operationally active in the Quarter, but supported.

**The Unseen Guild** — has supply chain contacts in the Quarter through the Halfling merchant house that facilitates river transport (referenced in the substances document). The merchant house does not know they are Guild-adjacent. The Quarter's elder network does not know either, which is unusual — this is one of the few things Tommas Wickham has not identified yet.
