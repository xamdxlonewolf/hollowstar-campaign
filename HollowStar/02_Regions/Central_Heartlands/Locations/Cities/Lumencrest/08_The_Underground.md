# The Underground

*This file covers everything beneath and behind the visible surface of Lumencrest — the literal tunnel network under the city, and the metaphorical underground of back-room operations, private clubs, and hidden services. Both meanings are real here and they overlap more than either would prefer.*

---

## Two Meanings, One Name

When Lumencrest residents say "the underground," they usually mean one of two things:

**The Literal Underground** — the tunnel network beneath the city. Sewer mains, infrastructure access tunnels for the Lumen Network, ancient construction passages that predate the city itself, and the things that have moved into those spaces over 1,200 years of a city operating above them. Rats. Refugees. Criminal enterprises. Things the city doesn't officially acknowledge.

**The Figurative Underground** — the network of private clubs, back-room services, off-the-books markets, and hidden arrangements that exist throughout the city above ground but beneath its public surface. The exclusive club with no sign whose entrance is a particular door in a Lamplight Ward alley. The information broker whose office is officially a tailor shop. The fight pit under a Manufactories warehouse that everyone in the district knows about and nobody discusses with city watch.

Both undergrounds are real. Both are dangerous in different ways. Both are accessible, with the right connections and patience. And they connect — the tunnel network and the above-ground hidden world share personnel, logistics, and the fundamental understanding that some things work better when they're not seen.

## The Literal Underground

### The Sewer System

Lumencrest's sewer system is a genuine engineering achievement — a city this large required sophisticated waste management or it would have killed itself with disease within a century. The main sewer mains are wide enough to walk through (barely, hunched, in the larger sections; crawling in the smaller). They are old, mostly stone construction dating back 600-800 years, and they connect everything below the city in a way that no official map perfectly accounts for.

The sewers smell like sewers. They are dark — no Network lighting below the access points — wet, and occupied by things that have adapted to that environment. Navigating them without light and without a guide is genuinely dangerous, not in a threatening-person sense but in a lost-and-the-passages-all-look-the-same sense. People die in Lumencrest's sewers with some regularity, usually because they went in without knowing what they were doing.

**What's down there:** Waste infrastructure (obviously). The maintenance passages of the Lumen Network's older sections — some of the original cable runs that power the city pass through sewer-adjacent tunnels. Emergency access passages to the Vault District's outer perimeter that the Vault Guard monitors carefully (see below). And people — a small community of city residents for whom the surface has become untenable, living in the larger dry chambers of the older sewer sections. The city is aware of them. They've made a calculation that removing them costs more than letting them be.

**Access points:** There are forty-seven known sewer access points across Lumencrest. The city maintains official records of thirty-nine. The other eight are either construction-era access points that were never formally recorded or have been established since without authorization. Shadowside has the highest density of informal access points; the Manufactories district has the most officially maintained ones (maintenance crews need regular access).

**The Vault connection:** The Vault District's underground complex connects to the sewer system at three known points, all of which the Vault Guard monitors with active magical sensing. There may be additional connections through pre-city infrastructure that neither the Guard nor anyone else has fully mapped. This is something the Guard thinks about.

### The Infrastructure Tunnels

Separate from the sewers — cleaner, better maintained, and considerably more restricted — are the tunnels housing the Lumen Network's main cable runs. These are not publicly accessible. They require Network maintenance authorization to enter, are regularly patrolled by Network technicians (and their Clank assistants), and are monitored by the same arcane sensing the Vault Guard uses for the sewer system.

However: the Infrastructure Tunnels run beneath every district. They connect the Vault District to the surface Network nodes across the city. Following them requires knowing the layout, which the city keeps classified, but the general routes can be inferred from where the surface infrastructure is located.

**Why this matters:** Someone with access to the Infrastructure Tunnels could theoretically move unseen between districts below ground. They could also reach nodes in the Network and, with appropriate expertise, affect how power is distributed — including cutting it to specific districts or, in theory, affecting the Heart's output channels.

### Pre-City Passages

The oldest sections of the underground predate Lumencrest's founding. When engineers excavated the Heart's location 1,200 years ago, they found evidence of an earlier civilization's construction beneath the current surface — passages, chambers, worked stone. Some of this was incorporated into the city's foundation infrastructure. Some was sealed. Some was explored and found to lead to nothing identifiable. Some was not explored.

The Luminarium has intermittent academic interest in these passages, but excavation beneath a live city is difficult and the Council has consistently deprioritized it relative to more immediate concerns. What is down there, below the city's own foundations, is genuinely unknown. The Starfall Seekers have a standing proposal to investigate. The Council has tabled it seven times.

## The Figurative Underground

### Access and Navigation

Getting into Lumencrest's above-ground hidden world is a process, not a transaction. You don't walk up to a door and knock and get let in. You spend time in the places where people who know things spend time — the Dark Lantern in Shadowside, the Night Market thoroughfare, the back section of the Night Pharmacy in Lamplight Ward, certain tables at Innovation Row in the Academic Quarter — and you demonstrate over time that you are worth knowing and not a liability. This takes days at minimum, weeks more typically.

Some things can accelerate the process: having a referral from someone already inside the network, having something genuinely useful to offer (a skill, a piece of information, an access that someone needs), or having a reputation that precedes you from somewhere else on the continent.

Some things guarantee you never get in: being city watch (or looking like you might be), asking about things too directly too soon, threatening anyone, or appearing to work for the Council or the Vault Guard in any capacity.

### Hidden Establishments and Services

**The Velvet Room** *(beneath a Lamplight Ward entertainment venue, access through the theatre's loading bay)* — an exclusive private club catering to Lumencrest's wealthy who want experiences not available through any licensed establishment. Membership is not applied for; it's offered. The current waiting list — which no one officially acknowledges exists — is eighteen months long. The establishment offers gambling at stakes that would concern the city's gaming commission if the gaming commission knew where it was, private entertainment of every variety, and the most reliably private conversations available in Lumencrest. No city watch. No recording. No exceptions made for anyone, regardless of political position.

**The Quiet Market** *(mobile, location changes weekly, announced through the Night Market contact network)* — the high-end equivalent of the Night Market, operating in a different location each week and serving clientele who can pay more than the thoroughfare traders receive. Hollostar shard fragments (legal gray area), restricted alchemical substances, information of significant value, stolen items of quality, rare goods from Vossomar via irregular trade routes, exotic creatures. Access requires knowing who to ask and being known by them.

**The Pit** *(beneath the Manufactories, access via a particular warehouse on Factory Row)* — an underground fighting arena that has been operating in various forms for eighty years. Current configuration: a pit roughly forty feet across, tiered viewing for about 200 people, betting at serious stakes, fights of variable rules from bare-knuckle to armed to things that don't have standard rules. The fights are consensual — no one fights who hasn't agreed to — but some of the agreements happen under economic pressure that makes "consensual" a careful word. The Pit is run by a former soldier named Davan Wreck who is very large and very specific about payment and very clear about what happens to people who cause trouble outside of the pit itself.

**The Meridian** *(Academic Quarter, behind Innovation Row, entrance through a research supply shop)* — a private club for academics, researchers, and intellectuals who want to discuss things that cannot be discussed in officially monitored environments. Not illegal in subject matter, necessarily — this is where radical ideas get proposed before they're publishable, where heretical interpretations of the Heart's failure get discussed, where the students and junior faculty who are worried about the classified research programs talk to each other. Monitored by the Council's intelligence operation. The members assume they're monitored and continue anyway, which suggests they've calculated that the value of the conversations outweighs the surveillance risk.

**The Sisterhood of the Silver Thread** *(multiple locations across Lamplight Ward and Shadowside)* — Lumencrest's organized guild of sex workers, operating openly in many respects and less openly in others. The Silver Thread negotiates licensing arrangements with the city (the work is not illegal; the city takes a percentage), maintains a mutual aid fund for members, provides security and medical services, and occasionally operates as an extraordinarily effective intelligence network. The Guild's head is a half-elf woman who goes by the name Calla who has held the position for twelve years and who the Council's intelligence operation treats with considerable caution. The Silver Thread knows things. It has always known things. This is structural — the clientele talks, and the Silver Thread remembers.

**The Free Hand** *(cell structure, no fixed location)* — the Clank liberation network, the most politically serious of Lumencrest's underground organizations. The Free Hand helps escaped Clanks reach Shadowside and eventually the city's underground for passage out, connects them with the removal service for arcane implants (see below), and advocates — carefully, through the Student Assembly and academic channels — for legal reform. The Free Hand is not violent. It is patient. It has been operating for twenty years. The Council knows it exists and has not been able to meaningfully disrupt it because it has no fixed structure to disrupt.

**The Remover** *(one person, contacted through the Free Hand or specific Night Market connections)* — a former Luminarium research assistant who left the institution thirty years ago and has since developed the only reliable method in Lumencrest for removing a Clank's arcane identification implant without killing them. The procedure is expensive (80 gold marks — more than most Clanks can accumulate in their service term), painful, and requires three sessions over two weeks. The Remover's identity is not publicly known. Their contact is reached through a specific sequence of Night Market conversations that takes about a week for someone starting from scratch. They have never been caught. They have removed approximately 400 implants. They keep count.

## The Tunnel Network as Adventure Space

For GMs: the underground tunnel network beneath Lumencrest is playable space with its own logic and hazards.

**Navigation:** Moving through the sewers without a guide requires a DC 14 Instinct roll every 30 minutes to avoid getting turned around. With a guide or a reliable map, this becomes automatic.

**Hazards:** Structural instability in older sections, toxic gas pockets in the sewer mains (rare but possible, especially in sealed older sections), territorial creatures in the deep older passages, and the very real risk of running into Vault Guard patrols near the underground complex access points.

**The Deep Passages:** The pre-city sections beneath the city's foundations are unmapped. Anything could be down there. This is intentional — leave it as a campaign discovery space.

**Key underground routes:**
- Shadowside to Lamplight Ward: Easy via main sewer (20 minutes, low risk)
- Shadowside to Manufactories: Easy via main sewer (25 minutes, low risk)  
- Lamplight Ward to Academic Quarter: Moderate via infrastructure-adjacent tunnels (35 minutes, requires finding access)
- Any district to Vault District perimeter: Hard (Vault Guard monitoring, narrow passages, arcane detection)
- Any district to the actual Heart chamber: Extremely hard — the city has spent considerable effort ensuring this is not practically possible. It may not be.
