# Lamplight Ward — Expanded District Guide

## Points of Interest, Vendors, and NPCs

_This document expands on Lamplight Ward as established in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]], adding the locations, people, and daily texture needed to run the district at the table. Lamplight Ward is Lumencrest's commercial and social center — the place where the city's middle class lives, works, spends money, and is currently beginning to notice that the comfortable arrangement they built their lives around may not be as stable as they thought. Cross-reference [[03_Halfling_Quarter]] for the Ward's southern food supply relationships, [[06_Academic_Quarter]] for the professional class that bleeds between both districts, and [[Controlled_Substances_Trade]] for the Night Pharmacy's full operational context._

---

## Running Lamplight Ward at the Table

Lamplight Ward is the district where the city's surface appearance is most convincing — and where the cracks in that appearance are most visible to anyone paying attention, because the surface was so carefully maintained that every chip shows.

**The look:** Wide streets, electric amber lamps every twenty feet, four-story stone buildings with commercial ground floors and residential above. Window boxes maintained with competitive pride. The cable car overhead, three stops running through the district's spine. The electric fountain at Merchant Square always on. At night, the Entertainment Quarter's custom lamp installations adding colors the standard Network fittings don't produce. It looks like a wealthy, functional city at the height of its confidence.

**The reality:** The eastern stretch of amber lamps has been unreliable for weeks and the repair queue is backed up. The factory-supply shop that closed two months ago on Merchant Square's east side has a FOR LEASE notice that no one has answered. At the Long Table and the Accord Arms common room, the conversations drop quieter when someone mentions the Council, or the brownouts, or what the Manufactories slowdown means for the supply of things the Ward's shops sell. The prosperous middle class's relationship with anxiety is newer than the Manufactories workers' relationship with it, but it is arriving.

**The day/night split:** More pronounced here than anywhere else in the city. The Ward during the day is commercial and purposeful — people going to work, shopping, conducting the business that makes the city function. The Ward at night is something else: the Entertainment Quarter at its fullest, the Night Pharmacy at its busiest, the Copperstring's upper floor full, Volta's at peak operation, the all-night taverns holding the city's insomniacs and shift-workers and people who simply prefer the world after midnight. Lamplight Ward is one of the few places in Lumencrest where a brownout at 2am affects a crowd rather than empty streets.

**The political temperature:** The Ward's residents were, as a demographic, the Illuminist faction's strongest base — they believed in the technical solution, they believed in the competence of the people running the Network, and they are now beginning a slow, uncomfortable process of revising that belief. This creates a specific social texture: people who are used to having faith in something trying to figure out how to be people who don't, without quite being willing to say that's what they're doing. The Long Table's political conversations are excellent evidence of this in real time.

## Merchant Square and Surroundings

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here as a full playable location)_

**The Square itself:** An open plaza large enough for a thousand people, usually at half that. The electric fountain at the center — a permanent fixture that the city would no more turn off than it would turn off the cable car, because the fountain is what Merchant Square means and Merchant Square is what the Ward means. The sound of the water is audible above the crowd at most hours. Players arriving in the Square for the first time should hear the fountain before they see it.

**The street food market:** Along the Square's northern edge, dawn to midnight. Forty-odd vendors on a good day, rotating with licenses and weather. The licensed stands have permanent fixtures; the unlicensed temporary ones cluster at the market's edges and are tolerated because the market is better with them in it. Quality ranges widely. Three vendors are worth knowing:

- _Osso's Meat Skewers_ — a dwarf named Osso who has been in the same corner of the northern edge for twenty-two years. Pork and vegetable skewers, 2 copper marks each, consistently excellent. The queue is always there. It moves.
- _The Grain Table_ — the Academic Quarter's lunch solution, a stall selling grain-based preparations with fresh herbs and preserved proteins at 3 copper marks a serving. Run by a rotating pair of Halfling Quarter suppliers who treat the Ward location as their premium outlet.
- _Lira's Sweet Things_ — a human woman who makes small confections from a cart and has a specific and reliable lineup of regulars who time their Merchant Square errands around when she arrives. 1 copper mark for most items. Worth the detour.

**The Guildhall Quarter:** Immediately south of Merchant Square. The major professional guilds maintain their halls here — the Merchant Guild's hall is the largest building on the south side, four stories of deliberate architectural authority. The Craft Guild is directly adjacent, newer but equally substantial. The Artificers' Guild occupies a narrower building with an extraordinary electric sign above the door that cycles through color sequences and that represents, in miniature, everything the Artificers' Guild is proud of. The Scribes' Association is half a block east and half the size, which reflects the relative economic power of written documentation versus manufactured goods, a fact the Scribes find galling.

### The Merchant Guild Hall

**What it is:** The most powerful economic organization in Lamplight Ward with enough influence on the Council to make it one of the most politically significant organizations in the city. The Guild licenses merchants, mediates commercial disputes, maintains the Ward's commercial standards, and represents merchant interests in Council deliberations. Membership is effectively required for any significant commercial operation in the Ward — the Guild's seal on a storefront means something, and the absence of it means something too.

**Current situation:** The Guild is nervous. The manufacturing slowdowns in the Manufactories are reducing the supply of goods the Ward's shops sell. The power brownouts are affecting commercial operating hours in ways that the Guild cannot quantify cleanly but can feel in transaction volumes. The Guild's leadership — specifically its current Chair, **Aldous Wren** (see NPCs) — has been to the Council twice in the past month. Both times the response was acknowledgment without commitment.

**For players:** The Merchant Guild is not a villain. It is an organization of people who have built their livelihoods around the city's commercial stability and are watching that stability develop questions. Guild members — the merchants and traders who use the hall for dispute resolution, licensing, and networking — are some of the best sources of information about the city's economic reality: what's not arriving on supply chains, what prices are doing, what the commercial districts in other cities are saying about Lumencrest.

**Getting inside:** The hall's public rooms — the licensing office, the dispute board, the common room — are accessible to anyone with commercial business. The upper floor, where the Guild's leadership conducts its more sensitive discussions, requires membership or an escort. The Guild employs a door assessor named **Merric** (human, 30s, impeccably dressed, extremely good at his job) who can be navigated around through membership credentials, a convincing professional context, or a Guild member willing to sign someone in.

**The Guild Chair:** **Aldous Wren** — see NPCs.

### The Accord Arms

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Merchant Square's north side, facing the fountain. The best-positioned inn in the Ward.

**What it is:** The Ward's most reputable inn and the standard recommendation for visitors who want reliable quality without the Radiant Core's prices. Forty rooms across four floors — twelve basic rooms (2 gold marks per night), twenty standard rooms (3 gold marks), eight premium rooms with private bathrooms and hot water (5 gold marks). The common room operates until 4am and serves food throughout. The staff are professional in a way that doesn't tip into coldness — they have seen everything that passes through a major city's best inn and they respond to all of it with equable competence.

**What it actually provides:**

- The most reliable beds in the Ward — the mattresses are real, the linens are laundered on a schedule, the rooms are maintained.
- The common room's 24-hour food service is one of the better kitchens in the Ward. Not the most exciting cooking, but consistently good: roasted meats, grain dishes, the Halfling Quarter bread that arrives each morning from the delivery network.
- The premium bathroom situation is worth the extra gold for anyone who has been traveling, sleeping in the Underground, or spending time in the Manufactories.
- **Information:** The Accord Arms' common room is one of the best passive intelligence-gathering locations in the city. Guild members, visiting merchants from other Accord cities, Council clerks eating lunch on the city's account, Academic Quarter researchers who prefer the Ward's noise to the Quarter's quiet — they all pass through. The staff know who is staying and will not say. The conversations at the common tables are less guarded than they should be.

**The proprietor:** **Joss Aldeth** — see NPCs.

### The Long Table

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** A street off Merchant Square's east side, between the Square and the Guildhall Quarter. Identifiable by the long table visible through the front window, because the interior is organized around a single communal table that seats thirty for dinner service.

**What it is:** The Ward's political tavern — not in the partisan sense, but in the sense that the people who run the city's administrative and commercial middle layer eat here, and eat together at the long table, and talk. The format is deliberate: the long communal table for dinner means that a Council clerk, a Guild merchant, a senior Craft Guild member, and a visiting Academic Quarter professor might share a meal without having chosen to share a meal, and this produces conversations that a booth arrangement would not.

**The breakfast and lunch service** runs in private booths — the communal table is specifically dinner. Players who want to talk to a specific person privately should aim for breakfast or lunch. Players who want to observe Lamplight Ward's professional class in its natural state should come for dinner.

**What's on offer:**

- _Dinner:_ The slow-roasted meat the base file references — a proper long-cook preparation that the kitchen starts at midday. Changes daily. Always good. 6-8 copper marks for the full dinner service at the communal table, which includes bread and a side grain preparation.
- _Halfling Quarter ales:_ The best selection available in the Ward outside of a dedicated ale house. Copperkettle Amber on tap. Two rotating craft ales from Brewers' Lane. The hop gin for those who want it.
- _Breakfast and lunch in booths:_ Lighter offerings — grain porridge, eggs, cold preparations, the morning bread from Halfling Quarter delivery. 2-4 copper marks.
- _Wine:_ A short but considered list, sourced through the Green Hearth cooperative's wine merchant contact. Better than the Ward's other options at comparable pricing.

**The political conversations:** Players who eat at the communal dinner table will hear, within a single evening: the current Guild position on the brownout situation, what the Council said at last week's session that wasn't in the official summary, at least one piece of information about the Manufactories that hasn't reached the Ironwright Collective yet, and the social dynamics of three or four professional relationships that tell them something useful about who in this district trusts whom. None of this is secret. It is simply the conversation of people who know things, eating together.

**The proprietor:** **Senna Mourne** — see NPCs.

### The Artificers' Guild and Legitimate Weapon and Equipment Supply

**Location:** The Guildhall Quarter, narrow building with the electric cycling sign.

**What it is:** Lumencrest's licensed artificers — the professionals who design, maintain, and certify electrical and mechanical equipment. The Guild's hall contains a small licensed shop on the ground floor that sells certified equipment, tools, and weaponry to the public. The word "certified" carries weight in a city where uncertified Network equipment has been implicated in three fires in the past five years.

**What the shop stocks:**

- Standard adventuring equipment at Guild-certified quality: packs, rope, tools, lanterns, climbing equipment. 110-120% of base prices in exchange for quality guarantee and the Guild seal.
- Weapons: the Artificers' Guild maintains a relationship with two licensed smiths on the Wire Road. Basic weapons available here; more specialized pieces available through the smiths directly or by order through the Guild.
- Electrical equipment: devices that interface with the Lumen Network, maintenance tools for Network-connected fixtures, the specialty items that travelers from non-Network cities come to Lumencrest specifically to acquire. The Guild is the only legitimate source for some of these.
- Custom orders: placed here and fulfilled by Guild artisans on a 3-10 day timeline depending on complexity. The Guild's artisans are the best in the city for electrical and mechanical work. The prices reflect this.

**The licensed smiths on the Wire Road — Halvern and Stoke:** Two independent smiths, both Guild members, operating side-by-side on the Wire Road. **Halvern** (dwarf, 60s) makes conventional weapons of exceptional quality — his work is the standard against which the Ward's other smiths are measured. **Stoke** (human, 40s) specializes in equipment that interfaces with the Lumen Network — specifically weapons with integrated electrical components, for which there is a specific and not-small market in Lumencrest given the Network's presence. Stoke's work is unusual enough that it attracts Academic Quarter researchers who want to understand it and Vault Guard officers who want to acquire it.

## The Entertainment Quarter

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

The Entertainment Quarter is Lamplight Ward's eastern edge running south from the main cable car line — roughly eight blocks of Lumencrest at its most alive after dark. During the day it is half-dormant: the venues' staff cleaning and restocking, the daytime businesses (two pharmacies, several food shops, a tailor who does formal wear for the Theatre's performers) going about their work without the nighttime crowd. After dark it is one of the most densely peopled stretches of the city.

---

### The Lamplight Theatre

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** The Entertainment Quarter's centerpiece — a proper purpose-built theatre with a façade that has been updated three times and each update has been an improvement. The electric marquee above the entrance lists the current production and has been doing so since the Network reached this district.

**What it is:** Lumencrest's most prestigious cultural venue. Four hundred seats in tiered arrangement — back gallery accessible at 3 copper marks (sight lines are acceptable), mid-tier at 8 copper marks, front tier at 2 silver marks, and six private boxes for 1 gold mark each. The boxes are the way to attend the Theatre if you have Radiant Core business to conduct in a setting that suggests cultural investment rather than a meeting room. They are booked weeks in advance and the waiting list includes three Council members and Mira Voss, who attends approximately twice a year under a different name.

**The company:** A permanent company of twelve players supplemented by touring acts. The permanent company performs the repertoire — classics, civic commissions, the occasional piece by a local writer who has gotten something past the theatre's director. The touring acts perform the unusual and the new. The intersection of these two is where the Theatre's best work happens.

**The candlelight production:** The brownout-driven candlelight performance three months ago has become a reference point in Lamplight Ward's cultural conversation. The audience stayed. The cast improvised. The reviews were the best the Theatre has received in a decade, which the Theatre's director, **Willa Casp** (see NPCs), has very complicated feelings about. She is proud of her company's performance and troubled by what it means that the Theatre was more honest without the infrastructure it was built to depend on.

**Backstage:** The Theatre's backstage is a working environment with its own social world — the permanent company lives in this building more than they live anywhere else, and the relationships within it are the kind that form when talented people spend most of their waking hours in close quarters. Players who develop a backstage connection (through the director, the lead players, or the backstage staff) will find a community that knows the Ward's nightlife and its social landscape from the specific perspective of people who perform for it nightly and observe it from the stage.

### The Copperstring

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Two streets north of the Theatre, identifiable by the copper-strung instrument hanging above the door and the music audible from the street most nights after 9pm.

**What it is:** A music venue and tavern operating on two floors with a genuine reputation for discovering talent — the city's better musicians have played the Copperstring's upper stage before anyone else booked them, and the acts that appear here now are the ones that will be playing larger venues in two years. The ground floor is a tavern that runs all night and draws a mixed crowd: Entertainment Quarter regulars, Theatre overflow, Lamplight Ward residents who prefer the Copperstring's energy to the Long Table's conversation, and people from other districts who come specifically for the music.

**What's on offer:** _Ground floor tavern:_

- Ale, spirits, wine at Entertainment Quarter pricing (slightly above Ward standard, below Radiant Core). The quality is genuine — the Copperstring's beverage buyers know their business.
- Food until midnight: a small menu of things that work in a noisy room — substantial, portable, not requiring cutlery. Meat rolls, fried preparations, the Halfling Quarter's export cheese plates served with fruit.
- The ground floor is all standing and high tables. No seating arrangements — the energy requires standing.

_Upper floor venue:_

- Seated, 80 people at capacity. A proper stage with the city's best regular sound design — the Artificers' Guild did the installation three years ago and the acoustic engineering is extraordinary for a room of this size.
- Entry: 2 copper marks on regular nights, 8 copper on featured act nights. Worth either price.
- A bar at the back of the upper floor, table service to seats, slightly premium pricing because the experience justifies it.

**The talent:** The Copperstring's current regular act, **Senna the Clear** (see NPCs — distinct from Senna Mourne at the Long Table, unfortunate name collision), performs Wednesday and Saturday nights and has been drawing crowds for six months. Their work is the kind that people describe to each other in terms of what it does to them rather than what it sounds like, which is the description of something genuinely good.

**The proprietor:** **Bram Hollowthorn** — see NPCs. Not related to Bram Kettlemarch. The Bram coincidence has been noted.

### The Night Pharmacy

_(Referenced across multiple documents — full expanded entry here)_

**Location:** The Entertainment Quarter's south end, near the Theatre. Blue lamp above the door, lit during operating hours (24 hours). The only establishment in the city that never closes.

**What it is:** The Night Pharmacy is one of the most carefully constructed fictions in Lumencrest: a licensed apothecary that is also the city's most useful pharmaceutical gray market, operated by a gnome who has been here for forty years and who has been simultaneously indispensable to city watch intelligence and operating a back-counter substance trade that city watch has decided not to look at directly. The arrangement works because Tessiel is genuinely useful to the watch, because the Night Pharmacy's existence makes the Ward safer rather than less safe (people who need things at 2am go to Tessiel rather than elsewhere), and because the alternative — disrupting the arrangement — would cost more in watch resources and social stability than it gains in legal consistency.

**The front counter (24 hours):**

- Standard pharmaceutical preparations for everything that a city's population of 80,000 needs at any hour: pain, fever, infection, sleep, the morning-after preparations that sell particularly well near the Entertainment Quarter
- Wound treatment supplies — the Night Pharmacy is where the city's late-night injury cases go before they become serious
- Contraception, reliably available, no questions asked, every night
- Equipment: bandages, suture kits, the basic medical supply chain
- Referrals: Tessiel maintains relationships with the city's licensed physicians and will, at any hour, connect someone who needs more care than she can provide with whoever can be reached

**The back counter (established customers, correct approach):**

- Fade — clean Guild supply, established customers only, she keeps records that no one has yet successfully obtained
- Velvetongue — the primary licensed-adjacent retail source for the Ward and Core social class
- Extended pain preparations beyond standard dispensing
- Specific preparations that exist in gray legal territory because the city's classification system hasn't caught up with the chemistry
- Information about the city's substance market, at a price, calibrated to what the person asking can offer in return

**What Tessiel knows:** More about what is chemically moving through Lumencrest than anyone except possibly Merry Thatch, and with a different kind of access — her picture is cross-district where Merry's is neighborhood-level. She knows the Fade dependency profile in the Academic Quarter by cohort. She knows what the Manufactories workers are using and in what volumes. She knows when a new substance has appeared on the market before any official body does because people bring their reactions to her. This knowledge is available, at her price, which is information in return rather than money — she has enough money.

**The proprietor:** **Tessiel Wane** — see NPCs.

### Volta's

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** A converted factory floor on the Entertainment Quarter's eastern edge — the largest single entertainment venue in Lumencrest by floor area. The building's exterior is deliberately unassuming, which is a choice; inside is not.

**What it is:** The city's most sophisticated entertainment establishment, operating in the space between the fully public and the specifically private with the ease of something that has been doing it long enough to have perfected the approach. The main floor is a dance hall with an electrical lighting system that is the work of the Artificers' Guild's most ambitious installation — color-shifting, programmed sequences, responsive to the music. The effect is, for people who haven't seen it, startling. For people who have, it is the reason they come back.

**The main floor:** Open from 10pm to 4am. Entry: 1 silver mark on standard nights, 2 silver marks on programmed event nights. The programming — specific theme nights with specific lighting sequences — runs twice a week and is announced the day before. The door policy is: dressed appropriately for the venue (the dress code is enforced by someone named **Petra** who is very large and very good at her job), not visibly impaired at entry, and not on the short list of people who are not welcome. The short list has eleven names and Petra knows all of them by face.

**What the main floor offers:**

- The lights, which are the reason
- Live music on a proper stage — the acts are booked for their energy rather than their artistry, which is the correct booking criterion for a dance floor
- A bar that runs the full length of the room's east wall, twelve staff, fast service, Entertainment Quarter pricing
- A specific cocktail list that Volta's developed over twenty years that is better than any individual item suggests and that the bar manager is quietly proud of

**The back rooms:** Accessed through a door beside the main bar, marked only with a stylized V. The back rooms offer private experiences: music in quieter configurations, more intimate social spaces, and the full-service adult entertainment that the base file references — fully legal under the Silver Thread's licensing framework, staffed by people who are compensated well and chose this work. The back rooms are not a secret. Volta's does not pretend they don't exist. The pricing is accordingly specific and the quality is consistent with an establishment that runs on reputation.

**The Silver Thread connection:** Volta's adult entertainment staff are Silver Thread members, which means their working conditions, safety standards, and income protections are Guild-maintained. Calla has a relationship with Volta's management that is not ownership — Volta's operates independently — but is an understanding. The Silver Thread's Lamplight Ward operations are more visible than the Shadowside house and more integrated into the district's social fabric. Volta's is the visible end of this.

**The proprietor:** **Volta** (see NPCs). First name only, by deliberate choice and long-standing practice.

### The Eastwick Cooperative

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Eastwick neighborhood, eastern side of Lamplight Ward. The cooperative's meeting hall occupies the ground floor of a building that is otherwise residential — families above, the Cooperative's operations below.

**What it is:** A fifty-year-old mutual aid organization that began as a social club for Eastwick's residential community and has been quietly and deliberately expanding its actual function for the past two years in response to what its chair has been observing. The Cooperative maintains the shared larder in the building's basement. It runs a needs-and-resources matching service for members experiencing economic disruption from the manufacturing slowdowns. It has, in the past six months, begun stocking beyond food — medical supplies, candles, lamp oil, the things a residential neighborhood needs if the Network becomes unreliable for an extended period. None of this has been publicly announced. The Eastwick community knows it's happening.

**The meeting hall:** Open to members — Eastwick residents, defined broadly by the Cooperative's chair as anyone who lives within four blocks and has registered, which is a low bar deliberately. Meetings twice monthly. A small library of practical reference materials. The larder door, which is always locked, which members understand is locked because what's inside represents significant collective investment and the precautionary value of that investment depends on it being intact.

**The chair:** **Brinne Halloway** — see NPCs. One of the most quietly effective people in this district.

## Eastwick Neighborhood

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

**What it is:** The residential neighborhood east of Merchant Square, between the commercial Ward and the Silverrun River. Quieter, family-focused, built around the assumption of three-generation residence. Currently showing the earliest signs of crisis stress in the Ward — the eastern lamp stretch has been unreliable for weeks, which in a residential area means children doing homework by candle, families managing evening routines around the dark patches, the specific low-level daily irritation that accumulates into something political.

**The Wren House Boarding House:** _(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_ A converted residential building run as a boarding house by the Aldeth family for three generations. Seven rooms, long-stay preferred, community feel. 6 silver per night, 3 gold per week. Guests eat breakfast with the family — a real family breakfast, not a service, in the kitchen of a house that has been welcoming paying guests since Joss Aldeth's grandmother opened the rooms. The specific quality the Wren House offers that no other accommodation in the Ward does: you become part of the Eastwick neighborhood within days, because you are living in it, eating in it, moving through it with a family who has been doing the same for three generations.

**Lida's Bake:** _(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_ Open from 3am to mid-afternoon, because the baking starts at 3am and Lida sees no reason to separate the bakery from the storefront. The bread is the bread that Lumencrest tastes like — a specific sourdough culture that Lida has been maintaining for twenty-three years. The rolls, the coffee, the fact that you can stand at the counter at 4am and be handed something just out of the oven — Lida's is what the Ward's nighttime population stops at before going home or before continuing. A pot of the strongest coffee in the Ward and a warm roll is 3 copper marks. **Lida Birch** (see NPCs) is the kind of person who knows the neighborhood at 4am specifically, which is its own kind of knowing.

## Copper Gate Sub-District

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

**What it is:** The Ward's southern edge, immediately inside the main south gate. The unofficial welcome zone: inns calibrated for newly arrived visitors, currency exchange, tour guides of variable quality. The best thing about Copper Gate is that it exists — the worst thing is that it's where everyone arrives and forms their first impression of Lumencrest.

**What experienced residents know:** Copper Gate's services are functional rather than excellent. But there are two establishments worth knowing:

**The Traveller's Mark Inn:** The most reputable of the four Copper Gate inns. Twenty rooms, clean, professional. 1 gold mark per night. The proprietor, **Henris Callow** (see NPCs), has been running it for fifteen years and has developed the specific skills of someone whose clientele is entirely arriving strangers: accurate assessment of what people need, efficient provision of it, and the particular ability to tell within a conversation whether someone is a straightforward traveller or someone whose presence in Lumencrest is going to be complicated. He does not report the latter to city watch. He does quietly ensure they know the Ward's geography and significant contact points, on the principle that people who know where things are cause less collateral disruption than people who don't.

**The Welcome Gate — Information and Tour Services:** One of the licensed information services operating in the Ward, the Welcome Gate explicitly targets newly arrived visitors. For 2 silver marks, a guided introduction to Lumencrest's public geography and services. For 5 silver marks, a full day's guide. The guides are variable — three are genuinely knowledgeable, two are adequate, one is actively misleading about which money exchange offers the best rates and why. **Tomm** (see NPCs), the best guide on staff, will tell you in the first ten minutes which establishments to trust and which not to, including this one's money exchange referral, which is the mark of someone who has decided that a reputation for honesty is a better long-term business strategy than a referral fee.

### The Wire Road Smiths — Halvern and Stoke

**Location:** Wire Road, three blocks east of Merchant Square. The two shops share a wall and have been doing so for twelve years, which has produced the specific complex relationship of neighbors who respect each other, compete professionally, and have each other over for dinner approximately once a month.

**Halvern's:** Conventional weapons and quality metalwork. The dwarf's work is the standard the Ward's other smiths measure themselves against. Commission work: 1-2 weeks depending on complexity. Custom requests are quoted after a brief conversation about what the commission involves — Halvern won't make a weapon he wouldn't make for himself, which means quality is guaranteed and a small number of specific applications are declined without drama.

**Stoke's:** Electrically-integrated equipment and weapons — the specialty that no one else in the Ward does reliably. Weapons with integrated electrical components, tools that interface with Network infrastructure, the specific category of devices that exist at the intersection of conventional craft and Lumencrest's electrical technology. More expensive than Halvern's equivalent conventional work. Delivery times range from three days for simple modifications to three weeks for custom work. Stoke will explain what he's doing if asked, in detail, because he considers his work more interesting than most of his customers initially think it is.

### The Halfling Imports Restaurant

_(Referenced in [[02_Lamplight_Ward]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** The Ward-Quarter border street, technically just inside Lamplight Ward's southern boundary.

**What it is:** The best food at the best price in the Ward, operated by the Halfling Quarter community as their Lamplight Ward retail presence. The kitchen is staffed by Quarter residents. The produce comes from the Quarter's supply networks. The menu is Halfling cooking at its most careful, presented in a room designed for Lamplight Ward's higher foot-traffic context — larger than a Quarter establishment, faster service, but the food's quality unchanged.

**What's on offer:**

- The full Halfling culinary range: stews, roasts, the preserved-and-roasted preparations, grain dishes with herb preparations, the pastries and sweets that the Ward's dessert market has developed a dependence on
- The Quarter's ales on tap, which at this location are consistently fresher than they'd be at any Ward establishment that sources through a distributor
- A daily special board that reflects whatever was best in the Quarter's market that morning
- 3-7 copper marks for main dishes. This pricing is the reason the room is always full.

**The manager:** **Soren Halfmarch** (Halfling, 40s) — Marta Copperkettle-Brum's cousin by marriage, which explains the direct supply line. He runs the Ward location with the specific professionalism of someone who knows his establishment's success depends on maintaining the Quarter's trust and the Ward's custom simultaneously.

### Calla's Establishment — Silver Thread, Lamplight Ward

_(The Silver Thread's primary licensed location — referenced across documents)_

**Location:** A building on the Entertainment Quarter's quieter northern edge, presented as a private social club. The signage is tasteful and minimal. The door has a quality of quiet authority.

**What it is:** The Silver Thread's primary Lamplight Ward operation — licensed, visible, operating fully within the city's legal framework for adult entertainment. Where the Shadowside house operates in Shadowside's specific gray arrangement, this establishment has proper licensing, pays the city's percentage, and is accordingly able to operate with a transparency that the Shadowside house cannot. The clientele here is the Ward's professional and wealthy class; the working conditions, safety standards, and income arrangements for the staff are Silver Thread guild standards throughout.

**The intelligence function:** This location's clientele talks more than they should because the professional atmosphere creates a misapprehension about what is and isn't being heard. Calla's intelligence function runs here as effectively as it runs anywhere in her network. The specific intelligence this location produces — the anxieties and plans and relationships of the Ward and Core's professional and wealthy class — is the most politically valuable in the network.

**Calla herself:** Rarely present in person. Her Lamplight Ward designate — a woman named **Shan Relle** (see NPCs) who has run this location for four years — manages day-to-day operations and intelligence reporting. Calla appears when she has specific reason to be here, which is occasional and not announced in advance.

### Tessiel's Information Service — The Back Counter

_(This is an expansion of the Night Pharmacy's intelligence function, given its significance)_

**How it works:** Players who want to buy information from Tessiel rather than pharmaceuticals need to approach correctly. She will not respond to a direct request for information about the city. She will respond to a specific, demonstrably genuine question about something she might know, accompanied by something she demonstrably doesn't. The exchange is calibrated: the value of what she gives matches the value of what she receives, in her assessment, and her assessment is accurate.

**What she can provide:**

- The current substance market's supply and distribution picture, district by district
- The dependency profiles of specific social or professional classes in the city — not named individuals, aggregate patterns that are specific enough to be actionable
- What is changing in the market — new substances appearing, existing supply chains disrupted, the first signs of something moving before it becomes visible elsewhere
- Specific confirmation or denial of whether a named person is a known substance user — she will not give this unsolicited but will confirm or deny with sufficient reason, and her records are accurate

**Her price:** She takes information in return. Not money. The value exchange is: something she doesn't know for something you need to know. Players who have been moving through the city's less visible layers will accumulate information that has value to someone who sits in one location watching what comes through the door. The calibration is hers and it is fair.

### Tessiel Wane

**Role:** Night Pharmacy proprietor; de facto intelligence hub for Lumencrest's substance trade **Ancestry/Age:** Gnome, appears 60s. Actual age is a question the city has stopped asking. **Location:** The Night Pharmacy, Entertainment Quarter. She has not left the building in more than a few hours at any stretch in four years, which her staff finds either admirable or concerning.

**Appearance:** Small in the way of gnomes, with white hair cut practically short and the specific quality of someone who is paying attention to more than what is in front of them at any given moment. Her clothing is functional — a pharmacist's apron over practical clothes — and she wears a specific pair of magnifying lenses around her neck that are used for close work and pushed up to her forehead when not in use. Her hands are precise in the way of someone who has been handling small quantities of significant substances for forty years and has developed an accuracy that is slightly beyond what hands normally achieve.

**Personality:** Economical in speech in a way that is not unfriendliness but efficiency. She charges for what she provides because she has assessed that the pricing discipline keeps the quality of exchange high — people who pay a real price ask a real question rather than fishing. She is not warm but she is fair, and she has a specific quality of respect for people who approach her honestly about what they need that produces a version of the interaction that is almost warm.

**What she knows:** The city's pharmaceutical and substance landscape more completely than anyone except possibly the Unseen Guild's logistics operation, and with a qualitative understanding of effects and consequences that the Guild's distribution focus doesn't produce. She has been tracking Hollostar Dust's supply disruption since before it became visible as a crisis — she noticed the quality change in what patients were bringing reactions to her six months before the price spike hit. She has three theories about the supply disruption. One of them is correct. She has not shared any of them.

**Working with her:** The information exchange model means that players need to come to her with something before they leave with something. The most valuable things players are likely to have that Tessiel doesn't: information about the Shard Division's operations, the tunnel passage the Dust supply uses, the identity of the arcane practitioners who activate Dust, and any intelligence about the Vault Guard's current Dust enforcement strategy that isn't public record. These are things she cannot observe from the Night Pharmacy and that she would trade very significant information for.

### Aldous Wren

**Role:** Merchant Guild Chair; the Ward's most visible establishment figure **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 57 **Location:** The Merchant Guild Hall most days. The Long Table for dinner most evenings.

**Appearance:** The specific appearance of a successful merchant who has been running a significant organization for fifteen years — well-dressed in the professional sense rather than the aristocratic, with the quality of someone whose physical presentation is a professional decision rather than a personal one. Grey hair, well-maintained. The posture of someone accustomed to being listened to.

**Personality:** Intelligent and genuinely concerned in a way that his position limits his ability to express. He is not a villain — he is a man who has built his professional life around the stability of the city's commercial system and is watching that system develop structural problems that he cannot fix from where he sits. He has been to the Council twice. Both times the response was acknowledgment without commitment. He is beginning to form a private opinion about what this means for the city's trajectory that he has not yet said out loud.

**What he wants:** Commercial stability, which in the current context means the power situation resolved in a way that doesn't destroy the manufacturing supply chain. He is not naïve about the probability of this. He is realistic about it in a way that has made his evenings at the Long Table more politically substantive than they used to be.

**What he knows:** The Ward's commercial reality at the operational level — what the Guild's transaction volume has done over the past two years (declining), what the supply chain disruptions look like from the merchant side (significant), what the visiting merchants from other Accord cities are saying about Lumencrest's commercial reputation (worse than the Council's public position acknowledges). He also knows specific things about the Council members' commercial interests — which faction members have financial exposure to the manufacturing sector, which ones have been quietly moving assets out of Network-dependent businesses — that he has not published and that would be significant if they became public.

**Working with him:** He will be professionally courteous with players who have legitimate commercial context and increasingly forthcoming with players who demonstrate that they're engaged with the city's actual situation rather than its official version. He is not a contact who will take dramatic action — his position requires him to work within the system. He is, however, a contact who can legitimately open doors to Guild resources, commercial intelligence, and the political layer of the Ward's professional community.

### Willa Casp

**Role:** Director of the Lamplight Theatre **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 44 **Location:** The Theatre, most of the time. Occasionally the Copperstring upper floor when she is not working, which is infrequent.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has been running a creative organization since her early thirties — perpetually in motion, perpetually thinking about something slightly adjacent to what she's currently doing. Her clothing is good but worn in a way that suggests the Theatre matters more than the clothing, which is true. She has the quality of attention of someone who watches people for a living and has developed the director's specific ability to understand what a person is doing versus what they think they're doing.

**What she wants:** The Theatre to be what she believes it can be, which is something more honest and more significant than what it has been comfortable being. The power crisis is, in a complicated way, useful to her ambitions — it is forcing an authenticity that the Theatre's established success had been avoiding.

**What she knows:** The Ward's cultural and social community at depth — the performers, the audience, the people who use the Theatre for social positioning and the people who come because it matters to them. She is one of the better reads of the Ward's social landscape because she watches it from the stage, which is a specific vantage point. She also has, through the touring acts' connections, a picture of what is happening in the cultural communities of other Accord cities that includes information about political and social situations in those cities that doesn't reach Lumencrest through official channels.

### Volta

**Role:** Proprietor of Volta's **Ancestry/Age:** Half-elf, appears 40s. Has appeared to be in their 40s for approximately fifteen years, which people have stopped remarking on. **Location:** Volta's, Entertainment Quarter. Present most nights from 9pm.

**Appearance:** The deliberate presentation of someone whose establishment is partly about atmosphere and who understands that the proprietor is part of the atmosphere. Tall, precisely dressed, with the specific quality of being entirely comfortable in every corner of their own space. Moves through the main floor with a quality of greeting that makes everyone they make eye contact with feel specifically welcomed without anyone feeling specifically singled out. This is a skill that cannot be taught.

**Personality:** Charming in the genuine sense — the warmth is real, not performed, which is what makes it effective. Volta is highly intelligent about people and about what people need from an evening at their establishment, and they provide it with a precision that is invisible because it is natural to them. They are also, behind the warmth, someone who has been running a complex entertainment operation for twenty years and who has a very clear sense of the line between what their establishment does and what it doesn't, and who enforces it without drama.

**What they want:** Volta's to continue being exactly what it is — which requires the city to remain stable enough for the entertainment industry to function, which means Volta has more investment in Lumencrest's survival than their nightclub proprietorship would suggest. They have been watching the power situation with the specific concern of someone who has an electrical lighting system that represents a significant portion of their establishment's identity and who has been quietly investigating what it would take to run it independently of the Network.

**What they know:** The Entertainment Quarter's social world from the inside — who comes to Volta's, what they do, what they say when they believe the atmosphere has made them safe. Volta does not run an intelligence service and would object to being described as doing so. They are, however, a person who notices things, and what they notice includes the specific behaviors of people who are, for example, Vault Guard officers not in uniform, Council members whose official positions would be complicated by their presence, and the specific patterns of activity that precede events the city notices.

**Working with them:** Volta is accessible as an establishment contact immediately — they are a public-facing proprietor who welcomes everyone. The more substantive access — what they've noticed, what they know about specific people who use their establishment — requires either a genuine relationship built over multiple visits or a situation where sharing what they know serves a purpose Volta believes in. They are not mercenary about this. They are, however, careful.

### Brinne Halloway

**Role:** Chair of the Eastwick Cooperative **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 46 **Location:** The Cooperative's meeting hall, Eastwick. Her own home, four buildings away. Lida's Bake at 4am more often than she would prefer, because the Cooperative's planning sessions run long.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone doing important work without the resources that important work usually gets — slightly worn, consistently capable, with the specific quality of someone who has been managing the gap between what is needed and what is available for long enough that the management has become second nature. Practical clothing in practical colors. Always carrying something.

**Personality:** Quietly fierce. She does not make speeches; she organizes. She has been watching the Ward's eastern neighborhood experience the early version of what the Manufactories has been experiencing for three years, and her response has been the same as it would have been to any practical problem: identify what resources are available, build the infrastructure that makes those resources useful, do this before the crisis rather than during it. She has a specific and accurate read on where Lamplight Ward is heading and it is less optimistic than most Ward residents' current position.

**What she wants:** The Eastwick community to be able to sustain itself through whatever comes next — the power crisis's next phase, the civic disruption that the Ironwright Collective's strike planning suggests is coming, the possibility that the Ward's commercial stability fractures in ways that affect the residential neighborhood that depends on it. She has been in contact with the Halfling Quarter's community network through the delivery relationship and she is aware of Tommas Wickham, though they have not met.

**What she knows:** The Eastwick neighborhood's current situation at the household level — who is in difficulty, what the actual supply situation is in the eastern Ward, what the community's capacity for collective action looks like. She also knows the Cooperative's stockpile situation, which is the most accurate picture of a residential community's emergency preparedness in the city, and she has drawn specific conclusions from building it about what the city as a whole is and isn't ready for.

**Working with her:** She will be direct about what the Cooperative needs and direct about what she knows in return for genuine engagement with the community's situation. She is not interested in people who want to talk about the crisis; she is interested in people who want to do something about it. The distinction is visible to her quickly.

**Starting position:** 0 (Visitor). Players are unknown quantities — welcome as customers, assessed as people.

**Gaining standing:**

- Regular custom at an establishment over a week, treated well: +1
- Demonstrating genuine engagement with the Ward's situation rather than extraction: +1
- Helping a Ward community organization (Eastwick Cooperative, Guild legitimate business): +1
- Being vouched for by Aldous Wren, Brinne Halloway, or Joss Aldeth: +1

- Attending the Long Table dinner repeatedly and engaging honestly: +1

**Losing standing:**

- Being identified as working against Ward commercial or community interests: -2
- Exploiting a Ward resident's trust or hospitality: -2
- Causing significant disruption to the Entertainment Quarter: -1
- Bringing dangerous official attention to an establishment the Ward values: -2

## Faction Presence — Lamplight Ward

**The Merchant Guild** — primary economic faction in the district. Nervous. Watching.

**The Craft Guild** — aligned with Conservators historically; more pragmatic than ideological currently; its members are the professionals who maintain the city's infrastructure and who know more specifically than the Council does what the Network's condition means in practical terms.

**The Eastwick Cooperative** — mutual aid; informal; increasingly radicalized by watching their community's situation worsen. Not aligned with any Council faction. Brinne Halloway has been in contact with Genna Cradle twice in the past month.

**The Silver Thread** — visible through Calla's licensed establishment. Intelligence flowing upward from here is the most politically significant in the network.

**The Illuminist Faction** — soft presence; the Ward is its demographic base; the base is currently experiencing a loss of faith in the technical solution that the faction's leadership has not caught up to.

**The Unseen Guild** — present through the Night Pharmacy arrangement (the watch tolerance is Guild-adjacent, though Tessiel is not Guild), through Volta's occasional use for specific meetings that require a controlled environment, and through the Merchant Guild's supply chain, where several importers are handling Guild-adjacent goods without full knowledge of the arrangement.
