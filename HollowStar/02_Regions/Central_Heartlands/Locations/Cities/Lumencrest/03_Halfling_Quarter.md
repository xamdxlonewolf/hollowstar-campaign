# The Halfling Quarter

**Population:** ~15,000 (predominantly Halfling; ~12,000 Halfling, ~2,000 human, ~1,000 other)  
**Power Status:** Mostly reliable. The Quarter runs more independent operations than other districts — the brewing and baking infrastructure runs on a combination of Network power and its own backup systems, so brownouts hurt less here than elsewhere.  
**Cable Car:** 1 station at the Quarter's northern entrance on Brewers' Lane  
**Access:** Fully open. Considered by many the most welcoming district in the city.

---

## Overview

The Halfling Quarter is the warmest district in Lumencrest, in every sense. The buildings are lower and the streets narrower than the rest of the city, but they're never cramped — they're scaled to the community that built them, and they have a coherence and intention the organic growth of larger districts lacks. Window boxes overflow with herbs and flowers. The building fronts are painted in warm ochres and clay-reds. The street-level doors are Halfling-height with decorative transoms above for taller visitors who arrive expecting to duck and find they don't have to.

The smell is the Quarter's true landmark. Before you see it, you smell it: yeast from the breweries, bread from the bakeries, roasting from the food vendors, the particular green smell of the herb gardens on the Common Green's eastern edge. This district produces most of the food and almost all of the beer that feeds the rest of Lumencrest. The Halfling Quarter doesn't just make the city feel comfortable — it makes the city possible.

The Quarter is not a ghetto. This is worth saying plainly: the Halfling community chose to concentrate here because community is the value they chose to optimize for. They could live anywhere in Lumencrest with their income. They mostly choose to live near each other, near the Common Green, near Brewers' Lane, near the families they've been next-door to for three generations. Urban Halflings who prefer city life over the agricultural villages of Greenhollow came here for the life this district offers, not because they were put here.

That said: the Quarter has opinions about the rest of the city. The Halfling elders who gather at the Speakers' Corner on the Common Green most evenings have been talking about Lumencrest's crisis with the particular clarity of people who built their livelihoods without depending on powered industry. They can see what's coming more plainly than the Manufactories workers who are living it, because they're watching it from outside.

## Key Locations

**The Common Green** — an open park, genuine grass, with a permanent covered market pavilion on the southern edge. The social heart of the Quarter. Children play here. The elderly gather here. Speakers' Corner — a raised stone platform where community members address the quarter on anything they feel needs addressing — runs most evenings and occasionally produces the most honest political commentary in the city.

**Brewers' Lane** — the main commercial thoroughfare. The twin brewery towers of Copperkettle & Brum visible at the northern end; smaller craft breweries filling the street below. The Halfling brewing tradition produces ales, lagers, and spirits that command premium prices across Lumencrest and are exported to every Accord city. The *Copperkettle Amber* is the best-selling beer in Lumencrest by volume. The *Brum Reserve* is the most expensive by price.

**The Bakery District** — south of the Common Green, parallel to Brewers' Lane. Open twenty-four hours. The bread made here feeds Lumencrest. Full stop. If the bakeries stopped, the city would notice within a day. The Halfling bakers know this. They are not aggressive about it but they are aware.

**Family Compounds** — multi-generational Halfling homes arranged around small private courtyards, accessed from the street through gates that are theoretically public and practically acknowledged as private. These are not estates — they're dense, warm, full of people related to each other. Getting invited into a family compound is a mark of genuine acceptance by the community.

**The Copper Kettle Pub** *(not the brewery; different name, same family)* — the Quarter's central gathering pub, run by the Kettlemarch family for four generations. The walls are covered in portraits of people who've sat at these tables over the decades. The ale is exceptional and the food is the best pub food in Lumencrest. Locals pack it every night. Visitors are welcome but are aware they're guests in someone else's space.

## Where to Sleep

**Pipwick's Rooms** — seven rooms above a bakery on the Common Green's south side. Simple, clean, breakfast included (exceptional breakfast; you're above a bakery). 7 silver a night. Run by Bessa Pipwick, who has been doing this for decades and greets every guest as if they're a cousin she was expecting.

**The Green Hearth Inn** — the Quarter's one proper inn, twelve rooms, dining room, taproom. Run as a community business by a cooperative of Halfling families who rotate management. 1gm per night. The dining room is open to non-guests and gets busy at dinner with Quarter residents who prefer not to cook that night.
