# The Manufactories — Expanded District Guide

## Points of Interest, Vendors, and NPCs

_This document expands on the Manufactories district as established in [[04_The_Manufactories]], adding the locations, people, and daily texture needed to run the district at the table. The Manufactories is Lumencrest's industrial heart — loud, dense, politically charged, and running out of patience. Cross-reference [[05_Shadowside_Expanded]] for the district's underground-adjacent population and [[08_The_Underground_Expanded]] for the tunnel network that connects the two worlds, including the Pit._

---

## Running the Manufactories at the Table

The Manufactories has a sound before it has anything else. Players entering from Lamplight Ward hear the shift before they see it — the rhythmic industrial percussion of machinery that runs through three shifts and never fully stops, the low thrum of electric motors under the heavier mechanical clatter, the occasional deep concussive sound from the Forge District's heaviest presses that you feel in the chest before you hear it in the ears. The air changes too: coal particulate, metallic sharpness, the particular hot-oil smell of powered machinery. By the time players are a block into the district they know they are somewhere different.

**The visual rhythm:** Wide delivery streets for cart traffic, narrowed worker alleys between factory blocks, the six-story residential buildings that crowd the spaces between industrial sites. The Manufactories is not ugly — there is a scale to it, a functional grandeur in the largest factory structures, the enormous chimney stacks that mark the Forge District's skyline. But it is not designed for the comfort of anyone who lives in it, and this is visible in every proportion.

**The political weather:** The Manufactories is the district where the city's power crisis has bitten hardest in the most tangible way. Everywhere else in Lumencrest, the crisis is a thing you read about or worry about abstractly. Here it is a thing that stopped three mills from running full shifts, put a cohort of assembly workers on reduced hours, and made the Clank Barracks' already-strained population more visibly strained. The anger is not abstract either. It is the very specific anger of people who know exactly what they are owed and are watching the people who owe it make speeches.

Players who enter this district and treat it carelessly — who are visibly dismissive of the workers, who drop Radiant Core names, who seem like they came here to look at something rather than talk to someone — will find the district cold immediately and difficult to warm. Players who treat it like what it is — a community of people doing hard work under increasing pressure — will find it opens quickly. The Manufactories is not hostile to outsiders. It is hostile to people who aren't paying attention.

**Shift changes:** The Forge District and most major factories run three shifts. Shift change happens at dawn, midday, and late evening. At each shift change, the streets fill suddenly with workers moving in and out — thousands of people in motion, the Halfhold Street taverns absorbing the off-coming shift and the canteens filling for the on-coming one. These moments are the most alive the district gets and also the easiest time to move through it without being noticed.

## The Forge District — Northern Manufactories

_Heavy industry, metalworking, weapons manufacturing. Three shifts, never fully dark._

---

### The Great Press

_(Referenced in [[04_The_Manufactories]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** The northern edge of the Forge District, identifiable by its chimney stack — the tallest structure in the Manufactories and one of the tallest in the city. The factory occupies a full city block.

**What it is:** Lumencrest's largest single factory, producing the Lumen Network's maintenance equipment, powered components for export, and the electric printing presses distributed across the Accord that carry Lumencrest's technical reputation into every major city on the continent. The Great Press is old — the current building is the third construction on this site over 400 years, each one larger than the last. The machinery inside is extraordinary: enormous automated presses, precision electric lathes, furnaces that run on diverted Network power at a scale no residential district receives. Currently operating at 65% capacity due to power rationing. The floor supervisors keep the productivity numbers from the workers and the workers know the numbers anyway.

**Getting inside:** Factory floors require employee status or an authorized visit, per the Labor Board's standard regulations. In practice, an authorized visit requires knowing someone in management who will sign the form. The Labor Board's authorization process takes 3-5 days and asks more questions than most people want to answer. The workers' entrance is watched during shift changes by a floor guard named **Holt** (human, 50s, built like the machinery he supervises, entirely fair and impossible to talk past without legitimate reason). The delivery entrance on the factory's west side is less supervised and has been used by people who had good reasons to be there that wouldn't survive the front door paperwork.

**What it contains:** Beyond the production floors — which are extraordinary and worth seeing even if they're not the goal — the Great Press has a management wing on the second story, a Network junction room that handles the factory's diverted power allocation (heavily locked, Vault Guard inspection quarterly), and a basement archive of production records going back 200 years that the city considers a historical resource and that Genna Cradle has been trying to get access to for three years to support the Collective's labor grievance documentation.

**The foreman:** **Rek Mast** — see NPCs. Not the same Mast as Professor Dorin Mast in the Academic Quarter; an unfortunate name duplication that has come up in administrative paperwork twice.

### The Ironwright Hall

_(Referenced in [[04_The_Manufactories]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Half a block off Halfhold Street, in the Forge District's residential fringe. A building that was a social club for factory managers sixty years ago and became the Ironwright Collective's meeting hall thirty years ago through a property transfer whose specifics the previous owners would prefer not to discuss.

**What it is:** Officially a social club. Practically the organizational center of the most significant labor movement in Lumencrest's history. The Hall has a large meeting room on the ground floor that holds two hundred people and has held more, a smaller strategy room accessible only to the Collective's core organizers, a noticeboards wall that functions as the district's labor intelligence clearing house — shift changes, management decisions, Labor Board policy updates, safety incidents, and the sixteen-item grievance list currently under nominal Council review — and a kitchen that runs food and drink during meetings at worker-affordable prices because Genna decided early that organizing on empty stomachs was inefficient.

**Meetings:** Open to any Manufactories worker. Held twice weekly — a general assembly on the fifth day and a specific-issue meeting on the second day. Attendance has been increasing. The fifth-day assemblies have been drawing three to four hundred people for the past two months, which requires spilling into the street. The city watch has been present at the last three, which the Collective notes in its records and discusses openly.

**The strategy room:** Accessible only through Genna or her two deputies. Contains the Collective's full documentation — the grievance files, the network of contacts in each factory, the communication records with the Council that demonstrate exactly how many times specific requests have been acknowledged without response. Also contains the preliminary strike planning, which is not a document Genna will show to outsiders until she has assessed them very carefully.

**For players:** The Ironwright Hall is one of the best places in the city to hear what is actually happening in the Manufactories. The general meetings are open and the information shared there is substantive. Players who attend and don't make themselves conspicuous will learn more in two hours than they'd get from a week of other investigation in this district. Players who actively help the Collective — carrying information, providing resources, doing something visible that advances what the workers need — will find Genna's inner circle accessible faster than almost any other significant faction in the city.

**The lead organizer:** **Genna Cradle** — see NPCs (expanded from [[NPCs]]).

### The Forge and Flagon

_(Referenced in [[04_The_Manufactories]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Halfhold Street, the Forge District end. The oldest continuously operating tavern on Halfhold Street — the building dates to the district's first expansion and the current proprietor is the fourth generation of the same family to run it.

**What it is:** The Manufactories' best tavern, which earns that title through atmosphere, food quality, and genuine community function rather than comfort or elegance. The room is large, always loud during peak hours, and furnished with salvaged timber benches and long communal tables that encourage the kind of shoulder-to-shoulder conversation that happens when people have had a long shift and need somewhere to put it. The kitchen is the real achievement — the food here is heavy, cheap, and excellent in the way that working-class cooking that has been refined over generations tends to be. The ale is mediocre and cold, which is exactly what the clientele wants.

**What's on offer:**

_Food:_

- The house stew: rotating base, always meat-heavy, served with a half-loaf. 3 copper marks. Consistent across four generations because the recipe hasn't changed.
- The press plate: sliced roasted meat, pickled vegetables, bread. Named for the Great Press. 4 copper marks. The biggest meal in the district for the price.
- Pies: meat, vegetable, or mixed. Made fresh each shift change so there's always one just out of the oven and one that's been sitting. Ask which is which. 2 copper marks each.
- A cheese board that actually has good cheese because the proprietor has a contact in the Halfling Quarter. 3 copper marks. Slightly incongruous in the context of everything else on the menu. Sells out every night.

_Drink:_

- House ale — mediocre, cold, 2 copper marks, this is what everyone drinks
- A dark stout from a Forge District brewer three streets over that is substantially better than the house ale but costs 3 copper and some regulars consider paying more than 2 copper for a drink a moral position they won't compromise
- Spirits at 4 copper marks, rotating stock, whatever was available through the Night Market's northern distribution
- A hot grain drink served in winter and during cold snaps that the proprietor insists is not tea, which it is

_Accommodation:_

- 5 silver for a common room bed (twelve beds, clean linens changed twice weekly)
- 8 silver for a curtained private space (four of these, more privacy than the common room but the same room)
- No private rooms. The proprietor has been asked about this many times. The answer has not changed.

**The function:** The Forge and Flagon is where shift change goes. The off-coming shift arrives, fills the room, talks about what happened during the shift — which means players who arrive at shift change and listen rather than talk will hear the current operational reality of the Forge District within an hour. It's also where Genna Cradle spends her evenings when she isn't in the Hall, which makes it a natural contact point for anyone trying to reach her without going through the formal Collective channels.

**The proprietor:** **Della Mourne** — see NPCs.

### The Labour Board — Forge District Office

_(Referenced in [[04_The_Manufactories]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** A city-operated building on the Forge District's main administrative street, one block from the cable car North Factory Hub station.

**What it is:** The bureaucratic machinery of Lumencrest's labor system, and one of the most practically important and emotionally exhausting locations in the city. The Labour Board processes worker registrations, manages Clank assignments, handles official employment disputes, and maintains the records that determine who is legally entitled to work in what capacity in the Manufactories. The line here is always long. The clerks are overworked, underpaid, and operating a system designed for a city that isn't currently in a power crisis with a workforce whose situation changes weekly.

**What it actually handles:**

- Worker registration and re-registration (required if a factory closes or reduces shifts — workers must re-register to be eligible for reassignment)
- Clank assignment management — matching Clanks to factory assignments, processing service-term paperwork, handling the complaints about assignment conditions that the Labour Board is required to receive and is not required to act on
- Employment dispute filing — a formal process that takes 4-6 weeks and has a 30% resolution rate, which the Collective has documented and submitted as part of their grievance list
- Safety incident recording — mandatory reporting from factory supervisors, compiled into reports that go to the Council quarterly. The Collective has alleged that some incidents are not being reported. The Labour Board has denied this. The Collective has the documentation.

**The clerk worth knowing:** Most Labour Board clerks are exactly what they appear to be — people doing a difficult job in an under-resourced office. **Mira Toll** (see NPCs) is the exception: a senior clerk who has been here for eighteen years and who has made a private, deliberate decision to be helpful to the right people in ways that stay within the boundaries of what she can do without losing her position. She is not a mole and she is not corrupt. She simply believes that the system she operates is causing harm and that small, careful helpfulness is the only response available to her.

## The Mill Quarter — Central Manufactories

_Textile production, light manufacturing, the district's quieter middle._

---

### The Spindle — Mill Quarter Tavern and Gathering Space

**Location:** The Mill Quarter's central street, across from the largest textile mill. Named for the mill spools that decorate the exterior — someone's idea of branding, thirty years ago, that has outlasted them.

**What it is:** The Mill Quarter's primary social space, and considerably different in atmosphere from the Forge and Flagon — quieter, more mixed-gender in its clientele (the textile mills employ more women than the Forge District's heavy industry), with an attached meeting room that various community groups use on a rotating schedule. Not a political space in the overt way that the Ironwright Hall is, but politically aware — the conversations here are more careful and more anxious than in the Forge District, reflecting a workforce that is slightly less organized and slightly more afraid of what happens if the mills keep cutting hours.

**What's on offer:**

- Standard tavern food at slightly higher quality than the Forge and Flagon — the cook here has more time and a smaller volume. 3-5 copper marks.
- Ale and spirits at comparable pricing to Forge and Flagon
- A private meeting room, bookable for 3 silver for two hours. Used by: the Ironwright Collective's Mill Quarter sub-committee, a women's mutual aid group that has been quietly stockpiling emergency resources for six months, a small Clank rights reading group that meets without announcement and whose members would prefer not to be named

**Accommodation:** Four private rooms upstairs, 8 silver a night. Quieter than anywhere in the Forge District. A good choice for anyone who needs to be in the Manufactories for several days and values sleep.

**The proprietor:** **Sanna Vorek** — see NPCs.

---

### Kess Aldman's Boarding House

_(Referenced in [[04_The_Manufactories]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** A residential building on the Mill Quarter's quieter western edge, indistinguishable from the workers' housing on either side except for a small painted sign (ALDMAN'S — ROOMS) above the door.

**What it is:** The Manufactories' best option for visitors with extended business in the district who need something more private than a tavern common room and less conspicuous than registering at the Labour Board's official visitor accommodation. Kess runs twelve rooms in a building she owns outright — she bought it seventeen years ago with money from an enterprise she now describes as "market trading" and that people who knew her then describe differently. She asks very few questions. This is a deliberate business decision that has made her the first recommendation from anyone in the district who needs to house someone without drawing attention.

**The rooms:** Clean, functional, single beds with quality linens (Kess sources from the mill contact she's had for fifteen years), a desk, a window that looks onto the alley rather than the street. She serves a basic breakfast included in the price. 7 silver a night. Weekly rate: 4 gold. She'll negotiate the weekly rate for guests she's decided are not going to cause problems. She will not negotiate the daily rate.

**What Kess knows:** Seventeen years of hosting people who had reasons not to stay elsewhere. She doesn't share what she knows — the value of her establishment is entirely built on this — but she notices things and she keeps them. Players who have been staying with her and need to find someone who has also been in the district without appearing on any official record will find, after they've established some trust, that she can confirm or deny whether certain descriptions match anyone she's housed recently.

**The proprietor:** **Kess Aldman** — see NPCs.

---

### The Night Pharmacy — Mill Quarter Branch

**Location:** A ground-floor shop on the Mill Quarter's main street. A small blue lamp above the door is lit during operating hours — the blue lamp is the Night Pharmacy's consistent identifier across all its locations.

**What it is:** Tessiel Wane's Night Pharmacy operates two locations — the primary in Lamplight Ward and this Mill Quarter branch, which runs with a different staff member but the same stock and the same policy. Open from mid-afternoon until late, serving the manufacturing workforce's medical and pharmaceutical needs at prices calibrated to worker wages rather than Lamplight Ward rates. The Mill Quarter branch is the primary source of non-Night-Market pharmaceutical supply for the Manufactories — the official city medical clinic in the Forge District is under-resourced and has a significant wait.

**What's available:** Standard pharmaceutical preparations for pain, infection, fever, and injury. Contraception. Several preparations that are technically in grey legal territory and that this branch stocks and sells without drawing the particular attention that the primary location might. Fade is not stocked here — Tessiel made a deliberate decision to keep the Mill Quarter branch clean of the harder substances on the basis that the Manufactories' Fade user population is better served by the direct Quiet Market channels and she doesn't want the Labour Board's attention on this location.

**The branch manager:** **Pev Solan** — see NPCs. Tessiel's most trusted employee, running this branch for six years.

## The Assembly Halls — Southern Manufactories

_Precision manufacturing, complex devices, Lumen Network components. The heaviest Clank presence in the district._

---

### The Clank Barracks

**Location:** The Assembly Halls' western edge, where the factory blocks give way to the workers' residential zone. Three buildings connected by covered walkways — a deliberate design that limits movement between the Barracks and the surrounding district during off-hours.

**What it is:** City-run housing for Clanks on assignment in the Manufactories — the largest single concentration of the Clank population in Lumencrest. Currently housing 1,800 registered Clanks, which is at the high end of the intended capacity of 1,600. The overcrowding is a consequence of the Assembly Halls' reduced shifts — Clanks whose factory assignments have been cut due to power rationing are still housed here because the city has not processed the administrative adjustment of their assignments, which takes 6-8 weeks through the Labour Board. The Clanks are housed and fed but not assigned, which means they are not earning their service credit hours, which means their service terms are effectively paused without formal acknowledgment that this is happening.

**The physical space:** Functional, not comfortable. Sleeping areas in long rooms, 30-40 Clanks per room, bunks of salvaged metal with city-issued bedding. A communal maintenance area where Clanks can perform basic self-maintenance — the city provides the standard maintenance materials but not the specialist materials that Clanks with specific construction types need and must source themselves. A meal hall that serves twice daily. A common area with benches and tables that has become the Barracks' de facto social center and is where the political organization that the Labour Board is worried about and the Free Hand is cultivating both happen, in the same conversations, usually.

**The social reality:** 1,800 Clanks in close quarters with significant idle time and accumulating grievances is a social environment the city did not plan for. The Barracks has developed its own internal structure over the years — older residents who have been here through multiple assignment cycles have informal authority; specific common-room tables belong to specific social groups by long-standing understanding; the maintenance area runs on a schedule negotiated between residents that the city official who oversees the Barracks doesn't know about and that works better than the city's official schedule would. The Free Hand has three active contacts inside. The Labour Board has one monitor who comes twice a week, takes notes, and does not speak to anyone.

**The Barracks administrator:** **Wren Cole** — see NPCs. A city appointee, human, 40s, who has been in this role for four years and has developed a complicated relationship with it.

**The resident Clank to know:** **Sable-Seven** — see NPCs. Not affiliated with the Sables in the Shadowside documents. A Clank who has been in the Barracks for eleven years and is the closest thing the population has to a formal representative. The Free Hand has been trying to move Sable-Seven out of the city for three years. Sable-Seven has declined each time, citing the 1,800 Clanks still inside.

---

### The Assembly Hall Floor — Authorized Visits

**Location:** The largest of the Assembly Halls occupies the southern end of the district, across from the Clank Barracks. Multiple connected factory floors producing precision instruments, Network components, and complex devices.

**For players:** Getting onto the Assembly Hall floor legally requires Labour Board authorization (3-5 days) or a management contact. Getting on without that requires either a worker willing to sign you in as a contracted visitor (a risk to that worker if discovered — sign-in forgeries carry a 20-silver fine and potential assignment loss for Clanks) or the delivery entrance approach. The delivery entrance is busy enough during shift changes that an additional body carrying something plausible creates minimal attention.

**What's worth seeing:** The scale of precision manufacturing happening here is remarkable — the Assembly Halls produce components that appear in devices across the continent, worked by Clanks whose mechanical capability makes the tolerance levels achievable. For players investigating the Clank situation, the Assembly Hall floor is also where the gap between what the city says Clank service involves and what it actually involves is most visible — Clanks performing skilled labor at a pace that human workers could not match, under supervision conditions the Ironwright Collective has flagged in their grievance documentation.

**The floor supervisor:** **Torren Ash** — see NPCs. A human supervisor who is neither villain nor ally — a person managing an operation that he did not design and whose structural problems he is aware of and has concluded are above his pay grade to address.

### The Pit

_(Referenced across earlier documents — fully expanded here)_

**Location:** Beneath the Assembly Halls, accessed through a concealed entrance in the lower maintenance level of the southernmost Assembly Hall building. The entrance is a heavy iron door, unmarked, set into a wall of the maintenance corridor that runs under the factory floor. It does not appear on any official building plan. It has been there for forty years.

A second access route exists through the Underground's tunnel network — a passage from the main Shadowside-to-Manufactories sewer route that branches southeast and surfaces in the same maintenance level from the opposite direction. This tunnel access is how Shadowside residents and Underground regulars reach the Pit without going through the Assembly Hall entrance at all. It adds twenty minutes of tunnel walking and exits at a connecting door that requires knowing which section of tunnel wall gives way if you push at the correct point — this is learned knowledge, passed person-to-person, not written anywhere.

**What it is:** A fight arena. Illegal in the technical sense that unsanctioned combat for wagering purposes is prohibited under the city's public safety ordinances. Practically tolerated in the sense that the Pit has been operating for forty years, the city watch has been made aware of its existence through various channels, and the current arrangement is that the Pit polices itself, keeps its violence contained below the floor of the Assembly Hall above, and provides a safety valve for the Manufactories' accumulated pressure that the city finds more useful than disruptive. The Pit also employs specific city watch officers as unofficial security on the most crowded nights. This is not acknowledged.

**The space:** A chamber roughly sixty feet across, with a fighting area marked by chalk and iron posts in the center, tiered standing areas cut into the chamber's stone walls, and a gallery level above that costs more to access and gets a better sightline. Oil lamps throughout, no Network connection — the Pit is entirely off-grid, which means it stays lit during brownouts and has become one of the few places in the Manufactories that draws a crowd during power disruptions. Capacity: two hundred standing, forty in the gallery. On major fight nights, both are exceeded.

**What happens there:**

- **Standard bouts** — consensual combat between willing fighters, no weapons, until one fighter yields or cannot continue. Most common format. Happens three nights a week.
- **Open challenge nights** — any attendee can challenge any fighter. The crowd determines whether the challenge is accepted by noise. This is how reputations get made and destroyed.
- **Clank bouts** — free Clanks and Clanks with the consent of their assigned supervisors (obtained through deliberate non-asking on the supervisor's part) fight in Clank-versus-Clank bouts that draw the largest crowds. A free Clank fighting for themselves in front of two hundred people is a social reality the Barracks has a complicated relationship with: some see it as dignity in the wrong context, some see it as the only context where dignity is currently available.
- **Challenge matches** — specific called fights between named individuals, usually settling something that couldn't be settled elsewhere. The Pit's arbitration function is informal but real: disputes that would otherwise escalate into something with wider consequences get resolved here instead.

**The wagering:** Organized by **Tomas Heck** (see NPCs), who runs the Pit's book, sets odds, and manages the money flow that makes the operation function. No wager is too small. No wager above 15 gold marks is accepted without Tomas knowing who's placing it, because large losing bets from unknown parties have caused problems before.

**The management:** **Dura Stoneback** — see NPCs. Has been running the Pit since she won it in a fight eighteen years ago. The previous manager challenged her. She accepted and won. The transition of management was smooth.

**Players in the Pit:**

- Attending as spectators: accessible without reputation — the Pit's entrance is through the maintenance corridor and the tunnel, both of which become navigable with a Manufactories +1 reputation or a guide from Shadowside.
- Fighting: any player character can register to fight through Dura. She will assess them (not a roll — she looks at them and decides) and slot them into an appropriate bout. A player character winning a challenging fight increases Manufactories reputation by 1.
- Causing problems: the Pit's security is real and motivated. Players who disrupt a bout, attack outside the ring, or cause the kind of trouble that would bring official attention to the Pit will be removed and blacklisted. Dura has no patience for this and does not give second chances.

## Halfhold Street — The District's Social Spine

_Running north-south through the Manufactories' residential center, connecting the Forge District to the Mill Quarter. Not a sub-district, but a location in itself._

---

### Halfhold Street Overview

**What it is:** The main social street for Manufactories workers — taverns, canteens, cheap shops, and the informal gathering points that working communities generate. No cable car stop, but the South Factory Hub is a ten-minute walk south. At shift change, the street is dense with movement; in the hours between, it settles into the quieter rhythm of people going about their days in a district where most of the spending money goes to food and rent.

**What lines Halfhold Street:**

- Three taverns (the Forge and Flagon at the northern end, the Spindle serving the Mill Quarter, and a third, smaller establishment at the street's midpoint called **The Rivet** that serves the residential population between the two factory zones)
- Two worker canteens run by the Labour Board — theoretically subsidized, practically charging full price for food that is worse than the taverns
- A handful of small shops: a cobbler (always busy — factory floors destroy boots), a tool-and-equipment vendor (secondhand machinery parts and tools, most of legitimate origin), a general goods shop, a seamstress-and-repairs operation that does more than clothing if you know to ask
- The Ironwright Collective's notice board — a freestanding board bolted to a corner wall that has been the district's labor information infrastructure for twenty years. The city has attempted to have it removed twice. Both times, the Collective reinstalled it within 48 hours and the city decided this was not the hill.

---

### The Rivet — Halfhold Street Mid-Point Tavern

**Location:** Halfhold Street, between the Forge District and Mill Quarter. Identifiable by the large iron rivet bolted to the sign — which is not the original sign and which was installed by a previous owner as a joke that became the establishment's identity.

**What it is:** The residential population's tavern — less political than the Forge and Flagon, less community-meeting-space than the Spindle, more just a place where people who live on Halfhold Street and its adjacent alleys drink and eat and talk without an agenda. This makes it useful in ways that the more organized establishments aren't: the conversations here are unguarded in the way of people who aren't in a context where they're performing a position. Players who want to understand what the district's non-organized population is thinking, feeling, and worrying about will find it in the Rivet faster than anywhere else.

**What's on offer:**

- Ale, spirits, food at Halfhold Street standard pricing
- A dartboard that is genuinely contested — the Rivet has a darts culture and players who can play will find it opens conversations
- The proprietor, **Jenten Wald** (see NPCs), who is one of the better sources of neighborhood-level intelligence in the district if approached correctly

---

### Halverson's Tools and Equipment

**Location:** Halfhold Street, two shops from the Ironwright notice board.

**What it is:** The legitimate face of a somewhat less legitimate operation. Halverson's sells secondhand and surplus tools and equipment — factory surplus, retired machinery components, work tools of all kinds at prices that reflect their condition rather than their original cost. The legitimate operation is real and the quality is good. The less legitimate operation: Halverson's will source specific items that are not in stock and not strictly available through channels he'll name, for customers he's assessed as worth the additional service. Factory components that are technically still assigned to a specific facility, maintenance equipment that has been reported decommissioned and hasn't been, specific Lumen Network parts that require an authorized work order through normal channels.

**The proprietor:** **Halverson** — see NPCs. He does not use a first name in business contexts. It may be that his first name is also Halverson, which would explain it.

## Notable NPCs — The Manufactories

---

### Genna Cradle

_(Full entry in [[NPCs]] — Manufactories-specific expansion here)_

**Role:** Lead organizer, Ironwright Collective. The most significant political figure in the Manufactories and one of the most significant in the city.

**Where to find her:** Ironwright Hall during meeting days. The Forge and Flagon most evenings. Wherever a problem in the district has come to her attention, which is frequently.

**Expanded detail:** Genna Cradle has been organizing Manufactories workers for fifteen years and the Council has been trying to neutralize her for six of those years. The co-option attempts — a seat on a labor advisory committee, an offer of employment as a Labour Board senior administrator — were declined with the specific courtesy of someone who has already thought through every version of that conversation. The discrediting attempts have found nothing usable because there is nothing usable. She is not corrupt, not personally motivated by anything except the work, and has no private life that creates leverage. The Council has concluded she is genuinely what she presents as, which they find more unsettling than if she had something to hide.

**The strike planning:** The Ironwright Collective is preparing for what may become the largest general strike in Lumencrest's history. Genna has not announced this. She is building the infrastructure for it — the communication networks across the district's factories, the strike fund that has been building for eight months, the mutual aid arrangements with the Halfling Quarter that would sustain workers through an extended work stoppage. She will not call the strike until she believes it will hold. She is close to believing this. What she needs before she calls it: confirmation of specific intelligence about the Council's actual response plans. Players who can get her that confirmation, reliably and verifiably, will earn the Collective's full trust in a single action.

**What she thinks of Clanks:** She is one of the few human labor organizers who has been consistently and publicly clear that Clank labor rights are labor rights. The Ironwright Collective's grievance list includes three items specifically about Clank assignment conditions. This position has cost her support from some workers who see Clanks as competition for shifts. She maintains it anyway, on the principle that a labor movement that excludes a class of workers is not a labor movement, it is a club.

**Working with her:** She will assess players quickly and accurately. Credentials mean nothing; actions mean everything. If players do something concrete that helps the Collective — not promises, concrete action — she will trust them proportionally to what they did. She will not pretend a small thing is a large thing, and she will notice if players pretend the same.

### Sable-Seven

**Role:** De facto representative of the Clank Barracks population. Longtime resident and the most politically significant Clank in the Manufactories. **Ancestry/Age:** Clank. Construction date: 31 years ago. Service term was 15 years; completed 20 years ago. Has been in Lumencrest since completion of service term. **Location:** The Clank Barracks, Assembly Halls western edge — in the common room most evenings, in the maintenance area most mornings.

**Appearance:** The oldest-generation Clank construction visible in Lumencrest — the joint work and materials from 31 years ago look different from current Luminarium production, heavier and slightly rougher in finish. Sable-Seven does not obscure this. They move with the specific deliberate quality of a Clank who has learned to make their movements legible to human observers — not theatrical, just clear. A long crack in the casing along the left forearm, repaired internally but visible on the surface, that has been there for eight years and that they keep as it is.

**Name:** Sable-Seven is a service designation, not a chosen name. In the Barracks, the other Clanks call them Seven. They have not chosen a new name because they do not feel they have finished the thing they are doing here and a new name feels like it belongs to what comes after.

**Personality:** Patient in the specific way of someone who has been waiting for a long time and has stopped being frustrated by the wait. They are precise and direct in conversation — Clank or not, they have had enough conversations where humans talked around what they meant and they stopped accommodating it. They will say exactly what they mean and they expect the same in return. They are not cold. They have a specific dry humor that emerges with people they have decided to trust, and when it does it is surprising enough that people laugh partly from the humor and partly from the surprise.

**What they want:** For the Barracks population to be acknowledged as people and treated accordingly — which in immediate practical terms means the assignment freeze to be resolved, the maintenance supply situation to be fixed, and the Labour Board's monitor to be replaced by someone who actually speaks to the residents. In larger terms: an end to Clank service indenture. They do not expect to see this in their functional lifetime. They are working toward it anyway.

**Why they won't leave:** The Free Hand has offered them extraction three times. Sable-Seven's response each time has been a version of the same thing: 1,800 Clanks cannot all be extracted, and a representative who leaves is not representing. They are aware that staying involves risk. They have calculated that the risk of staying is less than the cost of leaving.

**What they know:** The full internal social and political reality of the Clank Barracks — who is connected to the Free Hand, who is being watched by the Labour Board's monitor, who among the assigned supervisors is more or less brutal, what the Barracks population's mood is and how close it is to something that would be described in different terms by different parties. They also know the Assembly Hall floor in detail — eleven years of observation of how the facility actually operates, which is not identical to how the official documentation says it operates.

**Working with them:** Players who approach the Barracks with genuine intentions and don't perform a version of themselves that is visibly different from what they actually are will get a real conversation with Sable-Seven relatively quickly — they are a good reader of people. Players who want specific intelligence about the Barracks or the Assembly Halls, or who are working with the Free Hand and need a contact on the inside, will need to demonstrate that their involvement is not going to make things worse for the people already here. Sable-Seven's test for this is simple: they ask what the players are willing to lose for this. The answer doesn't have to be much. It has to be something.

### Dura Stoneback

**Role:** Manager and owner of the Pit **Ancestry/Age:** Dwarf, 51 **Location:** The Pit, every fight night. The Assembly Halls' lower maintenance level during setup. A specific table at the Forge and Flagon on off-nights.

**Appearance:** Short and exceptionally broad, with the build of someone who has been fighting and working for decades and whose body reflects both. Grey-brown hair cut extremely short. Three fingers on her left hand are not original — lost in a factory accident fifteen years ago and replaced with functional prosthetics that she sources from an Assembly Hall contact and maintains herself. Wears practical clothing in dark colors and a heavy leather vest that is older than most of the people who fight in her arena. Carries no visible weapons and has never needed any.

**Personality:** Economical. She does not say more than is required and has cultivated an environment where her silence is understood as either satisfaction or assessment, and both produce reasonable behavior from the people around her. She is not unkind — she runs the Pit with a consistent fairness that the fighters trust, and she has stopped bouts when she believed someone was genuinely in danger rather than simply losing. She is not soft. Both of these things are true simultaneously.

**How she got here:** She was a factory worker in the Assembly Halls for twelve years. She started fighting in the Pit because the money helped and because she was good at it. She fought the previous manager on an open challenge night — he called it, she accepted it, she won in four minutes. The Pit's senior regulars confirmed the tradition: winner takes management. She has been here since. She does not miss factory work.

**What she wants:** The Pit to keep running. This requires the city watch arrangement to remain stable, the fighters to remain genuinely consensual rather than coerced, and the wagering to remain organized enough that significant losses don't cause problems that overflow into the Pit itself. She is protective of all three conditions and addresses threats to any of them with her characteristic economy.

**What she knows:** Everyone who has come through the Pit in eighteen years — which is a significant portion of the Manufactories' working population and a specific subset of the Underground's connected individuals. She does not run information for money. She will, for fighters she respects, confirm or deny whether someone she knows has been in the Pit recently. This confirmation has been useful to people who were looking for someone who had gone to ground.

**Working with her:** Players who fight in the Pit are assessed by Dura directly and she remembers every fighter. A player who wins in a way she respects — not just wins, but fights with the quality she looks for, which has as much to do with how someone handles losing ground as how they perform when ahead — will find her a warm and specific contact. Players who try to fix bouts, pressure fighters, or cause the kind of trouble that threatens the Pit's arrangement will not be given a second chance.

### Tomas Heck

**Role:** Pit bookmaker **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 44 **Location:** At the Pit's entrance on fight nights, behind a specific table. The Forge and Flagon during the day.

**Appearance:** Compact and precise, with a ledger that appears to be the most important object in his life — because it is. Quick hands, a specific quality of attention that tracks multiple things simultaneously, clothing that is notably cleaner than the Pit's general atmosphere.

**What he knows:** The Pit's betting patterns — which means he knows, at an aggregate level, which fighters have support networks versus which ones are solo, which bouts have unusual wagering that suggests a fixed result (he flags these to Dura), and which regulars have been absent recently. He also knows the financial pressures of the Manufactories' working population more granularly than the Labour Board does, because people's bets tell the truth about their economic situation in ways their official records don't.

**Working with him:** Tomas will talk readily about the Pit's fighters and bouts — this is his domain and he enjoys it. Getting the second layer of what he knows requires either demonstrating that you have relevant information he'd value in trade, or becoming a regular bettor whose pattern he observes over several fight nights. He will not share information that would compromise the Pit's security or expose a fighter to harm.

### Wren Cole

**Role:** Clank Barracks administrator **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 43 **Location:** The Barracks administrative office (ground floor, eastern building), most days 8am-6pm. Sometimes still there at midnight.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has been doing a difficult job for four years and is only beginning to process that the difficulty is structural rather than circumstantial. Brown hair that is getting grey faster than his age would suggest. A permanent slight furrow. The specific quality of someone who prepares for each day with notes and arrives to find the notes immediately inadequate.

**Personality:** Genuinely well-intentioned in a system that does not reward genuine good intentions. He came to this role from the Labour Board's general administration, believing that the Barracks was a management challenge that could be managed well with the right approach. He has learned, slowly, that the problems in the Barracks are not management problems — they are political problems embedded in the system he administers, and the system does not give him the authority to address political problems. He is not corrupt. He is not cruel. He is a person who has concluded that the situation is wrong and has not yet decided what to do with that conclusion.

**What he can do:** Within the Labour Board's framework, Wren can expedite specific paperwork, ensure that maintenance supply requests are processed rather than lost, and flag specific situations for administrative review in ways that occasionally produce actual review. He has used all three of these levers multiple times. He knows their limits. He will use them again for the right situation.

**What he can't do:** Change the assignment freeze, address the overcrowding above his authority level, or act on the specific supervisor complaints he has documented and submitted and watched disappear into the administrative process.

**Working with him:** Players who identify that Wren is a person wrestling with a moral situation rather than an obstacle or a villain will find him much more useful than his official role suggests. He will not break his employment terms — he has a family and losing this position is not abstract. But within what he can do, he will help people whose goals align with the Barracks population's wellbeing. He needs to believe the help is genuine before he'll extend himself.

### Mira Toll

## Manufactories Reputation Track

**Starting position:** 0 (Stranger). Players are an unknown quantity in a district that is suspicious of unknown quantities by current political weather.

**Gaining standing:**

- Attending an open Ironwright Hall meeting without making yourself conspicuous: +1
- Doing something concrete that helps the Collective's documented goals: +2
- Fighting at the Pit and conducting yourself by the Pit's norms: +1
- Regular custom at a Halfhold Street establishment over a week: +1
- Demonstrated protection of the Barracks population or specific Clanks: +1
- Visible help to a Manufactories worker in a specific difficult situation: +1

**Losing standing:**

- Being identified as Labour Board informants or Council agents: -3
- Exploiting a worker's economic situation: -2
- Disrupting a Pit bout or violating the Pit's rules: -2
- Treating the district as background rather than community: -1 (slow accumulation)

**Reputation unlocks:**

|Standing|What Opens Up|
|---|---|
|+1 (Noticed)|Halfhold Street taverns serve you with genuine warmth; Genna will give you a real conversation; Sable-Seven will speak with you|
|+2 (Known)|Ironwright Hall's strategy room accessible with Genna's invitation; Halverson's additional service available; Mira Toll will expedite legitimate paperwork|
|+3 (Trusted)|Strike planning intelligence accessible; Pit access without guide; Sable-Seven will connect you to Free Hand contacts inside the Barracks; Wren Cole will act on requests within his authority|

## Faction Presence — The Manufactories

**The Ironwright Collective** — primary district faction. Reputation with the Collective is closely correlated with Manufactories district reputation but tracked separately. Helping the Collective without earning district trust is possible but unusual; earning district trust usually involves helping the Collective.

**The Free Hand** — active inside the Barracks, less visible in the rest of the district. Contacts through Sable-Seven and, more indirectly, through the Free Clank Cooperative in Shadowside.

**The Labour Board** — present as an institution rather than a faction. Individual Labour Board employees (Mira Toll, Wren Cole) are the points of useful contact; the institution itself responds to the Ironwright Collective's pressure in ways determined by Council priorities.

**The Unseen Guild** — present through the Pit's wagering network (not Dura Stoneback — she is not a Guild contact — but specific regular gamblers who report to Guild-adjacent figures) and through the Manufactories warehouse beneath the district. Not visible at street level but active.

**The Vault Guard** — minimal visible presence. Quarterly Network junction inspections at the Great Press and Assembly Halls. The Barracks monitor is nominally Labour Board but the Guard receives copies of their reports.
