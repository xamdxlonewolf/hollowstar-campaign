# Lumencrest — City Index

**Population:** ~87,000 (city proper) + ~15,000 transient workers, visitors, registered traders  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Government:** [[04_Factions/Council of Lumens|Council of Lumens]] (32-seat republican council, three warring factions)  
**Founded:** ~1,200 years ago (0 LE — the Lumencrest Era calendar starts here)  
**Unique Feature:** Only city in the known world with Hollostar-powered electricity  
**Current Crisis:** The Heart is dimming. ~2.5% power loss annually. Six brownouts in the last year. Nobody knows how to stop it.

## The City at a Glance

Lumencrest hits visitors in two waves.

The **first wave** is overwhelming — the lights, the noise, the scale, the cable cars humming overhead, streets lit bright as noon at midnight, shops open at every hour, the low electric hum that never stops. No other city looks or sounds or smells like this. Most visitors spend their first day just staring.

The **second wave** comes later, once familiarity sets in — and it's the realization that there are two cities here. The one on the surface, bright and orderly and impressive. And the one that runs beneath it, behind it, inside it — in tunnels and basement clubs and back-alley arrangements, where anything that cannot be bought openly is bought quietly, and where the rules of the surface city are suggestions at best.

Locals know both cities. Visitors eventually find the second one, usually because someone decides to show them.

## Key Numbers for Running the City

- **6 major districts** + Vault District (restricted)
- **4 cable car lines** connecting all major districts (see [[Infrastructure]])
- **~3,000 city-registered Clanks** (indentured, working toward freedom)
- **~400 free Clanks** (earned freedom or fled and had implants removed)
- **6 brownouts** in the past year — the last one was a full blackout lasting several minutes
- **3 Council factions**: Conservators, Illuminists, Expansionists — all gridlocked
- **Power coverage**: Radiant Core and Academic Quarter (full, reliable) → Lamplight Ward and Halfling Quarter (mostly reliable) → Manufactories (rationed) → Shadowside (none, never had it)

## The Sound of Lumencrest

There is a hum. Not loud — you barely notice it consciously — but it is always there. A low, steady electrical tone that comes from the older sections of the Lumen Network infrastructure, the distribution nodes, the street lamp casings. Visitors notice it immediately and can't stop noticing it. Residents stopped hearing it years ago. The first time a brownout hits and the hum suddenly *stops*, the silence feels wrong in a way that's hard to explain. Residents go quiet. They listen to the absence. Then the lights come back, the hum resumes, and everyone pretends to go back to normal.

## Lumencrest at Night

Lumencrest does not have a night. It has a second daytime that's slightly quieter than the first. The street lamps run on the Lumen Network — bright, steady, electric white-gold light — and every shop, tavern, and factory that can afford to do so stays open around the clock. The city runs in shifts. The Manufactories work day and night rotations. The Lamplight Ward's entertainment quarter hits peak business around the third hour of the morning. The Halfling Quarter's bakeries start production before dawn, filling the surrounding streets with warmth and smell. Shadowside is the only district that actually goes dark — and in Shadowside, that darkness is used deliberately.

## Lumencrest and the Clanks

Lumencrest created the Clank. This is not disputed. The Luminarium developed the first artificially-constructed sentient beings roughly 180 years ago as a solution to labor demand the city couldn't meet with organic workers alone. The city's legal position is that Clanks created by Lumencrest's facilities are city property, assigned to a term of labor service that earns them eventual freedom — they call it **indentured service**, not slavery, and the distinction matters to them even if it doesn't much change the daily reality for the Clanks involved.

A registered Lumencrest Clank wears a simple robe-style garment in grey or blue — not a uniform exactly, more a common practical clothing issued by the city. More importantly, every Lumencrest Clank has a small arcane implant installed during construction that can be scanned by authorized officials. The scan returns one of three readings: **SERVING** (with time remaining), **FREE** (term completed), or **FLAGGED** (escaped before term completion). 

Clanks are scanned at the city gates if identified entering. A FLAGGED reading means immediate detention. This is why escaped Clanks never come back to Lumencrest unless they've had the implant surgically removed — an expensive, painful procedure available in a few places on the continent, notably Ashenveil.

There is a small but growing movement within the city — centered in the Academic Quarter and Shadowside — arguing the entire system is slavery regardless of the terminology used. The Council officially dismisses this. Unofficially, the Illuminist faction is beginning to quietly discuss reform, partly on moral grounds and partly because free Clank workers might be more productive than indentured ones.
