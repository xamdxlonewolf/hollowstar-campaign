# Shadowside

**Population:** ~15,000  
**Power Status:** None. Shadowside has never been on the Lumen Network. The city planned to extend it here repeatedly and never did. Residents use candles, oil lamps, coal braziers, and small wood-fired heating units.  
**Cable Car:** No service. The nearest stations are on the southern edge of Shadowside, where it borders Lamplight Ward.  
**Access:** Open. No one checks anything here.

---

## Overview

Shadowside is the city's oldest embarrassment and its most honest neighbourhood. It exists at the edge of a city that defines itself by its light, unlit. It houses 15,000 people who were supposed to eventually get Network access and were told this repeatedly for six centuries. They stopped believing it roughly four centuries ago.

The result is a district that has adapted completely to its own reality. Shadowside doesn't need the Lumen Network. That is not to say its residents don't want it — some do, some have given up caring — but the neighbourhood functions without it, has always functioned without it, and is currently watching the rest of the city have a crisis about power instability with the particular dry interest of people who never had power to lose.

The district has two faces. The surface face — during daylight — is a dense, slightly chaotic residential and commercial area. The buildings are older than most of the city, some predating the Accord, many structurally questionable by Network standards but still standing. The streets are narrower and less organized than the planned districts. Traders operate from whatever space they can use — doorways, folding tables, blankets spread on the ground. The Maze Quarter, in Shadowside's heart, is a tangle of alleys and half-covered passages where newcomers get lost within minutes and locals navigate by memory.

The night face is different. When the rest of Lumencrest lights up from its Network lamps, Shadowside goes dark — genuinely dark, the kind of dark that only candle and lamp can break. The Night Market emerges, running openly along the main thoroughfare, selling everything from salvage goods to exotic animals to things it's better not to look too closely at. The district's underground connections surface: people who move between Shadowside and the literal underground tunnels beneath the city use the darkness as cover. The city watch patrols here minimally after dark. This is a calculated decision — the Council knows that heavy-handed policing of Shadowside would cost more in social stability than it would gain.

## Sub-Districts

### The Maze Quarter
The densest part of Shadowside, in the district's heart. Streets that narrow to shoulder-width passages, buildings that connect across the gap overhead, courtyards accessible only through unmarked doors. Navigation without local knowledge is genuinely difficult. Navigation with local knowledge is an advantage people in the Maze Quarter guard carefully.

**The Maze's function:** The district within the district. This is where the Night Market spills into permanent, less public trade. Every form of illegal good and service in Lumencrest has a contact point somewhere in the Maze. Finding the right contact requires introduction. Finding an introduction requires spending time in the district and proving you're not city watch. The process is not fast and is not supposed to be.

### Shantytown (Northern Edge)
The informal housing on Shadowside's northern edge against the city wall — structures built from salvage, maintained by residents who have no formal tenancy, theoretically cleared by the city periodically and rebuilt faster than they're removed. The most economically precarious part of Lumencrest above ground. The city watch clears it occasionally when it becomes too visible. It regrows within weeks.

### The Night Market Thoroughfare
The main north-south road through Shadowside is a permanent market during daylight — salvage goods, cheap food, second-hand everything. After dark it transforms: the permanent traders pack up, the Night Market traders arrive, and the thoroughfare runs as an open market selling goods that don't need daylight for a different reason.

**The Night Market sells:** Salvage and second-hand goods (legitimate), food and prepared meals (legitimate and excellent — Shadowside street food is some of the best in the city), black-market goods (contraband from other regions, things that avoided the gate check), substances of varying legality, stolen items presented as salvage, and information. Information is the Night Market's most valuable product and most experienced dealers in it operate here.

## Key Locations

**The Dark Lantern** — Shadowside's most important tavern, which in this context means a place where every kind of person in the district eventually comes and where the conversations are worth hearing. Run by a Ribbet named Solt who has been here for thirty years and who the city watch treats with careful neutrality. The ale is local, the food is good, and the curtained back section is where deals get made that shouldn't be made in full view. The Dark Lantern is the first introduction most visitors get to Shadowside's actual function.

**The Free Clank Cooperative** — a small building on the Maze Quarter's western edge that functions as a combination workshop, meeting space, and safe house for free and escaped Clanks in Lumencrest. Theoretically legal (free Clanks are free citizens). Practically watched by the city. The Cooperative repairs and resells repurposed machinery, which generates income and provides cover for its other functions. The escaped Clank community uses the Cooperative as a first contact point. The removal of arcane implants is not performed here — that's too risky. But the Cooperative knows who performs it, elsewhere in the city, for people who need to know.

**Pauper's Gate** — the northern city gate, maintained poorly relative to the southern and western gates, used by those who prefer less observation. No electric lighting at the gate itself. Guards present but fewer than other gates. The understanding on both sides is that this gate handles a different kind of traffic and everyone is making a calculation about how hard to look.

**The Salvation Hearth** — a charitable institution operated by the First Flame faith (Unified Ember denomination) providing meals, basic medical care, and short-term shelter for Shadowside's most economically vulnerable residents. One of the few points of sustained outside-world contact in the district. The priests here know the neighbourhood better than anyone from outside. They do not share what they know but they do assess people who come in carefully.

## Where to Sleep

**The Night's Rest** *(main thoroughfare)* — Shadowside's largest inn, which means twelve rooms and a common room. No electric lighting; oil lamps throughout. 4 silver for a bed in the common room, 6 silver for a private room. No questions asked. The rooms are clean by district standards. The lock on the door is good. The door itself is not.

**The Maze Quarter boarding houses** — several informal operations where a room can be had for 3-4 silver a night in a private residence. These are not inns in any formal sense. They are households who rent space to people who need it without asking why.

**The Salvation Hearth emergency beds** — free, for those in genuine need. The Unified Ember priests who run the Hearth are not naive about who uses these beds. They consider it part of the mission. They will sometimes ask for help in return — small tasks, errands, nothing that compromises the guest's situation. This is understood.
