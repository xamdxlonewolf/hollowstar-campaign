# The Radiant Core — Expanded District Guide

## Points of Interest, Vendors, and NPCs

_This document expands on the Radiant Core as established in [[01_Radiant_Core]], adding the locations, people, and daily texture needed to run the district at the table. The Radiant Core is Lumencrest's center of power — political, financial, and architectural. It never dims. It never openly acknowledges that everything below it is failing. Getting inside it, in any meaningful sense, is the work of reputation, leverage, and very careful social navigation. Cross-reference [[07_Vault_District]] for the Heart's physical location beneath this district, [[06_Academic_Quarter]] for the Board of Research's relationship with the classified programs, and [[Factions_and_Reputation]] for the three Council factions' full detail._

---

## Running the Radiant Core at the Table

The Radiant Core is the only district in Lumencrest where the city's crisis is not visible. Elsewhere — Shadowside's dark streets, the Manufactories' dimming floors, the Academic Quarter's midnight labs — the Heart's decline is present as a physical reality. In the Core, the lamps burn at full brightness, the estates glow from within, and the streets are clean and wide and lit by infrastructure that has never faltered.

This is not an accident. The Core's power priority is a policy decision that was made decades ago and has been renewed every year since, with decreasing public acknowledgment that a decision is being made. The residents of the Core did not ask for this protection specifically; they simply built their lives around infrastructure that works, and the infrastructure works because the Network's densest connections run directly beneath their streets. The political fact and the physical fact are entangled in ways that neither the Council nor the Core's residents are eager to examine.

**What players feel when they arrive:** The light is the first thing — cooler, whiter, more even than Lamplight Ward's amber. No flicker. No variation. Shadow falls straight. Combined with the wide streets and the quiet, purposeful foot traffic, the Core has the quality of a city that is operating normally, which is the most disorienting thing about it for players who have been spending time in the districts below.

**The guard presence:** Not aggressive, not theatrical. Two guards at every significant intersection, paired, with the quality of people who are present rather than patrolling — they are not moving through the district looking for something, they are stationed in it because they belong to it. They watch newcomers with a professional assessment that is not hostile and is not welcoming. It is the look of people doing their job in a place where they have every advantage.

**The social layer:** The Core's public streets are accessible. The Core's meaningful spaces are not. The Accord Chamber's public gallery, the Hall of Records, the Charter Office, the cable car station — these are open. The Council Member offices, the estate interiors, the private dining clubs, the rooms above the restaurant with no sign where the decisions that precede the Council votes are made — these require access that does not come from the street. The Core is a district where social currency is the actual currency, and earning it takes longer than any other form of entry except violence, which is not recommended.

**The implicit pressure:** The Core's residents and its political class are operating under a specific form of stress that is different from every other district's. They are not experiencing the crisis directly — they experience it as a management problem, a political challenge, a situation that requires handling. The gap between this and the reality experienced by the rest of the city is growing, and several of the Core's more perceptive residents are aware that the gap is itself a political vulnerability. Theron Ashwick has built his entire current strategy around this gap. Mira Ashton is trying to close it before it closes her.

## The Accord Chamber

_(Referenced in [[01_Radiant_Core]] — expanded here as a full playable location)_

**What it is:** The city's architectural centerpiece and the political stage where the three factions perform their gridlock for anyone in the gallery. 400 years old, built at the height of Lumencrest's confidence, lit from within at night so the dome is visible from miles outside the city. The gap between what it was designed to represent and what it currently contains is one of the most vivid single images of the city's situation.

**The physical space:** A grand circular chamber with tiered seating around a central floor — the Council table, the faction sections, the Chairperson's position at the chamber's formal head. The gallery runs above the Council floor on three sides. The fourth side contains the Chamber's formal entrance and the Vault Guard officers who are always present for Council sessions and who are not decorative.

**Getting into the gallery:** Public gallery seating is available for formal Council sessions, announced three days in advance by posting at the main gates and Merchant Square. First come. The gallery seats 80. Sessions that involve public interest topics — the Heart situation, power rationing, anything that has reached the broadsheets — fill in the first thirty minutes after the posting goes up. Sessions on administrative matters fill less reliably and the regulars in the back rows are worth knowing.

**What players can learn from the gallery:** A single session, watched carefully, reveals:

- Which faction is currently pressing and which is managing — the body language of confidence versus management is readable across a chamber floor.
- The specific Council members who are independent of faction alignment on specific issues — they are identifiable because they vote inconsistently with their section position.
- What the Council is not talking about, which is often more informative than what it is. A session on administrative road maintenance that follows a week of Academic Quarter rumors about the Heart timeline is a session where something is being avoided.
- Theron Ashwick's specific technique: he speaks less than his influence would suggest, and what he says is short, precise, and always addressed to the one or two Council members he is currently moving rather than to the chamber as a whole. Watching who he looks at when he's not speaking is the actual intelligence.

**The Council regulars:** Certain individuals attend every public gallery session — not Council members but political professionals whose job is to watch. **Demi Wren** (see NPCs), a political intelligence operative who works for a specific estate family, has been in the gallery's third row for four years. She will not acknowledge this to strangers but will, over time, become a useful contact for players trying to understand the Council's internal dynamics.

## The Council District

_(Referenced in [[01_Radiant_Core]] — expanded here)_

**What it is:** The administrative quarter of the Core — a cluster of connected working buildings that house the Council's offices, committee rooms, the city administrative archives, and the bureaucratic infrastructure of a major city's governance. Less grand than the Accord Chamber; these are buildings where work happens rather than authority is displayed. The aesthetic is confident stone and good electric lighting and the specific smell of a large administrative operation — paper and lamp oil and the particular indoor quality of a building that has been occupied continuously for centuries.

**Getting around the Council District:** The public-facing offices — Charter Office, Hall of Records, administrative reception for the Council Member offices — are open during daytime hours to any city resident with legitimate business. The working floors above and behind these public faces require either appointment, authorization, or a contact who can sign you in. The corridors that connect the Council Member offices are technically accessible to anyone who has passed the reception desk, which has produced a long history of creative interpretations of "legitimate business."

---

### The Charter Office

**Location:** Ground floor of the Council District's eastern building. Open during administrative hours.

**What it is:** Where city licenses, residency registrations, official documentation, and formal applications are processed. The Charter Office is the city's administrative interface — the place where individuals and organizations interact with the city's formal systems. It is staffed by clerks whose professional character ranges from the barely functional to the impressively competent, and players will encounter both.

**What's available here:**

- Residency registration — formal Lumencrest residency, with all the rights and obligations this creates. Takes 3 days, costs 2 silver marks, requires proof of accommodation and income that is interpreted with variable strictness depending on the clerk.
- Business and practice licensing — the formal documentation required to operate any professional service in Lumencrest. More expensive and slower than residency registration, more useful for players with long-term operations in the city.
- Document verification — the Charter Office will officially verify whether specific documents are genuine, for a fee, which is useful for players who need to know whether something Inkhand produced will hold up to scrutiny.
- Records access requests — formal requests to access city records beyond the Hall of Records' public collection. These take 5-10 days and are frequently denied; the useful outcome is usually a partial disclosure that tells players what exists even if it doesn't give them the content.

**The clerk worth knowing:** **Finn Aldric** (see NPCs) — a senior Charter Office clerk who has been here for twenty years and has developed, in that time, a very specific relationship with the city's formal documentation system. He processes what comes in front of him. What comes in front of him is influenced by several people who have established a relationship with him over years. He is not corrupt in the dramatic sense. He is a person who understands that paperwork is power and who has decided, privately, that some of the people who need paperwork to work correctly deserve it more than the system would naturally provide.

### The Hall of Records

**Location:** The Council District's western building, ground and first floor. Open during administrative hours.

**What it is:** Public legal records, property records, Accord treaty documentation, city administrative history going back 400 years. The largest civilian records collection in the city, organized by the city archivist's office and maintained continuously. Reading is free for any city resident. Copying or removing anything requires authorization that takes 3-5 days.

**What's in here that matters:**

_Property records:_ Every building in Lumencrest, its ownership history, any liens or legal complications. Players who want to know who owns a specific building — the Smoke Den's building, the Unseen Guild's warehouse, a specific estate compound — can find it here. The current owners of record are accurate; the beneficial ownership behind corporate or trust arrangements is less clear and sometimes deliberately obscured.

_Accord treaty documentation:_ The original Heartland Accord, all amendments, and the subsequent bilateral arrangements between Accord cities. Relevant to players who need to understand the legal framework around the Heart — specifically, whether the city's treatment of Hollostar technology is consistent with Accord obligations it has signed.

_City administrative history:_ The decisions that built Lumencrest — the Network's original construction authorization, the power priority decisions that have been renewed annually, the Clank service system's legal establishment. The documentation for all of these exists here, including the specific Council decisions that established the Core's power priority in a way that the current Council treats as infrastructure rather than policy. Finding this requires knowing it exists; the archivist's index describes it accurately but the description requires interpretation.

_The gap:_ As with the Academic Quarter's Archives, there is a gap in the Hall of Records. The Heart's classified assessment documentation — the internal studies that produced the official 3-5 year timeline — is referenced but not present. The references note that the full documentation is held under Research Board classification. The gap is smaller and less visible than the Academic Quarter's but is present to a researcher who knows to look for it.

**The city archivist:** **Hen Brask** (see NPCs) — thirty years in this building, knows the collection completely, and has developed a very specific philosophy about what public records are for.

### The Council Member Offices

**Location:** Three wings of the Council District's main building, organized by faction — eastern wing (Conservators), western wing (Illuminists, currently also housing the Chair's offices), northern wing (Expansionists). The staircase arrangement means the wings are accessible from the public-facing ground floor but require passing the reception desk and the reception desk's assessment.

**The political geography of the offices:** Each Councilor has an office, a secretary, and a reception space. The faction wings share committee rooms, meeting halls, and the informal spaces — the common corridors, the small kitchens, the window alcoves where the significant informal conversations happen — that are where real political work gets done. The physical arrangement of the building produces specific social consequences: Conservators and Expansionists never share a corridor, which means their coalition-building requires formal meetings; Illuminists and both other factions share the central staircase, which is where Mira Ashton does some of her most effective work.

**Getting to a Councilor:**

- Through the Council calendar (public, posted weekly at the main gates): Councilors have public office hours on rotating days. The hours are real and are used by constituents and lobbyists. The conversation available in a public office hour is genuine but not private.
- Through their secretary (private appointment): A letter requesting an appointment goes through the secretary, who assesses the requester's legitimacy. Senior Councilors' secretaries are very good at this. Junior Councilors' secretaries are somewhat more permeable.
- Through the social layer (estate events, private dining): The conversations that can happen at dinner in an estate cannot happen in an office. Getting to a Councilor socially is harder than getting to them officially and produces more useful access.
- Through connection with Demi Wren or equivalent political operators: People who understand the Council's internal geography can, for appropriate consideration, arrange the right conversation at the right moment. This is the most useful path and the most expensive.

### The Luminarium Administrative Offices (Core)

**Location:** A specific building in the Council District's southern section, connected to the main administrative complex by a covered walkway.

**What it is:** The administrative governance layer of the Luminarium that is deliberately situated here rather than in the Academic Quarter — in the Core because the Research Board is a Council-adjacent body and its members have Council connections they use. The Board of Research meets here twice monthly and makes decisions about research program authorizations that the actual researchers in the Academic Quarter then implement.

**The Board of Research:** Six members. Three Illuminist-connected, two Conservator-connected, one genuinely independent (Professor **Lina Voss** — no relation to any other Voss in this document; the name is unfortunately common in Lumencrest). The Board approved the classified programs. The Board was told less than the full picture when it approved them. Several Board members have since developed questions they haven't formally raised because raising them would require acknowledging that they approved something they didn't fully understand.

The independent member, Lina Voss, has been the swing vote on every significant Board decision for three years. She is currently composing a formal inquiry to Rector Marke about the third-floor classified programs. She has not sent it yet. She is deciding whether sending it through official channels is the correct approach or whether the official channels will produce a response that closes the question without answering it.

**Professor Lina Voss** — see NPCs.

## The Estates

_(Referenced in [[01_Radiant_Core]] — expanded here with social access mechanics)_

**What they are:** The walled residential compounds of hereditary Council families, major manufacturing guild shareholders, and Lumencrest's old money. High walls, private security, gardens that are an affront to the districts below. Not open. Not accessible from the street. Completely consequential to the campaign's political layer.

**Estate social life as a system:** The estates operate through invitations — dinners, salons, private receptions, the after-Council drinks that are where the real alignment discussions happen. The invitation system has specific mechanics:

_Who gives invitations:_ Estate family heads, their designated social secretaries, and — for the largest events — the organizer of a specific social function. Invitations to major estate events circulate through a network of social obligation and assessed interest: you are invited because you did something worth noticing, or because someone who was already invited decided you should be present, or because the host wants something from you that requires getting you in the room.

_How players get invitations:_

- **Social capital from other districts:** Being genuinely known and respected in Lamplight Ward, the Academic Quarter, or the Halfling Quarter creates enough of a reputation that estate families who track the city's social landscape notice. Bram Kettlemarch vouching for someone carries weight. Aldous Wren's professional recommendation carries weight. Willa Casp's endorsement in the right social context carries weight. None of these are guarantees; they are reasons to be considered.
- **A professional service:** Providing something an estate family needs — information, a problem resolved, a connection made that benefited them — is the most direct path. The estate families are not above transactional relationships; they simply dress them in social language.
- **Theron Ashwick or Mira Ashton's direct invitation:** The most powerful invitations in the Core come from faction leadership. These require that the player characters have become significant enough to be useful to one or both of the major faction leaders. When this happens, the invitation is also a signal — being seen at a specific estate event as one faction leader's guest positions you in the Core's social map in ways that have consequences.
- **The Quiet Market or Velvetongue connection:** Several estate families are Quiet Market clients or use the Silver Thread's Lamplight Ward establishment. Players with connections in those networks can leverage the connection — carefully, because bringing that leverage overtly would be socially catastrophic.

**What's inside:** The estate interiors are, in aggregate, where the real Radiant Core exists. The gardens, the private dining rooms, the library sitting rooms where faction members meet without the formal structure of the Council floor — this is where Lumencrest's political life happens in its most functional form. Players who achieve regular estate access will find themselves in the room where decisions are made before they become Council votes.

### The Three Significant Estates

**The Ashton Estate:** The Illuminist faction Chair's family compound. Mira Ashton was born here, has lived here her entire life, and conducts her most important work here rather than in the Council offices. The estate's library is where she meets individually with Council members she's trying to move. The dining room is where the Illuminist faction's strategy sessions happen. The garden, in good weather, is where she takes the conversations that she specifically does not want overheard — the estate is assumed to be monitored, and the garden, with ambient noise, is the best available privacy.

**The Ashwick Estate:** Theron Ashwick's compound, the largest in the Core. He has renovated and expanded it over fifteen years in ways that reflect his understanding of what social power looks like — the reception rooms are impressive without being ostentatious, calibrated to make guests feel received by someone significant without feeling intimidated. His private dining events are the Core's best social intelligence investment: he invites specific people together for specific reasons and the conversations that happen are the ones he has designed to happen. Players who attend an Ashwick dinner should assume that the seating arrangement and the guest list are intentional.

**The Vael Estate:** Sevan Vael's compound — the House Vael presence in the Core. Smaller than the Ashton or Ashwick estates, more internally focused. The Vael family entertains selectively and for specific purposes. Lyra Vael's presence here is occasional — she moves between the estate and contexts the Core's social map doesn't account for. Players who reach the Vael estate's inner social layer will find a family that is more prepared for the city's collapse than any other single institution, and a patriarch who knows more than he acknowledges and a daughter whose activities he has chosen not to examine.

### Aurum

_(Referenced in [[01_Radiant_Core]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** A side street off the Core's main plaza, between the Council District and the estates' western edge. No sign. A brass door handle that is the only thing on the exterior that is not stone. Twelve seats inside.

**What it is:** Arguable the finest restaurant in Lumencrest and possibly in the Heartlands. The base file's description is accurate: it does not advertise, it has no sign, it requires a reservation made weeks in advance through a referral from a previous guest. The food is extraordinary. The conversations over it are frequently more valuable.

**How it works:** _Reservations:_ Made only by referral — a current guest must recommend you to the proprietor, who assesses the recommendation and either extends an invitation or does not. There is no waiting list in the formal sense. You are either known to the network or you are not, and you do not become known by asking.

_The twelve seats:_ Four two-top tables arranged for privacy — the acoustics of the room are such that conversations at one table do not carry to another, which is a design feature that the proprietor arranged at significant expense and that is part of the value proposition. Two four-top tables for groups, which require explicit request. The seating is not reserved by preference; the proprietor assigns it based on the evening's full guest list, which means the composition of any given dinner service is curated.

_The food:_ The menu is not given to guests. The courses arrive. The proprietor determines the menu based on what is available and what the evening's composition suggests. The ingredients come from the Halfling Quarter's finest supply contacts, the wine from a specific merchant the proprietor has used for twenty years, and the preparation from a kitchen staff of three who have been here as long as the restaurant has. The result is what you would produce if you had the best possible ingredients, complete freedom from constraints, and twenty years of practice. It is not exotic or theatrical. It is simply correct.

_What happens here:_ Aurum is where the Core's most important off-record conversations occur. The combination of privacy, the social selection of the guest list, and the specific atmosphere of a meal that requires presence produces conversations that cannot happen elsewhere. Coalition agreements that preceded three Council votes were discussed at the two-top tables. The Vael family's initial arrangement with the Unseen Guild was formalized over dinner here. Players who reach Aurum's social access level have reached the level at which the city's real decisions are made.

**The proprietor:** **Cass Vorn** — see NPCs.

### The Central Hub Station

_(Referenced in [[01_Radiant_Core]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** A covered platform complex built above street level in the middle of the Core's main plaza. All four cable car lines converge here.

**What it is:** The only place in the Radiant Core where the city genuinely mixes — the station sees everyone, because the cable car is the way into and out of the Core for anyone who doesn't have estate transportation. Servants from the estates, Council clerks, contractors, academic researchers visiting the administrative offices, visiting merchants with Council business, players from any district — all on the same platforms, waiting for the same cars.

**The station as an intelligence location:** The mixing is real and the watch presence is real. The station is monitored consistently — city watch permanently stationed, Vault Guard passing through at irregular intervals, the specific category of official observer who is present without uniform and without acknowledgment. This makes it one of the worst places in the city to conduct any business that shouldn't be observed. It also makes it one of the best places to observe who is moving between districts and in what configuration — which faction members travel together, which visit which district on which schedule, who arrives from Shadowside in a car that also contains a Vault Guard officer who seems coincidental and isn't.

**The station staff:** The cable car station's operational staff — the car operators, the platform managers, the maintenance technicians — are a distinct community within the Core. They are not estate people and not Council people; they are working professionals who run the infrastructure that connects the city. Their community has the specific social texture of people who see everything that passes through a major transit hub and have developed the professional habit of not visibly noticing it. **Orren Tetch** (see NPCs), the station's senior platform manager, is the person in the Core who knows the most about who goes where without being positioned to profit from that knowledge directly. This is a significant resource for players who develop the relationship.

### The Founder's Rest

_(Referenced in [[01_Radiant_Core]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** The Core's south edge, near the main gate from Lamplight Ward. The only inn in the district.

**What it is:** The Radiant Core's sole accommodation option for visitors without estate invitations — aggressively priced (5 gold marks for a basic room, 15 for a good one) and worth it for the specific access it provides. Staying at the Founder's Rest means being present in the Core, visible in the Core's social geography, and available for the chance encounters that don't happen if you're sleeping in Lamplight Ward.

**The clientele:** Visiting Accord delegations, wealthy merchants with Council business, people whose expense accounts belong to someone else, and occasional players. The common room — small, well-appointed, open until midnight — is where the visiting professional class of the Accord's major cities conducts its pre-meeting conversations. What is said here is said by people who have concluded they are in a safe and private space, which they are not — the proprietor is discreet but perceptive and the walls are thinner than they look.

**The proprietor:** **Davathe Colm** — see NPCs.

### High-End Tailoring — The Three Core Shops

The Core has three tailoring establishments that serve the Council and estate families exclusively — appointment only, prices not posted, access by referral or existing client relationship. They matter to players primarily as social access points:

**Aldren's** — the most prestigious. Has dressed four consecutive Council Chairs. Players who arrive wearing something from Aldren's — obtained through a loan, a commissioned piece from a contact, or by being legitimately wealthy — are immediately read as Core-connected by anyone from this district.

**Vera Colm's** — Davathe Colm's sister's establishment, which explains the family relationship to the Founder's Rest. Slightly less prestigious than Aldren's, more accessible for new clients who are clearly serious. **Vera** (see NPCs) is the social tailor — she maintains relationships with her clients and the relationships carry information.

**The House** — the most discreet of the three. Serves estate families who prefer not to be seen at the same establishment as Council members. The proprietor's name is not public information. What the establishment produces is indistinguishable from Aldren's best work and occasionally better.

## Notable NPCs — The Radiant Core

---

### Mira Ashton

**Role:** Council Chair; Illuminist faction leader; the person nominally in charge of the gridlock **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 53 **Location:** Council Chair's office, western wing. The Accord Chamber during sessions. A small restaurant on the Core's south edge — **The Courtyard Table** — where she eats alone twice a week when the week allows, which it increasingly doesn't.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has been managing a crisis through sheer force of competence for three years and who is running a specific kind of reserve that will eventually run out. Sharp, measured, exactly as well-dressed as her position requires and not more. The quality of someone who makes no extraneous moves — every gesture, word, and social decision is considered, which produces both effectiveness and a quality of controlled exhaustion.

**Personality:** The base file's description is accurate and complete. She does not waste words. She will not commit to anything she cannot deliver. She is running on political debt she has accumulated over a decade and she knows the balance is getting low. What the base file doesn't capture is the specific quality of her intelligence — she is not a strategic genius in the formal sense, she is genuinely perceptive about people and what they will and won't do, and she has been using this perception to hold the Council's gridlock at a stable point rather than letting it tip into something worse. This is hard, invisible work. Nobody sees the crises that don't happen.

**What she wants:** Someone to solve the Heart problem so she doesn't have to keep managing the consequences. She has been trying to solve it through the research programs and she is beginning to suspect the research programs are producing something she hasn't been briefed on, which is Petra Vane's finding, which she is correct about. She is also managing the Expansionists' growing pressure for a military vote that she believes would destabilize the city in ways the power crisis alone would not.

**Working with her:** She is accessible through the public office hours process — she takes them seriously and the conversations are real. What makes her a more active ally is demonstrating either that players have information she doesn't have and that information is accurate, or that they have done something that reduced the problems she's currently managing. She will not give players access to classified information. She will, if she decides they are reliable, tell them where to look for it and what questions to ask. The most direct path to her genuine engagement is bringing her evidence of either Petra Vane's finding or the Shard Division's operations — either of these would change her current situation significantly and she would respond with proportional action.

**The Courtyard Table:** A 14-seat restaurant on the Core's south edge with no particular distinction except that she has been going there for fifteen years and the staff leave her alone. Players who know she eats here and arrive correctly — not obviously waiting for her, seated with their own business, making themselves worth noticing rather than approaching her directly — will find that she initiates the conversation when she decides to, and that conversation is more honest than anything available in her office.

### Theron Ashwick

**Role:** Senior Expansionist Councilor; the most effective political operator in the Council; building toward a military vote **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 58 **Location:** Expansionist wing of the Council offices. His estate, for the dinner events. The Accord Chamber gallery when he's watching sessions he's not participating in. Occasionally Aurum.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has decided what he looks like and maintained it for twenty years — well-dressed in the understated Core style, physically fit in the way of someone who made that decision deliberately, with the quality of total social presence that effective political operators develop. He makes everyone he's talking to feel like they are the person he most wanted to speak with today. This is a skill and it is conscious and it has been producing results for decades.

**Personality:** Charming, patient, dangerous in the specific way of someone who is right about the things he's right about and wrong about the consequences of the things he's right about. His fundamental read of the situation is not incorrect: the city is in crisis, the research programs are not producing results on the necessary timeline, and the Expansionist faction's proposal to address the energy supply through military action against the Endless Eye is a coherent response to a real problem. What he has not fully accounted for is what happens after — the political and human cost of the campaign he's building toward, and the possibility that the situation is more complex than the problem he has diagnosed.

**What he wants:** The military authorization vote to come before the next significant brownout while public anger in the Manufactories is at a level that makes the Council's non-Illuminist members receptive. He has been managing the vote count for six months. He is currently three votes short of passage. He knows who those three votes are and he is working toward each of them through the estate dinner schedule.

**What he knows:** The Council's internal vote count more accurately than Mira Ashton does, because he has been building toward this specific vote longer than she has been defending against it. He also knows something about the Shard Division that he has received through House Vael — Lyra Vael has told him a version of the experiments that he has chosen to interpret as evidence that Hollostar technology is more controllable than the Luminarium claims, which supports his position that military action to secure additional Hollostar resources is viable. He does not know that the version Lyra gave him is curated.

**Working with him:** He will be warm and genuinely interested in players who are interesting. He will tell them his position clearly and argue for it persuasively and will not pretend he's doing anything other than what he's doing. Getting information from him requires either being valuable to him — providing something he needs for the vote count — or being genuinely interesting to argue with about the underlying situation. He will, in the right conversation, reveal more about what he knows and what he's been told than he intends to, because he is confident in his position and confidence produces disclosure.

### Sera Coldwell

**Role:** Head of the Vault Guard; the person standing between the Heart and everyone who wants access to it **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 61 **Location:** The Vault District, primarily. The Council District for briefings. The Central Hub Station at irregular intervals for reasons she does not explain.

**Appearance:** Forty years in city security have produced a person who is entirely composed and entirely unimpressed. She carries no visible authority markers off-duty — no insignia, no formal clothing outside the Council briefings — and is recognizable to people who know her and invisible to people who don't, which is a professional quality she has maintained deliberately.

**Personality:** The base file's description: she has seen everyone and been impressed by almost no one. This is not contempt — it is the result of forty years of watching people manage their fear of significant things, and the pattern is regular enough that novelty is rare. She is not dismissive; she is calibrated. She takes threats seriously and non-threats efficiently. She does not perform toughness or authority; she has both and doesn't need to perform them.

**What she knows:** The Vault Guard's monitoring extends significantly beyond the Vault itself — Network activity anomalies across the city, the movement of people who shouldn't be in certain spaces, the specific signals that indicate when the Shard Division's tunnel activity is occurring. She has been building a picture of what is moving under Lumencrest's streets for six months. She does not know what the Shard Division is. She knows that something is using the pre-city tunnel passages at a frequency inconsistent with recreational Dust supply and at locations inconsistent with ordinary smuggling. She has not brought this to the Council because she does not have enough to make a specific allegation and she does not want the allegation to produce a political response before she understands what she's actually looking at.

**The unauthorized access:** She granted one unauthorized access to the Vault in her forty-year career. She will not discuss it. Players who find evidence of who received it and why will have found something significant to the campaign's background architecture.

**Working with her:** She is not accessible through social channels — she does not do estate dinners or the Accord Chamber gallery. She is accessible through the Vault District (see [[15_Vault_District_Expanded]]) or through the Council briefing schedule if players have established a legitimate security-related context. What moves her is evidence — specific, verifiable evidence of something that is actually happening beneath the city that affects the Heart's security. Players who bring her this will find her the most operationally effective ally in Lumencrest, because she has authority and she uses it without hesitation when she's certain.

### Sevan Vael

**Role:** House Vael patriarch; Council member (Conservator-aligned independent); the patient long game **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 54 **Location:** His Council office (Conservator wing). The Vael Estate. Aurum, occasionally.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has been playing a very long game and whose patience has become physical — settled, unhurried, with the quality of someone who has stopped being surprised by events because he predicted most of them years ago. Expensively but not ostentatiously dressed. The specific quality of social presence that three generations of deliberate positioning produces.

**Personality:** The base file's description is the full picture: a man who has spent three decades positioning his family to catch Lumencrest when it falls. He is not evil; he is a strategic actor operating in a system where strategic actors are the ones who survive. He has made decisions about what he won't know — specifically about Lyra's activities — that he would describe as appropriate delegation and that are, more accurately, deniability management. He is not stupid about this. He knows the risk. He has calculated that the benefit outweighs the risk unless something goes catastrophically wrong, at which point he will not hesitate to sacrifice Lyra to protect the House.

**What he wants:** The situation to develop to the point where the Vaels' position allows them to be the stabilizing force when the city fractures. He is not actively causing the fracture — he does not need to. He is positioning around it. He is three years ahead of where he needs to be, which is the correct amount of time ahead for someone operating at his level.

**Working with him:** He will be cordial, engaged, and apparently helpful to players who are doing things that don't threaten his position. He will provide specific information and specific access in exchange for specific value — the exchange is transactional even when it's conducted in social language. The most important thing about Sevan Vael is that he is one of the very few people in the city who has a complete strategic picture — who knows about the crisis, the factions, the Guild operation, and the Shard Division in outline terms — and who has not told anyone, because telling anyone would change the dynamic he's been building toward. Players who figure this out and decide to change that dynamic will find him either a significant ally or a significant antagonist, depending on whether their goals align with what the Vaels want to catch.

### Demi Wren

**Role:** Political intelligence operative; consistent Accord Chamber gallery presence; works for the Harwick estate family **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 36 **Location:** Accord Chamber gallery, third row. The Courtyard Table, sometimes. The Expansionist wing corridor between 10am and noon most days — she is watching the foot traffic and has been for four years.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has made being unremarkable a professional project — appropriately dressed for the Core without being distinctive, with the quality of attention that is invisible because it looks like the mild interest of someone waiting for something routine. She is very good at what she does.

**Personality:** Professional and specific. She collects political intelligence for the Harwick estate family's Council interests and she is very good at it. She will share what she knows in exchange for what she doesn't know, at a calibrated price, with people she assesses as reliable sources. She is not mercenary in the dramatic sense — she does not sell to the highest bidder regardless of consequence. She is a professional with a primary client and a secondary information market that she manages carefully.

**What she knows:** The Council's internal political dynamics — vote counts, alliance states, which Councilors are being moved and by whom — more currently and accurately than most people outside the three faction leadership circles. She has been watching Theron Ashwick's dinner guest lists for six months and has a nearly complete picture of his current vote-count strategy, including the three votes he's three short by and which of them he believes is the most moveable. She also knows something happened in the Accord Chamber three months ago — a closed session she couldn't observe — and has been trying to determine its content since.

**Working with her:** She is the most accessible intelligence resource in the Core's political layer. She is findable (the gallery, the corridor) and she will engage with players who have something worth trading. The calibration is real — she won't pay more than information is worth, but she knows what it's worth accurately. Players who have been moving through the city's lower districts and accumulating knowledge of the actual ground-level situation have information she cannot access from her position, and this is her primary demand for trade.

### Finn Aldric

**Role:** Senior Charter Office clerk; twenty-year institutional knowledge; specific and careful helpfulness **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 47 **Location:** Charter Office, second desk from the left. He has been at this desk for twenty years.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has processed a great deal of paper for a great deal of time and whose relationship with paperwork has become philosophical rather than procedural. Tidy, efficient, with the quality of someone who moves through their professional environment without friction because they understand it completely.

**Personality:** He is not dramatic about his position. He processes what comes in front of him and, for people whose situations he has decided warrant it, he finds ways to make the system work better than the system's default would produce. He is careful about this. He has never done anything that is technically outside his authority. The interpretive range within his authority is, in his considered view, quite wide.

**What he can do:** Expedite documentation. Route applications to specific reviewers who are more likely to assess them generously. Flag that a specific file needs review in a way that causes the review to happen faster. Provide informal confirmation that a specific document's format will or won't pass verification before the formal application is submitted — saving significant time and cost. He cannot grant access that isn't authorized, fabricate documentation, or override decisions above his authority level. Within these limits, he is more useful than he appears.

**Working with him:** He has been approached seventeen times in twenty years by people who wanted him to do something improper. He declined all seventeen. He has extended his informal helpfulness to approximately forty people over that period, in ways that were technically appropriate and specifically useful. The distinction is clear to him. Players who approach him with a legitimate purpose and demonstrate genuine need will find, over several interactions, that the system works slightly better than it would for someone without a relationship with him.

### Hen Brask

**Role:** City archivist, Hall of Records; thirty years of the collection **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 62 **Location:** Hall of Records, first floor office. Present every administrative day.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone who has been living in paper for thirty years and who finds this entirely satisfactory — comfortable, slightly dusty, with the quality of total orientation within a complex environment. He knows where everything is in a 400-year collection the way Oswin Fletch knows the Archives.

**Personality:** He has a philosophy about public records: they exist to be used by the public they document, and his job is to ensure they are usable. This philosophy produces a quality of helpfulness that is unusual in an official institution — he will tell you what is in the collection, how to find it, and what adjacent records might bear on what you're looking for, because he believes this is what a public archivist does.

**What he knows:** The Hall of Records' full collection, including the gaps — specifically the gap in the Heart assessment documentation and the specific referencing structure that points to the classified location of the full files. He knows the gap exists because he indexed it. He has had specific opinions about the public's right to know what documents exist, if not their content, for thirty years, and he has expressed them through the completeness of his public index. He will tell a researcher that specific documents exist and are classified. He considers this within his role and does not require persuasion.

### Cass Vorn

**Role:** Proprietor of Aurum **Ancestry/Age:** Half-elf, appears 50s **Location:** Aurum's kitchen and dining room, every evening the restaurant operates.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone whose work is the expression of their character — precise, composed, with the specific quality of total environmental control that great restaurant operators develop. Their clothing is functional professional, never conspicuous. They are the least visible person in Aurum's dining room and the most significant.

**Personality:** Cass Vorn does not discuss the conversations that happen at Aurum's tables. This is the foundation of the restaurant's value and they have maintained it without exception for twenty years. They are warm to guests in the specific way of someone who considers the experience of being fed well an act of genuine care, and they are entirely opaque about everything else. What they know about the city's power structure — from twenty years of hearing it discuss itself over extraordinary food — is not available through the restaurant relationship.

**What they want:** Aurum to continue being what it is, which requires the city to remain stable enough for a twelve-seat restaurant to be the most coveted reservation in Lumencrest. They have a specific and private relationship with the city's continued existence that they do not discuss. What they have observed in twenty years of curated dinner service has produced conclusions they have not shared with anyone, about the city's direction, that they are sitting with in the way that Aldis Marke is sitting with Petra Vane's finding. The parallel is not coincidental; they know each other.

She will share this with people who have become genuine regulars and whom she has decided are approaching the situation honestly.

## Radiant Core Reputation Track

**Starting position:** 0 (Visitor). The Core is open to visitors. Being known here is a different matter entirely.

**Gaining standing:**

- Attending Accord Chamber gallery sessions consistently with demonstrated political literacy: +1
- Being endorsed or referred by a figure with existing Core standing (Aldous Wren, Bram Kettlemarch, Willa Casp, Aldis Marke each sufficient): +1
- Providing accurate intelligence to Mira Ashton or Demi Wren that proves out: +1
- Receiving an estate invitation and conducting yourself appropriately: +1
- Being acknowledged by Cass Vorn for referral to Aurum: +1
- Resolving a situation that directly benefited a Council member or estate family: +2

**Losing standing:**

- Causing visible disruption in a Core public space: -2
- Being identified as connected to the Unseen Guild without sufficient social insulation: -3
- Misrepresenting credentials to gain access and being discovered: -2
- Publicly associating with the Shard Division's operations: permanent exclusion

**Reputation unlocks:**

## Faction Presence — Radiant Core

**The three Council factions** — present as their primary operating environment. The Radiant Core is where the Conservators, Illuminists, and Expansionists actually exist as political forces rather than positions. Their internal dynamics, alliances, and pressure points are all navigable from here.

**The Vault Guard** — present and independent. Sera Coldwell's authority overlaps with but does not report to the Council in the ordinary sense. She reports to the full Council in plenary session, not to any faction, which is a constitutional arrangement that both preserves her independence and limits her political leverage.

**House Vael** — present as a fourth independent force that none of the three factions fully account for. Sevan's Conservator alignment is tactical rather than genuine; he is the only Council member whose allegiance is genuinely in question and both Mira Ashton and Theron Ashwick know this and have been unable to move him.

**The Unseen Guild** — present through the social layer. The Guild does not operate in the Core overtly, but its connections to estate families through the Quiet Market, the Velvetongue supply chain, and the specific Vael arrangement mean it has influence in the Core's inner social layer that is not publicly visible. Lyra Vael is the primary conduit.
