# The Underground — Expanded District Guide

## Points of Interest, Vendors, and NPCs

_This document expands on the Underground as detailed in [[08_The_Underground]], adding the locations, people, and daily texture that make it playable at the table. The Underground operates on two layers — the literal tunnel network beneath the city's feet, and the street-level shadow world of establishments that don't officially exist or don't officially do what they do. Both layers are covered here. Cross-reference [[05_Shadowside]] for the above-ground face of this world; the two bleed into each other more than either would admit._

---

## A Note on Tone

The Underground is not romantic. Visitors who come looking for thrilling danger and exotic intrigue either find something much more mundane — or something much worse than they expected, because they weren't taking it seriously. The people who live and work here are not performing grit. They're getting through. The tunnels smell like what they are. The establishments that serve them are functional first and comfortable if there's coin left over. The food will keep you alive. The beds will let you sleep. Nobody is going to ask how you're doing.

What the Underground offers that the surface cannot is **no questions**. You pay, you get what you came for, you leave. That is the entire value proposition. For people who cannot afford questions — escaped Clanks, those with warrants in other cities, those whose business requires invisibility, those who simply cannot afford the above-ground rate for a meal and a floor to sleep on — this is everything.

## The Tunnel Level

_What exists in the literal underground beneath Lumencrest's streets._

---

### The Dry Chambers

**What it is:** In the oldest sections of the sewer network — where the original construction ran wide and the passages open into maintenance chambers large enough to stand in — a community has taken root. Nobody decided this was where people would live. It grew the way all desperate things grow: one person found a dry corner, then another, then someone with more permanence in mind started bringing down materials. The city knows the Dry Chambers exist. They've made the calculation that clearing them costs more in social instability than it saves in civic tidiness.

There are currently somewhere between forty and eighty people living in the Chambers at any given time — the number shifts with the seasons and the surface situation. Some have been here for years. Some arrived last week. Some are waiting for something to resolve before they go back up. Some have decided there's nothing to go back to.

**What it looks like:** The main chamber — the one people mean when they say "the Chambers" — is roughly the size of a warehouse floor, with a ceiling high enough that the smell disperses somewhat. The walls are old stone, running damp in the rainy season. Light comes from oil lamps hung on iron hooks that someone installed so long ago nobody remembers who. Sleeping spaces are demarcated by hanging cloth, salvaged timber partitions, or simply understood distance. There is a communal cook-fire area in the center where a vented chimney — improvised, imperfect, but functional — carries smoke up into a disused shaft.

**The social reality:** The Chambers have a hierarchy, but it's horizontal. Nobody governs it. Nobody owns it. Disputes are handled by whoever has enough social weight to handle them, which is usually a woman named **Harrow** (see NPCs). People share what they can and take what they need. Newcomers are watched until they're understood.

**For players:** The Dry Chambers are not hostile to outsiders if they arrive correctly — meaning, brought by someone already known here, or arriving obviously in need rather than obviously on business. Arriving with coin out and questions ready is the wrong way. Arriving cold, alone, and clearly in trouble will get you assessed and probably helped. This is not warmth — it's the practical recognition that everyone down here got here somehow.

_GM Note: The Dry Chambers are excellent for players who need to disappear, for NPCs who have "gone to ground," and as a place to encounter Lumencrest's most economically marginal residents who still know things about what moves under the city._

### Gutter & Basin — Tunnel Market

**Location:** A convergence point of three sewer mains approximately forty feet below street level in the Shadowside/Lamplight Ward border. Reached via two known access points in Shadowside (in the Maze Quarter's cellar network) and one in Lamplight Ward (behind the Night Pharmacy, through a trapdoor that Tessiel Wane officially knows nothing about).

**What it is:** The Underground's most literal market — a weekly gathering in a widened sewer junction where goods that cannot be sold anywhere else change hands. Gutter & Basin runs on the third night of every week (locals call it "low tide"), from roughly two hours after dark until the oil in the communal lanterns burns out, which is usually about three hours. Attendance varies. In good weeks, twenty to thirty traders. In bad weeks, eight. The smell is what it is.

**What's available:**

- **Salvage and scrap** — components pulled from the Lumen Network's older infrastructure sections by people who know the tunnel routes. Some of it is legitimate salvage from decommissioned equipment. Some of it was not decommissioned. Network maintenance won't know it's gone for weeks.
- **Stolen goods without papers** — a clear step below the Night Market's "salvage presented as salvage" fiction. Down here nobody pretends.
- **Medical supplies, off-license** — bandages, suture kits, basic alchemical preparations. No questions about why you need them. Significantly higher price than the licensed apothecaries above.
- **Documents** — forged residency records, transit papers, worker assignments. The quality varies. The seller called **Inkhand** (see NPCs) produces the most reliable work.
- **Information** — less structured than above-ground information markets, but cheaper and more dangerous in content.

**The rule:** No weapons drawn. No city watch within ten minutes in any direction (the tunnels ensure this). Anyone who breaks the first rule is removed from the second rule's protection. This has been demonstrated twice in the past year.

### The Remover's Station

_(Referenced in [[08_The_Underground]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Not in the tunnels, but accessible from them. A medical space set into the foundation level of a building on Shadowside's western edge, reached through a passage that begins in the main sewer and surfaces in a basement that belongs to a chandler's shop above. The chandler's shop is legitimate and the owner knows nothing verifiable.

**What it is:** A surgical space — clean, which is the first thing that surprises people. Whatever else the Remover is, they understand sterility. The space has a proper operating table (salvaged from a Luminarium medical facility, long ago), instrument trays, and better lighting than most of the Underground gets. The second thing that surprises people is how quiet it is.

**The procedure:** Removal of a Clank's arcane identification implant. Three sessions over two weeks. The implant is located at the base of the neck. It cannot simply be cut out — the arcane component is woven into the surrounding tissue in a way that requires careful unwinding over multiple sessions. Getting it wrong kills the patient. The Remover has not gotten it wrong. They do not discuss the ones they came close with.

**Cost:** 80 gold marks, paid half before and half after the final session. No negotiation. The Remover has turned away patients who couldn't meet the price. The Free Hand has, on occasion, pooled funds to cover a patient's shortfall. This is not guaranteed.

**The Remover:** See NPCs. They do not see more than one patient at a time. They do not see patients back-to-back. They rest between procedures in a way that suggests the work costs them something beyond the physical.

### The Passage Route — Shadowside to Manufactories

**What it is:** A known, semi-reliable route through the sewer mains connecting Shadowside's Maze Quarter access points to the Manufactories' Assembly Hall area. Used by the Free Hand for moving escaped Clanks, by smugglers moving goods, and by anyone who needs to cross between districts without appearing on a street.

**Navigation:** 25 minutes of walking, mostly crouched in the narrower sections. One junction where the correct branch is not obvious — unmarked except for a scratch pattern on the left wall that locals recognize. Getting the junction wrong adds 40 minutes and eventually surfaces in the Mill Quarter, which is fine but not the destination.

**Hazards:** The occasional maintenance patrol from the Network technicians, who use an adjacent infrastructure tunnel and sometimes pass access points into the sewer. Gas pockets in one sealed side-passage (the entrance is marked with a red cloth tied to a pipe — don't open that one). And rats, which are unavoidable and mostly uninterested in people.

**The toll:** There isn't one, officially. There is a man who sits at the Shadowside end of the route most nights, near the main access point, who will inform you that the route is "being maintained" by people who appreciate a contribution to its upkeep. Two silver marks is what he suggests. Refusing is allowed. He makes a note of faces.

_His name is **Doss**. See NPCs._

## The Street-Level Shadow Layer

_Above-ground establishments that exist in the Underground's ecosystem — the places that feed, shelter, fix, and fence for the people who need to be invisible._

---

### The Dark Lantern

_(Referenced in [[05_Shadowside]] and [[08_The_Underground]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Main thoroughfare, Shadowside. A permanent fixture — it has been in this building for at least sixty years, possibly longer.

**What it is:** The social center of the Underground's above-ground layer. Not because it's the most dangerous place or the most exclusive — it's neither. It's where everyone comes because everyone knows it's where everyone comes. If you need to find someone in this world, you start at the Dark Lantern. If you need to be found, you sit in the Dark Lantern and wait. If you need to send a message to someone you can't reach, you give it to Solt.

**The physical space:** A low-ceilinged room with mismatched furniture, four oil lamps (two working well, two working poorly), a bar of salvaged timber on iron supports, and a curtained section at the back that seats six at a push. No electric lighting — Shadowside doesn't have it — which means the Dark Lantern is lit by the same flickering amber that makes everything in this district feel like it's on the edge of going dark. The food comes from a kitchen behind the bar that is not large and is not fancy and produces things that are hot and filling and cost two or three copper marks.

**What's on offer:**

- Ale (local, mediocre, cold), spirits (imported via the Night Market, surprisingly varied), a house brew that Solt makes himself from a recipe he won't discuss
- Food: thick stew (the base changes weekly depending on what was available), bread, salted meat, occasional egg dishes when the supplier comes through
- The back section, for conversations that cost an extra five copper per person and guarantees no one else sits nearby
- Solt, who knows things. See NPCs.

**The unspoken rules:** No city watch. No scanning equipment. No violence inside — outside is the street's business, and the street is not Solt's problem. No asking Solt direct questions about specific people. All of these are understood, not posted.

**Accommodation:** The Dark Lantern does not have rooms. Solt does not want guests upstairs because upstairs is where Solt lives and Solt has standards.

### The Night's Rest

_(Referenced in [[05_Shadowside]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** Main thoroughfare, Shadowside, four buildings north of the Dark Lantern.

**What it is:** The only inn in Shadowside, which means it's the primary option for anyone who needs to sleep in the Underground's world and can't or won't use the Dry Chambers. Twelve rooms and a common room. No electric lighting. Oil lamps. The sign outside was painted by someone who was trying and has been weathered to approximate illegibility — locals know it by position, not by signage.

**The rooms:** Vary. Three of the twelve are private rooms with a locking door, a bed frame with a mattress that's been turned recently, and a window that you could fit through in an emergency but only if you're not large. The other nine are in two dormitory configurations — six beds and four beds respectively, with curtain dividers between them. The lock on the private room doors is, as the [[05_Shadowside]] file notes, better than the door itself. The door frame is salvaged timber in stone that's been here a long time.

**Cost:** 4 silver for a dormitory bed, 6 silver for a private room. No deposits. No names required — the ledger has a column for "name" that the proprietor fills with whatever the guest says, which is often not a name.

**The proprietor:** **Maret Dusk** — see NPCs. She runs the Night's Rest and has for eleven years, having bought it from the previous owner with money she won't account for. She is not warm. She is fair. There is a difference.

**What the Night's Rest actually provides:** The practical value here is the no-questions policy and the address. If you need somewhere to receive messages or be found without registering with anyone official, the Night's Rest will hold a message for a guest at the cost of one copper mark. It will also tell a city watch officer that no one by any given description is currently staying here, which is not always true but is consistently what it says.

### Brek's — Pawnbroker and Acquisitions

**Location:** A ground-floor shop on a side street off the main thoroughfare, three minutes from Pauper's Gate. No sign. The door is painted a particular shade of green that people in this district recognize.

**What it is:** The Underground's most useful pawnbroker. The word "pawnbroker" is technically accurate — Brek will hold your goods against a loan and return them when you repay with interest — but the more significant business is acquisition. Brek buys things without asking where they came from, and sells things without explaining the same. The shop's display cases hold a mixture of genuinely mundane items (tools, clothing, kitchenware) and things that are mundane only if you don't know what they are — Network components, sealed documents, a locked chest that's been there for two months with no explanation.

**What Brek deals in:**

- Pawned goods held at 30% of assessed value, 10% weekly interest
- Outright purchase at 25-40% of surface market value (Brek's assessment is final and accurate — he knows the market)
- Sale of acquired goods at surface market value or above if they're unusual
- "Facilitated acquisition" — if you tell Brek what you need and can pay for it, he will know someone who knows someone. His fee for this is 15% of the transaction value.

**What Brek will not touch:** Anything that came directly from the Vault District. He made this decision once and won't revisit it. He also won't touch Clank identification implants — not because of ethics but because the specific heat that would generate is not worth any price.

**The proprietor:** **Brek Alden** — see NPCs.

### The Sump — Food and Drink

**Location:** The Maze Quarter, down a passage wide enough for two people if neither is large, marked at the entrance by an iron ring bolted into the left wall that someone has tied a piece of green rope to.

**What it is:** A basement eating and drinking establishment that has been described as "a place where you can get a meal that will not kill you and probably won't make you sick." This is accurate and not underselling it. The Sump does not pretend to be more than it is. The benches are salvaged timber on stone supports. The table surfaces are boards. The ceiling is the underside of the building above, which has not fallen down yet. There are no windows. The smell is cooking and tallow candles and the faint sewer-adjacent quality that everything below street level in the Maze Quarter has.

**What's on offer:**

- A single meal option each day. You get what's being made. Typically a grain-based stew with whatever protein was available — dried meat, occasionally fresh, salted fish on days the market runs near the river. A quarter-loaf of bread. This costs three copper marks.
- Spirits by the cup — rough, local, effective. Two copper marks. No ale; the supplier arrangement for ale fell apart four months ago and hasn't been replaced.
- A version of tea made from dried herbs of unclear provenance that tastes approximately like tea and costs one copper mark.

**No menu. No choice. No complaint.** There is one woman running this operation and she is not taking requests.

**The proprietor:** **Finn Bracken** — see NPCs. She is not actually named Finn. Nobody in the Maze Quarter is entirely who their name says they are. But she's been Finn for eight years and it's stuck.

**Why it matters at the table:** The Sump is where you find out what's moving through the Maze Quarter. The regulars eat here every day because it's the cheapest reliable food in the Underground. They talk while they eat. Finn hears all of it. She will not share it with strangers but she will not stop people from overhearing it either. Sitting in the Sump for an hour is a reasonable way to learn something you didn't know.

### The Stitch — Underground Medical

**Location:** A two-room operation in the back half of what presents as a chandler's shop on the Shadowside/Lamplight Ward border. The chandler's goods are real. The chandler — an older man named **Tuck** who has done nothing wrong in his life and would like to keep it that way — is a front that he accepted in exchange for below-market rent fifteen years ago and has been too afraid to renegotiate since.

**What it is:** The Underground's medical service. Not a hospital — a place where injuries that cannot be treated at a licensed physician's without uncomfortable questions get treated without uncomfortable questions. Stab wounds, breaks that happened in circumstances no one wants to explain, the aftermath of beatings, the complications of procedures performed without appropriate care elsewhere. Also: contraception, substances that help with things people don't want to say out loud, and the occasional consultation from someone who can't afford the licensed healers in Lamplight Ward.

**What's actually available:**

- Basic wound treatment: cleaning, closure, binding. Done correctly. No infection, or significantly reduced risk.
- Bone setting for simple fractures
- Alchemical preparations for pain, fever, and infection — sourced through the Night Market's supply chain
- Long-term medicine for chronic conditions, discretely. Several Manufactories workers come here for conditions they can't afford to have known at work.
- Contraception — reliable, affordable, no commentary
- Detox support for substances that have gotten out of hand

**Cost:** Variable and negotiated. The practitioner's stated principle is "what you have." This is not actually true — she won't work for nothing — but she negotiates to the floor of what she thinks a patient can actually pay rather than the ceiling. Wound closure: 1-3 silver depending on severity. Bone setting: 3-5 silver. Ongoing medicine: 1-2 silver weekly.

**The practitioner:** **Vael** — see NPCs. She was a licensed physician. She isn't anymore. The reason is not discussed.

### The Silver Thread — Shadowside House

_(Referenced in [[08_The_Underground]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** A building on the Maze Quarter's southern edge that looks from the outside like a residential structure — which it is, for the people who work and sometimes live here. No sign. Access by the door on the left; the door on the right goes to a storage cellar that the building's legitimate tenancy records describe as the entire property.

**What it is:** One of the Sisterhood of the Silver Thread's Shadowside operations. Unlike the Guild's Lamplight Ward establishments — which are licensed, visible, and operating in Lumencrest's legal framework — this house operates in a grayer arrangement. The Guild has a licensing arrangement with the city (the work is legal; the city takes a percentage). The Shadowside house is part of that arrangement in the sense that the Silver Thread's leadership has included it in their paperwork. In practice, city licensing officials do not come to the Maze Quarter to inspect.

**What's offered:** The same services as any Silver Thread establishment, with the distinction that the clientele here is different. Less wealthy. More in need of discretion for reasons that have nothing to do with social reputation and everything to do with legal exposure. The Silver Thread serves this population deliberately — the Guild's mutual aid fund extends to the Shadowside house's workers and clients alike, and Calla's intelligence network benefits from a presence in the Underground's social world.

**The house manager:** **Tavi Renne** — see NPCs. Calla's designate for the Shadowside operation. Has been running this house for six years. Knows the Maze Quarter as well as anyone who wasn't born in it.

**Why this matters beyond the obvious:** The Silver Thread's Shadowside house is one of the better-connected points in the Underground. The clientele talks — people are inclined to talk in these circumstances — and the Silver Thread's intelligence function runs here as surely as it runs in the Lamplight Ward houses. If something is moving through the Underground and has involved anyone in the Silver Thread's circle, Tavi knows a version of it. Whether that version becomes available to players depends on what they offer in return.

### The Fence — Garwick's Trading Post

**Location:** On the Shadowside main thoroughfare, presented as a general goods shop. It is a general goods shop. It also deals in goods that don't have paperwork, which is the more significant business.

**What it is:** Where stolen items from above-ground end up after passing through the Night Market or coming directly from those who took them. Garwick's is not picky about provenance and asks only that the goods be undamaged and not directly traceable to the Vault District or the Luminarium's research programs (a rule he added after an incident two years ago that he does not discuss). He buys from anyone who walks in with something sellable. He sells to anyone who can pay.

**What's typically in stock:**

- Clothing and personal goods in the "salvage" category, ranging from genuinely salvaged to recently removed from an owner
- Tools and equipment — the useful, portable, sellable kind
- Electrical components and Lumen Network parts (see Gutter & Basin — some of the tunnel market's inventory surfaces here)
- Occasional higher-value items: jewelry, quality weapons, specific objects of interest
- A back shelf of locked cases with contents Garwick will discuss only with customers he's assessed as serious and solvent

**Garwick will not:**  
Buy anything he recognizes as belonging to a specific named person who might notice and has the means to make trouble. He is good at recognizing this. Buy Clank identification implants. Buy anything that looks like it came from the Vault. He has turned away more money than most Shadowside traders see in a year on the basis of these rules, and he intends to keep doing so.

**Cost:** He pays 20-35% of surface market value for acquisitions, adjusting for risk and condition. He sells at 60-80% of surface market value, which is the Underground's meaningful discount.

**The proprietor:** **Garwick Sol** — see NPCs.

### The Pauper's Gate Taproom

**Location:** Immediately inside Pauper's Gate, on the left — the first building you pass entering the city from the north. Has been a tavern for as long as the gate has been the gate.

**What it is:** The Underground's most public-facing establishment, in the sense that it sees everyone who comes through Pauper's Gate and Pauper's Gate sees a particular kind of traffic. City watch knows this. City watch patrols Pauper's Gate. City watch does not patrol the Taproom because the Taproom is where city watch's Pauper's Gate contingent drinks after a shift and the proprietor has maintained this arrangement for twenty years by being useful to them in small, specific ways that don't compromise anything important.

**What's on offer:**

- Ale — functional, three copper marks a cup
- Spirits — better than the Sump's, worse than the Dark Lantern's, two copper marks a measure
- Food: roasted meat skewers, bread, a cheese board that's actually a board with cheese on it
- News of what came through the gate in the last day, if you're someone the proprietor has decided to talk to
- A place to sit near the gate and watch who comes through, which has its own value

**Cost:** For the obvious services, see above. For the less obvious ones, the currency is reciprocal usefulness.

**The proprietor:** **Old Pen** — see NPCs. Nobody knows her first name. She may not know it anymore.

## Notable NPCs — The Underground

_All significant characters in the Underground's ecosystem. Cross-reference [[NPCs]] for city-wide figures who are also present in this district. Characters from [[08_The_Underground]] who operate in this space are expanded here._

### Solt

_(Full entry in [[NPCs]] — Underground-specific notes here)_

**Role:** Proprietor of the Dark Lantern. Shadowside's central information hub. De facto message relay for the Underground's above-ground network.

**Where to find him:** Behind the bar at the Dark Lantern. Every day. He has not taken a day away from the Dark Lantern in eleven years, which people note with either admiration or concern depending on who you ask.

**Underground-specific:** Solt's value in this world is his absolute neutrality. He has served Vault Guard officers and Free Hand couriers within the same hour and told neither about the other. This is not naivety — it is a deliberate and carefully maintained position that has taken thirty years to build. Everyone who uses the Dark Lantern as a meeting point, message drop, or safe space does so because they trust that Solt will not break. So far he hasn't. The day he does, or is perceived to, the Dark Lantern becomes worthless and thirty years of construction collapses.

**What he wants:** For the Dark Lantern to continue. Which requires the Underground to remain stable enough for a neutral space to exist. He is, therefore, quietly hostile to anyone who seems likely to destabilize the precarious arrangements that keep this world functional. He has never said this aloud. It is visible in who he serves with less attention than others.

**What he knows:** More than he says. Less than people think. He will occasionally, after weeks of regular presence and demonstrated trustworthiness, offer a piece of context — unprompted, phrased as general observation — that turns out to be exactly what a player needs. He does not do this on request.

### Calla

_(Full entry in [[NPCs]] — Underground-specific notes here)_

**Role:** Head of the Sisterhood of the Silver Thread. Present in the Underground primarily through her designate **Tavi Renne** and through the Silver Thread's intelligence network.

**Underground-specific:** Calla does not come to Shadowside. Calla sends Tavi and receives reports. The Underground knows of Calla more than it knows her. Her reputation here is of someone who has power and uses it carefully — which in this world translates to cautious respect. Nobody in the Underground tries to run anything past the Silver Thread's Shadowside operation without at least considering that Calla will hear about it.

### Harrow

**Role:** Informal leader of the Dry Chambers community  
**Ancestry/Age:** Half-orc, late 50s  
**Location:** The Dry Chambers, main chamber

**Appearance:** Taller than most of the people around her, which in the cramped space of the Chambers is more noticeable than it would be above ground. Grey hair cut short and practical. One ear is missing most of its upper half — an old injury she doesn't explain. Her hands are the hands of someone who has been doing hard work their entire life. She wears practical underground-appropriate clothing in layers.

**Personality:** Patient in the specific way of someone who has dealt with every kind of crisis and stopped being surprised by any of them. Not warm, exactly, but not cold either — deeply functional. She assesses people quickly and accurately and treats them accordingly. She is not sentimental about the Chambers or the people in it, but she is protective of it in the way a person is protective of something fragile that they've kept alive through consistent effort.

**What she wants:** For the Chambers to remain functional and undisturbed. The equilibrium is always one bad event from collapsing — a city watch sweep, a structural failure in the older passages, a conflict that gets out of hand. She manages against all of these simultaneously and is very tired.

**What she knows:** Everyone who has passed through the Dry Chambers in the past ten years. The full layout of the older tunnel sections in the Shadowside/Lamplight Ward border. At least one route to the Vault District's outer underground perimeter that she has never used and does not intend to. The Free Hand's general operational patterns — she is not part of the organization but has provided shelter for people the Free Hand was moving, on her own terms.

**What she can offer players:** Access to the Chambers as a safe location. Route information for the tunnel network that isn't on any official map. Introductions to people who live in the Chambers and have specific knowledge or skills. She does not offer these things to strangers. She offers them after players have demonstrated that their presence is not a liability.

**Working with her:** Arrive correctly — through someone she knows or in obvious genuine need. Don't bring city watch attention. Don't cause problems in the Chambers. After that, she is one of the most practically useful contacts in the Underground.

### The Remover

**Role:** Surgeon specializing in Clank identification implant removal  
**Ancestry/Age:** Unknown. Appears human, appears to be in their 60s. May not be either.  
**Location:** The Remover's Station (see above). Available by appointment only, arranged through the Free Hand or specific Gutter & Basin connections.

**Appearance:** Precise. Everything about them is precise — their movements, their instruments, their use of words. They dress in practical clothing in dark colors and wear their grey-streaked hair pulled back. Their hands are very still when they're not working and very deliberate when they are. They do not have a face that invites questions.

**Personality:** Professional in a way that borders on the impersonal. They are not unkind — there is a quality of care in how they work that suggests they take the responsibility seriously — but they are not warm. They do not make conversation during procedures. They explain exactly what they are doing, exactly what the patient will feel, and exactly what will happen if the patient moves when they're asked not to. They are good at their job in the way that requires no commentary.

**Background (what's known):** Former Luminarium research assistant. Left approximately thirty years ago. The reason is not publicly known; what the Free Hand's oldest members believe is that the Remover participated in the early Clank research programs, understood what they were creating, and left when it became clear the city had no intention of treating what they were creating as people. This is not confirmed and the Remover will not discuss it.

**What they want:** To keep doing this. They have removed approximately 400 implants. They keep count. They do not explain why they keep count.

**What they know:** The Clank identification implant's technical specifications in more detail than anyone currently employed by the Luminarium. The physiological and arcane interface of Clank construction, at a level that makes them uniquely qualified for this procedure and, potentially, for other things that require that knowledge. They have declined every inquiry about those other things.

**Working with them:** Don't come to them with anything other than a patient. They will see players as potential referrals through the Free Hand — trusted people who bring patients to them carefully — or as a complication. Becoming the first takes time and demonstrated reliability. The procedure itself, for a player character who is a Clank or accompanying one, is a significant experience — three sessions, each involving something that is painful and intimate and not comfortable. The Remover is present for all of it without flinching.

### Inkhand

**Role:** Document forger, Gutter & Basin regular  
**Ancestry/Age:** Gnome, 40s  
**Location:** Gutter & Basin on market nights; the rest of the time, a small workshop in the Maze Quarter's upper reaches that she's very particular about not having visitors to

**Appearance:** Small even for a gnome, with ink-stained hands that give her her working name and hair that she's stopped managing in any intentional way. Carries a satchel of tools that she keeps close in a way that makes it clear the satchel is the most important thing she owns. Wears magnifying lenses around her neck at all times — she is significantly short-sighted and the lenses are practical, not affectation.

**Personality:** Direct and slightly impatient with people who don't know what they want before they start talking to her. She asks two questions: what do you need and when do you need it. The answers to those questions determine everything else including price and whether she can help at all. She is not unfriendly — she has a dry humor that emerges when she's working on something interesting — but her time is the thing she protects most carefully.

**What she produces:**

- Residency documents for Lumencrest — the kind that pass a gate guard's check, not the kind that hold up to a Records Hall inquiry
- Transit papers for the Kingroad and Ironspine Road gate checkpoints
- Worker assignment documents — specifically Clank worker assignments, which she produces for the Free Hand at a reduced rate she will tell you nothing about
- Letters of introduction for Academic Quarter and Luminarium access — the easiest forgeries because the volume of legitimate documents makes discrepancy harder to spot
- She will not produce Council-level documentation or Vault District access papers. The former is too high-risk if caught; the latter she declines without explanation and with notable firmness.

**Cost:** 10-30 gold marks depending on complexity and urgency. Rush jobs (under 48 hours) are double. She does not barter.

**What she knows:** The current state of every documentation format used by the city's official apparatus, updated continuously because she has a contact inside the Charter Office who provides advance notice of format changes (she pays this contact in gold marks, not favors). She is not an information broker, but she knows the document side of who is moving through the city under false identity, which is its own kind of useful.

### Maret Dusk

**Role:** Proprietor of the Night's Rest  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 44  
**Location:** The Night's Rest, Shadowside main thoroughfare. She lives in the inn — a room on the third floor that is the only room she keeps genuinely private.

**Appearance:** Practical build, brown hair kept short for function rather than style, a scar along the left jaw that she doesn't hide but doesn't explain. Dresses in working clothes at all times because she is always working. Has the quality of someone who is paying attention to more things simultaneously than they're letting on.

**Personality:** Fair without being warm. She treats every guest with the same professional neutrality — the same check-in process, the same explanation of house rules (no violence inside, no city watch invited, messages held at one copper mark, no questions asked and none expected), the same flat assessment of whether the person in front of her is going to be a problem. She is accurate in this assessment approximately 90% of the time. The 10% she gets wrong, she handles without fuss.

**What she wants:** The Night's Rest to remain operational. She bought it with money that came from a situation she left behind, and the inn is the clean end of that transaction. She does not want to discuss where the money came from.

**What she knows:** Who has stayed in the Night's Rest for the past eleven years, which turns out to be a significant portion of the Underground's transient population and a useful cross-section of everyone who has passed through Shadowside while needing to be invisible. She does not share this information without significant reason. She has been offered significant money for it on three occasions and turned it down on the reasonable calculation that an inn with a reputation for discretion is worth more than any single payout.

**Working with her:** She will be professionally neutral and that's what you get until you've been a regular guest for long enough that she decides you're not a liability. At that point, she becomes something more useful — not warm, not a friend, but someone who will tell you when to avoid the inn on a specific night because someone you don't want to run into is there, or who will hold a message for you without writing it in a ledger. Small things. Important things.

### Finn Bracken

**Role:** Operator of The Sump  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, somewhere between 35 and 50 (the Underground ages people and also preserves them strangely)  
**Location:** The Sump, Maze Quarter. She is there when it's open and somewhere else entirely when it's closed.

**Appearance:** Medium height, dark, wearing an apron that has absorbed more cooking than any apron should survive. Her hands are efficient and her eyes are the thing that notice you when you come in — quick, assessing, deciding. She is not threatening. She is simply someone who has been operating in a space where reading people correctly matters and has gotten very good at it.

**Personality:** She does not talk while she's working, which is always. The Sump has no service staff — she cooks, she serves, she clears, she does this for however many people show up until the pot runs out. She will answer a direct question directly and then return to what she was doing. She will not be drawn into conversation, not because she dislikes talking but because the Sump is not that kind of place and she keeps it not that kind of place deliberately.

**What she knows:** What's being discussed at every table in the Sump, every day. She does not sell this. She does not share it without reason. The reason that has moved her in the past — and this is not common knowledge — is a genuine threat to the Maze Quarter itself. She has lived here for eight years. She intends to keep living here.

**What she might offer:** If players have established credibility in the Maze Quarter and something is developing that threatens the neighborhood's stability, Finn is one of the people who will find a way to be useful. This will be indirect — she will leave something on a table, or mention something off-hand to a regular who passes it on — rather than direct. She does not want a relationship. She wants the Maze Quarter to be fine.

### Vael

**Role:** Practitioner at The Stitch  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 38  
**Location:** The Stitch (back half of Tuck's chandler shop). Present most nights from about two hours after dark to several hours before dawn — the hours when people who've had incidents need care and can't wait.

**Appearance:** Tired in a specific way — not exhausted, more the tiredness of someone who has had a lot of long nights and has found a sustainable relationship with them. Dark hair, usually pinned back. Careful hands. Wears a long apron over practical clothing and carries a medical kit that is better equipped than you'd expect from a back-room operation — the supplies are not cheap and they're well-maintained. The apron has an old bloodstain that didn't come out. She has stopped noticing it.

**Personality:** Gentle in the way of someone who has learned that medical care goes better when patients are not afraid. Not soft — she is direct about what procedures involve and what outcomes are realistic — but careful about how she delivers information and how she touches people. She does not pass judgment on how injuries were sustained and enforces this in her space without needing to say it aloud.

**Background:** She was a licensed physician in Lamplight Ward. She isn't anymore, and the reason is not something she tells patients. What the Free Hand knows — and she works with the Free Hand when Clanks need medical care — is that she lost her license treating an escaped Clank without reporting it to the Labour Board as required. She did not consider whether to report. She treated the patient. The licensing board had questions about this. She stopped practicing above ground rather than answer them.

**What she wants:** To keep practicing medicine. The Stitch is not what she trained for — she trained to be the best in Lamplight Ward's Physician's Quarter, and she was, before she stopped — but it is medicine, and the people who come here need it more than the people who can pay the licensed physicians above do.

**What she knows:** The medical and physical reality of violence, malnutrition, substance dependency, and the specific injuries that people sustain in circumstances they can't explain. She has a particular knowledge of Clank physiology — maintenance and medical — that the Luminarium holds officially and she holds practically. This knowledge is available to players who are dealing with a Clank medical situation and who the Free Hand has vouched for.

### Brek Alden

**Role:** Proprietor of Brek's Pawnbroker and Acquisitions  
**Ancestry/Age:** Dwarf, 67  
**Location:** The green-doored shop off the main thoroughfare. He opens when he opens and closes when he's done, and this is not a schedule anyone else controls.

**Appearance:** Compact even for a dwarf, with a grey-streaked beard worn shorter than the traditional style and the hands of someone who has handled a lot of objects — not gentle hands, but precise ones. Wears an apron with many pockets. Uses a pair of magnifying lenses on a headband for assessment work and pushes them up to his forehead when he's just talking. Is almost always just talking.

**Personality:** Conversational in a way that's easy to mistake for friendliness and is actually a very efficient method of gathering information. He talks while he assesses goods. He asks questions that seem like small talk and are not. He has never in his life made a purely social remark. By the time a transaction is complete, he knows substantially more about the person he's dealing with than they realize, and he files this in a mental index that is impressive in its detail.

**What he wants:** To continue operating, which requires never holding anything that will bring the wrong kind of trouble to his door. The Vault District rule is not moral — he genuinely does not care how the item left the Vault — it is practical. Vault Guard will burn this shop to its foundation rather than let what they want slip out via a pawnbroker. He has thought this through.

**What he knows:** The provenance of approximately three-quarters of the significant stolen goods that have passed through Lumencrest in the past fifteen years, in the sense that he's seen them at some point in their movement. He is not an information broker and will not be used as one. He will occasionally confirm or deny whether a specific item is known to him, for reasons he decides based on who's asking and what they seem to be doing. He charges for this at the same rate as his acquisition fee.

**Working with him:** He will be friendly and efficient in the transaction sense. Building a relationship with Brek requires repeat business — showing up with things to sell or buy, consistently, over time — and the gradual recognition on his part that you are someone whose business model doesn't create complications for his. At that point, the "facilitated acquisition" service becomes available, which can get players almost anything that moves through Lumencrest's black market.

### Garwick Sol

**Role:** Proprietor of Garwick's Trading Post  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 51  
**Location:** Garwick's, Shadowside main thoroughfare

**Appearance:** Tall, running to lean, with the kind of long face that looks perpetually skeptical and is about 60% of the time actually skeptical. Wears his hair in a practical queue and dresses in decent quality clothing — not impressive, but better than the district standard, which he maintains as a marker of professional identity. The locked cases on the back shelf are never opened in front of other customers.

**Personality:** Reserved and evaluating. He watches before he speaks and speaks less than you'd expect from someone running a retail operation. He is not unfriendly — he will explain prices, describe goods, answer direct questions about what he has in stock — but he doesn't perform the ease of someone who wants you to like them. He wants you to buy and come back and not bring trouble. That's the entirety of the relationship he's offering.

**What he knows:** The flow of high-value goods through the Underground's fencing network — which items are moving, at what price, from where toward where. He doesn't share this, but he knows it in the way a person who has been paying attention for twenty years knows things.

**What the locked cases contain:** He rotates. Currently: a sealed document in a Luminarium format that he cannot read and has not been able to sell; a ring with a council family crest that he is waiting for the right buyer for; three Network maintenance components that shouldn't be available outside of an authorized work order; and something in a wrapped cloth that he won't unwrap for anyone.

### Doss

**Role:** Toll collector, Shadowside end of the Passage Route  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 30s  
**Location:** The access point near the Maze Quarter entrance to the Shadowside-to-Manufactories sewer route, most nights after dark

**Appearance:** Nondescript in a way that's clearly practiced — the kind of person you walk past and then can't quite reconstruct. Medium everything. Clothing in colors that don't catch light. He is good at being in a doorway.

**Personality:** Affable in a way that doesn't reach his eyes. He makes the toll request sound reasonable and ordinary because it is, in its way — someone maintains that route (in the sense of keeping it known and navigable) and someone should pay. He is not aggressive about it. He is also not flexible about it. And he keeps a running account of who uses the route, which he maintains for reasons that probably belong to someone above him in a hierarchy he hasn't mentioned.

**What he's actually doing:** Doss works for someone. He has not said who. The consensus in the Maze Quarter is that it's either the Free Hand (who needs to know who's using routes they depend on) or an independent operator running information about tunnel traffic to the highest bidder. Both explanations fit his behavior. Neither has been confirmed.

**Working with him:** Pay the toll. Two silver marks is the suggested amount; he'll accept one and a half without comment. Don't ask who he works for. If players eventually want to know who's moving through the route — specific people, specific goods — Doss is a path to that information, at a price substantially above two silver marks and after he's decided they're not a liability to his ongoing operation.

### Tavi Renne

**Role:** Silver Thread house manager, Shadowside  
**Ancestry/Age:** Half-elf, 32  
**Location:** The Silver Thread's Shadowside house, Maze Quarter southern edge

**Appearance:** Composed in the way of someone who has learned composure as a professional tool. Dark hair, usually down. Dressed in the Silver Thread's characteristic style — practical elegance, well-made without being showy. Has very specific and practiced body language that reads differently to different people, which is a skill she developed over years and deploys consciously.

**Personality:** Warmer than Calla, which is not a high bar, but genuinely so. She has a facility for making people feel seen without necessarily letting them see her, which is the core professional skill of someone in her position and also reflects something real about her character — she is genuinely curious about people and uses what she finds out carefully rather than carelessly.

**What she wants:** The Shadowside house to keep running, which requires it remaining useful to both its clientele and to Calla's intelligence network. She is not mercenary about the second part — she doesn't manufacture intelligence from thin air — but she's aware that the house's continued operation depends on it being a valuable node in the Silver Thread's network, and she works to keep it that way.

**What she knows:** The Shadowside house's intelligence function gives her a working picture of what's happening in the Underground's social world — relationships, tensions, movement, money. This is not the political-level intelligence Calla maintains from the Lamplight Ward houses, but it's the street-level version that sometimes matters more. She will trade information with people who have information she needs, after she's assessed them as safe to trade with.

### Old Pen

**Role:** Proprietor of the Pauper's Gate Taproom  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, appears late 60s  
**Location:** The Pauper's Gate Taproom. Somewhere else during the three hours a day when she's not there, but nobody has established where.

**Appearance:** Short, wide, white-haired, and carrying the accumulated weight of twenty years behind a bar in a way that's entirely comfortable. She moves slowly because she's learned that everything gets done if she moves at her pace, and quickly enough only matters when it matters. Her face is remarkable for its complete neutrality — not blankness, not coldness, just the absence of unsolicited expression. She will look at you and you will know she's decided something about you and she will not tell you what.

**Personality:** Old Pen is the oldest fixture in the Underground's immediate surroundings and she has survived this long by being consistently useful to everyone and dangerous to no one — or by being perceived this way, which amounts to the same thing. She talks to city watch about which way specific people went (truthfully, because she's not running anything that requires lying to city watch). She talks to those specific people about who's been asking (also truthfully, because the value of the taproom is that both sides know she'll do this and trust her to do it exactly). It's a very precise social engineering that she's been maintaining for twenty years.

**What she knows:** Everyone who comes through Pauper's Gate over the past twenty years, what they looked like, and in many cases where they went first. This is useful in the specific way of a detailed record of who's entered the city via its least-observed gate. She has occasionally been paid for entries in this record. She prices them at 3 silver marks each, which is the same whether the inquiry comes from the Underground or from a city watch officer she trusts, which is few of them.

**Working with her:** Buy a drink. Buy another. Come back tomorrow and do the same. After about a week, she'll acknowledge that you're there in a way that goes beyond professional courtesy. After two, she might mention something about someone that was relevant a week ago. After three, the information starts being current. This timeline does not accelerate for gold.

## Underground Faction Presence

The following factions from [[Factions_and_Reputation]] have active presence in the Underground. Reputation with them affects how the Underground's NPCs respond to players.

**The Free Hand** — most active faction in the Underground. Uses the Dry Chambers, Passage Route, and Remover's Station as operational infrastructure. Being known as a Free Hand ally opens most Underground doors. Being known as someone who caused a problem for the Free Hand closes them permanently.

**The Silver Thread** — present via the Shadowside house. Intelligence flows up to Calla. Being known to the Silver Thread, positively, provides a social lubricant in the Underground's social spaces that's difficult to replicate through other means.

**The Ironwright Collective** — present informally, through Manufactories workers who use the Underground for purposes their employers would not appreciate. Genna Cradle knows the Underground exists; she has contacts here but does not control them.

**The Conservators** — present only through intelligence monitoring. There is a city watch contact in the Underground (not named here — that's for GMs to place). The Underground knows there's a watch contact and has a rough idea of who it might be. Nobody is certain.

## Underground Reputation Track

The Underground functions as a de facto faction for reputation purposes. Players gain and lose standing in this world separately from their standing with any specific faction.

**Starting position:** Unknown (0). Players are strangers who don't have enemies yet.

**Gaining standing:**

- Surviving a first Gutter & Basin market without causing trouble: +1
- Being vouched for by Solt, Harrow, or any Free Hand member: +1
- Providing genuine material help to the Dry Chambers community: +1
- Successfully moving through the tunnel network without getting anyone caught: +1
- Referring a patient to Vael through appropriate channels: +1

**Losing standing:**

- Bringing city watch attention to an Underground establishment: -2
- Betraying a contact's confidence to an official authority: -3 (near-permanent)
- Violence inside a neutral space (Dark Lantern, Sump, Night's Rest): -2
- Being identified as working for the Vault Guard or Conservator intelligence: -3

**Reputation unlocks:**

|Standing|What Opens Up|
|---|---|
|+1 (Noticed)|Access to Gutter & Basin, will be served at the Dark Lantern without visible suspicion|
|+2 (Known)|Harrow considers requests, Doss shares route information, Brek's facilitated acquisition available|
|+3 (Trusted)|Dry Chambers as safe refuge, Remover accessible without Free Hand voucher, Tavi will trade intelligence|
