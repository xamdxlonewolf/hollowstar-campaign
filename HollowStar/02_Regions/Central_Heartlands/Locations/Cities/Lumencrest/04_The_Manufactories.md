# The Manufactories

**Population:** ~25,000 (the densest worker district; includes ~1,800 registered Clanks on assignment)  
**Power Status:** Rationed. The Manufactories receive prioritized allocation for the factory machinery, which means the residences and secondary facilities often run on reduced power. The most recent full blackout hit here longest — factory floors went dark for twelve full minutes and the restart procedures took another forty.  
**Cable Car:** 2 stations (North Factory Hub, South Factory Hub — positioned for worker commutes, not visitor convenience)  
**Access:** Open. Factory floors themselves require employee status or an authorized visit.

---

## Overview

The Manufactories are where Lumencrest's economic power lives in physical form. This is the district of heavy industry, powered machinery, and the labor that makes the city's manufacturing dominance real. A first-time visitor walking from Lamplight Ward into the Manufactories feels the city change before they see it: the air gets denser, carrying coal particulate and metallic sharpness; the sound shifts from the varied noise of commerce to the rhythmic, relentless sound of machinery; the street proportions change, widening for cart and delivery traffic, with narrower side streets crammed with workers' housing.

The factories run on Hollostar-powered electric machinery — automated lathes, powered mills, electric furnaces, assembly mechanisms that perform work no human or Clank could perform at the same scale. Lumencrest has been developing this technology for 600 years. The factories are extraordinary. They are also the thing most threatened by the power crisis, because automated machinery requires consistent power, and consistent power is the thing Lumencrest currently cannot guarantee.

Manufacturing is down 15-20% from its peak. Some factories have closed shifts. A few smaller operations have shut entirely. The workers who built careers in powered industry are watching their skills become conditional on power that may not be there tomorrow. The anger in this district is real, grounded, and looking for somewhere to go.

## Sub-Districts

### The Forge District (Northern Manufactories)
Metalworking, heavy industry, weapons manufacturing. The largest and loudest part of the district. The Forge District runs three shifts and genuinely never stops — even at reduced capacity, there are always fires burning and machines running. The air here has a constant metallic taste.

**Notable:** The Ironwright Collective — the Forge District's most organized labor group, technically a social club but functionally a union. Their current grievance list to the Council is sixteen items long. The Council has acknowledged receipt of nine of them.

### The Mill Quarter (Central Manufactories)
Textile production, processing, light manufacturing — the part of the district that produces goods for direct use rather than industrial components. The mills use electrically powered looms that produce Lumencrest's famous precision-woven textiles at speeds no other city can match. Three mills have halved their production hours due to power rationing. The owners are absorbing this quietly. The workers are not.

### The Assembly Halls (Southern Manufactories)
The district's most sophisticated manufacturing: complex devices, precision instruments, the electrical components that the Lumen Network itself requires for maintenance and expansion. The Assembly Halls are where Lumencrest makes the things that make Lumencrest run. They are also where the city's Clank population is most heavily concentrated — assembly work suits Clank capabilities, and the city assigns a high proportion of its indentured Clanks here.

**The Clank Barracks** — city-run housing for Clanks on assignment in the Manufactories. Functional, not comfortable, adequate by the city's own stated standards. Located on the Assembly Halls' western edge. The first point of contact for anyone trying to make connections with the Clank community inside the city.

### Workers' Housing
Interspersed throughout the district between factory blocks — dense residential buildings, most four to six stories, with shared facilities (communal bathing houses, laundries) per block. Not slums: the city mandates maintenance standards and they're mostly met. But the standard is "functional," not "comfortable," and the gap between functional and comfortable becomes more obvious in a brownout.

**Halfhold Street** — the main social street for Manufactories workers, lined with taverns, canteens, and cheap shops. No cable car stop, but the South Factory Hub is walkable. The politics here are the most straightforward in the city: people who do hard work and are being asked to accept that the work may stop are not particularly interested in rhetorical nuance.

## Key Locations

**The Great Press** — the city's largest single factory, producing the Lumen Network's maintenance equipment and the powered printing presses distributed across the Accord. Its chimney stack is the Manufactories' tallest structure. Considered a civic landmark. Currently running at 65% capacity.

**The Labour Board** — a city-operated office where workers register, where Clank assignments are processed, and where official employment disputes are filed. The line here is always long. The clerks are overworked and not particularly sympathetic.

**The Ironwright Hall** — the Ironwright Collective's meeting hall, technically a social club, practically a labor organizing center. Meetings here are open to any Manufactories worker. The Collective's speaker, a half-orc named Genna Cradle who has been organizing workers for fifteen years, is one of the most effective political speakers in the city. She is not on anyone's payroll except the Collective's. People have tried.

## Where to Sleep and Eat

Workers in the Manufactories mostly live in the district housing — long-term residents. Visitors have few options and most of them are trying to pass through quickly.

**The Forge and Flagon** *(Halfhold Street)* — the district's best tavern, which is not saying very much for accommodation but is saying quite a lot for atmosphere. The food is heavy, cheap, and excellent. The ale is mediocre but cold. Every conversation happening in this room is worth listening to. 5 silver for a bed in the common room, 8 silver for a private curtained space, no private rooms.

**Kess Aldman's Boarding House** *(Mill Quarter)* — a boarding house for longer-stay visitors with business in the Manufactories. Clean, quiet, 7 silver a night. Kess asks very few questions, which in this district is a deliberate business decision.
