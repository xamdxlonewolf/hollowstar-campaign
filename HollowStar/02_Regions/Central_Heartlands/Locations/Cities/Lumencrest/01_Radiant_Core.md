# The Radiant Core

**Population:** ~8,000 residents + significant daily foot traffic of officials, servants, contractors  
**Power Status:** Full and uninterrupted — the Core never dims, even during citywide brownouts  
**Cable Car:** Central Hub Station — all four lines converge here  
**Access:** Open streets, but checkpoints control entry to the inner estates and Council buildings. You can walk the Core's public streets freely. Getting into anything that matters requires a reason.

---

## Overview

The Radiant Core is the oldest part of Lumencrest that is still recognizably the city it became — the original settlement site, built around the point where the Heart of Lumencrest sits deepest underground. Power flows up from directly below, through the densest web of Lumen Network infrastructure in existence, and the Core is the only district that has never experienced a brownout. Not during the worst years of the current crisis. Not once.

The residents of the Core know this. The residents of every other district know this too, and they resent it.

The Core is a district of wide streets, tall buildings, excellent light, and permanent visible authority. Guards are not positioned aggressively — this isn't a city under martial law — but they are always there, in pairs, and they have the particular quality of guards who expect to be listened to and usually are. The architecture is older than the rest of the city: stone that's been here for six hundred years, maintained meticulously, occasionally updated but never replaced. It looks expensive because it is expensive and always has been.

At night, the Core glows. The street lamps here use the newest Network infrastructure and burn at full brightness — a cooler, whiter light than the amber of Lamplight Ward's older fittings. Combined with the illuminated windows of the estates and the floodlit Accord Chamber dome, the Radiant Core is visible from miles outside the city as a specific point of light brighter than the surrounding glow.

---

## The Atmosphere

Walking into the Radiant Core from Lamplight Ward or the Academic Quarter, players should feel:

- The streets are quieter despite being wealthier. Foot traffic is purposeful — people here are going somewhere specific.
- The light is different. Cooler, brighter, more even. No flicker. The shadows fall straight down.
- Everyone is dressed well. Not necessarily lavishly, but well. The standard here is higher and people meet it.
- The guards watch you but don't approach unless you look lost or out of place long enough to be noticed.
- The buildings are heavy and serious. Even the residential estates feel institutional — built to last, not built to charm.

## Key Locations

### The Accord Chamber
The city's architectural centerpiece and the Heartland Accord's formal meeting hall. A domed building with four tall towers at the cardinal points, built 400 years ago to celebrate the Accord's founding at a moment of supreme Lumencrest confidence. The dome is lit from within at night and visible from most of the city.

Inside: a grand circular chamber with tiered seating around a central floor, built to hold all five Accord city delegations simultaneously. It was designed for confident men and women conducting the business of a dominant civilization. It now hosts a Council that cannot agree on how to respond to a crisis that's been running for thirty years. The room is too big for the conversations being held in it. The Conservators, Illuminists, and Expansionists sit in their respective sections and speak at each other across a floor that used to mean something.

**Access:** Public gallery seating is available for Council sessions. Formal sessions are announced three days in advance by public posting at the main gates and Merchant Square. Getting a seat in the gallery requires arriving early. Getting a seat near the front requires either connections or persistence.

**What players can do here:** Watch Council sessions — a good way to get a read on the political situation without anyone needing to brief them. An observant party can track which faction is gaining ground by body language and vote counts alone.

### The Council District
The administrative quarter of the Core — a cluster of connected government buildings housing the Council's offices, committee rooms, the city archives (separate from the Luminarium's academic archives), and the bureaucracy that runs the city day-to-day. Less grand than the Accord Chamber; these are working buildings.

**Notable sub-locations:**
- **The Charter Office** — where city licenses, residency registrations, and official documentation are processed. Slow, reliable, and the city clerks have seen everything. Bribing them is difficult but not impossible if you know who to approach.
- **The Council Member Offices** — thirty-two offices for thirty-two Councilors, arranged by faction in three wings. Which wing a Councilor occupies is publicly known. Who visits which office, and when, is information that various parties pay well for.
- **The Hall of Records** — public legal records, property records, Accord treaty documentation going back 400 years. Open to any citizen for reading purposes. Copying or removing anything requires authorization.

### The Estates
The walled residential compounds of hereditary Council families, major shareholders in the manufacturing guilds, and old-money Lumencrest. These are not homes so much as fortified private neighborhoods — high walls, private security (not city watch, private), gardens that are almost offensively lush given the rationing happening in the Manufactories.

The estates are not open. You are not going inside without an invitation, a very good reason, or a very poor plan. The private guards are well-paid and employed specifically to turn away people who don't have business here.

**From the street:** You can see the tops of the larger manor buildings over the walls. The gardens are visible at some corners. At night, the estates glow from within — they have their own dedicated Network connections and their power is never rationed.

**Social access:** Invitations to estate events — dinners, salons, private receptions — are the social currency of the Core. Getting one is the work of reputation, connections, or being interesting enough that someone wants you present. Once you have one, the world of the Core's inner social life becomes available. This is where deals are actually made, alliances are actually formed, and the real decisions get discussed before the Council vote happens.

### The Luminarium (Administrative Buildings)
The Luminarium's main academic campus is in the Academic Quarter, but its administrative and governance offices — the Rector's office, the Board of Research, the licensing office for experimental procedures — are here in the Core. This matters because the Board of Research is technically responsible for approving the research programs being run in the Academic Quarter, and several of the most dangerous programs currently underway were approved by a Board that didn't fully understand what it was approving.

**The Board of Research** is staffed by senior Luminarium graduates with Council-adjacent connections. They are smart people who have made the mistake of trusting their former colleagues. They would like to not know about certain ongoing experiments. Several of them are beginning to suspect that not knowing is becoming untenable.

### The Central Hub Station (Cable Car)
All four cable car lines converge at the Radiant Core's central station — a covered platform complex built above street level in the middle of the Core's main plaza. The station is busy at all hours. It's the only place in the Core where people from every district genuinely mix — servants from the estates, clerks from the Council District, Clank workers on assignment, academics from the Quarter, visiting merchants from Lamplight Ward, all waiting on the same platforms.

The station is also watched. City watch maintains a permanent presence here. It is not a good place to conduct business that shouldn't be observed.

## People You Might Encounter

### Councilar Mira Ashton
**Role:** Current Council Chair (Illuminist faction), the person nominally in charge of the gridlock  
**Where to find her:** Her offices in the Council District, the Accord Chamber during sessions, and occasionally eating alone at a small restaurant on the south edge of the Core that she's been using since before she was Chair  
**What she's like:** Exhausted in the specific way of someone who has been managing a crisis by sheer force of personality for too long. Sharp, measured, genuinely good at reading people and situations. Does not waste words. Will not commit to anything she can't deliver. Currently running on political debt she's been accumulating for a decade and knows she's running low.  
**What she wants:** Someone who can actually solve the Heart problem so she doesn't have to keep managing the consequences of it not being solved.  
**What she's worried about:** That the Expansionists are going to force a vote on military action before the Illuminists have anything to show for the research programs. That the research programs are producing something she hasn't been briefed on. That she's right about both of these things.

### Theron Ashwick
**Role:** Senior Councilor, Expansionist faction's most effective speaker  
**Where to find him:** The Expansionist wing of the Council offices, the private dining clubs of the Estates, occasionally the Accord Chamber gallery watching sessions he's not participating in  
**What he's like:** Charming, patient, and dangerous in the way of someone who has been playing a very long game and believes he's winning it. He frames everything as pragmatism. He is pragmatic. He is also building toward something, and the something is an authorized military campaign against the Endless Eye.  
**What he wants:** The Council vote on military authorization to come before the next brownout, while the Manufactories workers are still angry enough to support aggressive action and before the Illuminists produce anything that could defuse the situation with a technical solution.

### Sera Coldwell
**Role:** Head of the Vault Guard, technically reporting to the Council but in practice reporting to whoever she trusts  
**Where to find her:** The Vault District, the Council District for briefings, occasionally the Central Hub Station for reasons she doesn't explain  
**What she's like:** Forty years in city security. She has seen everyone and been impressed by almost no one. She is the person standing between the Heart and everyone who wants access to it for reasons that are not entirely what they say. She has granted exactly one unauthorized access in her career and she is not going to discuss it.  
**What she knows:** More than she says. The Vault Guard's monitoring extends beyond the Vault itself — they track unusual Network activity, unauthorized access to the infrastructure below the city, and the movement of people who shouldn't be in the Core District.

## Sleeping in the Radiant Core

Accommodation in the Core is limited and expensive — this is not a district built for visitors. Options:

**The Founder's Rest** — the one actual inn within the Core, small and aggressively priced (5 gold marks per night for a basic room, 15 for something decent). Used primarily by visiting Accord delegations, wealthy merchants with business in the Council District, and anyone whose expense account is someone else's problem. The proprietor, a human woman named Davathe Colm, has been running it for thirty years and has heard everything. She is discreet to a fault.

**Estate Guest Rooms** — if you have an invitation and have impressed the right people, you may be offered a room in one of the estate compounds. These are not technically inns. They are private hospitality and the expectations that come with it are social, not monetary.

For most people without Council business or an estate invitation, the Core is a day-trip district. You come here for meetings, for the Accord Chamber gallery, for the Hall of Records. You sleep somewhere cheaper.

## What's Available Here

- **High-end tailoring** (three shops catering exclusively to Council members and estate families — appointment only, prices not posted)
- **Private courier services** (for sensitive documents — the Core has three licensed courier services that specialize in getting things to Council members without going through official channels)
- **Legal consultation** (five established firms in the Council District, all Council-connected, all expensive)
- **The finest restaurant in Lumencrest**, arguably in the Heartlands: **Aurum** — a twelve-seat establishment that does not advertise, has no sign, and requires a reservation made weeks in advance. The food is extraordinary. The conversations that happen over it are sometimes more valuable.

---

## Faction Activity Here

- **Conservators** — entrenched in the Council District's eastern wing. Run the city's legitimate governance apparatus. Under pressure.
- **Illuminists** — Mira Ashton's faction, currently holding the Chair. Academic connections into the Luminarium administrative offices. Nervous.
- **Expansionists** — Theron Ashwick's faction, increasingly confident. Using estate social events to build coalitions outside the formal Council.
- **The Vault Guard** — operates independently but must work with all three. Sera Coldwell trusts none of them completely, which is the correct call.
