# The Vault District

**Population:** ~1,000 authorized personnel (Vault Guard, senior technocrats, maintenance engineers, select researchers)  
**Power Status:** Hardened redundant systems. The Vault District has never experienced a brownout. If the rest of the city went completely dark, the Vault District would still be running.  
**Cable Car:** No public access. An internal transport system exists for authorized personnel.  
**Access:** Restricted. You can walk the streets immediately surrounding the outer wall. You cannot enter without authorization, an oath, and an escort. Getting caught inside without all three is the worst mistake you can make in Lumencrest.

---

## Overview

The Vault District is not on most visitors' maps of Lumencrest. It's not hidden — the walls are visible, the guards are visible, and any Lumencrest resident can point you toward it — but it's treated as a fact of the city rather than a destination. Like the city's foundations or the water pipes: real, important, and not something ordinary people have reason to think about.

The district exists to protect and manage the Heart — the intact Hollostar buried beneath the city that has powered Lumencrest for 1,200 years. The Heart is the city's most critical asset and potentially its most dangerous secret: if the full extent of its failure became public knowledge, the consequences for Lumencrest's political stability, economic standing, and population morale are difficult to overstate. The Vault District exists to ensure that access to the Heart is controlled, that information about its condition is managed, and that no one without authorization gets close to it.

## What You Can See From Outside

The outer wall of the Vault District is eight feet of solid stone topped with iron spikes and electric lighting that runs even brighter than the Radiant Core's street lamps. Guard stations are visible at every corner. The wall encloses approximately eight city blocks. From the street, you can see the tops of the buildings inside — administrative structures, the guard barracks, and the entrance structure for the underground complex.

**The Vault Gate building** — recognizable from the surrounding streets as a low, heavy stone structure with a single massive reinforced door. The door looks wrong in the way that engineered objects sometimes do — too heavy, too precisely fitted, too deliberately permanent. There are no windows on the building. The guards at the gate stand at strict intervals and do not engage with passers-by.

Approximately one authorized vehicle or group of authorized personnel enters or exits every two hours on average. Vault Guard patrols pass the outer wall on a thirty-minute rotation.

## Getting Inside

There is a legitimate path to the Vault District's outer administrative areas (not the underground complex). It requires:

1. A formal request filed with the Council's Research Board, citing purpose and credentials
2. A background review (takes three to seven days, longer if your background is complicated)
3. A Council member co-signing the access request
4. A personal oath administered by the Vault Guard commander, Sera Coldwell, or her designated deputy
5. An escort — you do not walk unaccompanied inside the walls

This process exists for legitimate researchers, engineers, and officials. It is not designed to be impossible. It is designed to filter out everyone except people with a genuine sanctioned reason to be here.

Getting into the underground complex — the actual Heart chamber — requires an additional authorization level above the above, granted only to the Illuminist research team leads and Vault Guard leadership. Four Council members have access. Most of the rest do not.

**Getting inside by other means:** The Vault District has not been successfully breached in its history. This is not an accident — the design, staffing, and monitoring systems are built around the assumption that someone will always be trying. The underground complex has its own access points, theoretically, via the ancient infrastructure tunnels beneath the city. These access points are known to the Vault Guard. Whether they're all accounted for is something the Underground section covers more fully.

## Sera Coldwell — Vault Guard Commander

*See full entry in [[NPCs]].* Short version: former city watch, forty years in security, personally appointed by the full Council (the only decision in recent memory all three factions agreed on). She trusts the Council as an institution and none of the faction leaders as individuals. She has not had an unauthorized access in her tenure. She is aware that the classified research programs in the Academic Quarter represent a governance failure that has not yet become a security failure, and she is watching carefully for the moment that changes.
