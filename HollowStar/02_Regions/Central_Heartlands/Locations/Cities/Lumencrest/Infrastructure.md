# Lumencrest — Infrastructure

*How the city actually works: the cable car system, the Lumen Network, the Clank labor system, and what happens when any of it fails.*

---

## The Lumen Network

The Lumen Network is the system of underground cables, distribution nodes, and transforming equipment that carries Hollostar energy from the Heart upward and outward through the city. It is the city's circulatory system. Without it, Lumencrest is a large settlement with impressive architecture. With it, it's a civilization.

### How It Works (Simplified)

The Heart — the intact Hollostar buried beneath the Vault District — continuously outputs energy. This output is captured by the containment system deep underground and stepped through a series of distribution nodes that transform it into the form the Network cables carry. The cables run through the Infrastructure Tunnels beneath the streets and branch into every building in the covered districts through wall-mounted junction points. Each building has a distribution panel (usually in a basement or utility room) that receives the city's power and branches it to the individual circuits — lights, machines, heating elements, whatever that building's infrastructure requires.

The city's engineers understand the operation of this system. They do not fully understand the underlying principles — the technology was partially developed by Lumencrest's founders over centuries of experiment, and some of it works in ways that cannot be fully explained by current theory. The Network technicians are expert operators of a system that is more thoroughly understood than it was 800 years ago but less thoroughly than anyone would like.

### Coverage

| District | Coverage Level | Status |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Radiant Core | Complete, hardened redundant systems | Never brownouts |
| Academic Quarter | Complete | Currently running at increased load |
| Lamplight Ward | Complete | Brownouts hit here early and visibly |
| Halfling Quarter | Mostly complete (95%) | Partial independent backup in brewing/baking |
| The Manufactories | Complete but rationed | Priority to factory machinery over residences |
| Shadowside | None | Has never been connected |
| Vault District | Hardened independent systems | Fully isolated from city grid |

### The Decline

Power output from the Heart has been declining for thirty years — roughly 2.5% per year on a declining curve. This doesn't sound like much until you compound it: thirty years of 2.5% annual decline means current output is approximately 47% of where it was when the decline began. The city was built for 100% output. It is currently managing on less than half.

The Council's official position is that the decline is being studied, solutions are being developed, and the city is managing effectively. The technical reality is that "managing effectively" means an increasingly complex rationing system that prioritizes the Radiant Core and Vault District above all others, the Manufactories' industrial machinery above its residences, and Shadowside not at all.

The system has approximately three to five years at current trajectory before output drops below the threshold required to maintain basic infrastructure — water pumping, sanitation, emergency systems. Below that threshold, Lumencrest stops being a city in any functional sense. The Council knows this. They have not said it publicly.

## Brownouts and Blackouts

### What Causes Them

The Network operates within a stable output range. When demand exceeds supply — either because output has dropped or because something on the demand side spikes — the system compensates by reducing voltage across the network. This produces a brownout: reduced power, dimmer lights, machines running slower or not at all.

When the imbalance is severe enough, or when a distribution node fails, the system doesn't compensate — it fails. This produces a blackout: complete loss of power in the affected zone.

There have been six brownouts and one complete blackout in the past year. The complete blackout — all of Lumencrest, all at once, several minutes — has not happened before in the city's history.

### What It Looks and Sounds Like

**Warning signs (Presence DC 10 to notice):**
- The hum drops in pitch, subtly, about thirty seconds before a brownout hits
- Lights in older lamp casings flicker briefly once or twice
- Powered machinery changes tone very slightly — a fraction of a second's variation in the cycle

**During a brownout:**
> *The lights dim. Not dark — more like the difference between noon sun and heavy overcast. The warm amber of Lamplight Ward's old street lamps becomes something cooler and less confident. Inside buildings, people instinctively reach for candles they may or may not have. Powered machinery slows — the cable cars decelerate to about half speed and continue running. Factories go to reduced operation. The hum drops to something lower and less steady. People go quiet for a moment. Then they resume what they were doing, because this has happened before and they've learned to manage.*

**During a full blackout:**
> *The hum stops.*
>
> *The lights go out. Not dimming — gone, all at once. The cable cars stop moving, hanging on their cables in darkness. In the Manufactories, factory machinery winds down with a sound like the city exhaling. The streets, which were bright as noon even at midnight, become lit only by moonlight, windows with private candles, and the orange glow of open fires from Shadowside.*
>
> *For approximately three minutes, Lumencrest is silent. Not quiet — silent. The background sound of a city running on powered machinery is gone, and what replaces it is the sound of people being very still.*
>
> *Then the hum returns. It surges — briefly louder than normal, as if the system is reminding itself how to work — and the lights come back, blinking on street by street as the Network restores from the Core outward. Within ninety seconds, everything looks normal again.*
>
> *Nobody wants to talk about it.*

### District Response to Brownouts

**Radiant Core:** Doesn't experience brownouts. The Core residents notice when the rest of the city dims through their windows. This observation is not comfortable.

**Lamplight Ward:** The amber street lamps here have older lamp casings that show voltage reduction more visibly than the Core's newer fittings. A brownout in Lamplight Ward looks particularly dramatic — the characteristic amber glow of the district drops to something dim and slightly greenish. Shopkeepers have candles behind their counters. They didn't used to.

**Halfling Quarter:** The Quarter's independent backup systems for its brewing and baking operations mean that the core food production continues through brownouts. The residential streets dim like Lamplight Ward. The brewery towers stay bright. This is quietly noted by Quarter residents as a source of practical satisfaction.

**The Manufactories:** Factory machinery takes priority in the rationing system, which means during brownouts the factories sometimes maintain partial operation while residential lighting in the same district goes out entirely. Workers walk home through their own neighbourhood's darkness while their factory floor stays lit. The resentment this produces is considerable.

**Shadowside:** Shadowside doesn't change during brownouts. The candles and oil lamps that light the district are entirely independent of the Network. In the aftermath of the big blackout, when the rest of the city was still processing what had happened, Shadowside residents were going about their business normally. Several of them found this funnier than others did.

## The Cable Car System

### Overview

Four cable car lines connect Lumencrest's major districts. The cars are powered by the Lumen Network — electric motors at the terminal stations drive the cables, which pull all the cars on the line simultaneously at a consistent speed. This means all cars on a line slow down together in a brownout and stop together in a blackout.

The cable car system was completed approximately 200 years ago and represents one of Lumencrest's most visible infrastructure achievements. Visitors from other cities treat riding the cable car as an experience; residents treat it as commuting.

### The Four Lines

**The Central Line** — east-west through the city's middle. Lamplight Ward East → Merchant Square → Central Hub (Radiant Core) → Luminarium Stop (Academic Quarter) → North Factory Hub (Manufactories). The busiest line, running at capacity during peak hours.

**The Wire Road Line** — follows the Wire Road. Lamplight Ward South → Merchant Square → Halfling Quarter Entrance → South Gate area. The most visitor-used line; connects the main entry point to the key commercial districts.

**The Factory Connector** — purely utilitarian. South Factory Hub → North Factory Hub → Shadowside Border. The Shadowside Border stop is the closest the cable car gets to Shadowside — it operates at the district's edge but does not enter it.

**The River Line** — follows the Silverrun River's edge. Eastwick (Lamplight Ward) → Academic Quarter → Luminarium Stop. Shorter line, used primarily by residents of eastern Lamplight Ward and the Academic Quarter.

### Fares and Access

Two copper marks per person per trip, regardless of distance. Children under twelve free. Clanks in city service pay full fare from their work allocation. Free Clanks pay the same as anyone else.

The cars are not segregated by class or ancestry. During peak hours on the Central Line, every ancestry in the city is represented in the same car. This is one of the few genuinely egalitarian institutions in Lumencrest, which is either a feature or an oversight depending on who you ask.

### Using Cable Cars for Scene Descriptions

Cable car rides are an opportunity for encounters, conversations overheard, and views of the city that orient players spatially:

> *From the Central Line between Merchant Square and the Luminarium Stop, you can see the Accord Chamber dome to your south, the Vault District walls to your west, and on clear days the Ironspine Mountains to the northwest. The city stretches below you in its districts — the dense amber warmth of Lamplight Ward, the darker gap of Shadowside against the northern wall, the chimney stacks of the Manufactories reading like punctuation in the western skyline.*

## The Clanks of Lumencrest

### The System

The Luminarium developed the first Clanks — artificially constructed sentient beings — approximately 180 years ago as a labor solution. The city's legal framework defines Clanks produced by Lumencrest's facilities as city property assigned to a term of indentured service. The term length varies based on the complexity and resource cost of construction: most Clanks serve between 15 and 30 years before earning free status.

During their service term, Clanks are assigned work by the Labour Board, housed in city-managed facilities (primarily the Clank Barracks in the Manufactories Assembly Halls section), and provided with basic maintenance supplies and nutrition. They are paid a small wage throughout their service — too small to significantly accelerate their freedom but real enough that the system can point to it.

The city's position is that this is not slavery. The Clanks were created to serve, their service term is finite, and they earn genuine freedom at its end. A growing number of people — centered in the Academic Quarter's Student Assembly and the Free Hand network — argue that creating a being capable of suffering and then requiring decades of labor as the price of its own freedom is, definitionally, slavery regardless of the endpoint. This argument has not yet produced policy change. It is getting louder.

### Clank Identification

Every Lumencrest Clank has a small arcane implant installed during construction, located at the base of the neck in a position that's accessible for scanning but not prominently visible. The implant can be read by any authorized scanner — carried by Vault Guard, city watch officers, and the Labour Board — and returns one of four states:

- **SERVING [XX years remaining]** — current indentured Clank in good standing
- **FREE** — term completed; full citizen status
- **AUTHORIZED VISITOR** — Clank built outside Lumencrest; not in the system
- **FLAGGED** — escaped; immediate detention protocol

City gate guards scan Clanks they identify entering. Whether a Clank is identified depends on how Clank-appearing they are — some Clanks pass as other ancestries in dim light or at a distance; others are immediately identifiable. The scans are not done on every person entering the city, only on those identified as likely Clanks.

A FLAGGED scan at the gate means the gate guard triggers an alert and three additional officers respond within sixty seconds. The Clank in question is detained pending Labour Board review. The review rarely goes well for the Clank.

### Clank Daily Life

**Serving Clanks** live in the Clank Barracks or in accommodation provided by their assignment (some household assignments include domestic living arrangements). They work assigned hours — usually eight to ten hours per day, six days a week — and have the remaining time as personal time, which they spend however they choose within city limits. Many spend personal time at the Free Clank Cooperative in Shadowside, the Academic Quarter's Clank advocacy groups, or in various underground social spaces that have developed around the Clank community.

**Free Clanks** live where they choose, work where they choose, and are full citizens. The ~400 free Clanks in Lumencrest are distributed across all districts except Shadowside — where a disproportionate number live, partly because housing is cheap and partly because Shadowside doesn't ask questions. Many work skilled trades they developed during their service terms. Some remain in the same work they performed while serving, now for wages. A few have become significant figures in various city institutions — there is a free Clank who holds a junior faculty position at the Luminarium, which has generated considerable institutional awkwardness.

**Escaped Clanks** are not in Lumencrest. They left and had their implants removed. If they're somehow back, they're either extremely brave or extremely desperate, and they're accessing the city through Shadowside's underground connections and keeping a very low profile.

## Water and Sanitation

The city's water supply comes from the Silverrun River and underground springs, pumped through a city-wide system by electrically powered pumping stations. Sanitation runs through the sewer network to treatment facilities on the city's eastern edge, also powered.

**The critical vulnerability:** If power drops below the threshold to run the pumping stations, water supply fails within hours. Sanitation fails within a day. Disease becomes a serious risk within a week. This is the scenario the Council isn't discussing publicly that every Network technician is running calculations about privately.

The city has emergency water reserves — cisterns and storage tanks that hold enough water for approximately three days. Beyond three days, the city would need to begin organized rationing from river access points, which is logistically possible but would require a degree of public acknowledgment of the crisis that the Council has so far avoided.
