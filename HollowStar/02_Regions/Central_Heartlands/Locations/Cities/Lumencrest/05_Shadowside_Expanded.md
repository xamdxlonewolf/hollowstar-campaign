# Shadowside — Expanded District Guide

## Points of Interest, Vendors, and NPCs

_This document expands on the Shadowside district as established in [[05_Shadowside]], adding the locations, people, and daily texture needed to run the district at the table. Shadowside is the above-ground face of Lumencrest's underclass — darker, denser, and more self-sufficient than any other district, and the direct surface layer above the Underground's tunnel network. Cross-reference [[08_The_Underground_Expanded]] for the tunnel-level operations that run beneath these streets; the two worlds connect here more than anywhere else._

---

## Running Shadowside at the Table

Shadowside has a rhythm. Players need to feel it before the locations make sense.

**Daytime Shadowside** is dense and functional and slightly chaotic in the way of a market that grew without a plan — which is exactly what it is. The streets are narrow enough that carts have to slow to a crawl and pedestrians shuffle sideways to let them pass. Traders work from wherever they can get a foothold: window sills, doorframes, folding tables that go back inside when the weather turns. The buildings are old stone with newer timber additions that lean toward each other overhead, blocking light and creating the semi-covered passages the Maze Quarter is built from. It smells like coal smoke, cooking fat, animals, and the particular human smell of 15,000 people living close together without electric heating or reliable hot water. It is not unpleasant, exactly. It is very much itself.

**Nighttime Shadowside** is different in a way that takes newcomers a moment to process. When the Lumen Network lights up the rest of the city, Shadowside goes dark — genuinely dark, the kind that the rest of Lumencrest forgot existed. Oil lamps and candles in windows create islands of amber light with real shadow between them. The Night Market appears on the main thoroughfare as if it was always there and the daytime market was the thing that didn't belong. People move through the dark with the ease of those who have been doing it their whole lives and can read a street by the smell of it. Visitors slow down. Locals don't.

**The city watch** patrols Shadowside minimally and knows it. Two officers on the main thoroughfare, a rotation past Pauper's Gate, and the deliberate fiction that the Maze Quarter is navigable for a pair of uniformed officers who don't know it. The Council has assessed this as the correct allocation. The assessment is correct: heavy-handed policing here would cost more in unrest than it would gain in compliance. Shadowside has been making this calculation for centuries and it has worked out both ways. Currently it works in the district's favor.

**The brownout non-event:** When the power crisis sends the rest of Lumencrest into dim amber anxiety and the city watch starts their incident-monitoring protocols, Shadowside notices nothing. The candles keep burning. The coal braziers stay warm. There is a dry, specific satisfaction among long-term residents when a brownout hits — the quiet knowledge that they were right not to wait for the Network. Nobody says this to outsiders. It doesn't need to be said.

## The Night Market — Detailed Operations

_(Referenced in [[05_Shadowside]] — expanded here as a full playable location)_

**Location:** The main north-south thoroughfare through Shadowside, running from Pauper's Gate south toward the Lamplight Ward border. Roughly 400 feet of active market space, narrowing at the Maze Quarter intersection where permanent stalls have encroached on both sides.

**Hours:** The Night Market doesn't open so much as arrive. Traders begin appearing about an hour after dark, and the market reaches full operation roughly two hours after sunset. It runs until the last trader decides to leave, which in practice is usually two to three hours before dawn — though some stalls persist until daylight and simply become the daytime market without transition.

**Layout:** The market is not organized by any authority. What organization exists is the accumulated habit of regular traders who have claimed the same patch of thoroughfare for years. The southern end — closer to Lamplight Ward — tends toward goods that could pass as legitimate in the daytime: salvage, second-hand clothing, tools, foodstuffs. Moving north toward Pauper's Gate and the Maze Quarter intersection, the inventory gets less explainable. The Maze Quarter's eastern mouth is where the information trade is most concentrated — a handful of individuals who appear to be waiting for someone, always are, and are not waiting for each other.

**What's available — by section:**

_Southern stretch (legitimate-adjacent):_

- Salvage goods from across the city — Network components, building materials, furniture, anything that could theoretically have been acquired by legitimate means
- Second-hand clothing, tools, kitchenware — the kind of market any large city has, just after dark for the particular reason that the sellers prefer not to have papers
- Food from several semi-permanent vendors who operate day and night (see: Mama Voss's Cart, below)
- Candles, lamp oil, coal — the items Shadowside runs on, available here cheaper than anywhere else in Lumencrest because the supply chain doesn't include the merchant guilds

_Central stretch (gray market):_

- Goods from outside Lumencrest that arrived without going through the gate officially — traveling via the Silverrun River's less-monitored sections, via Pauper's Gate's deliberate lack of close inspection, or via the tunnel connections to the underground
- Substances: dried herbs and preparations of varying legality, some alchemical, some from regional traditions the city hasn't bothered to classify
- Live animals — occasionally exotic, always in variable condition, always with no documentation
- Services: a handful of people who offer specific skills at night rates, including minor arcane work that isn't licensed, message delivery that doesn't go through official channels, and locksmiths who don't ask why you need a lock opened

_Northern stretch / Maze Quarter mouth (information and high-end black market):_

- Information is sold here the way goods are sold on the southern stretch — you ask for what you need, you hear a price, you pay it or you don't. The quality varies and there is no recourse if you're sold bad information, which keeps the better sellers honest because reputation is all they have
- High-value items that have moved through the Quiet Market's network and need a retail outlet — these appear rarely and are gone quickly
- The Quiet Market contact (see: The Quiet Market, below) operates from a fixed spot near the Maze Quarter wall on nights when the Market is being arranged — a middleman named **Joss Wren** (see NPCs) who takes inquiries and schedules access

**The Night Market's unspoken rules:**

- No violence on the thoroughfare. The market polices this itself — traders and regulars who have a stake in the market continuing to function will intervene in any altercation, collectively and without coordination. This has been enough every time so far.
- No city watch. This is maintained by the district's natural watch-sensing — locals who clock watch presence and pass it down the street ahead of them in the time it takes a patrol to walk a block. The night market does not scatter when watch appears; it simply stops being obviously what it is until they pass.
- Children present. Shadowside is a neighborhood, not just a market. Families live on this street. Kids run errands at night because this is when the market runs and this is when the errands get done. This is worth noting at the table — the Night Market is not an exclusively adult space and the presence of children gives it a texture that pure black-market operations don't have.

### The Dark Lantern

_(Introduced in [[05_Shadowside]] and [[08_The_Underground_Expanded]] — full entry here)_

**Location:** Main thoroughfare, Shadowside. The building has been a tavern longer than anyone currently living has been alive. The sign — a lantern painted on the exterior wall in black, faded to a suggestion — is original to the current proprietor's tenure, thirty years ago.

**What it is:** The social center of Shadowside and the above-ground anchor point for the Underground's network. Not glamorous. Not trying to be. The Dark Lantern is where everyone comes because it is where everyone comes, and it has been this way long enough that the fact of it perpetuates itself. If you need to find someone in this district, you start here. If you need someone to find you, you sit here and order something.

**The physical space:** Low ceiling, timber and stone, oil lamps that are maintained but not extravagant. Tables of mismatched wood, benches worn smooth. A bar of solid old timber with Solt behind it. The curtained back section — three tables, capacity for twelve if they're friendly — is available for an additional five copper marks per person and is understood to be private in the sense that nothing said there will be repeated by the staff. The kitchen is small and behind the bar and produces things that are hot, filling, and available.

**What's on offer:**

_Drink:_

- House ale — brewed locally by an arrangement Solt has had for twenty years, mediocre, cold, reliable. 2 copper marks a cup.
- Spirits — sourced through the Night Market, rotating selection, always at least three options. 4-6 copper marks depending on what it is.
- Solt's house brew — available only to regulars and only when Solt decides. A dark, herbed thing that doesn't taste like anything else in the city. No fixed price; he charges what he thinks you should pay, which is always fair and sometimes insulting.
- Tea — actual tea, not the Sump's herbed approximation. One copper mark. Solt considers tea the most honest drink available because nobody has ever pretended it was anything other than what it was.

_Food:_

- Thick stew, base rotating weekly. Whatever was available and could be bought without documentation. Usually grain, usually some protein, always hot. 3 copper marks with bread.
- Salted meat and bread — the backup when the stew runs out. 2 copper marks.
- On good days: eggs, roasted root vegetables, a soft cheese that a contact of Solt's brings irregularly. These appear on the bar without announcement and are gone within the hour.

**The back section:** The curtain is heavy canvas, which doesn't block sound perfectly but blocks it enough. The surcharge (5 copper per person) is the formal signal that this table is in use and is not for walk-ins. Solt will bring drinks to the back section but does not linger. Conversations held there are, by the Dark Lantern's long-standing social contract, not conversations Solt has heard.

**Regulars:** Shadowside's functional population — traders, workers, Night Market vendors between shifts, people who live three doors down and have nowhere better to go. Over any given week, almost everyone with business in the district passes through at least once. This is the point.

**The proprietor:** Solt — see NPCs.

### The Free Clank Cooperative

_(Referenced in [[05_Shadowside]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** The Maze Quarter's western edge, in a building that was a cooperage (barrel-making) operation before the current use and still has the large ground-floor workspace that cooperages require. The sign says COOPERATIVE WORKSHOP AND RESALE. This is accurate.

**What it presents as:** A repair and resale operation. Clanks bring in broken machinery — household items, small tools, components — fix them in the workshop, and sell the repaired goods at a stall that runs most days on the Maze Quarter's edge. The income is real. The work is real. Every Clank who works here is doing genuine labor on genuine goods.

**What it also is:** The first point of contact for escaped Clanks arriving in Shadowside. The social and community space for free Clanks who live in or pass through the district. A node in the Free Hand's network, though the Cooperative maintains the position that it is not formally part of the Free Hand and this is technically true in the sense that the Free Hand has no formal membership structure. The Cooperative knows who to contact for implant removal. It knows which routes through the tunnel network are currently safe. It knows which city watch officers are more thorough than others at the gate scans. It does not advertise any of this.

**The workshop:** The ground floor is genuine workshop — tools on the walls, worktables, a mechanical press for large components, the smell of oil and metal. Three or four Clanks working at most hours, doing actual repair work. Visitors are welcome on the ground floor; it's a shop. The upper floor is not a shop.

**What the upper floor is:** Meeting space, sleeping space for Clanks in transit who need a night before the next step in their route out, and a room where conversations about less-documented topics happen. Access requires either being a Clank (sufficient in itself) or being accompanied by someone the Cooperative knows.

**The workshop manager:** **Kael** — see NPCs. Kael is a free Clank who completed a service term eight years ago and chose to stay in Lumencrest, which is unusual. The reason they stayed is the Cooperative, which they've been running for six years and which they treat with the specific care of someone who knows exactly what it represents to the people who need it.

**What's for sale:** Repaired household goods, tools, and small machinery at 50-70% of new price. The quality of repair is excellent — Clanks understand machinery with a directness that human craftspeople often don't reach. This is genuinely the best place to buy a repaired item in Shadowside and several Lamplight Ward residents know this and make the trip.

**City watch attention:** The Cooperative is watched. Not constantly, not aggressively — a city watch officer swings past most mornings, notes that the workshop is operational, and continues. The Labour Board has inspected it twice in six years. Both times the Cooperative's documentation was in order. Both times the documents were real. The Cooperative is careful about what is real and what isn't.

### The Salvation Hearth

_(Referenced in [[05_Shadowside]] — expanded here)_

**Location:** A large building on Shadowside's eastern edge, at the border with Lamplight Ward. The building is old — older than the current faith that runs it — and shows its age in the way that buildings built to last show age: deep in the stone, in the worn steps, in the patches where different centuries of repair are visible in the wall. The First Flame sigil (a simple carved flame, Unified Ember denomination) is above the door. The door is never locked.

**What it is:** A charitable institution operated by the Unified Ember branch of the First Flame faith, providing meals, basic medical care, and emergency shelter. The priests here are not naive — nobody who has run a charitable operation in Shadowside for twenty years is naive — but they are genuinely committed to the work in a way that isn't performed. They see who comes in. They help where they can. They ask for nothing beyond the request itself.

**Services:**

- _Meals:_ Two per day — morning and evening, available to anyone who comes through the door. Simple food, reliably available, no documentation required. The evening meal is the larger of the two and draws a crowd; twenty to forty people most nights, more in winter.
- _Basic medical care:_ One of the priests, a woman named **Sister Avri**, has training in healing magic and conventional medicine both. She works the Hearth's medical space every morning. The care is genuine and competent for common conditions — illness, minor injuries, exhaustion, the standard ailments of poverty. She refers cases beyond her capability to Vael at the Stitch, with whom she has a working relationship that neither discusses publicly.
- _Emergency beds:_ Ten beds in the back room, available for people in genuine need. Free. The priests assess need in a way that is generous — they err toward helping rather than scrutinizing. The beds are clean. They come with a blanket. Nobody is asked to attend a service or demonstrate faith.
- _Occasional extras:_ Clothing in poor condition but functional, available from a donated-goods cabinet near the entrance. Soap. Basic literacy help for residents who want it — two priests run a reading group three evenings a week, ostensibly for scripture but practically for anyone who wants to learn.

**The priests' position in the district:** The Salvation Hearth has been here long enough that it's simply part of Shadowside. The priests are not outsiders conducting outreach — they live in the building, they buy their food from the Night Market, they know the Maze Quarter's layout. They are treated by the district with the particular cautious warmth of people who have consistently proven they're not going to report anything to anyone, which is the highest social currency Shadowside offers. City watch knows the Hearth won't cooperate with investigations. City watch has accepted this as part of the district's arrangement.

**The emergency beds as a player resource:** The Salvation Hearth will provide beds to players who present as genuinely in need — not adventurers looking for a cheap night, but people who have had a bad night and need somewhere safe. The priests assess quickly and are hard to fool, but they extend the benefit of the doubt generously. If players have done anything visible to help the district, this assessment tips in their favor.

**The lead priest:** **Brother Carwell** — see NPCs.

### The Hollow Road Crossing — The Border Bar

**Location:** At the point where the Hollow Road crosses from Lamplight Ward into Shadowside — the exact line where the street lamps end and the darkness begins. A building on the Shadowside side of this line that has been a bar for forty years and has a view of both worlds from its front windows.

**What it is:** The transitional establishment. Where the two cities of Lumencrest meet in a single room. The clientele is a mixture that exists nowhere else in the city — Lamplight Ward residents who came for the experience, or who have business in Shadowside and stop here first, or who simply like drinking on the edge of the known; and Shadowside residents who work near the border, or who want a drink without going to the Dark Lantern, or who like watching the Ward people try to figure out where they are.

**The physical space:** Larger than the Dark Lantern — the building is on a proper corner lot and was commercial before it was a bar. Two rooms: the front room with windows on the Hollow Road, which draws the curiosity traffic, and the back room with no windows, which is where the regulars go when they want to drink without being watched by people finding it interesting that they're in Shadowside.

**What's on offer:**

- Lamplight Ward ale on tap — the real thing, bought through a supplier arrangement that the bar's owner has maintained for years as a deliberate quality marker. 3 copper marks, more expensive than the Dark Lantern, appropriate for the location.
- Shadowside spirits alongside Ward wine — you can drink either world in this room, which is the point.
- Food: a proper kitchen, better than most Shadowside options. Meat pies, roasted vegetables, the kind of food that would be comfortable in a mid-range Lamplight Ward establishment. 4-6 copper marks.
- Information about Shadowside for Lamplight Ward visitors who ask the proprietor nicely and tip appropriately. This information is accurate and safe — nothing that would get anyone in trouble, everything a visitor needs to navigate without making dangerous mistakes.

**The proprietor:** **Lenne Cross** — see NPCs. Has run this bar for twelve years and understands the border better than anyone.

### Mama Voss's Cart

**Location:** Night Market thoroughfare, approximately in the center. During the day, the cart operates from the same spot as a daytime food stall. After dark, the same cart, the same food, the same woman, with a somewhat different clientele.

**What it is:** The best street food in Shadowside, which by extension is some of the best street food in Lumencrest. Mama Voss runs a cart operation that has no parallel for value — the food is good by any standard in the city, the portions are generous, and the price is accessible to people who are counting copper marks. The cart has been in the same spot for twenty-three years. The Night Market organizes itself in part around where Mama Voss is.

**What's on offer:**

- Spiced meat skewers — the signature. Pork or goat depending on what was available, marinated in a preparation she will not describe, cooked over charcoal that she starts at noon and keeps burning all day and night. 2 copper marks for three skewers.
- Flatbread with oil and roasted garlic — 1 copper mark, and this is the one item that the poorest Shadowside residents can reliably afford. She charges the same for everyone, which has its own statement in it.
- Vegetable stew sold in cups — changes daily. Always filling. 2 copper marks.
- On the days when she's had a good supply run: a rice preparation with dried fruit and spice that has no name she'll give you in the common tongue, from a regional tradition nobody in the city but her makes. She sells out in under an hour and doesn't apologize.

**Seating:** No seating. You eat standing or you take it with you.

**The proprietor:** **Mama Voss** — see NPCs. Not anyone's actual mother, as far as can be established. The name preceded her by about fifteen years and she adopted it when she took over the cart.

### The Lampblack — Tavern and Boarding

**Location:** A side street off the main thoroughfare, in Shadowside's mid-section between the Maze Quarter and the Hollow Road border. Identifiable by the black smear on the left doorpost — lampblack, applied deliberately, renewed periodically. Nobody has explained why. It predates the current proprietor.

**What it is:** The Lampblack is Shadowside's second tavern — not the Dark Lantern's social complexity or neutral function, just a straightforward drinking and sleeping establishment that serves the district's working population. Manufactories workers who live in Shadowside to afford the rent drink here after shift. Night Market traders wind down here. People who want to drink without being in a room where deals are made drink here.

**The physical space:** Single-story, longer than it is wide, with a bar along one wall and tables filling the floor. Low-burning oil lamps. Cleaner than you'd expect — the proprietor is particular about the tables and the floor and is visibly irritated when they're not maintained to his standard. The boarding section is upstairs: seven rooms, all private, all with working locks, for 5 silver a night. The mattresses are old but the linens are changed.

**What's on offer:**

- Ale — a different local supplier than the Dark Lantern, roughly equivalent quality. 2 copper marks.
- A whiskey of local origin that is not refined but is honest about what it is. 3 copper marks.
- Food: the Lampblack's kitchen runs until midnight. The cook, a heavyset Halfling named **Pell**, makes a rotating selection of simple dishes — nothing fancy, nothing small. Heavy plates of grains, roasted meat, stewed greens. 3-4 copper marks. The portions are Halfling-cook generous, which means substantial by any standard.
- The boarding rooms are not advertised outside but are known in the district. Maret at the Night's Rest will refer overflow there; the arrangement is reciprocal.

**The proprietor:** **Orin Marsh** — see NPCs.

### The Smoke Den

**Location:** A basement establishment in the Maze Quarter, accessed through a street-level door marked only by a blue cloth pinned above the frame. The stairs go down. The ceiling is low. The smoke is real.

**What it is:** Shadowside's establishment for substances. Not the Night Market's open sale, not the Stitch's medical dispensing — the Smoke Den is where you go to consume rather than acquire. A room of roughly thirty feet by twenty, with bench seating along the walls and cushioned floor sections in the center, lit by hanging oil lamps through colored glass that makes the light amber-red. The air is dense with the smoke of the three substances the Den serves, which is fine if you're there for it and less fine if you didn't know before you walked in.

**What's available:**

- _Hollow Pipe_ — a dried herb blend that is technically legal in Lumencrest, technically, in the sense that the Council has not specifically banned it and the substance predates the city's licensing framework. A mild preparation. Many Shadowside residents use it the way Lamplight Ward residents use wine — socially, regularly, unremarkably. 2 copper marks for a pipe.
- _The Red_ — an alchemical preparation from the Vossomar region that arrived via the Night Market and has no legal status in Lumencrest because nobody in the licensing apparatus has gotten around to classifying it. Effects: warmth, reduced pain, a quality of well-being that experienced users describe differently from each other. Dependency risk with frequent use. 8 copper marks per dose.
- _Dreamsmoke_ — available only by request, served only to known regulars, and the proprietor will tell you it isn't available if he doesn't know you. An arcane preparation of uncertain origin that the Night Market brings in irregularly. Strong. Duration of four to six hours. Not appropriate for anyone who needs to be functional within that window. 1 silver mark per dose.

**The rules of the Den:** You do not bring trouble in. You do not bring city watch in, obviously. You do not purchase and take out — the Den's licensing fiction depends on what happens here staying here. You do not bother other patrons. The staff (two, both large, both present at all times) enforce this simply and without extensive conversation.

**The proprietor:** **Renn Aldous** — see NPCs.

### Threadbare — Clothing and Equipment

**Location:** Main thoroughfare, Shadowside, daytime operation only (the space is rented to a Night Market trader after dark under an arrangement with the proprietor).

**What it is:** The district's best clothing and practical equipment shop — which means it sells functional things at prices Shadowside residents can reach, from a stock that mixes legitimate second-hand, Night Market acquisitions, and occasional new goods that came through supply chains the proprietor tracks but doesn't examine. If you need to not look like you came from somewhere else, Threadbare is where you start. If you need rope, a pack, a blanket, a knife, a set of lockpicks presented as "tradesman's tools," or a coat that won't attract attention in three different parts of the city, Threadbare has it or can get it.

**What's in stock:**

- Clothing across a wide range — second-hand, repaired, and clean. The proprietor is selective: nothing arrives on the shelf without being washed and assessed. Coverage from street-worker practical to something that wouldn't embarrass you in Lamplight Ward. Prices from 3 copper marks (worn but functional underclothes) to 8 silver (a good coat in a neutral color).
- Practical equipment: rope, bags, blankets, lanterns, tinderboxes, bedrolls — the traveler's kit and the laborer's kit, accessible pricing.
- Tools: general-purpose, some specialized. A section in the back that's discreetly organized for things that require discretion.
- Boots: Threadbare has a small cobbler's operation in the back corner — repairs only, not new-made. 3-6 silver for a solid repair, and the repairs hold.

**What the proprietor can get:** If something isn't in stock, the proprietor will tell you whether she can source it and at what price. Her sourcing network is the Night Market and specific contacts within it. Turnaround is usually two to three days. She doesn't source weapons beyond working knives, nothing that came from the Vault, and nothing she'd have to explain to city watch.

**The proprietor:** **Sable Mors** — see NPCs.

### The Quiet Market — Contact Point

_(Referenced in [[08_The_Underground]] — Contact point here in Shadowside)_

**Location:** Not a fixed location — the Quiet Market is mobile, meeting in a different place each week. But the person who arranges access operates from a fixed point at the northern end of the Night Market thoroughfare, near the Maze Quarter mouth, on nights the Market is being arranged.

**What it is:** The high-end equivalent of the Night Market, for goods and services that require a more selective clientele — Hollostar shard fragments, restricted alchemical substances, information of significant value, quality stolen items, rare regional goods, exotic materials. Access requires knowing the contact, having something worth bringing, and being vetted.

**The contact point:** A man called **Joss Wren** (see NPCs) occupies a particular spot near the Maze Quarter's eastern wall on specific nights — an irregular schedule that isn't publicly posted but is known in the district's network. He takes inquiries, assesses whether the inquirer is worth bringing to the Market's organizers, and arranges introductions for those who pass. He does not attend the Market himself. He does not know the location of any specific Market meeting until 24 hours before it occurs.

**Getting access:** Joss needs to know two things: that you're not city watch and that you have either something to sell that the Market wants or the coin to buy at the Market's prices. He takes two to four days to verify the first through his own network. The second is assessed in conversation. First-time access comes with an escort and the understanding that behaving badly at the Market is the permanent end of access.

### The Maze Quarter — Navigation and Points of Note

The Maze Quarter is not a single location but a navigable (for locals) sub-district that deserves its own entry as a player-facing space.

**Orientation:** The Maze Quarter occupies Shadowside's heart — a roughly six-block area where streets narrowed over centuries of organic construction until passages are shoulder-width and buildings connect overhead. The official city map of this area is inaccurate in ways that matter: three streets it marks as through-passages are dead ends; two areas it marks as buildings are in fact covered courtyards accessible only from the inside. Locals know this. Visitors learn it the hard way.

**Navigation rules:**

- Without a guide or established local knowledge, navigating the Maze Quarter requires a DC 13 Instinct roll every ten minutes to avoid becoming directionally confused. Failure doesn't mean lost — it means you've been going the wrong way and don't know it yet.
- With a guide, navigation is automatic.
- With a week of daily experience in the Maze Quarter, a character can navigate without rolls.
- Locals can tell when someone doesn't know the Maze. The tell is the way they look at intersections. This is noted.

**Points of note within the Maze:**

- _The Arch_ — a passage where two buildings connect overhead at the second story, creating a covered section about fifteen feet long. The Arch is a recognized neutral meeting point — disputes are brought here to be settled by whoever in the district has enough social weight to settle them. There's no formal arbitration; the Maze Quarter doesn't work that way. The Arch is where you go when you need witnesses.
- _The Eastern Courtyard_ — a private courtyard accessible only through an unmarked door on a passage that doesn't appear on official maps. Used by the Maze Quarter's longer-term residents as a genuine communal space — laundry, children's play, the kind of outdoor life that the covered passages don't allow. Outsiders who find it by accident are politely redirected. Outsiders who have been introduced to the community may eventually be invited.
- _The Forgotten Well_ — a working well in the Maze Quarter's center that predates the city's water infrastructure. Still draws clean water. The surrounding flagstones are worn smooth from centuries of use. Market traders fill their water containers here; residents carry buckets up from it when the district's water supply from the city's pumping system is disrupted. During a full blackout, when the pumps stop, the Forgotten Well is where Shadowside's residents go.

### Solt

_(Full entry in [[09_The_Underground_Expanded]])_

**Shadowside-specific:** Solt has been behind the Dark Lantern's bar for thirty years. He is not from Shadowside — he came here from somewhere else that he doesn't discuss — but he is of Shadowside in every sense that matters. The district treats him with the respect given to permanent fixtures that have proven their use. He is the first person any newcomer to the district should find and the last person anyone in the district wants as an enemy, not because Solt has power in the direct sense but because losing the Dark Lantern's neutrality would damage the social fabric of everything here.

### Kael

**Role:** Manager of the Free Clank Cooperative  
**Ancestry/Age:** Clank, appears mid-30s (actual construction date: 42 years ago; service term completed 8 years ago)  
**Location:** The Free Clank Cooperative, Maze Quarter western edge. Present most days and many nights.

**Appearance:** Kael's construction is clearly Luminarium-origin — the standard proportions and materials the Luminarium's program has used for eighty years, with the visible joint work and the slight luminescence at the eyes that Lumencrest Clanks share. Their grey-and-blue service robe is gone — they wear practical workshop clothing in dark colors. One hand has been repaired with different components than the original, visible in the slight color difference at the wrist. They did the repair themselves.

**Personality:** Measured and precise in the way of someone who was built with good reasoning capacity and has spent eight years deciding what to do with it. Not cold — there is warmth in how Kael interacts with Clanks who come to the Cooperative, a genuine quality of understanding that doesn't need to be performed. Cautious with humans and other organic people until they've proven their intentions, which is a posture that has served them well and which they're not inclined to change.

**What they want:** For the Cooperative to continue functioning and to keep being what it is — a genuine safe point for Clanks who need one. The Luminarium created Kael to perform labor. Kael has decided that this, the Cooperative, is what they will perform instead. The specificity of this decision is something they think about and don't often discuss.

**What they know:** The current status of approximately 200 Clanks who have passed through the Cooperative in the past six years — who reached safety, who didn't, who is still in transit. The Free Hand's operational patterns at the Shadowside level (Kael is not part of the Free Hand in any formal sense; the Free Hand has no formal sense; Kael helps where they can and coordinates where the interests overlap). The mechanics of Lumencrest Clank construction better than anyone outside the Luminarium's current research programs, because Kael has been living in a Clank body for 42 years and repairing other Clanks for six.

**Working with them:** Kael will be professionally neutral with strangers — the Cooperative's official face is a repair shop, and that's what they present. Bringing genuine usefulness to the Cooperative — a referral, a resource, demonstrated reliability over time — opens the back layer of what Kael can do. A character who is a Clank, escaped or free, is given considerably more immediate trust. Not unlimited trust — Kael has learned that trust with no basis is a liability — but a head start.

### Brother Carwell

**Role:** Lead priest of the Salvation Hearth  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 61  
**Location:** The Salvation Hearth, Shadowside eastern edge. Effectively always present.

**Appearance:** Tall, thin in the way of someone who has given away more food than they've kept over the years, with a close-trimmed white beard and hands that are always doing something — folding, sorting, carrying, helping. Wears the Unified Ember's simple grey robe without modification. Has a quality of stillness that coexists with constant activity — he moves and works continuously and yet gives the impression of someone who is never in a hurry.

**Personality:** Genuinely kind without being soft. He has heard every possible life situation walk through the Salvation Hearth's door and has responded to all of them with the same practical help rather than the judgment that would be easier. He is not without opinions — he has very specific views on the Council's treatment of Shadowside and on the Clank service system — but he keeps them out of the Hearth's services on the principle that people in need shouldn't have to earn help by agreeing with anyone.

**What he wants:** For the Hearth to keep doing what it does. He has been here for twenty-two years and has watched Shadowside get poorer as the city's power crisis has deepened — indirectly, through the rationing decisions that prioritize factory machinery over worker housing. He is angry about this in a contained way that he manages through work.

**What he knows:** More about Shadowside's ground-level situation than any city official and most people in the district's own network — the Hearth sees who is in genuine crisis and why, which is a different dataset from what the Night Market sees or the Dark Lantern hears. He does not share this information. He will, however, tell players when something they're doing has been helpful to people he cares about, and this signal is worth listening to.

**Sister Avri** _(associated NPC):_ The Hearth's medical practitioner. Human, early 40s, training in both healing magic and conventional medicine. Works the Hearth's medical space every morning and refers cases beyond her capacity to Vael at the Stitch, with whom she has a quiet working relationship. She is less guarded than Brother Carwell and more likely to give a direct answer about what she knows, particularly about medical situations. She has a deep skepticism about the Luminarium's approach to Clank physiology that she's happy to discuss at length.

### Lenne Cross

**Role:** Proprietor of the Border Bar, Hollow Road Crossing  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 39  
**Location:** The Border Bar, Hollow Road at the Lamplight Ward/Shadowside line

**Appearance:** Medium height, red-haired, with the particular quality of someone who is always tracking several things simultaneously without appearing to track any of them. Dresses better than the Shadowside average — good quality working clothes, not ostentatious — as a calculated signal to the Lamplight Ward clientele that they've crossed into somewhere with standards.

**Personality:** Social intelligence as a professional skill. Lenne's bar exists because of the border, and Lenne's success exists because she understands what each side of the border wants and provides the version of the other that they're looking for. Lamplight Ward residents get a controlled Shadowside experience — interesting, slightly edgy, safe enough. Shadowside residents get a bar where they can drink without being the subject of anyone's tourism. She manages this balance constantly and without appearing to manage it.

**What she wants:** The bar to keep running, which requires both sides of the border remaining distinct enough that a border bar has a reason to exist. She is, therefore, quietly invested in Shadowside remaining Shadowside — not its poverty, which she doesn't romanticize, but its character and self-sufficiency. Gentrification pressure from the Lamplight Ward side is something she watches with concern.

**What she knows:** The border traffic — who crosses from Ward to Shadowside and back, which Shadowside residents have connections in the Ward, what the Ward's current attitude toward Shadowside is at street level rather than Council level. She is an excellent source for the social and political texture of both districts, if players establish a reason for her to talk to them. She charges for this in the social currency of being interesting to talk to, which is a higher bar than it sounds.

### Mama Voss

**Role:** Operator of Mama Voss's Cart, Night Market thoroughfare  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 58  
**Location:** The cart, day and night market thoroughfare. She takes one night off per week — which night varies and she tells no one in advance.

**Appearance:** Short and broad, with grey-streaked black hair pinned back from her face for cooking. Permanent coal-smoke residue on her hands and forearms. The apron she wears has been repaired so many times it is now more repair than original fabric. She works with the efficiency of someone who has performed the same physical motions for twenty years and doesn't need to think about them.

**Personality:** Brusque in the middle of service — she has things to cook and people to feed and the line is long — and warm when she isn't. The warmth is real. She has a dry, flat sense of humor that she deploys when a customer says something that genuinely amuses her, which happens less often than she'd like. She is not easily impressed but is easily made to like someone, which requires only treating her food with appropriate respect and treating the people in line around you like human beings.

**What she knows:** Twenty-three years on the same stretch of thoroughfare means Mama Voss has watched the Night Market evolve and knows its rhythms better than most. She notices who's new, who's nervous, who's meeting who they claim not to know. She doesn't sell this information and isn't an information broker. She will, if she likes someone, mention something she noticed in the way of a person remarking on the weather — not hidden, just said. This is worth more than it costs.

**What she offers beyond food:** She is one of the better resources in Shadowside for answering the question "who do I talk to about X" — not the specific contacts (she doesn't traffic in those), but the direction. Which part of the market, which kind of person, which time of night. She knows the landscape.

### Orin Marsh

**Role:** Proprietor of the Lampblack  
**Ancestry/Age:** Half-elf, 52  
**Location:** The Lampblack. He lives above it, which means he's always within earshot.

**Appearance:** Neat in a way that stands out in Shadowside — clean clothing, maintained. Not formal, not expensive, just tended. He is angular and precise-looking, with light hair going silver at the temples and the particular half-elven quality of appearing somewhat younger than the number would suggest. Has the posture of someone who decided what they stood for and arranged themselves around it.

**Personality:** The Lampblack is his project and his principles. He runs it with specific rules — no deals made in the main room, no city watch, no violence, and the food is what it is and not apologized for — and maintains them without drama. He is not warm in the social performance sense. He is fair in the operational sense, which in Shadowside is considerably rarer and more valuable. He treats every customer the same on first contact and adjusts based on what they show him.

**What he wants:** A functional bar that doesn't attract trouble. He had trouble once, early in his tenure — an incident he won't discuss that left a mark on the building (patched, but visible if you know what you're looking at) and reorganized his approach to what he allows through the door. He is considerably harder to surprise than he was before that incident.

**What he knows:** The working population of Shadowside who drink in his bar — which is a different slice than the Dark Lantern's network-and-deals clientele. The Lampblack is where the district's actual laborers go. The people who work the factories across in the Manufactories and live here for the rent, the Night Market traders who need somewhere to decompress after the market closes, the long-term residents who want a drink without an agenda. Their concerns, their knowledge, and their current mood are things Orin tracks because they tell him what Shadowside is actually experiencing as opposed to what its visible network is doing.

### Sable Mors

**Role:** Proprietor of Threadbare  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 46  
**Location:** Threadbare, main thoroughfare, Shadowside. Daytime operation only.

**Appearance:** Practical in every particular — hair pulled back, clothing that demonstrates what the shop sells (well-maintained, neutral, functional), hands that are always moving between items on display. Has a measuring eye that she turns on customers in the first thirty seconds of any interaction, which is partly professional assessment of size and partly professional assessment of the person.

**Personality:** Direct and fair, with a sense of humor she reveals to people she's decided are worth it. She has a gift for immediate accurate assessment of what someone needs — not just what they asked for, but what the situation they're walking into requires. This makes her useful in ways that go beyond clothing. She has been asked for advice about more than wardrobe by enough people that she's stopped being surprised when it happens.

**What she knows:** Who needs to look different and why, which is a significant slice of Shadowside's passing population. She doesn't store this information in any organized way — it's not what she's here for — but she notices patterns. Someone buying a specific combination of clothing, in specific colors, while looking over their shoulder in a specific direction, tells her something. She doesn't share observations unless she thinks it serves a purpose she endorses.

**What she can source:** Through the Night Market and specific contacts, Sable can acquire things that aren't on the shelf within two to three days. Her network skews toward practical items rather than contraband — she's not a fence, she's a supplier. But her contacts overlap with the Night Market's supply chain in ways that make "practical items" a flexible category.

### Renn Aldous

**Role:** Proprietor of the Smoke Den  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 44  
**Location:** The Smoke Den, Maze Quarter basement. Present whenever the Den is open, which is from mid-afternoon until deep into the night.

**Appearance:** The smoke has settled into him — the smell of it, the slightly dimmed quality of his eyes in full daylight, the way he moves with the economy of someone who has spent years in low-ceilinged rooms. Medium height, brown-haired, with hands that are stained in ways that are not ink. Dresses in dark colors that don't show the smoke residue.

**Personality:** Calm with the specific quality of someone who has been around his products long enough to have a relationship with them. Not a user in the way that would compromise his operation — he is functional and sharp — but familiar. He does not moralize about what he sells. He does not advise. He provides what is asked for, at the stated price, with the stated rules. He is even-tempered under pressure in a way that has been tested and held.

**What he wants:** For the Den to keep running quietly. He has a good arrangement with the right people in the district — a contribution to the Night Market's informal protection fund, a contribution to someone adjacent to the city watch presence in the area — and he maintains it through consistency. He does not expand, does not get greedy, does not bring in products that would change the heat on his operation. The Dreamsmoke is the edge of what he'll carry and he knows it.

**What he knows:** Who uses his establishment and what they're using, which is a particular kind of knowledge about the Underground's population — their vulnerabilities, their needs, the states in which they're least guarded. He does not sell this. He has never sold this. It is, however, information that exists and that he is sitting on, and players who are looking for someone specific and have exhausted other channels might find it worth asking whether that person has been in.

### Joss Wren

**Role:** Contact point for the Quiet Market  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 35  
**Location:** The northern end of the Night Market thoroughfare, near the Maze Quarter mouth, on nights when the Quiet Market is being arranged (irregular, 1-2 times per week)

**Appearance:** Deliberate in his unremarkability — average height, average build, clothing chosen to be neither memorable nor suspicious. He does not look like someone who does what he does, which is the job requirement. The only consistent detail is a coin he turns over in his right hand when he's waiting, visible to those who know to look for it.

**Personality:** Professional in the way of someone who understands that his role is access, not relationship. He is pleasant without being warm, efficient without being cold. He does not waste words on social performance. He asks what he needs to know, tells what he needs to tell, and concludes conversations when they're concluded.

**What he does:** Joss vets Quiet Market inquiries. His job is to determine whether someone seeking access is safe enough to bring in — not city watch, not a liability, not someone who will cause a problem that collapses the Market's current location arrangement. He is good at this. He has been wrong twice in three years. Both times, the problem was caught early because he had also ensured there was someone watching the first-timers.

**What he knows:** The schedule of the Quiet Market, the current location (within 24 hours of each meeting), and the inventory categories that the Market's regulars are most interested in. He does not know the Quiet Market's suppliers, the identities of its primary organizers, or the specific contents of any given meeting's inventory. This is deliberate — he cannot tell what he doesn't know.

## Shadowside Faction Presence

**The Free Hand** — active throughout the district, most concentrated at the Free Clank Cooperative. Shadowside is the Free Hand's primary above-ground operational zone. Reputation with the Free Hand provides social access throughout the district.

**The Silver Thread** — present via the Shadowside house (detailed in [[09_The_Underground_Expanded]]). Calla's intelligence network is active here; the Silver Thread knows the Maze Quarter's social map.

**The Night Market's informal authority** — not a faction in the [[Factions_and_Reputation]] sense, but a social structure worth treating as one at the table. The Night Market's regular traders have collective interests and enforce them collectively (no violence on the thoroughfare being the primary one). Violating these norms is not a faction conflict — it's a social exile that affects every interaction in the district.

**City Watch (minimal presence)** — two officers on the main thoroughfare, a rotation past Pauper's Gate. They are not looking hard. They are, however, looking. The Maze Quarter is effectively unpatrolled.

**The Conservators** — present through the intelligence monitoring operation. There is a watch contact somewhere in Shadowside, as noted in the Underground document. Their identity is not established here; GMs place it where it serves the campaign.

## Shadowside Reputation Track

Shadowside reputation operates separately from the Underground track and separately from any specific faction, though it interacts with both.

**Starting position:** 0 (Stranger). Players are unknown — not hostile, just unread.

**Gaining standing:**

- Regular custom at a Shadowside establishment for a week or more: +1
- Visible help to the district (resolving a problem that affected residents, not just players): +1
- Being vouched for by Solt, Brother Carwell, Mama Voss, or Kael: +1
- Surviving and navigating the Maze Quarter without causing problems: +1
- Demonstrated protection of the Night Market's operation (warning of a watch sweep, preventing violence on the thoroughfare): +2

**Losing standing:**

- Bringing city watch attention to a specific establishment: -2
- Violence in a neutral space: -2
- Treating the district as scenery or its residents as obstacles: -1 (slow burn — this is the one that takes time to develop but is hard to reverse)
- Exploiting someone clearly vulnerable in the district: -3

**Reputation unlocks:**

|Standing|What Opens Up|
|---|---|
|+1 (Noticed)|Regulars at the Dark Lantern and Lampblack acknowledge you; Mama Voss will tell you which direction to go|
|+2 (Known)|Solt will relay a message; Sable can source items; Joss will consider a Quiet Market inquiry; the Maze Quarter stops being actively hostile to navigate|
|+3 (Trusted)|Kael provides Cooperative resources; Brother Carwell advises; Maze Quarter residents will actively help in a genuine crisis; the district treats you as one of its own in a specific and meaningful way|
