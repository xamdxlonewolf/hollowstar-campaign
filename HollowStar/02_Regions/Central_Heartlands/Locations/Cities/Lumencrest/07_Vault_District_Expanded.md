# The Vault District — Expanded District Guide

## Points of Interest, Personnel, and Access

_This document expands on the Vault District as established in [[07_Vault_District]], adding the locations, personnel, access mechanics, and physical detail needed to run the district at the table. The Vault District is the campaign's final layer — the place where everything else in Lumencrest ultimately points. It is not a district in the ordinary sense; it does not have taverns or markets or community spaces. It has a wall, a gate, personnel who live inside both literally and professionally, and beneath it, the Heart. Cross-reference [[01_Radiant_Core_Expanded]] for Sera Coldwell's full profile, [[06_Academic_Quarter_Expanded]] for the research connection to the underground complex, [[08_The_Underground_Expanded]] for the tunnel network beneath the city, and [[Controlled_Substances_Trade]] for the Hollostar Dust supply chain and the Shard Division's tunnel activity that Coldwell has been tracking._

---

## Running the Vault District at the Table

The Vault District should feel like a different register from every other location in Lumencrest. Where other districts have texture and noise and the productive chaos of people going about their lives, the Vault District has precision. Everything here is exactly where it is supposed to be. The guard rotations are exact. The lighting is brighter than anywhere else in the city and has no variation, no amber warmth, just clear white operational light that serves surveillance rather than ambiance. The silence inside the walls — described by the handful of researchers who have legitimate access — is the silence of an environment that has been designed against sound that shouldn't be there.

**What it communicates:** The Vault District is the city's most honest location. Everywhere else in Lumencrest something is being managed — the political messaging of the Core, the commercial optimism of Lamplight Ward, the manufactured equilibrium of the Night Market. The Vault District manages nothing except access. It says, through every physical detail, that what is inside is important enough to justify all of this, and that you are not inside, and that this is the correct situation.

**The emotional weight:** Players who have spent time in Shadowside knowing the brownouts happen because the power goes somewhere else — somewhere like this — and who then stand outside the Vault District's wall and look at the lights burning at full brightness above the iron spikes — that moment should land. The wall is the most visible single symbol of the city's political reality. Everything beneath it is why.

**The Heart:** The Heart is not a monster or a mechanism in the dramatic sense. It is a Hollostar — a living thing of a kind that produces energy, that has been producing energy for 1,200 years, and that is dying. It has been dying for thirty years. It will be dead in approximately 14 months if Petra Vane's analysis is correct. What it has been while dying — what it has been experiencing, if experience is the right word, what the long-term Hollostar Dust users' perceptions of its condition reflect — is something the campaign's story will answer. The Heart is not the villain. It is not a resource. It is something that the city has been consuming for twelve centuries and that is running out, and everyone in Lumencrest is about to discover what happens next.

## The Outer Perimeter

**What players can access without authorization:** The streets immediately surrounding the outer wall. These streets are public — the wall is a city structure and the surrounding pavement belongs to the city — but the Vault Guard's outer patrol circuit means that anyone lingering more than a few minutes will be approached politely and asked their business. There is no legal basis for detaining someone who is simply walking the perimeter. The Guard does not rely on legal basis; they rely on social pressure and the obvious fact of eight feet of stone behind them. Most people move along without needing to be asked twice.

**The outer wall:** Eight feet of solid stone, maintained meticulously — no moss, no weathering visible despite the age of the original construction. Iron spikes along the top. Electric lighting mounted at the wall's top edge, running brighter than the Radiant Core's street lamps, throwing clean white light down onto both the interior and the exterior pavement in a way that eliminates shadows at the base of the wall. Guard stations at each corner and at the midpoint of each wall segment — four corners, four midpoints, eight stations, each with two guards on permanent rotation.

The wall is older than the current building inside it. The original construction dates to the district's establishment, when Lumencrest's founders decided that the Heart's location required a permanent defined perimeter. Every subsequent generation has maintained and reinforced it. The current reinforcement layer — added approximately 40 years ago during the early awareness of the Heart's decline — includes arcane monitoring woven into the stonework that detects unauthorized approach from inside the tunnel network and passive energy monitoring that tracks any unusual Hollostar-derived activity near the wall's base.

**The Vault Gate building:** The single authorized entrance to the Vault District. The building is exactly what the base file describes — low, heavy, wrong-looking in the way of something that was engineered for a purpose rather than built for appearance. The reinforced door is the only architectural element that acknowledges what it's for; everything else about the building is deliberately undemonstrative. The guards at the gate stand at measured intervals and conduct every authorization verification in the same sequence, every time, without variation. The sequence takes approximately eight minutes for a known authorized person, twenty minutes for someone undergoing first-time entry processing.

**The patrol circuit:** Thirty-minute outer rotation. Two guards, moving in opposite directions, meeting at the midpoints. The timing is exact. Any player character observing the outer perimeter for a full hour will establish the rotation precisely, which tells them the window — not that the window is useful without solving the wall itself.

## The Legitimate Access Path — Full Detail

_(The base file summarizes this; the full mechanics are here for GM use)_

**Step 1 — Formal Request:** Filed at the Council District's Research Board office. The form requires stated purpose, credentials, and the name of the Council member co-signing. Purpose must be specific — "research interest" is insufficient; "research into the Network's secondary distribution infrastructure in support of Program 7's technical specifications" is the level of specificity required. The form is reviewed by the Board's administrative staff first, then escalated to the Board members for approval. Timeline: 5-10 days.

**Step 2 — Background Review:** Conducted by the Vault Guard's intelligence section. Covers known associations, any criminal record, any prior unauthorized access attempts, and any political affiliations that might represent a security concern. The review's practical scope is Lumencrest and the immediately connected regions — they cannot fully verify history from distant cities, which is a gap that sophisticated visitors with documented identities from outside the Accord have occasionally exploited. Timeline: 3-7 days, running concurrently with Step 1.

**Step 3 — Council Member Co-signature:** One Council member of any faction must formally co-sign the access request. This is the most significant practical barrier for players without Core connections — it requires either an existing relationship with a Councilor or a sufficient reputation in the Core's social layer that a Councilor will sign. Mira Ashton will sign for researchers she believes are working on genuine Heart-related problems. Theron Ashwick will sign for people who have given him reason to. Sevan Vael has signed two access requests in his current Council term, both unexplained.

**Step 4 — The Oath:** Administered by Sera Coldwell or her designated deputy. The oath is an actual oath — a formal arcane-witnessed commitment that the authorized individual will not share classified information obtained inside the Vault District without Council authorization. The arcane witness component is real: oath-breaking produces a specific and detectable arcane signature that the Vault Guard's monitoring system will identify. The oath does not prevent an individual from sharing what they learn; it creates a detectable trail when they do. Coldwell administers it herself for any individual she has concerns about.

**Step 5 — Escort:** A Vault Guard officer of Sergeant rank or above is assigned as escort for the duration of the visit. The escort does not leave the authorized visitor's side. The escort is armed. The escort has standing orders regarding what happens if an authorized visitor attempts to access locations not included in their authorization scope.

**The underground complex — additional authorization:** The above grants access to the outer administrative areas only. The underground complex — the tunnels, the monitoring stations, and the Heart chamber itself — requires a separate authorization granted by full Council vote. Currently held by four Council members (Mira Ashton, one Conservator senior, one Expansionist junior who received it for reasons the other factions questioned, and Sevan Vael — whose authorization nobody requested and nobody has explained). The Illuminist research team leads have a restricted access that allows them to the third monitoring level but not the Heart chamber directly.

## Inside the Walls — Authorized Area

**What the outer administrative area contains:** The surface-level Vault District inside the walls is not large — approximately two city blocks of buildings that serve the personnel who work here rather than any public function. Everything is functional, maintained to the same precise standard as the outer perimeter, and organized around the assumption that the people using it are working rather than living in any full sense.

---

### The Guard Barracks and Operations Center

**What it is:** The Vault Guard's primary operational base — a two-story building that contains sleeping quarters for the guard rotation currently on duty, the operations room where the monitoring systems are watched, the briefing rooms where shift handoffs happen, and Sera Coldwell's office. The most secure building in Lumencrest that isn't underground.

**The operations room:** The nerve center of the Vault District's security. Banks of monitoring equipment — arcane sensors tracking tunnel activity beneath the city, Network energy monitors showing real-time output from the Heart, the communication system that connects the surface operations to the underground monitoring stations. Three Vault Guard officers on duty at all times, rotating every four hours. The room never empties.

**What the operations room monitors:**

- Network energy output from the Heart, updated continuously. The graph on the primary display shows the output trend over the past 90 days. The slope of the line is visible to anyone in the room. It has been going in one direction for thirty years and has not changed.
- Arcane motion sensors covering the tunnel network beneath the city — a map display showing the monitored sections, with alert indicators for unusual activity. The sections shown as monitored are not all the sections that exist. The gap — the pre-city passages beneath the Maze Quarter — is visible as an unmapped region on the display to anyone who knows enough about the tunnel network to read the map correctly.
- The underground complex's access logs — who entered each level, at what time, how long they stayed, what monitoring anomalies were recorded during their presence.

**Sera Coldwell's office:** Adjacent to the operations room. A working space that contains no personal effects except a single framed document on the wall — the Vault Guard's founding charter, 400 years old, behind glass. Her desk has three active files at any given time. Players who access this office will find the current three files are: the tunnel activity anomaly report she has been building for six months; the access log for the one unauthorized Vault entry in her tenure; and a preliminary threat assessment on the Shard Division that does not yet have the Shard Division's name on it.

---

### The Technical Administration Building

**What it is:** The surface-level workspace for the engineers and senior technicians who maintain the underground complex's monitoring systems and the Heart's immediate infrastructure. Includes laboratory space for analysis of data from the underground sensors, a technical library of Network engineering documentation, and the coordination office for the Illuminist research team's surface-level operations.

**The research team coordination office:** The surface workspace for the Illuminist research team members who have Vault access. Currently occupied most days by **Senior Researcher Aldric Vane** (no relation to Petra Vane — the name is a coincidence that has been noted in the research community) and two junior researchers. The coordination office receives data feeds from the underground monitoring stations and compiles the reports that go to the Research Board and, through the Board, to the Council.

**What those reports contain:** The official output data, formatted to support the Council's 3-5 year timeline assessment. The raw data, which Aldric Vane processes before it reaches the reports, produces a different picture. He has been processing it for eight months. He is aware of what it shows. He has not reported it because the reporting would require acknowledging that the processed data and the raw data diverge — which is a professional risk he has been managing rather than addressing. He is one of three people in Lumencrest who have access to the raw Heart output data. The other two are Sera Coldwell (who has the monitoring logs but not the analytical capacity to interpret the divergence) and, now, Petra Vane (who arrived at the same conclusion from the Academic Quarter side without access to the raw data).

**Senior Researcher Aldric Vane** — see NPCs.

### The Personnel Quarters

**What it is:** Residential accommodation for the Vault District's permanent personnel — the guards and technical staff who are assigned to the district for extended rotations. Spare, functional, quiet. The forty or so people who live here full-time during their rotations have developed the specific community of people who share a highly constrained environment with a significant shared purpose — tight in the way of ship crews or expedition teams, with the social dynamics that produces.

**The community the players will never see:** The Vault District's permanent personnel are not accessible to outsiders and know things that are not accessible through any official channel. The maintenance engineers who work the underground complex's deepest accessible sections have been hearing something for three months — a quality of resonance in the Heart chamber's lower levels that the monitoring equipment doesn't capture and that they discuss among themselves and have not reported because they don't know how to describe it in technical language. The guards who have been on extended rotation develop relationships with the underground complex that are not professional — they know its sounds, its temperature variations, its specific quality of silence. None of this is documented. All of it is potentially relevant.

## The Underground Complex

**Access:** The underground complex is reached through the Vault Gate building's interior staircase — a descent of approximately sixty feet through reinforced tunnels to the first monitoring level. The complex has four levels, each requiring additional authorization above the surface access level:

**Level One — Outer Monitoring:** The first underground level, accessible to the full Illuminist research team. Contains monitoring stations for the Network's primary distribution infrastructure, sensor arrays tracking the Heart's surface-level energy output, and the communications relay that connects the deeper levels to the surface operations room. The level smells of stone and the specific warm-metal smell of functioning electrical infrastructure, undercut by something else — something that doesn't have a name that people in the upper levels of the city would recognize. The Vault Guard officers on rotation at Level One describe it as the smell of age.

**Level Two — Secondary Monitoring:** Accessible to research team leads and Vault Guard officers of Lieutenant rank or above. The monitoring becomes more specific here — individual sensor arrays tracking specific aspects of the Heart's output rather than aggregate readings. The temperature is lower. The smell changes — the warm-metal component recedes and the other smell becomes more present. Research team members who have spent extended time at Level Two describe a quality of awareness that they attribute to the proximity of something significant.

**Level Three — Proximate Monitoring:** Accessible to Council-authorized individuals only. The four Council members, the Vault Guard command staff, and Sera Coldwell specifically. Level Three is the closest authorized access point to the Heart — approximately 200 feet from the Heart chamber in a direct line, separated by reinforced infrastructure and the final access barrier. The monitoring equipment here reads the Heart's output directly rather than through the distribution network. What those readings show is what the raw data in Aldric Vane's files shows — and it is not what the reports sent to the Council show.

Players who reach Level Three and read the monitoring displays will understand, without being told, that the city has approximately 14 months. The display does not require interpretation. The slope of the line is unmistakable.

**Level Four — The Heart Chamber:** Not on any authorization list except Sera Coldwell's personal access, which she has used exactly once in her forty-year career. The Heart chamber is the physical location of the Hollostar — a space that was carved around it when the city was founded and that has not been substantially entered since. The monitoring is passive; sensors embedded in the chamber walls read the Heart's output through the stone without requiring anyone to be present.

What the Heart chamber actually is — its physical dimensions, the appearance of the Hollostar itself, the quality of the space around something that has been producing the energy of a city for 1,200 years and is dying — is described below, for the GM, for the moment the players reach it.

### The Heart Chamber — GM Description

_This section is for GM use only. It should be read before the players reach the chamber and used to set the scene at the table._

**The approach:** The passage from Level Three to the Heart chamber is not a dramatic architectural space. It is a maintenance corridor — stone, functional lighting, the kind of space built to be serviceable rather than significant. The only indication that it leads somewhere different is the temperature drop — sustained, consistent, colder than Level Three's already-cool air — and the sound, which begins approximately fifty feet from the chamber entrance. Not loud. Not identifiable as anything familiar. A resonance that the body detects before the ears do, felt in the chest rather than heard. Players who have been using Hollostar Dust and have the Resonant condition or the Grief condition will experience this differently — for them, the resonance is the Network's signal at its source, and it is not diminished here. It is the original, from which everything else derived, and it is fading.

**The chamber:** Larger than expected — forty feet across, the ceiling lost in darkness above the lights that were installed at the chamber's edge when the monitoring equipment was placed here. The floor is original stone, unworn because almost nobody has stood on it. The walls are original stone, marked at approximately shoulder height by a faint line — the accumulation of centuries of the Heart's energy output, a physical record of its presence embedded in the stone.

**The Heart:** In the chamber's center, the Hollostar.

It is approximately eight feet across at its widest point — smaller than the descriptions in the theoretical literature suggested it would be, which is something that affects everyone who sees it for the first time. It has no base, no mounting, no visible means of support; it simply is present, resting in a depression in the chamber floor that the stone formed around it naturally over twelve centuries of proximity to something that is not quite a physical object in the ordinary sense.

Its surface is crystalline — the same structure visible in Hollostar shard fragments, the same structure ground to powder in Hollostar Dust, but at a scale that makes the comparison feel wrong. The crystal structure catches the monitoring equipment's light and holds it, distributes it internally, produces the faint luminescence that makes the chamber feel lit even without the edge-mounted fixtures. In high output periods, twelve centuries ago, the chamber would have been bright enough to work in without additional lighting. Now, the luminescence is what it is — real, present, insufficient. The Heart is not dark. It is not what it was.

Its surface moves. Not mechanically — not in any way that produces a sound or a visible cycle. But over the course of minutes, watching, the crystal structure's internal light shifts. There is a periodicity to it. The period is approximately 14 months. This is what Petra Vane identified in the data and what Neva Strand modeled theoretically and what neither of them has seen directly. Seeing it directly is different from knowing it abstractly.

**What players feel in the chamber:**

_Anyone:_ The resonance is physical at this proximity — not painful, not overwhelming, but present in a way that makes the body aware of itself. Breathing feels deliberate. The air is the coldest point in the complex but does not feel cold in the way outdoor cold does; it feels like proximity to something that has different thermal properties than the environment around it.

_Players with Hollostar Dust dependency (Grief condition, any tier):_ The Grief resolves. Completely, immediately, without chemical assistance — in the Heart's direct presence, the ache that has been present since the dependency developed is absent. This is not relief. It is clarity. They are next to the thing they have been feeling the absence of, and it is here, and it is dying, and the understanding of those two facts arriving simultaneously is not comfortable. The GM should describe this privately, in a note or a quiet word, rather than announcing it at the table.

_Players with the Transformed condition (Hollostar Dust ability table):_ Whatever ability they developed — Network Sense, Shard Sight, Signal, any of the ten — activates fully, without the drug, at a level beyond what the drug produces. This is the source. For the duration of their time in the chamber, the ability functions at maximum capacity without any dependency impairment. What they perceive through it is the Heart's actual state: not the monitoring equipment's interpretation, not the research teams' models, but the direct experience of what is happening here. The GM determines what they see. It should be accurate. It should be significant. It should answer something they've been trying to understand and open something they didn't know to ask.

_Players with the Echo ability (result 10 on the ability table):_ Touching the floor or wall of the chamber triggers the Echo at full power. What they see is not the past 48 hours — the Heart's memories are longer than that. The GM may show them anything from the Heart's 1,200-year history that serves the campaign. This should be used once, carefully, for a scene that matters.

## The Tunnel Access — Unauthorized Routes

**What exists:** The pre-city passages beneath the Maze Quarter contain access points to the Vault District's underground complex that predate the Vault District itself — carved before the city, before the wall, before the authorization system. These passages are known to the Vault Guard in the sense that they know passages exist; they are not fully mapped because the survey work to map them completely would require months of underground work that has been repeatedly deprioritized against other resource demands.

**What the Vault Guard's operations room map shows:** The monitored sections of the underground complex's perimeter sensors cover approximately 85% of the pre-city passage network. The 15% gap is not random — it corresponds to the oldest, deepest sections beneath the Maze Quarter, carved in a different period than the sections the survey teams have assessed. The gap is visible on the map as an unmapped region. Players who see the map and who know the Maze Quarter's tunnel layout from the Underground documents will recognize the correlation.

**The access point:** Approximately 600 feet of pre-city passage, navigating from the Dry Chambers' deep connection through the oldest sections, ending at a stone door that is original construction — 1,200 years old, predating the lock technology the Vault Guard uses, sealed by an arcane mechanism that is a different type than anything in the current monitoring system. This mechanism is not monitored because it is not on the current system's spectrum. Harrow knows this passage exists and does not speak of it. The Remover knows approximately where it is and has never approached it. Sable (the second one, the mirrorpetal harvester) has been through it once. None of these individuals have disclosed this to any other party.

**What lies beyond the door:** Level One of the underground complex — not the administrative surface areas, but the first underground monitoring level, emerging from a maintenance alcove that appears on the original construction documents (in the Academic Quarter's Archives, in the section that requires the maintenance passage to access) and does not appear on any current floor plan.

**The GM's decision:** This route should be the campaign's most significant physical challenge — not a dungeon crawl, but a real navigation problem through the deepest Underground with real stakes. Getting through requires the tunnel knowledge (Harrow's connection, or the construction documents), the ability to open the arcane seal (which is the same mechanism type as the Hollostar Dust activation process — Tern Ashe's research applies, and this is not a coincidence the GM should undersell), and a plan for what happens once inside that accounts for everything that has been established about the Vault Guard's monitoring capability and the personnel rotation.

### Sera Coldwell

_(Full profile in [[15_Radiant_Core_Expanded]] — Vault District-specific additions here)_

**Her relationship with the district:** She has been here for forty years and the Vault District has been her entire professional identity for twenty of those years. She does not live in the personnel quarters — she has a residence in the Radiant Core — but she is in the operations room most mornings by 6am and most evenings until 10pm. The Guard officers who have been on extended rotation with her describe a quality of presence that is both consistent and wearing: she is always there, always watching, always aware of exactly what the monitoring displays show. They find this reassuring and occasionally exhausting.

**The one unauthorized access:** Sera Coldwell granted one unauthorized access to the Vault District in her career — specifically to the Heart chamber, Level Four, which she has entered only twice herself. The access was granted eleven years ago. The recipient was a long-term Hollostar Dust user whose perceptual abilities had reached a stage that Sera assessed, after a private consultation she will not document, as providing information about the Heart's condition that the monitoring equipment wasn't capturing. What that person saw in the Heart chamber, and what they told Sera, is the information she has been sitting with for eleven years. It is consistent with Petra Vane's finding. It is consistent with the raw data in Aldric Vane's files. It is what informed her decision, eight years ago, to expand the Vault Guard's tunnel monitoring without requesting Council authorization for the expansion, on the basis that she did not want the Council to know how close she was to knowing what she wasn't supposed to know.

**What she is waiting for:** Enough. She has been building a file for six months on the tunnel activity anomaly. She has the unauthorized access logs from the pre-city passages that the Guild's Dust supply and the Shard Division's subject transport share. She does not yet have a name for the Shard Division. She is three pieces of evidence away from bringing it to the full Council rather than the faction chairs, because she has assessed that the faction chairs cannot be trusted with this individually. Players who bring her those three pieces will find her move faster and more decisively than any other actor in the city, because she has been ready for eleven years.

### Senior Researcher Aldric Vane

**Role:** Illuminist research team lead, surface coordination; the person processing the raw Heart data **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 46 **Location:** Technical Administration Building, Vault District. The Academic Quarter's Faculty Housing, most nights — he commutes.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone doing something he knows is wrong and who has been doing it long enough that the wrongness has become a sustained low-level condition rather than an acute crisis. Well-maintained professionally — the Vault District requires it — with the quality of someone who is very precise about his work and less precise about his decisions.

**Personality:** Competent, anxious, and committed to a professional self-image that his current behavior is steadily undermining. He began processing the raw data eight months ago because he believed the divergence was a sensor calibration issue — a correctable error that he would fix and report. After eight months of attempting to find the calibration error, he has accepted that there is no calibration error. He has not accepted what this means about what he should do. He is in the space between understanding and acting that is the most dangerous place a person can be in a crisis.

**What he knows:** The raw Heart output data for the past three years, unprocessed, which shows the actual decline curve and the secondary periodicity that Petra Vane identified from the Academic Quarter side. He also knows that the reports he has been sending to the Research Board do not reflect this data. He knows what this means professionally and has not allowed himself to think past that to what it means for the city.

**What moves him:** He is not a bad person. He is a person who made a decision that seemed manageable and discovered that it wasn't, and who has been waiting for something external to change the situation so he doesn't have to change it himself. Players who bring him evidence that the situation has already changed — that Petra Vane's analysis exists independently, that Neva Strand's public lecture has already put the convergent finding into the public record — will find that the external change he was waiting for has arrived and that he responds to it with the specific relief of someone who no longer has to hold something alone.

### Deputy Commander Renn Ashford

**Role:** Vault Guard Deputy Commander; Sera Coldwell's operational right hand; the person who runs the District when she is in Council briefings **Ancestry/Age:** Human, 38 **Location:** Operations room, Vault District. Anywhere Coldwell sends him.

**Appearance:** The appearance of someone fifteen years into a career that he is very good at and that he chose deliberately — fit, precise, with the Vault Guard's characteristic quality of total environmental awareness. Younger than his position suggests, which reflects the specific quality of trust Coldwell places in him rather than his years.

**Personality:** Loyal to Coldwell in the specific way of someone who decided early that loyalty to a person with genuine integrity is the most reliable professional foundation available. He is not blindly obedient — he pushes back when he disagrees — but he has concluded, after seven years working with her, that her judgment is sound and that the cases where his assessment diverges from hers have more often been resolved in her favor than his. This produces a deputy who is an extension of Coldwell's thinking rather than a supplement to it, which is efficient and occasionally limits the operations room's ability to consider possibilities she hasn't.

**His specific value to players:** He is the contact within the Vault District who is most likely to be reachable before Coldwell is available. Getting information to Coldwell quickly, or getting her to move on something before the standard briefing schedule allows, requires going through Ashford. He will assess players the way Coldwell would assess them — rigorously, without social performance — and bring her what he decides merits her attention.

### The Maintenance Engineers — Community Note

**What they are:** Twelve maintenance engineers on rotating assignment, responsible for the underground complex's physical infrastructure. They are not Vault Guard; they are technical specialists with extended-clearance contractor status, embedded in the Vault District for rotations of 3-6 months. They live in the personnel quarters during rotation.

**What they know:** The underground complex's physical reality at the level of people who have been working in it — the temperature variations that the monitoring equipment doesn't fully capture, the specific acoustic properties of each level, and the sound that began approximately three months ago in the Heart chamber's lower section. It is not loud. It is not describable in terms that map to their engineering training. It is present. They have discussed it among themselves and concluded that it is probably resonance from the Network's infrastructure interacting with the chamber's geometry. This explanation satisfies none of them but it is the only technical explanation available and they have been working with it.

**Reaching them:** The maintenance engineers are not accessible from outside the District under normal circumstances. A player character with legitimate Vault access — even the limited outer administrative access — will encounter engineers at the Technical Administration Building. Engineers off-rotation (in the city, on their between-assignment period) are findable through their professional guild registrations in Lamplight Ward. An engineer who has recently completed a Vault District rotation and is currently in the city proper is the most accessible version of this knowledge, and there is one — **Kell Marsh** (human, 40s, currently between rotations, staying at Kess Aldman's boarding house in the Manufactories, which says something about where engineers tend to be from) — who has been trying to describe the sound to his wife for three weeks and who will talk about it with anyone who seems like they might know what it means.

## Vault District Access — Summary for GM

**What the players need to get inside legitimately:**

1. Radiant Core reputation +2 minimum (to reach a Councilor willing to co-sign)
2. A stated purpose that the Research Board assesses as legitimate
3. Either Mira Ashton's co-signature (most likely for players working against the crisis) or another willing Councilor
4. Clean enough backgrounds to pass the review — this is the detail that matters for players with Underground histories
5. Willingness to take the oath, understanding what the arcane witness means

**What the players need to reach the Heart chamber legitimately:** Impossible through normal channels. The Council authorization for Level Four access does not exist in a form that can be obtained. Even Mira Ashton cannot grant it unilaterally — it requires full Council vote, which currently does not have the political architecture for passage on any reasonable timeline.

**What the players need to reach the Heart chamber via the tunnel route:**

1. The tunnel knowledge — Harrow's connection or the Academic Quarter Archives' construction documentation
2. The ability to open the arcane seal — Tern Ashe's research, or a character with sufficient arcane capability and knowledge of Hollostar-mechanism interaction
3. A plan for Level One that accounts for the monitoring equipment and the patrol schedule
4. Enough. A reason that is worth what reaching it costs.

## The Heart — What It Means at the Table

The Heart is the campaign's destination in the literal and structural sense. Everything that happens in Lumencrest — the power crisis, the political gridlock, the Shard Division's experiments, the disappearances, the Hollostar Dust supply chain, the classified research programs, the street-level dependency of 80,000 people on something that is running out — all of it originates here and all of it will resolve here, one way or another.

The resolution is not predetermined. What players do when they reach the Heart, and what they have brought with them when they arrive — the information from Petra Vane and Neva Strand, the evidence for Sera Coldwell, the connection to Tern Ashe's research, the relationship with Sable-Seven and the Free Hand, the intelligence from Demi Wren about the vote count, the truth about the Shard Division — determines what the resolution is.

Several resolutions are possible. None of them are simple. All of them change Lumencrest permanently.

The city has approximately 14 months. The players know this, or will know it, before they reach the Heart. The question the campaign asks is what they do with the time they have.

## Vault District — There Is No Reputation Track

The Vault District does not have a reputation track. It has an access system. Either you are authorized or you are not. Either you have found the route through the pre-city passages or you have not. Social standing in Shadowside or the Radiant Core creates the conditions under which authorization becomes possible; it does not substitute for it.

What the Vault District has instead of a reputation track is the accumulated weight of every relationship the players have built across every other district — every NPC they've helped, every piece of intelligence they've gathered and used responsibly, every decision they've made about what kind of people they are in this city. That is what they carry through the gate or through the tunnel. That is what they bring into the chamber with them.

The Heart does not assess reputation. It is simply there, and dying, and what happens next depends on what the players decide to do.

## Faction Presence — Vault District

**The Vault Guard** — the sole authority within the walls. Independent of all three Council factions in legal structure; influenced by all three in practical politics in ways that Coldwell manages by giving none of them more than she gives the others, which produces the specific political situation of an indispensable institution that everyone wants to align and no one can claim.

**The Illuminist faction** — present through the research team, which has authorized access to Levels One and Two. Their relationship with the Vault District is the most fraught of the three factions because they are closest to the information and therefore most responsible for what is and isn't reported.

**The Conservators and Expansionists** — no direct presence inside the walls. Their Council members hold access authorizations that are unused — neither faction's leadership has exercised their Level Three access in the past two years. Both have been making this decision for reasons they have not made explicit.

**The Unseen Guild / Shard Division** — not present inside the walls and not able to reach inside through conventional means. The tunnel route, if the Shard Division knew about it, would change this. They do not know about it. Sera Coldwell has been ensuring they don't by maintaining the monitoring gap's existence as classified information that has not reached any Council briefing. This has been a deliberate decision. It is one of the three things she has not told the Council.

**Mirrorpetal (Sable's connection):** Sable has been through the pre-city passage to the Heart chamber once. What they saw informed what they do with the petals — the specific quality of choosing who receives one, the specific accuracy of their assessment of readiness. They have not told anyone what they saw. The mirrorpetal's interaction with the Transformed condition, specifically the extended vision for players who have that condition, is what Sable understands will happen for the right person at the right time. They have been waiting for that person. Whether it is one of the players is the GM's determination.
