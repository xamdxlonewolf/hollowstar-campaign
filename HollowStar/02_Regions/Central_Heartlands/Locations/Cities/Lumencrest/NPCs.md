# Lumencrest — Notable NPCs

*All significant named characters in Lumencrest. Each entry includes appearance, personality, what they want, what they know, and where to find them.*

---

## Council and Political Figures

### Mira Ashton
**Role:** Council Chair, Illuminist faction leader  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 54  
**Location:** Council District offices (daily), Accord Chamber during sessions, a small restaurant on the Core's south edge (evenings, alone)

**Appearance:** Tall, grey-haired before her time, moves quickly. Dresses practically for a Councilor — good quality but not ostentatious. Has a habit of tucking her pen behind her left ear when she's thinking and not realizing she's done it. Dark circles under her eyes that haven't gone away in two years.

**Personality:** Exhausted, precise, and genuinely good at reading people. Does not waste words. Will tell you what she can't help you with before she tells you what she can, because she'd rather establish honest limits upfront than have to revise later. Has a dry sense of humor that emerges in moments of particular stress. Under all the exhaustion is someone who cares very much about this city and is frightened of what happens to it.

**What she wants:** A solution to the Heart's failure that she can present to the Council before the Expansionists force a military vote. She has been buying time for three years. She is aware the time is running out.

**What she knows:** The full extent of the Heart's decline (worse than public figures suggest). The existence of the classified research programs. That one of those programs has produced something the researchers haven't reported to her, which she knows because Rector Marke's reports have developed a very specific quality of careful non-disclosure she has learned to recognize. That the Expansionists have been coordinating with Steelwatch's Marshal-General outside official channels.

**What she doesn't know:** What the unreported research finding is. Whether it's good or catastrophic. This is what keeps her awake.

**Working with her:** She will be formal and careful at first meeting. She pays attention to how people present themselves under pressure — calm, honest assessment of capability matters more to her than impressive credentials. If she decides someone is useful and trustworthy, she will give them as much access as she can and pay generously. She will not ask people to do things she considers clearly unethical. She will ask them to do things in gray areas and acknowledge they're gray areas.

### Theron Ashwick
**Role:** Senior Councilor, Expansionist faction's most effective speaker  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 61  
**Location:** Council offices (Expansionist wing), private dining clubs in the Estates, Accord Chamber gallery (watching sessions he's not in)

**Appearance:** Silver-haired, well-dressed without being flashy, always looks like he got a full night's sleep regardless of whether he did. Warm smile. Remembered handshake — he learns names before meetings and uses them.

**Personality:** Patient, charming, and running a very long game he believes he's winning. Frames everything as pragmatism: not "we should seize the Endless Eye" but "the alternative to securing additional power sources is the collapse of everything Lumencrest has built." He is not wrong that Lumencrest needs a solution. He is pursuing a solution that serves his political position and the manufacturing interests that fund it while being genuinely convinced it's the right answer.

**What he wants:** A Council vote authorizing military preparation for action against the Endless Eye. He wants this before the Illuminists produce any result that could make the vote unnecessary.

**What he knows:** The manufacturing guilds' financial situation — they need the crisis resolved in a way that positions them for post-crisis dominance. The Steelwatch connection (Marshal-General Corvain is ready to move if authorized). The approximate timeline on the Heart's failure per his own advisors' calculations.

**Working with him:** He will be immediately charming and will make whatever he's asking seem reasonable. He pays exceptionally well and does not micromanage. He will not tell players the full picture of what they're working toward until it's too late to change it easily. He is not evil — he genuinely believes what he's doing is the right answer. He is also willing to cause significant harm to achieve it.

### Sera Coldwell
**Role:** Vault Guard Commander  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 64  
**Location:** Vault District, Council District for briefings, occasionally the Central Hub Station for reasons she doesn't explain

**Appearance:** Compact, grey-haired, moves like she's still in her forties. Uniform is impeccable. Carries nothing visible but you would not want to find out what she does carry. Has a face that gives away nothing, which took forty years to develop.

**Personality:** The clearest thinker in Lumencrest's security apparatus. Does not work on assumptions — only on what she can verify. Has dealt with every kind of threat to the Vault in her career and has a finely calibrated sense of which threats are serious and which are posturing. Is not unkind, but kindness is not her priority.

**What she wants:** The Heart to be secured. By which she means: for the political situation to not compromise the security situation. She has watched three Council factions use the crisis for political positioning and is aware that all three pose different kinds of threat to the Vault's integrity.

**What she knows:** Everything that moves through or near the Vault District, including things the faction leaders don't know she knows. The full capability of the monitoring systems — both their coverage and their gaps. That the classified research programs in the Academic Quarter represent a governance failure that could become her problem if something in one of those labs produces something dangerous.

**One unauthorized access:** She has granted exactly one in her career, to someone she will not name, for reasons she will not discuss. She has not regretted it. She has also never done it again.

**Working with her:** She will not be approached easily. Initial contact requires either official business (rare) or someone who can make a compelling case that they have information she needs. She will assess very carefully. If she decides to work with someone, she expects complete honesty and will know if she's not getting it. She pays in access and information rather than gold, which is often worth considerably more.

## Luminarium and Academic Quarter

### Rector Aldis Marke
**Role:** Rector of the Luminarium  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 68  
**Location:** Luminarium Rector's office (mornings), the Archives (afternoons), the Four Constants cafe (evenings, often)

**Appearance:** Large man running to fat in his later years, with an impressive beard that's gone white and stays impressive anyway. Rumpled in the specific way of someone who was once meticulous and has given up on the detail now that the important things need full attention. Reading glasses he forgets to remove when he stops reading.

**Personality:** Terrifying intellect, genuine warmth, and a practical ethics that has led him to make several decisions he would not be comfortable defending publicly. He approved research programs he didn't fully understand because he trusted his researchers and the city needed results. He is beginning to suspect this was wrong. He is not sure yet what to do about that.

**What he knows:** The existence and general nature of all seventeen research programs, including the six classified ones. That one classified program has produced something the lead researcher hasn't reported. That he has been writing his reports to the Research Board with sufficient careful language to avoid technically lying while also not technically disclosing things that would trigger a review.

**Working with him:** He responds to genuine intellectual engagement and honest assessment. He will be a valuable ally for players who need Luminarium access and can demonstrate they are trying to help rather than cause harm. He is not easy to approach — he is busy and guards his time — but the Four Constants cafe in the evenings is where he processes, and he is less guarded there than in his office.

### Petra Vane
**Role:** Research Fellow, Luminarium (classified program lead)  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 34  
**Location:** Research Lab Seven in the Luminarium's restricted wing, the Four Constants cafe (late nights, alone)

**Appearance:** Short, dark-haired, perpetually slightly startled-looking. Has been wearing the same three sets of clothing on rotation for six months because she keeps forgetting to buy more. Hands are stained with whatever chemical process she's currently running.

**Personality:** Brilliant and genuinely frightened. She has found something in her research that she doesn't understand and that seems to suggest the Heart's failure is not a mechanical problem — it is something else, something that doesn't fit the theoretical framework she was trained in. She cannot report this because she cannot explain it, and she cannot explain it because she hasn't found anyone she trusts to think it through with.

**What she's found:** Something that suggests the Heart is not failing — it is *responding*. To what, she doesn't know yet. The implication, if she's right, is significant and the implications downstream of that implication are things she's been carefully not thinking about too directly.

**Working with her:** She needs someone who can help her think through what she's found without the constraints of the official program's mandate. She is not good at trust but she is desperate for someone to trust. Approach her at the Four Constants late at night, when she's exhausted and less guarded. Demonstrate genuine capability rather than credentials. Ask good questions.

## Underground and Shadowside

### Solt
**Role:** Proprietor of the Dark Lantern, Shadowside's central information hub  
**Ancestry/Age:** Ribbet, ~70 (hard to tell with Ribbet)  
**Location:** The Dark Lantern, Shadowside main thoroughfare, every day, all day

**Appearance:** Green-brown skin, wide-set eyes that miss nothing, moves with the economy of motion of someone who has been behind a bar for forty years. Wears an apron that has been clean once.

**Personality:** Neutral is not the right word — Solt has opinions about everything. But he keeps them, because the Dark Lantern's value is that everyone can come here without worrying about whose side Solt is on. He has served city watch inspectors and fugitives within twenty minutes of each other and maintained the same professional courtesy to both. This is an achievement. He maintains it consciously.

**What he knows:** More than anyone in Shadowside about what moves through the district. He doesn't sell information. He will, over time and with demonstrated trustworthiness, provide context when someone asks about something he knows about. The distinction he maintains is that selling information makes him an information broker and information brokers get targeted. Providing context to established regulars is hospitality.

**Working with him:** Spend time in the Dark Lantern. Don't ask him direct questions about things you want to know. Be consistent, be useful to the district in some way that's visible, be not-city-watch obviously. After a week of regular presence, he'll start talking more freely. After three weeks, he might say something unprompted.

### Calla
**Role:** Head of the Sisterhood of the Silver Thread  
**Ancestry/Age:** Half-elf, apparent age mid-40s  
**Location:** A licensed Silver Thread establishment in Lamplight Ward's Entertainment Quarter (official address); actual location varies

**Appearance:** Elegant in the way of someone who has studied elegance and mastered it. Always seems to have slightly more information about you than you'd expect from a first meeting. Does not dress to attract — dresses to observe while being observed.

**Personality:** Operates from behind absolute professional composure. Has seen every kind of person in her years running the Guild and has very few remaining illusions about any of them, including herself. Is genuinely protective of her members and this is the thing that, if you discover it, is the crack in the composure.

**What she knows:** A substantial portion of what happens in private conversations in Lumencrest, filtered through twelve years of managing an organization whose members are present for those conversations. She trades information for information that is useful to the Guild — which means information that might affect her members' safety, the Guild's legal standing, or the city's stability in ways that would change the operating environment.

**Working with her:** She will not be approached in her official capacity for information — she'll redirect you politely. She'll be approached through a mutual contact who can vouch for you, or through demonstrating that you have something genuinely valuable to offer. The conversation, when it happens, will be pleasant, efficient, and will leave you feeling she learned more from it than you did. This is accurate.

### Genna Cradle
**Role:** Lead organizer of the Ironwright Collective  
**Ancestry/Age:** Half-orc, 47  
**Location:** Ironwright Hall (Manufactories), the Forge and Flagon (evenings), wherever there's a problem she's decided to address

**Appearance:** Large, grey-streaked hair worn practical, hands that have done factory work even though she's been organizing for fifteen years. Presence that fills a room without her trying to fill it.

**Personality:** Direct, precise, and furious in the controlled way of someone who has been angry for fifteen years and learned to make the anger useful. She doesn't hate the system — she has very specific, well-documented problems with specific decisions that specific people made. She argues from evidence. She is one of the most effective public speakers in Lumencrest. The Council is aware of this and has been quietly trying to find a way to either co-opt or discredit her for six years. Both have failed.

**What she wants:** The power rationing in the Manufactories addressed in a way that prioritizes workers' residences alongside factory machinery. The Labour Board reformed to address the most exploitative elements of Clank service assignment. A response from the Council that isn't "we acknowledge your concerns."

**Working with her:** She will be skeptical of outsiders quickly and fair to them quickly. If you're not city watch and not an obvious plant, she'll give you a real conversation. If your actions over time align with what she says your intentions are, she'll trust you. She is loyal to people who are loyal to what she's fighting for, not to her personally.

## At the Gates and In the Streets

### Tomm
**Role:** Senior guide, Welcome Gate information service, Copper Gate  
**Ancestry/Age:** Half-elf, 29  
**Location:** The Welcome Gate, Copper Gate, most days

**Appearance:** Practical footwear and clothing — comfortable at any speed. Worn official district map extensively annotated in small handwriting.

**Personality:** Direct about what his service can and can't offer. In the first ten minutes tells new arrivals the Welcome Gate's currency exchange referral has a kickback and Merchant Square's licensed booths are better — costing himself the referral fee. Proved correct by repeat business.

**What he knows:** Full navigable geography including informal routes and unofficial access points. Street-level social landscape — which establishments are what they appear to be. One of the best first contacts for players needing city knowledge without faction embedding.

**Working with him:** Hire through Welcome Gate (2 silver intro, 5 silver full day) and ask for Tomm specifically. Honest orientation without agenda.

### Old Pen
**Role:** Proprietor of the Pauper's Gate Taproom  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, appears late 60s  
**Location:** Pauper's Gate Taproom — immediately inside north gate, left side

**Appearance:** Short, wide, white-haired. Moves slowly on purpose. Face remarkable for complete neutrality — not blank, not cold, absence of unsolicited expression.

**Personality:** Oldest fixture in the Underground's immediate surroundings. Survived twenty years by being useful to everyone and dangerous to no one — or being perceived that way. Truthful to city watch about which way people went; truthful to those people about who's been asking. Both sides trust her to do exactly that.

**What she knows:** Everyone through Pauper's Gate twenty years — appearance and often first destination. Gate-traffic inquiries priced at 3 silver marks whether buyer is Underground or trusted watch.

**Working with her:** Buy drinks. Return regularly. Week one: acknowledgment beyond courtesy. Week two: week-old intel. Week three: current information. Timeline does not accelerate for gold.

### Senna Wold
**Role:** Junior Illuminist Councilor; representative for eastern Lamplight Ward  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, early 40s  
**Location:** Council sessions; lives in Eastwick, eastern Lamplight Ward

**Appearance:** Less polished than senior Councilors — dresses like someone who lives in the Ward she represents. Often looks specifically worried rather than performing concern.

**Personality:** Genuinely frightened in a way her faction colleagues mask with technical confidence. Knows the eastern Ward at household level — lamp failures, repair queue, Eastwick stress. Speaks rarely in Council; when she does, it's specific and local.

**What she wants:** The power crisis acknowledged as the beginning of something, not routine maintenance. Has a specific fear this is the opening phase of civic fracture.

**What she knows:** Eastern Ward infrastructure reality before it reaches Council agendas. Aware of Eastwick Cooperative stockpiling through constituent contact. Suspects official Heart timeline is optimistic — hasn't gone on record.

**Hook:** Party helping Eastwick or visible in Ward community may draw her out for a frank conversation no senior Councilor would permit

### Captain Isen Varr
**Role:** City watch captain — Lamplight Ward / Shadowside border deployments  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 45  
**Location:** Border protests, Eastwick response; rarely Radiant Core

**Appearance:** Command posture under strain. Uniform correct; eyes track too many moving parts at once.

**Personality:** Containment over confrontation. Not corrupt — overwhelmed. Will remember faces that helped during the hub blast.

**What he knows:** Protest organizers are not radicals (Tessa Morr, Docken Hale). Investigation will stall without outside leads. Vault Guard notified; not his jurisdiction.

**Working with him:** Cooperate during the blast response; provide witness statements he can file. **Presence DIF 12** to avoid sweep detention if party was near hub.

### Tessa Morr
**Role:** Protest organizer — Ironwright-adjacent, Eastwick resident  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 34  
**Location:** Lamplight Ward / Shadowside border actions; Eastwick Cooperative network

**Appearance:** Work-worn hands, loud voice used to being heard over crowds. Ironwright banner not worn — carried.

**What she wants:** Power rationing rollback, not violence. Public distance from bombers immediately after blast.

**Working with her:** Credibility with Manufactories workers and Eastwick mutual aid. Intro to Genna Cradle if party earns trust post-blast.

### The Gate Sergeant (South Gate)
**Name:** Aldus Brenn  
**Role:** Senior gate guard, South Gate  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, 52

Not a villain, not a hero — a city employee who has been doing this job for twenty-two years and has a good read on people. He is the first authority figure players encounter in Lumencrest. He's seen everything come through this gate and has calibrated instincts. He is not corrupt (bribing him will go poorly). He is not cruel. He will wave through almost anyone who isn't obviously a Clank, isn't obviously armed to military scale, and doesn't give off the specific energy of someone who came here to cause a problem.

He knows things about who has come through the gate recently that could be very useful, and he might share them with people he decides to trust, in a casual-conversation way, over time.
