# Lumencrest — Active Timeline and City Calendar

## Act One: The Fracture

_This document tracks what is happening in Lumencrest on a day-by-day basis, independent of player action. It assumes players arrive on Day 1, before the first hub explosion. The timeline shows what happens if players do nothing — every entry is a living world event that players can accelerate, prevent, redirect, or simply witness. Use this to maintain the pressure that makes Act One feel real._

_Cross-reference [[Lumencrest_NPC_Index]] for which NPCs are involved in each event. Cross-reference the Campaign Arc document for the full structural context._

---

## BEFORE THE PLAYERS ARRIVE — Context

The following has already happened before Day 1:

- **18 months ago:** The Shard Division began operations. The first Shadowside disappearances occurred.
- **8 months ago:** Aldric Vane began suppressing raw Heart output data in his reports to the Research Board.
- **6 months ago:** Sera Coldwell began her tunnel anomaly file.
- **4 months ago:** Petra Vane identified the secondary periodicity in the Heart's output. She has run the analysis seven times.
- **3 months ago:** The maintenance engineers in the Vault District began hearing the resonance in the Heart chamber's lower section.
- **2 months ago:** Theron Ashwick's military vote was tabled for the second time. The Ironwright Collective's strike fund crossed its operational threshold.
- **1 month ago:** Neva Strand submitted her theoretical modeling result to the Research Board for review. The Board classified it without publishing it. She withdrew it from the classification process and scheduled a public lecture.
- **2 weeks ago:** The Hollostar Dust supply crisis became visible at street level — prices up 40%, cut product appearing at unverified sources, two documented overdoses in the Maze Quarter.
- **4 days ago:** Wren's cell finalized the plan for the first hub explosion.
- **Yesterday:** The first large organized protest at the Lamplight Ward / Shadowside border, focused on power rationing. City watch present. Tense but controlled.

## THE CALENDAR

_Each entry notes: what happens, who is involved, what players might observe or interact with, and what consequences follow if unaddressed._

### DAY 1 — Arrival

**The city as players first experience it**

The lights are on. The cable cars run. The fountain in Merchant Square sounds like a city operating normally. Pauper's Gate is busy — the usual traffic plus a visible handful of people who have the look of people who have been traveling for a long time and arrived somewhere they didn't plan to. The first Frostwell refugees, quietly.

**What's visible:**

- Protest remnants — the previous day's demonstration left chalk writing on the Lamplight Ward / Shadowside border that city workers are in the process of cleaning. Players who read it will see: WE PAID FOR THIS LIGHT.
- The Night Market is smaller than usual — three vendors who usually work the substance section are absent. The Dust supply disruption is making the Night Market's chemistry end nervous.
- Solt is quieter than usual at the Dark Lantern. Someone he knows didn't come back from an errand three days ago.

**GM note:** Don't explain these things. Let players observe them. They are texture on Day 1 that becomes meaningful retroactively.

---

### DAY 2 — The Explosion

**Sessions 1-2 climax**

**Time:** Mid-afternoon. The protest at the Lamplight Ward border has reformed — larger than yesterday, more organized. A city watch contingent is present. The mood is loud but not violent.

**The explosion:** A Lumen Network distribution hub on the Ward's eastern edge — three blocks from the protest, close enough that the crowd hears it. The hub is not the main distribution node; it's a secondary feed hub that serves the eastern Ward residential neighborhood and a section of the Entertainment Quarter. The explosion is targeted — not massive, not random. Someone who knew what they were doing.

**Immediate effects:**

- Eastern Ward loses power. Eastwick neighborhood goes dark.
- A section of the Entertainment Quarter, including the Copperstring, loses power mid-afternoon.
- Three people in the immediate area of the hub are injured, one seriously.
- The protest crowd panics — some scatter, some surge toward the blast site, city watch response shifts from protest management to emergency response.
- The Vault Guard is notified immediately. They do not deploy to the street — this is not their jurisdiction — but the operations room notes it.

**What players can do:**

- Help the injured (establishes character; Ora Finch at the Mending Room is the best referral)
- Pursue — someone was at the hub in the minutes before the explosion. There are people who saw something. Ash was watching from a rooftop two blocks away and left quickly.
- Get caught in the watch sweep — city watch is pulling people in for questioning who were in the area
- Notice things: the blast pattern, the component type (not standard incendiary — something that knew the hub's specific architecture), the specific hub that was chosen (secondary feed; not maximally destructive; calculated)

**Wren and Ash:** Not in the area. Watching from a distance. Ash is troubled. Wren is calculating.

**What happens next regardless of players:**

- City watch investigation begins. It will not go anywhere productive without player involvement.
- The Council is convened in emergency session the following morning.
- Theron Ashwick makes a statement calling this an act of anti-civilization terrorism.
- Sevan Vael calls for calm and measured response.
- The protest organizers (not connected to the radical wing) immediately and publicly condemn the explosion and distance themselves from it.

### DAY 3 — Emergency Council Session

**Players will hear about this; may attend the gallery**

**Morning:** The Accord Chamber convenes. The gallery fills in ninety minutes. The session is two hours of the factions performing their positions:

- Ashwick (Expansionist): This is the consequence of insufficient security. Military readiness matters. Motion to increase Vault Guard's city-wide authority.
- Ashton (Illuminist): This is the consequence of social conditions. Investigate the specific actors. Do not expand security powers broadly.
- Vael (Conservator-aligned): Calls for a cross-faction investigation committee. This is accepted. The committee will meet. It will not produce results.

**Players in the gallery will observe:**

- Ashwick speaks to Vael twice during the session in the way Demi Wren would recognize — he's moving him toward something, and it isn't the committee.
- Ashton is controlled but visibly tired.
- The one Council member who looks specifically worried rather than performing concern: a junior Illuminist representative named **Senna Wold** (unnamed until now — she knows the eastern Ward, she lives there, she has a specific fear that this is the beginning of something).

**Afternoon:** The Eastwick neighborhood is still dark. Brinne Halloway is at the Cooperative building organizing candles and lamp oil distribution. Lida's Bake is open and warm and the line goes around the block.

**Evening:** Speakers' Corner. Bram Kettlemarch speaks. Not about the explosion directly — about what it means that when the power goes out in one part of the city, other parts don't notice. He names no perpetrators. He says the city has been teaching people that their section of it doesn't matter. He stops speaking when he's said what he came to say.

### DAYS 4-7 — The Investigation Opens

**Sessions 3-5 approximately**

**Day 4:** Neva Strand's public lecture. Ember Lecture Hall. The title: _Secondary Periodicity in Hollostar Energy Output — A Theoretical Model_. The hall fills — more than usual for a theoretical physics lecture because the timing after the explosion has made anything touching the Heart feel urgent.

The lecture presents, in full technical detail, a model suggesting the Heart's failure timeline is significantly shorter than official estimates. She says "approximately 14 months" once. The room is silent for a moment. Then people start talking.

The two Vault Guard observers in the back are very still. They leave before questions begin. One goes directly to a communication relay; the other goes directly to the Vault District's operations room. Coldwell is informed within the hour.

Aldis Marke, who is in the gallery's front row, leaves before the lecture ends. He does not speak to Strand afterward. He goes back to his office. He sits there for two hours before doing anything else.

**Players who attend:** They now know the 14-month timeline from a public source. This changes what they can do with that information — it isn't classified anymore. It is, however, being contested by the Research Board within 48 hours.

**Day 5:** The Research Board issues a statement: Professor Strand's model is based on incomplete data and does not reflect the Board's current assessment. The official 3-5 year timeline stands. The Board's statement is written by someone who has not read Strand's paper carefully — it addresses the method rather than the result, which is a tell. Neva Strand publishes a response that addresses each specific objection methodically. The Academic Quarter's common rooms are extremely loud for the rest of the week.

City watch investigation into the hub explosion has produced zero leads. They have interviewed 23 people. None of them saw anything useful, or if they did, they haven't said so. The investigation is quietly deprioritizing.

**Day 6:** The first player-connected disappearance: if players have been in Shadowside for any sessions, someone they've met or spoken to has gone missing. Not dramatically — they're just not where they usually are. The Dark Lantern, the Sump, wherever they established themselves as regulars. Solt mentions it if players come in. He says it carefully, in a way that suggests it's not the first time he's mentioned something like this to a specific person.

**Day 7:** A second, smaller protest. This one is in front of the Merchant Guild hall in Lamplight Ward — labor-organized, Ironwright Collective participation, focused on the manufacturing slowdown rather than the explosion directly. Genna Cradle is present. She is not leading the protest; she is watching it with the specific attention of someone taking a measurement.

### DAYS 8-14 — Threads Developing

**Sessions 5-9 approximately**

**Day 8:** Tessiel Wane contacts someone — a city watch officer she has an arrangement with — to report a specific anomaly: the Hollostar Dust supply has changed in a way that suggests the disruption is upstream from the Night Market, not at the Night Market level. She frames this as a standard market intelligence report. The watch officer notes it. It goes into a file that Sera Coldwell's intelligence section will see in approximately two weeks.

**Day 9:** The Eastwick power is restored. Not fully — the replaced hub node operates at 70% capacity. Brinne Halloway notes in the Cooperative's records that the repair prioritized commercial connections over residential ones, and that three households on the neighborhood's eastern edge are still on reduced power. She files a formal complaint with the Charter Office. She does not expect a response.

**Day 10:** Professor Strand receives a formal letter from the Research Board informing her that her access to the Luminarium's research facilities is under review. She responds with a letter to Aldis Marke directly, bypassing the Board, requesting a meeting. He agrees to the meeting. The meeting is scheduled for Day 16.

**Day 11:** Someone in the Manufactories — a floor supervisor at one of the smaller Assembly Hall factories — posts a copy of the Research Board's public statement on the Ironwright Collective's notice board with three words written beneath it: _They know too._ Genna Cradle takes the note down and puts it in her files.

**Day 12:** Ash meets someone at the Pauper's Gate Taproom. Old Pen doesn't know who he is — he's not a regular. He is meeting with a contact the radical wing uses for material supply. Players who are tracking unusual movements might pick up the edge of this. Ash is nervous in a way the supply contact attributes to inexperience.

**Day 14:** The first contested disappearance — someone visible enough that their absence gets discussed at the Dark Lantern. A woman who ran a stall in the Night Market's substance section. Not a significant political figure. But she had a name. Solt knows it. Three other Night Market regulars notice she's gone.

If players have been developing the Shadowside disappearance thread, this is the moment it becomes something they can't ignore. If they haven't been tracking it, Solt will mention it — carefully, once — and let them decide what to do with it.

### DAYS 15-21 — Pressure Building

**Sessions 9-14 approximately**

**Day 15:** Second hub explosion attempt — smaller, fails. Someone tried to access a distribution junction in the Manufactories and was interrupted by a maintenance worker who came in on an irregular shift. The maintenance worker saw nothing clearly. City watch is informed. The investigation reopens with slightly more urgency.

The critical detail: the methodology of the attempt is identical to the first explosion's. Same component type, same approach. Players who investigated the first explosion and recognized the methodology will identify the connection immediately.

**Day 16:** Neva Strand meets with Aldis Marke. The meeting lasts three hours. Neither of them speaks about it publicly afterward. Marke returns to his office and sits at his desk for a long time. Strand goes to the Four Constants and drinks coffee until past midnight. Maren Solith doesn't ask what happened. She leaves the coffee coming.

The content of the meeting: Marke told Strand that her modeling result is consistent with classified findings he cannot share. He told her he is aware of the situation. He told her he is deciding how to proceed. She told him he had already decided by not acting, and that the decision was wrong, and that she intended to continue speaking publicly.

**Day 17:** Sevan Vael attends a private dinner at Aurum. The guest list: Theron Ashwick and one other Council member — a mid-tier Conservator whose vote Ashwick needs. Cass Vorn notes the configuration of the evening with the specific attention of someone keeping track of something.

**Day 18:** Calla Voss (Student Assembly) publishes a formal statement demanding the Research Board release the full classified program findings. It is signed by 89 students. The Board does not respond. The statement circulates to Lamplight Ward's Evening Broadsheet, which publishes a summary. It is read at Speakers' Corner the following evening by Pip Underwell, who adds her own paragraph connecting the suppressed Heart findings to the power rationing affecting the Clank Barracks.

**Day 20:** A Frostwell refugee — a young woman, Nine Hearths community, arrived at Pauper's Gate nine days ago — is found dead in the Underground's tunnel network. She was not one of the Shard Division's subjects; she died of exposure, alone, in an area of the tunnel that doesn't connect to the dry chambers. Harrow finds her. She handles the situation quietly. She tells no one except the two people she trusts most in the Chambers. Players who have established trust with Harrow will be told. It is the most direct version of what the Frostwell crisis looks like at ground level.

**Day 21:** Theron Ashwick announces publicly that he is preparing a motion for military authorization. Not a surprise — he's been building toward this for months. But putting it public changes the dynamic. Mira Ashton gives a press statement that is careful and measured and does not actually say she'll stop it.

Demi Wren, in the gallery at the announcement, updates her vote-count notes. Three votes short. Maybe two.

### DAYS 22-28 — The Convergence

**Sessions 14-20 approximately**

**Day 22:** Lina Voss sends the inquiry to Aldis Marke. It is five pages. The first paragraph describes what she knows. The second describes what she suspects. The third describes what she needs to know to make a fully informed Research Board decision on the classified programs. The fourth asks for a response within five days. The fifth notes that if she does not receive a substantive response, she will table her own motion at the Research Board's next meeting to de-classify all findings.

Marke reads it three times. He begins composing a response and stops twice. The response is not yet sent on Day 22.

**Day 23:** The Collector pauses Lumencrest operations. Mira Voss has assessed that the players' investigation is getting close enough to warrant caution. The last disappearances in Lumencrest occurred three days ago. The Northern Expanse operation is unaffected.

Players who have been tracking the disappearances will notice the pause. The absence of something is data.

**Day 24:** The Ironwright Collective calls a general assembly. Four hundred and twelve people fill the Hall and overflow into Halfhold Street. Genna Cradle addresses them for forty-five minutes. She does not call a strike. She says: _When we call, everyone must be ready._ The assembly votes unanimously to authorize strike action when the leadership calls it. She still needs the Council intelligence before she calls.

**Day 25:** Ashwick's military authorization motion is formally tabled for Council vote — scheduled for Day 32. Seven days. Mira Ashton begins working the vote count. She is starting from behind.

The Academic Quarter receives the news at the Four Constants, where it is discussed for six hours. Calla Voss (Student Assembly) begins a counter-petition. Neva Strand writes an op-ed for the Evening Broadsheet arguing that military action against the Endless Eye is based on a fundamental misunderstanding of what Hollowstars are. She does not yet know what they are — but she knows the energy interaction model is wrong in specific ways she can articulate.

**Day 26:** Players have been in the city 26 days. This is the moment to check: what do they know? What have they done? The threads they've pulled will have advanced differently based on their choices. This is the natural session to do a beat check — what is the current state of each active thread?

The world state at Day 26 regardless of player action:

- [HUB] Wren's second attempt failed; she is planning the larger coordinated attack for Day 35
- [SHARD] The Collector is paused in Lumencrest but active in the Northern Expanse
- [HEART] Neva Strand's findings are public but contested; Petra Vane's are suppressed; Lina Voss's inquiry is with Marke unsent
- [VOTE] Day 32 vote scheduled; Ashwick 2-3 votes short; Vael's conditional support is the deciding factor
- [VAEL] Nothing visible has changed. This is by design.
- [LABOR] Strike authorization held pending intelligence; Collective at full readiness
- [CLANK] Assignment freeze continues; Barracks overcrowded; Sable-Seven watching

**Day 28:** Aldis Marke responds to Lina Voss. The response is three paragraphs. The first acknowledges her inquiry. The second says the classified findings are under active review. The third says he will provide a substantive briefing to the Research Board within ten days.

Lina Voss reads the letter and schedules a meeting with Neva Strand.

### DAY 32 — The Military Vote

**Act One major political beat**

This vote happens on Day 32 regardless of player action. What the outcome is depends entirely on what players have done in the preceding 31 days.

**If players have done nothing about the vote:** It passes. 7-5. Sevan Vael's vote is the margin. Mira Ashton's statement afterward is precise and does not show the depth of what this means.

**If players have exposed Vael's conditional support language:** Vael withdraws his support 24 hours before the vote. The motion fails 5-7. Ashwick immediately begins working the count for a re-tabling in 30 days.

**If players have brought Petra Vane's finding or the raw data to Lina Voss before the vote:** Lina Voss votes against. The motion fails 5-7. The Research Board calls an emergency session. Everything accelerates.

**If players have done both:** The motion fails 4-8. Ashwick is visibly angry for the first time in anyone's public memory. Vael gives a brief statement that is perfectly calibrated to suggest he was always going to vote against it.

**The consequences of the vote passing:** Act One's final beat accelerates. Wren's coordinated attack, if it hasn't been stopped, becomes politically useful to the Expansionists — evidence that the city needs the decisive action they're providing. Mira Ashton's political position weakens significantly. The Ironwright Collective calls a strike regardless of intelligence because the Council's direction is now clear. And in the Northern Expanse, something changes in how the Frostwell refugees talk about Lumencrest.

### DAYS 29-35 — The Larger Attack

**Act One climax, Sessions 20-28**

**Day 29:** Wren has reassessed after the second attempt's failure. She knows someone is getting close — she doesn't know it's the players specifically. She accelerates the timeline. The larger coordinated attack is planned for Day 35: three distribution hubs simultaneously, during a public event that will concentrate emergency response resources elsewhere.

**Day 30:** Ash approaches someone in Shadowside. Not a player directly — a person players have met. He is clearly looking for something. He asks a question that doesn't make complete sense unless you know what he is. If players are connected in Shadowside, this information reaches them within a day.

**Day 31:** Marke sends a private letter to Petra Vane. It is seven sentences. The last sentence is: _I believe you are correct and I have been wrong to delay._ He schedules a meeting with Mira Ashton for Day 33.

**Day 33:** Marke meets with Mira Ashton. The meeting is one hour. Players with Radiant Core access may not know what was said, but they will see Ashton's demeanor change after. She is not less controlled. She is more deliberate. Something she suspected is now confirmed.

**Day 35 — The Coordinated Attack (if Wren hasn't been stopped):**

Three simultaneous explosions. The hubs targeted are:

- The primary Manufactories distribution junction (affects factory power across the district)
- The Lamplight Ward main feed node (affects the entire Ward)
- A Network relay node near the Academic Quarter (partial power to the Luminarium's research facilities)

The combined effect: rolling brownouts across three districts simultaneously. In the Manufactories, machinery stops mid-operation. In Lamplight Ward, the Entertainment Quarter goes dark at the worst possible hour. In the Academic Quarter, the classified research facilities lose power for 40 minutes.

The human cost is specific: two people in the Manufactories are caught in machinery when it stops unexpectedly. Both survive, one with serious injury. In Lamplight Ward, a panic in the Volta's crowd produces injuries. In the Academic Quarter, a research experiment in progress is destroyed — three months of work, irreplaceable.

Ash witnesses the Manufactories consequence. He was watching from across the street. He sees the workers who were hurt being helped out. He does not help them. He walks away. He is not the same person after this as before it.

**If Wren has been stopped:** One or two of the three attacks occurs — whatever Wren could salvage from a compromised operation. The consequences are proportionally smaller. Ash's crisis still occurs — it just involves a smaller event with the same human face.

### ACT ONE CLOSE — DAYS 36-42

**Sessions 24-28**

The city is changed. How much depends on the vote outcome and the attack outcome. The following is true regardless:

**The Heart timeline is public.** Between Neva Strand's lecture, the Research Board's increasingly untenable contestation, and Lina Voss's inquiry, the 14-month finding is working its way into public conversation. Not everywhere — not a banner on the Council wall. But people in the Academic Quarter and Lamplight Ward know, and what people know in those districts eventually reaches the others.

**The Ironwright Collective is at the edge of calling.** Genna Cradle is waiting for one more thing. If players have brought her the Council intelligence she needs, she is ready. If they haven't, she is still waiting — patiently, at cost, because the moment is almost right but not quite.

**Shadowside is quieter than usual.** The disappearances have paused. The Night Market's substance section is running cut-rate Dust that is dangerous and everyone in the Maze Quarter knows it. The Dry Chambers is three people larger than it was at the start — refugees who couldn't find anywhere else.

**The Vault Guard's file has a name.** Not the Shard Division. But Coldwell's tunnel anomaly document now has enough specificity that it has moved from "anomaly" to "pattern." She needs three more pieces. She is three more pieces away from going to the full Council rather than any individual faction chair.

**The tug outward:** Wherever it comes from — a dying radical wing member's last words, a letter from a Starfall Seeker researcher, a refugee's description of what the northern sky looks like at night, a piece of information from Tern Ashe about what the shard activation mechanism implies about where the shards come from — the players have a reason to leave Lumencrest and look at the larger world.

Act Two begins.

## LIVING WORLD EVENTS — BACKGROUND

_These events occur regardless of player involvement and can be used as texture at any point in Act One. They are not tied to specific days — use them when the scene needs something happening._

**The Evening Broadsheet:** Lumencrest's primary daily publication. It publishes what it can verify, which is less than what it suspects. The Broadsheet's senior editor, **Winn Castile** (not profiled elsewhere — use as needed), has been trying to verify the Heart timeline story for three weeks and keeps hitting classification barriers. If players give Winn something verifiable, it gets published.

**Cable Car Disruptions:** The cable cars run on Network power. During brownouts, specific lines go down. The South Factory Hub line is most vulnerable — it's the longest run and uses the most power. During the Act One brownouts, expect one or two cable car service disruptions. This is background texture but matters practically for players who depend on the cable car for transit.

**The Frostwell Refugees:** Their numbers grow slowly through Act One. By Day 42 there are approximately 200 in Lumencrest, clustering at Shadowside and Copper Gate. The Salvation Hearth is at capacity. Brother Carwell is making decisions about resource allocation that he finds uncomfortable. If players engage with the refugees directly, they hear stories that prefigure Act Two.

**Night Market Supply Shifts:** The Hollostar Dust crisis develops across Act One. Track it as: early sessions (Day 1-14) — prices elevated but supply exists; mid sessions (Day 15-28) — cut product appearing alongside clean; late sessions (Day 29-42) — clean product scarce enough that desperate users are accepting dangerous supply. The two documented overdoses become more as Act One closes.

**Speakers' Corner Weather:** Bram Kettlemarch speaks on: Day 3 (the explosion and what it reveals), Day 10 (the Research Board statement and what suppression costs), Day 18 (Pip Underwell's Clank barracks statement), Day 25 (the military vote and what it would mean), Day 38 (whatever has happened in the preceding sessions). Players who attend consistently see the city's public political consciousness developing in real time.

## ACT ONE — WHAT THE PLAYERS SHOULD KNOW BY THE END

_Not all of this needs to come from quests or plot beats. Some of it comes from being in the city and paying attention._

By the end of Act One, players who have been engaged should know:

- **The Heart has approximately 14 months.** Whether they learned this from Petra Vane, Neva Strand's lecture, the raw data in Aldric Vane's files, or some combination — they know the official timeline is wrong.
- **Someone is making people disappear in Shadowside.** They may know it's the Shard Division, or they may know only the pattern. Either is enough to carry into Act Two.
- **The Green Covenant radical wing bombed the hubs.** Whether they stopped them or not, they know who did it and why — and that the why is rooted in legitimate grievance.
- **The city's political situation is unstable in specific ways.** They understand the faction dynamics, House Vael's position, and what the military vote means.
- **Something larger is happening beyond Lumencrest.** The refugees, the night sky stories, the information trail — they have a direction.

They do not yet know:

- What Hollowstars actually are
- Where the Shard Division is operating (fully)
- What the Dragon Turtle is
- What Frostvault contains
- What the Sleeping Gate does

Those are Act Two.
