# Flamekeep

**Population:** ~9,000 | **Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]] - [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/The_Rivermark_Sovereignty|Rivermark Sovereignty]]  
**Location:** Southern Ironspine Mountains, eastern slopes  
**Ancestry:** Drakona majority (~75%), mixed others  
**Identity:** Mountain mining city, Starsilver source, The Stone Seat's home

---

## THE CITY

Flamekeep is carved into the southern Ironspine Mountains on the eastern slopes — a Drakona city built where the Starsilver veins run deepest. The city is part fortress, part mine, part sacred site to the Drakona who have lived here for over 400 years (predating the Rivermark Sovereignty itself).

**The architecture:** Drakona building style — stone carved with precision, fireproof by necessity, built to last millennia. The city terraces up the mountainside in three levels:

**The Forges** (lowest level) — Metalworking district where Starsilver is refined
- Massive forges burn hot enough to work the rare metal
- Workshops where Drakona craftsmen create enchanted items
- Heat from forges warms the entire lower city
- The smell of molten metal is constant

**The Mines** (middle level) — Living quarters and mine entrances
- Residential district carved into mountain
- Mine shafts descend deep into eastern Ironspine
- The Starsilver Vaults (heavily guarded ore storage)
- Communal halls where miners gather

**The Aerie** (highest level) — The Stone Seat's residence and ceremonial spaces
- Overlooks the entire city and eastern valleys
- The Hall of Stone (where The Stone Seat holds court)
- Ceremonial spaces for Drakona fire-keeping rituals
- Defensive positions (the city has never fallen)

## STARSILVER

Flamekeep's entire existence revolves around Starsilver — the rare metal found only in these eastern Ironspine veins. The metal holds magical enchantments better than any other known material, making it extraordinarily valuable to enchanters, artificers, and anyone working with magical items.

**The mining:** Drakona expertise combined with careful extraction. The veins are finite and Flamekeep knows it. Mining is deliberate, sustainable, focused on long-term productivity rather than short-term maximum yield.

**The refining:** The forges burn hot enough to purify Starsilver — a temperature only Drakona fire-breathers can consistently maintain. This gives Flamekeep complete control over the refined metal trade. Raw ore exists elsewhere (theoretically) but refined Starsilver comes from here.

**The trade:** Strictly controlled. The Stone Seat personally approves major Starsilver sales. Most goes to Lumencrest (Accord relationship), some to trusted enchanters across the continent, none to unknown buyers. Theft attempts are common. All have failed.

**The security:** Flamekeep's defensive position combined with Drakona strength makes the city nearly impregnable. The Starsilver Vaults have never been successfully breached. Guard rotations are constant. Miners double as militia.

## THE STONE SEAT

**Ironheart** (Drakona, 203, male) has held The Stone Seat on the Confluence Council for 47 years. He is Flamekeep's voice in Sovereignty governance and embodies the city's values: strength, patience, protection of what matters.

**The position:** Unlike River Voices (20-year terms) or Seasonal Speakers (annual rotation), The Stone Seat serves until voluntarily stepping down. This reflects Drakona values — leadership is proven through time, not elections. Ironheart will hold the seat until he chooses to pass it to a successor he judges worthy.

**His stance:** Cautious on the Sixth Seat campaign. Respects the Accord alliance but values Sovereignty independence. Concerned that formal Accord membership might compromise Flamekeep's control over Starsilver trade. Willing to be convinced but demands concrete assurances.

**His presence:** When Ironheart speaks in Council, others listen. Not because he demands it, but because 203 years of lived experience and 47 years of proven leadership command respect. His word carries weight across the entire Sovereignty.

## DRAKONA CULTURE

Flamekeep maintains distinct Drakona cultural practices while being fully integrated into the Sovereignty:

**Fire-keeping:** Ancient Drakona tradition of tending eternal flames. The Aerie houses the Flame of Flamekeep — burning for 400+ years, symbol of continuity and strength.

**Hoard-right:** Modified from ancient Drakona practices. Instead of personal hoards, Flamekeep maintains collective wealth (the Starsilver reserves, refined metal stores, city defenses). Individual Drakona take pride in contributing to collective strength.

**Scale-bonds:** Close family units, extended clan networks, fierce loyalty to proven allies. Once Flamekeep accepts someone as trustworthy, that trust is absolute. Betrayal is remembered across generations.

**Mountain home:** Drakona connection to stone and mountains runs deep. Flamekeep Drakona see themselves as protectors of the mountain and its gifts (Starsilver). This is spiritual responsibility as much as economic interest.

## RELATIONSHIP WITH THE SOVEREIGNTY

**Integration:** Flamekeep is fully part of the Rivermark Sovereignty despite cultural distinctiveness. The Stone Seat ensures mountain perspectives are never ignored in governance.

**Trade contribution:** Starsilver is the Sovereignty's unique resource. Flamekeep's control over this trade gives them enormous influence. They use it carefully, supporting overall Sovereignty interests.

**Military strength:** Drakona warriors from Flamekeep form elite units in the River Guard. Mountain knowledge and Drakona strength make them formidable defenders.

**Respect:** Twinwater governs, but Flamekeep is respected as essential. The relationship works because both sides recognize mutual dependence.

## ECONOMY

**Starsilver** (primary) — Rare metal mining, refining, controlled export

**Other minerals** — Tin, zinc, rare earth elements from eastern Ironspine (less glamorous but steady income)

**Metalworking** — Drakona craftsmen produce exceptional quality metal goods (weapons, armor, tools)

**Enchanted items** — Small workshop industry creating magical items using Flamekeep's Starsilver supply

**Mountain guides** — Flamekeep Drakona know the eastern Ironspine better than anyone, hire out as guides/scouts

---

## CURRENT CHALLENGES

**Starsilver greed:** The unique metal attracts constant unwanted attention. Theft attempts (all failed), espionage attempts (some successful at gathering information, none at stealing ore), external powers trying to secure exclusive trade deals.

**Vein depletion concerns:** The Starsilver veins are finite. Current extraction rates are sustainable for ~200 years. Flamekeep leadership is already planning for the post-Starsilver future, but the city's entire economy depends on this resource.

**Sixth Seat question:** Ironheart and Flamekeep leadership are divided. Formal Accord membership could mean pressure to increase Starsilver availability to Lumencrest. Independence allows controlled trade. The decision matters enormously to the city's future.

**Basin merchant pressure:** Starfall Basin's Merchant Circle wants Starsilver access. They've offered extraordinary prices for exclusive contracts. So far Flamekeep honors Accord relationships, but the offers keep escalating.
