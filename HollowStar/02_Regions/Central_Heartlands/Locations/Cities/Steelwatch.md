# Steelwatch

**Population:** ~28,000 (80% military personnel or dependents)  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Location:** Northern border, between Northern Expanse and Frostwell Tundra  
**Government:** Military Stratocracy — Marshal Council (5 officers)  
**Commander:** Marshal-General Thalia Corvain (human, 52, elected by soldiers)  
**Founded:** ~350 years ago as Heartland Accord's northern defense

---

## OVERVIEW

Steelwatch is fortress-city built for single purpose: defend Heartlands from northern threats. The entire city is fortification — 40-foot granite walls (the Iron Walls), strategic positioning commanding northern approaches, garrison of professional soldiers.

For 350 years, Steelwatch has stood ready. For 350 years, major invasion has never come. This is both success and crisis — the city exists to fight enemies that don't materialize. Purpose is questioned. Soldiers grow restless. Identity uncertain.

**The question:** What does garrison city do when there's nothing to garrison against?

---

## STRUCTURE

### The Iron Walls

Forty-foot granite walls surrounding entire city. Thick enough for soldiers to march on top, defensive positions every 50 yards, designed to withstand siege.

**Gates:** Only two—North Gate (faces potential threats) and South Gate (faces Heartlands). Both heavily fortified, easily sealed.

**Maintenance:** Constant. Entire city contributes to wall upkeep. This is shared duty, shared identity.

### Districts

**The Garrison (Central, ~15,000):**
Active military personnel. Barracks, training grounds, armories, command complex. This is where soldiers live, train, prepare.

**The Dependents Quarter (~8,000):**
Families of soldiers, retired veterans, support staff. They live behind the walls, share military culture, but aren't active duty.

**The Forge District (~3,000):**
Weapon and armor manufacture. Steelwatch makes its own equipment, exports surplus. Mix of military craftsmen and civilian contractors.

**The Market (~2,000):**
Commercial district. Less developed than other cities—soldiers don't need extensive markets, most supplies are military-issued.

## GOVERNMENT

### Military Stratocracy

Steelwatch is governed by soldiers who elect their leaders. Qualification: 10+ years military service in good standing.

**The Marshal Council:** Five senior officers (Marshal-General plus four Marshals) elected to 10-year terms. Majority vote on all decisions.

**Current Council:**
- **Marshal-General Thalia Corvain** (overall commander, 52, human, 3rd term)
- **Marshal Kael Ironhart** (infantry command, 48, half-orc)
- **Marshal Senna Quickblade** (cavalry, 44, human)
- **Marshal Dorian Stormwatch** (logistics, 56, human)
- **Marshal Feln Ravenshield** (intelligence, 61, dwarf)

**Elections:** Every 10 years, all soldiers with sufficient service vote. Campaigning is minimal—reputation and record determine outcomes.

**Thalia Corvain:** Third term as Marshal-General. Respected, competent, increasingly concerned about city's purpose crisis. Quietly supports Lumencrest Expansionists because conflict would give Steelwatch mission again.

---

## MILITARY STRUCTURE

**Standing force:** ~15,000 active soldiers
- 10,000 infantry
- 3,000 cavalry
- 2,000 specialists (engineers, scouts, archers, support)

**Training:** Continuous. Steelwatch maintains readiness through constant drills, exercises, war games. Without active threats, training is only purpose.

**Equipment:** High quality. Combination of Steelwatch-manufactured and Ironhold-imported. Well-maintained, abundant stockpiles.

**Philosophy:** Professional military, not conscripts. Soldiers are career fighters, trained to high standard, disciplined, proud.

---

## ECONOMY

**Primary funding:** Heartland Accord provides annual budget. In exchange, Steelwatch coordinates Accord military defense and provides troops when needed.

**The arrangement:** Other cities pay for standing professional army. Steelwatch defends collective interests. Works well when threats exist.

**Current issue:** Cities question cost when no threats materialize. Steelwatch budget debates happen annually. "Why fund army that never fights?"

**Secondary income:**
- Weapon/armor manufacture (export surplus)
- Mercenary contracts (Steelwatch soldiers serve as hired professionals when Accord permits)
- Training services (other cities pay Steelwatch to train their guards)

**The problem:** If Accord cuts funding, Steelwatch collapses. The city exists because other cities pay for it.

## CULTURE

### Military Values

**Discipline:** Obedience, order, structure. Civilian life feels chaotic to Steelwatch residents.

**Martial pride:** Excellence in combat is highest achievement. Soldiers who distinguish themselves in training exercises are celebrated.

**Egalitarianism:** Within military, merit matters more than birth. Anyone can rise through ranks with skill and service.

**Spartan lifestyle:** Minimal luxury, functional living. Excess is viewed as weakness.

### Daily Life

**Military schedule:** Wake at dawn, morning drills, duties, afternoon training, evening maintenance, rest. Six days active, one rest day.

**Food:** Communal mess halls, simple nutritious meals. Not gourmet, but abundant and well-prepared.

**Recreation:** Physical competitions, war games, weapon practice. Steelwatch "fun" involves martial skills.

**Family life:** Difficult. Military demands leave little personal time. Some soldiers remain unmarried. Those with families balance duties carefully.

### The Purpose Crisis

What happens to soldier city when there's no war?

**Generation divide:**
- **Veterans (30+ years service):** Remember when northern threats felt real. Believe in mission.
- **Mid-career (10-30 years):** Never saw major combat. Question whether mission still relevant.
- **New recruits:** Join because it's stable employment, tradition, or lack of alternatives. Many don't believe in existential threats anymore.

**The restlessness:** Soldiers trained for war, preparing constantly, never deployed. Energy builds with no release. Some turn to training obsession. Others to depression. Some to dangerous eagerness for conflict.

**Marshal-General Corvain's concern:** Her soldiers need purpose. If Heartlands don't provide legitimate mission, some will find purpose elsewhere—potentially problematically.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO HEARTLAND ACCORD

**Role:** Military coordinator for Accord. When threats emerge, Steelwatch commands unified response. Other cities provide troops, Steelwatch provides leadership.

**The reality:** No major threats have emerged requiring coordination. Steelwatch's role is theoretical.

**Perception by other cities:**
- **Lumencrest:** Respectful of military professionalism but questioning funding necessity
- **Kingsrest:** Appreciates martial tradition, supports Steelwatch budget
- **Ironhold:** Values disciplined competence, buys Steelwatch equipment
- **Sablemark:** Skeptical of militarism, questions whether threats exist

**The budget debate:** Every year, cities argue about Steelwatch funding. Lumencrest Conservators want cuts (save money for Heart research). Kingsrest supports maintenance (security matters). Ironhold pragmatic (professional military has value). Sablemark skeptical (but doesn't control votes).

## CURRENT SITUATION

### The Identity Crisis

Steelwatch built for war that never came. Success means irrelevance. The city needs to be needed.

**Three perspectives:**
1. **Traditionalists:** Threats are real, readiness essential, peace is temporary.
2. **Realists:** No threats exist, city must adapt or become obsolete.
3. **Opportunists:** Create threats if necessary, because purpose matters more than peace.

Marshal-General Corvain is navigating these factions carefully.

### The Expansionist Connection

Thalia Corvain quietly supports Lumencrest's Expansionist faction. Why?

**The logic:** If Lumencrest moves against Endless Eye, Steelwatch finally has mission. Coordinating military operation, actual deployment, real purpose.

**The morality:** She's not warmongering—she doesn't want unnecessary war. But if conflict comes, Steelwatch needs to be central to it. Better to be needed than question existence.

**The secrecy:** She can't say this publicly. Advocating for war to justify budget would destroy reputation. She supports Expansionists privately while maintaining public neutrality.

### The Restless Garrison

15,000 professional soldiers with nothing to fight. Some outcomes:

**Training obsession:** Push harder, perfect skills, wait for opportunity.

**Mercenary interest:** Some soldiers seeking Accord permission for external contracts (fight somewhere, earn money, gain experience).

**Dangerous eagerness:** Subset of soldiers actively hoping for conflict. They've trained entire careers, never tested themselves. They want war because that's what they're for.

**Depression/resignation:** Others losing purpose, questioning life choices, considering leaving military.

Marshal Council managing these dynamics carefully. Wrong move and garrison becomes liability instead of asset.

### The Budget Threat

Lumencrest's crisis means Accord has less money. Steelwatch budget is obvious target for cuts.

**The calculation:** Cut Steelwatch funding → save money → allocate to more urgent priorities (like Lumencrest's Heart research).

**Steelwatch's fear:** Reduced budget means reduced force. Reduced force means reduced relevance. Eventually, dissolution.

**Corvain's strategy:** Make Steelwatch indispensable. Provide value beyond sitting garrison. Training services, equipment manufacture, intelligence gathering. Prove worth before budget cuts come.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Restless Squad:** Soldier unit acting suspiciously. Planning unauthorized mercenary deployment? Desertion? Something worse? Marshal Council hires party to investigate quietly.

**The Budget Mission:** Steelwatch needs to prove value before Accord budget debate. Party hired to find/create situation requiring military response. Ethical complications ensue.

**The Northern Expedition:** Something unusual reported in Northern Expanse or approaching Frostwell impact zone. Steelwatch sends mixed military/civilian expedition (party included) to investigate. Finally, actual mission.

**The Expansionist Conspiracy:** Party discovers evidence that Corvain is coordinating with Lumencrest Expansionists. What do they do with information?

**The Mercenary Contract:** Foreign power offers Steelwatch lucrative mercenary contract. Some Marshals support (money, experience, purpose). Others oppose (abandoning primary mission). Council divided, party hired to investigate foreign power's true intentions.

**The Wall Threat:** Something from north testing Iron Walls—probing defenses, assessing strength. Exactly what Steelwatch was built for. Except nobody knows what it is or if it's real threat or elaborate test.
