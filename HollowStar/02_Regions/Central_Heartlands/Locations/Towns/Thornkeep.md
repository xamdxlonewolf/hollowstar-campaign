# Thornkeep

**Population:** ~3,200  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Location:** Edge of Thornwood forest  
**Economy:** Logging, timber trade, forest management  
**Notable:** Sustainable logging model, druid cooperation

---

## OVERVIEW

Thornkeep is logging town on [[Thornwood|Thornwood]] forest edge. Unlike aggressive clear-cutting operations, Thornkeep practices sustainable forestry—selective logging, replanting, forest health maintenance. This is partly environmental consciousness, mostly pragmatic: kill forest, kill livelihood.

**The cooperation:** Local druids (Path of Spirits followers) work with loggers. Druids identify which trees can be cut without harming forest. Loggers follow guidelines. Everyone benefits.

---

## THE LOGGING OPERATION

**Method:** Selective harvest. Mark specific trees, cut those only, leave forest intact. More labor-intensive than clear-cutting but sustainable long-term.

**Production:** Lower volume than aggressive logging but consistent year after year. Thornkeep has logged Thornwood for 150 years without depleting it.

**Quality:** Thornwood trees produce excellent timber—strong, straight-grained, resists rot. Thornkeep timber commands premium prices.

**The druid agreement:** Druids mark harvest areas each spring. Loggers follow marks exactly. Break agreement, druids withdraw cooperation, forest becomes hostile. Nobody breaks agreement.

---

## THE DRUID CIRCLE

**The Thornwood Circle:** Small group (8-12 members) of Path of Spirits druids maintaining forest health. They live in forest, rarely visit town, communicate through designated intermediary.

**Current Circle Keeper:** **Elder Rowan Greenmantle** (human, 67, female) - respected by loggers, maintains agreement, increasingly concerned about external pressures.

**The balance:** Druids allow logging because Thornkeep respects forest. If that changes, cooperation ends. Loggers know this.

---

## EXTERNAL PRESSURE

**Lumencrest wants timber:** With Heart failing, Lumencrest needs more lumber for alternative power sources, construction, fuel. They're offering premium prices for increased Thornwood harvests.

**The temptation:** Loggers could make significant profit by exceeding sustainable limits. Short-term gain, long-term disaster.

**Town divided:**
- **Traditionalists:** Honor druid agreement, maintain sustainability
- **Opportunists:** Take Lumencrest money, harvest extra, deal with consequences later
- **Moderates:** Negotiate carefully, maybe increase harvest slightly without breaking agreement

**Current status:** Town council discussing. Elder Rowan watching. If town breaks agreement, druids will respond. Nobody wants to find out how.

---

## TOWN CULTURE

**Forest respect:** Even opportunists respect forest's power. Difference is whether respect means preservation or careful exploitation.

**Logging families:** Multi-generational. Grandfathers taught fathers taught sons. Knowledge passes through families.

**Woodcraft pride:** Thornkeep loggers are skilled. They can fell tree precisely, process timber efficiently, assess wood quality expertly. This is respected profession.

**Community bonds:** Small town, everyone knows everyone. Disputes happen but community maintains cohesion.

---

## CURRENT SITUATION

The Lumencrest pressure is increasing. Opportunist faction gaining support. Traditionalists holding line. Elder Rowan issued warning: Break agreement, lose cooperation permanently.

**The stakes:**
- Accept Lumencrest deal: Short-term profit, risk destroying sustainable model
- Refuse deal: Maintain cooperation, miss significant economic opportunity
- Try compromise: Negotiate with both druids and Lumencrest, possibly satisfy nobody

Decision approaching. Town is tense.

---

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

- Hired by Lumencrest to convince Thornkeep to increase harvest
- Hired by druids to prevent agreement violation
- Hired by town council to investigate whether increased harvest is actually sustainable
- Investigation: Someone logging illegally in Thornwood, threatening agreement
- Mediation: Find compromise between economic pressure and environmental limits
