# Coppertown

**Population:** ~5,000  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Location:** Eastern hills near Ironhold territory  
**Economy:** Copper mining, ore processing, smelting  
**Government:** Company town—Copper Ridge Mining Company effectively controls everything

---

## OVERVIEW

Coppertown exists because rich copper veins were discovered here 80 years ago. The Copper Ridge Mining Company bought land, sank shafts, built town to house workers. Everything here serves the mine.

**The reality:** This is company town. The company owns most buildings, employs most residents, sets most rules. Town council exists but company influence is absolute.

---

## THE MINE

Three major shafts, extensive underground network. Produces copper ore processed in town smelters, exported to Lumencrest (electrical components), Ironhold (bronze alloys), other manufacturers.

**Working conditions:** Dangerous. Cave-ins, gas pockets, flooding. Miners die regularly. Company pays death benefits (modest) and hires replacements.

**Pay:** Better than farm labor, worse than skilled trades. Enough to survive, not enough to escape.

**The workforce:** Mix of locals, migrant workers seeking employment, some dwarven miners (brought expertise, paid more, creates tension).

---

## THE LABOR TENSIONS

**The issue:** Workers want better pay, safer conditions, less company control. Company wants maximum production at minimum cost.

**Current situation:** Informal labor organizing. No formal union (company forbids it) but workers coordinating. Talking about strike. Company threatening replacement workers.

**The divide:**
- **Radicals:** Want immediate strike, force concessions
- **Moderates:** Want negotiation before action
- **Company loyalists:** Fear losing jobs, oppose organizing

**Key figure:** Miner named **Garrett Stonehammer** (human, 42) is organizing workers. Company watching him closely.

**The danger:** If strike happens, company might bring in Steelwatch troops to break it (legal but provocative). Violence possible.

---

## THE SMELTERS

Ore processing happens in town. Three large smelters running constantly, turning raw copper into bars for export.

**Conditions:** Hot, toxic fumes, dangerous work. Pays slightly better than mining but shorter lifespan (lung damage common).

**Environmental impact:** Smoke, waste slag, water pollution. Company doesn't care. Residents suffer.

---

## LIFE IN COPPERTOWN

**Company housing:** Most workers live in company-owned housing. Rent deducted from wages. Maintenance minimal.

**Company store:** Only major shop in town. Prices inflated. Workers effectively paid in company credit.

**The cycle:** Work in mine, live in company house, buy from company store, die in company debt. Hard to escape.

**Recreation:** Three taverns (privately owned, somehow survive), small market, occasional traveling entertainers. Mostly, people drink and complain.

---

## CURRENT CRISIS

The strike is coming. Workers determined, company immovable, outside authorities (Heartland Accord) watching. If violence breaks out, implications extend beyond single town.

**Adventure hooks:**
- Hired by company to infiltrate labor organization
- Hired by workers to negotiate or protect strikers
- Hired by Accord to mediate before violence erupts
- Investigation: Are miners' complaints legitimate or exaggerated?
- Discovery: Mine hitting dangerous or valuable discovery
