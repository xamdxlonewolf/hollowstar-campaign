# Additional Accord Settlements

Two more settlements filling the western Central Heartlands (Accord territory).

---

## Pinehaven

**Population:** ~2,200  
**Location:** Northern Ironspine Mountains foothills, adjacent to forest (likely Thornwood approaches)  
**Identity:** Logging and mining town, mountain-forest transition

Pinehaven sits in the northern foothills of the Ironspine Mountains where the mountains meet dense forest (approaching Thornwood). The town operates both logging operations in the forest and small-scale mining in the mountain foothills, serving as a transition community between mountain and woodland economies.

**The location:** Built where a mountain stream provides water and power for sawmills. Forest to the north/northwest, mountain slopes to the south/east. The town sits in the transitional zone.

**Economy:**
- **Logging** (primary) — Pine and hardwood harvesting from surrounding forests
- **Small-scale mining** — Foothills contain modest copper and tin deposits
- **Sawmills** — Water-powered mills process timber for construction and trade
- **Mountain guides** — Residents know the northern Ironspine routes

**Population:** Human majority (~60%), dwarven (~25%, connected to Ironhold operations), others (~15%)

**Relationship to larger settlements:**
- Supplies timber to Steelwatch (relatively close, north)
- Some connection to Ironhold (via mountain routes, dwarven workers)
- Independent enough to avoid direct control by either city

**Character:** Working-class, practical, rough around edges. Loggers and miners don't have time for pretense. Community bonds through shared hard work.

**Current activity:** Increased demand for timber (Accord construction projects, refugee housing in Northern Expanse being built with Pinehaven lumber). The town is prospering but working harder than ever.

**Notable figure:** **Mill-Master Kael Pineborn** (human, 56, male) — Owns largest sawmill, coordinates timber operations, practical leader

**Geographic position:** 
- Northern Ironspine Mountains foothills (northern side, Accord territory)
- Adjacent to forest (Thornwood approaches or similar northern forest)
- Mountain-forest transition zone
- Relatively near Steelwatch (north), distant from other major Accord cities

## Saltwick

**Population:** ~7,000  
**Location:** Western coast, significant coastal trade port  
**Identity:** Large coastal town, maritime trade, fishing, shipbuilding

Saltwick is the Accord's second-largest coastal settlement (after Ironmouth) and the western coast's primary general-purpose port. Unlike Ironmouth (industrial focus) or Southmere (Kingsrest's port), Saltwick operates independently as a major coastal trade hub.

**The harbor:** Excellent natural harbor, protected bay, deep water anchorage. The harbor is why Saltwick exists — geography created a perfect port location and the town grew around it.

**Districts:**
- **The Docks** — Working waterfront, cargo operations, fishing fleet berths
- **Shipwrights' Quarter** — Shipbuilding yards, rope-walks, sail-making, maritime crafts
- **Merchant's Row** — Trade offices, warehouses, factor houses, deal-making
- **The Heights** — Residential, overlooks harbor, wealthier merchant families
- **Fishers' District** — Older section, original fishing village around which the port grew
- **The Market** — Daily market, fish market (famous), general goods

**Economy:**
- **Maritime trade** (major) — Coastal shipping, connects Accord to western sea routes
- **Fishing** (significant) — Large fishing fleet, processes and ships fish inland
- **Shipbuilding** (growing) — Constructing merchant vessels and fishing boats
- **Port services** — Warehousing, ship repair, provisioning, customs

**Trade routes:** Saltwick ships Accord goods (grain, manufactured items, metals) west and south along coast. Receives luxury imports, southern goods, maritime products. The town is wealthier than its size suggests because it controls significant trade volume.

**Relationship to the Accord:**
- Nominally under Lumencrest authority (distant)
- Practically self-governing through Merchant Council (informal body of major trading houses)
- Pays taxes to the Accord, operates independently otherwise
- Harbor-Master is technically Lumencrest appointment but selected from local candidates

**Political position:** Saltwick is large enough to want Accord representation but not large enough to demand it. Some residents advocate for expanding the Accord to include major towns like Saltwick. Others prefer current autonomy.

**Current Harbor-Master:** **Captain Senna Saltwise** (human, 49, female) — Manages port, former ship captain, respected by merchant community and dock workers alike

**Merchant Council (informal):**
- **Factor Bren Goldwater** (human, 58, male) — Wealthiest merchant, trades with Salt Shore
- **Ship-Master Vera Brightwave** (human, 44, female) — Owns largest shipbuilding operation
- **Fisher-King Torn Netwise** (human, 61, male) — Leads fishing fleet, represents traditional community
- **Warehouse-Lord Kael Stone** (dwarf, 167, male) — Controls storage facilities, Ironhold connections

**Rivalry with Keeper's Bay:** Salt Shore's Keeper's Bay is larger, more established, and dominates northeastern coastal trade. Saltwick is secondary but growing. Some competitive tension over maritime routes and shipping contracts. Not hostile — just business competition.

**Current situation:**
- **Increased trade volume** — Something is driving more coastal shipping. Saltwick is prospering but stretched for dock space.
- **Shipbuilding expansion** — Demand for new vessels is high. Saltwick is building more ships than ever.
- **Labor shortage** — Growing economy needs more workers. Town is attracting migrants but housing is tight.
- **Sixth Seat interest** — If Rivermark joins the Accord, how does that affect Saltwick's status? Some residents are watching the Sixth Seat campaign hoping it opens door for their own representation.

**Notable cultural feature:** The **Festival of Tides** (twice yearly, spring and autumn) — massive celebration when fishing season peaks. Music, food, ship blessings, the town's biggest social events. Visitors come from other Accord cities for the festival.

**Geographic position:**
- Western coast, excellent natural harbor
- Significant coastal settlement, second-largest Accord port
- West/northwest of other Accord cities (relatively isolated on coast)
- Maritime connections to Salt Shore (south), potentially Silverbelt Coast (east via sea routes)
- Coastal but not at a major river mouth (harbor is the natural feature, not river access)
