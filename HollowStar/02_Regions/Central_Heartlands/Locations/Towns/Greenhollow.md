# Greenhollow

**Population:** ~8,000 | **Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Location:** Western Accord territory, agricultural heartland  
**Ancestry:** Halfling majority (~70%), human (~20%), others (~10%)  
**Identity:** The Shire, traditional Halfling agricultural community, cultural hub

---

## THE TOWN

Greenhollow is what Halflings call "proper living" — rolling green hills, well-tended farms, comfortable homes built into hillsides, and a community that values good food, honest work, and leaving things better than you found them.

**The Shire character:** Greenhollow operates on traditional Halfling principles. Work hard during the day, enjoy good food and company in the evening, help your neighbors, maintain your land, and don't cause unnecessary trouble.

**Architecture:** Halfling-scale homes built into hillsides (cooler in summer, warmer in winter), round doors, windows facing gardens. Human visitors feel too large. Halflings feel it's properly sized.

---

## ECONOMY

**Agriculture:** Greenhollow produces exceptional crops — grain, vegetables, fruits, herbs. The soil is rich, the climate favorable, and Halflings know farming.

**Market town:** Twice-weekly market draws buyers from surrounding region. Greenhollow produce has reputation for quality.

**Brewing & baking:** Halfling expertise. Greenhollow's breweries and bakeries supply the region. The brown ale is famous. The seed cakes are legendary.

**Craft goods:** Small-scale but high-quality. Halfling artisans produce tools, textiles, furniture built for Halfling comfort (which means quality construction, just smaller).

---

## CULTURE

**Traditional Halfling living:** This is the Halfling cultural model. Work the land, feed your family and community, maintain traditions, live comfortably without excess.

**The Great Harvest Festival:** Annual celebration at harvest's end. Three days of feasting, music, dancing, and giving thanks for good crops. Everyone in the region is invited (and most come — the food is worth it).

**Family names matter:** Greenhollow families have farmed the same land for 10+ generations. Your name carries your family's reputation. Most families maintain that reputation carefully.

**The contrast with Lumencrest:** Some Halflings prefer big city living (Lumencrest has ~15,000 Halflings in its Halfling Quarter). Others prefer Greenhollow. Neither judges the other's choice, but each knows their way is right.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO LUMENCREST

**The divide:** Lumencrest Halflings are urban, commercial, integrated with other ancestries. Greenhollow Halflings are agricultural, traditional, Halfling-focused. Both are valid Halfling life paths.

**Trade connection:** Greenhollow supplies food to Lumencrest. Lumencrest provides manufactured goods and services Greenhollow can't produce. Economic relationship works well.

**Family ties:** Many Halflings have relatives in both places. Family reunions happen. Arguments about "proper Halfling living" happen. Everyone goes home knowing they're right.

---

## GOVERNANCE

**The Council of Elders:** Seven eldest family heads govern by consensus. Decisions affect everyone, so everyone's voice matters (through family representatives).

**Current Council Head:** **Elder Mira Greenleaf** (Halfling, 127, female) — Oldest member, wisest farmer, best seed cake recipe (secret, will not share)

**Practical focus:** Greenhollow governance addresses real problems — crop rotation, water rights, market coordination, festival planning. No grand politics. Just making things work.

---

## CURRENT STATUS

**Prosperity:** Greenhollow is comfortable, stable, prosperous in Halfling terms (which means everyone eats well, homes are maintained, no one lacks necessities).

**Refugee question:** Some Frostwell refugees have reached Greenhollow seeking farm work. The town is carefully absorbing small numbers (~50 total). Halflings are practical — if you work hard and fit in, you're welcome. If you don't, you're not.

**The Lumencrest comparison:** While Lumencrest deals with complex politics and Hollostar crisis, Greenhollow focuses on crops, weather, and next harvest. Both communities judge the other's priorities. Both are right from their perspective.
