# Millhaven

**Population:** ~3,800  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Location:** Western Accord, agricultural heartland  
**Economy:** Grain milling, flour production, baking  
**Notable:** Regional bread basket, feeds multiple cities

---

## OVERVIEW

Millhaven is milling town where grain from surrounding farms is ground into flour and processed for export. The town's waterwheels power multiple mills along the Millrace River—constant turning, constant grinding, constant production.

**The name:** "Haven for mills"—and it is. Seven large mills, dozen smaller operations, entire town economy built on processing grain.

---

## THE MILLS

**The Seven Great Mills:** Large operations owned by established families. Compete fiercely but cooperate when necessary (flooding, bandit threats, political pressure).

**The Lesser Mills:** Smaller operations, family-run, process specialty grains or handle overflow from Great Mills.

**Power source:** Water wheels on Millrace River. Reliable flow means reliable operation. Mill owners maintain river health obsessively.

**Production:** Massive. Millhaven processes grain from across Western Accord, exports flour to Lumencrest, Kingsrest, Steelwatch, beyond.

---

## THE MILL FAMILIES

Seven families control Great Mills. They're wealthy, influential, intermarried. They effectively govern town through economic power.

**The council:** Officially, town has elected council. Realistically, mill families decide everything. Council votes align with mill family interests.

**Competition:** Fierce but not destructive. Families compete for contracts, efficiency, quality. They don't undermine each other (would hurt everyone).

**Succession:** Mills pass through families. Primogeniture mostly, though competence matters. Incompetent heir might be bypassed for capable sibling.

---

## CURRENT PROSPERITY

Millhaven is doing well. Crop failures elsewhere actually benefit them (their region's harvests remained good, prices up, demand high).

**The concern:** Climate shifts causing failures elsewhere could hit here eventually. Mill families watching weather patterns carefully.

**The investment:** Mill families upgrading equipment, improving efficiency, stockpiling reserves. Prosperity provides opportunity to prepare.

---

## CULTURE

**Work-focused:** Mills run constantly. Six-day week, rest day observed. The rhythm of turning wheels shapes daily life.

**Practical:** Miller culture values function over form. Buildings are well-built but plain. Food is hearty but simple.

**Guild system:** Millers' Guild is powerful, maintains standards, trains apprentices, mediates disputes. Membership is essential for operating mill professionally.

**Community:** Despite competition, mill families support each other. When flood damaged Lesser Mill last year, Great Mills helped with repairs (after ensuring their own operations were safe, of course).

---

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

- Sabotage: Someone damaging mill equipment. Competition turned criminal or outside threat?
- Water rights: Upstream users diverting water, threatening mill operations. Negotiate or intimidate?
- Quality dispute: Flour shipment allegedly contaminated. Investigate whether accident, negligence, or deliberate.
- Succession crisis: Mill family heir died, siblings fighting over inheritance. Family hires party to investigate suspicious death.
- River threat: Something in river affecting water wheels. Environmental issue or supernatural?
