# Emberford

**Population:** ~4,000  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Location:** River crossing on the Emberfork, one mile south of [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Locations/Landmarks/Ember_Grove|Ember Grove]]  
**Economy:** River trade & ford toll, timber processing, farming  
**Notable:** Council seat for grove protection politics; House Vael timber offer (Gold Month 700 AR)

**Run-time reference:** Full menus, prices, gossip — `gm_sessions_site/emberford.html`

## OVERVIEW

Emberford is a proper town, not a village — though it still carries the feel of a place that grew up from one. It clusters on both banks of the Emberfork: the older east side (council hall, temple, inn) and the newer west bank (mill, lumber yard, tradesman's warehouse) that followed the timber trade.

The name comes from local legend: a forest fire centuries ago reached the river's edge and died. "Ember" reached "ford." Geological evidence shows fire scarring around but not within the nearby grove.

**Current tension (700 AR, Gold Month):** House Vael has presented a formal timber rights offer for the eastern managed woodland — 50 acres/year at 18 gold/acre, well above regional rates. The council deferred one week. Economic need (bridge repair, south road, stalled Accord subsidy) competes with Timberfall's cautionary history and druidic protection of the grove's spirit boundary.

## GOVERNANCE

**Council of seven elders.** [[Elder Marta Dawnbrook]] chairs — iron-grey hair, practical, genuinely conflicted about the Vael offer. Decisions are slow and argued for months; most residents consider this appropriate.

**Key council dynamics (Session 1):**
- **Fen Crane** (mill) — supports Vael offer; bridge and infrastructure pressure
- **Bren Ashfield** (farmer) — raised Green Covenant / logging incident concerns
- Thorn Oakwise attends as grove guardian; not a voting member but thirty years of presence

---

## THE FORD & BRIDGE

**Emberfork ford:** Shallow stone crossing upstream of the stone bridge. Free for foot traffic; carts pay toll on the bridge (2 copper/cart, 1 copper/horse). Elder Marta's family built the bridge three generations ago.

**Bridge proposal (background):** Lumencrest engineers proposed a permanent bridge two years ago. Would eliminate ford income — town divided on long-term growth vs. immediate economic collapse. Stalled in Heartland Accord debate; Kingsrest mediating.

## THE GROVE CONNECTION

[[Ember_Grove|Ember Grove]] (~500 acres) sits one mile north. Protected by Path of Spirits druids led by [[Thorn Oakwise]]. House Vael's offer targets **eastern managed woodland** adjacent to the grove's spirit boundary — not the grove itself, but Thorn's position: *"This isn't a timber question."*

Some residents want increased logging revenue. Druids warn of spiritual consequences. The Vael offer's eastern edge specificity is partly a probe of how close operations can approach the boundary (Unseen Guild interest — GM only).

## FAITH & CULTURE

**Six-Fold Faith:** Hearthkeeper temple on east square — Aldous Penn, 50s, broad hands, says little and means all of it.

**Seasonal tie:** Harvest Acknowledgment ceremony at grove edge each Gold Month — farmers bring cider and grain; druids return blessed herbs. Eighty-year tradition; attendance slightly down this year.
