# Whalers Rest

**Population:** ~800  
**Location:** Southwestern coast, western peninsula  
**Identity:** Whaling village, simple fishing community, hardy coastal people

---

## THE VILLAGE

Whalers Rest is a small coastal fishing village on the southwestern peninsula that specializes in whaling — one of the few Central Heartlands communities still practicing the dangerous, profitable tradition.

**The harbor:** Small, sheltered cove that protects the whaling fleet. Four dedicated whaling ships (large, reinforced, crewed by specialists) plus a dozen smaller fishing boats.

**The economy:** Almost entirely based on whaling and associated industries — processing whale products (meat, oil, bone), selling to inland markets, maintaining the specialized ships and equipment needed.

**The people:** Hardy, practical, close-knit. Whaling is dangerous and the community honors those lost to the sea. Memorials line the clifftop overlooking the harbor.

---

## WHALING TRADITION

**Why they whale:** Whale oil is valuable (lighting, lubrication), whale meat feeds the village and sells inland, whale bone has various uses (corsetry, crafts, structural elements). One successful whale hunt can fund a family for months.

**The danger:** Whaling is deadly. Ships damaged, crew killed, expeditions that don't return. Every family in Whalers Rest has lost someone to the sea.

**The fleet:**
- **The Seastorm** — Oldest ship, 40 years service, Captain Bren Wavecutter
- **The Harpoon's Call** — Fastest ship, 25 years, Captain Sera Saltborn  
- **The Deep Hunter** — Largest ship, 15 years, Captain Kael Ironhook
- **The Wandering Tide** — Newest ship, 8 years, Captain Mira Brightspray

**Hunting grounds:** The whaling ships venture into deep waters southwest of the peninsula, following seasonal whale migrations. Expeditions last 2-4 weeks. The community waits, watches, and prays.

---

## RELATIONSHIP TO LARGER SETTLEMENTS

**Sablemark:** Closest major city (northeast along coast). Whalers Rest sells most of its whale products there. Sablemark merchants buy at bulk rates and sell at premium. The village accepts this because Sablemark provides market access they couldn't achieve alone.

**Independence:** Whalers Rest is technically under Sablemark's sphere but practically autonomous. Sablemark doesn't govern them, just trades with them. The village prefers it this way.

**Hazelhaven:** Distant overland connection. Occasionally trades preserved whale meat for grain. Not a major relationship.

---

## CULTURE

**Sea reverence:** The ocean provides and the ocean takes. The community respects this balance. Ceremonies before each expedition. Memorials for those lost. Gratitude for successful returns.

**Tight community:** Everyone knows everyone. Families intermarried across generations. Collective survival mentality. An injury to one is everyone's concern.

**Practical skills:** Every resident can swim, mend nets, read weather, handle small boats. Children learn young. Those who can't adapt to the sea life usually leave.

**Storytelling:** Long winter nights filled with tales of legendary hunts, lost ships, and the one that got away. Oral tradition keeps community history alive.

---

## CURRENT SITUATION

**Declining whale populations:** The hunting grounds aren't what they were. Longer expeditions for smaller catches. The older captains remember when whales were plentiful. Now they're working harder for less.

**Younger generation leaving:** Hard life, dangerous work, uncertain future. Some young people are leaving for Sablemark or other cities where opportunities feel less deadly.

**Adaptation question:** Should the village diversify into standard fishing? Or double down on whaling expertise? The community is divided. Change feels like giving up tradition. Tradition feels like slow decline.

**Weather unpredictability:** Sea conditions becoming less reliable. Storms where they shouldn't be. Calm when there should be wind. The old patterns the captains relied on are shifting.

**Captain Bren Wavecutter** (human, 68, male) — Seastorm captain, oldest active whaler, community elder, resists change

**Captain Sera Saltborn** (human, 42, female) — Harpoon's Call captain, most successful hunter, advocates for adaptation
