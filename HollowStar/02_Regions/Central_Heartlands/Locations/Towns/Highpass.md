# Highpass

**Population:** ~2,400 | **Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Location:** Northern Central Heartlands, only safe pass through northern mountains to Desert of Glass and Skyspine volcanic flats  
**Government:** Loosely organized; Pass-Captain Verna Cole (elected by residents)  
**Identity:** Remote mountain pass town, fresh starts, frontier justice, the only safe route north

---

## THE PASS

Highpass exists because geography demanded it. The northern mountains form a natural barrier between Central Heartlands and the Desert of Glass / Skyspine volcanic flats. Most passes are deadly — rockslides, narrow ledges, weather that kills. Highpass sits at the one reliable route.

**Why it matters:** Anyone traveling overland between Central Heartlands and the northern territories must come through here. Most travelers choose sea routes (faster, safer), but those who can't afford ships, can't risk being remembered at ports, or need to move goods sea captains won't carry — they come to Highpass.

**The route:** Five-day journey through the pass in good weather. Ten days in bad. Highpass is the last resupply point heading north, first civilization heading south. The town charges fair prices and maintains the pass route (clearing rockslides, marking dangerous sections, operating emergency shelters).

## THE TOWN

**The Lower Gate** — Southern entrance, Accord-side
- Customs post (nominal — Accord and Rivermark authority is distant)
- Stables, supply shops, last chance provisions
- The Waystone Inn (largest, most reliable)

**The Upper Town** — Built on the pass slope itself
- Residential quarter carved into mountainside
- Mixed architecture (whoever built it knew what they were doing in that moment)
- Workshop district (wagon repair, metalwork, cold-weather gear)
- The Pass-Captain's office (dispute resolution, emergency coordination)

**The Northern Gate** — Northern exit, Desert-side
- Watchtower (monitors weather, approaching travelers)
- The Last Stop tavern (exactly what it sounds like)
- Guide services for hire (Desert of Glass is deadly without knowledge)

## THE PEOPLE

**Fresh starts:** Many Highpass residents are people starting over. Failed merchants, disgraced soldiers, families escaping debts, those running from something. The town doesn't ask. You prove yourself by contribution, not background.

**Outlaws (some):** Highpass attracts those avoiding legal attention. The remote location and weak authority presence helps. Most are small-time — thieves, smugglers, con artists running schemes elsewhere. The Pass-Captain's standing order: "Don't shit where you eat." Crime within Highpass is dealt with harshly. Crime elsewhere is not her problem.

**Honest workers (most):** Despite the reputation, most Highpass residents are regular people doing necessary work. Pass guides, innkeepers, stable hands, shopkeepers, families. The town functions because these people make it function.

**Travelers (constant):** ~200-300 people passing through at any given time. Merchants, adventurers, refugees, prospectors, those with reasons to avoid more official routes.

## FRONTIER JUSTICE

**The Pass-Captain:** Elected position, currently held by **Verna Cole** (human, 48, on her third 2-year term). She maintains order through reputation, fairness, and the understanding that Highpass only works if everyone trusts the system.

**The Rules:**
1. No violence in town (duels outside the gates if you must)
2. No theft from residents or travelers (we all depend on trust)
3. Disputes go to the Pass-Captain (her word is final)
4. Pay your tabs (credit is community decision)
5. Maintain the pass (everyone contributes labor when needed)

**Enforcement:** No official guard. The Pass-Captain has deputies she trusts (currently 6). Community enforcement backs them up. Break the rules badly enough, you're expelled north or south (your choice) with no supplies and no guide. The mountains handle the rest.

## ECONOMY

**Pass services** — Guide fees, route maintenance, emergency shelter operation

**Resupply trade** — Last chance provisions north, first resupply south; prices fair but firm

**Lodging** — Multiple inns, boarding houses, stables (always busy)

**Specialized gear** — Cold weather clothing, mountain climbing equipment, survival supplies

**Information brokerage** — Knowledge about routes, conditions, what's happening north/south (valuable)

**Unofficial services** — Smuggling facilitation, identity discretion, "no questions asked" wagon storage

## REPUTATION

**In the Accord cities:** Highpass is barely acknowledged. Too remote, too small, too lawless to matter. Lumencrest knows it exists. Ironhold has distant mining interest. Others ignore it.

**In Rivermark:** Similar distance. The Sovereignty knows the pass exists but has no presence there.

**Among travelers:** Essential. Highpass has a reputation for being rough but fair. You can trust the Pass-Captain. You can't trust everyone else, but that's your problem.

**Criminal networks:** Known as a good place to disappear briefly, move questionable goods, or make contacts without official scrutiny. Not a major criminal hub (too small, too isolated) but useful for specific purposes.

---

## CURRENT SITUATION

**Increasing traffic:** Something is driving more travelers through the pass lately. Verna doesn't know what — political instability south? New opportunities north? Refugees from somewhere? Traffic is up 30% over last year.

**Weather getting worse:** Pass conditions are becoming less predictable. Rockslides more frequent. Temperature swings more extreme. The five-day journey is less reliable.

**Outsider interest:** A merchant from Lumencrest visited last month, asking questions about formalizing trade routes through the pass. Verna is suspicious — formal attention usually means formal control, which kills what makes Highpass work.

**The con man problem:** Three different con artists have been working Highpass recently, each running schemes on travelers. Verna wants them gone but can't prove anything. One of them might actually be skilled enough to be useful if she could recruit them instead.
