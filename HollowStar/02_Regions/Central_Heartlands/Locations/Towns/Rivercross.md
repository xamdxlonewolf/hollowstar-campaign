# Rivercross

**Population:** ~4,500  
**Region:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Central_Heartlands_Index|Central Heartlands]]  
**Location:** Silverrun River, crossroads between regions  
**Economy:** River trade, warehouse storage, smuggling  
**Notable:** Neutral ground where questionable deals happen

---

## OVERVIEW

Rivercross sits at convergence of Silverrun River and major east-west road. This makes it natural crossroads for trade—and smuggling. The town has reputation for asking few questions as long as goods move and taxes (official and unofficial) are paid.

**Character:** Pragmatic, morally flexible, profitable. If you need something moved quietly or deals made discretely, Rivercross facilitates.

---

## THE GEOGRAPHY

**River port:** Docks handle cargo from upstream/downstream. Warehouses store goods temporarily. Merchants negotiate trades.

**Road junction:** East-west highway crosses north-south river route. Everything passes through here eventually.

**Strategic value:** Control Rivercross, control significant trade. Various powers aware of this. Town maintains independence through careful neutrality.

---

## THE UNOFFICIAL ECONOMY

**Smuggling:** Open secret. Goods avoiding Heartland Accord taxes move through here. Town council takes cut, authorities look other way.

**Black market:** Things you can't buy legally elsewhere available here. Weapons, restricted materials, information, services.

**Grey markets:** Not quite illegal, not quite legal. Questionable contracts, dubious goods, deals that work better in shadows.

**Why it persists:** Everyone benefits. Smugglers get haven. Town gets income. Accord cities get goods that technically didn't bypass taxes (wink). System works.

---

## GOVERNANCE

**Town Council:** Seven merchants elected by property owners. "Elected" is generous—council seats bought and sold like any commodity.

**Current council:** Mix of legitimate merchants and criminal bosses. Distinction is blurry. They maintain order because chaos is bad for business.

**Law enforcement:** Town guard exists. They enforce laws that matter (murder, arson, major theft). They ignore laws that don't (smuggling, black market, moral crimes).

**The balance:** Just enough law to keep order, not enough to hurt profits.

---

## NOTABLE FACTIONS

**The River Rats:** Smuggling guild, controls waterborne trafficking. Leader is "Old Corvin" (human, 68), semi-retired pirate who now coordinates instead of stealing.

**The Crossroads Compact:** Merchant association, legitimate traders who need town's discretion. They pay protection money to River Rats, maintain appearances of legality.

**Town Guard:** Not corrupt, exactly—pragmatic. Captain Maren Swiftwater (human, 44) keeps peace, ignores what she's told to ignore, intervenes when violence threatens business.

---

## VISITING RIVERCROSS

**What to expect:** Nobody asks questions. Pay your fees (official and unofficial), conduct your business, leave. Don't cause problems, nobody causes problems for you.

**Where not to go:** The Dockside warren after dark (unless you know someone). The Blind Mule tavern basement (auction house for stolen goods, invitation only).

**Who to know:**
- **Corvin** (River Rats, if you need something moved quietly)
- **Captain Swiftwater** (Town Guard, if you need protection or are trying to seem legitimate)
- **Merchant Tobin Greycoin** (Crossroads Compact, if you need legitimate cover for questionable deals)

---

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

- Hired to move contraband through town (courier mission with complications)
- Hired to intercept contraband moving through town (enforcement mission against smugglers)
- Investigation: Who's stealing from smugglers? (Someone bold enough to rob criminals)
- Protection: Escort legitimate merchant through town where everyone assumes they're smuggling
- Negotiation: Mediate dispute between River Rats and new criminal organization muscling into territory
