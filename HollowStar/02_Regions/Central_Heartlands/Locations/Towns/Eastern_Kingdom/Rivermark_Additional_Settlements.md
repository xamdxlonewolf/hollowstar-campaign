# Rivermark Sovereignty - Additional Settlements

New settlements filling the Rivermark Sovereignty territory.

---

## Silverford

**Population:** ~3,000  
**Location:** Silverfork River, south of Twinwater, river crossing  
**Identity:** River crossing town, trade waystation, connects Twinwater to coastal settlements

Silverford sits on the Silverfork River south of Twinwater where a natural ford makes crossing easiest. The town operates ferry services and provides waystation facilities for travelers and goods moving between the capital (north) and Goldport (south on coast).

**Economy:** Ferry operations, river trade, waystation services (lodging, supplies, wagon repair)

**Population:** Mixed (elven ~50%, human ~30%, others ~20%)

**Notable feature:** The town's name comes from the river ford where the water, when sunlit, shimmers silver (mix of natural clarity and distant shard influence from Goldwater upstream)

**Geographic position:** Silverfork River, between Twinwater (north) and Goldport (south), central Rivermark territory

## Goldport

**Population:** ~16,000  
**Location:** Southern coast at Silverfork River mouth, where river meets sea  
**Identity:** Major coastal city, maritime trade, Rivermark's primary port

Goldport is the Rivermark Sovereignty's largest coastal settlement and primary maritime trade hub. Built where the Silverfork reaches the sea, the city handles coastal trade, fishing, shipbuilding, and serves as the Sovereignty's main connection to seaborne commerce.

**The harbor:** Deep water port, extensive docks, shipbuilding facilities, handles both fishing fleet and trade vessels

**Districts:**
- **The Docks** — Working waterfront, shipyards, warehouses, sailor's quarter
- **Merchant's Row** — Trade offices, factor houses, counting houses, deals happen here
- **Rivergard** — Where river meets sea, administrative buildings, customs house
- **The Heights** — Residential, wealthier families, overlooks harbor
- **The Delta** — River mouth district, fishing village absorbed into growing city

**Trade role:** Goldport ships Rivermark goods (Starsilver items, Faun medicinal plants, timber, agricultural products) to coastal markets and receives imports the Sovereignty can't produce. The city connects river commerce (flowing from Twinwater and beyond) to maritime networks.

**Relationship to Twinwater:** Goldport is large enough to have political weight but recognizes Twinwater as capital. Goldport's River Voice on the Confluence Council represents maritime/commercial interests that sometimes conflict with inland priorities.

**Strategic importance:** If Rivermark joins the Accord as Sixth Seat, Goldport becomes the Accord's major eastern coastal presence. This maritime access is one reason Lumencrest cautiously supports the Sixth Seat campaign.

**Current Harbor-Master:** **Captain Vess Tidewarden** (human, 56, female) — Manages port operations, coordinates river-sea trade transfer, practical authority

**Goldport's River Voice:** **Merchant-Lord Saren Goldriver** (elf, 234, male) — Represents city on Confluence Council, wealth and commerce focus, supports Sixth Seat (trade benefits)

**Geographic position:** Southern coast, at mouth of Silverfork River where it meets the sea, southern Rivermark territory

## Timberfall

**Population:** ~2,000  
**Location:** Western Rivermark, large forest west of Goldwater Crossing (renamed from Southflow Crossing)  
**Identity:** Lumber town, forest operations, timber processing

Timberfall sits in a large forest west of Goldwater Crossing, operating as the Sovereignty's primary lumber center. The forest here provides river valley hardwoods different from northern or southern varieties — sought for fine furniture, instruments, and shipbuilding.

**The forest:** Dense hardwoods, managed sustainably by elven forest-keepers working with human loggers. Not sacred like ancient forests elsewhere but respected and maintained for long-term productivity.

**Operations:** Selective logging (not clear-cutting), timber milling, floating logs down the Goldwater to Goldwater Crossing then on to other settlements.

**Population:** Human majority (~55%), elven (~30%), mixed (~15%) — the human loggers work with elven forest knowledge

**Notable figure:** **Forest-Master Kael Ironbark** (elf, 198, male) — Manages sustainable logging, balances productivity with forest health

**Geographic position:** Western Rivermark territory, large forest west of Goldwater Crossing, forested region

## Ironsprings

**Population:** ~1,200  
**Location:** Southern Rivermark, small river flowing from southern Ironspine Mountains  
**Identity:** Small mining town, Ironspine foothills, water source

Ironsprings is a small town on a minor river (the **Ironspring Brook**) flowing from the southern Ironspine Mountains. The town mines secondary metals and processes mountain runoff that has mineral properties (hence "Ironsprings" — water with iron content).

**Mining:** Small-scale operations, tin and zinc primarily, supports Flamekeep's broader mining network

**The springs:** Natural springs with high iron content, believed to have health properties, some locals use for wellness (probably placebo but harmless)

**Population:** Mixed (human ~45%, dwarven ~35%, Drakona ~15%, others ~5%)

**Economic role:** Supplements Rivermark's mining output, provides mountain metals without requiring access to major Starsilver operations

**Geographic position:** Southern Rivermark, small river (Ironspring Brook) from southern Ironspine Mountains, foothills

## The Meadow Villages

**Combined Population:** ~2,000 (four villages, ~500 each)  
**Location:** Plains west of Meadowvale  
**Identity:** Rural agricultural cluster, Faun farming communities

West of Meadowvale in the open plains, four small villages form a loose agricultural cluster: **Greenmeadow**, **Sunfield**, **Brightwater**, and **Harvest Home**. These are primarily Faun farming communities practicing traditional agricultural methods.

**Character:** Rural, seasonal, quiet. These villages are what Meadowvale was before it grew larger. Small-scale farming, tight communities, deep connection to land cycles.

**Relationship to Meadowvale:** The villages look to Meadowvale for market access and cultural leadership but maintain independence. When Seasonal Speakers rotate from Meadowvale, they often visit these villages first.

**Combined output:** Grain, vegetables, some medicinal plants, support Sovereignty food security

**Population:** Faun majority (~75%), scattered elven/others (~25%)

**Geographic position:** Western Rivermark plains, west of Meadowvale, cluster of four small villages that can be marked together on maps but are distinct communities
