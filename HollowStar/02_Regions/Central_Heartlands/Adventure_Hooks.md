# Adventure Hooks — Central Heartlands

*The Central Heartlands are the starting region for most campaigns in this world — the political center, the economic engine, the place where continental decisions get made. The hooks here range from street-level problems in specific districts to faction moves with continental consequences. They are designed to pull players deeper into the world rather than send them away from it.*

*Level ranges are suggestions, not limits. Most hooks can scale.*

---

## LUMENCREST (10)

### 1. "What the Numbers Say"
**Hook:** Lyra Finch approaches the party at a Manufactories tavern — she's heard they're not connected to any faction. She needs the power distribution records from the Lamplight Ward grid office. Not stolen — photographed, copied, witnessed. She needs them to be real.
**Goal:** Get inside the grid office, document the distribution inequality between Radiant Core and Manufactories, get out without being identified as her people
**Complication:** The grid office has recently added a second guard shift — someone on the Council knows something is being looked for
**Twist:** The records show something beyond inequality: the Radiant Core is drawing significantly more power than it should need. Something in the Vault District is consuming it
**What this leads to:** Professor Greaves' discovery. The unauthorized Vault experiments. The Heart's actual timeline.
**Level:** 1-3

---

### 2. "Professor Greaves Needs a Courier"
**Hook:** A halfling student from the Luminarium approaches the party with a sealed document tube and very careful instructions. The professor needs this delivered to a specific address in the Starfall Basin — by hand, not by post, not by any official channel. He'll pay three times the going rate.
**Goal:** Get the document to its destination without it being intercepted
**Complication:** Two different factions are watching anyone leaving Lumencrest from the Luminarium — they know something is being moved, they don't know what
**Twist:** The document is a copy. Greaves sent the original a week ago by a different route. He's testing which route is compromised. The party is the test.
**What this leads to:** Greaves trusts the party if they succeed; gives them the real data. The faction watching the route is now aware of the party.
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 3. "The Third Informant"
**Hook:** Sera Vex hires the party for a specific job: identify which of three named people in Lyra Finch's organizing group is the third Council informant (she knows about two, needs the third confirmed)
**Goal:** Surveil the three suspects over 48 hours without being made
**Complication:** One of the three suspects is also watching for surveillance — they know someone is looking
**Twist:** The third informant isn't one of the three. It's someone not on Vex's list — someone Lyra trusts completely
**What this leads to:** Lyra's movement becomes more or less compromised depending on what the party does with this information; Vex owes them a significant favor
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 4. "The Brownout Window"
**Hook:** The party is hired — employer deliberately vague — to be in a specific Lamplight Ward building during the next brownout, recover something from a third-floor office, and leave before power returns
**Goal:** Clean extraction during a 20-40 minute power window
**Complication:** The brownout schedule has been irregular lately — the window might be shorter or might not come at all
**Twist:** The thing they're recovering is evidence that the Council has been misrepresenting the Heart's decline rate to the public — the real numbers are significantly worse
**What this leads to:** Depending on who hired them and what they do with the evidence, this can end very differently. The employer may not be who they claimed.
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 5. "Initiate Thornroot's Problem"
**Hook:** Kael Thornroot asks the party for help with something he's clearly uncomfortable asking. A member of his congregation — a Luminarium researcher — gave him something before disappearing. He doesn't understand it. He's afraid of what it means. He needs someone to help him understand it before deciding what to do.
**Goal:** Analyze the document (partial Greaves data), determine its implications, help Kael decide who — if anyone — should receive it
**Complication:** The researcher who gave it to him is missing. Not officially — they just stopped coming to services and stopped being seen. Kael has been told they're on assignment.
**Twist:** They weren't sent on assignment. They were quietly removed from the Luminarium after submitting a formal protest about experimental safety conditions. They're in the Shadowside, scared, and trying to figure out what to do.
**What this leads to:** Finding the researcher leads to more complete data and a direct witness to the unauthorized Vault experiments
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 6. "Halvor's Off-Book Problem"
**Hook:** Captain Dren Halvor asks the party to look into something he can't touch officially: a series of incidents in the Shadowside that pattern like organized pressure — businesses being leaned on, specific families being encouraged to leave — but with no obvious organizer
**Goal:** Identify who's running the pressure campaign and why
**Complication:** Two of his officers are involved — not as organizers, but as paid facilitators. He suspects this but doesn't have proof.
**Twist:** The pressure campaign is Expansionist Council members clearing the Shadowside of people who might resist forced labor conscription — a contingency they're quietly preparing for the Endless Eye operation
**What this leads to:** Evidence of the Expansionists' military planning before it becomes public; Halvor becomes a committed ally
**Level:** 3-5

---

### 7. "The Vault Incident"
**Hook:** There's been an incident in the Vault District. Not public — officially a routine maintenance issue. But three Vault workers were seen leaving by a side exit and haven't been seen since, and the Council has quietly increased Vault District guard presence
**Employer:** Multiple possible — Vex (wants to know what happened), Greaves (suspects this is connected to the unauthorized experiments), a Vault worker's family (wants to know where their person is)
**Goal:** Find out what actually happened and where the three workers are
**Complication:** The Vault District is genuinely locked down — getting in requires either a very good cover or a very good contact
**Twist:** The "incident" was a partial discharge event — the Heart released a pulse that affected the three workers. They're not dead. They're changed. The Council is trying to figure out what they've become before deciding what to do with them.
**What this leads to:** Direct evidence of Greaves' countdown theory; the three workers know things about the Heart's internal state that nobody else does
**Level:** 4-6

---

### 8. "What Null Knows"
**Hook:** The party receives a message with no sender — a meeting location, a time, and the words "bring no faction identification." The meeting is with one half of the Null partnership (the one outside Lumencrest). They want to give the party something. They want something in return.
**Goal:** Meet Null's outside partner, receive their data package, complete their requested task
**What they want in return:** Confirmation of one specific fact inside Lumencrest — something only someone physically present can verify. The inside partner can't check it without being exposed.
**Complication:** Someone else received the same message — the party isn't the only set of people Null is testing
**What this leads to:** The most complete picture of the Heart's real timeline available anywhere; a connection to the inside partner who is embedded in the Luminarium
**Level:** 4-6

---

### 9. "The Endless Eye Vote"
**Hook:** The Expansionists are two Council votes away from authorizing the military operation against the Endless Eye. Both undecided votes are people who could be moved — but both are also people being pressured by the other side. The party is hired to provide protection for one of them while they make their decision.
**Employer:** Councilor Ashton (protecting the undecided votes is her last line of defense)
**Goal:** Keep the Councilor safe from pressure (which includes both physical intimidation and blackmail attempts) through the vote
**Complication:** The Councilor they're protecting has a secret that the Expansionists already know about and are preparing to use
**Twist:** The Councilor will vote with the Expansionists anyway. Not because of the blackmail — because they genuinely believe it's the right call. The party has to decide what to do with this.
**Level:** 4-6

---

### 10. "The Heart Warning"
**Hook:** Professor Greaves gives the party his complete data and a single request: get this to the people in this city who need it. All of them. Before the Council buries it again.
**Goal:** Distribute Greaves' findings to: Lyra Finch (so workers can prepare), Initiate Thornroot (so the First Flame congregation can respond), Vex (so the Shadowside has warning), and one undecided Council member (so the vote goes differently)
**Complication:** The Council knows something is circulating. They're looking for it. Getting to four separate people without being caught requires coordination.
**Twist:** Vaela Thessin of the Somnarchs already has the data — she's been watching to see who else gets it and how they respond. She'll approach the party after they've made their deliveries.
**What this leads to:** The Somnarchs become available as allies; the Council's response to the data going public becomes the next major political event
**Level:** 5-8

## IRONHOLD (6)

### 11. "The Red Ore"
**Hook:** Three miners from the deep mine's lowest active level have been removed from duty — behavior changes, aggression, strange claims about sounds and visions. The mine foreman hires the party quietly (not through official channels) to go down and retrieve samples from where the miners were working
**Goal:** Reach level 7 of the deep mines, find the vein the miners were working, bring back samples
**Complication:** The miners' accounts disagree with each other in specific ways that suggest they weren't experiencing the same thing
**Twist:** The ore isn't causing the symptoms. Something below the ore level is. The ore is just the first physical evidence of it.
**What this leads to:** Forge-Mistress Kara Coalforge's secret samples; the deeper mystery Grimbar is sitting on
**Level:** 3-5

---

### 12. "Throk's Condition"
**Hook:** Throk Stonefist approaches the party with an unusual proposition: he'll share information about the secret Lumencrest treaty (which would be enormously valuable) in exchange for one thing — the party must go into the deep mines and come back with evidence of what's down there, delivered directly to him before anyone else
**Goal:** Deep mine investigation, specific evidence retrieval, delivery to Throk
**Complication:** Grimbar has already hired someone for a similar job. The two investigations are going to collide underground.
**Twist:** Throk's reason for wanting the evidence first isn't political — his granddaughter is one of the affected miners, and he wants to know what happened to her before he decides whether to tell anyone about it
**Level:** 4-6

---

### 13. "The Forgework Proposal"
**Hook:** Forge-Mistress Kara Coalforge needs the party to acquire shard material — specifically, material from outside Ironhold's acquisition channels, so it doesn't show up in the Clan Council's inventory records. She's not hiding the experiment. She's hiding that she's already started it.
**Goal:** Source a shard fragment from outside official channels (options: the Shadowside in Lumencrest, a specific Salt Shore salvage contact, or a Starfall Basin broker)
**Complication:** The fragment she needs has specific properties — most available fragments won't work. The party needs to actually know what they're looking for.
**Twist:** The test pieces she's already made are drawing attention. Something in the deep mines is responding to them.
**Level:** 3-5

---

### 14. "The Clan Succession"
**Hook:** High Thane Grimbar asks the party to investigate something delicate: someone has been spreading information about the secret Lumencrest treaty within Ironhold — not publicly, but to specific influential people. He doesn't know who. He can't investigate without revealing he knows the treaty's contents.
**Goal:** Identify the source of the information leak without revealing that Grimbar is the one looking
**Complication:** There are three people who could have known — and one of them is Grimbar's most trusted advisor
**Twist:** The leak is intentional, but not from any of the three expected sources. It's coming from inside Lumencrest — someone there wants the treaty public before the Endless Eye vote
**Level:** 4-6

---

### 15. "Something Below Level Nine"
**Hook:** A miner who shouldn't be talking is talking — to the party, specifically, because they've been seen around Ironhold and look like people who go places others don't. She went down to level nine two weeks ago. She came back. Her crew didn't.
**Goal:** Find out what happened to the crew; go to level nine and return with her crewmates or confirmation of what happened
**Complication:** Level nine has been officially sealed. Getting there requires either authorization (difficult) or going around it (dangerous)
**Twist:** Her crew is alive. They found something — a chamber that shouldn't exist, with contents that suggest the deep mines intersect with something pre-Hollostar. They can't find their way back out because the tunnel geometry has changed.
**Level:** 5-7

---

### 16. "Kara's Test Pieces"
**Hook:** Forge-Mistress Kara Coalforge needs the party to do something she cannot do herself: take her three test pieces out of Ironhold, have them assessed by someone with genuine shard expertise (the Luminarium, the Somnarchs, or the Starfall Basin), and bring back whatever assessment they give — without the pieces being confiscated
**Goal:** Transport the test pieces, find qualified assessment, return with findings
**Complication:** The pieces are doing something in transit — minor, inconsistent, but noticeable. They shouldn't be doing anything.
**Twist:** The Luminarium assessor recognizes what they are immediately and becomes extremely interested in who made them and how. This attention is not entirely welcome.
**Level:** 4-6

---

## KINGSREST (6)

### 17. "The Missing Harvest Reports"
**Hook:** Crown Prince Cedric hires the party as unofficial investigators — he needs the actual harvest yield reports from three noble estates in the agricultural belt, not the reports those estates submitted to the Accord
**Goal:** Visit three estates, find the real numbers (not the reported numbers), get out without the estates knowing what was found
**Complication:** Two of the three estates have already been approached by Accord inspectors — they're on guard
**Twist:** The real numbers are worse than Cedric expected, but the interesting thing isn't the numbers — it's what the estate managers have been told to report, and who told them
**What this leads to:** The food reserve discrepancy Cedric and Lyra Finch are both circling; evidence that the Accord's agricultural data is being manipulated
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 18. "The Rose Festival Murder"
**Hook:** The Rose Festival is in three weeks. The master gardener — the person responsible for preparing the famous rose gardens — was found dead in the gardens this morning. King Aldric asks for a quiet investigation: not the city guard, not the court. People who can move without creating an incident.
**Goal:** Identify the killer and motive before the Festival
**Complication:** The gardener's death looks accidental. The detail that makes it not-accidental is easy to miss and easy to remove — the party needs to find it before the scene is cleaned
**Twist:** The gardener discovered something in the garden soil — something that doesn't belong in cultivated ground. The murder is to keep that discovery quiet.
**What this leads to:** Soil fertility evidence connecting to Harlan Greaves' data; or a buried item of political significance, depending on GM direction
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 19. "Valorspire Remembers"
**Hook:** Graffiti has been appearing on Kingsrest walls — the phrase "VALORSPIRE REMEMBERS" in old script, always in places that require climbing or unusual access. The city guard can't catch whoever is doing it. Lady Rosalind Vey approaches the party: she wants to find them before the crown does. Her reasons differ from the crown's.
**Goal:** Track the graffiti artists, make contact before arrest
**Complication:** There are two separate groups doing the graffiti — one nostalgic, one with a political agenda — and they don't know about each other
**Twist:** One group is descendants of the pre-Thornhart noble families who lost status in the unification. The other is connected to a Lumencrest faction testing how Kingsrest's public responds to instability. Very different problems.
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 20. "What Lady Vey Needs"
**Hook:** Lady Rosalind Vey hires the party to confirm a specific piece of intelligence: did a particular Lumencrest Expansionist Council member meet with a Kingsrest noble at a specific location on a specific date? She needs witnesses, not hearsay.
**Goal:** Confirm or deny the meeting through investigation
**Complication:** The Kingsrest noble being investigated is someone the party has already met (if they've done other hooks) — this complicates the relationship
**Twist:** The meeting happened — but it wasn't an Expansionist recruiting Kingsrest nobility. It was a Kingsrest noble approaching the Expansionists with an offer. Kingsrest is less unified behind the crown than it appears.
**Level:** 3-5

---

### 21. "The Princess's Request"
**Hook:** Princess Helena — younger sibling to Crown Prince Cedric, 17 years old, not officially involved in anything — sends a message through an unusual channel. She needs the party to locate a person who has gone missing: her former tutor, who left the palace under unclear circumstances six months ago.
**Goal:** Find the tutor, determine what happened, report back to Helena
**Complication:** Helena's father doesn't know she's asking. Her brother doesn't either. The tutor's disappearance was not investigated officially.
**Twist:** The tutor left voluntarily after discovering something about the palace records — specifically, something about the Accord's food reserve accounting that connects to what Cedric is already investigating. They're in Lumencrest. They're scared. They don't know Helena is looking.
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 22. "The King's Unofficial Errand"
**Hook:** King Aldric approaches the party through an intermediary. He needs something done that cannot involve the crown officially: confirm that the Accord food reserve figures have been falsified, and identify who authorized the falsification. He wants to know before confronting the Accord.
**Goal:** Gather evidence of the manipulation and identify its source
**Complication:** The trail leads partly into Lumencrest's Council records, which are not publicly accessible
**Twist:** The falsification was authorized by someone who genuinely believed it was preventing panic — a well-intentioned cover-up that has become a much larger problem than the truth it was hiding
**Level:** 4-6

---

## STEELWATCH (5)

### 23. "The Deserter Company"
**Hook:** A company of Steelwatch soldiers deserted eighteen months ago — fifteen people. Not court-martialed, not pursued aggressively, just gone. They've been operating as a mercenary company in the region between Steelwatch and Kingsrest. Marshal-General Corvain wants them found and brought back — quietly, without tribunal.
**Goal:** Locate the company, make contact, determine what they want
**Complication:** The company is not doing anything illegal. They're providing genuine security services. Some of the communities they protect don't want them found.
**Twist:** They deserted because they were ordered to do something they refused — and the order came from Commander Ironside, not from Corvain. They deserted to avoid having to obey or disobey.
**Level:** 3-5

---

### 24. "The Northern Lights"
**Hook:** Steelwatch scouts have been reporting strange light patterns at the northern border — not aurora, not fire, not signal lights. Something else. Corvain needs a small team to investigate without triggering the political situation that a formal military patrol would create.
**Goal:** Reach the northern observation point, document the light source, return with findings
**Complication:** One previous scout team went to investigate and returned changed — not harmed, but different. They can't explain what they saw.
**Twist:** The lights are connected to the Frostwell situation — early indicators of the approaching Hollostar's energy affecting the northern sky, visible from Steelwatch's latitude. Something ancient in the northern terrain is responding to it.
**Level:** 4-6

---

### 25. "Sergeant Flint's Package"
**Hook:** Sgt. Mara Flint approaches the party at a non-military establishment. She has intelligence that she believes needs to reach specific people — but she can't send it through official channels without it being intercepted, and she can't deliver it personally without leaving Steelwatch. She needs couriers she can trust.
**Goal:** Deliver three separate intelligence packages to three separate recipients in three different cities
**Complication:** Each package is being looked for. Someone already knows it's moving.
**Twist:** One of the three recipients is not who they claimed to be when they established contact with Steelwatch intelligence. Flint suspects this. She included a test in that package.
**Level:** 3-5

---

### 26. "The Crucible Deaths"
**Hook:** Three soldiers have died in Steelwatch's Crucible training program in the last four months — all ruled accidents. A veteran soldier who knew all three doesn't believe it. She can't investigate officially. She approaches the party during a brief leave window.
**Goal:** Investigate the deaths, determine if they were accidents
**Complication:** The Crucible's training records are military documents — access requires authorization or creativity
**Twist:** They weren't murders. They were the result of training exercises being modified — by someone who wanted to push the soldiers harder to meet a deployment timeline. The deaths were negligent, not malicious. The person responsible is Commander Ironside, who believes the timeline is necessary and the deaths were acceptable cost.
**Level:** 3-5

---

### 27. "Corvain's Back Channel"
**Hook:** Marshal-General Corvain hires the party as an informal diplomatic channel: carry a message to Rider Commander Sera Windmane in Elarindë (Northern Expanse) and return with her reply. No official documentation. No Accord identification. Two weeks each way.
**Goal:** Reach Elarindë, deliver the message, wait for a reply, return
**Complication:** Someone at Steelwatch knows about the back channel — not its contents, but that something unofficial is happening. The party will be followed for the first part of the journey.
**Twist:** The message is a request to jointly pre-position supplies for Frostwell evacuation support — which both commanders are doing without Accord authorization, because the Accord hasn't authorized anything. The reply from Windmane includes intelligence that Corvain needs but wasn't expecting.
**Level:** 4-6

## REGIONAL (6)

### 28. "Harlan's Soil Samples"
**Hook:** Harlan Greaves needs soil samples from four specific locations — two near Kingsrest, one near Ironhold, one near Lumencrest — delivered to a Green Covenant researcher at a specific location in the Verdant Crescent without Accord documentation
**Goal:** Collect the samples, transport them across the Accord territory boundary
**Complication:** Accord border checkpoints have recently started inspecting organic materials — new regulation, officially for pest control. The real reason is unclear.
**Twist:** Two of the four sample locations are on noble estates that have recently changed their soil management. The samples from those locations will show evidence of deliberate Hollostar shard depletion — someone is mining the soil's residual shard content, and the resulting fertility loss is being blamed on natural decline
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 29. "The Starwheat Claim"
**Hook:** A small farming family has found what looks like a shard-mother lode beneath one of their fields — traces of ancient Hollostar material that explains why their land is still fertile when neighbors' is failing. Three different people are trying to acquire the land.
**Employer:** The family (they want to understand what they have before deciding anything)
**Goal:** Identify what the discovery actually is, assess its value, help the family understand their options
**Complication:** One of the three acquisition attempts is from a Lumencrest Illuminist Council member — the claim is being expedited through back channels
**Twist:** The shard-mother lode is real, but it's not a natural deposit. It's a fragment of a pre-Hollostar structure — something built, not fallen. Whoever built it was doing something with shard energy that current scholarship doesn't have a framework for.
**Level:** 3-5

---

### 30. "The Emberwood Warden"
**Hook:** A Green Covenant Warden has been blocking traffic on the King's Highway for six days — standing in the road, refusing to move, claiming the route passes through land that belongs to the forest. The city of Kingsrest wants it resolved. So does the Covenant. Neither wants the confrontation that resolving it would require.
**Goal:** Talk to the Warden, understand what they're protecting, find a resolution that doesn't require either side to admit they were wrong
**Complication:** The Warden is right about the land. The Highway was extended forty years ago without proper Covenant consultation. The legal situation is genuinely complicated.
**Twist:** The Warden isn't blocking the road about land rights — that's the official position. They're blocking it because a construction crew working the road extension hit something underground that the Warden believes should not be disturbed. The land rights dispute is cover for the real concern.
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 31. "The River Source"
**Hook:** Fish kills are happening in a Silverrun River tributary — three farms' water supply contaminated. Someone upstream is the cause. A farmers' coalition hires the party to trace it.
**Goal:** Identify the source of contamination
**Complication:** The contamination source is upstream of Ironhold's outer mining operations — politically sensitive
**Twist:** It's not the mines. It's a Lumencrest-contracted surveying operation, taking water samples and running preliminary tests for a potential Accord infrastructure project that Ironhold doesn't know about. The contamination is accidental. The secrecy is not.
**Level:** 2-4

---

### 32. "Old Mara's Price"
**Hook:** Old Mara approaches the party and tells them one specific, accurate, verifiable fact about something they've been investigating or worried about. Then she says: *"I'll tell you the thing that matters next. Show me something that surprised you."* She means it literally — an object, an observation, a piece of information they found unexpected. Then she'll talk.
**Goal:** Give Mara something genuine; receive what she considers the thing that matters next
**Complication:** The party needs to figure out what she would consider genuinely surprising — not to them, but to her, and she's seen a lot
**Twist:** What she tells them in return is not about their current investigation. It's about something they haven't started asking about yet. It will be accurate.
**Level:** Any

---

### 33. "The Accord Vote"
**Hook:** The Expansionist motion to authorize the Endless Eye military operation is coming to a vote in three weeks. Multiple factions are trying to influence it — including some who are not official Accord parties. The party becomes aware that an outside actor is attempting to forge evidence that will swing a key vote.
**Employer:** Could be Ashton (stop the forgery), Vex (determine who's behind it), Flint (whose intelligence it's based on), or nobody — the party finds out independently
**Goal:** Identify the forgery, trace it to its source, decide what to do
**Complication:** The forgery is sophisticated — distinguishing it from genuine intelligence requires expertise the party may not have
**Twist:** The evidence being forged is based on something real — something that was true but is being exaggerated and recontextualized. The genuine version would not swing the vote. The forged version would. The question is whether the underlying truth matters anyway.
**Level:** 5-8

## COMMON THREATS

**Lumencrest:** Brownout crimes (looting during power outages), Council informant networks, Shadowside criminal organizations, Vault District security forces operating outside normal jurisdiction

**Ironhold:** Deep mine incidents, clan proxy disputes, restricted-zone enforcement, caravan ambushes on the Iron Road

**Kingsrest:** Noble family disputes spilling into public space, harvest protests from agricultural belt, Accord inspector friction

**Steelwatch:** Deserter incidents, patrol disputes with Northern Expanse settlements, recruitment press-gangs (informal, unofficial, happening anyway)

**Regional:** Transformed creatures increasing in frequency near shard-depleted farmland, road banditry spiking as economic pressure increases, Veilmarket expansions into new towns

---

## REWARDS

**Money:** Standard fees from named NPCs who hire honestly; Lumencrest Council political favors (worth money if you know how to spend them); Ironhold craft goods (quality weapons, armor, tools — the real thing, not export grade)

**Information:** This is the primary currency of the Central Heartlands. What factions know, what they're hiding, who's connected to whom — all of it has value to someone. The party will accumulate information faster than money if they're paying attention.

**Access:** Introduction to Greaves, Vex, Flint, or Throk opens doors that money can't. Each is a node in a network.

**Experimental technology:** The Luminarium has things it shouldn't have made yet. Some of them work. Some of them work in unexpected ways.
