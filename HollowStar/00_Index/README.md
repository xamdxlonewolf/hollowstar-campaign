# World of Hollowstars - Campaign Setting

> *"The stars fell once. They're falling again. This time, we'll be here to witness it."*

---

## Core Concept

A world where **stars fall from the sky**, leaving behind cosmic energy and mysterious artifacts. These fallen stars—called **Hollowstars**—have shaped civilization, granted impossible power, and remain fundamentally unknowable despite centuries of study.

Now, after 700 years of silence, something is changing. The old stars are dimming. The darkness is growing. And a new star approaches the frozen north, promising transformation or catastrophe.

### Central Themes

- **Cosmic Mystery:** Hollowstars are beautiful, dangerous, and beyond mortal comprehension
- **Fading Light:** The powers that shaped civilization are failing—Lumencrest's Heart dims, the Endless Eye shrinks
- **Political Intrigue:** Factions compete for dwindling resources while the real threat looms
- **Ancient Awakening:** Long-dormant sites stir simultaneously—coincidence or pattern?
- **Hope Amid Darkness:** The world changes, but heroes can still make a difference

### Tone

- **Cosmic Horror:** 2/10 (mysterious and unsettling, not sanity-breaking)
- **Political Intrigue:** 7/10 (factional conflicts, moral grays, competing interests)
- **Wonder:** 8/10 (beautiful impossible things exist and matter)
- **Hope:** 5/10 (darkness is real, but not inevitable)

## The Hollowstars

### What Are Hollowstars?

Stars that fall from the night sky, leaving cosmic energy and transformed landscapes where they land. **No one truly understands their origin or purpose**—they are fundamentally unknowable despite centuries of study.

**Known Facts:**
- **Four recorded falls** over ~10,000 years (possibly more lost to history)
- **Only two intact Hollowstars** remain accessible: Lumencrest's Heart and the Endless Eye
- **Thousands of fragments** scattered across the world (shards, splinters, dust)
- **Last fall:** 700 years ago (created the Endless Eye in Starfall Basin)
- **Next fall:** Imminent—approaching star visible in northern sky, projected impact in Frostwell Tundra

### The Known Hollowstars

#### 1. Lumencrest's Heart (Central Heartlands)

**Status:** Intact, buried beneath city, **currently failing**

The buried second Hollostar powers Lumencrest—the only city in the world with magical "electricity." For 1,200 years it provided limitless energy. Then, 30 years ago, it began dimming.

**Current crisis:**
- Power declining 2.5% annually
- Brownouts 2-4 times per month (unthinkable previously)
- Manufacturing down 15-20%
- No one knows how to fix it

**Political impact:** Lumencrest's technological dominance is ending. The Council of Lumens is paralyzed between three factions with incompatible solutions.

**→ Details:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Locations/Cities/Lumencrest|Lumencrest]]

#### 2. The Endless Eye (Starfall Basin)

**Status:** Intact, visible as permanent lake, **currently shrinking**

Created 700 years ago when the third Hollostar fell in desert wasteland. The impact created permanent freshwater lake in impossible location—water that should not exist but does.

**Current crisis:**
- Water levels measurably decreasing (first time ever)
- Shoreline receding slowly but continuously
- Unknown if this is temporary fluctuation or permanent decline

**Political impact:** The Merchant Circle (Fairies and Ribbet who control trade) is divided on how to respond. Panic spreads among desert communities dependent on the Eye.

**→ Details:** [[02_Regions/Starfall_Basin/Locations/Cities/The_Endless_Eye|The Endless Eye]]

#### 3. The First Hollostar (Unknown Location, ~10,000 years ago)

The oldest known fall. No intact star remains—only ruins, fragments, and legends. Pre-Hollostar civilizations are proven to have existed (ruins in Frostwell, structures elsewhere). What the first star destroyed or transformed is unknown.

#### 4. The Approaching Star (Frostwell Tundra, imminent)

**Visible in northern sky:** Growing brighter nightly, trajectory calculated by astronomers and Somnarchs.

**Projected impact:** Frostwell Tundra, frozen wasteland inhabited by Nine Hearths villages, dwarves, and Drakona.

**Expected effects:**
- Rapid ice melt (Frostwell is permafrost—massive environmental change)
- New power source (everyone wants it)
- Transformation of northern ecosystem
- Unknown cosmic effects

**Political reality:** Every major faction is positioning to control or study the impact site. The race is on.

## The Growing Darkness

### Observable Phenomena

**Confirmed changes** (not superstition—measurable, real):

1. **Lumencrest's Heart dimming** - First power failures in 1,200 years
2. **The Endless Eye shrinking** - Water levels decreasing for first time
3. **Ancient sites awakening** - Multiple locations activating simultaneously:
   - Ember Cradle (Frostwell) warming after 10,000 years frozen
   - Verdant Crescent forests becoming more active
   - Isle Sleeper stirring beneath island
   - Glittering Marshes shards glowing brighter
4. **The approaching star** - Fourth Hollostar falling toward Frostwell
5. **Night sky changing** - Constellations shifting subtly, colors wrong, astronomers concerned

### Timeline Synchronization

**Critical observation:** Ancient sites began activating ~30 years ago—the same time Lumencrest's Heart started dimming.

**Theories:**
- Sites respond to approaching star (Somnarchs believe this)
- Network of connected ancient locations (Green Covenant hypothesis)
- Natural cycle reaching conclusion (Path of Spirits interpretation)
- Coincidence (most scholars' public position, few private beliefs)

**Truth:** Unknown. Each theory may hold pieces of larger puzzle.

## Religions & Belief Systems

**→ Full religious details:** [[05_Religion/Religious_Overview|Religious Overview]]

### Six-Fold Faith (Polytheistic, Hierarchical)

**Adherents:** Dominant across settled regions  
**Age:** Ancient (predates recorded history)

**The Six Gods (Three Pairs in Hierarchy):**

**Tier 1: The Primarchs (Supreme Pair)**
- **The Architect** - Order, structure, creation, law
- **The Weaver** - Fate, patterns, connections, destiny

**Tier 2: The Mediators (Middle Pair)**
- **The Sentinel** - Protection, vigilance, boundaries, defense
- **The Wanderer** - Travel, change, exploration, freedom

**Tier 3: The Intimates (Mortal-Touched Pair)**
- **The Hearthkeeper** - Home, family, community, comfort
- **The Gravewalker** - Death, memory, endings, transformation

**Worship:** Temples to all six, priests specialize, laypeople pray to relevant god for situation.

**Current interpretations of darkness:** Divine conflict? Message from gods? Test of faith? Theology divides.

**→ Details:** [[05_Religion/Six-Fold_Faith|Six-Fold Faith]]

### First Flame Faith (Monotheistic)

**Adherents:** Growing rapidly (~300 years old)  
**Age:** Newer religion gaining converts

**Belief:** One god (Eternal Flame) with six aspects corresponding to Six-Fold gods.

**Philosophy:** Unity over division. All divine power flows from single source. Six-Fold worships aspects, not source.

**Current interpretations:** Broken covenant? Test of devotion? Flame dimming calls faithful to reignite it?

**→ Details:** [[05_Religion/First_Flame_Faith|First Flame Faith]]

### Path of Spirits (Animistic, Nature-Focused)

**Adherents:** Rural communities, traditional peoples  
**Age:** Ancient (possibly oldest surviving faith)

**Belief:** Spirits inhabit natural world (trees, rivers, stones, animals). Honor spirits, maintain balance, live harmoniously.

**Practice:** Druids mediate between people and spirits. Seasonal ceremonies. Ancestor veneration integrated.

**Current interpretations:** Natural cycle reaching culmination? Death of old spirits, birth of new? Balance disrupted—must restore it?

**→ Details:** [[05_Religion/Path_of_Spirits|Path of Spirits]]

### Forge-Father Faith (Ancient Dwarven)

**Adherents:** Cinderhome dwarves primarily  
**Age:** Ancient (nearly extinct elsewhere)

**Belief:** The Forge-Father (first dwarf, teacher of metalworking, ancestor-deity) taught dwarves craft and honor.

**Philosophy:** Craft is prayer. Quality honors ancestors. Fire and stone are sacred.

**Unique status:** Cinderhome is last community maintaining this faith actively. Everywhere else, dwarves adopted Six-Fold or other religions.

**→ Details:** [[02_Regions/Desert_of_Glass/Locations/Cities/Cinderhome|Cinderhome]] (includes Forge-Father Faith description)

### Accord Reckoning (Primary System)

**Adoption:** 400 years ago with Heartland Accord formation  
**Year 0:** Third Hollostar fall creating Endless Eye (700 years ago)  
**Current year:** 700 AR

**Structure:**
- 12 months × 30 days
- 5-6 festival days outside regular calendar
- Total: 365-366 days

**Months:** Firstmonth, Thawmonth, Seedmonth, Bloommonth, Greenmonth, Sunpeak, Harvestmonth, Heatmonth, Goldmonth, Fallmonth, Darkmonth, Frostmonth

**Usage:** Governance, trade, agriculture, official record-keeping

### Spirit Moons (Alternative System)

**Age:** Ancient (predates known civilization)  
**Adherents:** Path of Spirits, Green Covenant, traditional communities

**Structure:**
- 13 months × 28 days (lunar cycles)
- Cyclical time view (years not numbered sequentially)
- Dating by natural events and relationships

**Philosophy:** Time is circle, not line. Patterns repeat. Seasons matter more than years.

## Unique Features & Wonders

### Cindrath the Ancient

**Location:** The Furnace volcano, Desert of Glass  
**Type:** Ancient red dragon (only known ancient dragon)  
**Age:** Millennia (possibly predates first Hollostar)

**Personality:** Too ancient for good/evil to matter. Patient to point of appearing bored. Ruthlessly fair in bargains.

**What she offers:** Knowledge (witnessed millennia), power (blessings/curses), prophecy (pattern recognition), artifacts (from dead civilizations).

**What she wants:** Items of interest, services mortals can perform, entertainment from watching outcomes.

**Drakona connection:** Many Drakona believe she's their ancestor/creator. She doesn't clarify but treats them gently.

**→ Details:** [[02_Regions/Desert_of_Glass/Locations/Cities/Ashenveil|Ashenveil]] (includes Cindrath section)

### The Escaped Clanks (Ember Refuge)

**Location:** Desert of Glass, hidden volcanic valley  
**Population:** ~600 sentient constructs

**Story:** Lumencrest creates magical constructs (Clanks) for labor. Some achieved self-awareness, escaped, established free community.

**Philosophy:** Peaceful idealists seeking recognition as people, not property.

**Internal division:** Moderates want negotiation. Militants believe violence inevitable. Tension building.

**Lumencrest position:** Escaped Clanks are stolen property. Return them.

**Galactic reality:** Too far, too dangerous to enforce. Unofficial negotiations happening but public demand continues.

**→ Details:** [[02_Regions/Desert_of_Glass/Locations/Towns/Ember_Refuge|Ember Refuge]]

### The Glittering Marshes Shards

**Location:** Glittering Marshes  
**Density:** Highest concentration of Hollostar fragments on continent

**Effects:**
- Marsh glows at night (bioluminescent ecosystem catalyzed by shard-light)
- Organisms near shards grow healthier, more potent
- Deep Bloom fungi integrated shards into mycelium (unique case)

**Strategic value:** Everyone wants access. Marsh residents refuse. Shards are part of ecosystem—removing them causes damage.

**Current crisis:** Lumencrest increasingly desperate for fragments to study. Green Covenant warns against exploitation.

**→ Details:** [[02_Regions/Glittering_Marshes/The_Shards|The Shards]]

### The Awakening Ancient Sites

**Synchronized timeline:** All began activating ~30 years ago

**Active sites:**
- **Ember Cradle (Frostwell):** Warming after 10,000 years frozen, heat from below, Spine Stones emerging
- **Verdant forests:** Trees more active, spirits more present, ancient awareness stirring
- **Isle Sleeper:** Rhythmic tremors, heat vents, interior forest dying
- **Glittering Marshes:** Shards glowing brighter, ecosystem changes

**Connection theory:** Sites are networked, responding to same trigger (approaching star? Natural cycle?).

**Implication:** Understanding one helps understand others. Global phenomenon, not local anomalies.

## History & Timeline

### The Age Before (Unknown to ~10,000 years ago)

**Evidence:** Pre-Hollostar ruins in Frostwell and elsewhere prove civilization existed before first known star-fall.

**Knowledge:** Almost none. Ruins contain architecture unlike anything built since. No organic remains. Languages unknown.

**Implication:** First Hollostar destroyed or transformed whatever came before.

### First Hollostar Fall (~10,000 years ago)

**Impact:** Unknown location  
**Effects:** Destroyed previous civilization, created current world

**Evidence:** Ruins scattered across Frostwell. Frostvault (buried complex) contains functioning ancient mechanisms.

**Fragments:** Possibly scattered everywhere. Hard to distinguish first-fall fragments from later ones.

### Second Hollostar Fall (~1,200 years ago)

**Impact:** Central Heartlands (created Lumencrest's Heart)  
**Effects:** Buried intact star provides power to Lumencrest

**History:** Discovered buried, excavated partially, tapped for energy. City built around it, grew to dominance through technological advantage.

**Current status:** Failing for first time. Civilization built on it faces existential crisis.

### Third Hollostar Fall (700 years ago)

**Impact:** Starfall Basin desert (created the Endless Eye)  
**Effects:** Permanent freshwater lake in impossible location

**History:** Desert wasteland transformed overnight. Communities formed around water. Merchant Circle developed, controlled trade.

**Current status:** Shrinking for first time. Water levels measurably decreasing. Crisis looms.

### Fourth Hollostar Fall (Imminent)

**Projected impact:** Frostwell Tundra  
**Visible:** Growing brighter in northern sky nightly

**Expected effects:**
- Rapid permafrost melt
- Ecosystem transformation
- New power source
- Unknown cosmic phenomena

**Political reality:** Every faction positioning for control or study. Race to impact site underway.

## For the Game Master

**→ Full GM Notes (philosophy, clocks, faction tracker, secrets):** [[08_GM_Notes/GM_Notes_Index|GM Notes Index]]

### Keeping Mystery Alive

**What players can discover:**
- Hollostar effects and properties (measurable, studiable)
- Historical patterns (documented falls, effects)
- Ancient site connections (evidence gathering)
- Factional motivations (political intrigue)

**What should stay mysterious:**
- Ultimate origin of Hollowstars
- True nature of awakening entities
- Whether darkness can be stopped
- What fourth fall will bring

**Balance:** Players should feel they're making progress understanding world while core mysteries remain tantalizingly out of reach.

### Adapting to Player Choices

**The approaching star:** Timeline can accelerate or delay based on player actions. Study might predict impact better. Interference might change trajectory. Unknown if it can be prevented.

**Factional outcomes:** No predetermined "right" answer. Lumencrest might be saved or fall. Tide King might unify coast or be resisted. Four Houses might resolve Caldren crisis peacefully or violently. Player choices matter.

**Ancient awakenings:** Sites respond to investigation, interference, or being left alone differently. Players shape what emerges.

### Key Themes to Emphasize

- **Choice matters** - No predetermined outcome
- **Moral complexity** - Most factions have valid concerns
- **Wonder and danger** - Hollowstars are both beautiful and threatening
- **Connections** - Seemingly separate events might be linked
- **Hope persists** - Darkness grows but isn't inevitable
