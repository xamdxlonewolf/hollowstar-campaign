# World of Hollowstars - Campaign Setting

## Core Concept

A world where **stars fall from the sky**, leaving behind cosmic energy and mysterious artifacts called **Hollowstars**. These fallen stars have shaped civilization, granted impossible power, and now—after 700 years of silence—something is changing. The darkness is growing, the old stars are dimming, and a new star approaches.

### Central Themes
- **Encroaching Darkness:** A creeping sense that something is coming, already here in places, growing stronger
- **Beautiful Unknowable:** Hollowstars are wondrous, dangerous, and beyond mortal comprehension
- **Fading Hope:** Communities band together even as the world shifts beneath them
- **Political Intrigue:** Factions compete for power and resources while the real threat looms

### Tone Guidelines
- **Cosmic Horror:** 2/10 (mysterious and unsettling, not sanity-breaking)
- **Political Intrigue:** 7/10 (factional conflicts, moral grays, competing interests)
- **Hope:** 5/10 (darkness is real, but heroes can make a difference)

## The Hollowstars

### What Are Hollowstars?

Stars that fall from the night sky, leaving cosmic energy where they land. No one truly understands their origin or purpose—they are **unknowable, beautiful, and dangerous**.

**Key Facts:**
- Last recorded fall: **700 years ago** (created The Endless Eye)
- Only **two intact Hollowstars** are known to exist
- **Shards** are scattered across the world, sources of power and conflict
- **No living person** has witnessed a fall—only ancient, unreliable records exist

### The Two Intact Hollowstars

#### 1. Lumencrest's Heart (Central Heartlands)
- Powers the entire city with magical "electricity"
- Only place in the world with this technology
- **Currently dimming** for the first time—brownouts, power spikes, failing systems
- Political and technological center of the known world

#### 2. The Endless Eye (Starfall Basin)
- Created 700 years ago when the last star fell
- Endless water source in the middle of an eastern desert
- Controlled by Fairies and Ribbet folk (frog-people)
- **Recently began shrinking**—first recorded decrease, small but alarming
- Shards in the area grant temporary water-breathing

### Hollostar Effects

**Energy Transformation:**
- Creatures exposed to Hollostar energy become warped, mutated
- Physical changes: crystalline growths, bioluminescence, unnatural behaviors
- Examples: Starblind Prowlers, Glimmerfish, Glasswyrms

**Uses:**
- Power source (Lumencrest's grid)
- Environmental transformation (The Endless Eye's lake)
- Magical enhancement (shards grant temporary abilities)
- Unknown potential (feared and sought after)

## The Growing Darkness

### Observable Signs

**Confirmed Phenomena:**
1. **Lumencrest dimming** - First power failures in living memory
2. **The Endless Eye shrinking** - Water levels measurably decreasing
3. **Stars looking wrong** - Constellations shifting, colors off, something approaching
4. **Approaching Hollostar** - Astronomers and mystics agree: a new star is falling

**Campaign Element:**
- **Countdown mechanic** - Star will fall in Frostwell Tundra
- **Player actions matter** - Can accelerate or delay the fall
- **If it lands:** Rapid melting of ice, transformation of the region, new power source
- **Everyone wants it** - Factions positioning, race for control

### What People Believe

Different faiths interpret the darkness differently:
- **Six-Fold Faith:** Divine conflict or message
- **Monotheists:** Test of faith or broken covenant
- **Nature Spirits:** Natural cycle or death of the stars
- **Scholars:** Unknown phenomenon requiring study
- **Common folk:** Terror, hope, confusion

**Truth:** Unknown. Each belief may hold pieces of a larger puzzle.

## Factions & Organizations

### The Council of Lumens (Lumencrest)

**Type:** Republican council, aristocratic families + technocrats

**Goal:** Maintain power, solve the dimming crisis, expand influence

**Power Base:** Lumencrest, Central Heartlands

**Internal Factions:**

#### The Conservators
- **Belief:** "Shut down Hollostar experiments before we destroy it completely"
- **Leaders:** Old aristocratic families, traditionalists
- **Support:** Commoners suffering from power rationing
- **Tactics:** Political maneuvering, appeals to caution

#### The Illuminists
- **Belief:** "We need MORE power—find shards, study harder, the answer is in the stars"
- **Leaders:** Technocrats, inventors, ambitious scholars
- **Support:** Manufacturers dependent on power, academics
- **Tactics:** Sponsoring expeditions, aggressive research, public spectacles

#### The Expansionists
- **Belief:** "The Endless Eye has a working star. Negotiate... or take it."
- **Leaders:** Military families, merchant princes
- **Support:** Those who see opportunity in crisis
- **Tactics:** Military buildup, diplomatic pressure, espionage

**Stance on Darkness:** Divided, but all believe the solution involves Hollowstars

**Hooks:**
- Players hired by one faction against another
- Escort expeditions to find shards
- Investigate sabotage in the Manufactories
- Diplomatic mission to The Endless Eye

---

### The Merchant Circle (The Endless Eye)

**Type:** Trade consortium, Ribbet elders + Fairy circle-leaders

**Goal:** Protect the lake, maintain trade routes, preserve their way of life

**Power Base:** Starfall Basin, The Endless Eye

**Internal Factions:**

#### The Traditionalists (mostly Fairy)
- **Belief:** "Never touch the star, respect the gift"
- **Leaders:** Fairy circle-elders, druidic advisors
- **Support:** Long-time residents, nature-worshipers
- **Tactics:** Ritual protection, political obstruction, appeals to tradition

#### The Pragmatists (mostly Ribbet)
- **Belief:** "Study it NOW before it's too late"
- **Leaders:** Ribbet merchants, underwater city councils
- **Support:** Younger generation, those who see Lumencrest's fate
- **Tactics:** Calling for research, forming study groups, seeking outside experts

#### The Profiteers
- **Belief:** "Sell access to the star, get rich while we can"
- **Leaders:** Merchant captains, unscrupulous traders
- **Support:** Opportunists, desperate families
- **Tactics:** Secret deals, black market shard sales, courting Lumencrest agents

**Stance on Darkness:** Terrified—their entire existence depends on the lake

**Hooks:**
- Players hired to mediate internal disputes
- Protect the lake from Lumencrest agents
- Investigate who's stealing shards
- Escort refugees if the lake fails

## Religions & Belief Systems

### The Six-Fold Faith (Hextheism)

**Dominant Religion:** Central Heartlands, Northern Expanse, widespread

**Core Belief:** Six gods in three hierarchical tiers govern existence

**Structure:** Three tiers of divine pairs, representing fundamental forces

---

#### Tier 1: The Primarchs (Supreme Pair)

The original and most powerful, representing cosmic fundamentals.

##### The Architect
- **Domain:** Order, Structure, Civilization, Law
- **Symbol:** Compass and square
- **Followers:** Lawmakers, builders, city-dwellers, scholars
- **Values:** Progress, reason, stability, planning
- **Prayers for:** Justice, construction projects, contracts, governance
- **Portrayed as:** Masculine-coded, distant but watchful, patient, exacting

##### The Weaver
- **Domain:** Chaos, Change, Wilderness, Transformation
- **Symbol:** Tangled threads or wild vines
- **Followers:** Travelers, artists, rangers, those embracing change
- **Values:** Freedom, adaptability, natural law, creativity
- **Prayers for:** Safe journeys, inspiration, healing, weather
- **Portrayed as:** Feminine-coded, ever-present, mercurial, vital

**Theological Tension:** Are they in conflict or balance? Both. Always.

---

#### Tier 2: The Mediators (Middle Pair)

Born from or serving the Primarchs, more directly involved with mortals.

##### The Sentinel
- **Domain:** Protection, Honor, Justice, Warfare
- **Symbol:** Shield with a flame
- **Followers:** Warriors, guards, judges, protectors
- **Values:** Courage, duty, righteousness, sacrifice
- **Prayers for:** Victory, protection, fair judgment, courage
- **Portrayed as:** Gender-neutral or both, eternally vigilant, stern but fair
- **Connection:** Enforces The Architect's law with The Weaver's adaptability

##### The Wanderer
- **Domain:** Freedom, Trade, Secrets, Travel
- **Symbol:** Road winding into stars
- **Followers:** Merchants, spies, adventurers, tricksters
- **Values:** Curiosity, independence, cleverness, exchange
- **Prayers for:** Profitable deals, safe travel, hidden knowledge, escape
- **Portrayed as:** Gender-fluid, laughing, cloaked, impossible to pin down
- **Connection:** Bridges The Architect's structure with The Weaver's chaos

**Theological Tension:** Are they servants or children? Partners or rivals? Yes.

---

#### Tier 3: The Intimates (Mortal-Touched Pair)

Closest to mortal concerns, most commonly prayed to.

##### The Hearthkeeper
- **Domain:** Home, Family, Healing, Community
- **Symbol:** Hearth flame
- **Followers:** Parents, healers, farmers, homemakers
- **Values:** Love, compassion, nurturance, belonging
- **Prayers for:** Childbirth, healing, harvest, domestic harmony
- **Portrayed as:** Feminine-coded, warm, present in every home, gentle but fierce

##### The Gravewalker
- **Domain:** Death, Memory, Transition, Ancestors
- **Symbol:** Lantern in darkness
- **Followers:** Morticians, historians, the bereaved, the dying
- **Values:** Acceptance, remembrance, guidance, passage
- **Prayers for:** Peaceful death, honoring the dead, closure, transition
- **Portrayed as:** Masculine-coded, quiet, patient, neither frightening nor comforting—just present

**Theological Tension:** Life and death are inseparable. Both are sacred.

**Recent Months:**
- Lumencrest begins dimming (first power failures)
- The Endless Eye measures first shrinkage
- Astronomers confirm: a star is approaching
- Factions mobilize, panic spreads
- The darkness is no longer a feeling—it's measurable

**Campaign Timeframe:**
- Countdown to Hollostar fall in Frostwell Tundra
- Player actions can accelerate or delay
- Factions race to position themselves
- The world holds its breath

## Campaign Hooks & Starting Points

### Starting Scenarios

#### 1. The Dimming City
**Location:** Lumencrest
**Hook:** Players hired by one Council faction to [investigate sabotage / find shards / spy on rivals]
**Tone:** Political intrigue, urban investigation, moral gray areas
**Stakes:** The city's survival, factional power struggles

#### 2. The Shrinking Lake
**Location:** The Endless Eye
**Hook:** Players are travelers stranded when water rationing begins; must navigate internal conflicts
**Tone:** Cultural exploration, environmental mystery, community tension
**Stakes:** An entire region's survival, competing philosophies

#### 3. The Seeker Expedition
**Location:** Any region
**Hook:** Players join (or are hired to stop) a Starfall Seeker expedition to find an ancient fall site
**Tone:** Adventure, exploration, moral questions about knowledge
**Stakes:** Powerful shard discovery, what will be done with it

#### 4. The Northern Lights
**Location:** Frostwell Tundra
**Hook:** Players in the north when strange phenomena begin; locals fear the approaching star
**Tone:** Frontier survival, mystery, cosmic wonder/dread
**Stakes:** First witnesses to the fall, shaping what happens next

#### 5. The Green Resistance
**Location:** Verdant Crescent
**Hook:** Players caught between Green Covenant extremists and Lumencrest agents
**Tone:** Moral complexity, wilderness survival, guerrilla warfare
**Stakes:** Environmental protection vs. desperate need for power

## Notes for the GM

### Daggerheart Integration

**Hope & Community:**
- Factions can be allies, not just enemies
- Communities band together in crisis
- Victories should feel meaningful even if small
- The darkness is real, but so is resistance

**Collaborative Storytelling:**
- Let players define their characters' relationships to Hollowstars
- Allow player backgrounds to introduce new factions or perspectives
- Encourage players to theorize—some might be right
- Their choices genuinely shape the world

**Mechanics Notes:**
- Hollostar shards can grant temporary abilities (use Hope/Fear mechanics)
- Transformed creatures use Fear/Stress
- Political intrigue benefits from social encounters
- Countdown to star fall creates urgency

### Keeping Mystery Alive

**Don't Answer Everything:**
- Some questions have no definitive answer
- Contradictory evidence keeps players guessing
- NPCs speculate; they don't know either
- The truth can be mosaic, not monolithic

**Reveal Through Play:**
- Let players discover lore through exploration
- Ancient texts are fragmentary and biased
- Witnesses to the same event tell different stories
- Magic and miracles blur the line between belief and reality
