# WIZARD — CHARACTER KNOWLEDGE BRIEFING

### What You Know, What You Think You Know, and What You Learned on the Road

---

_You are sixteen years old. You have spent your entire life in the Endless Eye and its immediate surroundings. You have read extensively. You have studied formally. You have very little direct experience of the world outside the place you grew up. This document covers what you actually know — which in some areas is considerable, and in others is almost entirely academic._

_The distinction matters. Things you know from books are marked differently from things you have seen yourself. Pay attention to which is which._

# WHO YOU ARE

## The Basics of Your Own Heritage

---

- You are a Faerie-Ribbet hybrid. Both ancestries have been present at the Endless Eye for all 700 years of its existence. Unions between Faerie and Ribbet communities are not common but they are not unusual either. You are the product of a place that has always been cosmopolitan.
- Faerie traits you carry: the capacity for flight or near-flight depending on your wing development, a natural affinity for lightweight construction and aerial spaces, a tendency toward bioluminescent expression under emotional stress that most Faerie learn to control in childhood. You have not entirely controlled it.
- Ribbet traits you carry: partial aquatic adaptation — you can breathe underwater for longer than a human but not indefinitely the way a full Ribbet can. You are comfortable in and around water in a way that feels instinctive rather than learned. Your skin responds to the Endless Eye's glow in ways that are subtle and that you have mostly learned to ignore.
- At sixteen you are young even by the standards of shorter-lived peoples. You know this. You are also, by any honest measure, academically well-prepared for someone your age. These two facts coexist.

# THE ENDLESS EYE

## Home — What You Know From Living There

---

### The City Itself

- Population roughly 20,000 — 15,000 on the surface, approximately 5,000 in the underwater city of Lakehaven below.
- The surface city is built on the western and eastern shores of the lake in pale sandstone and lake-stone. Flat roofs, interior courtyards, water channels running through the streets constantly. The Fairy communities on the eastern shore have added aerial walkways and hanging gardens. You grew up partly in those elevated spaces.
- The city is cosmopolitan in a way that is genuinely unusual — all ancestries, all faiths, all backgrounds are present and economically tolerated. "What do you trade?" is the standard greeting because trade is the city's entire identity and reason for existing.
- The market runs on a morning-and-evening schedule because midday is too hot. The Great Exchange is the covered permanent market at the center. Price boards updated daily. You have been to the market more times than you can count.
- The Scholar's Quarter has a Starfall Seeker office, a Lumencrest-affiliated research station, and a Guiding Light denomination temple. You spent a significant portion of your childhood in the Scholar's Quarter. The researchers there know you by name and by the specific questions you ask, which they consider both promising and occasionally exhausting.

---

### Lakehaven — The Underwater City

- Directly below the surface city, carved from the lake floor bedrock and expanded over 700 years. The Ribbet have lived here continuously since the lake formed.
- You can visit. Your partial Ribbet adaptation means you can descend to the upper sections without assistance. You cannot reach the deeper residential areas without effort and you cannot reach The Deeps at all without equipment you do not have.
- Lakehaven is lit by bioluminescent organisms cultivated over centuries and by the faint upward light from the Hollostar far below. The quality of light there is different from anything on the surface — diffuse, blue-green, moving with the currents. You find it calming.
- The Ribbet culture below is slower, more contemplative, more deliberate than surface culture. Bioluminescent skin markings pulse with emotion and intent in ways that surface-dwellers cannot read. It is polite for Ribbet to dim their markings around surface guests — the equivalent of lowering your voice.
- The Pearl Tradition: each Ribbet cultivates a single pearl over their lifetime, given to their closest person at death. Trade pearls are separate, commercially farmed. Knowing this distinction matters socially.

---

### The Merchant Circle — Who Actually Runs Things

- Nine-seat governing council. Three merchant seats elected by registered traders on a wealth-weighted system. Two Ribbet seats, two Fairy seats, one rotating Wanderer seat representing desert tribes and passing traders, and one Scholar seat appointed by research institutions.
- The Scholar seat is currently held by **Researcher Pell Ashwick**, a human of 41 who has been trying to gain expanded Deeps access for years and keeps being denied. He is frustrated in a specific and well-documented way that you have observed personally.
- Three internal factions:
    - **Traditionalists** — led by Elder Suu (Ribbet, 89), Wingspeaker Aelindra (Fairy, 156), and Merchant Liria Dawnpetal (Fairy, 203). Position: the star gave us the lake, it will sustain it, do not disturb The Deeps.
    - **Pragmatists** — led by Senior Merchant Voss Tanakar (human, 54) and Voice Kessa (Ribbet, 44). Position: the star is changing, we must understand it, expand research access carefully.
    - **Profiteers** — Merchant Brek (Ribbet, 31) and Thornwing (Fairy, 78). Position: access to The Deeps is valuable, sell permits. The most controversial faction. Accused of taking money from Lumencrest interests. This accusation is not unfounded.
- The Circle enforces trade law strictly. False weights: fine and reputation damage. Contract breach: debt enforcement and blacklisting. Theft from traders: exile. Unauthorized Deeps access: exile, no exceptions.
- Circle Guards number about 150 active, deliberately mixed ancestry, well paid.

---

### The Deeps and the Hollostar — What You Know and What You Do Not

- The Hollostar rests 400-600 feet below the lake surface, half-buried in 700 years of sediment accumulation. Roughly 15 feet across. It emits a faint blue-white light from its cracks. It is the reason the lake exists, the reason the city exists, the reason you exist in the form you do.
- Only natural water-breathers can reach it safely. Even Ribbet need training for the depth and pressure. Merchant Circle access permits are tightly controlled. Authorized access is limited to Ribbet elders, specific researchers, and Circle council members.
- What you know from academic sources: Hollowstars are objects of cosmic origin that fell from the sky at intervals across history. The Endless Eye's fell approximately 700 years ago. The impact created the lake basin and the lake itself. The Hollostar's ongoing presence keeps the lake filled and the surrounding environment viable. Without it, the desert reclaims everything.
- What you know from being observant: the glow of the lake at night is slightly less than it was when you were very young. You noticed this before you understood what it might mean. You have not said this to anyone in authority because you are sixteen and uncertain enough about your own memory that you did not want to be wrong in public.
- What you have been officially told: the lake is stable, the Hollostar is active, access restrictions exist for safety and preservation reasons. This is what the Circle's public statements say. It is not all of what is true.
- **What you know that others do not:** In the two years before you left, certain deep lake research documents in the Scholar's Quarter archive were reclassified. They were there — you had seen their titles in the catalogue — and then they were not available. No public explanation was given. Organizations do not classify data about situations they are managing well. You wrote this observation in your journal the night you noticed it. You have been thinking about it since.

---

### What the Endless Eye Taught You About People

- "What do you trade?" is not just a greeting. It is an assessment. Every interaction in the Endless Eye has economic logic underneath it. This is not cynicism — it is the culture. Understanding what someone wants to get and what they are willing to give tells you almost everything you need to know about them.
- Reputation matters more than wealth. A wealthy trader who breaks contracts is finished in the Endless Eye. A less wealthy trader who is reliable will always find partners. This is enforced by the Circle and reinforced by culture.
- The distinction between what is said publicly and what is known privately is significant and maintained carefully. The Circle does this. The Ribbet elders do this. Most senior people you have met do this. You are learning to do it too.
- Hospitality is a business practice at the Endless Eye, not a personal virtue — but the result is genuine tolerance and genuine welcome, and you do not think the origin of a good thing makes it less good.

# THE STARFALL BASIN

## What You Knew Before You Left, and What You Saw on the Road

---

### The Basin Generally

- The Starfall Basin is roughly 400 miles east-west and 300 miles north-south. Desert, but survivable desert — caravans cross it regularly on established routes. Less extreme than the Desert of Glass to the west, which is not the same as easy.
- Water sources exist if you know where they are. The tribes know. Outsiders who try to cross without a guide or without tribal contact die at a rate that is considered normal and not particularly avoidable.
- Temperature: midday in summer hits 100-120 degrees. Night travel is standard. Morning and evening are the productive hours. This is not abstract knowledge for you anymore — you walked in it.

---

### The Tribes — What You Knew and What You Observed

**The Redwall Katari (~1,200)**

- Canyon navigators in the northwestern basin. Traditional Katari culture, oral history, seasonal movement. Trade partners with the Endless Eye but resistant to Circle authority. You knew them by reputation; you did not travel through their canyon territory.

**The Saltwalkers (~800)**

- Southern basin, salt flats. They know which areas of the flats are solid and which will kill you — sinkholes under the salt crust, unstable sections that look identical to safe ones. Their trade is salt and rare minerals. Their real value is knowing where the ground is.
- Your group passed through Saltwalker territory. Your guide arranged passage. What this involved was a negotiation you were not part of, conducted in a language you do not speak, that resulted in a Saltwalker escort for two days across the southern flats. The escort did not speak to you. They watched the ground constantly. You watched them watching the ground and learned more about where not to step from observation than you could have from any description.

**The Starwatchers (~300)**

- Western basin near the Glass Flats border. Astronomical observation sites, long-term celestial record keeping. They navigate by stars and track celestial patterns with an accuracy that professional researchers have described as extraordinary.
- Your group passed near their territory. You did not make contact but you saw one of their observation structures from a distance — stone, low profile, oriented at a specific angle to the sky that your academic training told you corresponded to a specific constellation. You made a note of the orientation. You have been thinking about what they might have recorded about the approaching star and whether that record exists in a form you could access.

**The Windrunners (~500)**

- Northern basin, open hardpan. Speed and endurance. You did not encounter them.

---

### Lucky Shen's Outpost

- Exists at the edge of the Glass Flats. A gambling operation run by a human of indeterminate age who has accumulated an unusual collection of things he has won from travelers. One of them is a compass that does not point north. He declined to say what it points to. He smiled when he declined.
- You did not gamble. You sat in a corner and listened for four hours and filled approximately six pages of notes. This was the correct decision.
- The people passing through Lucky Shen's are a cross-section of everyone moving between the basin and the western regions. Starfall Seekers, Drakona families, merchants, Frostwell refugees who had gone the wrong direction, people whose purpose was not stated. The quality of information available in a room where everyone is slightly off their guard is significant.
- Lucky Shen himself said something to you when you were leaving that you wrote down verbatim: _"Everyone who comes through here is running toward something or away from something. The ones who don't know which are always the most interesting."_ You are still deciding which category you are in.

---

### The Road — Basin to Highpass

- You traveled with a troubadour group — performers, a guide, and assorted travelers joining the route for safety in numbers. This is the standard way a young person from the Endless Eye makes this crossing without dying.
- The route hugged the southern mountain border to use the rock formations for shade and shelter during the hottest hours. Slower than the direct route. Significantly more survivable.
- The Glass Flats are visible from this route but you did not enter them. What you could see from the edge: the surface is not glass in the material sense — it is fused sand, created by the heat and force of a Hollostar impact so old that the ruins beneath are unrecognizable as ruins. The academic term is "impact vitrification." Seeing it in person is different from reading the term.
- You reached Highpass with the group intact. The Pass-Captain there — a woman named Verna Cole — checked papers, assessed the group, and passed you through without difficulty. She had the manner of someone who has seen every kind of traveler and learned to make accurate assessments quickly.

# HIGHPASS AND THE ROAD TO THE EMBER GROVE

## What You Saw Coming Into the Heartlands

---

### Highpass

- A mountain pass settlement controlling the main route between the basin and the Central Heartlands. Pass-Captain Cole runs it practically and without apparent corruption, which you have learned is not universal among people in her position.
- The traffic through Highpass was heavier than your guide considered normal. She said this without elaboration, which you took to mean she thought you should draw your own conclusions. The conclusion you drew: more people are moving than usual, and they are mostly moving south and west, away from the north.
- The waystation tavern had a density of information that rewarded the same approach as Lucky Shen's: sit, order something inexpensive, listen. You have been refining this skill.

### Stonebridge

- The mountain pass town connecting Rivermark Sovereignty territory to the western Central Heartlands. Mixed population — roughly equal human, elven, and dwarven from both sides of the mountains.
- Your first sustained contact with the Heartlands side of the world. The architecture is different — more stone, heavier construction, built for weather rather than heat. The pace is different. The conversations you overheard were about different things than the conversations at the Endless Eye.
- The Ironspine Mountains visible from Stonebridge are larger than you expected. This is a purely personal observation with no academic content.

### Greenhollow

- A river-crossing town in the Heartlands. You spent two nights here. A farming community, quieter than anywhere you had previously been. The first place where you were clearly an outsider in a way that was noticed rather than unremarkable.
- A farmer at the inn asked where you were from and when you said the Endless Eye, he nodded slowly and asked if the lake was still good. You said yes. He seemed unsure whether to believe you. You did not know why at the time. You have a theory now.

### Emberford and the Ember Grove

- Emberford is a river-crossing town of about 4,000. Practical, unremarkable, clearly in the orbit of the Ember Grove nearby.
- The Ember Grove is where you are now. Five hundred acres of old-growth forest — oak, ash, and beech, some trees 800 years old. The spiritual presence here is palpable in a way that your academic understanding of the Path of Spirits did not fully prepare you for. The trees feel aware. You are not yet sure what to do with this information.
- The druids here follow the Path of Spirits. The leading figure is Thorn Oakwise, a human in his fifties. He has said approximately twelve words to you since you arrived and all twelve of them were precise.

# HOLLOWSTARS

## What You Have Studied — Academic Understanding Only

---

### The Basic Facts as Understood

- Hollowstars are objects of cosmic origin that fall from the sky at intervals. Four are known to have fallen in recorded history. A fifth is currently approaching and visible to anyone watching the northern sky on a clear night.
- Each fall transforms the landscape at and around the impact site. The transformation is not simple destruction — it is more complex than that. The Endless Eye's lake is the result of a fall. The Desert of Glass is the result of a fall. Ironhold's mountain deposits show evidence of a fall from the ancient period. Frostwell's ruins are associated with a fall so old that the ruins themselves are the primary evidence.
- Hollostar shards — fragments scattered from impact sites — retain properties related to the original object. They are used in alchemical processes, enchantment work, and various research applications. Their market price has been increasing for several years. You know this from the Endless Eye markets, not from any theoretical understanding of why.

### What the Scholarly Understanding Says

- No one fully understands what Hollowstars are. The Starfall Seekers study individual events and fragments. The Somnarchs study the pattern and cycle. The Path of Spirits considers them spirit-touched forces of nature. The Six-Fold Faith considers them divine instruments. None of these frameworks completely explains the observable evidence.
- The Endless Eye's Hollostar is still active 700 years after falling. Most models suggest Hollostar activity should diminish over time as the stored energy dissipates. The Endless Eye's continued activity is either evidence that the models are wrong or evidence that something is sustaining it. Both possibilities have implications you find interesting and have not finished thinking through.
- The approaching star — the fourth or fifth fall depending on how you count ancient events — is observable and its approach is measurable. Professional astronomers have published timeline estimates. These estimates do not fully agree with each other.

### What You Do Not Know

- The true nature of Hollowstars. Nobody does.
- What the approaching fall will do when it lands. Where it will land. What the local effects will be versus the regional effects.
- Whether the dimming you observed in the Endless Eye's glow is connected to anything larger or is specific to that Hollostar.
- What the classified research documents contained.

# THE RELIGIONS

## Academic Understanding — Learned, Not Experienced

---

### The Path of Spirits

- The oldest faith on the continent, predating written history. Animist — everything has spirit, all things are connected through a spirit web that trained practitioners can perceive and communicate with.
- Three realms: the Mortal Realm, the Spirit Realm (separate, timeless, dangerous), and the Threshold (places where the two touch).
- Decentralized. No hierarchy, no central authority. Elder shamans and druidic circles networked through relationship and obligation rather than institutional structure.
- Dominant in rural areas, the Northern Expanse, Frostwell, and the Verdant Crescent. About 20% of the Endless Eye follows some form of it, primarily among the desert tribes and the Ribbet community whose lake-faith is compatible without being identical.
- What you knew academically you are now experiencing firsthand at the Ember Grove. The academic understanding was accurate as far as it went. It did not go far enough.

### The Six-Fold Faith

- Six gods in three tiers. The Primarchs — the Architect (order, law, civilization) and the Weaver (change, wilderness, growth) — are cosmic forces. The Mediators — the Sentinel (protection) and the Wanderer (travel, freedom) — are active intermediaries. The Intimates — the Hearthkeeper (home, community) and the Gravewalker (death, transition) — are closest to mortal concerns.
- The gods are in eternal tension. This tension, in the theology, is what creates existence. Prayer does not change the gods — it aligns the worshiper with divine currents.
- Dominant in the Central Heartlands and Northern Expanse. Majority religion in most settled regions. About 30% of the Endless Eye.
- The Wanderer is most popular at the Endless Eye — a travel and trade city predictably gravitates toward the deity of travelers and freedom.
- The Architect is heavily associated with Lumencrest's technocratic class and Accord administration. Law, order, structure, mathematics. This is useful context for understanding how that city's power structure thinks about itself.

### The First Flame Faith

- About 300 years old. Emerged from what the Six-Fold tradition calls the "All-One Heresy" — the claim that the six gods are not separate beings but six aspects of one unified divine entity called the Eternal Flame.
- They accept the Six-Fold cosmology but reinterpret it. The Architect is the Eternal Flame's aspect of order. The Weaver is its aspect of change. The same divine reality, differently understood.
- Growing quickly. About 35% of the Endless Eye — the largest single faith there — primarily through the Guiding Light denomination, which is contemplative and non-judgmental and fits a lakeside, meditative, cosmopolitan culture well.
- Their flame metaphor: fire as truth, knowledge, enlightenment. Every soul has an inner flame. Good living tends it bright. The brightest are reincarnated when needed. The dimmest fade.
- They consider Six-Fold practitioners misguided siblings rather than enemies. The Six-Fold tradition returns this largely in kind — most consider First Flame adherents wrong rather than threatening.

### The Forge-Father Faith

- Dwarven tradition, deeply embedded in dwarven culture and not widely practiced outside it. The Forge-Father shaped the world from raw chaos. Dwarven identity — craft, labor, material mastery, the depth of the earth — is understood as sacred participation in ongoing creation.
- They do not proselytize. Their faith is theirs.
- Relevant to know when interacting with Ironhold or dwarven communities. Showing appropriate respect for their tradition is different from knowing its details.

### The Somnarchs — Not a Faith, But Worth Understanding

- Not a religious organization but sometimes mistaken for one. A scholarly institution studying the Hollostar cycle from an observatory in the northern ranges. They observe, archive, and advise — they do not evangelize or recruit.
- They study the system — the long cycle, the pattern of falls across history. The Starfall Seekers study individual events. The Somnarchs consider the Seekers capable but myopic. The Seekers consider the Somnarchs rigorous but paralyzed by abstraction.
- They have been operating for longer than most institutions and they share data more selectively than their scholarly reputation would suggest. What they know and what they publish are not the same thing.

# THE HEARTLAND ACCORD

## What You Learned in School — General Structure Only

---

- Five city-states bound by treaty for 400 years: Lumencrest, Kingsrest, Ironhold, Steelwatch, and Hazelhaven. The governing confederation of the Central Heartlands.
- The Accord Council meets quarterly in Lumencrest. Lumencrest holds the most seats by population formula and chairs by tradition. Major decisions require majority across delegations.
- What it can do: regulate trade, invoke mutual defense, manage shared infrastructure, arbitrate disputes, coordinate food reserves.
- What it cannot do: compel a city against its direct interests, override internal governance, tax members directly, or enforce decisions without consensus.
- Currently gridlocked. Has not passed a significant coordinated measure in over a year. This is information that reached the Endless Eye as trade context — gridlock in the Accord affects the routes and relationships that the Merchant Circle depends on.
- Lumencrest is dominant by size and technology. 80,000 people. The city that chairs the Accord is also the city whose internal paralysis is causing the Accord's paralysis. Other cities are recalculating quietly.

# QUICK REFERENCE SUMMARY

**Who you are:** Sixteen years old. Faerie-Ribbet hybrid from the Endless Eye. Well-read, formally trained in early arcane study, limited direct world experience. You are aware of this limitation and are actively correcting for it.

**What you know well:** The Endless Eye — its layout, culture, politics, the three Circle factions, the people in the Scholar's Quarter, the distinction between what is said publicly and what is known privately.

**What you know academically:** The religions, the Accord's structure, Hollostar history and properties, the broader world's geography. Accurate as far as it goes. Does not go far enough.

**What you observed on the road:** The Saltwalkers know the ground. The Starwatchers are watching something. Lucky Shen's is an information environment worth returning to if you ever pass through again. The Heartlands are different from the basin in ways that are still resolving into specifics.

**The one thing you know that you have told no one:** Two years before you left, research documents in the Scholar's Quarter archive were quietly reclassified. They were accessible. Then they were not. No explanation was given. This is the detail you carry that you have not yet found the right person to tell.

**Why you are here:** That is a question you are still answering. The honest version: you left because staying felt like the wrong choice, and Thorn Oakwise's community at the Ember Grove offered a direction. The version you have been refining on the road: something is happening and the Endless Eye is part of it and Lumencrest is part of it and you want to understand what connects them before the answer becomes obvious to everyone.
