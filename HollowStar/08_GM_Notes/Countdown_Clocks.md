# Countdown Clocks

All active timelines as of campaign start — **700 AR, Late Goldmonth**. These clocks run whether or not players engage with them. Use them to create urgency, generate session hooks, and make the world feel like it moves.

---

## HOW TO USE THESE

Each clock has **stages** — what's already happened, what's coming, and what happens if nobody intervenes. Advance stages when time passes in-world or when player actions accelerate/delay them. Not every clock needs to advance every session — pick the ones most relevant to where the party is and what they're doing.

**The clocks are interconnected.** Lumencrest's Heart failure accelerates the Accord political crisis. The Frostwell evacuation failure worsens the Northern Expanse refugee crisis. The Endless Eye's decline drives Merchant Circle desperation. When one clock advances, check whether it triggers movement on others.

## CLOCK 1: THE FOURTH FALL
**Type:** World clock — advances automatically, cannot be stopped  
**Current stage:** 3 of 6  
**Estimated time to impact:** 14 months (Null's calculation) / "2-5 years" (Lumencrest official estimate)

| Stage | What's Happening | In-World Signs |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| ✅ 1 — Distant Approach | Star visible only through instruments | Somnarchs tracking for decades |
| ✅ 2 — Visible at Night | Visible to naked eye as bright moving point | Growing public awareness, religious reactions |
| ✅ 3 — Daytime Visible *(current)* | Visible in daylight; growing brighter weekly | Fear spreading, factions mobilizing, refugees moving |
| 4 — Full Approach | Dominates night sky; size visible; ancient sites fully awake | Mass evacuations, faction lines hardening, transformed creature surge |
| 5 — Final Weeks | Fills sky; daylight has red tint; aurora borealis continent-wide | Complete Frostwell evacuation or deaths; Lumencrest Heart crisis peaks |
| 6 — Impact | The fall. Everything changes. | Campaign inflection point |

**If players accelerate:** Getting Lumencrest's real timeline data (Null's 14 months) to factions pushes them to Stage 4 behavior immediately.  
**If players delay:** Nobody stops the star. But preparation can shift the aftermath dramatically.

## CLOCK 2: LUMENCREST'S HEART
**Type:** Crisis clock — advances with each failure, can be slowed but not stopped  
**Current stage:** 3 of 5

| Stage | What's Happening | In-World Signs |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| ✅ 1 — Minor Fluctuations | Occasional brownouts, dismissed as maintenance issues | Ignored by most |
| ✅ 2 — Regular Failures | Scheduled rolling blackouts; manufacturing drops 15% | Economic strain; Council debates begin |
| ✅ 3 — Accelerating Decline *(current)* | 20% production loss; Vault District increased activity; Council paralyzed | Public anxiety; labor unrest; Expansionists gaining |
| 4 — Critical Failure | 40% production loss; residential power unreliable; Heart chamber emergency readings | Council splits publicly; Expansionists move on Endless Eye; First Flame surge |
| 5 — Collapse | On impact day: Heart fails completely (Somnarch prediction). Lumencrest goes dark. | Political earthquake; everything the city exports stops; 90,000 people without power |

**If players intervene:** Warning the Council about Stage 5 (Heart failure on impact day) before Stage 4 gives time to prepare alternative power. Not fix — *prepare*. Stage 5 still happens; Stage 5 with preparation looks very different.  
**If players don't:** Stage 5 hits without warning. The Expansionists' gambit (seizing the Endless Eye) will have already happened, probably catastrophically.

## CLOCK 3: THE ENDLESS EYE'S WATER
**Type:** Crisis clock — slow decline, accelerating  
**Current stage:** 2 of 5

| Stage | What's Happening | In-World Signs |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| ✅ 1 — Initial Decline | Subtle shoreline recession; only careful observers notice | Merchant Circle monitoring quietly |
| ✅ 2 — Confirmed Shrinkage *(current)* | 8% volume loss over 20 years; shoreline visibly receded; water prices rising | Merchant Circle divided; black market water trade beginning |
| 3 — Economic Crisis | 20% loss; water rationing; agricultural failure begins | Merchant Circle fractures; refugee movement from Basin begins; Lumencrest Expansionists mobilize |
| 4 — Collapse | 40% loss; Eye viable but only for drinking water; Basin civilization threatened | Mass exodus; Accord emergency session; Lumencrest moves militarily toward Eye |
| 5 — The Hollowing | At impact: old Hollostar energy drawn toward approaching star. Eye accelerates catastrophically in final weeks. | Either flash-crisis or, if party warned people, managed evacuation |

**If players intervene:** The Ribbet elder who's been communing with The Deeps for 40 years hears something new. Investigating what he's hearing gives crucial early warning of Stage 5's acceleration.  
**If players don't:** Stage 3 hits within 6-12 months of campaign start. Merchant Circle fractures. Lumencrest Expansionists use the crisis as justification for military action.

## CLOCK 4: FROSTWELL EVACUATION
**Type:** Human clock — lives in the balance, players can directly influence  
**Current stage:** 3 of 5

| Stage | What's Happening | In-World Signs |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| ✅ 1 — Early Warning | Somnarchs announce publicly; most dismiss | Small voluntary evacuations begin |
| ✅ 2 — Voluntary Exodus | ~40% of population has left; Stayer/Leaver split established | Northern Expanse towns absorbing refugees; Nine Hearths politics fractured |
| ✅ 3 — Political Deadlock *(current)* | ~60% evacuated; confederation council paralyzed; Stayers dug in | Hearthspeaker Mava desperately seeking votes; Durn unmovable; winter approaching |
| 4 — Forced Choice | Final evacuation window closes; remaining ~5,000 people have last chance | Party can directly save or lose these lives |
| 5 — Impact Zone | Anyone still in Frostwell at impact faces catastrophic danger | What survives depends on who stayed, who prepared, and what the party did |

**If players intervene:** Breaking the council deadlock (evidence for Durn, support for Mava, resolving the Sunken Library tablet question) shifts ~1,500-2,000 lives. Deep-Keeper Varra's information about Ember Cradle's sealed chamber may change the Drakona's calculus entirely.  
**If players don't:** Stage 4 passes. Roughly 2,000 people die or transform in the impact zone. Ember Cradle Drakona are among them — which becomes cosmically significant depending on what's in the sealed chamber.

## CLOCK 5: THE NORTHERN EXPANSE REFUGEE CRISIS
**Type:** Social clock — driven by Frostwell clock and Northern Expanse politics  
**Current stage:** 2 of 4

| Stage | What's Happening | In-World Signs |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| ✅ 1 — Trickle | Small groups arriving; towns managing | Generosity and some tension |
| ✅ 2 — Flood *(current)* | Thousands monthly; towns at capacity; food prices up 30%; no Warden of the North coordinating | Frontier towns making incompatible decisions; some closing gates |
| 3 — Social Fracture | Town populations doubled; resources strained past breaking; anti-refugee violence emerging | King Elowen forced to act or lose northern loyalty; Celebrantford (ground zero) in crisis |
| 4 — Resolution (two paths) | Either: Warden appointed, resources flow, crisis managed. Or: northern towns secede from crown authority, handle it themselves | Campaign-length consequence either way |

**If players intervene:** Resolving the Warden of the North appointment (finding Elowen's fourth option, or pushing him toward one of the three) breaks the deadlock. Getting Accord food shipments to the Expanse buys time.  
**If players don't:** Stage 3 hits within 3-4 months. The northern frontier effectively stops listening to Elowen. This becomes a political crisis that outlasts the Hollostar impact.

## CLOCK 6: THE GREEN COVENANT / LUMENCREST CONFLICT
**Type:** Political clock — slow escalation toward open violence  
**Current stage:** 3 of 5

| Stage | What's Happening | In-World Signs |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| ✅ 1 — Diplomatic Tension | Disagreements over logging access; formal protests | Timber prices high in Heartlands |
| ✅ 2 — Active Resistance | Lumencrest operations blocked; Covenant advisors in edge towns | Some logging camps abandoned |
| ✅ 3 — Thorn Escalation *(current)* | Three logging camps burned (10 years ago); cold standoff since; Warden of the Reach operating independently | Maren managing Thorns problem quietly; Lumencrest Expansionists preparing military option |
| 4 — Open Conflict | Expansionists force through military timber operation; Covenant responds with full Thorn action | First deaths; L'Empire Verdienne may intervene; edge towns forced to choose sides |
| 5 — War or Resolution | Either: Accord-level intervention forces settlement. Or: Lumencrest's crisis (Heart failure) makes timber irrelevant — but the violence has already happened | Long-term scar on Covenant-Accord relations regardless |

**If players intervene:** Exposing the Warden of the Reach's unauthorized Lumencrest alliance forces Maren to address it — which could improve or worsen things depending on how it's handled.  
**If players don't:** Stage 4 triggers when Lumencrest's Expansionists make their move (likely tied to Heart Clock Stage 4 — desperation drive).

## CLOCK 7: THE ISLE OF THE BROKEN SPHERE — THE SLEEPER
**Type:** Ancient clock — slow but accelerating, existential if ignored  
**Current stage:** 3 of 6

| Stage | What's Happening | In-World Signs |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| ✅ 1 — First Tremors | Rhythmic shaking begins; dismissed as geological | 30 years ago |
| ✅ 2 — Spine Stones | Geometric formations emerge from fault line; interior forest begins dying | 20 years ago |
| ✅ 3 — Acceleration *(current)* | Tremors every 6 hours; dead zone ~200 yards/year; Elder Tess in dialogue with something | Present |
| 4 — Active Communication | Elder Tess's dialogue becomes two-way; Spine Stones glow; Galapa community splits (stay vs. flee) | Triggered by approaching star passing certain proximity |
| 5 — The Answer | The Sleeper responds to impact — either protecting the island, emerging, or resonating catastrophically with the fourth fall's energy | Impact-day event |
| 6 — Aftermath | What the Sleeper did defines the Isle's future and reveals something about all Hollostar sites | Post-impact |

**If players intervene:** Reaching Elder Tess and facilitating the communication she's been having (rather than just observing it) accelerates Stage 4 in a controlled way — players may be able to actually communicate with what's beneath before the impact.  
**If players don't:** Stage 4 happens on its own schedule (~4-6 months from campaign start). The Sleeper wakes without intermediaries.

## CLOCK 8: THE VEILMARKETS' SECRET
**Type:** Information clock — Sable's decision has a deadline  
**Current stage:** 2 of 3

| Stage | What's Happening | In-World Signs |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| ✅ 1 — Assembling | Sable piecing together the full picture from network intelligence | Not visible to outside |
| ✅ 2 — Deciding *(current)* | Complete picture assembled; Sable determining whether to sell, share, or act | Unusual Veilmarket information-buying spree in recent months |
| 3 — Action | Sable makes a move — and the secret becomes a factor in the campaign | What Sable decides depends on what faction or group has been most useful/trustworthy to the party |

**If players engage:** Building relationship with the Veilmarkets (or Sable directly) influences what Sable does with the secret. A party that has been useful and demonstrated genuine commitment to something gets a very different offer than a party Sable has used as tools.  
**If players don't:** Sable sells to the highest bidder. The secret enters the world through the worst possible channel.
