# Secrets & Revelations

Master list of every significant secret in the vault. Organized by tier — how world-shaking the revelation is when it lands. Each entry has who holds the secret, how players might find it, and what changes when they do.

Use this to plan your revelation arc. The best campaigns reveal Tier 3 secrets early (session hooks), Tier 2 secrets mid-campaign (perspective shifts), and Tier 1 secrets late (everything recontextualized).

---

## TIER 1 — CAMPAIGN-DEFINING REVELATIONS
*These change how players understand the entire setting. Handle with care. Each should land as a pivot point, not a data dump.*

---

### 1A — What the Hollowstars Actually Are
**Who knows:** Cindrath (fully — she watched the first fall as a young dragon and understood what she saw). The pre-Hollostar ruins contain the answer in fragmentary form across multiple sites. Sira Vantholm (Somnarchs) is close on the mechanics but wrong on the nature. Denn Harrowfield has a theory he hasn't shared.  
**Who thinks they know:** Everyone, incorrectly. Every faction's model is wrong in at least one fundamental way.

**The reveal:** The Hollowstars are not objects, resources, or divine instruments. They are the pre-Hollostar civilization itself — compressed into a form of matter-energy that doesn't exist naturally in this world during a desperate flight from something that would have consumed them entirely. The civilization that built the Frostwell ruins, the Resonance Network, the Sleeping Gate — they didn't send a signal and wait for an answer. They sent *themselves*, converting their essential nature into stars capable of surviving the void, always intending to return.

The escaping changed them beyond recognition. What arrived is not what left. What leaks out as electricity from the Heart and water from the Endless Eye is not a resource — it is them giving themselves away, transforming into what the land most needs, because that transformation is the only form of existence still available to them. The Heart and the Eye are not failing. They are dying. They have been dying since they landed. The giving and the dying are the same process.

The fourth fall is the last wave of the evacuation fleet. After it, there are no more coming.

**→ Full details:** [[08_GM_Notes/The_Hollow_Truth|The Hollow Truth]]

**How players find it:**
- Cindrath knows and will tell players who ask the right question: not "what are the Hollowstars?" but "what did you see when the first one landed, and why did you stay?"
- Fully deciphering the Sleeping Gate's markings reveals receiving infrastructure, not warning systems
- Varra Ironmantle's Greywall records name the Resonance Network's purpose
- Elder Suu's changed meditations point toward presence and gradual absence, not geology
- Thessara Windroot's account of the third fall's first hours describes what emerged from the crater

**What changes:** Every faction's response to the crisis has been built on a wrong assumption. The Heart cannot be saved — it is dying and the dying is the point. The Endless Eye's decline is not geological. The pre-Hollostar ruins are not warnings or records — they are the reception infrastructure a civilization built for its own return. Players now know something that reframes everything that came before. What they do with it — who they tell, what they protect, whether they can answer the thing inside the fourth star before it finishes dying — is the campaign's final question.

---

### 1B — Thessara's Account of the Third Fall
**Who knows:** Only Thessara Windroot. 700 years, no one else.  
**Who thinks they know:** Everyone believes the Endless Eye formed by standard Hollostar impact physics.

**The reveal:** Whatever she saw in the crater's first hours — see the options in Running_the_World.md, or let it emerge from what players have been theorizing. Her account directly contradicts the established history of how the Endless Eye formed — which means every faction's understanding of what Hollowstars *do* on impact is incomplete or wrong.

**How players find it:**
- Reaching Thessara in the Deep Deepwood (she rarely leaves; getting to her requires Green Covenant trust)
- She will not tell strangers. She needs to assess whether they can handle it and whether they'll use it well.
- If the Sleeper clock reaches Stage 4, the Spine Stones' connection to the third fall becomes a secondary path to the same revelation

**What changes:** The Merchant Circle's entire case for "the Eye will recover" is based on wrong assumptions. The Somnarchs' impact models are wrong. The religious interpretations are wrong. And whatever Thessara saw — the figure, the opening, the void — implies the fourth fall may produce the same effect in Frostwell.

---

### 1C — Vaela's Fifth Fall Calculation
**Who knows:** Only Vaela Thessin. She has told no one.  
**Who thinks they know:** Nobody. The Somnarchs' public position is that they track known cycles.

**The reveal:** There is a fifth fall in the data. Longest-cycle, high uncertainty, but present. Surviving the fourth fall may be preparation for another. The cycle doesn't stop.

**How players find it:**
- Deep trust with Vaela, earned over multiple sessions of Somnarch work
- She will only share this with people she believes can hold it without being destroyed by it
- Finding the Somnarchs' most restricted archive records independently

**What changes:** The campaign's emotional core shifts. Surviving isn't winning — it's buying time for the next generation to face the same question. This is devastating or clarifying depending on who the players are. Either way, it reframes everything that came before.

### 1E — The Isle Is Alive
**Who knows:** Nobody alive knows the full truth. Cindrath knows — she has known for centuries — but has never been asked the right question. Elder Tess has felt the truth through her meditation and interpreted it correctly in spirit (vast, ancient, aware, curious) without knowing its nature. A Veleth-sensitive Simiah near the Isle's coastal waters would feel something unprecedented in the sea around it — not marsh-network resonance, but something enormous registering as presence in living water.  
**The secret:** The Isle of the Broken Sphere is not an island. It is a Dragon Turtle of immeasurable age — one of the largest creatures alive, which settled in the Silver Belt Sea millennia ago and became still long enough for soil, forest, and civilization to form on its back. The "volcanic peaks" are its shell ridges. The fault line is its shell seam. The Spine Stones are its dorsal spines rising through the accumulated earth. The rhythmic tremors are its heartbeat. The heat from the fissures is its body warmth. There are no volcanoes. There never were.  
**The Galapa:** Their relationship with the Sleeper is real. The creature has registered their centuries of offerings and ceremony as intentional presence — something on its back that is trying to communicate. It finds this interesting. When Elder Tess meditates near the Spine Stones, she is genuinely reaching it. It is curious about her.  
**What changes:** Everything about the Isle recontextualizes. The "evacuation vs. permanence" debate becomes irrelevant — you cannot evacuate a creature that decides to move, and you cannot build permanent infrastructure on something alive. The Galapa's grief over losing the Broken Sphere takes on new dimension: they built their culture on a living being that has been dimly aware of them the entire time. And when it moves, it does not destroy — it travels. The Isle goes with it.

### 1D — The Sealed Chamber Beneath Ember Cradle
**Who knows:** Deep-Keeper Varra Ironmantle (what's in the records). Flame-Keeper Sorith (that the seals are softening — but not what's inside). Nobody knows what's actually in the chamber right now.  
**What's in the chamber:** Your decision. Suggestions:
- The physical remains of the being that created the Drakona — not Cindrath, something older
- Pre-Hollostar construction tools and records that explain what the Hollowstars are (connects to 1A)
- A Hollostar fragment of significant size that has been dormant — and is now waking with the fourth fall's approach

**How players find it:**
- Varra is dying and wants to pass on what the Greywall records say — but her clan will try to stop her
- The seals softening is already observable to anyone near Ember Cradle
- Frostwell evacuation NPCs mention the dwarves are acting strangely around the lower levels

**What changes:** Depends on what's inside. Whatever it is, it's been sealed for a reason, the seals are weakening, and the fourth fall's approach is the cause. Players have to decide: open it deliberately, or let the impact open it without warning.

## TIER 2 — PERSPECTIVE-SHIFTING REVELATIONS
*These don't change the world, but they change how players understand the people in it. Best delivered mid-campaign when players have relationships at stake.*

---

### 2A — Kaida's Divine Silence
**Who knows:** Only Kaida.  
**The secret:** The Eternal Flame has not given her guidance on the crisis. She has been leading by personal authority alone. The most powerful spiritual figure on the continent is operating without divine input at the moment of greatest need.

**How players find it:** She tells them. She tells very few people. She tells the ones she trusts with her sealed letters — because those people need to understand why the letters matter more than she does.

**What changes:** Kaida becomes human. The First Flame's unity is resting entirely on one elderly Giant's willpower. Players who care about the church now have a reason to help her that isn't theological.

---

### 2B — Elowen's Real Reason
**Who knows:** Elowen.  
**The secret:** He's not slow because he's indecisive. All three Warden candidates solve the immediate crisis and create a structural problem in 30 years — one he can see clearly and they cannot, because none of them will live long enough to face it. He is sacrificing present harm to prevent future harm. He is not sure he's right. He has never been less sure of anything.

**How players find it:** Elowen tells them directly if they ask the right question and he trusts them. Lord Chancellor Miren Ashvale knows — he's been Elowen's confidant for 20 years.

**What changes:** Elowen stops being an obstacle and becomes a tragedy. Players may disagree with his calculus — that's the point. There's no clean answer. If they push him toward a decision, they're accepting partial ownership of the 30-year consequence.

---

### 2C — Maren's Records
**Who knows:** Maren Stillwater, and Warden of Memory Chit (who wants to use them).  
**The secret:** Previous ancient wakings ended with settlements being "incorporated" — absorbed by the forest, not destroyed. The forest didn't attack. It *grew around them*. The 2,000-year records have a theory about why.

**How players find it:** Chit approaches them if he believes they can use it. Maren tells them when she's decided the moment for silence has passed. Breaking into the Memory archives is another option.

**What changes:** The ancient waking stops being an unknown threat and becomes a known one with precedent. The precedent is horrifying in one sense, fascinating in another. What were those settlements like after being incorporated? Are there traces? Is anyone still in there?

---

### 2D — The Grey Sails Funding
**Who knows:** The funding House (one of the Four Houses).  
**The secret:** One of the Four Houses is covertly funding the Grey Sails pirate fleet to disrupt the others' shipping. It's been working. It's about to become undeniably visible.

**How players find it:** Following the money through Silverbelt Coast merchant contacts. A Grey Sails captain who wants out. House Sera's Alliance contact who knows more than she should.

**What changes:** The House responsible faces expulsion from the Council of Banners. The balance of Four Houses power shifts immediately. If it's the House the players have been working for, they're implicated.

---

### 2E — The Merchant Circle's Black Market Source
**Who knows:** The source (not Deepsound, who Moonblossom suspects).  
**The secret:** The black market water sales are being run by a junior Circle member with Lumencrest Expansionist connections — not for profit, but to create enough crisis pressure to force the Circle toward the military alliance with Lumencrest that the Expansionists want.

**How players find it:** Following the black market water supply chain. An informant in Saltmere who noticed unusual Lumencrest visitor patterns. The junior Circle member is not subtle — just careful.

**What changes:** The Merchant Circle's internal conflict stops being about personalities and becomes about external manipulation. Lumencrest Expansionists are already inside the Circle's decision-making. This reframes the Endless Eye's entire political situation.

---

### 2F — "Null" Is Two People
**Who knows:** Only the two of them.  
**The secret:** The anonymous astronomer is a partnership. One inside Lumencrest (the suppression data source). One outside (the independent calculations). They have never met in person. Together they know more about the timeline than any single faction.

**How players find it:** Tracing one of Null's message drops carefully. The inside partner is a junior Vault District technocrat with unusual observation equipment. The outside partner is somewhere in the Northern Expanse.

**What changes:** The players now have two assets inside the most important information networks on the continent — but only if they protect both of them. The Lumencrest insider is in genuine danger if their identity becomes known.

---

### 2G — Voss's Past
**Who knows:** Nobody. Two people knew, and both are dead.  
**The secret:** Voss left his Firbolg community under circumstances that connect to the current crisis in a way he hasn't pieced together. The specific connection is yours to design — suggestions: his community was one of the first to notice the approaching star's effects on Firbolg spiritual traditions; his family was involved in pre-Hollostar ruin excavation; the community he left is now in the Frostwell evacuation zone. Whatever it is, it makes the crisis personal in a way he didn't know it was.

**How players find it:** Investigating the Firbolg communities in the Northern Expanse. Old records in the Frostwell evacuation settlements. Something Voss finds himself when he starts looking.

**What changes:** Voss becomes fully three-dimensional rather than a powerful neutral. He has a stake. Players who've built relationship with him now have a reason to help him discover it — and reason to worry about what he does when he does.

## TIER 3 — SESSION HOOKS AND LOCAL REVELATIONS
*These are the secrets that generate individual sessions or short arcs. Deliver them freely — they're the fuel, not the payload.*

---

### 3A — Professor Greaves' Discovery
**Who knows:** Talan Greaves (Lumencrest Illuminist).  
**The secret:** His research shows the Heart's failure rate is not linear — it's accelerating in a specific pattern that matches exactly the timeline of the third fall's impact 700 years ago. The Heart is counting down to something, not simply failing. He doesn't know what happens at zero.  
**Hook:** He needs someone to take his data to the Somnarchs without it going through Council channels.

### 3B — The Sunken Library Tablet
**Who knows:** Hearthspeaker Mava (Frostwell).  
**The secret:** A Ribbet diver found a tablet in the Frozen Lake's depths. It says something specific about the star's arrival that changes the evacuation calculus. Mava has been sitting on it because she doesn't know if it helps or destroys the vote.  
**Hook:** The party can read it. What it says is yours — but it should matter to the evacuation decision.

### 3C — Deep-Keeper Varra's Window
**Who knows:** Varra Ironmantle (Frostwell dwarves).  
**The secret:** The Greywall clan records include a construction date for Ember Cradle and a description of what's in the sealed lower chambers. She's the last person alive who's read the originals. She's dying. Her clan will try to stop her from talking.  
**Hook:** She's reached out through an intermediary. She wants to tell someone before she goes.

### 3D — The Deep Bloom Integration
**Who knows:** Deepsilt elders (Glittering Marshes).  
**The secret:** The largest fragment in the Deepsilt cluster is approaching the size of a small intact star. If Lumencrest learned about it, they'd do anything to access it. The elders have been keeping the true size secret for generations.  
**Hook:** A Seeker expedition is getting uncomfortably close to the cluster. The elders need help redirecting them — without revealing why.

### 3E — The Endless Eye's Deeps
**Who knows:** Elder Current-in-Stone (Ribbet, Saltmere).  
**The secret:** He's been communing with The Deeps beneath the Eye for 40 years. Something is down there that isn't the Hollostar. It has been present for as long as he's been listening. In the last six months it has changed — become more present, more distinct. He doesn't know what it is. He's afraid to find out.  
**Hook:** He'll talk to people who don't represent any faction's interests.

### 3F — Warden of the Reach's Unauthorized Alliance
**Who knows:** Dusk (Green Covenant), their Lumencrest contact.  
**The secret:** Dusk has been running a secret alliance with Lumencrest's anti-Expansionist faction — sharing information in exchange for political interference against the Expansionists. Maren has not authorized this. The other Wardens don't know.  
**Hook:** The party stumbles onto evidence of the communication. They have to decide what to do with it before Dusk finds out they know.

### 3G — Shard Prices and the Veilmarkets
**Who knows:** Sable (Veilmarkets), certain Seeker buyers.  
**The secret:** Black market Hollostar fragment prices have tripled in the last year. The buyers aren't researchers — they're from a single organization that's been purchasing through multiple identities. Sable knows who, and hasn't told anyone because the buyer is paying extremely well.  
**Hook:** Someone else has noticed the pattern and is asking questions. The party is asked to find out who's buying — or to stop whoever's asking.

### 3H — Mirelen's Two Weeks
**Who knows:** Mirelen Starweaver, the Merchant Circle representative.  
**The secret:** The Circle's offer to Rivermark expires in two weeks. Mirelen hasn't answered. She's decided — she just hasn't told anyone what she decided. The representative is about to make a second visit to confirm.  
**Hook:** The party learns about the first meeting through Veilmarkets intelligence. They have two weeks to either support her decision, change her mind, or expose the negotiation to the Accord.

### 3I — The Ember Cradle Seals Softening
**Who knows:** Flame-Keeper Sorith (Frostwell Drakona).  
**The secret:** The seals on the lower chambers of Ember Cradle aren't made of ice. They're made of something else — and that something is responding to the approaching star's proximity by softening. Sorith doesn't know what's inside. She knows the seals are giving way.  
**Hook:** She needs help she can't ask for from her own people — investigating what's behind the seals without triggering panic among the Drakona who consider the Cradle sacred.

### 3J — Watcher Aren's Data Conflict
**Who knows:** Watcher Aren (Frostwell), Lumencrest's official astronomers.  
**The secret:** Aren's impact window calculation conflicts with Lumencrest's official estimate. He's checked eleven times. His data puts impact inside 14 months. Lumencrest's official estimate says 2-5 years. One of them is wrong. If it's Lumencrest, the evacuation window is almost closed.  
**Hook:** Aren needs someone to take his data to Lumencrest's Vault District and compare it against their raw observations — not their published conclusions. He can't go himself; he won't leave his post.

### 3K — The Pulse Is Changing
**Who knows:** Mist-of-Branches (senior Listener, Canopy Run) and two other *Veleth* practitioners. The Deepsilt elders have been informed but have not shared it further.  
**The secret:** The Deepsilt shard cluster's pulse — the bioluminescent rhythm the Listeners have read for generations — has been shifting in character for approximately fourteen months, coinciding with the approaching star becoming visible in daylight. Not faster, not slower. Different. The best description: it used to feel like water settling. Now it feels like water waiting. No one in the marsh knows what this means. No Somnarch, Seeker, or Lumencrest researcher knows it's happening.  
**Hook:** A party who earns deep trust with Canopy Run — or who has a *Veleth*-sensitive Simiah PC — learns about the change. Getting this information to the Somnarchs would significantly advance their understanding of what the approaching star is doing to existing Hollostar sites. The Deepsilt elders will not be happy about it leaving the marsh.

## REVELATION PACING GUIDE

**Early campaign (Sessions 1-5):** Tier 3 secrets freely. These are session starters and relationship builders. Players should be discovering one or two per session.

**Mid campaign (Sessions 6-15):** Tier 2 secrets begin landing. These reframe the people players have come to know. Space them — one major Tier 2 per 2-3 sessions. Let players sit with each one before the next.

**Late campaign (Sessions 16+):** Tier 1 secrets. Never more than one per session block. These should feel earned. The best moment for 1A (what the Hollowstars are) is just before the Faction Tension clocks reach their crisis points — players need to know before everyone starts making irreversible decisions.

**Impact day session:** Everything converges. At least one Tier 1 should still be unresolved going into the impact — something players learn *during* the event itself. The best candidate is 1D (the sealed chamber opens whether anyone wants it to or not).
