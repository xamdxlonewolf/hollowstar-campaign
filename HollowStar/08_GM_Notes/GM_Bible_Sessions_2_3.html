The Registry Flags a Name (Act 1 — Gate Entry · 😨 1 Fear)

What triggers this During the gate queue, before the party reaches the officer. Best when the table is relaxed — use it to remind them the city's welcome is conditional.

GM Say "The line stops. Not long — thirty seconds — but long enough. Ahead of you, a Frostwell woman with a cloth bundle is asked to step aside. The officer checks a ledger, speaks quietly to a second watchman, writes something down. She goes pale, then nods, and is waved through with a look that says she knows she's being noted. The line moves again. You pass through normally. But you saw how fast the machinery engages when a name matters."

GM Info — What's actually happening The woman is not on a criminal warrant — she's on a relief registry mismatch . Frostwell arrivals are supposed to check in at a Council relief station within 24 hours; she didn't know. The watch logs her for follow-up, not arrest. This is bureaucracy, not malice — but it looks like malice from the queue. If a PC has a warrant: swap the NPC for their situation. The fear spend buys you the beat without a surprise TPK — the officer asks them aside, verifies, and either passes them (mistaken identity, old warrant) or gives them a choice: resolve it now (fine, contact, bribe) or don't enter. Don't hard-stop the campaign without an out. Basil: If the Guild runner reported the party, this is NOT when that surfaces — registry is city watch, not Guild. Save Guild awareness for the tail spend.

The Hum Spikes Once (Act 1 — Gate Entry · 😨 1 Fear)

What triggers this Immediately after they cross the gate threshold, or when they first touch a Network-connected fixture (lamp post, iron railing). Works best paired with Greer or Lurielle in front.

GM Say "The hum you've been hearing since the ridge — constant, low — spikes. Not louder exactly. Deeper. Every electric lamp on the street flickers in the same instant, a single synchronized stutter, then steadies. A Clank three paces ahead doesn't pause. A vendor doesn't look up. You do."

GM Info — What's actually happening Micro-fluctuation in Heart output — the same class of event that causes brownouts, at negligible scale. Network-connected infrastructure registers it everywhere at once. Locals have learned to ignore sub-threshold spikes. Newcomers haven't. Lurielle: Knowledge DIF 12 — the spike's harmonic profile matches her Heart Stone notation from Session 1 (if used). Same family, different amplitude. She can write this down. Greer: Instinct DIF 11 — the spirit layer flinched. Something below the city turned in its sleep. Not waking. Adjusting.

Optional — Lurielle places the frequency Stat:Knowledge · DIF:12

Failure: Wrongness noted. No pattern yet.

Partial: Same family as Ember Grove threshold. Different source depth.

Success: Heart Stone + Network + Endless Eye pre-classification data — three points on a line she can't finish yet. Critical journal entry.

A Clank Stops and Looks at Lurielle (Act 1 — Gate Entry · 😨 1 Fear)

What triggers this After implant scan at the gate (if Clanks are being processed nearby) or when the first Clank passes the party on the street. Lurielle-specific.

GM Say "A Clank in a grey service robe completes the gate scan — FREE status, waved through — and walks past your group. It stops. Turns its head. Looks at Lurielle for one beat longer than navigation requires. You feel the attention like a hand on your shoulder. Then it continues, pace unchanged. Nobody else noticed."

GM Info — What's actually happening The Clank's sensory array picked up Hollowstar-adjacent biological resonance — same class of detection as the shard bear's pull toward Lurielle. The Clank has no context for what it sensed. Its behavior is an artifact of Luminarium-standard perception hardware, not malice or recognition. If Lurielle reacts publicly: Instinct DIF 12 from nearby locals — 'first time seeing a Clank?' Pity or amusement, not alarm. Clank politics beat: Greer may read the room's indifference as its own kind of horror. Optional character moment — no roll required.

The Car Shudders Mid-Line (Act 3 — Cable Car · 😨 1 Fear)

What triggers this Mid-ride between North Expanse and Central Hub — the highest, most exposed segment. Use when the table is comfortable and enjoying the view.

GM Say "The car shudders — a lateral hitch, like the cable's tension went wrong for half a second. The mechanism in the ceiling chatters. The attendant doesn't look alarmed. He taps the speaking tube, listens, says two words you can't hear. 'Tension adjustment,' he tells the car, almost bored. 'Happens.' A dwarf by the window goes back to reading. A woman who was asleep opens one eye, closes it. You are the only people holding the handrail."

GM Info — What's actually happening Minor cable maintenance lag — the Network hiccupped and the automated tension compensator was a fraction slow. Not dangerous on its own. Happens more often than officials admit. The attendant's calm is real; so is the dwarf's glance at the ceiling, which says more than last month . Don't turn this into a disaster scene unless players panic and do something stupid. The fear is social: locals know something newcomers don't.

Optional — Read the locals' reaction Stat:Instinct or Presence · DIF:12

Failure: Attendant's reassurance lands. Maybe it's fine.

Partial: This isn't the first time they've heard 'happens.' Frequency is increasing.

Success: The dwarf's glance + the asleep woman's single eye = shared public knowledge the city doesn't discuss with outsiders. Brownouts are infrastructure, not rumor.

Brownout Flicker — Eastern Ward (Act 3 — Cable Car · 😨 1 Fear)

Then they come back, not all at once, a staggered relight that looks ashamed. Someone in the car swears quietly. The Clank at the window doesn't react. Nobody explains it to you."

The Protest Crowd Surges Early (Morning — Border Protest · 😨 1 Fear)

What triggers this During the mid-morning protest assembly, before speakers have the crowd's full attention. Use when players are observing or lightly involved — not if they're deep in a Brinne scene (save Brinne fear for that).

GM Say — Read aloud "Someone at the front shoves someone back. It's not a fight — it's a mistake that becomes momentum. Fifty people push forward before the organizers can shout hold. City watch stiffens, batons visible but not raised. Chalk dust kicks up from the pavement where yesterday's slogans were scrubbed. The crowd is one surge away from becoming something the watch can't pretend is a demonstration."

GM Info — What's actually happening The surge is not Green Covenant orchestration. It's organic frustration + one agent provocateur (unaffiliated petty criminal paid 2 silver to shove). The radical wing wants distance from today's blast — Wren's cell planned for mid-afternoon precisely to avoid protest linkage. This Fear spend foreshadows how easily the city will conflate the two.

Success: They help organizers push the surge back. Speaker thanks them by name. +1 civic trust with legitimate protest wing — useful when Council blames protestors tonight.
