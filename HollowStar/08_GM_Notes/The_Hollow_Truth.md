## What the Hollowstars Actually Are

Ten thousand years ago, a civilization existed on this continent. Not the civilizations that came after — older, larger, more thoroughly in conversation with the nature of the world. They built the Frostwell ruins. They built the Sleeping Gate and the Frostvault and the Resonance Network at the Ember Cradle. They understood things about this world that the current age has only half-remembered in fragments.

They also understood that something was coming for them. Not a natural disaster, not a war — something that would consume the very substance of what they were. A collapse not of their civilization but of their existence. What it was exactly is not preserved in any record, because the records themselves were compressed into the escape.

Facing total extinction, they did the only thing available to them. They compressed themselves into the stars and fled.

Not their bodies. Not their memories as memories. Whatever fundamental nature constituted their being — the essential substance that made them *them* — was compressed by the process of escaping into something that does not exist naturally in this world. A form of matter-energy with no name in any current language, that behaves according to principles no current scholar has a framework for, because the civilization that understood those principles no longer exists in a form that can explain them.

The signal was not a message sent to strangers. It was the sound of their own compression and launch — the noise a civilization makes when it converts itself into something that can survive the void. The Resonance Network, the Sleeping Gate, the Ember Cradle's sealed chamber — they built all of this *before* they fled, as receiving infrastructure for their own eventual arrival. They always intended to return. They never anticipated how long it would take, or what the returning would be like.

What arrived is not them anymore. The escaping changed them.

## What Leaks Out Is What They Are

The Heart of Lumencrest produces electricity. The Endless Eye produces water. These are not side effects. They are not resources being harvested from an inert object. They are the Hollowstars giving themselves away.

Each one landed and began slowly converting its essential substance into what the land and the people around it most needed. The Heart found a settlement of scholars with knowledge and community but no power to sustain it, and gave electrical energy — genuine, sustained, derived from something that does not exist anywhere else in the world. The Eye found an empty desert with no reason for civilization, and gave water — a freshwater lake in the middle of wasteland, the one gift that made everything else possible.

This giving is not intentional in the way a person's decision is intentional. It is more fundamental than that. The substance they became during the escape has one property above all others: it transforms into what surrounds it lacks. It cannot help doing this. The giving is the only form of existence still available to them after the compression changed what they were.

Which means the depletion is not a malfunction. It is not age. The Heart and the Eye are declining because they have been giving themselves away for centuries, and they have a finite amount of themselves to give. They are dying. They have been dying since they landed. The giving and the dying are the same process.

The Merchant Circle's classified data on the Endless Eye's water level is accurate. The Lumencrest Council's private measurements of the Heart's output decline are accurate. What neither institution understands is that no intervention will reverse this. You cannot stabilize a dying thing by studying it. You can only be present for what it is trying to do before it finishes.

## The Fourth Fall

The fourth Hollostar has been in transit for approximately 10,000 years — the time since the first one landed. The interval between falls is not random. It is approximately the travel time from wherever they launched to here. They did not all leave at once. They left in waves, the way a civilization evacuates — some first, some later, some after the last possible moment.

The fourth one is the last wave. There are no more coming after it.

It is also the most changed. The longer the transit, the more the compression altered what was inside. The first arrival — the one that landed in Frostwell 10,000 years ago, the one the pre-Hollostar civilization built the Resonance Network to receive — was the most intact. Still recognizably something that had recently been a civilization. Still capable of communication in forms the builders could understand. The civilization made contact. Something went wrong, or went exactly right in a way that ended the civilization — the records don't exist to say which, because the civilization that made them was gone afterward.

The second and third arrivals — the Heart and the Eye — were more changed. By the time they landed they had been in transit long enough that direct communication was no longer possible. They could only do what they had become: give themselves to what surrounded them.

The fourth one has been traveling the longest. It is the most transformed. What it will give Frostwell — what Frostwell needs that it can provide — is the GM's decision and should emerge from what the campaign has revealed about what Frostwell's people are losing. But the giving will happen regardless of whether the Resonance Network activates.

What the Resonance Network changes is not the giving. It changes whether contact is possible.

## What Contact Means

The Resonance Network was built as receiving infrastructure. The Sleeping Gate is an aperture. The Ember Cradle is the anchor. The Frostvault is a processing node. Together, when the fourth fall impacts at the correct location with the Warmstone catalyst in place, they do not prevent the impact. They do not change what the Hollostar gives. They open a channel between what is inside the star and what is outside it — briefly, for the duration of the impact's peak energy, which is measured in minutes.

During those minutes, something can be communicated.

What the thing inside the fourth star is trying to communicate, after 10,000 years of transit and compression and the slow process of giving itself away through the first three landings, is a single question expressed in a form that has no precise translation into any current language:

*Was any of this received? Did anyone understand what we were trying to do? And is there anything left of what we were — anything that survived, that remembered, that kept something of us in it — or did we give everything away and receive nothing in return?*

This is not a threat. It is not a demand. It is the question of something that has been dying slowly for ten thousand years and wants to know, before it finishes, whether the dying meant anything.

The answer the players can give — through their actions during the campaign, through what they preserved, who they protected, what they understood — shapes what the contact means and what the world looks like afterward.

## Why the Previous Civilization Is Gone

They made contact with the first arrival. What the contact revealed is not preserved. But the civilization built the Resonance Network knowing that contact would be possible, which means they had some theory about what they would find. Their theory was almost certainly wrong in at least one important way — the escaping had changed what was inside more than they anticipated. What they found was not themselves returning. It was something that had been themselves, transformed beyond recognition, trying to give the only thing it still had.

The civilization did not survive the contact. This does not mean the contact destroyed them. It may mean that what they understood from the contact changed them so fundamentally that what they became afterward is not legible as the same civilization. The ruins are not the ruins of a catastrophe. They are the ruins of a transformation — a civilization that passed through something and came out as something else entirely.

What they became is distributed through the world in ways that no current scholar recognizes as intentional. The Path of Spirits' ancestor presence in the natural world. The Veleth sensitivity that some Simiah develop through long proximity to shard-rich environments. The particular weight that certain places have — the Ember Cradle, the Sleeping Gate, the deep marsh under Deepsilt — that practitioners of every faith describe as presence without being able to name what is present.

The pre-Hollostar civilization did not become the Hollowstars. The pre-Hollostar civilization became the world's capacity to receive them.

## Faction Implications When This Becomes Known

**The Somnarchs:** Their entire model of Hollostar cycles is correct in mechanics and wrong in nature. They have been tracking the travel schedule of a civilization's evacuation fleet without knowing it. Vaela Thessin's five-cycle calculation — she has the data right. There is no sixth wave. The fifth fall in her data is an artifact of the fourth fall's impact energy interacting with the existing Hollostar sites. The cycle ends with the fourth.

**The Hexarchs:** The theological question of whether Hollowstars are divine is answered in the most destabilizing possible way: they are not gods, but they are not not-gods either. They are beings — or were beings — that the world's current moral categories cannot fully hold. The Radical Sect's position (the stars are divine agents) is wrong in letter and correct in spirit in a way that will cause a significant fracture.

**The First Flame:** High Flame Kaida's silence from the Eternal Flame during the crisis suddenly makes sense and simultaneously becomes more frightening. If the Hollowstars are what they are, then the Flame that the First Flame worships — the unified divine reality — may be something much older and stranger than the theology accounts for. Kaida will not share her conclusions publicly. She will share them with someone she trusts completely.

**Lumencrest:** The Heart is dying and cannot be saved. The Illuminists' research has been asking the wrong question for its entire existence. The Expansionists' plan to seize the Endless Eye is doubly monstrous once the truth is known — they would be exploiting the death of something that gave itself to feed a city. Whether this matters to the Expansionists is a question the players may have to answer.

**The Merchant Circle:** The Endless Eye is not a natural resource declining for geological reasons. The water is a gift that is being exhausted. The theological implications within the Traditionalist faction — who have been communing with the lake's Hollostar for decades — are significant. Elder Suu has been hearing something change in her meditations for a year. She was right that it is not just geological. She was not prepared for what it actually is.

**The Path of Spirits:** Of all the world's traditions, the Path of Spirits is the closest to having been right all along. The ancestor presence in the world, the spirit networks, the capacity that some practitioners develop to feel what surrounds them — all of it is the inheritance of the pre-Hollostar civilization's transformation. Path practitioners will understand the contact in the Frostwell ruins differently than scholars will. They will recognize what they are feeling. They will not have the words for it, but they will know it is not new.

## What the Fourth Fall Gives Frostwell

This is deliberately left for the GM to decide in collaboration with the campaign's direction. The answer should emerge from what Frostwell's people have been losing throughout the story.

The framework: the Hollowstar gives what the land and people most need. The Heart gave power to a civilization that had knowledge but no energy. The Eye gave water to a desert that had potential but no life. What does Frostwell have in abundance? Cold, isolation, ancient preserved knowledge, ruins, memory — the oral tradition of nine communities built entirely around the idea that forgetting kills. What is Frostwell losing? Its people are scattering. Its hearth-communities are fracturing. The most fundamental cultural value — shared warmth, collective fire, nobody alone in winter — is shattering precisely as the star arrives.

Possible directions:
- Something geothermal that makes the transformed post-impact tundra habitable in a new configuration, creating warmth that draws scattered communities back to a new center
- The Frostvault's ancient mechanisms become a genuine archive — not just the preserved records of the pre-Hollostar civilization but something that makes those records accessible, that bridges the ten-thousand-year gap in a way that scholars and shamans and ordinary people can approach
- The sealed Ember Cradle chamber opens and what is inside becomes a new kind of anchor for community — not a hearth exactly, but something that functions the way hearths have always functioned: a place that is warm, that is safe, that people come back to

What it should not be: a weapon, a power source for Lumencrest, or anything that makes the question of who controls it more important than the question of what it means.

## GM Notes: Revealing This

**The earliest breadcrumbs** (sessions 1-5): The pre-Hollostar ruins behave strangely near Hollostar sites. The Resonance Network connections feel designed for something, not against something. The Ember Cradle warms in a pattern that doesn't fit natural geothermal activity. Path of Spirits practitioners feel something in the deep marsh and the deep tundra that they describe as familiar but can't place.

**The middle layer** (sessions 6-12): Deep-Keeper Varra's records name the Resonance Network and describe its purpose. The Sleeping Gate's changing symbols, when fully documented, describe a receiving process rather than a warning. Elder Suu's changed meditations start pointing toward presence, not just decline. Thessara Windroot's account of the third fall's impact — whatever she saw in the first hours — suggests what opened was not nothing.

**The late revelation** (sessions 13+): Cindrath knows. She has known for centuries. She watched the first fall from a distance as a young dragon and understood something about what she saw that no mortal civilization has pieced together since. She will tell the players if they ask the right question. The right question is not "what are the Hollowstars?" The right question is "what did you see when the first one landed, and why did you stay?"

**The contact itself**: If the Resonance Network activates during the fourth fall, the contact is brief, overwhelming, and not fully translatable. The players experience something — not language, not vision exactly, something between sensation and understanding — that carries the weight of what the thing inside the star is trying to ask. What they do with that experience, whether they can answer it, and what their answer means for the world afterward is the campaign's final question.
