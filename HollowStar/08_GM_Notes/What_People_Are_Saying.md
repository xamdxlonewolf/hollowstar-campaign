# What People Are Saying

*Street-level voices across the continent — 700 AR, Late Goldmonth. These are things ordinary people are saying in taverns, at markets, on docks, in workshops. Not official positions. Not faction statements. What actually circulates.*

*Each entry is followed by what it's really about — the GM layer beneath the rumor. Use these to fill silences, flavor ambient conversation, or seed player attention toward things that matter.*

*Format: the rumor as it sounds, then the truth underneath.*

---

## HOW TO USE THIS

Drop these into scenes naturally. The dockworker who mentions the water price increase isn't a plot hook — she's just talking. But if a player asks a follow-up question, you have something real to follow up with. The rumors that are partially wrong are usually more interesting than the ones that are accurate.

**A note on partial information:** Ordinary people rarely know the full picture. They know what they've seen, heard from someone who heard from someone else, or what they've concluded from observable evidence. Their conclusions are often wrong in specific, interesting ways.

## LUMENCREST

*"The brownout schedule changed again. Third time this month. Now they're saying 'variable availability' instead of a schedule. That means they don't know anymore when it's going to happen."*
→ True. The Heart's discharge pattern is becoming less predictable. The grid office is covering this with language rather than information.

*"The Radiant Core has never had a brownout. Not once. You know what's in the Vault District that needs that much power? Neither do I. Neither does anyone."*
→ The unauthorized Vault experiments are consuming significantly more power than the Council has officially acknowledged. The Radiant Core's perfect record is not coincidence.

*"I heard three Luminarium researchers asked to leave. Not fired — asked. There's a difference. You ask someone to leave when you don't want what they know becoming public."*
→ Partially accurate. The three workers from the Vault incident were not Luminarium researchers — they were Vault technical staff. But the Luminarium has quietly removed two junior researchers in the past six months who were getting too close to Greaves' data.

*"The Conservators used to be the reasonable ones. Now even they're saying nothing? That's not reasonable. That's afraid."*
→ True. The Conservator faction has been effectively paralyzed by the gap between what they know (from Ashton's sealed briefings) and what they can say publicly.

*"First Flame congregation doubled in the Manufactories last year. Doubled. My grandmother would have called that a sign. I'm not sure she's wrong."*
→ Accurate. The First Flame growth in Lumencrest is running faster than the central leadership expected and is outpacing their capacity to doctrinally form new members.

*"Someone in the Halfling Quarter is buying preserved food in bulk. Not a family — bulk. Like you're storing for two hundred people. Nobody knows who."*
→ True. A Halfling Quarter community leader is quietly organizing a communal emergency reserve. She hasn't told anyone her source, which is someone inside the Luminarium who shared their personal risk assessment.

*"The Night Market moved twice last month. That means someone official is looking for it. When officials look for the Night Market, it means they want something from it, not that they want to shut it down."*
→ Accurate. An Expansionist Council member is trying to source something through the black market that they can't acquire through official channels — specifically, intelligence about the Endless Eye's defenses.

*"A woman came through last week. Somnarch, I think, or close to one. Asked three questions, bought a meal, left. One of the questions was about how long the lights have been flickering. She wrote down what I said."*
→ True. Vaela's field operatives are conducting informal data collection on public awareness of the Heart's decline. They're calibrating what ordinary people know vs. what the Council has admitted.

## KINGSREST

*"The Rose Festival's been quieter the last two years. The gardens are beautiful — same as always, Her Majesty sees to that — but the crowds are smaller. People are saving the travel money."*
→ True, and the reason is economic anxiety in the agricultural belt. The festival draws heavily from farming communities. Farming communities are tighter with discretionary spending than they've been in decades.

*"The harvest numbers from the Calloway estate came in. I didn't see them, but the factor did, and then he spent that evening at The Hearthstone drinking alone. Make of that what you will."*
→ The Calloway estate's real yields are about 60% of reported Accord figures. The factor is someone who was asked to sign off on falsified reports and is now uncomfortable with what he signed.

*"Prince Cedric's been asking around about the Accord granary inspections. Not officially — through people. He's careful about it. He's been careful about it for months. That's not a man who just started worrying."*
→ True. Cedric's investigation has been running six months. The fact that people are noticing he's asking carefully means his operational security is imperfect.

*"'Valorspire Remembers.' I walked past three of them this morning. The guard painted over two of them last week. They're back. Whoever's doing it, they have access to the old alphabet — that's not common knowledge."*
→ The two separate groups doing the graffiti are both escalating independently without knowing the other exists. The guard response is making both groups more committed.

*"Lady Vey hosted another salon. Fourteen people this time. Nobody I'd call a troublemaker. But she invites people for reasons, and her reasons aren't usually social."*
→ Lady Rosalind Vey's salon this month included two junior Accord trade administrators she's been quietly cultivating — people who have access to agricultural requisition records and are beginning to ask the same questions she is.

*"The King looks tired. He looked fine at midsummer. He doesn't look fine now. Nobody at court is saying anything, but they're looking at Cedric differently."*
→ Aldric IV's health has declined slightly in the past two months — not a crisis, but observable to people who see him regularly. The court is quietly recalibrating around the succession question.

## IRONHOLD

*"Three more off level seven. That's seven now who've asked for reassignment since the summer. You don't get seven experienced miners asking off the same level because of bad luck. Something's there."*
→ Accurate count. The behavioral changes in miners who've worked near the anomaly are consistent enough that other miners are drawing conclusions from the pattern.

*"Kara Coalforge requisitioned samples from the deep collection. She didn't tell anyone why. She's allowed to — she's Forge-Mistress. But she didn't tell anyone why."*
→ True. The requisition went through normally. The unusual part is the absence of any stated research purpose, which Kara omitted deliberately.

*"The Ironbeard-Stonefist vote is coming. The vote is always coming, but this time Throk actually has the numbers. Three, maybe four. That's enough to deadlock the Council for the first time in sixty years."*
→ Partially accurate. Throk has three votes. He needs four to deadlock. His fourth is someone who doesn't know they're his fourth yet.

*"I heard Grimbar had visitors. Not official — came through the lower gate, not the Great Stair. That's the entrance you use when you don't want to be announced."*
→ True. Grimbar had an informal meeting with a Starfall Seeker researcher who wanted to discuss the deep mine anomalies. Grimbar declined to share information and asked the researcher to leave. The researcher left but is still in the city.

*"My cousin works forge support on level four. Says the heat from the lower levels has been running about two degrees warmer than usual for three months. Forges were adjusted for it. Nobody said why."*
→ The Dragon Turtle parallel: the same biological warmth rising through the Isle of the Broken Sphere is the same kind of signal. In Ironhold's case, the warmth from the deep mines is geological but anomalous — something below is generating heat in a non-standard pattern.

*"Throk's granddaughter hasn't been seen at her usual station in three weeks. Official story is she's on extended shift rotation. She's never done extended shift rotation. She's the most reliably present person in her crew."*
→ True. She's one of the miners who experienced the anomaly. Throk has her in quiet isolation while he decides what to do — not as punishment, but because he doesn't want her talking before he understands what she experienced.

## STEELWATCH

*"The training schedule changed. More field exercises, fewer maintenance rotations. That means someone's preparing for something, not just maintaining readiness. There's a difference."*
→ Commander Ironside modified the schedule six weeks ago without a Marshal Council vote. Corvain knows and hasn't reversed it because she's not sure she disagrees with the decision.

*"Someone came through the south gate with Accord administrative papers, asked two questions about northern patrol routes, and left. Accord administrators don't ask about patrol routes. That's not their job."*
→ A Lumencrest Expansionist faction operative is doing pre-operational reconnaissance for the potential Endless Eye mission — gathering route data, troop availability, supply logistics. They're using Accord administrative cover.

*"The Crucible had three deaths this year. It had one death in the previous seven years. The official reports all say accidents. Three accidents in one year in a program that had one in seven years isn't accidents."*
→ True. The training intensification is producing fatalities at a rate inconsistent with the accident framing. The soldiers who know Ironside modified the schedule know the connection but haven't found a way to report it that doesn't implicate senior command.

*"Sergeant Flint's been spending time at The Threshold again. That's not a bar you go to for the drinks. That's a bar you go to when you want to talk to someone without the conversation being Steelwatch's business."*
→ Flint has made contact with someone outside Steelwatch intelligence — specifically, someone who can receive her intelligence packages without them entering official channels. She's building the courier network.

*"Marshal-General sent a personal courier north. Not along the King's Highway — north-northeast, which is the Elarindë route. Personal couriers to Elarindë aren't a thing that happens. Not officially."*
→ True. Corvain's back-channel to Rider Commander Sera Windmane has been active for three months. The soldiers who've noticed the courier are drawing accurate conclusions but have no incentive to report them.

## FROSTWELL TUNDRA

*"Hearth Six voted. Sixty-two to fifty-eight. The first Hearth to vote formally on leaving. Sixty-two stayed. But you heard me — fifty-eight. That's not a comfortable margin for something they were certain about last year."*
→ True. The Stayer majority is holding but shrinking as the star's approach becomes more visible in the northern sky. Each month the count shifts slightly toward Leavers.

*"The Ember Cradle is warm. I don't mean warm like a fire — I mean warm like it's breathing. I went there in the spring with my cousin and we both felt it. The stone was warm. The stone doesn't do that."*
→ The ancient Drakona ruins at Ember Cradle are responding to the approaching Hollostar — the same phenomenon affecting the Verdant Crescent, the Isle, the Glittering Marshes. Frostwell locals have observed it directly and are reaching their own conclusions.

*"Three outsiders with Lumencrest papers are camped at the northern observatory. They've been there a month. They're measuring something. Every few days they send a rider south. Whatever they're measuring, they're sending it to someone who wants to know fast."*
→ The Lumencrest contingent at Frostvault is in active correspondence with the Council. What they're sending is increasingly alarming — the star's approach is ahead of official projections. The riders are increasingly urgent.

*"The pilgrims from the Path of Spirits have stopped going back. Forty came up in the spring. Maybe twelve have gone home. The rest are staying. That's not how pilgrimages work."*
→ Path of Spirits pilgrims who have witnessed the approaching star and the Ember Cradle's warmth are experiencing what they interpret as a sacred moment they cannot leave. The local Drakona community is uncomfortable with the growing outsider presence at their sacred site.

*"My aunt is a Leaver. She left three weeks ago with fourteen people. She said she'd send word when they reached the Northern Expanse. I haven't heard."*
→ The first organized Leaver groups are beginning to move. The journey south is hard at this season. Several early-moving groups have encountered difficulties — not disaster, but delays and hardship. Word is slow because the conditions are hard.

*"The Barad-Khelek dwarves haven't closed their gate in living memory. It's been closed twice this month. That means their elders are meeting about something they don't want interrupted."*
→ The Barad-Khelek Clan Council is in active deliberation about whether to formally commit to the Frostwell evacuation effort — specifically, whether to open their tunnels as a migration route, which would allow Leavers to travel through the mountain rather than over it.

## NORTHERN EXPANSE

*"The river towns are full. I've been to Greenhollow four times a year for twenty years. It's never been this full in autumn. These people aren't trading — they're stopping."*
→ The early Frostwell Leavers are arriving and finding the Northern Expanse's river towns unprepared for the volume. Most are staying longer than planned while they figure out where they're actually going.

*"Elowen hasn't made a public announcement about the Frostwell situation in three months. When the king goes quiet, it means he's deciding. When he's deciding this long, it means the options are all bad."*
→ Accurate. Elowen's deliberation time has exceeded even his own advisors' patience. The decision about Frostwell refugee support has real costs either way and he is genuinely uncertain.

*"An eagle chose a girl from the Coldfast Exile community. First Exile-born rider in memory, maybe ever. The Riders are accepting her, but the older ones are having conversations about it."*
→ True, and significant. The Coldfast Exiles are a community of people who were expelled from their home settlements for various reasons — not criminals, but controversial figures. An eagle choosing one of their children is the eagle making a statement the tradition says cannot be refused.

*"Someone bought the entire stock of preserved winter provisions from the Lakemerch storehouse. The entire stock. Not individual families — someone coordinating. Nobody knows who's organizing it."*
→ Rider Commander Sera Windmane is pre-positioning supplies for the Frostwell evacuation support that she and Corvain are coordinating outside official channels. The procurement is happening through civilian brokers to avoid Accord attention.

*"The forest elves near the Deepwood haven't been at the autumn market. Two years running now. They always come to the autumn market. The frontier people have started asking each other what they think it means."*
→ The forest elf community near the Deepwood has withdrawn from ordinary contact because the ancient waking in the Verdant Crescent has been visible in smaller ways in their forest for two years. They are occupied with something they're not ready to explain to outsiders.

## GLITTERING MARSHES

*"The guides are routing differently. The eastern channel, the one that runs past the old shard beds — they've been going around it for a month. They haven't said why. Guides who don't explain route changes are guides who've seen something."*
→ The shard pulse changes have affected certain channels' behavior enough that the Listeners are recommending avoidance. The guide system is responding to Veleth data that it can't explain to outsiders in terms outsiders would understand.

*"Mist-of-Branches hasn't left Canopy Run in three weeks. She's usually everywhere. When she stays in one place, she's listening to something."*
→ True. The Veleth signal has been intensifying and Mist-of-Branches is maintaining continuous contact with the network rather than her usual traveling pattern.

*"The bioluminescence under the eastern platforms is different. It used to pulse slow — once, pause, once. Now it's doing something else. The children have noticed. When children notice something about the water, the adults should probably ask what they've noticed."*
→ The children's observation is accurate. The pulse rhythm has shifted in a way that's visible even to non-Veleth practitioners. The Listeners know what it means. Most adults are in the uncomfortable position of having noticed something and not having language for it.

*"A Simiah came back from the Salt Shore last month. She'd been gone two years. She said the rivers there are different from here but you can feel them the same way. She said it like it was obvious. It isn't obvious."*
→ A Veleth practitioner returning after extended time at a river network away from the marsh. Her casual reference to "feeling" the rivers is the first public indication that the ability works in connected water generally — most marsh residents have assumed it was marsh-specific.

*"Deepsilt sent someone up. To Canopy Run, which Deepsilt never does — they send messages, not people. Whatever the message was, it needed a person to deliver it."*
→ The Deepsilt elder delegation mentioned in The Marsh Sight document — something about the deep-channel pulse changes required direct conversation rather than the message system. Canopy Run knows Deepsilt knows. The information has not left the marsh.

## STARFALL BASIN

*"Water prices went up twelve percent last season. They went up eight percent the season before. At this rate, in five years the Basin is priced out of its own product."*
→ The Endless Eye's decline is driving water scarcity faster than the Merchant Circle's official projections have acknowledged publicly. The price increases are the market's read on the real situation.

*"Someone on the Circle is selling black market water. Not Saltmere — internal. The above-table price and the below-table price have been different for four months. That's not smugglers. That's someone with access."*
→ True. The Merchant Circle member who is the source is not Deepsound (who Moonblossom suspects) — it is a junior Fairy council member who has been quietly positioning family assets in anticipation of the water shortage becoming critical.

*"The Ribbet deep divers have been going down further than usual. You can tell because they're taking longer to surface. Something's changed in the Deeps."*
→ The Endless Eye's decline is affecting the lake's bioluminescent ecosystem in the deep sections — the Ribbet divers are following their traditional relationship with the light, which is moving deeper as the Eye weakens. They haven't reported this formally. It's being observed.

*"A Katari route-keeper came through and asked whether the road north — the one that goes toward Frostwell — had been cleared recently. Nobody's cleared that road in years. He seemed unsurprised by this but took notes."*
→ The Katari are observing the Frostwell situation from the perspective of route-keepers: if large populations are going to move, existing routes need assessment. The route-keeper asking isn't planning migration — they're doing early reconnaissance for others who will need the information.

*"The Star's Birth festival is in two months. Someone on the planning committee has been asking whether to keep the traditional candle-circle ceremony or if it's 'inappropriate given current events.' The debate has been going on for six weeks without resolution."*
→ The Star's Birth festival — celebrating the original Hollostar fall — is genuinely complicated this year given the Eye's decline and the approaching fourth star. The ceremony is a celebration of a star-fall. People are uncertain whether celebrating that is appropriate, prescient, or darkly ironic.

## SALT SHORE

*"Voss came to Salthook personally. Not a representative — Voss. He walked the docks, talked to the harbor workers, ate at the public table. He hasn't done that in years. He does that when he's worried about something and wants people to know he's paying attention."*
→ True. Saltmere's influence expansion into the Shore has reached a stage where Voss needs visible presence to reassure his network. The personal visit is strategic, not social.

*"The Saltmere factor at Tidewrack bought three buildings last month. Not renting — buying. When they buy instead of rent, they're planning to stay longer than a trade season."*
→ Saltmere's expansion strategy has moved from commercial influence to physical presence in specific Shore towns. The building purchases are the first stage of what amounts to establishing a permanent administrative structure.

*"A diver came up from the Sunken Crown site. Not one of ours — someone with Lumencrest papers. He went down, came up, left the same day without talking to anyone. What's at the Sunken Crown that Lumencrest wants to see personally?"*
→ The Lumencrest Expansionist faction has been quietly assessing pre-Hollostar sites for energy extraction potential. The Sunken Crown is one of twelve sites on their internal list. They don't know what Voss knows about it.

*"Four fishing boats from Coralbreak. Galapa boats, Isle registration. Moored overnight, left without trading. I've never seen Isle boats this far north. Whatever they needed to see, it wasn't the market."*
→ Galapa from the Isle are making quiet reconnaissance of potential evacuation destinations as the Sleeper situation intensifies. They're not ready to announce this purpose. They're gathering information about where people go when islands become uninhabitable.

## SILVERBELT COAST

*"House Vethara bought out two more independent captains this month. That's nine this year. At this rate there won't be an independent fleet within three years. Some of the captains are selling because they want to. Some of them aren't being asked."*
→ Captain Rynn Saltblood's expansion of Vethara's fleet is accelerating. The "not being asked" captains are receiving offers structured so that refusing is commercially nonviable — not overtly threatening, just economically decisive.

*"The Coral territory markers have moved. Not much — maybe fifty yards. But the Coral don't move their markers. In ten years of dealing with them, I have never seen the markers move. Something changed."*
→ The Coral (sea people) are responding to changes in coastal water conditions — the same broad network of changes affecting shard-rich water environments across the continent. The marker movement is boundary reassertion in response to feeling unsettled in their territory.

*"Lumencrest has been pushing the timber commission harder. Forty percent above last season's request. The Houses have been saying no. Lumencrest hasn't dropped the ask, just... gone quiet about it. That's not dropping it."*
→ The Lumencrest Council's timber request is connected to Vault District construction — expanding the experimental facilities. The Houses' refusal has slowed the project but not stopped it; Lumencrest is sourcing timber through alternative channels, which is more expensive and less reliable.

*"A Green Covenant field agent has been in Silverport for two weeks. She's been visiting every timber merchant, every shipper. She's polite. She's thorough. She is absolutely mapping the Heartlands supply chain."*
→ True. The Covenant is documenting Lumencrest's timber acquisition patterns as part of the broader conflict over Heartlands forestry. The data will eventually be used as leverage — either publicly or in private negotiations.

## VERDANT CRESCENT

*"The Faun haven't been to Thornedge market in three years. Three years. My father traded with them every autumn. They've stopped coming and nobody has explained why, and I'm starting to think the explanation is something nobody wants to say out loud."*
→ The Faun community that traditionally attended Thornedge market stopped visiting when they began avoiding the area near the ancient waking site — which is between their seasonal path and Thornedge. They know something is wrong there. They haven't found a way to explain it that outsiders will receive without panic.

*"The Thorns posted a notice at Heartgrove. Not a threat — just a notice. 'This road now requires Covenant escort.' It's a road that's been open for forty years. What's on that road now that requires escorting?"*
→ The unauthorized Thorns action is in its preparation phase. The escort requirement is protecting their operational movements from observation — framed as safety, actually about controlling who sees what on that route.

*"Something moved in the old grove last night. Not an animal — something that moved through the canopy at a speed nothing natural does, and it left no tracks. Three people saw it. All three told different stories about what it looked like."*
→ The ancient waking is producing observable phenomena in the deep Verdant Crescent that edge-settlement residents are witnessing without framework. The "different stories" are accurate — what people are seeing genuinely presents differently to different observers.

*"Archcovenant Maren Stillwater was at Heartgrove for a week. She's usually in the deep forest. When she comes to Heartgrove, she's dealing with something political. When she stays a week, it's not going well."*
→ True. Maren is managing the Thorns situation and the ancient waking simultaneously, and the two problems are beginning to intersect in ways she hadn't anticipated.

## DESERT OF GLASS

*"The pilgrimage route to Cindrath has been busy this season. Not the usual traffic — more, and different people. Some of them don't look like pilgrims. They look like people who heard something and want to see it with their own eyes."*
→ Word of Cindrath's increased activity — she's been more accessible and more talkative than usual as the crisis deepens — has reached communities far enough out that unusual travelers are arriving. Not all of them are there for religious reasons.

*"Ember Refuge is expanding. Third time in five years. Clanks don't reproduce, so expanding means arrivals — Clanks who've been somewhere else and decided to come here. That means somewhere else stopped being tenable."*
→ True. Clanks in Lumencrest's labor pool have been quietly leaving — not in organized groups, just individually choosing to disappear rather than wait for the Heart's failure to create a crisis for them. Ember Refuge has been absorbing them. Nobody in Lumencrest has officially noticed the attrition.

*"Someone from the Infernis radical faction was in Ashenveil last week, asking about Cindrath. Not asking to see her — asking about her. What she's done, who's visited her, what she's said. That's not reverence. That's assessment."*
→ The Infernis radical faction ("awakening the demon within") is trying to determine whether Cindrath's recent increased accessibility represents an opportunity. They want to know if she's sympathetic to their position before approaching her. She is not.

*"The Skyspine vents have been more active this month. The geomancers in Cinderhome say it's normal variation. The Drakona herders say the geomancers haven't been out to the Skyspine in six months and wouldn't know normal variation if it burned them."*
→ The Skyspine activity is a low-level manifestation of the same deep-site activation affecting other ancient locations. The geomancers' assessment is based on old data. The herders are right to be skeptical.

## ISLE OF THE BROKEN SPHERE

*"The tremors are irregular now. They used to be every four hours — you could set a schedule by them. Now they come whenever. That change happened three weeks ago and nobody has explained it."*
→ The Dragon Turtle is beginning to shift position rather than simply pulse. The transition from rhythmic to irregular is the creature's body starting to move — muscles and joints that haven't flexed in millennia beginning to do so. This is the earliest physical indicator of approach to actual movement.

*"Brint's departure numbers for the last month are the highest they've been. He won't say that officially, but his face says it. When the harbormaster looks like that, you watch which direction the ships are going."*
→ True. The departure rate from the Isle has increased 40% over the previous month. Most departures are young families — the demographic Maret Shell-Young has been organizing. Elder Tess is aware. She doesn't have a response yet.

*"The Meridian Survey has been anchored for three weeks. They came to survey and they haven't left. Survey vessels don't anchor for three weeks. Either they found something, or they can't agree on what they found."*
→ Captain Holdfast's survey crew is in an internal standoff. Researcher Dann wants to continue investigation. An embedded agent wants to transmit findings to Lumencrest immediately. Holdfast is managing the situation by not making a decision about departure, which is itself a decision.

*"Elder Tess has been sitting at the Spine Stones for two days. Just sitting. She doesn't do that — she does ceremony, she does deliberate practice, but not two days of sitting. She's waiting for something."*
→ True. The Dragon Turtle's transition from rhythmic pulse to irregular movement has produced a change in what Elder Tess perceives in meditation. Something that was distant is closer. She's waiting to understand what it's saying before deciding what to do.

## CROSS-REGIONAL — THINGS TRAVELERS ARE SAYING

*These are things that circulate through the roads and trade routes — picked up in Lumencrest, repeated in Kingsrest, arriving in Frostwell garbled but recognizable.*

---

*"Lumencrest's lights are going out. Not someday — already. The Manufactories went dark for six hours last week. Six hours."*
→ Accurate. The brownout duration is increasing. The Manufactories is not the only district experiencing extended outages anymore.

*"There's a star falling. North. Everyone north of Steelwatch has seen it. It's not falling yet — it's getting closer. Slower than the stories say. Slower and more certain."*
→ True. The approaching fourth Hollostar is visible on clear nights in the northern sky — not as a falling star yet, but as a star that grows slightly larger each month. The people who've seen it describe the effect as "a light that knows where it's going."

*"The Somnarchs are giving briefings. They've never given briefings. They observe and archive and stay out of things. When they start briefing factions, they've decided something changed."*
→ True. Vaela's decision to brief all three Council factions separately (giving each a different piece) is unprecedented and has been noticed by people adjacent to all three factions. Nobody outside the Somnarchs understands why she's doing it this way.

*"High Flame Kaida hasn't spoken publicly in two months. She's usually giving addresses every few weeks. When she goes quiet, her followers get loud. There's a doctrinal dispute — the kind you don't announce."*
→ True. Kaida has no divine guidance to share and is managing the denomination crisis through silence rather than misdirection. Her followers are filling the quiet with their own interpretations, which diverge in uncomfortable ways.

*"The old woman they call Mara was in Kingsrest last month. Then in Ironhold two weeks later. Then someone says they saw her at the Salt Shore. Either there's more than one of her or she moves faster than she should."*
→ Old Mara does move faster than she appears to. She has a specific itinerary that she has kept to herself, and the seventh sign she's been waiting for is getting close.

*"Whatever's happening in Frostwell — the star, the evacuation, all of it — it's going to come south. Maybe not this year. Maybe not next. But a people in motion has to go somewhere. Every region is going to have to decide how they feel about that."*
→ True, and this is the rumor that most accurately captures the continental stakes. It is being said everywhere and believed everywhere, and the decisions it's forcing have barely begun.
