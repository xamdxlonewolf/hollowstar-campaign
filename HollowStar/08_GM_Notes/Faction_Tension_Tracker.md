# Faction Tension Tracker

Current state of every major faction at campaign start — **700 AR, Late Goldmonth**. Includes internal tensions, key relationships, what they're doing right now, and what happens if players never engage with them.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS

**The "If Ignored" column is not a punishment.** It's what the world does without player input. Some factions stabilize on their own. Some deteriorate. This gives you a baseline to diverge from based on player choices.

**Update this document as the campaign progresses.** Mark faction states, note key relationship shifts, track when players have significantly affected a faction's trajectory.

## CONTINENTAL FACTIONS

---

### Council of Lumens (Lumencrest)
**Current state:** Paralyzed. Three factions (Conservators, Illuminists, Expansionists) cannot reach consensus on any significant decision. The Heart dims while they debate.

**Internal tensions:**
- Conservators want to protect the Heart and maintain current Accord relationships — increasingly seen as doing nothing
- Illuminists want aggressive research; some running unauthorized Vault experiments Councilor Mira Ashton doesn't fully know about
- Expansionists believe the only solution is acquiring the Endless Eye by force; gaining support as crisis deepens

**Right now:** Councilor Ashton (current chair) is desperately trying to keep the Council from publicly splitting. The Vault District is running more experiments than anyone on the Council has authorized. Professor Talan Greaves is hiding a discovery about the Heart's failure rate.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 3: Unauthorized Vault experiments trigger minor incident. Council factions use it to attack each other. Public confidence drops.
- Month 6: Expansionists gain majority support. Military planning for Endless Eye operation begins.
- Month 10: Expansionists force Council vote. Military operation authorized. Merchant Circle responds with trade blockade.
- Impact day: Heart fails completely without warning. 90,000 people in darkness.

**What players can change:** Warning the Council about Heart failure timing (Null's data). Exposing the unauthorized Vault experiments. Finding whatever Greaves is hiding. Blocking or enabling the Endless Eye military operation.

### The Somnarchs
**Current state:** Urgent outreach. Factions that ignored them for decades are suddenly taking meetings. Eight members are refusing evacuation at Frostvault. The Heart Warning decision is unresolved.

**Internal tensions:**
- Rhen Duskhollow (field ops) believes the order should be actively interfering in factional politics, not just advising
- Vaela's non-intervention philosophy is being tested by the urgency
- Eight observatory holdouts represent a genuine split between those who see witnessing as duty and those who see it as suicide

**Right now:** Vaela is meeting separately with all three Lumencrest Council factions, giving each a different piece of the picture. Intentionally. She hasn't decided about the Heart Warning. Archive copies are being distributed to three locations.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 2: Heart Warning decision reached by default — they say nothing. Vaela believes silence is the lesser harm.
- Month 8: Frostvault abandoned by most, holdouts remain.
- Impact day: Frostvault holdouts document the impact. Most die. The data survives.

**What players can change:** The Heart Warning decision. Getting the holdouts out. Vaela's fifth fall calculation — who learns about it and when.

### The Hexarchs
**Current state:** Fractured. Maintaining public unity while pursuing incompatible agendas privately.

**Internal tensions:**
- Hexarch Aldren Voss-Karath (Architect) is the dominant political force, pragmatic
- Hexarch of the Wanderer is sympathetic to First Flame positions — quietly
- Radical sect (2 of 6 Hexarchs) privately believe the Hollowstars are the gods
- Moderate majority wants the radical sect suppressed; Voss-Karath is holding them back to see if they're right

**Right now:** The radical sect has sent a researcher to Frostwell to observe the approaching star "theologically." Voss-Karath knows about this and hasn't stopped it. Moderate Hexarchs don't know he knows.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 4: Radical sect researcher returns with data. Internal Hexarch argument escalates past manageable.
- Month 7: Voss-Karath makes his decision about the radical sect — either suppression or quiet endorsement.
- Impact day: The theological crisis of the Hollowstars being the gods (or not) breaks the Hexarchs publicly.

**What players can change:** The researcher's findings. Whether the moderate Hexarchs learn about Voss-Karath's inaction. The timing of the internal rupture.

### The First Flame
**Current state:** Opportunistic expansion. Growing fastest among urban populations frightened by institutional failure. Internal coherence maintained by Kaida's personal authority — which has a limited lifespan.

**Internal tensions:**
- Purifying Flame wants to declare the crisis divine judgment and pursue aggressive reclamation of Hollostar use from "unworthy" hands
- Scholarly Flame is publishing research that will eventually contradict Purifying Flame doctrine
- Kindling is converting people who aren't fully theologically formed — creating a large, loosely attached membership
- The Unbound are recruiting from all six denominations at historic rates

**Right now:** Kaida has received no divine guidance. She hasn't told anyone. She is managing the denominations through personal authority alone and it is working for now.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 5: Scholarly Flame publishes the contradictory research. Purifying Flame responds publicly. First visible First Flame doctrinal split.
- Kaida's health decline: ongoing. Succession crisis comes when it comes.
- Impact day: Every denomination interprets the impact according to their own theology. Without Kaida's authority, no unified First Flame response.

**What players can change:** Kaida's sealed letters. The succession problem. The Scholarly Flame research — suppress, publish, or find a third path.

### The Veilmarkets
**Current state:** Watching carefully, positioning for advantage, sitting on information.

**Internal tensions:** None visible. Sable runs a tight operation. The tension is Sable's internal decision — sell the secret, share it, or act on it personally.

**Right now:** Veilmarkets information-buying activity has spiked. Sable is assembling the final pieces of something. Regional bosses have been told to maintain strict neutrality and not to favor any faction's requests over another.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 4: Sable makes a decision. The secret goes to whoever has been most profitable to the network.
- Subsequent: Whatever the secret contains, it enters the world through the worst possible channel.

**What players can change:** Who Sable trusts. Who gets the secret. Whether it becomes a weapon or a gift.

---

### Starfall Seekers
**Current state:** Racing north. Multiple competing expeditions. No accountability, no coordination, dangerous.

**Right now:** Denn Harrowfield's expedition is the best-organized and closest to useful answers. Three other expeditions are ahead of his geographically but less careful. One of them is about to excavate something they shouldn't.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 2: Reckless excavation triggers a site event. Multiple Seeker deaths. Somnarchs furious. The site is damaged.
- Month 6: Harrowfield reaches Frostwell. His expedition is the last organized research effort before the impact window.

**What players can change:** What the reckless excavation releases. Whether Harrowfield's data survives. Whether his theory about the Hollowstars gets out before or after the impact.

### The Tide King (Salt Shore)
**Current state:** Expanding steadily. Three communities in negotiation. Saltmere relationship deteriorated to cold war.

**Internal tensions:** None from Voss — the organization reflects him. Tension is external: the communities that have been under protection long enough to notice they can't actually leave.

**Right now:** Voss is pursuing the three negotiating communities (Harborgate, Driftwood Point, Shallowsend). One is willing, one is reluctant, one is actively looking for alternatives. He's also quietly investigating something about his past that one of his regional factors stumbled onto.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 3: Two of three communities join. Voss's network reaches critical mass on the northern Salt Shore.
- Month 8: Saltmere attempts to organize a counter-coalition. Voss responds.
- Impact day: Tide King network becomes essential coastal crisis response infrastructure — whether Voss intended this or not.

**What players can change:** The third community's choice. What Voss discovers about his past. Whether the Saltmere confrontation becomes violent.

### Green Covenant (Verdant Crescent)
**Current state:** Cold standoff with Lumencrest. Maren managing the Thorns from the inside. Ancient waking happening in the background.

**Internal tensions:**
- Thorns faction is operating beyond Maren's authorization — Warden of the Reach (Dusk) is running an unauthorized Lumencrest alliance
- Warden of Memory (Chit) wants the 2,000-year records used, not buried
- Maren carrying the records' most dangerous information alone

**Right now:** The Thorns have a major action planned targeting a Lumencrest contractor. Maren doesn't know the details. Dusk does. The ancient waking is accelerating faster than Maren has told her Wardens.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 2: Thorns action executes. First fatalities in the Covenant-Lumencrest conflict.
- Month 5: Maren forced to either condemn the Thorns publicly (fractures the Covenant) or defend them (escalates conflict with Accord).

**What players can change:** The Thorns action. Whether the 2,000-year records get shared. Whether Maren finds the "third option" the ancient waking might require.

### The Merchant Circle (Starfall Basin)
**Current state:** Divided. Three internal positions on the Eye crisis producing gridlock.

**Internal tensions:**
- Fairy elder Moonblossom wants to maintain the status quo and manage decline quietly
- Ribbet merchant prince Deepsound wants aggressive action — expand water rationing, increase prices, extract maximum value now
- The third faction (young mixed-heritage councilors) wants regional confederation that gives up Merchant Circle control in exchange for wider support

**Right now:** Black market water sales are already happening. Someone on the Circle is the source. Moonblossom suspects Deepsound. She's wrong.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 4: Black market source identified. Internal crisis. Circle fractures along existing fault lines.
- Month 7: Deepsound's faction moves for emergency powers. Crisis governance begins.
- Impact: Eye's decline accelerates catastrophically. Merchant Circle unable to manage the crisis in fractured state.

**What players can change:** The black market source (it's not who anyone thinks). Whether the third faction's confederation idea gets traction. Whether the Circle is unified or fractured when the Eye acceleration hits.

### The Four Houses (Silverbelt Coast)
**Current state:** Deadlocked by House Caldren's succession crisis.

**Internal tensions:**
- House Caldren's heir is dead; succession disputed between two claimants
- Houses Vethara and Aldric are maneuvering to benefit from Caldren's weakness
- House Sera is quietly in contact with House Aldric behind Vethara's back (Lady Sera considering a switch)
- The Grey Sails pirate fleet is operating on someone's authorization — the Houses all suspect each other

**Right now:** Caldren succession hearing scheduled in 6 weeks. The hearing will be contested. Someone is funding the Grey Sails and it's about to become visible.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 2: Caldren succession contested. Council of Banners deadlocked.
- Month 4: Grey Sails funding source becomes known. House responsible faces expulsion from Council.
- Month 6: Silverbelt Coast political stability collapses. External powers (Tide King, Merchant Circle) move in.

**What players can change:** The succession outcome. Who's funding the Grey Sails. Whether Sera's defection happens.

### King Elowen Arathas (Northern Expanse)
**Current state:** Moving too slowly for the crisis, for knowable reasons he hasn't shared.

**Internal tensions:** The vacancy of the Warden of the North creates a coordination vacuum that's causing direct harm. Elowen knows this. His three candidates each solve the immediate crisis and create a worse one in 30 years.

**Right now:** Celebrantford (refugee ground zero) is on the edge of declaring independent emergency governance. Elowen's Chancellor knows. Elowen doesn't yet.

**If players never engage:**
- Month 2: Celebrantford declares emergency self-governance. Technically legal. Sets precedent.
- Month 4: Three other frontier towns follow. Elowen's authority over the northern frontier is effectively gone.
- Month 8: Elowen makes a Warden appointment. The political damage from 8 months of drift is already done.

**What players can change:** Finding Elowen's fourth option. Getting the Celebrantford information to him before it becomes a precedent. The Warden appointment itself.

---

### Rivermark Sovereignty (Central Heartlands East)
**Current state:** Patient outward, actively maneuvering inward.

**Internal tensions:** The younger/Faun majority vs. older elves and some Drakona who prefer maintained independence. Mirelen's undisclosed Merchant Circle offer is the real tension — it exists only in her mind, but it will surface.

**Right now:** The Sixth Seat vote is stalled pending Lumencrest's stability. Mirelen has been contacted by the Merchant Circle. She hasn't answered. She has two weeks before the Circle withdraws the offer.

**If players never engage:**
- Two weeks: Merchant Circle offer expires. Mirelen lets it expire. Campaign continues as normal.
- OR: Mirelen takes the offer. Rivermark pivots east. The Sixth Seat campaign ends. The Accord loses its eastern buffer.

**What players can change:** Everything hinges on whether they're involved when Mirelen makes her choice. With party engagement, the offer becomes a plot point. Without it, it resolves quietly one way or the other.
