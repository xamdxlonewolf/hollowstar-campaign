# The Starfall Seekers

**Type:** Loose scholarly and exploratory network — not a unified organization  
**Power Base:** No fixed territory. Offices in the Endless Eye, Lumencrest, and the Silverbelt Coast. Field operations wherever Hollowstars have fallen.  
**Size:** Unknown. Hundreds of active members; thousands of informal participants. No central membership roll.  
**Leader:** No central authority. **Expedition Leader Tam Coldridge** is the most organized and prominent current figure.  
**Primary Goal:** Find, study, and understand Hollostar fragments and fall sites — though individual motivations vary wildly  
**Alignment:** Fragmented. Ranges from rigorous scholars to reckless treasure hunters to obsessive zealots, often in the same expedition.

---

## CORE IDENTITY

The Starfall Seekers are what happens when a subject becomes so compelling that it attracts everyone — the careful, the careless, the brilliant, and the dangerous — without any filtering mechanism. They are not an institution. There is no charter, no hierarchy, no code of conduct that binds all Seekers to each other. What binds them is a shared object of obsession: the Hollowstars.

The network emerged organically over centuries as researchers, explorers, and fragment hunters discovered they needed each other — shared maps, shared data, shared access to sites too large for a single expedition. They cooperate when cooperation is useful and compete when it isn't. They publish contradictory research. They steal from each other. They also, occasionally, produce genuine scholarly breakthroughs that no single member could have achieved alone.

**What distinguishes the Seekers from the Somnarchs:**  
The Somnarchs study the *system* — the cycles, the patterns, the long view. The Seekers study the *event* — individual Hollowstars, specific fragments, immediate effects. The Somnarchs consider the Seekers capable but myopic. The Seekers consider the Somnarchs rigorous but paralyzed by abstraction. Both assessments have merit.

---

## WHO THE SEEKERS ARE

The network contains multitudes. Broad categories:

**Legitimate Scholars**  
Researchers affiliated with universities, temples, or independent institutions who study Hollostar science with rigor and ethics. Publish their findings. Share data. Object when colleagues cut corners. They are a minority, but they produce most of the network's actual knowledge.

**Treasure Hunters**  
In it for the fragment market. Hollostar shards are valuable and getting more valuable as the fourth fall approaches. Treasure hunters move fast, document minimally, and sell quickly. They damage sites. They also find sites that scholars would never reach. The Seekers' relationship with treasure hunters is one of the network's permanent internal tensions.

**Expedition Opportunists**  
People who attach to Seeker expeditions for their own purposes — access to restricted areas, protection for travel through dangerous regions, cover for activities they'd rather not explain. Some are useful. Some are liability.

**Zealots**  
A significant minority who have developed religious or philosophical frameworks around the Hollowstars that go beyond scholarship. Some overlap with the radical Hexarch sect's position (the stars are divine). Some have developed entirely idiosyncratic theologies. Most are harmless. Some have made dangerous decisions based on beliefs that have no evidentiary basis.

**Independent Operators**  
Seekers who work alone or in small groups, publishing or selling what they find without organizational affiliation. Some of the network's most significant discoveries have come from independent operators. Some of the network's worst accidents have too.

## KEY FIGURES

### Expedition Leader Tam Coldridge
The most organized and prominent Seeker currently active. Runs the largest Hollostar expedition on the continent — currently based in Frostwell, studying the approaching star's effects on the ancient ruins and local shard deposits.

Coldridge is unusual among Seekers in having genuine institutional discipline: rigorous documentation, ethical standards about site preservation, and a willingness to share data with the Somnarchs when data-sharing serves the larger goal. This makes some Seekers respect them enormously and others consider them insufficiently ambitious.

**Current state:** The expedition is operating under conditions that are getting more dangerous as the fourth fall approaches. Coldridge has calculated a discrepancy in the impact timeline — their data suggests the fall is coming faster than the Somnarchs' published estimate. They haven't released this yet. They are checking it a third time.

**Goals:** Document everything. Resolve the discrepancy. Be present at the impact. They are aware this last goal may be fatal. They have decided this is acceptable. The other Seekers in the expedition have been given the option to leave; none have taken it.

## ACTIVITIES

**Fragment excavation** — Digging at known fall sites, recovering shards and studying their properties in situ. The largest ongoing excavation is in the Frostwell region, where the ancient ruins suggest a significant deposit from the first fall.

**Fragment trade** — Buying, selling, and brokering shard transactions. The legitimate scholarly wing objects to this; it drives prices up, pulls fragments out of research contexts, and creates a market that attracts dangerous people. The treasure hunters object to the objections.

**Astronomical observation** — Tracking the approaching star. Multiple independent Seeker observation posts exist across the continent. Their data does not always agree with each other, which is one of the network's current internal problems.

**Ancient site research** — Studying the ruins left by the pre-Hollostar civilization and the transformed landscapes from previous falls. The Frostvault, the Heart's crater formation, the Endless Eye basin. Sites that predate recorded history.

**Publication** — Contradictory, competitive, occasionally brilliant. The Seekers produce more published research on Hollostar science than any other source, including the Somnarchs (who tend toward internal documentation rather than public dissemination). The quality range is enormous.

## RELATIONSHIPS WITH OTHER FACTIONS

**Somnarchs** — Collegial rivalry. They share data more often than either publicly admits. The philosophical divide is real. The practical cooperation is also real. Neither organization is stupid enough to let the friction become hostility during a crisis that requires both of them to function.

**Lumencrest / Council of Lumens** — Multiple layers. The Conservators fund legitimate Seeker research through official channels and use the research to maintain their position on the Council. The Illuminists want more access to Seeker data than the Seekers want to give. The Expansionists have used Seeker expeditions as cover for intelligence operations.

**Merchant Circle (Endless Eye)** — The Seekers maintain an office in the Endless Eye and have been trying to gain expanded access to The Deeps for years. The Merchant Circle has declined repeatedly. The Pragmatist faction on the Circle is the closest to sympathetic; the Traditionalists consider Seeker interest in the Hollostar a threat to the lake's stability.

**Six-Fold Faith** — Complicated. The moderate Hexarchs fund specific Seeker expeditions quietly. The radical sect has attempted to embed theological observers in Seeker expeditions. The Scholarly Flame denomination of the First Flame is the Seekers' most reliable religious partner for legitimate research collaboration.

**Veilmarkets** — Transactional. Seekers who need fragments that aren't on the official market use Veilmarket channels. Seekers who object to this spend a lot of time being frustrated by colleagues who don't. The Veilmarkets' fragment prices have increased substantially as Seeker demand has grown.

**Green Covenant** — Tense. Several Seeker expeditions have damaged Verdant Crescent sites. The Covenant has physically blocked two excavation attempts in the past decade. Coldridge's expedition has a better relationship with the Covenant than most — they documented and repaired damage rather than disputing liability, which the Covenant noticed.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**Race to the Site:** A new fall site (or a newly discovered fragment deposit) has been identified. Multiple Seeker factions are moving toward it — with different intentions, different methods, and incompatible claims. The party arrives in the middle of a three-way dispute about who gets access, how the site should be handled, and whether the find should be published, sold, or hidden.

**The Discrepancy:** Coldridge's timeline discrepancy becomes known to the party before it becomes public. The fourth fall may be coming sooner than anyone has told the public. Who should know, and when, is suddenly a decision the party is positioned to influence.

**The Dangerous Experiment:** A Seeker researcher has been conducting unauthorized experiments with a significant fragment. The results are concerning enough that Coldridge has sent word asking for outside assistance — the researcher is a senior member of the network and Coldridge doesn't have the authority to stop them directly. The party is asked to assess the situation and, if necessary, intervene.

**The Frostwell Expedition:** The party joins, encounters, or is hired to assist Coldridge's expedition. This puts them in the Frostwell Tundra with the fourth fall approaching, a significant ancient ruin in the vicinity, a community of people who may not survive what's coming, and the most complete Hollostar data set on the continent. Things escalate.

**The Stolen Data:** Someone has stolen research from a Seeker expedition — not fragments, but documentation. Maps, measurements, analysis of what lies in the lower chambers of a specific ruin. Several parties would pay for this. The Seekers want it back. The party is caught in the middle.

## GM NOTES

**Tone:** The Seekers should feel simultaneously inspiring and alarming. The legitimate researchers are doing genuinely important work under genuinely dangerous conditions, motivated by real curiosity and real courage. The reckless elements are genuinely reckless — not cartoonishly evil, just people who care more about what they find than the cost of finding it. The tension between these elements is the Seekers' defining characteristic.

**Coldridge specifically** is a character players should be able to trust — reliable, honest, clear about their own limits and motivations. Their willingness to die for the work should not be played as suicidal; it should feel like a considered decision made by someone who has thought it through. That makes it more unsettling, not less.

**The fragment economy** is a background element that can become foreground whenever useful. Shard prices rising, black market access, dangerous buyers — all of this connects the Seekers to the Veilmarkets, to Lumencrest's resource anxiety, and to the broader question of who gets to have Hollostar technology.
