# Council of Lumens

Lumencrest's governing body and the most powerful political institution in the Central Heartlands.  
For regional dynamics and the Heartland Accord, see [[04_Factions/Central_Heartlands/Power and Politics|Power and Politics]].

---

**Type:** Republican council  
**Base:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Locations/Cities/Lumencrest|Lumencrest]]  
**Reach:** Central Heartlands, significant influence across the continent  
**Public Mandate:** Govern Lumencrest, steward the Heart, maintain regional stability  
**Current Reality:** A body paralyzed by internal faction conflict at exactly the moment the crisis demands decisive action

---

## The Heart of Lumencrest

**What it is:** The second Hollostar - an intact fallen star that powers Lumencrest's unique electrical technology. The only functioning, controlled Hollostar energy source on the continent.

**When it fell:** Approximately 3,000 years ago (estimates vary - records from that era are fragmentary). The impact created what is now Central Heartlands' most fertile region.

**The discovery:** Ancient civilization (possibly same as built Frostwell ruins) knew how to harness Hollostar energy. Their knowledge was lost. Lumencrest founders rediscovered fragments of this technology ~1,200 years ago when founding the city on the impact site.

**How it works:** The Heart sits deep beneath Lumencrest in heavily fortified vault. Technocrats channel its energy through complex infrastructure - the Lumen Network - providing electrical power to the city. This technology is Lumencrest's greatest advantage and deepest secret.

**The crisis:** The Heart is dimming. Slowly but measurably. Power output declining ~2% annually for last 30 years. Nobody knows why. Nobody knows how to stop it.

**The current state:** Brownouts occur 2-4 times per month (was 1-2 times monthly a year ago), lasting 15-60 minutes. Official explanation is "scheduled maintenance." Nobody believes this. Manufacturing output down 15-20%. The city's golden age is ending and the Council is paralyzed over what to do.

## History: How Lumencrest Rose

### The Founding (1,200 Years Ago)

Scholars and engineers discovered the buried Hollostar while excavating ancient ruins. They recognized its potential - controllable energy, unlike the dangerous shards scattered elsewhere. The city of Lumencrest was founded specifically to harness this power.

### The Illumination (800-600 Years Ago)

Lumencrest perfected Hollostar energy extraction. Electrical lighting, powered machinery, technologies impossible elsewhere. The city exploded in population as people migrated seeking opportunity. While other cities relied on muscle, water, or wind power, Lumencrest had electricity.

### The Accord Formation (400 Years Ago)

As Lumencrest grew powerful, neighboring cities faced choice: resist or cooperate. The Heartland Accord formed - five major cities coordinating trade, defense, infrastructure. Lumencrest dominated from the start but maintained fiction of equality.

### The Modern Era and the Crisis

For centuries, Lumencrest's dominance was unquestioned. The technology gap widened. Other cities resented dependence but couldn't challenge Lumencrest's supremacy.

**Then, 30 years ago, the Heart began dimming.**

The Council has spent three decades arguing about solutions while the crisis slowly accelerates.

## Structure

The Council seats **thirty-two members** — a deliberate mix of old aristocratic families who've held seats for generations and technocratic meritocrats who earned their place through expertise or wealth.

**Seat types:**
- 20 seats: Hereditary (old aristocratic families)
- 10 seats: Technocratic (elected by guilds, academic institutions, manufactories)  
- 2 seats: Popular (elected by citizen vote, 5-year terms)

Major decisions require a majority vote (17 of 32). In practice, no major decision has passed cleanly in over a year. The three internal factions have turned every vote into a negotiation, and negotiations into delays.

**Current chair:** **Councilor Mira Ashton** (Illuminist) - the chair rotates annually. She is not beloved but she is effective at keeping the body from completely fracturing, which may be the most anyone can ask right now.

Meetings are held in the Accord Chamber in Lumencrest's civic quarter — a grand room designed for a city at the height of its confidence. It feels wrong now. People speak more quietly than the architecture expects.

## The Three Factions

### The Conservators (Currently ~11-12 seats)

**Core belief:** The experiments are making it worse. Shut them down before the Heart fails entirely.

**Who they are:** Primarily the old aristocratic families — names that have held Council seats for four and five generations. They remember when Lumencrest's power was stable and self-sustaining, and they blame the Illuminists' aggressive research programs for destabilizing something that was working.

**Their support base:** 
- The rationing class - factory workers on reduced hours, merchants whose supply chains depend on powered infrastructure
- Citizens whose lives have gotten materially worse since dimming started
- Religious groups who see the Heart as sacred
- Anyone who just wants things to go back to normal

**What they want:** 
- Full moratorium on experimental shard extraction and energy research
- Stabilization of what remains
- 40% power reduction to let Heart recover (if it can)
- Diplomatic outreach to the Endless Eye as long-term solution — negotiated, not seized

**Their problem:** They don't have a solution, they have a preference. "Stop making it worse" is not a plan for a city whose power source is dying. The Illuminists ask this every time, and the Conservators don't have a good answer.

**Key figures:** 
- **Lord Edran Voss-Harwick** - Eldest of old families, 68, on Council for 30 years. Deeply conservative, deeply connected, genuinely frightened (which he will not show).

**Recent setbacks (last 2 years):**
- Lost 3-4 seats to other factions as crisis worsens
- Voluntary conservation program failed (asked for 20% reduction, achieved only 3%)
- Heart decline accelerated despite conservation measures (from 2% to 2.5% annually)

---

### The Illuminists (Currently ~12-13 seats, plurality)

**Core belief:** The answer is more knowledge, more research, more effort. We haven't found the solution yet because we haven't looked hard enough.

**Who they are:** The technocrats, the inventors, the ambitious scholars who built Lumencrest's reputation for innovation. They represent the city's self-image as a place that solves problems through ingenuity.

**Current operations:** Running seventeen simultaneous research programs, four of which are classified even from other Council members, and two of which Councilor Ashton privately believes are genuinely dangerous.

**Their support base:** 
- Manufacturing sector dependent on electrical power
- Academic institutions (research funding flows from Illuminist policies)
- Starfall Seekers (Illuminists fund them and share information)
- Anyone whose livelihood depends on Lumencrest remaining technological center

**What they want:** 
- More resources for research, fewer restrictions on methodology
- Access to shard sites across continent (especially Glittering Marshes)
- Working agreement with Endless Eye for study access to functioning Hollostar
- Hazelhaven tech assistance program to continue (proof of concept for Lumencrest influence)
- Access to Glittering Marshes shard concentration beneath Deepsilt (desperately wanted)

**Their problem:** They've been the dominant faction for six years and the Heart is still dimming. At some point "we need more time and resources" becomes indistinguishable from "we don't know what we're doing."

**Key figures:** 
- **Councilor Mira Ashton** - Current chair (rotates annually), 52, brilliant inventor. Sharp, results-driven, increasingly aware that her faction's credibility is tied to problem that may not be solvable on her timeline.
- **High Curator Myren Holt** - Not Council member but most important technical advisor. His prototype reactor is either breakthrough that saves Lumencrest or the thing that ends it. Nobody is certain which.

**Recent victories (last 2 years):**
- Secured massive expedition funding (five expeditions currently active)
- Built new research facility despite Conservator opposition
- Gained 2-3 seats in recent elections (citizens want solutions)

**Recent setbacks:**
- Multiple expeditions found nothing useful or lost personnel (expensive, little return)
- Laboratory accident (experimental shard reactor exploded, killed 12 researchers - PR disaster)
- Glittering Marshes negotiations stalled (communities refuse shard access)

---

### The Expansionists (Currently ~8-9 seats, growing fast)

**Core belief:** The Endless Eye has a working Hollostar. Diplomacy has failed. It's time to take it.

**Who they are:** The military families and the merchant princes — people whose power is expressed through force and control rather than research or tradition. They have been a minority faction for most of the Council's history. The crisis is changing that.

**Their support base:** 
- Growing and increasingly mainstream
- A year ago considered dangerous radicals, now invited to dinners
- Military families seeking purpose and glory
- Desperate citizens willing to consider aggressive solutions
- Those who believe Lumencrest's survival justifies any action

**What they want:** 
- Military buildup along eastern approach to Starfall Basin
- Covert intelligence operations inside Endless Eye
- Council declaration that Lumencrest's survival constitutes legitimate claim on Endless Eye's Hollostar
- When moment is right: action

**Their calculation:** Only serious if Heart fails catastrophically. Current strategy is building capability while hoping not to use it. But the more they build, the more tempting it becomes.

**Their problem:** 
- No operational plan that doesn't risk war with Starfall Basin
- Endless Eye has formidable defenses and the Somnarchs (whose capabilities nobody fully understands)
- Several faction members running covert programs without Council sanction (Ashton knows, hasn't surfaced yet)
- L'Empire Verdienne warned that invading Endless Eye would result in trade sanctions

**Key figures:** 
- **Marshal-General Thalia Corvain** (Steelwatch) - Not Council member but military officer whose quiet support lends faction credibility
- **Merchant-Lord Davan Creel** - Within Council, most effective Expansionist operator. Patient, well-funded, willing to play long game.

**Recent victories (last 2 years):**
- Gained 4-5 seats (fastest growing faction, largest election gain)
- "Defensive" military expansion approved by Council
- Successfully placed long-term intelligence agent in Endless Eye

**Recent setbacks:**
- Four Accord cities issued joint statement opposing military adventurism (forced public disavowal)
- Secondary agent in Endless Eye discovered and expelled (Endless Eye now suspicious)
- L'Empire diplomatic warning about trade sanctions (economic pressure Lumencrest can't ignore)

## The Current Paralysis

The three factions have produced a Council that cannot act decisively on anything:

- Conservators block Illuminists' most aggressive proposals
- Illuminists block Expansionists' military buildup requests  
- Expansionists vote with whoever advances their position on any given day
- Councilor Ashton manages this through exhaustion and political debt

**The trend:**
- **Conservators losing** (their solution isn't working, citizens blame them for rationing)
- **Illuminists holding** (promise innovation but haven't delivered breakthrough)
- **Expansionists surging** (as other solutions fail, desperate measures look reasonable)

Meanwhile the Heart dims. The rationing continues. The Hazelhaven tech program degrades. The Accord meetings get harder. And the city watches its government argue while the lights flicker.

The Council's credibility is not yet broken — Lumencrest still functions, the research continues, the walls are still standing. But there is a growing sense among ordinary residents that the people in that chamber do not have a solution and are afraid to admit it.

## The Timber Question

**What they want:** Increased timber shipments from Verdant Crescent for construction (alternative infrastructure less power-dependent), fuel (backup power sources), and export (economic resources to fund everything).

**How they're pursuing it:**
- Merchant negotiations with forest edge towns (offering premium prices)
- Diplomatic overtures to L'Empire Verdienne (requesting increased lumber trade)
- Subtle pressure on Green Covenant (through empire intermediaries)

**Why it won't work:**
- Green Covenant controls forest, strictly limits sustainable harvest
- L'Empire respects Covenant authority (non-interference agreement)
- Forest edge towns won't exceed Covenant limits (lose logging rights entirely)
- Lumencrest doesn't have leverage to force it

**Current status:** Frustrating stalemate. Some increased timber but nowhere near what they want. Illuminists advocate more aggressive approaches. Expansionists suggest conquest (which nobody takes seriously... yet).

## Campaign Moves

**The Classified Programs:** One of the Illuminists' four classified research programs has produced something they don't understand and are afraid to report. Someone needs to find out what it is before it becomes everyone's problem.

**The Drift:** A moderate Conservator is being slowly cultivated by the Expansionists. If they flip, the balance shifts. Someone is running this operation deliberately.

**The Audit:** The Hazelhaven delegation has formally requested an audit of how Council funds allocated to their tech assistance program were actually spent. The Illuminists are stalling. There's a reason.

**The Moratorium Vote:** The Conservators are three votes away from forcing a research moratorium through the Council. The Illuminists need those votes to hold. The price of keeping them is something the party might be asked to deliver — or prevent.

**The Glittering Marshes Obsession:** Illuminists desperately want access to Deepsilt's shard concentration. Marsh communities refuse. Someone might try alternative methods of obtaining shards - purchase, theft, force. None have worked yet.

**The Intelligence Network:** Expansionists have agents in Endless Eye. One just discovered something that makes invasion look more feasible than previously thought. Question is whether they'll share this with full Council or act independently.
