# The Somnarchs

**Type:** Prophetic scholarly order  
**Power Base:** No fixed territory — itinerant, with a hidden observatory in the Frostwell Tundra  
**Size:** Small (~200 full members, ~500 affiliated observers worldwide)  
**Leader:** The Arch-Somnarch (currently **Vaela Thessin**, elf, ~380 years old)  
**Primary Goal:** Understand and document Hollostar cycles — not to prevent them, but to survive them  
**Alignment:** Neutral observers, but increasingly drawn into factional politics as the fourth fall approaches

---

## CORE IDENTITY

The Somnarchs are the world's oldest continuous scholarly institution. They predate the Heartland Accord, the Six-Fold Faith's formal codification, and most currently existing kingdoms. Their founding claim is simple and disturbing: **the stars have fallen before, in patterns, and they will fall again.** For most of their history, this made them eccentric curiosities. Now, with the fourth Hollostar visible in the northern sky each night, the world is finally listening.

They do not call themselves prophets. They find the word inaccurate and slightly insulting. Prophecy implies divine revelation. What the Somnarchs practice is **pattern recognition across geological time** — the reading of cycles so long that no single civilization has ever survived one complete rotation. Their predictions are not visions. They are calculations.

This distinction matters to them enormously. It matters to almost no one else.

**What makes them different from the Starfall Seekers:**  
The Seekers study individual Hollowstars — their properties, their fragments, their immediate effects. The Somnarchs study the *system*. They want to know why the stars fall, what cycle governs them, what the pattern means over ten thousand years. They consider the Seekers capable but myopic. The Seekers consider the Somnarchs rigorous but detached from practical reality. Both assessments are partially correct.

## HISTORY

The order's own records claim founding approximately **9,000 years ago** — shortly after evidence suggests the first Hollostar fell and destroyed whatever civilization preceded the current age. The earliest Somnarchs were survivors attempting to understand what had happened to their world. Their founding question: *Was this random? Or will it happen again?*

Eight thousand years of observation later, their answer is **yes, it will happen again, and here is roughly when.**

Whether their oldest records are genuine or later fabrications is hotly debated among scholars outside the order. The Somnarchs don't engage with this debate. They consider the question irrelevant — the records produce accurate predictions regardless of their origin.

**Key historical moments:**

- **~3,000 years ago:** Somnarchs predicted the second Hollostar fall within a 200-year window. Records suggest they warned several kingdoms; none prepared adequately. The fall that created Lumencrest's Heart occurred near the center of their predicted range. This is the oldest verifiable Somnarch prediction.

- **~800 years ago:** Somnarchs predicted the third fall within a 50-year window. Warned eastern desert communities. Some moved. Most didn't. The fall created the Endless Eye. Communities that moved to the predicted general impact zone were destroyed. Communities that moved away thrived. This is considered their greatest practical success, though it is rarely discussed publicly (it required admitting the Somnarchs were right).

- **~30 years ago:** Somnarchs formally announced the fourth fall was entering its approach window. This announcement was ignored, mocked, or quietly filed away by every major government. The order began its current period of aggressive outreach — dispatching members to every major city, ancient site, and factional headquarters to make their case in person.

- **Present:** With the fourth star now visible to the naked eye, the Somnarchs have gone from ignored to uncomfortably relevant. Factions that dismissed them for decades are now seeking private audiences.

## STRUCTURE & MEMBERSHIP

The order is small by design. **They do not recruit widely.** Membership requires demonstrated aptitude for long-term pattern analysis, a willingness to spend years in remote observatories, and — most critically — the psychological capacity to care deeply about events that will outlast your grandchildren.

**Internal Ranks:**

### The Arch-Somnarch
Supreme authority on doctrine and interpretation. The position is held for life, selected by the Conclave from among senior Somnarchs. Currently **Vaela Thessin**, an elf who has served for nearly 100 years. She is one of the longest-serving Arch-Somnarchs in recent history, having held the position through the entire modern visible approach of the fourth star.

**Vaela Thessin:** Quiet, precise, and deeply tired in the way of someone who has been saying the same correct thing for over a century to people who didn't listen. She is not bitter — bitterness requires surprise. She is patient. She is also, for the first time in her long tenure, genuinely frightened, because this fall will happen *during her lifetime*, and knowing what is coming does not make watching it arrive easier.

She took the Arch-Somnarch position at a relatively young age (for an elf) — 280 years old — and has held it for nearly a century. She is nearing the end of a natural elven life, which means she will almost certainly live to see the impact. She finds this thought clarifying rather than comforting.

### The Conclave (12 Senior Somnarchs)
Senior members who have served 50+ years. Each oversees a domain of study: fall mechanics, ancient ruins, creature transformations, fragment behavior, historical pattern analysis, etc. The Conclave makes collective decisions on doctrine, outreach, and resource allocation.

**Notable Conclave Members:**

**Rhen Duskhollow** (human, 58) — Head of field operations. Manages the network of traveling observers. Believes the order has been too passive and should be actively interfering in factional politics to position people well for the aftermath. Quietly in tension with Vaela's non-intervention philosophy.

**Elder Moss-in-Stone** (Fungril, ~200 years old) — Liaison to the Glittering Marshes. One of the few non-humanoid Somnarchs. Studies the relationship between shard clusters and longer cosmic cycles. Almost never leaves the marshes. Communicates with the wider Conclave through written correspondence.

**Sira Vantholm** (dwarf, 134) — Master of the Frostvault excavation records. Believes the pre-Hollostar civilization understood the cycles and tried to leave warnings in the ancient ruins. Has devoted her career to deciphering the Sleeping Gate's markings. Getting closer, she thinks. Maybe.

### The Watchers (~150 members)
Full members with specialized assignments — observatory maintenance, field research, outreach missions, fragment documentation, or the continuous astronomical observation that is the order's lifeblood. Most spend the majority of their careers at remote sites.

### The Affiliated (~500 observers)
Scholars, astronomers, druids, and others who share data with the Somnarchs without formal membership. The order maintains an extensive network of correspondents who send regular sky reports, shard observations, and anomaly notes. In exchange, the Somnarchs share (carefully selected) findings. Most affiliates don't know how much the order actually knows.

## THE OBSERVATORY AT FROSTVAULT

The Somnarchs' primary installation is built around and partially within the Frostvault — the ancient buried complex in the central Frostwell Tundra. The ruins contain astronomical chambers the Somnarchs believe were built by the pre-Hollostar civilization specifically for tracking the star cycle.

**The original observatory chambers** (pre-Hollostar construction):
- Stone channels carved to capture starlight at precise angles
- A circular chamber whose ceiling maps the night sky as it appeared ~10,000 years ago
- Unknown mechanisms that still function despite being 10,000+ years old
- Markings the Somnarchs have partially deciphered — enough to confirm the builders knew about cycles, not enough to understand everything they knew

**Somnarch additions** (built over 3,000 years):
- Surface towers with modern optical instruments
- Living quarters (sparse, functional, cold)
- The Archive — their accumulated 9,000 years of records
- An emergency shelter designed to survive a Hollostar impact within 500 miles (theoretically)

**Current status:** Most staff evacuated when the evacuation order went out. A skeleton crew of eight Somnarchs has refused to leave. They intend to observe the impact from as close as survivable. Vaela has not ordered them to go. She understands the impulse completely.

## BELIEFS & DOCTRINE

### On Hollostar Falls
The Somnarchs believe the four known falls are part of a **cycle approximately 3,000 years in length** — though the intervals are not perfectly regular. They do not know what *causes* the cycle. They have theories:

- **The Cosmic Clock theory:** The falls are mechanical — some astronomical system beyond the world's sky operates on these intervals. No intent, no meaning, just physics.
- **The Hunger theory:** The Hollowstars are drawn to the world by something — energy, life, accumulated civilization. The world itself attracts them.
- **The Warning theory (Sira Vantholm's work):** The pre-Hollostar civilization believed the cycle was intentional — a pruning mechanism. The ruins were built to help survivors understand, not prevent.

Vaela's official position: *"We don't know why. We know when. Knowing when is enough to survive."*

### On the Current Crisis
The Somnarchs believe the simultaneous awakening of ancient sites is **not coincidental**. Their interpretation: the approaching star is generating a field effect that is activating dormant Hollostar energy worldwide. The failing of Lumencrest's Heart and the Endless Eye's shrinkage are symptoms of this — old Hollostar energy is being drawn toward the approaching star.

**Implication they have not announced publicly:** If their theory is correct, Lumencrest's Heart will fail completely when the fourth star lands. Not gradually — completely. The city's power source will die on impact day, possibly from halfway across the continent.

They are not sure whether to say this out loud. Vaela is still deciding.

### On What Happens After
The Somnarchs study the aftermath of previous falls with grim attention. Their records suggest:
- The first fall destroyed the previous civilization and created a 2,000-year recovery period
- The second fall transformed the Central Heartlands but left civilization intact
- The third fall created the Endless Eye and was largely beneficial for regional populations

**Pattern:** Each fall has been less devastating than the previous one. The world adapts. Survivors learn. The ruins in Frostwell exist because someone was smart enough to build them deep and strong.

The Somnarchs are cautiously optimistic — not about preventing the fall, but about the world surviving it. "Cautiously" is doing significant work in that sentence.

## RELATIONSHIPS WITH OTHER FACTIONS

### Starfall Seekers
**Relationship:** Collegial rivalry, intermittent cooperation  
The Seekers do excellent ground-level work the Somnarchs couldn't do with their limited numbers. The Somnarchs hold historical knowledge the Seekers can't access independently. They share data more often than either publicly admits. The philosophical divide (study the event vs. study the system) creates genuine friction, but neither organization is stupid enough to let that friction become hostility during a crisis.

### Lumencrest / Council of Lumens
**Relationship:** Complicated. Strained.  
The Conservators don't want to hear the Somnarchs' public theory about Heart failure at impact. The Illuminists want everything the Somnarchs know, immediately, regardless of consent. The Expansionists are interested primarily in whether Somnarch knowledge can be weaponized. Vaela meets with all three factions separately and tells each of them what they need to know. None of them get the full picture. This is intentional.

### Green Covenant
**Relationship:** Respectful alignment  
The Covenant's belief in ancient cycles resonates with Somnarch doctrine. The Somnarchs have quietly confirmed aspects of Covenant theory (that the forest awakening is connected to the approaching star). The Thorns faction makes the Somnarchs nervous — accurate predictions about ancient patterns don't help if people use them to justify violence.

### Path of Spirits
**Relationship:** Warm, theologically adjacent  
Spirit-practitioners' sense of time as cyclical rather than linear aligns naturally with Somnarch cosmology. Several Somnarchs personally follow the Path. Vaela has a long-standing correspondence with senior druids about whether the Hollowstars have spirit-nature.

### Six-Fold Faith / Hexarchs
**Relationship:** Tense neutrality  
The Hexarchs find the Somnarchs' long-cycle framing theologically inconvenient — it suggests the world operates on mechanical principles independent of divine will. The Somnarchs don't contradict the gods' existence, they just don't require it for their calculations. The radical Hexarch sect that believes the stars *are* the gods finds the Somnarchs fascinating and terrifying in equal measure.

### Isle of the Broken Sphere (Galapa)
**Relationship:** Unwanted advisors  
The Somnarchs have been sending prophetic warnings to the Galapa for a decade. The Galapa receive them politely and largely ignore them. The Somnarchs understand this response — they've gotten it from every civilization they've ever warned — but find it no less frustrating. They believe the Isle will need to evacuate before impact. They are trying not to push so hard that the Galapa stop listening entirely.

### Veilmarkets
**Relationship:** Guarded mutual use  
The Veilmarkets want Somnarch knowledge — advance warning of world-changing events is extremely profitable. The Somnarchs occasionally use Veilmarket channels for information that official routes can't obtain. Both parties maintain plausible deniability about the relationship. Neither fully trusts the other. Both find the arrangement useful enough to continue.

## CURRENT ACTIVITIES

**The Frostvault Vigil:** Eight members remain at the observatory to document the impact. They are also attempting to finish deciphering the Sleeping Gate markings before the star lands. Time is running out.

**The Heart Warning Decision:** Vaela and the Conclave are actively debating whether to formally tell Lumencrest that their power source will likely fail on impact day. Arguments for: they need to prepare. Arguments against: it will cause panic that kills more people than the actual power failure. No decision has been made.

**The Shard Network:** Somnarchs are quietly collecting documentation on shard behavior across all regions — particularly the Glittering Marshes and the Frostwell ruins. They believe the shard network will behave dramatically differently after impact and want baseline data.

**Outreach to Frostwell Holdouts:** Several Somnarchs are working with Nine Hearths communities that refused to evacuate, not to force evacuation, but to help them identify locations with the best survival probability if they insist on staying. One Somnarch has taken up residence in Coldfast specifically for this purpose.

**The Archive Copies:** The order is quietly copying their most critical records and distributing them to trusted locations worldwide. If Frostvault is destroyed in the impact, the knowledge survives. Three copies exist: one in the Glittering Marshes (with Elder Moss-in-Stone), one in Lumencrest's restricted archives, one somewhere in the Verdant Crescent that only Vaela knows.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Proof Request:** A Somnarch approaches the party with a research request — visit three of the awakening ancient sites (Ember Cradle, the Verdant forest stirrings, the Isle's tremors) and document specific observable phenomena. The order needs corroborating data for their impact-site network theory. Simple enough — until another faction is found to be following the party, wanting the same data for entirely different reasons.

**The Warning They Won't Give:** The party discovers the Somnarchs know the Heart will fail at impact. Someone needs to decide whether Lumencrest learns this, when, and how. The Somnarchs ask the party to help think through the ethics. Or the party finds out and has to choose whether to tell Lumencrest themselves, bypassing the order entirely.

**The Frostvault Vigil:** Party is hired (or chooses) to reach the Frostvault and either help the eight remaining Somnarchs complete their work, evacuate them by force if necessary, or retrieve their research if the worst happens. The impact timeline may have accelerated. Getting there and back is not guaranteed.

**The Stolen Prediction:** Someone has stolen a partial copy of Somnarch calculations — specifically the impact coordinates. Several factions would pay enormously to know exactly where the star lands before anyone else. The Somnarchs need the party to recover it before it reaches market, or at least find out who bought it.

**The Last Affiliate:** An old astronomer in a small Northern Expanse river town has been sending regular sky reports to the Somnarchs for forty years. Reports stopped three weeks ago. The Somnarchs are worried — not just about a person, but about what she might have observed in her last weeks of recording that made someone want her silenced.

**Vaela's Request:** The Arch-Somnarch requests a private meeting with the party. She has a task she cannot give to her own members — too close to the order, too known to watching factions. She needs people with no obvious connection to the Somnarchs. The task: verify whether a specific ancient ruin in the Desert of Glass is what her oldest records claim it is. She won't say what that is until they agree to go. She will pay well. She will also tell them, quietly, that if the ruin is what she thinks it is, the fourth fall may be the least of anyone's problems.

## GM NOTES

**What the Somnarchs actually know (and haven't said publicly):**
- The fourth fall will accelerate, not slow, the awakening of ancient sites
- Lumencrest's Heart will likely fail completely at impact, not gradually
- The pre-Hollostar civilization appears to have survived at least one previous cycle — the Sleeping Gate and Frostvault were built *after* a fall, by people who knew another was coming
- There may be a fifth fall. Their longest cycle calculations suggest the world has been through this at least twice before. What ended the previous recovery periods is not recorded. They find this deeply worrying.

**What even the Somnarchs don't know:**
- The ultimate origin or purpose of the Hollowstars
- Whether the simultaneous site awakening is cause or effect
- Why the 700-year gap between the third and fourth falls is longer than their model predicted
- What the Sleeping Gate was actually designed to do

**Tone guidance:** The Somnarchs should feel like people who know too much and can do too little. They are not villainous, not heroic, not neutral in the comfortable sense — they are *correct*, and being correct is exhausting and isolating when nobody believes you until it's almost too late. Interactions with Somnarchs should leave players feeling the weight of geological time pressing against immediate crisis.
