# The Stillward Enclaves

**Type:** Loose affiliation of isolationist communities  
**Power Base:** Scattered — deep forests, mountain valleys, remote islands, desert edges  
**Size:** Unknown total. Individual enclaves range from 50 to a few thousand. No central census.  
**Leader:** No central authority. Each enclave governs itself. Some have formal councils; some follow a single elder; some operate by consensus.  
**Primary Goal:** Survive what is coming by staying out of it  
**Alignment:** Isolationist. Not hostile to outsiders by default — but not welcoming, either. The degree varies enormously by enclave.

---

## CORE IDENTITY

The Stillward Enclaves are not an organization. They are a phenomenon — communities that independently arrived at the same conclusion: the world beyond their borders is becoming increasingly dangerous, and the safest response is to stop being part of it.

Some Enclaves have existed for generations, founded by people fleeing old wars or political upheavals. Others are newer — communities that watched the Hollostar crisis develop over the past thirty years and began quietly withdrawing. What connects them is not ideology or shared governance but a shared orientation: **stillness over movement, depth over breadth, survival over ambition**.

The word "Stillward" comes from their own terminology — to move stillward is to move toward quiet, toward self-sufficiency, away from the entanglements of the wider world. It is not a retreat, in their framing. It is a choice.

---

## THE SPECTRUM

No two Enclaves are identical. The range of isolationist practice is wide:

**True Isolationists**  
Complete withdrawal. No trade with outsiders. No visitors allowed. Some have existed in genuine isolation for decades. Their self-sufficiency is real — they grow their own food, make their own tools, teach their own children. Outsiders are turned away at the border, firmly but without violence (usually).

These are the Enclaves that worry the outside world most — not because they are threatening, but because nobody knows what they have accumulated, what they know, or what they would do if pushed.

**Selective Traders**  
Trade specific goods with outside communities but maintain strict limits on what enters and who. A Selective Trader enclave might sell preserved food or quality craft goods to nearby towns while admitting no travelers, accepting no outsiders as residents, and sharing no information about internal matters.

**Storm Preparers**  
Communities that have been actively stockpiling resources, reinforcing structures, and documenting their preparations in anticipation of a major disruption. Some Storm Preparers are willing to shelter refugees from outside their community — on their terms, under their conditions, after assessment. Others are not.

Storm Preparers tend to have the most sophisticated understanding of the Hollostar crisis. Several have independently calculated impact timelines that come close to Somnarch estimates — through observation, not access to Somnarch data.

**Theological Enclaves**  
Communities organized around a specific interpretation of the Six-Fold Faith, Path of Spirits, or (rarely) First Flame that diverged from mainstream practice and withdrew to develop their theology without interference. Some are peaceful communities of genuine spiritual depth. Some have developed beliefs that outside clergy consider dangerously heterodox. The Hexarchs have varying degrees of concern about specific Enclaves but generally lack the ability to do much about them.

---

## WHAT THEY HAVE

The question the outside world keeps asking is: **what are the Enclaves sitting on?**

The answer, in many cases, is significant.

Communities that have spent decades in self-sufficient isolation develop deep expertise in the resources of their specific region. Forest Enclaves know their forests in ways no outside forester does. Mountain Enclaves have charted deposits that official surveys missed. Desert-edge Enclaves have water sources that regional governments don't know about.

Some Enclaves also have **historical records** that predate the Heartland Accord — documents, oral histories, or physical artifacts from periods of the world's history that are poorly documented elsewhere. The Witness Hexarch has been trying to access specific Enclave archives for years. Several Enclaves know this and have declined.

One Enclave in the Verdant Crescent edge is believed to have records predating the third Hollostar fall. What those records say is unknown. The Somnarchs are very interested. The Enclave has not responded to three separate outreach attempts.

## RELATIONSHIPS WITH THE WIDER WORLD

**Lumencrest / Central Heartlands** — Mostly indifferent. Enclaves in the Heartlands region are small enough that the Council of Lumens has never seen them as worth engaging. As the crisis deepens and resource competition increases, this may change. Storm Preparer enclaves are quietly being assessed by Expansionist-aligned Heartlands operatives.

**Green Covenant** — Complex. Forest Enclaves in Verdant Crescent territory exist in an uncomfortable relationship with the Covenant. The Covenant respects the Enclaves' right to their land, but some Enclave practices conflict with Covenant forest management principles. The Thorns faction has been harder on Enclaves that they believe are damaging the forest. Most forest Enclaves have found ways to coexist.

**Somnarchs** — Frustrated respect. The Somnarchs know some Enclaves have information they need. Most Enclaves will not share it. The Somnarchs have learned that pushing harder makes access less likely, not more. Several Somnarch affiliated observers have spent years building relationships with specific Enclaves, slowly, with no guarantee of success.

**Path of Spirits** — The warmest relationship of any outside faction. Spirit-practice's emphasis on deep connection to specific places aligns naturally with Enclave orientation. Path practitioners are more likely to gain access to Enclave communities than representatives of any other institution.

**Refugees from Frostwell** — The arriving Frostwell evacuation is the Enclaves' most immediate current challenge. Some Storm Preparers are being approached to shelter refugees. A few have agreed to take small numbers. Most have not. The question of whether and how to respond to a genuine humanitarian crisis is the most significant internal debate many Enclaves have had in years.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Archive Request:** The party is sent (by the Somnarchs, the Witness Hexarch, or a scholar of their acquaintance) to access records held by a specific Enclave. The Enclave has refused institutional requests. They may respond differently to individuals without obvious factional affiliation — or they may not. What the records contain, if accessed, complicates something the party already thinks they know.

**The Refugee Question:** A Stillward Enclave on a major route south from Frostwell is being approached by a wave of evacuees. The Enclave is divided — some members want to help, others believe opening the border will destroy everything they've built. The party arrives in the middle of this debate. How they handle it will determine what happens to several hundred refugees, and to the Enclave itself.

**The Enclave That Knows:** Rumors suggest a specific Enclave has been tracking the approaching star independently for decades — and has concluded something different from both the Somnarchs and the mainstream scholarly community. The conclusion has to do with where the star lands. Getting into the Enclave to find out what they know is not straightforward.

**The Stockpile:** An Expansionist-aligned operative has identified a Storm Preparer enclave with resources the Heartlands badly needs. They want the party to broker access — or, if the Enclave won't deal, to assess what it would take to acquire the resources through other means. The Enclave knows someone is watching. They haven't decided how to respond.

**The Splinter:** A small group has left a major Enclave under bad circumstances — a theological dispute, or a conflict over the refugee question, or a leader who went too far. They are looking for help establishing themselves somewhere new. They have knowledge from inside the Enclave that the outside world would find very valuable. They know this and are trying to decide what to do with it.

## GM NOTES

**Tone:** Enclave communities should feel genuinely reasonable from the inside. They are not cults. They are not survivalist extremists (mostly). They are people who looked at the world and made a defensible choice. The frustration of dealing with them should come not from their hostility but from their coherence — they have thought through their position, and arguments from outside rarely address the actual reasons they withdrew.

**The information they hold** is a long-term resource the GM can use strategically. Not every Enclave has something critical — but the party shouldn't know which ones do until they've invested in the relationship.

**The refugee crisis** is the Enclaves' defining challenge in the campaign's current moment. How different Enclaves respond will reveal their character. Some will open their doors at significant cost to themselves. Some will close them at significant cost to the refugees. Most will do something complicated in between.
