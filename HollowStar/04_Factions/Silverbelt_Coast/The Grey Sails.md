# The Grey Sails

**Type:** Organized pirate fleet  
**Base:** Mobile — Silver Belt Sea, with informal anchorages in unpatrolled coves  
**Size:** ~400 active crew across 8-10 vessels; 2-3 larger warships, remainder fast attack craft  
**Leader:** **Captain Ilysse Dawnrak** (see below)  
**Primary Goal:** Profit through raiding, extortion, and selective privateering — with an eye toward becoming something more permanent  
**Alignment:** Pragmatic criminal. Not ideologically driven — opportunistic, adaptive, increasingly strategic.

---

## CORE IDENTITY

The Grey Sails are not random pirates. They are a disciplined, coordinated fleet that has survived and grown by being smarter than the political structure they operate against. The Four Houses' inability to agree on unified naval command creates predictable patrol gaps — between House territories, during succession crises, when two Houses are feuding. The Grey Sails don't just exploit these gaps. They map them, maintain them, and quietly work to keep the Houses from resolving their coordination problems.

A pirate fleet that benefits from political dysfunction has an interest in maintaining that dysfunction. The Grey Sails understand this. Their intelligence operations are nearly as important as their naval operations.

**What makes them dangerous:** Not just their seamanship, though that is genuine. It's that they think like a government without the constraints of one. They plan in seasons, not raids.

---

## HISTORY

The Grey Sails formed roughly thirty years ago from the remnants of three smaller pirate operations that were being individually squeezed out by improving House naval coordination. Their founder — a former House Caldren navigator named **Elias Greywater**, now dead — recognized that consolidation was the only path to survival. He brought the three crews together through a combination of persuasion, bribery, and one targeted assassination of a captain who refused to cooperate.

Under Greywater the fleet built its current operational model: strict discipline, shared intelligence, a code that governs how prizes are divided, and a deliberate policy of not killing crew members of raided vessels unless directly necessary. The no-unnecessary-killing rule is not mercy — it's economics. Dead sailors mean grieving families, which means political pressure on the Houses to actually coordinate. Alive sailors, ransomed or released, means manageable inconvenience.

Greywater died eight years ago in a storm. His first mate, **Ilysse Dawnrak**, took command and has held it since. She has continued his strategic approach and added her own: the fleet is now actively positioning for a transition from piracy to something resembling legitimacy — a private naval force that the Houses might eventually prefer to pay rather than fight.

## CAPTAIN ILYSSE DAWNRAK

**Ancestry:** Human-Katari mixed, age 39  
**Appearance:** Dark complexion, salt-bleached hair cropped close, missing the tip of her left ring finger (told three different stories about why, all plausible). Dresses practically. The one visible luxury is a compass that predates the Four Houses — she says it belonged to someone who knew where they were going.  
**Personality:** Patient, calculating, and genuinely charismatic in the particular way of someone who has led dangerous people through dangerous situations and come out ahead. She does not raise her voice. People pay more attention when she speaks quietly.

**Goals:** Make the Grey Sails into something that outlasts her. Specifically: establish the fleet as a recognized maritime security force — legitimized through one of the Houses or through direct negotiation — before the current political instability closes the window. She believes the Caldren succession crisis is the best opportunity she'll get.

**Her read on the situation:** House Caldren is leaderless. Its southern patrol lanes are effectively empty. Steward Orvyn Glast is competent but has no naval authority. The Grey Sails have been operating in Caldren territory for two years with minimal resistance. When a legitimate Caldren heir is installed, they will need to rebuild naval capacity quickly. Dawnrak wants to be the solution they hire rather than the problem they inherit.

**What she doesn't know:** The Tide King Voss has extended his protection network into Silver Belt merchant traffic. Several ships the Grey Sails have raided in the past eight months were paying Voss's cut. Voss knows. He is building a response. The Grey Sails are operating in waters they think are ungoverned that are, in fact, becoming very governed.

**Secret:** Dawnrak has a back-channel with a single Four Houses figure — she will not say which one — who has been providing her with advance patrol schedule information for six years. This is what allowed the Grey Sails to survive the period when House coordination briefly improved. The source is still active. If revealed, it would constitute the worst kind of betrayal within House politics.

## FLEET STRUCTURE

**The Greywarden** — Dawnrak's flagship. Modified merchant vessel, rebuilt for speed and cargo capacity. The fleet's command center and archive; Dawnrak keeps meticulous records of patrol patterns, prize manifests, and intelligence assessments.

**The Saltwind** — The fleet's primary warship. Heavier armament, slower than the flagship. Captained by **Orren Blackwater** (dwarf, age 82), the fleet's oldest sailor and its tactical expert. Former House Mourne naval officer — his defection was a scandal in Vetheris fifteen years ago and is still occasionally referenced in Houses political arguments.

**Fast attack craft (6-8 vessels)** — Smaller, faster, used for scouting, interception, and coordinated harassment. Each has a designated captain who reports to Dawnrak directly.

**Intelligence cell** — Not a vessel but a function. Three or four individuals who maintain identities in Tidewall, Vetheris, and one Caldren port town. Their job is to provide advance warning of House naval movements and intelligence on valuable cargo movements. The cell in Tidewall has been identified as a Grey Sails contact by the Harbor-Master; this has been reported up the House Vethara intelligence chain and not yet acted upon.

## HOW THEY OPERATE

**Target selection:** The Grey Sails do not raid randomly. They prioritize: vessels carrying Lumencrest-bound luxury cargo (high value, politically unsympathetic recipients), Vethara-flagged merchants (House Vethara is their primary naval antagonist), and vessels caught in patrol gaps that leave them genuinely unprotected.

**They avoid:** vessels carrying food for working populations, First Flame relief shipments, fishing boats (as a rule — individual captains may violate this), and anything with obvious Grey Covenant or Path of Spirits markings. These avoidances are not entirely principled — they prevent the kind of broad popular resentment that turns a manageable problem into a crusade.

**The ransom economy:** Captured cargo is sold through Veilmarket-adjacent channels. Captured crew members are offered ransom terms (House-affiliated officers bring higher ransom than common sailors). Vessels are occasionally kept; more often stripped and released. The economics are carefully managed — enough profit to maintain the fleet, not enough to give the Houses a reason to sacrifice the coordination problems and come after the Grey Sails seriously.

**The Caldren gap:** Since the Caldren succession crisis began, the Grey Sails have operated freely in the southern Silver Belt waters. This has been extremely profitable. It has also made them visible in a way they weren't before. Visibility is a risk Dawnrak is managing.

---

## THE VOSS PROBLEM

The Tide King Voss has been quietly expanding his protection network north into Silver Belt merchant traffic. Several merchant captains who found the Grey Sails' raids unprofitable started paying Voss's cut instead, and Voss extended his network to cover them.

The Grey Sails don't know the extent of this. They have noticed that several vessels they've raided recently had private security that seemed to know the fleet's approach patterns — suggesting intelligence Voss's network could provide. Dawnrak has flagged this as something requiring investigation. She has not yet connected it to Voss specifically.

**The collision course:** If the Grey Sails continue operating in Silver Belt waters without recognizing Voss's territorial interests, they will eventually raid enough of his protected vessels to force a response. Voss's options are escalation (direct conflict with a fleet he's not currently positioned to beat), negotiation (recognizing the Grey Sails as a power requiring their own arrangement), or indirection (working through the Houses to remove the problem using their naval capacity). He hasn't decided which yet.

## RELATIONSHIP WITH THE FOUR HOUSES

**House Vethara** — Primary antagonist. Admiral-Lord Cassius Vethara considers the Grey Sails a personal insult. He has conducted three major suppression operations in the past decade; all three succeeded in temporarily disrupting the fleet but failed to destroy it. He believes the fleet has a House-level intelligence source. He is correct.

**House Mourne** — Complicated. Lady-Admiral Sera Mourne has been less aggressive in her anti-piracy operations than her position might suggest. She is aware that Orren Blackwater (the Grey Sails' second captain) was a Mourne naval officer before his defection. She has not moved against him specifically, which some of her naval officers have noticed.

**House Aldric** — Adversarial but principled. Lord-Admiral Brennan Aldric despises the Grey Sails on traditional grounds (piracy dishonors the sea, full stop) and would commit full naval resources to their suppression if he could get House coordination. His failure to get that coordination frustrates him enormously.

**House Caldren (Stewardship)** — The Grey Sails' current primary operating area. Steward Glast knows the Grey Sails are exploiting the succession crisis. He doesn't have the naval authority to do anything about it. He is documenting everything for the eventual heir — whether that becomes an asset or a liability depends on what kind of heir Caldren gets.

**Captain Rynn Saltblood** (House Vethara naval officer) knows the Grey Sails' leader personally — old naval service connection from before Dawnrak's career turned criminal. She has not reported this connection up the chain. She hasn't decided whether she would if pressed.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Arrangement Offer:** Dawnrak approaches the party as intermediaries — she needs a message delivered to a specific House figure proposing terms. She won't approach directly (too much history). The party can deliver the message, investigate its implications, or sell the information to one of the Houses. All three choices have consequences.

**The Voss Investigation:** Someone has hired the party to find out why Grey Sails raids in Silver Belt waters seem to have advance intelligence. Following this thread leads to the fleet's House informant — and to the question of what to do with that information and who benefits from having it.

**Orren's Secret:** The dwarf captain of the Saltwind has a reason for leaving House Mourne that wasn't the official story. Someone wants to know the real story — possibly Sera Mourne herself, who has always suspected there was more to it. Finding Orren and getting the truth out of him requires getting aboard a Grey Sails vessel.

**The Legitimacy Play:** Dawnrak's move to transition the fleet toward legitimacy is accelerating as the Caldren succession resolves. She needs someone to make a specific arrangement with the incoming heir — and she needs that someone to have no visible connection to the Grey Sails. The party is offered a significant fee to be the intermediaries in a negotiation that will either legitimize a pirate fleet or blow up spectacularly.

**The Collision:** A Grey Sails raid on a Voss-protected vessel puts the party in the middle of a three-way confrontation — the Grey Sails demanding their prize, the Tide King's people demanding it back, and the vessel's actual owners trying to survive the negotiation. Nobody's hands are entirely clean.

## GM NOTES

**Tone:** The Grey Sails should not feel like villains. They are an organized criminal enterprise that operates with discipline and a kind of inverted integrity — their rules are real, their word (within those rules) is good, and their strategic thinking is genuinely impressive. Working with them is possible. Working against them is possible. Underestimating them is dangerous.

**Dawnrak** is the most important character in any Grey Sails story. She is rational, she is patient, and she is playing a longer game than most people she deals with. She will tell the party the truth about her goals if asked directly — she finds deception wasteful. What she won't volunteer is the information that disadvantages her.

**The legitimacy arc** is the faction's long-term trajectory. Whether it succeeds, fails, or gets redirected depends on player engagement with the Caldren succession, the Houses, and the Voss situation. It is one of the more interesting political outcomes available in the Silverbelt region.
