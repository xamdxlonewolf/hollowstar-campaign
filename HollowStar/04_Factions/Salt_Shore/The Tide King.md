# The Tide King

> *"He's not a good man. He's not a bad man. He's the right man, and that's rarer."*
> — Common Salt Shore saying

---

## VOSS — THE TIDE KING

**Full name:** Voss (no family name — discarded it, won't say why)  
**Ancestry:** Firbolg  
**Age:** 51  
**Base:** Brinehaven, though he moves constantly  
**Title:** Self-given — "Tide King." Said without irony. Accepted without argument.

### Appearance

Tall even by Firbolg standards (7'2"), with the weathered, unhurried quality of someone who has been at sea for thirty years. Grey-streaked fur, close-cropped. One eye replaced with a deep amber glass eye — he had it made in [[Saltmere]] years ago, before things soured. Dresses practically (no crown, no finery — just good quality gear maintained well). The only ornamentation: a necklace of tide tokens, one from each town under his protection. He adds one when a town joins. He's never removed one.

### Personality

**Calculating and fair.** Not warm — he doesn't need you to like him. He needs you to understand the arrangement and trust that he'll honor his side of it. He always does.

- Listens more than he speaks
- When he speaks, people stop talking
- No visible anger (the quiet ones are always worse)
- Genuinely fair in disputes — people bring problems to him because his rulings stick
- Remembers everything (never takes notes; never needs to)
- **Cares about the Salt Shore** — not sentimentally, but like a captain cares about his ship

**What he isn't:** Cruel, capricious, greedy beyond his cut, or interested in power for its own sake. He rules because the alternative is chaos, and chaos is bad for everyone including him.

**What he is:** A man who built something from nothing and intends to keep it.

## HOW VOSS BECAME THE TIDE KING

### The Before (30 Years Ago)

Voss grew up in a Firbolg community in the northern forests - traditional, isolated, peaceful. He left at 21 for reasons he won't discuss. Discarded his family name. Never went back.

Took work on fishing boats. Good with his hands, strong, learned fast. Within three years he was running his own boat. Within five, he owned three boats and employed crews.

**The turning point:** Year 12 of his fishing career, a pirate crew raided his fleet. Killed two of his people, took a season's catch. The local authorities did nothing - no unified Salt Shore law, no protection, just isolated towns doing what they could.

Voss decided that was unacceptable.

### Building the System (20 Years Ago)

Started simple: Organized fishing fleet captains into mutual protection agreement. If pirates hit one fleet, all fleets responded together. It worked. Pirates moved to easier targets.

Other fleets wanted in. Voss said yes - for 5% of catch value to fund patrol boats. They agreed. The money went where he said it would - patrol boats, not his pocket. People noticed.

**The expansion:** Merchants asked for the same arrangement. Then salvagers. Then smugglers (who paid more for discretion). Voss said yes to everyone who'd pay and follow rules. The percentage system was born.

Within five years, he was coordinating protection across half the Salt Shore coast. Within ten years, most of it. People started calling him "Tide King" - meant as joke initially, but it stuck because it was accurate.

### The Consolidation (10 Years Ago to Present)

Voss realized fragmented towns were vulnerable - to pirates, to outside powers trying to buy influence, to their own inability to coordinate. He began pushing for political consolidation.

**The pitch:** Towns keep local governance but acknowledge his authority over maritime trade and dispute resolution. In exchange: protection, arbitration, and access to his network.

Some towns agreed immediately (especially those already benefiting from his protection). Others resisted. Some still resist.

**His method:** Economic pressure (better trade terms for cooperative towns), strategic support (funding town improvements in exchange for cooperation), occasionally force (pirates who refuse his terms find enforcement visits).

He's patient. He's been building this for 20 years. He'll take another 20 if needed.

### Why "Tide King"

**His explanation:** "The tide doesn't ask permission. It comes in, it goes out, you adapt or you drown. I'm the same."

**The real reason:** He saw the Salt Shore drowning in chaos - no unified law, no coordination, vulnerable to anyone strong enough to take advantage. He decided to be the tide that lifts all boats instead of the storm that sinks them.

## HOW THE TIDE KING SYSTEM WORKS

### The Arrangement

**Anyone operating in Voss's waters pays a percentage. His waters are expanding.**

- Fishing fleets: 5% of catch value (modest, sustainable)
- Merchant ships passing through: 2% of declared cargo
- Smugglers: 10% (higher risk, higher cut — he turns a blind eye in return)
- Salvage operations: 15% of recovered value
- Pirates operating in his waters: 20% OR they leave — no third option

**"His waters" currently means:**
- All Salt Shore coastal and island waters (absolute, established)
- Silver Belt Sea (quietly expanding — ~30-40% of merchant traffic already pays him, routed through Salt Shore intermediaries)
- The Silverbelt Houses don't know yet. The merchants paying him prefer it that way — Voss's protection is more reliable than House naval patrols and cheaper than Grey Sails losses.
- His goal: Full Silver Belt coverage within 5 years. Not through force — through making himself indispensable before anyone notices.

### What You Get in Return

- Disputes settled (Voss arbitrates — rulings are final and enforced)
- Protection from outside interference (mostly)
- Access to his network (information, contacts, safe harbors across Salt Shore)
- If you're desperate — genuinely desperate — Voss has been known to waive the cut entirely and help anyway

### The Consolidation: Town by Town

Voss's authority isn't uniform across the Salt Shore. Here's the current state:

**FULLY INTEGRATED (pay, cooperate, acknowledge authority):**
- **Brinehaven** (~11,000) - His seat, complete control
- **Tidewrack** (~2,400) - Elder Tobas is vocal Voss supporter, provides intelligence
- **Tidepost** (~1,400) - Salt operations benefit from his trade network, loyal
- **Gullhaven** (~900) - Small, pragmatic, cooperates fully

**COOPERATIVE BUT INDEPENDENT (pay percentage, resist political authority):**
- **Reaper's Bay** (~1,600) - Pays for protection, doesn't acknowledge broader authority
- **Saltholm** (~1,200) - Madam Cress maintains strict neutrality, pays but doesn't take sides

**CONTESTED (economic pressure, divided loyalties):**
- **Driftmere** (~1,800) - Took Saltmere's deal, caught between obligations to Saltmere and pressure from Voss. Town regrets it, can't escape
- **Shallowmere** (~700) - Contested waters between Salt Shore and Silverbelt, unclear authority

**RESISTANT (refuse authority, may or may not pay):**
- **Keeper's Bay** (~35,000) - Shipbuilding capital, operates independently, too large to force. Acknowledges Voss as regional leader while completely ignoring him in practice. Biggest problem.

**ISLANDS (special arrangements):**
- **Lantern's Rest** (~500-1,500) - Nine-Lanterns pirates, pay 20% to operate, independent otherwise
- **Wrecker's Rest** (~450) - Salvagers, pay percentage, maintain independence

**The math:** Voss has full authority over ~20,000 people, partial authority over ~15,000, contested authority over ~2,500, and Keeper's Bay (~35,000) basically ignores him. About 55% real control, 75% if you count those who pay but resist political authority.

**His goal:** 90%+ within 5 years. He's patient but determined.

### The Rules (Absolute)

1. **Pay your cut honestly.** He finds out if you don't (he always finds out). First offense: doubled next payment. Second offense: you leave Salt Shore permanently.
2. **No violence against fishing communities.** Fishermen and their families are untouchable. Anyone who harms them answers to Voss personally.
3. **No slaving.** The one thing that gets you killed rather than exiled.
4. **Disputes go to Voss.** You don't settle them yourself with violence. You bring them to him.

### Enforcement

**The Tide Crew:** Voss's personal fleet (~12 ships, ~400 sailors)
- Not the largest force on the water — but the most coordinated
- Captains personally loyal (not just paid — loyal)
- Spread across Salt Shore waters at all times

**The Shore Eyes:** Network of informants in every town
- Mostly ordinary people who report unusual activity
- Paid in favors, not coin (harder to bribe away)
- Voss knows what's happening in his territory faster than anyone

## THE NINE-LANTERNS ARRANGEMENT

**What they are:** Professional pirate fleet, ~9 ships, ~400 pirates, based in Lantern's Rest island. They target cargo, not people - thieves, not murderers.

**The Deal:**
- Nine-Lanterns pay Voss 20% of their take to operate in his waters
- They don't prey on Salt Shore fishing towns (absolute rule)
- They can use Lantern's Rest as haven
- Voss's Tide Crew doesn't hunt them
- In exchange: They operate freely, refit openly, sell stolen goods through his network

**Why Voss tolerates pirates:** Because the alternative is spending massive resources hunting them while they scatter to other regions and return when convenient. Better to have them paying, following rules, and operating where he can watch them.

**The pirate perspective:** 20% is steep but worth it. Safe harbor, no harassment, access to fences and buyers through Voss's contacts. They profit more under his arrangement than running from every coastal authority.

**The tension:** Some Salt Shore towns hate that Voss tolerates pirates at all. He points out that the Nine-Lanterns don't prey on fishing communities (his absolute rule) and mostly target merchant shipping from outside the Salt Shore. The towns still hate it but accept it because Voss's protection works.

**The fragility:** This arrangement holds only while both sides benefit more from cooperation than conflict. If the Nine-Lanterns break rules (especially preying on fishing towns), Voss will hunt them. If Voss demands too much, they'll leave his waters. Both know this. Both stay careful.

**Current status:** Stable for 8 years. Nine-Lanterns leader **Captain Sable Wrack** (human, 43, female) respects Voss's authority and enforces the fishing town rule ruthlessly within her fleet. As long as she leads, arrangement continues.

## THE KEEPER'S BAY PROBLEM

**The situation:** Keeper's Bay (~35,000) is the Salt Shore's largest city, shipbuilding capital, and economically independent. They operate completely outside Voss's authority while technically being part of his claimed territory.

**Why it's a problem:**
- Keeper's Bay controls ~58% of Salt Shore's total population
- The Harbor Council (seven merchant houses) governs independently
- They have resources to resist any pressure Voss could apply
- Their shipbuilding expertise makes them economically untouchable
- Other towns watch: if Keeper's Bay ignores Voss successfully, why shouldn't they?

**What Keeper's Bay does:**
- Acknowledges Voss as "regional leader" in polite public statements
- Completely ignores his authority in practice
- Doesn't pay percentage (claims their shipbuilding serves regional interests so they're exempt)
- Settles own disputes without bringing them to Voss
- Operates their own harbor security (not his Tide Crew)

**Voss's dilemma:**
- He can't force Keeper's Bay into submission (they're too large, too wealthy, too economically critical)
- Economic pressure won't work (they're self-sufficient and have continental trading partners)
- Military action would be disaster (his Tide Crew would lose, and even if they won, destroying the shipbuilding capital would hurt everyone including him)
- But tolerating their independence undermines his authority everywhere else

**What he's tried:**
- Diplomatic outreach to Harbor Council (politely ignored)
- Offering better trade terms (they don't need them)
- Funding political candidates in Harbor Council elections (his candidates lost)
- Suggesting joint operations for mutual benefit (Keeper's Bay says "we'll consider it" and doesn't)

**Current approach:** Publicly maintain fiction that Keeper's Bay acknowledges his authority (they play along with polite statements). Privately, accept that for now, consolidating the rest of the Salt Shore is more important than fighting battle he can't win.

**The hope:** If Voss successfully consolidates the other 40,000+ Salt Shore residents under his authority, Keeper's Bay's independence becomes less threatening. They'll be outlier rather than example. Maybe then they'll negotiate seriously.

**The fear:** That Keeper's Bay's successful independence inspires other towns to try the same. If multiple towns decide they don't need him, the whole system collapses.

**Harbor Council's perspective:** They don't oppose Voss - they just don't need him. Their shipbuilding expertise and continental trade contacts provide everything his network would offer. Why pay percentage and acknowledge authority when independence works fine?

**The wildcard:** If Voss successfully consolidates the rest of Salt Shore and gains real regional power, Keeper's Bay might decide joining is better than remaining isolated. But that's years away at best.

## THE SALTMERE PROBLEM

**What's happening:**

Grand Auctioneer Sable Marchant of [[Saltmere]] wants Salt Shore trade flowing through The Block. The Salt Shore currently trades independently — goods move without Covenant cut, without Saltmere's infrastructure.

Sable's approach is patient and financial:
- Funding town council candidates friendly to Saltmere trade agreements
- Offering Salt Shore merchants preferential rates at The Block (undercuts local trade)
- Quietly spreading stories about Tide King enforcement being "unpredictable" (false, but repeated enough)
- One council in Driftmere already flipped (now pushing for formal Saltmere trade treaty)

**What Voss knows:**

Everything. His Shore Eyes are good.

**What Voss hasn't decided:**

Whether to respond politically (counter-fund councils, make competing offers) or send a message (one of Saltmere's Salt Shore agents has had a very bad week).

**The complication:**

Voss isn't wrong that Saltmere offers some things he can't — legitimacy, The Block's auction access, legal protection in other regions. Some Salt Shore merchants genuinely want it. He can't simply call them traitors.

**What he needs:**

People he doesn't know — outsiders without established loyalties — who can investigate what Saltmere is actually doing, how deep it goes, and whether there's a way to cut the head off without making martyrs.

## VOSS'S OPERATION

### Brinehaven (Base)

The Tide King's flagship The Greywater is moored in Brinehaven's inner harbor when he's in port. He keeps a house in the Harbormaster's Quarter — not a palace, not a fortress. A large, practical house with a good view of the harbor and room for meetings.

### The Tide Fleet

**Flagship:** *The Greywater* — mid-size warship, fast, well-armed  
**Notable captains:**
- **Captain Ren Saltblood** (human, age 34) — best navigator on the coast, Voss's most trusted
- **Captain Dusk** (Katari, age 28) — fastest ship, used for urgent matters
- **Captain Morrow** (Halfling, age 55) — oldest, handles the fishing fleet relationships

### The Inner Circle

Beyond his captains, three people know Voss's full operation:

**Harbormaster Liss** (Ribbet, age 44) — manages Brinehaven port, Shore Eyes coordinator, the one who actually tracks money

**The Ledger** (unknown ancestry — goes by The Ledger, won't say more) — handles the actual accounting of cuts, disputes about payments, enforcement of financial arrangements

**Voss's daughter, Sela** (Firbolg, age 23) — doesn't officially exist in his operation but everyone knows she does his information work in places he can't go. Smarter than her father, more dangerous than she looks.

## RECENT VICTORIES AND SETBACKS

### Victories (Last 2 Years)

**Tidewrack Integration (18 months ago):**
Elder Tobas publicly backed Voss's consolidation and brought his town (~2,400) fully into the system. This was significant - first town to voluntarily acknowledge political authority, not just pay percentage. Other towns noticed.

**The Kraken's End Incident (14 months ago):**
Pirate crew called the Kraken's End broke Voss's absolute rule - raided fishing village, killed three people. Voss's Tide Crew hunted them for two months. Found them. No survivors. Message sent: the fishing town rule is non-negotiable. Even the Nine-Lanterns approved.

**Silverbelt Shipping Expansion (ongoing):**
Approximately 35-40% of merchant traffic in Silverbelt waters now pays Voss percentage through intermediaries. The Silverbelt Houses don't know yet. Voss is building economic presence before announcing political authority. If this works, his control expands significantly without firing shot.

**Gullhaven Integration (6 months ago):**
Small harbor town (~900) voluntarily joined after Voss mediated dispute with neighboring town. Quick, fair resolution impressed them. They now pay and acknowledge authority. Small victory but symbolic - towns are choosing him.

### Setbacks (Last 2 Years)

**The Driftmere Disaster (11 months ago):**
Driftmere (~1,800) took Saltmere's deal - exclusive fish sales contract with House Dorian at premium prices. Town is now caught between contractual obligations to Saltmere and pressure from Voss. They want out but can't break contract without massive penalty. Voss failed to counter-offer in time. Now watches Saltmere influence spread.

**Keeper's Bay Harbor Council Election (8 months ago):**
Voss funded three candidates for Harbor Council seats. All three lost. Keeper's Bay sent clear message: they're not interested in his authority. He spent significant political capital for nothing.

**The Wrecker's Rest Incident (5 months ago):**
Salvage dispute between Wrecker's Rest and mainland town. Both sides brought dispute to Voss. He ruled for Wrecker's Rest (they were right by his rules). Mainland town accepted ruling but resentment lingers. Some question whether Voss favors islands over mainland. He doesn't, but perception matters.

**Shore Eyes Exposure (3 months ago):**
Saltmere agents discovered and exposed three of Voss's informants in different towns. Not killed - just publicly revealed. Now those informants are compromised. Voss has replacements but the exposure shows Saltmere is learning his methods. Information war is heating up.

**Shallowmere Contested Waters (ongoing):**
Shallowmere (~700) sits in disputed waters between Salt Shore and Silverbelt. Both Voss and Silverbelt Houses claim authority. Town is playing both sides for better terms. Voss can't force the issue without open conflict with Silverbelt powers. Frustrating stalemate.

### The Trend

**Victories:** Strategic, methodical, building foundation slowly  
**Setbacks:** Mostly involving outside powers (Saltmere, Silverbelt) interfering in his consolidation

**His assessment:** He's winning the internal Salt Shore consolidation (towns are choosing him more often than resisting). But external pressures from economic powers are accelerating. He needs to move faster than planned or risk losing ground to Saltmere influence.

---

## WHAT VOSS WANTS

Short term: Stop Saltmere's creep without starting a war he doesn't need

Long term: Die in his bed, which at 51 with his history is not guaranteed

What he'd never admit: He built this for the Salt Shore more than for himself. Watching Saltmere buy it one council seat at a time is the only thing that comes close to making him angry.
