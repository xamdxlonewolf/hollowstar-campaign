# The Hexarchs

**Type:** Theocratic council — six high priests, one per god of the Six-Fold Faith  
**Power Base:** Central Heartlands and Northern Expanse primarily; temple presence in every region  
**Size:** Six council members; thousands of priests, clergy, and lay faithful beneath them  
**Leader:** No single leader — council of equals, though **Hexarch Aldren Voss-Karath** (Hexarch of the Architect) holds dominant political influence  
**Primary Goal:** Maintain the Six-Fold Faith's institutional authority and guide the faithful through a crisis that is straining their theology to its limits  
**Alignment:** Institutional. Committed to order, continuity, and the faith — in that priority, more often than they would admit.

---

## CORE IDENTITY

The Hexarchs are the highest authority of the Six-Fold Faith — six high priests representing the six divine aspects, collectively guiding one of the continent's oldest and most widely followed religions. For most of history, their authority has been comfortable. The world operated on timescales the Six-Fold Faith could explain. Gods acted through events that could be interpreted. The faithful had answers.

The fourth Hollostar is not a thing the Six-Fold Faith was built to explain.

It is too large, too indifferent, and too measurably physical to fit cleanly into any of the faith's existing frameworks. Each Hexarch is responding to this problem differently, and the council is fracturing along the lines of those responses — quietly, for now, but the fractures are deepening.

---

## THE SIX HEXARCHS

Each Hexarch oversees the priesthood and doctrine for one of the six divine aspects. They are elected by senior clergy within their aspect's tradition, serve for life, and hold equal formal authority on the council.

### Hexarch Aldren Voss-Karath — The Architect
*Aspect: Law, order, structure, institutions*

The most politically powerful figure on the council. Has survived four internal Hexarch crises by being the last person anyone wanted to move against. More flexible than he appears and more ruthless than anyone expects.

His position on the approaching star: it is a test of institutional resilience. The faith will survive by demonstrating that it can guide people through chaos — not by having easy answers, but by maintaining structure when structure is all that's left.

**Secret:** He knows about the radical sect within the Hexarchs that privately believes the Hollowstars are the gods. He has not moved against them because their theory, if proven, would solve a theological problem he has been unable to resolve for decades. He is waiting to see if they're right before deciding whether to suppress or embrace the idea.

### Hexarch of the Wanderer
*Aspect: Journey, change, adaptation, the road*

The most theologically flexible Hexarch. Quietly sympathetic to First Flame positions — which creates friction with the more conservative members of the council, though he is careful about where he expresses this. Believes the approaching star may represent a necessary change rather than a catastrophe.

His private view: the Wanderer's aspect has always been about moving through transitions, not preventing them. The faith's job right now is helping people walk through what's coming, not pretending it isn't.

### Hexarch of the Hearthkeeper
*Aspect: Home, community, sustenance, the living*

The most practically focused member of the council. Deeply concerned with what happens to ordinary people — the communities that will be disrupted, the refugees from Frostwell, the factory workers in Lumencrest if the Heart fails. Finds the abstract theological debate about the star's divine nature exhausting when there are immediate human needs being unmet.

Has been quietly directing temple resources toward community preparedness in ways that exceed the council's formal decisions. Voss-Karath is aware of this and has chosen not to raise it.

### Hexarch of the Deep
*Aspect: Death, endings, cycles, what lies beneath*

One of the two members of the radical sect who privately believe the Hollowstars are the gods — or more precisely, that the Hollowstars are the physical manifestation of the cycle of endings and renewals that the Deep's aspect governs. Finds the Somnarchs' mechanical explanation of the cycle theologically fascinating and compatible with belief, not opposed to it.

Has sent a researcher to Frostwell to observe the approaching star "theologically." Has not told the full council this is happening. Voss-Karath knows. The moderates don't.

### Hexarch of the Flame
*Aspect: Creation, transformation, fire, the new*

The second member of the radical sect. Less measured than the Hexarch of the Deep — more inclined toward the idea that the star's fall is an *act of divine will* rather than a natural cycle. This is the view that most threatens the Six-Fold Faith's institutional stability, because it implies the gods are doing something to the world, and the faith has no framework for what the appropriate response to that would be.

Currently trying to establish contact with First Flame leadership, which the rest of the council views as theologically provocative.

### Hexarch of the Witness
*Aspect: Knowledge, memory, truth, history*

The quietest member of the council. Deeply invested in historical documentation. Has been working with the Somnarchs' affiliated scholars (unofficially) to cross-reference ancient records. Believes the faith's oldest texts contain references to previous falls that later clergy interpreted as metaphor. Is beginning to suspect they weren't metaphor.

## THE INTERNAL FRACTURE

The council is split into three informal positions, and the split is widening:

**The Institutionalists (Voss-Karath, Hearthkeeper):** The faith's job is to maintain order and community through the crisis. The theological questions can be resolved after. Stability now.

**The Moderates (Wanderer, Witness):** The faith needs to engage honestly with what the approaching star means. Refusing to address the theological question directly will cause more damage than the question itself.

**The Radical Sect (Deep, Flame):** The Hollowstars are, in some meaningful sense, divine. The faith has been interpreting its own theology too narrowly for centuries. This crisis is a revelation, not a catastrophe.

**What players can affect:** The researcher sent to Frostwell will return with data. What that data says — and whether it reaches only the radical sect or the full council — will shape how the fracture resolves. A Hexarch council that ruptures publicly in the middle of a continental crisis is a very different political situation than one that manages to hold together under strain.

## RELATIONSHIPS WITH OTHER FACTIONS

**First Flame Faith** — Official position: theological rivals, the "All-One Heresy" given institutional form. In practice: peaceful coexistence with ongoing friction. The Flame Hexarch's private outreach to First Flame leadership is currently the most destabilizing element of this relationship. Moderate Hexarchs cooperate with moderate First Flame clergy on community welfare with quiet regularity.

**Somnarchs** — Tense neutrality. The Somnarchs' mechanical long-cycle framing is theologically inconvenient. The Somnarchs don't require divine will for their calculations. The radical sect finds the Somnarchs simultaneously validating and disturbing — validating because the cycle confirms something intentional is happening; disturbing because the Somnarchs would describe it as physics.

**Lumencrest / Council of Lumens** — The faith has significant presence in Lumencrest, including the Scholarly Flame denomination. The Hexarchs have political relationships with all three Council factions, and those relationships are being strained as the Council's internal crisis deepens. The Hexarchs want Lumencrest stable; a collapsing Lumencrest destabilizes the faith's most important urban presence.

**Green Covenant** — Respectful distance. The Covenant's Path of Spirits-adjacent theology overlaps in places with Six-Fold Faith cosmology but diverges on institutional authority. The Covenant doesn't recognize the Hexarchs' spiritual authority over the forest or its people.

**Stillward Enclaves** — The Enclaves contain a notable proportion of devout Six-Fold Faith practitioners whose theology has evolved in isolation. The Hexarchs consider some Enclave doctrines heterodox. The Enclave communities consider the Hexarchs out of touch. Neither is wrong.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Researcher Returns:** The party intercepts or encounters the researcher sent to Frostwell by the radical sect. They have data. What that data says, who sees it first, and whether it reaches the full council or only the radical sect is suddenly in the party's hands.

**The Moderate's Request:** The Hexarch of the Witness approaches the party quietly — not as a council representative, but as an individual. She has found something in the oldest temple archives that she needs verified against physical evidence at an ancient site. She doesn't want the council to know she's asking. She will pay. She will also, if the party asks, tell them what she suspects the evidence means.

**The Theological Crisis:** A prominent Six-Fold Faith temple in a major city has experienced a schism — a significant portion of the congregation has embraced the radical sect's view that the Hollowstars are divine. The local priests are in over their heads. The Hexarchs want the situation managed without a public rupture. The party is asked to help. Neither side is entirely wrong.

**Voss-Karath's Offer:** The Hexarch of the Architect makes the party a quiet offer. He needs information gathered about the radical sect's activities — specifically, what the researcher found in Frostwell and what the Flame Hexarch has been communicating to First Flame leadership. He will pay in access, influence, and institutional protection. The implied threat for declining is not stated.

## GM NOTES

**Tone:** The Hexarchs should feel like a genuinely well-meaning institution under impossible pressure. None of them are villains. Voss-Karath's ruthlessness is in service of an institution he believes in. The radical sect's beliefs are not obviously wrong. The moderates' desire to engage honestly is admirable and possibly too slow. Players should be able to sympathize with every position while recognizing that the fracture itself is dangerous regardless of who is right.

**The theological question** (are the Hollowstars divine?) is intentionally left open by the vault. The GM's answer to this — or the decision to leave it permanently ambiguous — shapes how every Hexarch-related plotline resolves.

**Voss-Karath** knows more than he says in every conversation. He is not deceptive in a manipulative sense; he is strategic in an institutional sense. He will tell the party the truth about his goals if asked directly. He will not volunteer information that complicates his position.
