# L'Empire Verdienne

The dominant political power of the Verdant Crescent's western coast and peninsula — a strict, organized, genuinely capable empire that has provided stability and prosperity to its people for three generations. The only major continental power that has historically matched Lumencrest's ambition and has been waiting, patiently and deliberately, for the right moment to surpass it.

---

**Type:** Empire  
**Capital:** [[02_Regions/Verdant_Crescent/Locations/Cities/Montauseau|Montauseau]]

**Trade:** Primary continental gateway for goods from [[Vossomar]] via the Rouçon trading kingdom across the Great Depths

## What the Empire Is

L'Empire Verdienne was built by the Montauseau family on the western peninsula over three generations — a deliberate project of consolidation, trade development, and military investment. Where Lumencrest built its dominance on Hollostar technology, the empire built its dominance on institutional strength: a capable military, reliable governance, and control of the western trade routes that connect this continent to Vossomar across the Great Depths.

The empire provides for its people in ways Lumencrest often does not. Housing standards, food security, medical access through a network of state-supported healers — these are not luxuries in the empire, they are baseline expectations of governance. The strictness of imperial law exists in direct relationship to this provision: you are cared for, and in exchange you follow the rules, contribute your service, and do not undermine the institutions that make the provision possible.

This is not a soft place. Imperial law is enforced without sentiment. The military is large, well-trained, and used when necessary. But the population trusts the empire in ways that Lumencrest's citizens, increasingly anxious about rationing and institutional paralysis, do not trust their Council. That trust is the empire's most significant strategic asset and Empress Carmine understands this clearly.

## Empress Carmine Montauseau

**Age:** 34 | **Ancestry:** Human-Elven | **Reign:** 10 years

Her father, Emperor Aldric Montauseau, died unexpectedly at 61 — healthy one season, gone the next. He left the crown to his eldest daughter with a letter that those who saw it describe as containing exactly three sentences. Nobody has said what the sentences were.

Carmine was 24 when she inherited. The nobility expected a period of instability — a young empress learning the role, factional jockeying for influence, perhaps a regent arrangement. She did not allow any of it. Within eighteen months the three noble families who had made quiet moves toward consolidating influence had been formally rebuked, economically restricted, and in one case stripped of a provincial governorship. Done publicly, done legally, done without cruelty. Done completely.

Ten years later the empire is more consolidated than it was under her father and more prosperous than it has been in a generation.

**What she wants:** To be Carmine the Great. Not as vanity — as legacy. She wants her family's name to mean something permanent. She wants the empire she inherited to be larger, more secure, and more capable when she passes it on than when she received it. The conflict with Lumencrest is the clearest path to that legacy, but only if it is won cleanly and at the right moment. A costly war that weakens the empire is worse than no war. She will not move until she is confident of success.

**Her intelligence:** She is aware of Lumencrest's Hollostar decline. Not the precise numbers — nobody outside the Conservators knows those — but the pattern. Her agents in Lumencrest (she has agents in Lumencrest) have been reporting the signs for three years. The rationing, the research panic, the political paralysis. She is building a picture. She is patient. She is not rushing.

**Her character:** Genuinely cares about her people in a way that is not performed. Walks the city without heavy escort. Knows the names of her military commanders' families. Has visited the service labor camps for asylum seekers — not for politics, to see whether the system is working. It is, mostly. She made two changes based on what she found. This is how she governs.

**Her restraint:** She will not go to war because it would be satisfying or because she could. She will go to war when her advisors tell her the moment is right, when the strategic picture is clear, and when she has weighed the cost to her people against the gain. She has been weighing it for three years. The scale is moving.

## Structure and Governance

**The Empress:** Supreme authority. Not a figurehead — Carmine's direct governance is active and detailed. She reads reports, she asks questions, she makes decisions that her council sometimes disagrees with and she sometimes revises. The empire functions because she functions.

**The Imperial Council:** Seven advisors — military, trade, internal affairs, foreign intelligence, law, infrastructure, and the court archivist. They advise. She decides. The relationship is functional and occasionally contentious. She wants honest counsel, not agreement. Several of her advisors have told her things she did not want to hear. They are still on the council.

**Provincial Governors:** The subordinate cities and their surrounding territories are governed by appointed governors who report to the council. Governors who govern well keep their positions. Governors who govern poorly or corruptly are replaced, publicly, with explanation. The position comes with real authority and real accountability.

**The Military:** The empire's standing army is the largest on the continent outside of Lumencrest's combined forces. Professional, long-service, well-supplied. Not conscript — the empire pays well enough that recruitment is rarely a problem. The navy is smaller but capable; the western trade routes require it.

## The Asylum System

Anyone born outside the empire who seeks residence — refugees, escaped Clanks, displaced families, individuals fleeing persecution — is accepted under the imperial asylum system with one condition: a mandatory service period of two to three years in either the military or state labor (farming, infrastructure, construction, state services).

This is not punitive. It is practical. The empire provides housing, food, medical care, and a wage during the service period. At the end, the person receives full imperial citizenship, a land grant or housing credit, and permanent residency rights.

**The Clank situation:** Escaped Clanks from Lumencrest's research programs arrive occasionally — rarely in groups, usually alone or in pairs, having made a dangerous journey west. The empire accepts them under the same asylum system as everyone else. The service requirement applies equally. Several Clanks have completed their service and are now free imperial citizens — working as smiths, engineers, laborers, soldiers. A small Clank community has formed in Montauseau's outer districts.

Lumencrest knows the empire shelters escaped Clanks and is furious about it. The empire's official position is that asylum seekers who complete their service obligations are imperial citizens and the Council of Lumens has no jurisdiction over imperial citizens. Lumencrest has made diplomatic complaints. The empire has responded with politely worded letters that contain no change in policy.

This is one of several standing grievances that would have become flashpoints if either side were willing to push hard enough. Neither has been willing. Not yet.

## The Forest Non-Interference Agreement

The empire's authority ends at the forest's edge. This has been true since before the empire existed in its current form — the Montauseau family's predecessors learned that the forest cannot be governed and that attempting to govern it creates enemies you cannot defeat.

The Green Covenant's position is clear: the forest is not imperial territory, has never been imperial territory, and will not become imperial territory. The empire accepts this. In exchange, the Covenant does not interfere in imperial governance of the coastal and peninsula territories, does not support or shelter imperial dissidents in the forest, and maintains the trade routes through forest-edge settlements that both sides benefit from.

The agreement is unwritten. It does not need to be written. Both sides have tested its edges enough times to understand precisely where they are.

**Current status:** Stable. The empire's interest in the forest is primarily trade-focused — forest goods, medicinal plants, timber from sustainable edge operations. The Covenant's interest in the empire is primarily buffer-focused — a strong empire on the western coast prevents Lumencrest from ever approaching the forest from that direction. They are useful to each other without being allies.

## The Lumencrest Cold War

The empire and Lumencrest have been in a state of armed peace for sixty years — two periods of actual conflict before that, both inconclusive, both expensive, both ending when Lumencrest's Hollostar advantage proved decisive in the final stages.

The empire has never forgotten this. The empire has never stopped preparing.

**What the empire knows:** Lumencrest's Heart is failing. The rate of decline is uncertain but the direction is not. The political paralysis inside the Council is real — the empire's agents have confirmed three separate sources on the factional deadlock. The Expansionists are the most dangerous faction because their objectives directly threaten imperial trade routes and regional dominance.

**What the empire is doing:** Quietly increasing military readiness without triggering Lumencrest's intelligence apparatus into alarm. Building food and supply reserves. Strengthening relationships with subordinate cities to ensure imperial solidarity if conflict comes. Watching the Frostwell situation — a fourth Hollostar core landing anywhere on the continent changes the strategic picture significantly.

**What Carmine is waiting for:** The moment when Lumencrest's Heart falls below the threshold that makes their military advantage real. Not when it fails entirely — before that, when the gap closes enough that an imperial campaign becomes viable without catastrophic loss. Her advisors are divided on when that moment arrives. She is listening carefully and has not decided.

**Lumencrest's awareness:** The Council knows the empire watches them. They suspect agents at high levels but have not confirmed specific identities. The Expansionists consider this a reason to act now, before the situation gets worse. The Conservators consider this a reason to stabilize the Heart at any cost. The Illuminists consider this a reason to find a solution faster. Nobody is wrong. Nobody agrees on what to do.

## Trade with Vossomar

The empire's other major strategic advantage is its position as the primary western gateway for trade with the continent of Vossomar across the Great Depths. The Rouçon kingdom on Vossomar's eastern coast has been trading with Montauseau for over a century — exotic goods, materials not found on this continent, knowledge and craft traditions that have no equivalent here.

Vossomar has no Hollowstars. Its civilizations developed entirely differently — without the energy windfalls and disruptions that have shaped this continent's history. Rouçon merchants find this continent's Hollostar-dependency fascinating and slightly alarming. They do not discuss their own continent's problems in trade negotiations, which the empire has noted.

The trade routes across the Great Depths are treacherous, long, and exclusively navigated by the empire's most experienced captains and their Rouçon counterparts. No other continental power has successfully established a western trade route. Lumencrest has tried twice. Both attempts were unsuccessful in ways that the empire had nothing to do with — officially.

## Relationships

- **[[04_Factions/Verdant_Crescent/The Green Covenant|The Green Covenant]]:** Non-interference, mutual utility. Not allies. Not enemies. A stable arrangement both sides benefit from maintaining.
- **[[04_Factions/Central_Heartlands/Council of Lumens|Council of Lumens]]:** Cold war, armed peace. Sixty years of preparation on both sides. The empire is watching the clock.
- **[[04_Factions/Veilmarkets|Veilmarkets]]:** The empire has a crime problem like any major power. Nine-Lanterns' Salt Shore network occasionally touches imperial waters. The empire's response to Veilmarket operations is direct and does not involve negotiation.
- **[[02_Regions/Verdant_Crescent/Locations/Towns/Edge_Settlements|Edge Settlements]]:** Complex. The edge settlements are not imperial territory — they operate under Covenant-observed agreements. But they trade with Montauseau, use imperial roads, and are economically connected to the empire whether or not they are politically part of it. Carmine is not pressing the question of formal incorporation. She does not need to.

## Campaign Angles

- **The Spy Question:** Lumencrest's Council believes imperial agents operate at senior levels. Finding, exposing, or protecting those agents — depending on who the party works for — is a thread that connects Montauseau to everything happening in Lumencrest.
- **The Timing Problem:** Someone close to Empress Carmine believes the moment has arrived — that Lumencrest is weak enough now for the empire to act. Someone else believes waiting longer is still correct. The party lands in the middle of this debate, possibly without knowing that's what they've walked into.
- **The Clank Community:** A Clank who completed their service and is now a free imperial citizen knows something about Lumencrest's research programs that nobody in power has heard yet. They've been trying to report it through proper channels for six months. The channels have been unresponsive. They approach the party.
- **The Rouçon Connection:** A Rouçon trade ship arrives with a request that is not about trade. Something is happening on Vossomar and the Rouçon delegation needs the empire's help with it — but they will only negotiate with someone who understands both continents. The party becomes that intermediary.
