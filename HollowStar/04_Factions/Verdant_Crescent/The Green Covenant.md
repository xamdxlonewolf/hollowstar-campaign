# The Green Covenant

> *"We do not own the forest. We are answerable to it."*

---

## OVERVIEW

The Green Covenant is the continent's oldest and most powerful nature-focused order. Not a government, not a religion, not an army — something harder to categorize and harder to oppose. They are based in the Verdant Crescent's deep forest at the Heartgrove, but their influence extends wherever forests, rivers, and wild places exist across the continent.

**What they are:** A dedicated order of practitioners — druids, rangers, scholars, and guardians — bound by oath to protect the ancient natural balance.

**What they are not:** The government of the Verdant Crescent (settlements are independent), a religious institution (though spirituality is woven through everything), or a peaceful organization (they have never started a war, but they have ended two).

**Base:** Verdant Crescent (Heartgrove), with members in Glittering Marshes and scattered across rural areas

**Philosophy:** *"The stars are wounds in the world. Stop picking at them."*

---

## RELIGIOUS FOUNDATION

**ALL Green Covenant members MUST follow the Path of Spirits.** This is non-negotiable.

- Not all Path of Spirits followers are Green Covenant (most aren't — the Covenant is relatively small)
- Members can be shamans, druids, or common practitioners
- But you cannot be Green Covenant without being a spirit-worshiper

**Why:** The Covenant views their mission as spiritually mandated — protecting nature is protecting the spirits themselves. The forest is not merely trees and soil; it is living consciousness deserving reverence and defense.

**The spirits:** Ancient entities dwelling in old growth forests, deep waters, sacred groves. The Covenant sees itself as voice and hand of these spirits in a world increasingly dominated by those who don't listen.

---

## THE ARCHCOVENANT

**Title:** The Archcovenant — singular leader, chosen by the Heartgrove itself  
**Current:** **Maren Stillwater** (human, age 67)

**How selection works:** When an Archcovenant dies or steps down, candidates spend three days alone in the Heartgrove. The forest chooses — candidates emerge either confirmed or not. No one has ever explained exactly what happens inside. Those who emerge chosen rarely describe it.

A living vine appears around the chosen's left forearm when they're selected. Nobody tells candidates this will happen. Nobody can explain it. Maren's vine appeared on her second day in the Heartgrove.

**Maren Stillwater:**
- Only the third human to hold the title (most Archcovenants have been Elf or Simiah)
- Selected 12 years ago — controversial at first (too short-lived, critics said)
- Has proven herself through her handling of Lumencrest's eastern incursions
- Currently carrying the weight of the ancient waking alone — has told her inner circle, not the wider Covenant
- Believes the waking is connected to the Hollowstars crisis but cannot prove it yet
- **Quote:** *"The forest has been patient for ten thousand years. It is running out of patience. I am trying to understand why before everyone else has to find out."*

**Her burden:** Maren knows more than she's telling. The 2,000-year-old records in the Memory archives describe previous ancient wakings. Those records also say: *"The last time the deep forest turned outward, three settlements ceased to exist. Not destroyed. Simply... incorporated."*

She hasn't decided whether to tell the other Wardens.

## STRUCTURE

**The Archcovenant** — singular authority, final word on Covenant matters

**The Five Wardens** — regional leads, each responsible for a forest zone or Covenant interest:

**Warden of the Heartgrove** — Protects the sacred center, manages the Deep Forest settlements
- Current: **Elder Fenn Mossgrow** (Simiah, age 154)
- Eldest member, deeply traditional, knows the Deep Forest better than anyone alive

**Warden of the Edge** — Manages relations with border settlements and outside world
- Current: **Thalia Greenmark** (human, age 44)
- Diplomatic but firm, maintains "the list" (those who violate agreements mysteriously find forest travel difficult)
- Handles timber negotiations with L'Empire and edge towns

**Warden of Waters** — Rivers, wetlands, the Glittering Marshes connection
- Current: **Kael Streamrunner** (Ribbet, age 67)
- Manages forest-marsh boundary, coordinates with marsh communities
- Expert in water ecology and watershed protection

**Warden of Memory** — Maintains the Covenant's historical records and ancient knowledge
- Current: **Seren Longbranch** (Simiah, age 203)
- Guardian of the sealed archives, knows things Maren hasn't asked about yet
- Only Warden besides Maren who's read the 2,000-year records

**Warden of the Reach** — Covenant's agents outside the Verdant Crescent
- Current: **Daven Ashwood** (half-elf, age 89)
- Intelligence, diplomacy, interference in other regions
- Has agents in Lumencrest, Heartlands, even Starfall Basin
- Currently operating without Maren's full authorization (preparing response to Expansionists)

**Grove Keepers** — Individual settlement/grove representatives, report to relevant Warden (20-30 active)

**Rangers** — Forest patrol, monitoring, first response (~200 active)

**Initiates** — New members, 3-year probationary period (~100 current)

**Total active Covenant members:** ~400-500 (small, elite, widely distributed)

## MISSION AND PRACTICE

**The Core Oath:**
*"I place the balance before myself, before my kind, before my preferences. The forest endures. I am its voice for as long as I am worthy."*

**What they protect:**
- Ancient trees (especially the Covenant's twelve Sacred Groves)
- River systems and watersheds
- Wildlife migration routes
- The Heartgrove's deep mystery
- The balance between settlement and wild — sustainable use is permitted, extraction is not

**How they operate:**
- Covenant members embedded in every major settlement (monitoring, advisory)
- Rangers patrol forest edges (not armed patrols — observers who can call for response)
- The Reach: Agents operating outside the Crescent (intelligence, covert operations)
- Diplomatic: First response is always negotiation
- Escalation: When diplomacy fails, actions are taken that nobody claims responsibility for

**The cautious militancy:**
The Covenant will act to protect the forest — burned logging camps, sabotaged equipment, "accidents" in extraction operations. But they never act publicly, never claim responsibility, never leave evidence connecting to Covenant leadership. Lumencrest knows. They can't prove it.

## THE INTERNAL DIVIDE: MODERATES AND THE THORNS

The Covenant presents unified face to outsiders. Internally, tension exists between majority and radical minority.

### The Moderates (Majority, ~85%)

**Philosophy:** Work within systems, escalate only when necessary, maintain sustainable relationships with settlements

**Approach:**
- Negotiate logging agreements with forest edge towns
- Embed advisors in settlements to guide sustainable practices
- Respond to violations with warnings, then measured force
- Believe long-term survival requires cooperation with non-Covenant peoples

**Leadership:** Archcovenant Maren, most Wardens (especially Edge and Waters)

**Tactics:**
- Education and diplomatic pressure
- Economic incentives for sustainable practices
- Protective action only when agreements violated
- Cautiously extreme but controlled

### The Thorns (Extremist Minority, ~15%)

**Philosophy:** The forest is dying by a thousand cuts. Negotiation has failed. Stronger action is needed now.

**Who they are:** Secret faction within Covenant, organized informally, no official leadership structure. Membership suspected but not confirmed. They take the Core Oath seriously — *before my kind, before my preferences* — and believe protecting the forest requires actions the moderate leadership won't authorize.

**What they want:**
- End all logging near ancient groves (not reduce, end)
- Active sabotage of Lumencrest's timber operations
- Preemptive strikes against Expansionist military buildup
- Force settlements to retreat from forest edge, not just slow expansion

**What they've done (suspected):**
- The three burned logging camps (10 years ago) — officially unsanctioned Covenant action, whispered to be Thorn operation
- Recurring "accidents" at Timberwatch sawmills (equipment failures, structural collapses)
- Disappearance of two Lumencrest surveyors mapping Deep Forest approaches (4 years ago, never found)

**What they're planning:**
Unknown to most Covenant, possibly unknown even to Maren. The Thorns operate through informal networks. Some suspects:
- Disrupting Lumencrest's supply lines more aggressively
- Sabotaging Council research programs studying shard extraction
- Direct action against Expansionist military camps if they enter Crescent territory

**Leadership (suspected):**
- No confirmed leader, but **Warden of the Reach Daven Ashwood** is suspected of Thorn sympathies
- Some Grove Keepers and Rangers are likely members
- Operates through cells - members know only their immediate contacts

**Maren's dilemma:**
She knows Thorns exist. She suspects who some members are. She hasn't moved against them because:
- She partly sympathizes (Lumencrest is dangerous, forest genuinely threatened)
- Cracking down could fracture Covenant when unity is essential
- Thorns have been careful (no deaths, minimal evidence, plausible deniability)
- With ancient waking happening, she needs all Covenant focused, not fighting internally

But if Thorns escalate too far, Maren will have to choose between controlling her own people and maintaining the forest protection they provide.

## RELATIONSHIP WITH LUMENCREST

**Hostile. Specifically and carefully hostile.**

Lumencrest's expansion requires timber. Timber requires logging. Logging has been moving progressively closer to Verdant Crescent territory for forty years. The Covenant has responded:

**Stage 1 (30 years ago):** Formal diplomatic protest. Ignored.

**Stage 2 (20 years ago):** Covenant agents embedded in Lumencrest's lumber operations (sabotage, delays, "accidents"). Costly but not decisive. This was official Covenant policy, authorized by previous Archcovenant.

**Stage 3 (10 years ago):** Three Lumencrest logging camps on the forest edge burned. No one was harmed. No one claimed responsibility. Lumencrest knows it was Covenant action. Maren (newly selected Archcovenant) neither confirmed nor authorized it. Suspected to be Thorn operation.

**Current:** Cold standoff. Lumencrest pushes at edges; Covenant responds; neither escalates to open conflict. Yet.

**The Expansionist faction in Lumencrest** wants to force the issue — take the timber by force if necessary. The Covenant's Warden of the Reach has intelligence on this and is preparing a response. Maren has not authorized it yet. Daven has not asked for authorization.

**The timber question:**
- Lumencrest wants dramatically increased lumber for construction, fuel, export
- Green Covenant strictly limits sustainable harvest
- L'Empire Verdienne respects Covenant authority (non-interference agreement protects Deep Forest)
- Some forest edge towns (Timberwatch especially) are tempted by Lumencrest's premium prices
- Covenant pressures these towns through embedded members and "the list"

**What prevents open war:**
- L'Empire's protection means attacking Verdant Crescent brings Lumencrest into conflict with maritime empire
- Covenant doesn't want war (forest damaged regardless of who wins)
- Lumencrest Conservators and Illuminists oppose military escalation
- Only if Lumencrest faces complete collapse would Expansionists risk it

## ON THE DARKNESS

**"This is what happens when you violate natural law."**

The Covenant interprets the Hollowstars crisis — the dimming of Lumencrest's Heart, the approaching fourth star, the ancient waking — as interconnected symptoms of cosmic imbalance.

**Their theology:**
The Hollowstars are wounds in the world. Each fallen star disrupted natural order. The first Hollostar (10,000 years ago) destroyed previous civilization. The second (Lumencrest's Heart, 3,000 years ago) has been exploited for power. The third (Endless Eye, 700 years ago) created unnatural geography. Now the fourth approaches.

**The Darkness they speak of:**
- Lumencrest's Heart failing (light dimming, darkness returning)
- The forest's ancient consciousness responding to cosmic threat
- The fundamental imbalance between human ambition and natural limits
- The consequences of treating Hollowstars as resources instead of catastrophes

**Covenant perspective:**
*"You built a city on a wound in the world. You powered it by picking at the wound. Now the wound is infected and you wonder why it's killing you. This is not mystery. This is consequence."*

Most Covenant members believe Lumencrest's crisis is self-inflicted through Hollostar exploitation. They don't celebrate it, but they're not surprised. Natural law violated produces natural consequences.

The question dividing moderates and Thorns: Should Covenant help Lumencrest survive, or let consequences teach the lesson?

## THE ANCIENT WAKING — WHAT THE COVENANT KNOWS

**What's happening:**
- The Heartgrove's oldest trees are changing growth patterns (Simiah elders noticed first, 8 years ago)
- Underground root networks expanding faster than any natural rate
- Three Sacred Groves have begun producing bioluminescence they haven't shown in recorded history
- Animal behavior shifting — species that avoid each other gathering at certain deep-forest locations
- The Covenant's Memory Warden has found references in records 2,000 years old: *"When the deep forest turns its attention outward, something has reminded it of what was taken."*

**What it might be:**
- A natural cycle on a scale longer than recorded history
- A response to the Hollowstars crisis (Maren's theory — the forest sensing something cosmic)
- Something the Covenant's predecessors sealed away, waking now
- Something that wants to communicate

**What Maren has decided:**
Do not tell the settlements until she knows more. The forest is not attacking — it is *activating*. She needs to understand the difference between a threat and an awakening before people panic.

**What she hasn't decided:**
Whether to tell the other Wardens about the 2,000-year-old records. Those records describe previous wakings. They say: *"The last time the deep forest turned outward, three settlements ceased to exist. Not destroyed. Simply... incorporated."*

**Current status:** Accelerating. Changes are visible to Deep Forest residents now. Covenant is preparing without announcing. Maren carries the weight alone, except for Warden of Memory Seren, who knows what the archives contain.

## RECENT VICTORIES AND SETBACKS (LAST 2 YEARS)

### Victories:

**Edge Town Agreements Renewed (18 months ago):**
Successfully renegotiated sustainable logging agreements with five forest edge towns including Thornvale and Greenmark. Terms favor Covenant limits. Towns chose Covenant relationship over Lumencrest money.

**Glittering Marshes Coordination (16 months ago):**
Warden of Waters established formal information-sharing with marsh communities about watershed threats. Mutual protection agreement signed.

**L'Empire Reaffirmation (12 months ago):**
L'Empire Verdienne publicly reaffirmed Deep Forest protection in diplomatic statement. Message clearly directed at Lumencrest Expansionists.

**Timberwatch Pressure Success (8 months ago):**
Family in Timberwatch who took Lumencrest money for excess logging found their operation mysteriously unprofitable. Equipment failures, worker departures, "bad luck." They stopped.

### Setbacks:

**Warden of Reach Operating Independently (ongoing):**
Daven Ashwood has agents preparing aggressive response to Lumencrest Expansionists without Maren's authorization. She knows. She hasn't stopped him. But if he acts, she loses control of escalation.

**Thorn Activity Increasing (last year):**
More suspected Thorn operations - sabotaged equipment, burned supply wagons, intimidation of surveyors. Nothing provably fatal yet. But escalating. Maren can't crack down without fracturing Covenant.

**Ancient Waking Visibility (6 months ago):**
Bioluminescence in Sacred Groves now visible from some edge settlements. Questions being asked. Covenant deflecting but can't hide much longer.

**Timberwatch Elder Killed (4 months ago):**
Town elder who supported Covenant logging limits died in "accident." Circumstances suspicious. Town now more afraid of Lumencrest than Covenant. Relationship damaged.

### The Trend:
Covenant maintaining forest protection but control slipping. Thorns operating more boldly. Ancient waking becoming public. Lumencrest pressure increasing. Maren managing multiple crises while carrying secret knowledge about what might happen if forest fully wakes.

## CAMPAIGN HOOKS

**The Thorn Dilemma:**
Party discovers evidence of planned Thorn operation against Lumencrest. Do they report to Maren (who might stop it but fracture Covenant) or let it proceed (risking war)?

**The Incorporated Settlements:**
Warden of Memory Seren needs someone to investigate what "incorporated" means in 2,000-year records. Find the three settlements that ceased to exist. What happened? Are people still there, somehow changed?

**The Warden's Agents:**
Warden of the Reach recruits party for covert mission in Lumencrest. Discover he's preparing strike against Expansionist military buildup. Maren hasn't authorized this. Whose side are you on?

**The Ancient Waking:**
Deep Forest residents report trees moving, animals behaving strangely, voices in the night. Covenant investigating. Party hired as outside observers (Covenant worried their own biases affect judgment). What's really waking?

**The Timber Spy:**
Lumencrest agent infiltrated forest edge town, gathering intelligence for Expansionist invasion plans. Covenant knows. Party must extract agent quietly before Thorns find them first (Thorns won't be gentle).

**Between Moderates and Extremists:**
Party has contacts in both Covenant mainstream and suspected Thorns. Both sides want party's help. Choose one or try to bridge divide before Covenant civil conflict erupts.
