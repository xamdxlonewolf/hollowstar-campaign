# The Veilmarkets

**Type:** Decentralized criminal and information network  
**Power Base:** No fixed territory — embedded in trade cities, ports, and border towns across the continent  
**Size:** Unknown. Estimated hundreds of active operators; thousands of casual contacts  
**Leader:** **Director Sable** (identity, ancestry, age unknown) — continental coordinator, closest thing to central authority  
**Primary Goal:** Profit. Survival. Positioning for whatever comes next.  
**Alignment:** Mercenary. Not evil by conviction — just unbothered by the costs their work imposes on others.

---

## CORE IDENTITY

The Veilmarkets are not a guild, a syndicate, or a conspiracy. They are an *ecosystem* — a network of operators, fronts, information brokers, couriers, and fixers who share infrastructure, protocols, and a loose code of conduct without sharing loyalty to any cause larger than the network itself.

They traffic in everything profitable: goods that can't move through official channels, information that governments would prefer stay quiet, services that require discretion, and — increasingly — Hollostar fragments, which have become the most valuable commodity on the continent as the fourth fall approaches.

**What sets them apart from ordinary criminal organizations:**
- **Information is their primary product.** Moving goods is secondary. The Veilmarkets' real value is knowing things: what's happening, where, who wants it kept quiet, and who would pay to know it.
- **Decentralization is a feature, not a flaw.** No single point of failure. Regional bosses operate independently. Destroying one cell doesn't touch the others.
- **Reputation for fair dealing within their own terms.** The Veilmarkets have survived for generations because they deliver what they promise and don't betray paying clients without reason. This is meaningfully different from trustworthiness, but it is not nothing.

## STRUCTURE

### Director Sable
The closest thing the Veilmarkets have to a central authority. Sable coordinates between regional operations, manages continental-scale information flow, and makes decisions that affect the whole network. Their true identity — ancestry, age, even gender — is unknown. They appear differently at every meeting. Not through magic; through careful, deliberate craft.

The one constant is quality. Whatever they're wearing fits perfectly. Whatever accent they're using sounds native. They are professionally pleasant, genuinely curious about people, and have spent decades learning how to read and use them. They have a reputation for fair dealing within the Veilmarkets' own terms.

**Current state:** Sable has assembled a picture of something none of the major factions know — pieced together over years through the network's information flow. They have not decided what to do with it. They are watching to see who will pay the most, but privately, they are not certain this particular secret should be sold to anyone.

### Regional Bosses
Each major region has one or more operators running local cells. They report loosely to Sable but make most decisions independently. The Endless Eye, Lumencrest, and the Silverbelt Coast each have established presences. Frostwell is thin — always has been, too remote and too cold for profitable operations. That is changing as the fourth fall draws attention north.

### Operators and Contacts
The bulk of the network. Street-level fixers, inn owners who ask no questions, ship captains who carry sealed cargo, merchants who pass messages alongside legitimate goods. Most know only their immediate handler. The network's horizontal opacity is intentional.

## WHAT THEY TRAFFIC

**Information** — Their most valuable product. Who is meeting with whom. What the Merchant Circle is hiding about the Endless Eye. What Lumencrest's Expansionists are actually planning. What the Somnarchs know but won't say publicly. Information arrives through a thousand channels and gets assembled, sold, or held depending on its value.

**Hollostar fragments** — The fastest-growing market. Shards, splinters, dust — anything with Hollostar energy. Buyers include Lumencrest researchers, Starfall Seekers, wealthy collectors, and individuals the Veilmarkets don't ask too many questions about. Prices have tripled in the past year.

**Contraband goods** — Restricted materials, cross-border shipments avoiding tariffs, goods that certain governments would prefer not arrive at certain destinations.

**People** — Extraction of individuals who need to move without official documentation. Not trafficking in the predatory sense; the Veilmarkets move people who want to be moved. It is still lucrative.

**Services** — Access to things money can't officially buy. Information deletion. Arranged meetings between parties who can't be seen together. Disappearing inconvenient evidence.

## RELATIONSHIPS WITH OTHER FACTIONS

**Somnarchs** — Guarded mutual use. The Somnarchs occasionally need information channels that official routes can't provide. The Veilmarkets want advance warning of world-changing events. Both parties maintain plausible deniability. Neither trusts the other.

**Lumencrest / Council of Lumens** — Complicated. The Expansionists use Veilmarket channels for intelligence operations they can't run officially. The Conservators would prefer the Veilmarkets didn't exist. The Illuminists have quietly purchased information on multiple occasions and pretend they haven't.

**The Merchant Circle (Endless Eye)** — Wary coexistence. The Veilmarkets have a significant presence in the Endless Eye's underground economy. The Merchant Circle tolerates it because disrupting the network would cost more than the disruption is worth, and because several Circle members quietly benefit from Veilmarket access.

**Starfall Seekers** — Transactional. Seekers with money and no ethics buy fragments through Veilmarket channels. Seekers with ethics resent the Veilmarkets driving up shard prices and moving artifacts out of scholarly reach.

**Six-Fold Faith** — Officially antagonistic. In practice, certain temples quietly use Veilmarket information services. The Hexarchs don't ask where their intelligence comes from. The Veilmarkets don't volunteer the answer.

**Everyone else** — The Veilmarkets have contacts in every major faction. They sell to all sides. Neutrality is their most valuable asset, which is why the current crisis — where factions are starting to demand loyalty — is the most dangerous period in the network's recent history.

---

## THE CURRENT CRISIS AS OPPORTUNITY

Every major disruption in history has made the Veilmarkets stronger. Governments collapse; the network adapts. Trade routes close; the Veilmarkets find others. The approaching fourth fall is the largest disruption in living memory, and Sable is positioning accordingly.

**What the Veilmarkets are doing right now:**
- Buying information aggressively — anything related to the fall, Hollostar energy, ancient sites, or faction plans
- Quietly stockpiling high-value portable goods in distributed caches
- Mapping safe routes through regions that will be disrupted
- Watching the Somnarchs, the Seekers, and the Frostwell evacuation situation with careful attention

**The risk they are managing:** Factions are going to want allies, not contractors, as the crisis deepens. The pressure to pick a side will intensify. Sable's position is that neutrality serves the network better than allegiance — but that calculation has limits, and the limits are approaching.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Sealed Package:** The party is hired (through intermediaries) to transport a sealed package from one city to another. No questions. Standard Veilmarket contract. The package is, of course, not what they were told it is — and someone else knows what's inside and wants it badly enough to follow them.

**The Information Auction:** Sable is holding an unusual event: an auction for a single piece of information, sold to the highest bidder, contents unknown until purchase. Multiple factions are sending representatives. The party is hired by one faction to attend — or to make sure a rival faction doesn't walk away with the winning bid.

**The Burned Contact:** A Veilmarket operator in a regional city has gone silent. They were mid-transaction on something significant. Sable wants to know if they're dead, compromised, or running. The party is dispatched to find out — and to retrieve whatever the operator was carrying.

**The Price of Neutrality:** A faction the party is aligned with puts pressure on them to use their Veilmarket contacts to obtain something damaging about a rival. The Veilmarkets will sell it. But Sable will also know the party used the network politically — and will remember that the next time someone asks about them.

**What Sable Knows:** The party discovers, through some chain of events, that Sable has assembled a complete picture of something critical — possibly related to the fourth fall, possibly to one of the awakening sites, possibly to a faction secret with continental implications. Sable is willing to share it. The price is not money.

## GM NOTES

**Tone:** The Veilmarkets should never feel like a cartoon criminal organization. They are pragmatic, professional, and genuinely useful. Working with them has costs — in reputation, in what they now know about the party, in the debts that accumulate — but the costs aren't obvious at first. The relationship should feel transactional and slightly too comfortable until it isn't.

**Sable specifically** should feel unknowable in a way that isn't threatening so much as simply *opaque*. They are pleasant. They deliver what they promise. And they are clearly doing a calculation the party can't quite see.

**The secret Sable is sitting on** is a GM decision — it should connect to something the players are already invested in. Options: the exact impact coordinates of the fourth fall, evidence of which faction is actually responsible for the Endless Eye's decline, the true identity of someone the party trusts, or confirmation of the Somnarchs' worst private theory.
