# The First Flame (Faction)

**Type:** Monotheistic church — political and spiritual institution  
**Power Base:** Distributed; strongest in Lumencrest, Desert of Glass, and coastal trade cities  
**Size:** Large and growing — fastest-expanding faith on the continent  
**Leader:** High Flame Kaida (Giant, elderly, 50 years in power)  
**Structure:** High Flame → Flamekeepers (9 currently) → Denomination priests → Congregations  
**Primary Goal:** Spread the truth of the Eternal Flame's unity; maintain internal cohesion during crisis  
**Alignment:** Spiritually neutral, politically opportunistic in the current crisis

> *For full theological detail, denominations, sacred practices, and gameplay integration, see [[05_Religion/First Flame Faith|The First Flame Faith]]*

---

## POLITICAL IDENTITY

The First Flame is not merely a religion — it is an institution with growing political weight. Three hundred years old, it has expanded from a theological fringe movement (the "All-One Heresy," as the Hexarchs called it) into a mainstream faith with significant presence in every major region. As the current crisis shakes confidence in traditional institutions, the First Flame's message of unified divine purpose is finding new audiences.

**What makes them politically significant:**
- Growing membership across all social classes, including urban educated classes who once dismissed them
- The Scholarly Flame denomination has genuine research value — Lumencrest's Council of Lumens uses their work
- The Kindling denomination is the most effective missionary operation on the continent, active in every port city
- High Flame Kaida commands personal respect even from non-believers — her legendary pilgrimage and 50-year tenure give her moral authority
- The Unbound schism is a growing internal threat that could fracture the church's political unity

## POWER BASE BY REGION

**Lumencrest (Central Heartlands) — Significant**  
The Scholarly Flame denomination is embedded in Lumencrest's intellectual culture. They collaborate with Council of Lumens Illuminists on Hollostar research. Uncomfortable position — their theology says Lumencrest's exploitation of the Heart is wrong, but their scholars value the research access. The Unified Ember denomination is also active here, building bridges between the faith and majority-Hextheist population.

**Desert of Glass — Strong**  
The Purifying Flame denomination is dominant here, particularly in volcanic communities like Lavaspire. The faith's fire imagery resonates deeply in a region where fire is survival. Cinderhome dwarves are the notable exception — Forge-Father Faith runs too deep.

**Coastal Trade Cities (Salt Shore, Silverbelt Coast) — Growing**  
The Kindling denomination spreads along trade routes. Port cities are their most fertile ground — mobile populations, mixing cultures, people far from home who want community. The Unbound schism also concentrates here, which creates internal friction.

**Starfall Basin — Moderate**  
The Guiding Light denomination has found resonance at the Endless Eye — its contemplative approach fits the region's thoughtful culture. Underwater meditation chambers in The Endless Eye are among the faith's most unusual sacred spaces.

**Frostwell Tundra — Thin but meaningful**  
The Radiant Path denomination's Great Pilgrimage ends at Frostwell — pilgrims travel from across the continent to the frozen north as an act of ultimate faith. High Flame Kaida herself originated here, which gives the region symbolic weight far beyond its small First Flame population.

**Northern Expanse, Verdant Crescent, Glittering Marshes — Limited**  
Path of Spirits dominates these regions. The First Flame has small congregations in river towns but hasn't made significant inroads against deeply rooted animist traditions.

## LEADERSHIP

### High Flame Kaida
The supreme spiritual authority of the First Flame. A Giant of Firbolg descent from Frostwell Tundra, elderly, 50 years in power — the longest-serving High Flame in the faith's history. Her legendary three-year pilgrimage across the continent (frozen wastes to volcanic plains to the Central Heartlands) is the most documented and verified act of devotion in First Flame history, and unites all six denominations in respect even when they agree on little else.

**Her current problem:** The Eternal Flame has not given her clear guidance on the crisis. A leader chosen by divine sign, facing the biggest crisis of her tenure, without clear divine direction — this is deeply troubling to her and she has told no one.

**Her public position:** "Study, prayer, and unity."

**Her private fear:** She is elderly. If she dies during the crisis, choosing a new High Flame amid chaos could shatter the church's political unity permanently.

### The Flamekeepers (9 currently)
Advisors and administrators, not regional rulers. They tend eternal flames, mediate between denominations, counsel the High Flame, and maintain doctrinal consistency. They are explicitly forbidden from taking sides in denominational disputes or wielding political power directly — a rule that exists because two ambitious Flamekeepers caused the Unbound schism 80 years ago.

## THE UNBOUND SCHISM

**Founded:** ~80 years ago by two expelled Flamekeepers  
**Core difference:** Reject the High Flame's divine authority — same theology, no hierarchy  
**Current status:** Small but growing, concentrated in coastal cities  
**Threat level:** Increasing — they are effective missionaries who recruit *from* the main church

The Unbound preach that no mortal should claim to speak for the Eternal Flame. In an era where traditional authorities are visibly failing, this message has appeal. High Flame Kaida publicly calls them "wayward siblings." Privately, their growth worries her more than almost anything else.

## RELATIONSHIPS WITH OTHER FACTIONS

**Six-Fold Faith / Hexarchs**  
Officially: "We worship the same reality, just understand it better." In practice: peaceful coexistence with ongoing theological friction, competition for converts, and occasional genuine cooperation between moderates on both sides. The First Flame's growth worries conservative Hexarchs.

**Council of Lumens**  
The Scholarly Flame's research collaboration gives the faith a foothold in Lumencrest. The Conservators are wary of them. The Illuminists find them useful. The Expansionists don't care about theology, only leverage.

**Starfall Seekers**  
The Scholarly Flame collaborates with the academically-minded Seekers. Theological tension exists — some Seekers consider the faith's framing of Hollowstars as divine gifts to be an obstacle to objective research.

**Somnarchs**  
Theologically adjacent — both deal in long cycles and pattern. The Guiding Light denomination finds Somnarch cosmology spiritually resonant. Official First Flame position is curious rather than hostile.

**Veilmarkets**  
The First Flame publicly condemns them. The Veilmarkets avoid the faith where possible — religious institutions with genuine community loyalty are harder to corrupt and less profitable to antagonize than secular ones.

**Green Covenant**  
Limited interaction. The Kindling denomination has tried missionary work in Verdant Crescent with minimal success — Path of Spirits runs too deep.

## CURRENT STATUS & TENSIONS

**Growing at the wrong time:** The faith is expanding rapidly just as the crisis demands unity. More members means more denominational friction, more Unbound recruitment opportunities, more factions within the church itself.

**The Kaida succession problem:** She is elderly and has no obvious successor. The divine selection process requires the current High Flame to die before a new one is chosen. If she dies before the fourth star falls, the interregnum could be catastrophic.

**Denominational fracture risk:** When the fourth star lands, every denomination will interpret it differently. Purifying Flame will say it's divine judgment. Scholarly Flame will say it's measurable physics. Radiant Path will say it validates rejecting Hollowstar dependence. Kindling will use it to convert. High Flame Kaida knows this is coming and has no plan that satisfies all six.

**The Unbound timing:** A crisis that makes people distrust authority is exactly when an anti-authority schism grows fastest. The Unbound have never been better positioned.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Eternal Flame Went Out:** A flame that has burned for 200 years extinguished overnight. Omen? Sabotage? The Flamekeepers are terrified and need discreet investigators — if word spreads, it could be read as a sign that the Eternal Flame is abandoning the faithful.

**The Succession Problem:** High Flame Kaida has privately asked the party to carry sealed letters to three specific people in three different regions — to be delivered only if she dies. She won't say what's in them. She will say the letters matter more than she does.

**Unbound vs. Purifying Flame:** A coastal city is on the edge of violence. Purifying Flame priests are calling the Unbound congregation apostates. The Unbound refuse to yield their temple. The city watch doesn't want religious violence on their streets. The party is asked to mediate — or choose a side.

**The Scholarly Flame's Discovery:** A Scholarly Flame researcher in Lumencrest has found something in Hollostar data that contradicts a core theological position of the Purifying Flame. If published, it could fracture the church. She's asking for advice — suppress it, publish it, or find a third option.

**The Radiant Path Pilgrimage:** Escort a group of Radiant Path pilgrims to Frostwell — the same route High Flame Kaida once walked. The star's approach has made the journey more dangerous. The pilgrims consider the danger proof they should go.
