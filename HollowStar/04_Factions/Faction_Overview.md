# Factions Overview — World of Hollowstars

Factions are the engines of political drama in Hollowstars. Most have legitimate concerns. Most are also capable of terrible decisions. Players will rarely find a clean villain or a spotless ally — only people and institutions doing what they believe is necessary.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS OVERVIEW

**For GMs:** Each faction has goals, internal tensions, and a current crisis posture. Use these to drive NPC behavior, generate conflict, and reward players who pay attention to factional dynamics. Most factions will take actions that affect the world whether players engage with them or not.

**For players:** Factions are employers, obstacles, allies, and sources of information. Reputation with one faction often affects standing with others. No faction is universally trustworthy.

---

## CONTINENT-WIDE FACTIONS

These factions operate across multiple regions and cannot be avoided for long.

---

### Council of Lumens
**Type:** City government (three internal factions)  
**Base:** Lumencrest, Central Heartlands  
**Reach:** Dominant influence across all Central Heartlands; significant economic leverage continent-wide

The governing body of Lumencrest, split into three factions with incompatible solutions to the same crisis. The Conservators want to protect the Heart at all costs. The Illuminists want to study it aggressively. The Expansionists want to acquire outside resources by any means necessary. All three agree the Heart is failing. None agree on what to do.

**Current posture:** Paralyzed. Decisions require consensus none of the three factions will give. Meanwhile the Heart dims.

**Player hook:** Every faction wants agents who aren't obviously affiliated with the Council. Deniable, capable, and ideally willing to work against the other two factions' interests.

**→ [[04_Factions/Council of Lumens|Full details]]**

---

### The Somnarchs
**Type:** Prophetic scholarly order  
**Base:** Frostvault Observatory (Frostwell Tundra); members itinerant worldwide  
**Reach:** Small but present everywhere — a Somnarch correspondent exists in almost every major city

The world's oldest continuous institution, devoted to understanding Hollostar cycles across geological time. They have been predicting the fourth fall for decades. They are now, uncomfortably, being proven right. Currently debating whether to publicly announce that Lumencrest's Heart will likely fail completely at the moment of impact.

**Current posture:** Urgent outreach. Factions that ignored them for years are suddenly requesting private meetings.

**Player hook:** They need agents with no obvious Somnarch affiliation for sensitive verification tasks. They pay well and tell you things nobody else knows.

**→ [[04_Factions/Somnarchs|Full details]]**

---

### Starfall Seekers
**Type:** Loose research network  
**Base:** No fixed location — expeditions worldwide  
**Reach:** Present wherever Hollostar fragments or ancient ruins exist

Scholars, treasure hunters, and obsessives united by interest in Hollowstars. No central authority, no unified ethics, no accountability. Some are genuine researchers. Some are dangerous. All are desperate to be at the impact site when the fourth star falls.

**Current posture:** Racing north. Multiple competing expeditions heading toward Frostwell.

**Player hook:** They hire frequently, pay inconsistently, and their digs have a tendency to go catastrophically wrong in ways that require outside rescue.

**→ [[04_Factions/Starfall Seekers|Full details]]**

---

### The Hexarchs
**Type:** Religious council  
**Base:** Spread across Central Heartlands and Northern Expanse  
**Reach:** Wherever the Six-Fold Faith has temples — most of the civilized world

Six high priests, one for each god of the Six-Fold Faith. Deeply divided on how to interpret the current crisis. Some see it as divine test. Some see it as punishment. A radical sect quietly believes the Hollowstars *are* the gods. They have enormous institutional influence and no consensus on how to use it.

**Current posture:** Fractured. Individual Hexarchs pursuing incompatible agendas while maintaining public unity.

**Player hook:** Individual Hexarchs make powerful patrons — or powerful enemies. Their theological divisions can be exploited or bridged.

**→ [[04_Factions/The Hexarchs|Full details]]**

---

### The First Flame
**Type:** Monotheistic church  
**Base:** Growing presence in Central Heartlands and beyond  
**Reach:** Expanding rapidly, especially among younger urban populations

A 300-year-old faith that believes in one god with six aspects — the same divine reality the Six-Fold Faith divides into separate deities. Growing quickly. Politically significant. In complicated theological tension with the Hexarchs that sometimes spills into real conflict.

**Current posture:** Opportunistic expansion. The crisis has shaken faith in traditional institutions; the First Flame offers a unified answer.

**Player hook:** Their Pontifex is gathering influence rapidly. It's unclear yet whether their intentions are purely spiritual.

**→ [[04_Factions/The First Flame|Full details]]**

---

### Veilmarkets
**Type:** Criminal network  
**Base:** Decentralized — regional bosses in every major city  
**Reach:** Everywhere. Seriously, everywhere.

The continent's most successful criminal organization, built on information, logistics, and the understanding that someone always needs something moved quietly. Not specifically focused on Hollowstars, but deeply aware that cosmic crises create profit opportunities. Also aware they're not immune to the end of the world.

**Current posture:** Watching carefully, positioning for maximum advantage, quietly worried.

**Player hook:** They're the fastest route to black market resources, forbidden information, and connections that don't officially exist. The price is always more than it seems.

**→ [[04_Factions/Veilmarkets|Full details]]**

---

### Stillward Enclaves
**Type:** Isolationist communities  
**Base:** Remote regions — deep forests, mountain valleys, hidden islands  
**Reach:** Minimal by design

Communities that withdrew from the wider world, convinced that isolation is the only reliable survival strategy. They range from peaceful self-sufficient villages to paranoid stockpilers. None of them want to be found. Some of them are hoarding things the wider world desperately needs.

**Current posture:** Sealing borders. The approaching star validates everything they believed.

**Player hook:** Getting into an Enclave requires either trust-building or deception. What's inside may be worth the effort.

**→ [[04_Factions/Stillward Enclaves|Full details]]**

## REGIONAL FACTIONS

These factions are rooted in specific regions but their actions ripple outward.

---

### Central Heartlands

**The Heartland Accord** — The 400-year-old confederation holding four city-states together. Governance by consensus that currently can't reach any. Not a single faction so much as an arena where factions fight.

**The Rivermark Sovereignty** — Elven-Faun kingdom east of the Ironspine Mountains. Allied with the Accord for 135 years, currently seeking formal membership as the Sixth Seat. Controls Starsilver deposits and the eastern trade routes. Patient, deliberate, increasingly pressured by Starfall Basin merchant interests.

**→ [[04_Factions/Central_Heartlands/Power and Politics|Power and Politics]]** | **[[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/The_Rivermark_Sovereignty|Rivermark Sovereignty]]**

---

### Salt Shore

**The Tide King** — Voss built a coastal protection network from scratch. Pay a percentage, receive security. It works. It's also increasingly resembling an empire, which some coastal communities are beginning to notice and resist.

**→ [[04_Factions/Salt_Shore/The Tide King|The Tide King]]**

---

### Silverbelt Coast

**The Four Houses** — Noble houses governing through the Council of Banners. Currently deadlocked by House Caldren's succession crisis. The other three houses are maneuvering carefully.

**The Grey Sails** — Pirate fleet operating in coastal waters. More organized than pirates have any right to be. Someone is funding them, and it's not obvious who.

**→ [[04_Factions/Silverbelt_Coast/The Four Houses|The Four Houses]]** | **[[04_Factions/Silverbelt_Coast/The Grey Sails|The Grey Sails]]**

---

### Starfall Basin

**The Merchant Circle** — Fairy and Ribbet coalition controlling trade at the Endless Eye. Divided three ways on how to respond to the Eye's shrinkage. The window for organized response is closing.

**→ [[04_Factions/Starfall_Basin/Merchant Circle|Merchant Circle]]**

---

### Verdant Crescent

**The Green Covenant** — Forest protectors ranging from diplomatic moderates to the violent Thorns extremist faction. Unified by love of the forests; divided on what they're willing to do to protect them.

**→ [[04_Factions/Verdant_Crescent/The Green Covenant|The Green Covenant]]**

---

### Northern Expanse

**The King and the Crown** — King Elowen Arathas has ruled for centuries. He is patient, wise, and moving at a timescale that feels like negligence to humans facing a refugee crisis right now.

**→ [[04_Factions/Northern_Expanse/The King and the Crown|The King and the Crown]]**

## THE CRISIS POSTURE SUMMARY

As of campaign start (700 AR, Late Goldmonth):

- **Most urgent:** Council of Lumens (Heart failing now), Merchant Circle (Eye shrinking now), Somnarchs (impact in 2-5 years)
- **Positioning for advantage:** Tide King (consolidating coast), Veilmarkets (information hoarding), First Flame (recruiting during uncertainty)
- **Reacting defensively:** Four Houses (internal crisis), Stillward Enclaves (sealing borders), Hexarchs (theological fracture)
- **Active in the field:** Starfall Seekers (racing north), Green Covenant (escalating resistance), Somnarchs (outreach expeditions)
