## THE HEARTLAND ACCORD

The Heartland Accord is the Central Heartlands' governing confederation — five city-states bound by treaty into a body that coordinates trade, mutual defense, infrastructure, and dispute resolution. It is 400 years old. It has never been tested the way it is being tested now.

**The founding fiction:** Five equal partners, each sovereign within their territory, cooperating voluntarily for shared benefit. The fiction worked well enough for centuries because Lumencrest's dominance was so total that the other cities found it easier to benefit from the arrangement than fight it. That calculation is changing.

### Structure

**The Accord Council** meets quarterly in Lumencrest's Accord Chamber — a grand room built for a city at the height of its confidence that now feels slightly too large for the conversations happening inside it. Each city sends representatives; Lumencrest holds the most seats by population formula, but major decisions require a majority across delegations.

**Member cities and their voting weight:**
- **Lumencrest** — 12 seats (population-weighted, plus Chair rotation)
- **Kingsrest** — 7 seats
- **Ironhold** — 6 seats
- **Steelwatch** — 5 seats
- **Smaller towns and Rivermark** — 2 seats collectively

**Formal powers:**
- Inter-city trade regulation and tariff setting
- Mutual defense obligations (each city contributes forces to shared defense)
- Shared infrastructure (roads, bridges, river management)
- Dispute arbitration between member cities
- Food reserve coordination — the most contentious current issue

**What the Accord cannot do:**
- Compel a city to act against its direct interests
- Override a city's internal governance
- Tax member cities directly (operates on voluntary contributions)
- Enforce decisions without consensus — which means when consensus fails, nothing is enforced

### The Accord in Practice: Currently

The Accord has not passed a significant coordinated measure in fourteen months. Every major vote has collapsed into factional negotiation. The food reserve coordination — theoretically the Accord's most essential current function — has become a source of open suspicion rather than cooperation.

**Crown Prince Cedric's investigation** (unofficial, Kingsrest) has identified discrepancies between what Kingsrest's farms are reporting and what the Accord's food reserve records show. Someone is either misrepresenting yields to the Accord or misrepresenting Accord distributions to member cities. This has not become public. When it does, it will threaten the Accord's legitimacy at exactly the moment the Accord needs to function.

**Ironhold's secret Lumencrest treaty** (70 years old, never ratified publicly) commits Ironhold to specific military support obligations in exchange for technology transfers already received. High Thane Grimbar needs to quietly dissolve this before the Accord discovers it, because the Accord's mutual defense framework explicitly prohibits bilateral military arrangements between members.

## LUMENCREST — THE COUNCIL OF LUMENS

*See [[04_Factions/Council of Lumens|Council of Lumens]] for full detail.*

The dominant power in the Heartlands — and the faction whose internal paralysis is doing the most damage to regional stability. The Council's three factions (Conservators, Illuminists, Expansionists) have turned every decision into a negotiation that serves none of the three positions.

**Current chair:** Councilor Mira Ashton (Illuminist) — effective at preventing complete fracture, unable to build the consensus needed to act.

**The Accord relationship:** Lumencrest chairs the Accord Council by tradition and dominates it by population weight. This means the Council's internal paralysis is also the Accord's paralysis. Other cities have noticed. Some are quietly exploring what their options would look like without Lumencrest.

## KINGSREST — THE THORNHART MONARCHY

*Population: ~30,000. White limestone city, agricultural heartland, hereditary monarchy.*

Kingsrest is the Accord's conscience — or at least, it plays that role. Where Lumencrest innovates and destabilizes, Kingsrest preserves and endures. This has been both the city's strength and its limitation: it has resisted every disruption, which means it has also resisted every improvement Lumencrest has tried to export.

**Government:** Hereditary monarchy under the Thornhart line. King Aldric Thornhart IV has ruled for 82 years (extended lifespan — the family carries a minor trait that adds decades to human longevity). He is not incompetent; he is cautious in ways that were virtuous for 70 years and are now potentially dangerous.

**Political character:** Kingsrest's nobility is old, landed, and conservative. The city's merchant class is smaller than Lumencrest's and more traditional. Political decisions move through consensus of noble families in a way that feels stable until it needs to move fast.

**Current tensions:**
- **The food reserve question:** Cedric's investigation is finding gaps. Someone in the Accord supply chain is misrepresenting something. Aldric knows Cedric is looking; he's letting him look, which means he suspects the answer matters.
- **Lumencrest pressure:** As the Heart dims, Lumencrest's Expansionist faction has been quietly pressuring Kingsrest to commit more agricultural output to Accord reserves — which is another way of saying Lumencrest's reserves. Kingsrest's nobility finds this presumptuous. The polite word for their response has been "non-committal."
- **Reform currents:** Lady Rosalind Vey leads a quiet republican reform movement that believes the monarchy should voluntarily cede authority to an elected council. She has three Expansionist plants in her network that she is aware of and using to pass disinformation. Crown Prince Cedric is aware of her work and has not decided what he thinks about it.

**Key figures:** King Aldric Thornhart IV, Crown Prince Cedric Thornhart, Lady Rosalind Vey

## IRONHOLD — THE CLAN COUNCIL

*Population: ~35,000 dwarves plus several thousand others. Mountain city, millennia of continuous habitation.*

Ironhold is certain of itself in a way no other Heartlands city is. It has existed for 2,800 years. It has outlasted every political arrangement the surface world has ever built. The Heartland Accord is, from Ironhold's perspective, a recent development — and recent developments are given proportionate weight.

**Government:** High Thane elected by the Clan Council, which represents Ironhold's major clans. Decisions run through clan deliberation — slow, thorough, nearly impossible to manipulate from outside. The downside is that it's also nearly impossible to move quickly.

**Political character:** Status is vertical and literal — Ironhold is built in terraces, and elevation corresponds to clan seniority. Arriving in Ironhold, you are assessed before you are addressed. Competence is respected across clan lines; lineage is respected within them.

**Current tensions:**
- **The deep mines:** Something has changed in the lower levels over the past eight months. The materials coming up are strange. High Thane Grimbar knows and hasn't told the Clan Council. Forge-Mistress Kara Coalforge has unauthorized samples and has been running experiments. Clan Elder Throk Stonefist wants evidence before anyone else gets it. Three powerful Ironhold figures, all pursuing the same mystery independently.
- **The Lumencrest treaty:** Seventy years of secret mutual obligation, never ratified publicly. Grimbar needs this dissolved before it becomes a scandal or a liability. Throk has known about it for forty years and hasn't used it — yet.
- **Accord participation:** Ironhold's Clan Council has been debating whether the Accord still serves Ironhold's interests. The debate has not produced a conclusion. That itself is notable — Ironhold doesn't usually leave things unresolved.

**Key figures:** High Thane Grimbar Ironbeard, Forge-Mistress Kara Coalforge, Clan Elder Throk Stonefist

## STEELWATCH — THE MILITARY COUNCIL

*Population: ~28,000, majority military families. Fortress city, continental garrison function.*

Steelwatch was built with a purpose: to garrison the Heartlands' northwestern approach against incursion, and to serve as the Accord's military arm if the mutual defense clause was ever invoked. The incursion never came. The clause has never been invoked. Steelwatch has spent most of its existence being very good at a job that hasn't been necessary.

**Government:** Marshal-General elected by the Military Council (senior officers from Steelwatch's combined forces). Three-term limit; Marshal-General Thalia Corvain is in her third term. Civilian population has a formal advisory role that is taken seriously — unusual for a military governance structure and a deliberate part of Steelwatch's founding charter.

**Political character:** Restless, purposeful, increasingly frustrated by the Accord's inability to act decisively. Steelwatch's identity is built around military service as civic virtue. A city whose function is to respond to crisis watching the Accord fail to respond to an obvious crisis is a city with existential tension.

**Current tensions:**
- **The Frostwell question:** Marshal-General Corvain has been running a back-channel with Rider Commander Sera Windmane (Frostwell) about evacuation support — logistical planning for refugee movement that the Accord hasn't authorized because the Accord can't agree on anything. This is operating well outside her mandate.
- **Commander Brant Ironside** has modified training schedules without a Council vote and has been approached by an Expansionist-affiliated contact about an unauthorized mission that would take Steelwatch forces outside the Accord's territorial jurisdiction. He hasn't agreed. He hasn't refused. He's been approached three times.
- **Sergeant Mara Flint** has three years of intelligence showing the actual regional situation is consistently worse than the official picture — by about three months in every category. She also has an unauthorized Luminarium report about the Heart's real versus reported output that she obtained through channels she'd rather not explain.
- **Accord pressure:** Lumencrest's Expansionist faction has been quietly lobbying for a Steelwatch military commitment that would, effectively, put Steelwatch's forces in service of Lumencrest's Endless Eye agenda. Corvain has declined. The pressure is increasing.

**Key figures:** Marshal-General Thalia Corvain, Commander Brant Ironside, Sergeant Mara Flint

## THE ACCORD'S FAULT LINES

The Heartland Accord is held together by mutual interest and institutional inertia. Both are weakening. The specific points of fracture:

**The food reserve falsification:** Someone — either within the Accord's administrative structure or within a member city's reporting — has been misrepresenting food reserve data. When Cedric's investigation surfaces this publicly, every city will wonder whether the discrepancy benefited them or cost them, and trust will collapse accordingly.

**Lumencrest's deteriorating leverage:** The Accord's power imbalance has always depended on Lumencrest's technological and economic dominance. As the Heart dims, that dominance erodes. The other cities have been recalculating quietly. If Lumencrest needs the Accord more than the Accord needs Lumencrest, the dynamics shift entirely.

**Ironhold's treaty:** A secret bilateral military commitment between Lumencrest and Ironhold, discovered, would constitute a fundamental violation of the Accord's framework. Both parties have reasons to keep it quiet. Discovery is not certain. It is possible.

**Steelwatch's unauthorized preparations:** Corvain's back-channel with Frostwell and Ironside's pending decision on the Expansionist approach are both operating outside Accord authorization. If either becomes known before the crisis resolves, Steelwatch's political position becomes untenable.

**What holds it together:** The cities genuinely need each other. Kingsrest's food, Ironhold's materials, Steelwatch's security, Lumencrest's technology — these are real interdependencies. The Accord doesn't need to be trusted to be useful. It just needs to be useful enough that breaking it costs more than maintaining it.

That calculation has not yet tipped. It is tipping.

## SOCIAL HIERARCHY

**Urban (all cities):**

The comfortable class — skilled craftspeople, merchants, guild members, professionals, minor nobility — built its identity on the Heartlands' prosperity. It is watching that identity erode as the manufacturing slowdown (Lumencrest), the food uncertainty (everywhere), and the Accord's dysfunction (visible to anyone paying attention) grind against what they were promised the world would be.

The working class has less identity investment in the status quo and more direct exposure to its failure. Factory workers in Lumencrest during brownouts. Tenant farmers whose yields are declining. Dockworkers in Kingsrest watching the trade volumes shift. The First Flame is growing fastest here — not because the message has changed but because the audience has.

**Rural:**

The Heartlands' agricultural identity is its oldest and increasingly its most fragile. Soil that has been enriched by Hollostar shard deposits for centuries is slowly losing that enrichment. Harlan Greaves' thirty years of soil data show structural decline — not cyclical variation, not bad years, but a one-way trend. The Farmers' Union leadership knows. The Accord's agricultural committee has been given the data. The data has been filed.

**The Rivermark Sovereignty** (east of the Ironspine Mountains) participates in the Accord through its two shared seats but maintains significant cultural and political independence. River-town culture — self-reliant, practically-minded, more comfortable with Faun and non-human communities than the western cities — creates its own political texture. The Rivermark is watching the Accord's dysfunction with a certain amount of satisfaction and a growing amount of concern.
