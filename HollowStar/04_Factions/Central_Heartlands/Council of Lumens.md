# Council of Lumens

Lumencrest's governing body and the most powerful political institution in the Central Heartlands.  
For regional dynamics and the Heartland Accord, see [[04_Factions/Central_Heartlands/Power and Politics|Power and Politics]].

---

**Type:** Republican council  
**Base:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Locations/Cities/Lumencrest|Lumencrest]]  
**Reach:** Central Heartlands, significant influence across the continent  
**Public Mandate:** Govern Lumencrest, steward the Heart, maintain regional stability  
**Current Reality:** A body paralyzed by internal faction conflict at exactly the moment the crisis demands decisive action

---

## Structure

The Council seats thirty-two members — a deliberate mix of old aristocratic families who've held seats for generations and technocratic meritocrats who earned their place through expertise or wealth. Major decisions require a majority vote. In practice, no major decision has passed cleanly in over a year. The three internal factions have turned every vote into a negotiation, and negotiations into delays.

The Council chair rotates annually. The current chair, **Councilor Mira Ashton**, is an Illuminist — which the Conservators consider a disaster and the Expansionists consider irrelevant. She is not beloved but she is effective at keeping the body from completely fracturing, which may be the most anyone can ask right now.

Meetings are held in the Accord Chamber in Lumencrest's civic quarter — a grand room designed for a city at the height of its confidence. It feels wrong now. People speak more quietly than the architecture expects.

## The Three Factions

### The Conservators

**Core belief:** The experiments are making it worse. Shut them down before the Heart fails entirely.

**Who they are:** Primarily the old aristocratic families — names that have held Council seats for four and five generations. They remember when Lumencrest's power was stable and self-sustaining, and they blame the Illuminists' aggressive research programs for destabilizing something that was working. They are not wrong that the instability accelerated after the research push began. They may be wrong about what that means.

**Their support base:** The rationing class. Anyone whose life has gotten materially worse since the dimming started — factory workers on reduced hours, merchants whose supply chains depend on shard-powered infrastructure, residents dealing with unreliable lighting and heating. The Conservators have become the voice of people who just want things to go back to normal, even if normal is no longer available.

**What they want:** A full moratorium on experimental shard extraction and energy research. Stabilization of what remains. Diplomatic outreach to the Endless Eye as a long-term solution — but negotiated, not seized.

**Their problem:** They don't have a solution, they have a preference. "Stop making it worse" is not a plan for a city whose power source is dying. The Illuminists ask this every time, and the Conservators don't have a good answer.

**Key figure:** **Lord Edran Voss-Harwick**, eldest of the old families, sixty-eight years old, has been on the Council for thirty years. Deeply conservative, deeply connected, and genuinely frightened — which he will not show.

---

### The Illuminists

**Core belief:** The answer is more knowledge, more research, more effort. We haven't found the solution yet because we haven't looked hard enough.

**Who they are:** The technocrats, the inventors, the ambitious scholars who built Lumencrest's reputation for innovation. They represent the city's self-image as a place that solves problems through ingenuity. They are currently running seventeen simultaneous research programs, four of which are classified even from other Council members, and two of which Councilor Ashton privately believes are genuinely dangerous.

**Their support base:** The manufacturing sector, the academic institutions, and everyone whose livelihood depends on Lumencrest remaining the technological center of the continent. Also, quietly, the Starfall Seekers — the Illuminists have been the Council faction most willing to fund and share information with outside researchers.

**What they want:** More resources for research, fewer restrictions on methodology, access to shard sites across the continent, and ideally a working agreement with the Endless Eye that gives them study access to a functioning Hollostar. They are also the faction pushing hardest for the Hazelhaven tech assistance program to continue — they designed most of it and consider it a proof of concept for what Lumencrest can do at scale.

**Their problem:** They've been the dominant faction for six years and the Heart is still dimming. At some point "we need more time and resources" becomes indistinguishable from "we don't know what we're doing."

**Key figure:** **Councilor Mira Ashton**, current chair. Sharp, results-driven, increasingly aware that her faction's credibility is tied to a problem that may not be solvable on her timeline. Also **High Curator Myren Holt**, not a Council member but the Illuminists' most important technical advisor — his prototype reactor is either the breakthrough that saves Lumencrest or the thing that ends it, and nobody is certain which.

---

### The Expansionists

**Core belief:** The Endless Eye has a working Hollostar. Diplomacy has failed. It's time to take it.

**Who they are:** The military families and the merchant princes — people whose power is expressed through force and control rather than research or tradition. They have been a minority faction for most of the Council's history. The crisis is changing that. Every month the Heart dims further, more moderate members drift their way.

**Their support base:** Growing and increasingly mainstream. A year ago the Expansionists were considered dangerous radicals. Now they're being invited to dinners. The city is frightened and frightened cities start listening to people who project certainty, even when the certainty is "we take what we need."

**What they want:** Military buildup along the eastern approach to the Starfall Basin. Covert intelligence operations inside the Endless Eye. A Council declaration that Lumencrest's survival constitutes a legitimate claim on the Endless Eye's Hollostar. Then, when the moment is right, action.

**Their problem:** They have no operational plan that doesn't risk a war with the Starfall Basin, which has its own formidable defenses and the Somnarchs, whose capabilities nobody fully understands. Also, several members of the faction are running their own covert programs without Council sanction, which Councilor Ashton knows about and is choosing not to surface yet.

**Key figure:** **Marshal-General Thalia Corvain** of Steelwatch is not technically an Expansionist — she's a military officer, not a Council member — but her quiet support lends them credibility they wouldn't otherwise have. Within the Council, **Merchant-Lord Davan Creel** is the faction's most effective operator: patient, well-funded, and willing to play a long game.

---

## The Current Paralysis

The three factions have produced a Council that cannot act decisively on anything. The Conservators block the Illuminists' most aggressive proposals. The Illuminists block the Expansionists' military buildup requests. The Expansionists vote with whoever is more likely to advance their position on any given day. Councilor Ashton manages this through exhaustion and political debt.

Meanwhile the Heart dims. The rationing continues. The Hazelhaven tech program degrades. The Accord meetings get harder. And the city watches its government argue while the lights flicker.

The Council's credibility is not yet broken — Lumencrest still functions, the research continues, the walls are still standing. But there is a growing sense among ordinary residents that the people in that chamber do not have a solution and are afraid to admit it.

## Campaign Moves

- **The Classified Programs:** One of the Illuminists' four classified research programs has produced something they don't understand and are afraid to report. Someone needs to find out what it is before it becomes everyone's problem.
- **The Drift:** A moderate Conservator is being slowly cultivated by the Expansionists. If they flip, the balance shifts. Someone is running this operation deliberately.
- **The Audit:** The Hazelhaven delegation has formally requested an audit of how Council funds allocated to their tech assistance program were actually spent. The Illuminists are stalling. There's a reason.
- **The Moratorium Vote:** The Conservators are three votes away from forcing a research moratorium through the Council. The Illuminists need those votes to hold. The price of keeping them is something the party might be asked to deliver — or prevent.
