# The Merchant Circle

**Type:** Trade consortium and governing council  
**Base:** The Endless Eye, Starfall Basin  
**Authority:** Governs all commerce, lake access, law enforcement, and civic infrastructure in the Endless Eye  
**Size:** 9 council seats; several hundred Circle Guards; administrative staff  
**Current Situation:** A governing body managing a crisis it has classified, arguing about how to respond to a problem it hasn't fully admitted publicly

---

## ORIGIN AND STRUCTURE

The Merchant Circle formed approximately 600 years ago — a century after the Endless Eye came into existence — when the Endless Eye's population became large enough to need formal governance and no single group was powerful enough to impose it unilaterally. The compromise solution: a council with proportional representation, consensus-based decision-making, and explicit protections against any single community dominating the others.

It has worked. The Endless Eye is one of the most genuinely diverse governing arrangements on the continent. What it is not built for is moving fast under pressure.

### Council Composition (9 seats)

**3 Merchant seats** — Elected by registered traders (wealth-weighted voting). In practice, this tilts toward the wealthiest trading families and the most established merchant houses.

**2 Ribbet seats** — Elected by the underwater community. Represent Lakehaven and the Deeps access questions. Historically the most cohesive voting bloc on the council — until the current crisis split them.

**2 Fairy seats** — Elected by the surface Fairy community. Represent the largest permanent non-Ribbet ancestral community.

**1 Wanderer seat** — Rotating appointment representing the desert tribes and passing trade communities. Holds significant swing-vote power because its holder typically has no factional allegiance to either the Ribbet or Fairy communities.

**1 Scholar seat** — Appointed by research institutions with active Endless Eye presence. Currently occupied by Researcher Pell Ashwick, who has been using the position to advocate aggressively for Deeps research access and generating friction with every other seat in the process.

### Decision-Making

Major decisions require 5 of 9 votes. In practice, the current three-faction split means almost nothing passes cleanly. The Pragmatists hold a nominal majority when Dust-Chief Orah votes with them — which she does inconsistently, on her own calculation of Katari interests rather than any factional loyalty.

**What the Circle can do:**
- Set tariffs and regulate trade across all Endless Eye commerce
- Control access to the lake and The Deeps (permits, authorization, enforcement)
- Operate the Circle Guard (law enforcement and harbor security)
- Manage the agricultural terraces and water infrastructure
- Arbitrate commercial disputes
- Speak for the Endless Eye in diplomatic contexts

**What it cannot do:**
- Override the Ribbet community's rights to their underwater home
- Sell or grant ownership of the lake or the Hollostar
- Expel ancestral resident communities
- Act in ways that systematically disadvantage a represented community without that community's agreement

## THE THREE FACTIONS

### The Traditionalists

**Core belief:** The Hollostar is a gift — a living thing, not a resource. The appropriate response to its distress is spiritual, not scientific. Aggressive study of the Deeps risks accelerating the decline.

**Members:** Elder Suu (Ribbet), Wingspeaker Aelindra (Fairy), Merchant Liria Dawnpetal (Merchant seat)

**Their case:** Two previous Hollostar falls have shown that the stars are not well understood by anyone. Every time scholars have attempted aggressive study of Hollostar energy, something has gone wrong. Caution and reverence have kept the Endless Eye stable for 600 years. Urgency-driven research is the path most likely to destroy what they're trying to save.

**Their weakness:** They do not have an answer to Voice Kessa's data. The lake level is measurably declining. "Commune with it more sincerely" is a position that gets harder to defend as the decline becomes visible to more people.

**Elder Suu's complication:** She has been communing with The Deeps for forty years, and in the past year she has begun hearing something in those meditations that wasn't there before. She hasn't told the council what she's hearing. She hasn't decided whether it changes her position. It may change everything.

### The Pragmatists

**Core belief:** The Hollostar is declining. It may be stoppable with the right information. The way to get the right information is careful, respectful research, conducted with proper authorization and documentation. The time to do this is now, before the decline becomes catastrophic.

**Members:** Senior Merchant Voss Tanakar (de facto leader), Voice Kessa (Ribbet), Researcher Pell Ashwick (Scholar seat)

**Their case:** Reverence for the Hollostar doesn't contradict understanding it. The lake level has dropped six inches over the measured period. At the current rate, the decline becomes visible to the general public in roughly twelve months. Better to have answers before panic sets in than to be caught scrambling when the crisis becomes undeniable.

**Their weakness:** They have been the dominant coalition for two years and the decline has continued. They have better data now than they did two years ago. They do not yet have answers. At some point "we need more time and better access" becomes indistinguishable from "we don't know what we're doing."

**The Lumencrest problem:** The Pragmatist position on expanded Deeps research requires more qualified researchers than the Endless Eye has. The most qualified available researchers are Lumencrest-affiliated. Accepting Lumencrest researchers means accepting Lumencrest's agenda, which several Pragmatists recognize as exactly what the Expansionist faction wants.

### The Profiteers

**Core belief:** The lake is probably going to fail. The Hollostar's decline is too advanced and too poorly understood to be reversed. The correct response is to extract maximum value from current conditions.

**Members:** Merchant Brek (Ribbet), Thornwing (Fairy)

**Their case (stated):** Being pragmatic about an irreversible situation is not cynicism — it's realism. If the lake fails, the Endless Eye needs resources and relationships to survive the transition. Building those now, through monetizing current Deeps access, is responsible long-term planning.

**Their case (actual):** Brek is selling unauthorized access to Hollostar water from The Deeps through a black market operation that he believes the council doesn't know about. Voss Tanakar suspects. Deepwatcher Selin has documented an unexplained increase in traffic near The Deeps that doesn't match authorized access records. Brek is in communication with Lumencrest's Expansionist faction, who have been providing him with funds in exchange for information about Deeps conditions.

**Thornwing's position** is more complicated than pure profiteering. He genuinely believes coordination with Lumencrest is the only path that gives the Endless Eye any leverage over its own situation. He is wrong about the Expansionists' intentions and right that the current approach is failing.

## CURRENT STATE

### The Classified Data

The Merchant Circle has formally classified the lake decline data. The stated reason: preventing panic. The actual reason: the three factions cannot agree on what to say, so they have agreed to say nothing publicly while the argument continues internally.

The Circle Guards have standing orders not to discuss lake levels with non-Circle members. The Ribbet community's elders know the data — they've been watching the decline directly for years and some of it predates the Circle's measurements. Several scholars (including Researcher Pell Ashwick) have independently estimated the decline from observable data and are not being especially quiet about their estimates.

The classification is holding. It will not hold indefinitely.

### The Lumencrest Pressure

Lumencrest's Conservator faction wants the data classified further — they've been applying pressure through diplomatic channels to prevent any public acknowledgment that two Hollowstars are declining simultaneously. The Illuminist faction wants everything the Circle knows, immediately. The Expansionist faction has an agent embedded in the Endless Eye (Merchant Brek as an unwitting conduit, and at least one other that Voss hasn't identified yet) and is already acting on information they shouldn't have.

**The Covert Operation:** Lumencrest's Expansionists have a long-term intelligence agent inside the Circle Guard structure. This agent has been instrumental in the Expansionists' assessment that the Endless Eye's Hollostar could be accessed and partially harvested without destroying the lake — a calculation that is both more sophisticated and more wrong than the Circle realizes.

### Deepwatcher Selin's Discovery

Ribbet elder Selin has been documenting The Deeps for forty years. In the past several months she has observed something that doesn't fit any category she has: movement in The Deeps near the Hollostar that isn't the Hollostar's normal activity and doesn't match any known aquatic creature. She has reported this to Elder Suu. She has not reported it to the wider council. Whatever is happening in The Deeps, it is not only declining.

## ADVENTURE HOOKS

**The Classified Leak:** Someone inside the Circle has been leaking decline data to parties outside the Endless Eye — not Brek's Lumencrest channel, a different one. Voss hires the party to find the source. The investigation reveals the Circle's internal situation is more fractured than it appears, and raises the question of whether some information shouldn't stay classified.

**The Access Dispute:** The party needs access to The Deeps for reasons of their own — or has been hired by a researcher who does. Getting legitimate Circle authorization requires navigating three factions with incompatible positions. Getting unauthorized access requires getting past the Circle Guard and the Ribbet community's own protective measures. Both paths have costs.

**Brek's Operation:** The party stumbles onto evidence of the unauthorized Hollostar water trade — either as buyers, investigators, or because someone who was selling to Brek wants out. Exposing it brings down a Circle member and potentially triggers a political crisis. Not exposing it means the Expansionists keep their intelligence channel. The options in between are creative and morally complicated.

**The Wanderer Vote:** Dust-Chief Orah is being approached by multiple factions to secure her vote on a specific Deeps access proposal — the first one that actually has a chance of passing if she supports it. She hasn't decided. She'll speak to the party if they're introduced through the right channel and she thinks they're being honest about their interest. She is an excellent judge of whether people are being honest.

**Selin's Observation:** The party is hired (by Selin, by Elder Suu, or they stumble onto it) to investigate the movement she's been documenting. Getting to The Deeps without authorization requires Ribbet guides, cold-water adaptation, and survival skills. What they find changes the nature of the question from "can we save the lake" to something more complicated.

## GM NOTES

**The Circle as obstacle vs. ally:** The Circle functions differently depending on which members the party develops relationships with. Voss is a reliable employer who keeps his word and pays well — and who occasionally asks for things that are in his interest before the party's. Voice Kessa is an honest ally who will give the party real information and real asks. Elder Suu requires patience and respect; she will not be rushed, and trying to rush her closes doors.

**The Deeps mystery** (Selin's observation) is a long-form hook that should develop slowly. Whatever is in The Deeps is not hostile, but it is significant, and its significance connects to the broader question of what Hollowstars actually are. The GM should decide what it is before players get close enough to find out.

**The three-faction deadlock** is not permanent — it resolves (one way or another) when either the public learns about the decline or one faction's position becomes untenable. Players can accelerate either outcome, slow it, or shape which faction's position survives the resolution.
