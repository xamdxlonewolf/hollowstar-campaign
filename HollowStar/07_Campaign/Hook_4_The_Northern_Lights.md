# Campaign Guide: The Northern Lights

**Starting Hook:** Hook 4 — The Northern Lights  
**Region:** Frostwell Tundra, with reach into the Northern Expanse  
**Tone:** Frontier survival, community bonds, the world's largest thing happening at close range  
**Recommended Party Size:** 3–5  
**Starting Level:** 1–2  
**Campaign Arc:** 2–3 tiers of play; local survival stakes grow into continental consequence

---

## Overview

This campaign begins on the ground, in the cold, in a community deciding whether to leave or die. The party is present — locals, stranded travelers, pilgrims, researchers, or people who ended up in the wrong place at the right time — when Ashenvale Hearth holds its final vote on evacuation.

Everything in Hook 4 starts small and gets large. The party helps a family. Then a village. Then they learn what's underneath the ruins that everyone has been walking past for ten thousand years. By the time the fourth fall arrives, the party is positioned at the center of something that will change the world — and they've earned that position by caring about the people in it.

**What makes this hook work:** The Frostwell is intimate. Eighteen thousand people spread across nine hearths — everyone knows everyone. The party's actions have visible consequences. They help Elder Mira's hearth and she remembers. They fail to warn Ironspire and people die. There is no going home, resetting, and trying again. The cold and the approaching star are real. That reality makes everything matter.

---

## Session 0 Checklist

Work through these before the first session.

### Questions for Players

**Where is your character from?**
The answer shapes everything. Characters from the Nine Hearths have deep roots and a personal stake in the stay/flee debate. Characters who arrived recently (traders, scholars, pilgrims, soldiers) bring outside knowledge and outside blindspots. Mixed origins work well — one local, two travelers, creates natural tension about what the tundra means.

**Why are you still here?**
Everyone in Frostwell at campaign start has either chosen to stay or been stranded by the early snow. Both are valid. Both have story. A character who chose to stay has something they won't leave behind. A character who was stranded is in an unfamiliar crisis in unfamiliar terrain. Ask each player to answer this before Session 1.

**What do you know about the star?**
Characters from the Nine Hearths have been watching it grow brighter for years. A Lumencrest researcher knows the official estimates. A Path of Spirits pilgrim knows what the shamans say about it. A Katari trader has heard rumors at every waystation. Nobody knows the full picture. Everyone has a piece.

**Do you have anyone in the region you care about?**
The most important question. A grandmother in Coldfast who voted to stay. A research colleague whose judgment you trust. A childhood friend who left last spring and you haven't heard from. These connections are the engine of the campaign. If a player can't answer this at Session 0, help them create one.

**What's your character's relationship to leaving?**
This is the campaign's central question in miniature. Do they want to go south? Do they feel responsible for the people who are staying? Are they the kind of person who runs or the kind who digs in? Answers don't need to be fixed — they should be able to change as the campaign develops.

### Table Decisions

**Tone calibration:** Frostwell is a survival setting with genuine death stakes. Discuss how the table wants to handle NPC deaths, failed evacuations, and the possibility that some people the party tries to save will not survive. The campaign can be softened (consequences softened, near-misses rather than deaths) or run hard (consequences real). Decide together.

**Starting point:** All five party members in Ashenvale Hearth at campaign start is the cleanest opening. Alternatively, they arrive in Ashenvale separately over the first session — different entry points, different first impressions, assemble around the evening meeting.

**The stay/leave question:** Does any party member personally want to stay in Frostwell? This changes the emotional core of the campaign. A party of all Leavers is running a refugee story. A party with Stayers is running a survival story. A split party is running both simultaneously and that tension is rich.

## The World at Campaign Start

**700 AR, Late Goldmonth.** Autumn in the Frostwell. The sky is clear most nights and the approaching star is unmistakable — a bright, moving point that has grown noticeably brighter over the past month. The first early snows came two weeks ago. The southern pass is still open. It will close within six weeks.

**The Nine Hearths:**  
Four have voted to leave: Longmarch, Iceholm, Winterhold, Snowmere. Most of their populations are already gone or going. Five voted to stay: Coldfast, Frostvale, Whitepeak, Frostridge, The Last Hearth. They are not moving.

Ashenvale is not one of the Nine Hearths — it is a smaller independent settlement, roughly 400 people, human and Giant mixed, that has been watching the larger villages debate and has not yet reached its own decision. This is where the campaign begins.

**The outsiders:**
Three outside groups are in the tundra. Lumencrest's research team (Lead Researcher Odalys Venn, 8 staff) is based at Frostvault, documenting and gathering data. The Starfall Seekers expedition (Expedition Leader Tam Coldridge, 11 researchers) is operating from a field camp near the Ember Cradle and Frostvault. A band of Path of Spirits pilgrims (Pilgrim-Speaker Maret, ~30 followers) arrived two months ago and has been creating friction near the Ember Cradle.

**The clocks running:**
- The fourth fall is 14 months away by Watcher Aren's calculation (the party doesn't know this yet)
- The evacuation window closes in 6 weeks when Silver Pass becomes impassable
- The Ember Cradle is warming faster than Flame-Keeper Sorith has admitted
- Ironspire Hearth has gone silent — three weeks without contact

## Act One: The Cold and the Vote

*Levels 1–3. The party arrives, becomes part of the community, and faces the first major decision.*

### Session 1: The Meeting

**The opening:** The party is in Ashenvale when Hearth-Keeper Mira (human, 58, the settlement's de facto leader) calls an evening meeting in the main hall. The approaching star is visible through the smoke-hole. Two families have already left. Two more are packed. The rest of the community is watching her.

She opens the meeting by asking the party — because they've just arrived from the south — to describe what they saw. Not their opinion. What they saw. She wants the community to have real information before deciding.

**What the party can honestly report from the south:**
- Northern Expanse towns are taking refugees but the welcome is thinning
- Silver Pass is passable but crowded
- Food prices are rising at every waystation south of the mountains
- People who left Longmarch three weeks ago looked exhausted but intact

**What the meeting decides:** Leave it open for the first session. Mira will call a formal vote at the next meeting (in three days). The party has three days to learn what they can and say what they want to say before that vote.

**The people the party meets:**
- **Elder Kael Stormwatcher** (Giant, 71, Stayer) — has lived in Ashenvale his entire life. His wife is buried here. His children left last spring. He will not leave. He doesn't want to argue about it.
- **Danna Brightseam** (human, 34, Leaver) — has three children, the youngest four years old. She has already made her decision. She is waiting for the community's decision to see if she leaves alone or with company.
- **Pell** (halfling, 22, undecided) — Ashenvale-born, never been south of the mountains. The star looks beautiful to them. They don't know what to do with that.

**End of session hook:** That night, a messenger bird arrives from Icewatch. The message is for Hearthspeaker Mava (confederation leader, based at Frostmeet, two days' travel), but the bird got the wrong coop. Mira shows it to the party. It reads: *"Calculations confirmed. Fourteen months. Mava — tell them."*

Nobody in Ashenvale knows what this means. Mira asks the party to carry it to Frostmeet.

### Sessions 2–4: Frostmeet and the Confederation

**The journey to Frostmeet** (one day on foot in good conditions, harder with early snow) introduces the tundra's dangers and its beauty. The party should experience: the scale of the open tundra, the aurora at night, the difference between safe and unsafe ice, the small cairns that mark the route.

**Frostmeet** is not a permanent settlement but a neutral meeting-ground used by the Nine Hearths confederation — a large heated lodge with surrounding structures, maintained year-round by a rotating steward. Currently it is perpetually occupied because confederation business never stops.

**Hearthspeaker Mava** (human, 47, Frostmeet's current resident and the confederation's de facto chair) has been waiting for this message. She is not surprised by the fourteen months, but she is frightened by it. It confirms what she privately suspected and makes the political situation dramatically worse: the public estimate is two to five years. If fourteen months becomes known, some Stayer villages that might eventually be persuaded will have no time to be persuaded.

**The Sunken Library tablet** (see Frostmeet details): Mava has been sitting on a piece of information she obtained through a Ribbet diver who managed to reach the bottom of the flooded chamber beneath Frostmeet. A single recovered tablet says, in partial translation: *"When the second fire returns, the gate must be open. Those who seal it seal themselves. Those who open it —"* The rest is damaged. She doesn't know what it means. She doesn't know if sharing it helps or destroys the evacuation vote.

**The Stayer problem:** The party will encounter Elder Durn of Coldfast (human, 68, immovable) at Frostmeet for a confederation meeting. He is not a villain. He is a man whose entire identity is built around surviving the tundra by staying put and trusting accumulated knowledge. He has never run. He doesn't know how. The party cannot argue him out of his position with words. They can potentially change his position with evidence — specifically, if they can prove Coldfast specifically (not just the tundra generally) is in the impact zone. That proof requires Icewatch's data, which the party now has a thread toward.

**Act One decision point:** The party learns what Mava knows, what Aren knows, and what Durn won't hear. They have to decide: do they try to change the political situation before the evacuation window closes, or do they focus on something else? Both are valid. The consequences are different.

**Act One threads opening:**
- Ironspire Hearth has gone silent. Mira's message (via Mava) is that she'd like someone to check on them
- Tam Coldridge's expedition is two days from Frostmeet. Mava mentions them — says Coldridge has been trying to meet with her about something for two weeks. She hasn't had time.
- A Drakona pilgrim named **Reth** has been waiting at Frostmeet for six weeks. Sorith sent them. They have something to say that they haven't been able to say to the right person. They approach the party.

## Act Two: The Ruins and the Reckoning

*Levels 3–5. The party learns what is underneath the tundra, what the ruins mean, and what the fourteen-month deadline actually implies.*

### The Ironspire Investigation

Ironspire Hearth is the most isolated of the Nine — a small community (220 people at last count) built near a cluster of pre-Hollostar ruins. Their last message: *"The old place is awake. We are staying to watch it."*

**What the party finds:** Ironspire is intact. Its people are fine, physically. But something happened three weeks ago at the ruin cluster nearby — a structure that has been dead and frozen for ten thousand years opened a section of itself. Not violently. The ice around a particular section of wall simply melted, and the stone behind it is warm. The Ironspire residents are taking shifts watching it. They haven't gone inside. Their elder, **Frost-Keeper Bren** (woman, 60s, Path of Spirits practitioner), believes it is a message from the spirits. She believes staying to witness whatever comes next is her responsibility.

**What's inside (if the party investigates):** A short corridor leads to a chamber. The chamber is empty except for symbols carved into every surface — the same symbols seen at the Sleeping Gate, at Frostvault, at the Ember Cradle. In the center of the floor is a depression, circular, the size and shape of something that should be sitting in it but isn't.

**The significance (GM layer):** The ruins across Frostwell are not separate structures. They are components of a single system. The builders designed them to do something when a Hollostar approached. The individual sites are pieces; the Sleeping Gate is the mechanism; and whatever was supposed to sit in that depression at Ironspire is missing — has been missing for ten thousand years, which is why the system has never activated. What was it? Where is it? These are questions for later tiers. For now, the party knows the ruins are connected and waking.

**Act Two consequence:** The party can share this with Coldridge (who will immediately understand the architectural implication and become very excited and very quiet), with Mava (who will add it to the weight of things she has to decide about), or with nobody, sitting with the knowledge themselves. Each choice shapes what resources they have for the climax.

### Tam Coldridge and the Discrepancy

**Meeting Coldridge:** The Seekers' base camp is a collection of insulated tents and one permanent structure near the Ember Cradle approach. Coldridge is genuinely glad to meet the party — they've been told about them through Mava and through Reth's account of what the party has been doing.

Coldridge lays out the discrepancy directly: their data says fourteen months (matching Aren's calculation, which they've now been shown). The Somnarchs' published estimate says two to five years. The Lumencrest official estimate also says two to five years. Two independent calculations from the field agree with each other and disagree with the institutions. This is either two field researchers making identical errors, or the institutional estimates are wrong.

**The Lumencrest problem:** Odalys Venn's team has different data. Coldridge has tried twice to compare notes; Venn has been politely unavailable. Coldridge suspects Venn knows her data matches theirs and is under instructions to not confirm it until Lumencrest decides how to release the information — or whether to release it at all.

**What Coldridge wants:** Someone who can get Venn's data honestly. Not stolen — but Venn might talk off the record, under the right conditions, to people who aren't in the Seeker/Lumencrest political dynamic.

**The deeper ask:** Coldridge has decided they will be at the impact site when the star hits. They are not asking the party to do the same — they are clear that this is a choice with mortal consequences. But if the party has any interest in witnessing what happens at the Ember Cradle when the fourth fall arrives, Coldridge's expedition is the safest and most prepared group to do it with.

### The Ember Cradle

**Getting access:** Ashscale Denn (Drakona, 38, guardian captain) is the door. The party has to demonstrate respect for what the Cradle is — not just claim it — before Denn lets them past the surface approach. This is a conversation, not a check. What the party says matters.

**What they experience:** The Ember Cradle is warm. Not geothermally warm — alive-feeling warm, the way a room feels when someone has just left it rather than the way a furnace heats a room. The standing pillars at the surface are enormous. The symbols carved into them are the same symbols from Ironspire and the Sleeping Gate. Between the pillars, the ground radiates heat that the party can feel through their boots.

**Flame-Keeper Sorith** is the most important NPC in this act. She is frightened and she is honest about it. She tells the party three things she hasn't told the Seekers:

1. The warming rate doubled six months ago. The sealed lower chamber is now warmer than any natural geothermal source should produce.
2. Three nights ago, she heard sound from behind the sealed door. Not mechanism-sound. Not stone-settling. Something that moved, and stopped, and moved again.
3. Deep-Keeper Varra Ironmantle of the Greywall Dwarves sent a message eight months ago, asking Sorith to meet with her before the star arrived. Sorith sent a reply saying she would. She hasn't gone yet. Varra is 203 years old and reportedly dying. If Sorith's information and Varra's information belong together, waiting is running out of time.

**The sealed door:** The party can examine it. They cannot open it — not because it's mechanically locked, but because it doesn't function on physical mechanisms. The symbols on the door glow in a pattern that Frost-Keeper Bren (from Ironspire) would recognize if she were here. The pattern matches something in the oldest Path of Spirits oral tradition — a phrase that translates roughly as: *"Open when the fire returns, not before."*

**Act Two decision point:** The party now has pieces from multiple directions: the ruins are connected, something is behind the sealed door, Varra knows what the original builders put there, Coldridge has the timeline, and the evacuation window is closing. What do they do first, and what do they sacrifice to do it?

## Act Three: Before It Hits

*Levels 5–7. The pieces come together. The window closes. The party chooses what they're here for.*

### Getting Varra's Knowledge

The Greywall Dwarves hold in the southern mountains — three days from the Ember Cradle in good conditions, potentially impassable in a major storm. Clan-Chief Berrik Stoneshield (dwarf, 156, practical and opportunistic) will negotiate passage for a price that is high but not unreasonable given the crisis.

**Deep-Keeper Varra Ironmantle** (dwarf, 203, dying slowly) has been waiting. She has been waiting for the right people since she first read the original documents forty years ago and understood what they meant. She says, when she sees the party: *"I wasn't sure it would be you. But it had to be someone."*

**What she knows:** The Greywall clan's Deep Reckoning records include a document predating the first Hollostar fall — a construction log for what the original builders called the Resonance Network. The Ember Cradle is the anchor point. Frostvault is a processing node. The Sleeping Gate is the aperture. And there is a fourth component: a catalyst object called the Warmstone, which was placed in a specific chamber at the Ironspire ruin site. When a Hollostar approaches close enough, the Network is designed to activate — not to stop the fall, but to shape where the energy goes on impact. To do something with it rather than simply absorb it.

**The problem:** The Warmstone has been missing from Ironspire for ten thousand years. Without it, the Network activates partially and incompletely. The original builders' notes on what incomplete activation produces are... unclear. Varra believes incomplete activation caused the catastrophic failure of whatever civilization built the Network in the first place.

**Varra's clan problem:** Her clan doesn't know she's sharing this. When the party leaves with the information, Clan-Chief Berrik will learn what was shared. He will send people to retrieve the party — not violently, but urgently. He wants the information back. Or he wants the Greywall clan's role in whatever happens to be recognized. This is negotiable. Everything with Berrik is negotiable.

**The Warmstone question:** Where is it? This is the act's central investigation. Possible locations (choose one or let the investigation point to the answer):
- At the bottom of the Sunken Library beneath Frostmeet, which is why Mava's tablet mentioned it and why the Library is flooded
- In the possession of the Greywall clan — Berrik didn't know what it was until Varra told him (and in telling the party, Varra has inadvertently made clear that Berrik might have it)
- Somewhere in Frostvault's deepest unexplored level — the depression in the center of the unexplored chamber is the right size and shape
- Taken south by evacuees who found it near Ironspire and kept it as a curiosity, now several weeks down the Silver Pass route

### The Final Evacuation Window

Six weeks from campaign start, the Silver Pass closes. After that, anyone in Frostwell who wants to leave is walking a death route or waiting until spring. Spring is after the impact.

**The political situation at this point:**
- Ashenvale has voted (whatever the party's involvement produced)
- Coldfast is dug in (unless Durn has been moved by Coldridge's specific impact-zone data)
- The Last Hearth (Elder Mira Lastlight, Giant, 92) has decided with quiet finality to stay and witness whatever the star brings

**What the party can still do:**
- If they have Aren's confirmed 14-month data, presenting it with Coldridge's corroboration to Durn gives him the specific proof about Coldfast's location that might move him
- If they found the Sunken Library tablet and the rest of the tablets (they need multiple dives, equipment, and Ribbet assistance), the full translation adds context to the "gate must be open" message
- Village-Chief Petra Thornholt (Giant, 34) needs escort capacity for the last organized evacuation column — the party can make this run faster and safer than it would otherwise be

**Who stays no matter what:** Elder Durn, unless specifically persuaded. Elder Mira Lastlight. Flame-Keeper Sorith and Ashscale Denn (and the Drakona pilgrims). Tam Coldridge and the Seekers. Pilgrim-Speaker Maret and some of her followers. Watcher Aren at Icewatch, watching until the last moment.

**The party's choice:** Go or stay. There is no right answer. Going means they survive, witness the impact from safety, and work with what they know afterward. Staying means they are at the Ember Cradle when it happens — which is where the Warmstone needs to be placed, if they have it, and where the anonymous invitation pointed.

### The Night of the Fall

**If the party stays:** This is the campaign's climax. They are at the Ember Cradle with Coldridge, Sorith, Maret's pilgrims, and whoever else didn't leave, on a clear winter night with the star filling a third of the sky.

**With the Warmstone, correctly placed:** The Resonance Network activates. Frostvault hums. The Sleeping Gate opens (visibly, from a distance). The Ember Cradle's sealed door opens from the inside. What comes through is not a threat — but it is something the campaign has been building toward, and what it is should be determined by everything the party learned about the original builders, their purpose, and what the Network was designed to do. The impact still happens. The star still falls. But the energy goes somewhere specific rather than simply detonating.

**Without the Warmstone, or with it placed incorrectly:** The Network partially activates. The Ember Cradle sealed door opens — but incompletely, and whatever is on the other side is confused by the incomplete signal. The impact is harder. The aftermath is stranger. The party survives, barely, and the world is different in ways that will take time to understand.

**The anonymous invitation:** Whoever sent it knew the Network existed and knew someone needed to be there to place the Warmstone. They should have a face by the climax — either someone in the campaign the party trusted, or a revelation about the original builders' continuation in some form. Decide which before the campaign reaches this point.

## Key NPCs Quick Reference

### Core Community

**Hearth-Keeper Mira** (Ashenvale, human, 58)  
The leader who asks good questions and makes decisions with the information she has. Not a hero — a tired, capable person trying to protect her community. She will remember every choice the party makes in Ashenvale, for good or ill.  
*Use her for:* grounding the party in what matters locally, delivering community consequences

**Elder Kael Stormwatcher** (Ashenvale, Giant, 71)  
The Stayer with roots so deep he can't imagine them elsewhere. Not wrong about the value of what he's defending. Possibly wrong about whether it's worth dying for.  
*Use him for:* the emotional core of the stay/leave question; he should be hard to leave behind

**Danna Brightseam** (Ashenvale, human, 34)  
Three children, has already decided. Watching the community, waiting for the vote, preparing to leave with or without them. Her children's faces are the most human argument for leaving.  
*Use her for:* urgency; the practical reality of what leaving actually requires

### Confederation

**Hearthspeaker Mava** (Frostmeet, human, 47)  
Brilliant administrator in a crisis that administration cannot solve. Sitting on two pieces of information she hasn't decided to share. Desperate for the party to be the people she can trust with both.  
*Use her for:* political information, the Sunken Library thread, the feeling that someone in charge is doing their best and it may not be enough

**Elder Durn Frostborn** (Coldfast, human/Giant blood, 68)  
Immovable by argument. Potentially movable by specific evidence about Coldfast's position in the impact zone. His grandchildren left two weeks ago. He hasn't spoken of it. He knows.  
*Use him for:* the cost of certainty; the question of whether his position is wrong or just wrong for this situation

**Watcher Aren** (Icewatch, human, 29)  
Has confirmed the 14-month figure eleven times. Will confirm it again. Terrified by what it means. Intellectually compelled to stay and watch. His data is the thing that makes everything else urgent.  
*Use him for:* establishing the stakes; the discrepancy thread; the quiet horror of knowing something nobody else knows

### The Ruins

**Flame-Keeper Sorith** (Ember Cradle, Drakona, 61)  
Has more information than she's shared with anyone. Is frightened enough to start sharing it now. Her faith and her fear are both genuine.  
*Use her for:* the Ember Cradle's secrets; the Varra connection; the sealed door

**Deep-Keeper Varra Ironmantle** (Greywall Hold, Dwarf, 203)  
Dying. Has been waiting for someone to give the Resonance Network information to. When she gives it, her clan will try to take it back.  
*Use her for:* the central revelation; the Warmstone thread; the weight of very old knowledge meeting its moment

**Expedition Leader Tam Coldridge** (Starfall Seekers, human, 52)  
The most prepared person in the tundra. Has already decided to stay for the impact. Will share everything they know with the party, freely.  
*Use them for:* information synthesis, the discrepancy thread, a model of what calm looks like in the face of certain danger

**Reth** (Drakona scholar, sent by Sorith, 38)  
Has been waiting at Frostmeet for six weeks to find the right people. The party is the right people. Their message: Sorith needs help she can't ask for officially.  
*Use them for:* the introduction to the Ember Cradle; bridge between the Drakona community and the party

### Outsiders

**Lead Researcher Odalys Venn** (Lumencrest, human, 44)  
Genuinely scientifically motivated. Also reporting to Expansionists. Uncomfortable with this but not uncomfortable enough to stop. Has data matching Aren's and Coldridge's that she is under instruction not to confirm publicly.  
*Use her for:* the data corroboration thread; moral complexity about institutional loyalty; she can be a genuine ally if the party gives her a reason to choose them over her instructions

**Pilgrim-Speaker Maret** (Path of Spirits, human with Giant heritage, 55)  
Sincere. Charismatic. Absolutely certain this is the most important spiritual event in living memory. Creating friction near the Ember Cradle. Has a piece of the puzzle from the oldest oral Path of Spirits traditions that no researcher has thought to ask about.  
*Use her for:* the spiritual interpretation of what the Network is for; the sealed door translation; friction with Drakona authority

## What the Party Can Change

### Lives Saved

| Action | Approximate Lives Affected |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Breaking Durn's certainty with Coldfast-specific impact data | ~1,500 (Coldfast) |
| Organizing the final evacuation column efficiently | ~400–600 (stragglers) |
| Getting accurate timing to Snowmere's fragmenting community | ~200 |
| Persuading Elder Mira Lastlight with the Resonance Network information | ~100 (The Last Hearth — but she may choose to stay anyway, knowingly) |

### What the Party Cannot Change

The star falls. The impact happens. Frostwell is not saved. What can be changed is how many people survive, whether the Resonance Network activates, and what the activation produces. These differences are enormous and real — but the party should understand from early in the campaign that they are not here to prevent the apocalypse. They are here to decide what kind of apocalypse it is.

### The Aftermath (Post-Campaign Seeds)

What happens after the fourth fall depends entirely on what the party did:

- **Resonance Network activated correctly:** The impact produces the largest Hollostar shard field in recorded history — and the shards behave differently than any previous fall. Something in the Network shaped the energy. What that means is the seed of the next campaign arc.
- **Coldfast evacuated:** Elder Durn's people arrive in the Northern Expanse as refugees. If the party made the right enemies getting them out, Durn's arrival creates political complications. If they made the right friends, Durn arrives as an asset.
- **Lumencrest learns the Resonance Network exists:** The Expansionists immediately understand what the new shard field means. Clock 6 (Green Covenant conflict) accelerates. Clock 2 (Heart failure) takes a new shape.
- **The sealed chamber at the Ember Cradle:** Whatever is behind it is now in the world. What it is should be the beginning of the next question, not the answer to this one.

## Running the Tundra

### The Cold as Mechanic

Don't make cold damage a trap. Make it a resource. Every decision about warmth shapes what the party can do and when. Traveling in a blizzard is possible — it costs something. Camping without shelter is possible — it costs more.

**Daggerheart integration:** Use Stress to track cold exposure rather than separate cold damage. Entering a warm hearth after an exposed crossing might clear Stress. A night in an emergency snow shelter might not. The party always has options; the options always have costs.

**The buddy system rule:** Frostwell culture. The party will hear it from locals. If they follow it, nobody gets lost. If they don't follow it, someone eventually does. Don't punish them for forgetting once. Do follow through on the second time.

### The Aurora

The aurora borealis is the tundra's most important visual element. Use it every clear night. Let it be beautiful before it's significant. When Icewatch's aurora patterns start spelling something, the party should already have the aurora as part of their mental map of this place — so the change reads as change, not as a new feature.

The aurora message (Hook 12 from Adventure Hooks) should appear in the background of Sessions 3–5, becoming a foreground thread when the party is ready for another layer.

### Silence and Intimacy

The tundra is quiet. Use that. The best Frostwell sessions will have long pauses — sitting with Mira by the hearth-fire, waiting on the ice for a storm to pass, looking at the star from a snow slope in the dark. Don't fill every moment with plot. The intimacy of survival creates the emotional investment that makes the climax hit.

### The Star

Every session, describe it. It gets brighter. In Session 1 it's a notable moving point. By Session 8 it's the brightest thing in the sky. By Session 12 it's visible in daylight. The party should feel its approach physically, not just as plot information.
