# Starting Hooks

Five fully developed ways to begin the campaign. Each is a complete opening scenario with an inciting scene, immediate complications, and threads that connect to the world's larger situation.

**How to use these:** These aren't rigid scripts. Read the one that fits your group, lift what works, discard what doesn't. Most of the best campaigns start with one hook and drift into others as player choices pull them somewhere unexpected. That's the point.

**Mix and match:** The threads at the end of each hook point to the same larger world. A campaign that starts in Lumencrest will eventually bump into the same things as one that starts in Frostwell — just from a different angle with different relationships already in place.

## Hook 1: The Dimming City

**Location:** Lumencrest, Central Heartlands
**Tone:** Political intrigue, urban investigation, creeping institutional dread
**Good for:** Players who enjoy faction politics, moral ambiguity, city-based play
**Opening Season:** Late Goldmonth — harvest festival delayed because the Manufactories district can't afford the power draw

---

### The Situation

Lumencrest is the most powerful city on the continent. It is also quietly falling apart, and the people in charge cannot agree on what to do about it. The Heart — the Hollostar buried beneath the city that powers everything — is failing. The Council knows. Most of the public doesn't. The gap between what's officially true and what's actually happening is widening every week.

The party arrives in Lumencrest at a specific moment: a brownout in the Manufactories district has lasted four days instead of the scheduled eight hours. Someone in the Council's technical division is not telling the whole story. The Vault District has been running unauthorized experiments that three different Council factions claim to know nothing about. And a mid-level bureaucrat named **Toras Vel** (human, 38, perpetually nervous) has sent a message through a mutual contact asking to meet someone discreet — not faction-affiliated, not from the city — and willing to carry information somewhere without asking too much about it.

### The Opening Scene

The party is in the Lamplight Ward when the power drops. Not a scheduled brownout — those are announced. This one just happens: street lights out, the hum of the Lumen Network gone, the peculiar silence of a neighborhood that runs on electricity suddenly not running. It lasts eleven minutes. When it comes back, there's no announcement, no explanation.

A Manufactory worker near the party — **Danna, halfling, 40s, oil-stained hands** — says, quietly: *"That's the third unannounced drop this month. I've been working the forges for twenty years. They always announce the drops."*

Toras Vel's message finds the party that evening.

### What Toras Vel Knows

He's a data recorder in the Council's power monitoring office. His job is to log Heart output measurements and file them. Three months ago he noticed his filed numbers were being edited before reaching the Council report. The numbers he's filing show faster decline than what the Council is officially receiving.

He doesn't know who is editing them or why. He has his original logs. He wants someone who isn't inside this system to have them — not necessarily to publish them, just to have them, so that if something happens to him, the information doesn't disappear with him.

He is not brave. He is frightened. He is doing this anyway because he has a daughter in the Manufactories district and he needs to know she's going to be okay.

### Immediate Complications

**The edited data** points either to a Conservator cover-up (keeping the crisis from looking worse to prevent panic) or an Illuminist fabrication (making their research look more urgent than it is). Or something the party hasn't considered.

**Councilor Mira Ashton** (Illuminist chair) is aware that data irregularities exist. She has not surfaced them publicly because she doesn't yet know which faction benefits from the irregularity and she won't fire a weapon she can't aim. She would be very interested in what Toras has — and her interest would not necessarily be safe for him.

**The Vault District's unauthorized experiments** are being run by Professor Talan Greaves, who has discovered something about the Heart's failure pattern that he has told no one. What he's found might change everything. What it might be is something the party can investigate, and Greaves is the kind of person who will talk to the right strangers before he'll talk to the Council.

**The Shadowside district** knows something the official picture doesn't — they've never been on the grid, which means they notice power-related phenomena differently. The Night Market there has been seeing unusual traffic: people selling shard-adjacent technology that doesn't match anything in Lumencrest's legitimate catalog. Someone is building something without authorization.

### Threads Leading Out

- Toras's data connects to the Somnarchs' timeline calculations — the numbers, properly interpreted, confirm what Vaela Thessin has been predicting
- The Expansionists will move on the Endless Eye when the Council fractures; the party can accelerate, delay, or redirect this depending on what they do
- Councilor Ashton's classified research programs include one she's genuinely lost control of — it connects to the deep mine situation in Ironhold
- The Veilmarkets have been watching Lumencrest's internal situation and buying specific goods quietly; Director Sable has already decided something the party hasn't

## Hook 2: The Shrinking Lake

**Location:** The Endless Eye, Starfall Basin
**Tone:** Cultural immersion, community tension, slow-burn crisis management
**Good for:** Players who enjoy diverse communities, moral complexity without clear villains, gradual revelation
**Opening Season:** Late Goldmonth — trade season peak, city at maximum population

---

### The Situation

The Endless Eye is the most cosmopolitan city on the continent: Fairy in aerial districts, Ribbet beneath the surface, Katari trading through on desert routes, merchants from everywhere passing through. It runs on commerce and the miracle of a star-made lake in the middle of a desert. The lake is visibly receding.

The Merchant Circle has classified the decline data. The Ribbet community knows — they've been watching it for years. The surface populations are beginning to notice. The three council factions (Traditionalists, Pragmatists, Profiteers) cannot agree on what to say, what to do, or who gets to decide.

The party arrives as the city is beginning to understand something is wrong without being told what it is.

### The Opening Scene

The party is crossing one of the Endless Eye's lake-level bridges when they notice the waterline marks on the bridge supports — high-water stains that stop well above the current surface. A Ribbet dock worker named **Pearl-Keeper Jibb** (Ribbet-Fairy mix, 19, perpetually damp) is unloading cargo nearby and notices what the party is looking at.

*"Six inches in two years,"* he says, without being asked. *"I know, because I've been measuring. The Circle says that's within normal variation."* A pause. *"I've been doing this job for three years. There's no normal variation. The lake doesn't vary."*

He's not supposed to be talking about this. He just did anyway.

### What's Actually Happening

The Hollostar at the lake's center is doing something the Merchant Circle doesn't understand. The decline is measurable and consistent. Deepwatcher Selin has observed something in The Deeps — movement near the Hollostar that doesn't match any known category. Elder Suu has been communing with the lake for forty years and is hearing something new in it that she cannot name.

The classified data shows the decline accelerating — not toward sudden catastrophe, but toward the moment when everyone in the city will be able to see it. The Circle has maybe eighteen months before the crisis becomes impossible to manage quietly. They have been arguing about those eighteen months for two years.

### Immediate Complications

**Voice Kessa** (Ribbet, Pragmatist council seat) is looking for people who aren't formally Circle-affiliated to do some work she can't visibly authorize — data collection in the lake's outer reaches that would require diving permits she can't issue without triggering a council vote she'd lose. She's willing to pay. She's not entirely willing to explain why.

**Merchant Brek** (Ribbet, Profiteer) is running an unauthorized trade in Hollostar-touched water from the Deep. The party may stumble onto a transaction, or be approached by one of his contacts who is having second thoughts.

**Wingspeaker Aelindra** needs specific ritual components gathered from three sites around the lake — she believes a ceremony connected to the lake's founding can communicate with the Hollostar in a way research cannot. She needs people willing to take her position seriously and capable of handling difficult terrain. The gathering is genuinely dangerous; she is honest about this.

**Dust-Chief Orah** (Katari, Wanderer seat) has been approached by both the Traditionalist and Pragmatist factions to secure her vote on a key research motion. She hasn't decided. She's watching the party, having heard about them through Katari networks. She will speak to them directly if they approach with honesty and without an agenda.

### Threads Leading Out

- Deepwatcher Selin's observation of movement in The Deeps is the hook into the Hollostar's actual condition — understanding it requires multiple dives and a willingness to sit with genuinely uncertain information
- The Lumencrest Expansionists have an agent in the city that Voss Tanakar suspects but hasn't identified; the party can find this before Voss does, giving them leverage with the most powerful person in the Circle
- The Starfall Seekers have a presence in the city; Tam Coldridge's timeline discrepancy connects to the lake's situation in ways neither the Seekers nor the Circle have put together yet
- The Katari desert routes hold oral history about what happened during the third fall that isn't in any archive — it becomes relevant when the party starts understanding the Hollostar's behavior

## Hook 3: The Seeker Expedition

**Location:** Open — begins anywhere, quickly goes to Frostwell
**Tone:** Adventure, exploration, escalating stakes, moral questions about knowledge
**Good for:** Players who want movement, discovery, a reason to see multiple regions
**Opening Season:** Late Goldmonth — expedition season ending, which makes this one unusual for starting now

---

### The Situation

Expedition Leader Tam Coldridge (they/them, human, 40s, methodical and quietly courageous) is running the most significant Hollostar research expedition currently active — based in Frostwell, studying the ancient ruins and the approaching star's effects on local shard deposits. They have calculated a discrepancy in the impact timeline. Their data says the fourth fall is coming faster than the Somnarchs' published estimate. They are checking it a third time before going public.

They need people who aren't formally Seeker-affiliated, can keep a confidence for a defined period, and are capable of independent judgment in difficult situations. The party comes recommended through a mutual contact.

### The Opening Scene

Coldridge meets the party in whatever city they're currently in — they've sent a representative ahead, or they're passing through and the meeting is brief and practical. They lay out the offer plainly: travel with the expedition to Frostwell, assist with data collection and site work over the next several weeks, be present when Coldridge releases their findings. Pay is fair. Danger is honestly described. The one condition is that nothing leaves the expedition until Coldridge is ready to release it.

*"I want independent witnesses,"* Coldridge says. *"Not because I distrust my people. Because when this data becomes public — and it will — I want people who can say they watched us collect it and saw nothing dishonest. This is going to be contested. I need it to be uncontestable."*

### What the Party Walks Into

The Frostwell expedition is operating in a region simultaneously experiencing the Nine Hearths' evacuation crisis and the ancient ruins waking up in ways nobody expected. The dig site has been producing not just shard deposits but architectural evidence that whoever built the ruins had a specific relationship with Hollostar events — designed for them, anticipated them. Coldridge is not releasing this yet either. The party will figure it out.

The expedition has an internal problem: a senior Seeker named **Drev Halloran** (dwarf, 60s, brilliant and reckless) has been running unauthorized experiments on a significant fragment removed from the site. Coldridge has sent word asking Halloran to stop. Halloran has not stopped. Coldridge cannot force the issue without internal Seeker politics exploding at the worst possible moment.

### Immediate Complications

**The timeline discrepancy** is more significant than Coldridge let on in the recruitment meeting. The data suggests not just that the fall is coming faster, but that the impact site is different from current projections. If they're right, whoever is preparing for the fall in the wrong location is preparing in the wrong location.

**The Nine Hearths evacuation** is happening around the expedition — families moving south, hearth-leaders arguing about whether to go, Drakona at the Ember Cradle making their own calculations. The expedition is a resource the hearths have been eyeing. Coldridge has been careful to help when asked and not impose. The party may find themselves making choices Coldridge can't officially make.

**Rider Commander Sera Windmane** (Eagle Riders, Northern Expanse) has been conducting aerial surveys of the approach trajectory and her data matches Coldridge's in one specific and alarming way. They have not yet compared notes. The party can facilitate this — or the information gap can persist until it matters.

**Drev Halloran's experiment** is going somewhere that worries the other expedition researchers. What he's attempting is not inherently impossible — a technique for temporarily amplifying shard output. What he hasn't told anyone is that his last test produced something not in any existing research: a physical response from the fragment. It moved. Slightly. Once. He doesn't know what that means. He is trying to make it happen again before he tells anyone.

### Threads Leading Out

- The ancient ruins' architecture connects to the Age Before — evidence of a civilization that understood Hollostar falls as predictable events and built infrastructure around them, which raises the question of why that civilization doesn't exist anymore
- Coldridge's timeline data, once released, reshapes how every faction responds — the party can influence who gets it first and frames the narrative
- The Frostwell Dwarves (Barad-Khelek) have deep records in their own internal calendar that they haven't shared with outsiders; the expedition wants access, and the Dwarves have been watching the expedition for three months deciding whether to trust it
- The Heart Warning — whether the Somnarchs tell Lumencrest that the Heart will fail on impact day — connects to Coldridge's timeline. The timeline changes the calculus.

## Hook 4: The Northern Lights

**Location:** Frostwell Tundra, Northern Expanse border
**Tone:** Frontier survival, community bonds, cosmic scale rendered human
**Good for:** Players who want grounded stakes, genuine community, the world's largest thing happening at close range
**Opening Season:** Late Goldmonth — last caravans of the season, snows coming

---

### The Situation

The Nine Hearths are nine villages in the frozen north, each built around a shared hearth that is both practical infrastructure and cultural center. They've been here for generations. The approaching star is visible every clear night, growing brighter. Evacuation discussions are happening — some hearths are already moving, some are refusing.

The party is in the north. Maybe they're locals. Maybe they came with the last autumn caravan and the first early snow cut off the southern route. Maybe they arrived with the Seekers or a Somnarch-affiliated contact. The specific reason matters less than the result: they are present in a community deciding whether to leave everything it has built, and something is waking up in the ancient ruins across the tundra.

### The Opening Scene

The party is in **Ashenvale Hearth** (one of the Nine, population ~400, has been debating evacuation for three months without deciding). The hearth-fire is the center of community life — cooking, heat, social gathering, story-keeping. That evening, the approaching star is visible through the smoke-hole in the hearth-roof: a bright, moving point, visibly larger than it was a month ago.

**Hearth-Keeper Mira** (human woman, 58, the community's de facto leader) calls a meeting. Two families have already packed and left for the south. Two more are ready to go. The rest of the community is watching her. She doesn't have an answer. She has a question: *"What do we owe the people who will come after us, and what does staying cost them?"*

She's not asking rhetorically. She's asking because the party just arrived from the direction the evacuees are heading, and she wants to know what the south looks like right now.

### What's Happening Around Them

The ancient ruins scattered across the tundra — preserved in ice for millennia — have been behaving differently for about eight months. The Drakona at the Ember Cradle have been reporting this to each other, not to outsiders. The Frostwell Dwarves (Barad-Khelek) have their own deep-sense of it through stone and have been quietly sealing certain tunnels. The Nine Hearths' elder shamans — Path of Spirits practitioners — have felt it through the spirit landscape.

Nobody has put together that they're all noticing the same thing. The party can be the connective tissue.

**Tam Coldridge's expedition** is operating in the region and has better data than anyone on the ground. The party may find or be directed to them. Coldridge's timeline discrepancy is relevant here: if the fall is coming faster than official estimates, the evacuation window is shorter than anyone has told the hearths.

### Immediate Complications

**Hearth-Keeper Mira** needs two things: an accurate picture of what's happening in the south (capacity, attitude toward refugees, which roads are still passable in early winter) and an honest assessment of what the ruins' activation means. She will make her decision with whatever information she has. The party can improve the quality of that information.

**An Elder at Ironspire Hearth** (the most isolated of the Nine) has stopped responding to messenger birds. The last communication was three weeks ago, brief, and concerning: *"The old place is awake. We are staying to watch it."* Mira wants to know if they're okay. She also wants to know what "awake" means.

**The Ember Cradle** — sacred site for Frostwell Drakona, believed connected to the first Hollostar fall — is exhibiting phenomena the Drakona have never seen. A Drakona scholar named **Reth** has been trying to get this information to someone who can act on it for six weeks. The party is the first promising contact they've had.

**The Early Snows** are sealing off routes faster than expected. Someone planning to go south will need to decide soon — before the first major storm closes the mountain passes. This is a deadline that doesn't care about the larger crisis.

### Threads Leading Out

- The ruins connect to the Age Before and to everything the Seekers are investigating — there is pre-fall architecture here suggesting foreknowledge of Hollostar cycles
- The evacuation connects to the Northern Expanse's refugee crisis (Clock 5) — where these people go and whether they're welcomed is partly determined by King Elowen's decisions and partly by what the party does here
- Marshal-General Corvain's unauthorized Frostwell preparations (Steelwatch) can become real support if the party makes contact and gives her reason to move faster
- The Path of Spirits shamans here have information about the spirit landscape's response to the approaching star that no faction has thought to ask for

## Hook 5: The Green Resistance

**Location:** Verdant Crescent, edge settlement / deep forest border
**Tone:** Moral complexity, wilderness, institutional conflict without clear heroes
**Good for:** Players who want nuanced faction politics, nature-focused play, a fight where both sides have legitimate points
**Opening Season:** Late Goldmonth — Lumencrest survey teams appearing at forest edge; tension high

---

### The Situation

The Verdant Crescent's Deep Forest is protected by the Green Covenant — a druidic organization with genuine legal standing, L'Empire Verdienne's diplomatic backing, and a history of defending the forest effectively. Lumencrest wants access to forest resources and has been pushing at the edges through survey teams, land claim registrations in edge settlements, and funding to local lords who might be persuaded to invite Lumencrest representatives in.

The Covenant is not unified. The mainstream leadership (Archcovenant Aldren Mosswood, practical, 40s) wants to hold the legal line without escalating. A radical splinter faction called the Thorns operates through sabotage and increasingly through violence — targeting Lumencrest-affiliated operations. The mainstream doesn't publicly acknowledge the Thorns. Privately, some of them are relieved the Thorns exist.

The party arrives in an edge settlement — one of the towns at the forest's boundary with relationships on both sides.

### The Opening Scene

The party arrives in **Fernwatch** (edge settlement, ~800 people, mixed ancestry, economically dependent on both forest trade and Lumencrest-connected merchants) to find a Lumencrest survey team setting up equipment in the town square. They have a legitimate land survey warrant from the regional lord. Two Covenant-affiliated rangers are watching from a rooftop across the square. The rangers are not doing anything. Yet.

The town's headwoman, **Sera Dunmoss** (human, 50s, tired), intercepts the party before they've fully arrived. She's heard they're not from here. *"Good,"* she says. *"That means you don't have a side yet. I have three problems and I could use someone who doesn't have a side."*

Her three problems: the survey team is going to find something that changes the warrant's scope. Two of her town's young people went to meet the Thorns last week and haven't come back. And the forest behind the town has been making sounds at night for the past month that it didn't used to make.

### What's Actually Happening

The forest is waking up. The Heartgrove's oldest trees are changing growth patterns, animal behavior is shifting, and the shamans in deep settlement Leafholm have been receiving messages through the mycelial network they don't fully understand. Archcovenant Mosswood is trying to understand what "waking" means before deciding whether it's a threat or something else.

Lumencrest doesn't know this. Their survey team is here because the Expansionists calculated specific forest resources are extractable without triggering the L'Empire protection clause. They are right about this in a narrow legal sense and completely wrong about what it would mean in practice.

The Thorns have an operation planned against the survey team — not lethal, but damaging enough that it will require a Lumencrest response. That response will either escalate or expose the Thorns depending on how the party positions itself.

### Immediate Complications

**The missing young people** from Fernwatch are with the Thorns. They went looking for something they believed the Covenant mainstream wouldn't give them: direct action. They're not in danger from the Thorns — they chose to be there. They are in danger from what the Thorns are about to do.

**The survey team's lead** (human, Lumencrest Academic Quarter, genuinely believes this is neutral scholarly work) has found something in preliminary measurements outside the warrant scope: an anomaly in the forest's subsurface that looks, to their instruments, like a significant shard deposit. They haven't reported this yet — they're rechecking their data. When they do report it, the scope of Lumencrest's interest changes completely.

**Archcovenant Mosswood** needs to know about both the survey data and the Thorns' planned operation before either happens. He can't be seen asking for this — the Covenant's position depends on not appearing to have known about Thorns operations. The party can be the path that information travels without anyone officially knowing they provided it.

**The forest sounds** are real. Something in the deep forest is communicating through the root network in a pattern that Leafholm's shamans have heard once before — in the oldest oral records, describing what the forest did during the third Hollostar impact. The forest remembers. It is doing again what it did then.

### Threads Leading Out

- The deep forest's awakening connects to the Glittering Marshes' shard pulse changes — something continent-wide is responding to the approaching star
- L'Empire Verdienne's involvement gives the party a diplomatic lever against Lumencrest operating on a scale the Covenant alone cannot deploy
- The Thorns' existence is an open secret that could become a public crisis — how it becomes known, and who shapes that narrative, has significant consequences for the Covenant
- The Simiah of Canopy Run have Veleth — the capacity to feel the forest's spirit network directly — and have been sensing the same continent-wide changes that nobody on the surface has connected yet

## Choosing and Mixing

### Which Hook Fits Your Group?

| If your players want... | Consider... |
|------------------------|-------------|
| City politics, investigation, moral gray | Hook 1 (Lumencrest) |
| Diverse community, slow burn, cultural depth | Hook 2 (Endless Eye) |
| Movement, exploration, discovery | Hook 3 (Seekers) |
| Community bonds, intimate stakes, cosmic scale | Hook 4 (Frostwell) |
| Faction conflict, nature, two legitimate sides | Hook 5 (Verdant Crescent) |

### Combining Hooks

These hooks are not separate campaigns. They're five doors into the same world.

**Hooks 1 + 3:** Lumencrest political situation and Coldridge's timeline discrepancy converge naturally — the data the party holds in Frostwell is exactly what the Lumencrest factions need, and the factions are exactly the problem determining whether that data helps or makes things worse.

**Hooks 2 + 5:** The Endless Eye's crisis and the Verdant Crescent's awakening are, at a deep level, the same phenomenon — two Hollostar-connected places responding to the approaching star. A party that goes to both will start seeing the pattern before most factions do.

**Hooks 3 + 4:** Coldridge's expedition and the Frostwell community are physically in the same place. These can be run as a single opening — the party arrives in Frostwell already with Coldridge, or encounters the hearths first and finds the expedition nearby.

### Starting Location and Faction Relationships

Whatever hook you choose, worth establishing at Session 0:
- Does the party start together, or does the hook bring them together?
- Do any characters have pre-existing relationships with factions in the region?
- What does each character have to lose if things go badly? (Often more useful than backstory.)
