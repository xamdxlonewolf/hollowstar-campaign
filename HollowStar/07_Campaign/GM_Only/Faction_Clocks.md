# FACTION CLOCKS — GM Only

> *For every faction: what they want right now, what they will do in the next 3–6 months if unopposed, and who suffers if they succeed. Use these as the pressure behind every session. The world moves without the players. Their job is to decide what that means.*

---

## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

Each faction entry has three parts:

**CURRENT ACTION** — What is already in motion. This is happening now, regardless of the players.

**NEXT MOVE (if unopposed)** — What happens in 3–6 months if nobody interferes. Give players a window to act before this resolves.

**WHO SUFFERS** — Concrete, named people or communities. Abstract suffering is not pressure. Specific suffering is.

At the end of the document: a **Countdown Calendar** — all the clocks mapped to a rough timeline so you can see when things collide.

## COUNCIL OF LUMENS (Lumencrest)

### The Expansionists — ACTIVE CLOCK

**Current Action:**
*The Meridian Survey* is already en route to the Isle of the Broken Sphere. Merchant-Lord Davan Creel (Expansionist faction leader) arranged this expedition through procedural maneuvering — technically within emergency research powers, practically a significant overstep. Simultaneously, Expansionist agents are maintaining three ongoing operations: (1) above-market timber extraction offers to Verdant Crescent edge settlements, (2) a Frostwell research team tracking the Hollostar's approach, (3) quiet diplomatic pressure on the Merchant Circle for Endless Eye shard access.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 3–4 months):**
*The Meridian Survey* returns with initial data on the Isle. Whatever the geological report says, Creel will present it to the Council as proof that the Isle contains a viable alternative power source. A second ship — already being prepared — launches before the full Council can debate the first voyage. The Expansionist faction gains its first formal foothold outside the Heartlands.

**Who Suffers:**
The Galapa community on the Isle, whose only home on the continent becomes a target for resource extraction. Elder Tess, whose careful management of the Sleeper's awakening is disrupted by outside instruments and personnel. The Merchant Circle in Starfall Basin, whose refusal of Endless Eye access now has a competing precedent to cite against them.

---

### The Conservators — PASSIVE CLOCK

**Current Action:**
Controlling information about the Heart's accelerating decline. The true rate (4 years to non-functional, not 8) is known to three Conservators and one clerk who has not yet decided what to do about it. The Conservators are blocking Illuminist access to the Vault District's deep chambers and maintaining rationing schedules that protect the Radiant Core at the expense of the Lamplight Ward.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 4–6 months):**
When the Heart's decline becomes undeniable to the wider Council, the Conservators will propose a formal state of emergency that concentrates power in their hands. The rationale will be "managed decline" — controlled shutdown of non-essential city functions to preserve the Heart as long as possible. In practice this means the Manufactories district loses power first, then the Lamplight Ward entirely. Roughly 8,000 people lose heated homes and lit streets in a Northern city.

**Who Suffers:**
Lamplight Ward working families. Manufactories workers already dealing with reduced hours. The unofficial guild in Cinderhome's situation will be repeated in Lumencrest — people who had stability finding the floor gone.

---

### The Illuminists — REACTIVE CLOCK

**Current Action:**
Running an unauthorized research program at the University of Applied Light — attempting to understand whether the Heart can be "fed" rather than replaced. Two researchers have been granted limited Vault access through a technicality. They have found something in their readings that they haven't reported yet: the Heart isn't fading uniformly. It's fading at the edges and *intensifying* at the center — as if it's contracting rather than dimming. This makes no sense under any current model.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 2–3 months):**
The unauthorized research gets discovered by the Conservators and shut down. The two researchers lose their positions. Their data — the anomalous contraction reading — gets sealed in the Vault with the rest of the restricted material. The question of what the contraction means goes unanswered.

**Who Suffers:**
The researchers (career destruction). The wider world — the contraction reading is the first clue that the Heart is not simply fading but *doing something*, which means Dreamer-level activity is occurring under Lumencrest. Someone should know this. Nobody will.

## STARFALL SEEKERS

**Current Action:**
Scholar Elira Swiftpaw is leading the largest Seeker expedition in twenty years into the northern Frostwell Tundra — a newly identified fall site near the projected impact zone of the approaching star. Her team is running three concurrent research tracks: ancient fall site analysis, trajectory calculation refinement, and energy signature mapping. The expedition has produced readings that contradict existing models: the approaching Hollostar is resonating with ground-level anomalies in a pattern that suggests intentionality rather than random trajectory. Elira has these findings but has not published them — she is still trying to understand what they mean and whether publication would be responsible. Secondary operations: the Ironfall Crew is under contract for a mysterious Frostwell expedition from an unknown patron, and the Kingsrest Archive is overwhelmed processing incoming crisis-related research requests.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 2–3 months):**
Elira's unpublished findings get leaked — either by a team member who believes the world needs to know, or by someone who obtained them through the Kingsrest Archive. The findings hit every major institution simultaneously: the Hollostar is not just larger than historical records suggest, it is responding to something on the ground. The pattern of ancient falls was not random. The implications are that someone or something has been influencing where stars land. Panic, not coordination, follows. The Illuminists push for immediate aggressive research. The Expansionists cite this as justification for resource seizure. The Hexarchs fracture over theological interpretation. Nobody actually addresses what "responding to something" means.

**Who Suffers:**
The Nine Hearths, whose stay/flee crisis becomes exponentially worse when they learn their region may have been "chosen." Elira herself, whose career credibility and possibly physical safety become collateral in the institutional responses. Any community in the projected impact corridor that has been told "it's just a natural event" and now learns it is not.

## VEILMARKETS

**Current Action:**
Three active operations relevant to the campaign: (1) Brinehaven cell brokering information about the Isle of the Broken Sphere — selling to anyone who asks, including the Lumencrest agent aboard the *Meridian Survey*. (2) A Lumencrest undercity cell facilitating shard smuggling out of the Vault District — small quantities, but the Conservators don't know the leak exists. (3) A Frostwell cell running supply arbitrage — buying Nine Hearths food stores at crisis prices, reselling to Central Heartlands buyers. Technically legal. Practically predatory.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 3–5 months):**
The Brinehaven cell's Isle intelligence attracts a buyer who is not Lumencrest — a private party willing to pay significantly more. The Veilmarkets broker the deal without knowing (or caring) who the buyer is. The information sold includes Elder Tess's name and her continental-connection theory.

**Who Suffers:**
Elder Tess — someone now knows she has a theory that powerful people would very much prefer not to spread. The Nine Hearths communities, whose food security is being financially eroded precisely when they need it most. The Lumencrest Vault, slowly bleeding shard material whose absence won't be noticed until it matters.

## THE GREEN COVENANT

**Current Action:**
Maren Stillwater has convened an emergency private session of the Five Wardens. The ancient waking's acceleration has moved beyond "monitor" into "respond" — but Maren does not yet know what response is appropriate. Two things are happening in parallel: (1) The Warden of the Reach (Dusk) is deepening their unauthorized alliance with Lumencrest's anti-Expansionist faction — trading forest intelligence for resources without Maren's knowledge. (2) The Warden of the Edge is sitting on data about how many edge settlement families have taken Lumencrest money — the number is larger than Maren knows.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 2–3 months):**
Warden Dusk's unauthorized alliance produces its first concrete result: a Lumencrest research team enters the Verdant Crescent's eastern edge under Dusk's protection, without Maren's authorization. When Maren discovers it (and she will), the Covenant fractures internally at exactly the moment it needs to act on the ancient waking. The Warden of the Reach faces expulsion. The Lumencrest team is already inside.

**Who Suffers:**
The Green Covenant's institutional coherence — the one force that has successfully resisted Lumencrest expansion. Edge settlement families who took Lumencrest money, who are now exposed when Maren finally gets accurate numbers. The ancient waking site, which now has unauthorized researchers near it.

## THE HEXARCHS

**Current Action:**
The Hexarchal Council is being pressured by political allies (particularly from Lumencrest and Kingsrest) to issue a unified doctrinal statement on the Hollostar crisis. Hexarch Davan Solm (Voice of the Architect) is drafting one — a Test Position statement emphasizing order, endurance, and institutional stability. Hexarchs Lira Vaunt (Voice of the Weaver) and Senne Pale (Voice of the Gravewalker) are blocking it, arguing any statement issued now will be wrong. Meanwhile, Hexarch Orvyn Tallow (Voice of the Hearthkeeper) has been running unauthorized expanded relief programs for four months, drawing on temple reserves the Council never approved. An Architect's-Faith auditor has discovered the discrepancy and is preparing a formal complaint. Hexarch Brennan Holt (Voice of the Sentinel) has been approached by the Expansionists for a formal blessing on military action toward the Endless Eye — he has not said yes, he has not said no.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 3–4 months):**
Solm's doctrinal statement passes the Council by narrow majority (Solm, Holt, and one other convinced vote). The statement endorses institutional stability and implicitly criticizes "reckless transformation" — language that can be read as supporting Lumencrest's Conservators. The Transition Position (Vaunt and Pale's view that this is change to navigate, not a test to pass) becomes unofficial heresy. Temple priests who have been quietly adopting that position face pressure to conform. The star-worshipper fringe, seeing the institutional faith fail to speak to their experience, grows. Tallow's unauthorized relief programs get shut down mid-crisis, cutting support to thousands of families. Holt's refusal to give the Expansionists their blessing becomes a "yes" by inaction — the Expansionists cite his public statements regardless of whether he formally endorsed them.

**Who Suffers:**
Communities relying on Hearthkeeper relief programs that suddenly end. Temple priests caught between institutional doctrine and what their congregations actually need. The Hexarchs' own institutional credibility, which takes damage whether the statement passes or fails. Anyone hoping the Six-Fold Faith could be a stabilizing force during the crisis — that window closes.

## THE FIRST FLAME — EMBERKNIGHTS

**Current Action:**
The Emberknight covert program is running three active operations: (1) Staged infrastructure failures in Lumencrest's outer districts to demonstrate the Council's incompetence and create openings for First Flame aid, (2) Intelligence gathering on Lumencrest's classified research programs through a source inside the University, (3) Positioning operatives in the Frostwell Tundra ahead of the star's impact to be the first organized response on the ground. The operations are coordinated by Flamekeeper Irius Vann from the Central Heartlands main temple. The public-facing First Flame (pilgrim relief, community support, theological outreach) continues without knowledge of the covert program. Initiate Kael Thornroot has been conducting unauthorized research into Hollostar energy properties and has made a discovery he has not yet reported to his superiors — the First Flame's theological interpretation of the stars as divine tests may be literally, physically correct in ways the faith does not understand.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 2–3 months):**
One of the staged infrastructure failures in Lumencrest goes wrong — a water main break intended to be quickly "aided" by First Flame relief workers floods a residential block, causing actual harm instead of symbolic inconvenience. The truth of what happened starts leaking through multiple channels (a civilian who saw something, a guard who asks the wrong questions, an Emberknight with a conscience). When the staged operations are exposed, every First Flame relief operation becomes suspect. Communities that were genuinely helped by First Flame aid turn on them. The Hexarchs' accusations of heresy gain material evidence. The covert program collapses, taking the public legitimacy with it. Kael's discovery about Hollostar energy and divine reality gets buried in the scandal — nobody listens to theology from an organization that just got caught staging disasters.

**Who Suffers:**
The families in the flooded block (direct harm). Every community that trusted First Flame relief and now questions whether they were being manipulated. The First Flame's moderate faction, who genuinely believed in helping people and are now discredited by association. Kael himself, whose theological breakthrough becomes toxic by connection to the scandal.

## STILLWARD ENCLAVES

**Current Action:**
Elder-Coordinator Mara Thessen of Holdfast (Northern Expanse mountain valley enclave) has made the decision to prepare for refugee intake despite significant internal opposition. She is expanding food reserves, preparing temporary housing in the valley's outer terraces, and drafting integration protocols. The opposition faction within Holdfast believes accepting refugees will compromise the enclave's independence and is organizing to block the preparations. The inter-enclave courier network has carried news of Holdfast's internal debate to other enclaves — several Preparer communities are watching to see whether Thessen succeeds or fails before committing to similar positions. Meanwhile, Stonehold (Salt Shore island, Purist enclave) has refused to share its unique medicinal compound with the wider enclave network despite increasing pressure. Someone outside the Stillward network has learned about the compound's existence and properties. That information is now circulating through Veilmarket intelligence channels.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 2–3 months):**
Refugees from a collapsing Frostwell community arrive at Holdfast's valley entrance before Thessen's preparations are complete and before the internal opposition has been resolved. The opposition faction moves to physically bar entry. Holdfast fractures — roughly 40% of the community splits off, taking supplies and leaving the valley to found their own enclave elsewhere. The refugee families are caught in the middle: accepted by a weakened community that can barely support them, or turned away to face winter with nowhere to go. Simultaneously, an outside party (possibly Lumencrest Illuminists, possibly independent researchers, possibly something worse) attempts to reach Stonehold by force to obtain the medicinal compound. Stonehold's isolation is breached. The compound's properties become public knowledge, making every Stillward enclave with valuable resources a potential target.

**Who Suffers:**
The refugee families, who become the cause of a community schism they did not ask for. Holdfast itself, which loses nearly half its population and much of its institutional knowledge. The 40% who leave, who now have to build from scratch in harsher conditions. Stonehold's community, whose isolation philosophy is proven correct by being violated. Every other Stillward enclave, who must now decide whether to prioritize independence (Stonehold's way) or pragmatic preparation (Holdfast's way) while knowing both paths have just failed.

## THE SOMNARCHS

**Current Action:**
Aelindra Voce has sent two of her Four Rememberers out — one toward Frostwell (to observe the Ember Cradle's warming and make contact with Flame-Keeper Sorith), one toward the Isle (to assess the fault line). Both are traveling as ordinary scholars. Neither has made contact yet. Voce herself is at the Endless Eye, running maintenance rituals that are increasingly insufficient. The Eye's dream-bleed radius has expanded from roughly half a mile to nearly three — people in the outer settlements of the Starfall Basin are starting to have unusually vivid, coherent, inexplicable dreams.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 4–6 months):**
The dream-bleed from the Endless Eye reaches Starfall Basin's population centers. The Merchant Circle, already divided, begins receiving pressure from frightened communities. The Pragmatist faction seizes on the dreams as evidence of "the Hollostar's influence" and proposes opening the Eye's core to direct study. The Somnarchs will not be able to stop this through maintenance rituals alone. Voce needs allies — people who can interfere directly with the Pragmatist proposal and buy her time.

**Who Suffers:**
Starfall Basin residents with no framework for what they're experiencing — prophetic dreams, déjà vu epidemics, sudden inexplicable inspirations followed by weeks of mental fog. The Merchant Circle's coherence. Anyone who ends up as a study subject for Pragmatist researchers probing the Eye's core.

# REGIONAL FACTIONS

## VOSS — THE TIDE KING

**Current Action:**
Voss has identified that the Grey Sails have raided three ships paying his Silver Belt protection cut. He has not acted yet. He is mapping the full extent of the overlap — finding every Grey Sails captain who has unknowingly touched his interests — before responding. Simultaneously, his Saltmere intelligence has revealed that two of Brinehaven's established merchant families have moved significant operations to Saltmere. He has identified this as a structural threat, not a personal insult, and is planning accordingly.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 3–4 months):**
Voss makes contact with the Grey Sails through an intermediary — not a threat, an offer. Pay the cut and operate freely in his waters, or find the Silver Belt increasingly hostile. He simultaneously offers the two defecting Brinehaven merchant families a sweetened arrangement to return — better terms, no hard feelings, demonstrating that loyalty pays better than defection. If the Grey Sails refuse (likely — they have a culture of independence), he begins making specific ships disappear. Not all of them. Enough.

**Who Suffers:**
Grey Sails crews who don't know they've already been marked. Saltmere, whose economic momentum gets quietly undercut. Any merchant trying to operate in the Silver Belt without paying anyone — the window for free navigation is closing.

## THE FOUR HOUSES (Silverbelt Coast)

**Current Action:**
The Caldren succession crisis has entered its eighth month with no heir confirmed. House Vethara is funding the search for a controllable Caldren heir. House Mourne is quietly approaching House Aldric about a potential alliance that would isolate Vethara. House Caldren's acting steward is desperately trying to appear functional while the House's treasury bleeds. The Council of Banners has not met in full session in three months — Caldren keeps finding reasons to delay.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 4–5 months):**
Vethara produces a Caldren heir — a distant cousin they've spent eight months making financially and politically dependent. The other Houses recognize what Vethara has done. House Mourne completes its Aldric alliance. A cold split forms: Vethara-Caldren (controlled) versus Mourne-Aldric. The Council of Banners becomes functionally deadlocked. The Grey Sails, watching the dysfunction, expand their operations into the Caldren patrol gap with new confidence.

**Who Suffers:**
Caldren's coastal towns, now under Grey Sails pressure with no functional House defense. Merchant shipping through the region, paying both Voss's cut and Grey Sails losses. The House Mourne river communities, who get caught in the trade disruption while their lord is playing politics.

---

## KING ELOWEN — NORTHERN EXPANSE

**Current Action:**
The Warden of the North appointment has been pending for two years. Elowen knows he needs to decide. He has convened a private evaluation of the three candidates (Stonefield, Mael, Coldmarch) and has not yet told any of them. The frontier towns are sending increasingly urgent messages about refugee pressure and the failed harvest. Lord Chancellor Ashvale has begun managing crisis decisions without waiting for royal authorization — technically overstepping, practically necessary.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 2–3 months):**
Elowen appoints Advisor Theron Mael — the court-aligned elf who will enforce open borders as policy. The appointment satisfies Lakemere. It infuriates every frontier town that has been managing arrivals without resources. Captain Brek Coldmarch, the Giant refugee who is actually the right person for the job, refuses any formal role in protest and becomes the informal leader of frontier towns coordinating independently of the crown. A parallel governance structure emerges — the crown's official policy, and what the frontier actually does.

**Who Suffers:**
Frontier towns under resource pressure without crown support. Refugees who fall between the official policy and the frontier's actual capacity. Coldmarch, who is carrying the weight of both groups while being formally powerless.

---

## THE GREY SAILS

**Current Action:**
Exploiting the Caldren patrol gap aggressively. Three Grey Sails captains are in the gap simultaneously — more than they'd normally risk in one area. They don't know about Voss's tracking. Captain Rynn Saltblood (House Vethara) has been in contact with someone she knows in the Grey Sails leadership — old naval service connection — and is receiving intelligence about House movements in exchange for information about Vethara's naval schedule. She hasn't told anyone. She justifies it as keeping options open.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 3–4 months):**
The Grey Sails receive Voss's offer and refuse. Voss begins the quiet campaign. The three captains in the Caldren gap start disappearing — ships found drifting, crews absent, no evidence. The Grey Sails leadership doesn't understand what's happening yet. They increase presence in the gap, assuming it's a House operation. It isn't. Meanwhile, Saltblood's intelligence-sharing gets one step closer to detection by House Vethara's internal security.

**Who Suffers:**
Grey Sails crews (directly). House Caldren's coastal communities, who lose even the informal deterrent the Grey Sails' fear of House response provided. Saltblood, when her side deal unravels.

## THE GREEN COVENANT (see also continent-wide above)

**Current Action:**
The Warden of the Edge has compiled the full list of edge settlement families with Lumencrest financial ties. 23 families. The Warden has been sitting on this for six weeks, deciding what to tell Maren. The Warden of Waters has made first contact with Elder Broum Shell in the Glittering Marshes, attempting to establish whether the shards' stirring is connected to the ancient waking. Elder Broum has been politely noncommittal for three meetings.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 2–3 months):**
The Edge Warden finally presents the list to Maren. Maren's response — whether measured or reactive — determines whether edge settlements become Covenant allies or Covenant problems. If Maren reacts by cutting off the 23 families (likely under pressure), Lumencrest gains 23 willing informants with legitimate forest access. If she forgives and absorbs, she signals that Lumencrest money has no consequence.

**Who Suffers:**
The 23 families, regardless of which way this goes. The Verdant Crescent's political coherence, which is only as strong as the edge settlements' loyalty.

## THE MERCHANT CIRCLE (Starfall Basin)

**Current Action:**
The Profiteer faction has been secretly negotiating with a Lumencrest-adjacent buyer for shard access to the Endless Eye's outer deposits. Not the core — the smaller fragments in the accessible perimeter. They believe this is meaningless, the Traditionalists are overreacting, and they could be very wealthy. The negotiation is three meetings from conclusion.

**Next Move (if unopposed, 6–8 weeks):**
The deal closes. The Profiteers' buyer is not actually Lumencrest — it's a Veilmarkets broker working for an unknown third party. The shards begin moving. The Traditionalists discover the negotiation (via a leak from the Pragmatist faction, who are angry they weren't included). The Merchant Circle's three-way internal tension becomes a three-way crisis. The Somnarchs' ability to maintain the Eye's stability depends partly on the perimeter shards being undisturbed. They start disappearing.

**Who Suffers:**
The Starfall Basin communities experiencing dream-bleed — which gets worse as perimeter shards are removed. The Merchant Circle's institutional legitimacy, which has kept the Eye protected for two centuries. The unknown third party's identity — when it's eventually traced — will be worse than Lumencrest.
