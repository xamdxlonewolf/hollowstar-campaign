# THE TRUTH — GM Only

> *This document is the load-bearing foundation of the entire setting. Nothing in it needs to be shared with players. Everything in it should inform every ruling, every NPC motivation, every moment of wonder or dread at the table.*

---

## THE DREAMER

### What It Is

The world is alive. Not metaphorically — not in the way that people say "the forest lives" or "the sea has moods." The world is a single, vast, sleeping consciousness. It has no name anyone knows. It has no face. It has no temple. It predates language, predates mortals, predates the continent itself.

The Dreamer was the raw creative force that shaped this world into existence — landmasses, oceans, the first stirrings of life, the weave of magic. Then something broke it. Something inflicted a wound so deep that the only way to survive was to stop. To sleep. To dream, rather than act.

It has been sleeping ever since.

**The Dreamer's form is the world itself.** Mountains are the ridges of its brow. Oceans rise and fall with its breathing. Ley lines are the neural pathways of its dreaming mind. The warmth in the Frostwell Ember Cradle is the pulse of something enormous, far below. The Isle of the Broken Sphere's fault line is the Dreamer's flesh cracking under the pressure of a shifting dream. The Glass Flats are a fossilized nightmare — a thought so violent it fused the sand into glass when it erupted, centuries ago.

This is not poetry. This is what is actually happening.

---

### The Hollowstars

The Hollowstars are calcified thoughts.

When the Dreamer enters a particularly vivid or turbulent dream phase, certain thoughts crystallize — become dense and self-contained, coherent enough to sustain themselves. They rise. They fall back when the dream shifts. They are not sent. They are not aimed. They simply *happen*, the way a sleeping person mutters a word they will not remember.

**What determines the impact:**

- **Intact landing (coherent thought):** A lucid, self-sustaining thought embeds in the earth and continues *thinking* in place. It becomes a localized law of reality drawn from the Dreamer's subconscious. Lumencrest's Heart is a thought of *relentless motion and invention* — electricity as pure kinetic dream-logic. The Endless Eye is a serene thought of *sanctuary and nurturing growth* — barren stone transformed into living water and green. These cores are not power sources. They are *ideas*, still dreaming.

- **Explosive landing (fragmented or nightmare thought):** An incoherent or nightmare-tinged thought shatters on impact. The shards are jagged pieces of fractured dream — powerful because they are literally pieces of the world's mind, dangerous because they carry the madness of a thought that couldn't hold itself together.

- **Deep embedding (core memory thought):** Rarely, a thought of extraordinary age and density descends slowly, sinks deep, and does not shatter. These are the oldest fragments — not ideas but *memories*. The Frostwell Ember Cradle is one. Whatever lies beneath the Glass Cathedral is another. The third is unknown. Disturbing a core memory risks stirring the Dreamer directly.

**Why the long silences between falls:**
The Dreamer cycles through sleep phases. The Starfall Era (~700 years ago) coincided with feverish, thrashing dreams — the wound flaring, the mind churning. The current 5-year gap is a deep, stable phase. Few thoughts are crystallizing. The world is quieter than it has been in centuries.

This quiet is either the mind healing — or sinking toward something final.

---

### The Wound

No one knows what caused it. The Dreamer does not know what caused it. The wound predates memory, predates record, predates anything that has ever lived on this world.

It still festers. In the deep ocean trenches no vessel has reached. In the polar voids where no settlement survives. Somewhere beneath the wildest, most geologically unstable ground on the continent.

The wound leaks. That leaking is what triggers crystallization — the pulses that cause thoughts to harden and rise. Without the wound, there would be no Hollowstars at all.

**This is deliberately unanswerable.** If players pursue it to the end of the world, they find evidence of something — a scar, an absence, a void-shaped memory — but no origin. The wound is the setting's irreducible mystery. It exists. That is all.

---

### Awareness

The Dreamer has no awareness of the surface world in any meaningful sense.

Mortals are not figments. They are not thoughts. They are more like the microorganisms that live in a sleeping person's gut — real, alive, consequential to each other, entirely outside the awareness of the body they inhabit.

**The exception:** When something disturbs a core — when a thought is mined too aggressively, when a core memory is cracked or disrupted — the Dreamer *twitches*. Not with intention. Not with judgment. The way a sleeper reacts to pain without waking. This twitch manifests as:

- Prophetic dreams sweeping populations near the disturbance
- Sudden magical surges or voids
- The landscape physically shifting — stone moving, water reversing, deep heat rising
- The sensation, reported by every sensitive or shard-exposed person in the region, of *being watched by something that cannot see*

The Isle of the Broken Sphere is the current most visible example. The Sleeper — the island's name for what is happening — is the Dreamer twitching in its sleep because the approaching Hollostar is resonating with something beneath the fault line.

## THE CYCLE

### What It Is

The cycle — civilizations rise, Hollowstars fall, things reset — is not designed. It is not enforced. It is not a test or a punishment or a gift.

It is weather.

The Dreamer's sleep has phases. Stable phases produce calm, long periods of growth. Turbulent phases produce falls, disruption, collapse. Civilizations that build during stable phases are shattered during turbulent ones. The rubble becomes the foundation for the next stable phase. No one planned this. No one is watching. It simply happens, the way seasons happen.

**This is the most important thing a GM needs to know about this setting:** There is no cosmic justice here. There is no arc toward redemption or punishment. The world does not care about the people living on it. The people living on it have to care about each other, because nothing else will.

---

### Can It Be Changed?

Yes. The cycle can be broken. There is exactly one way: wake the Dreamer.

If the Dreamer wakes — truly, fully, returns to consciousness after its immeasurable sleep — the cycle ends. Hollowstars stop. The wound either heals or kills. The Dreamer's active consciousness reshapes reality in ways no one can predict.

**What happens if the Dreamer wakes:**
Unknown. Genuinely unknown, even to this document. The Somnarchs believe mortals are thoughts in the Dreamer's mind and that waking it would either make them real in some new way or erase them entirely. The mad arcanists who have glimpsed the Dreamer's mindscape believe waking it would be the most beautiful and most terrible thing that has ever happened. Both may be right. Neither has been tested.

**What is known:** Waking the Dreamer requires disturbing all three core memory thoughts simultaneously, or cracking the wound itself. Neither is achievable by accident. Both would require deliberate, massive, coordinated effort by people who understood what they were doing.

No one currently alive understands what they are doing.

## THE CURRENT MOMENT — WHY EVERYTHING IS HAPPENING NOW

The approaching Hollostar is a coherent thought — one of the largest and most vivid ever recorded by the Starfall Seekers' instruments. It is descending during a deep-sleep phase, which means the usual buffering of turbulent dreams is not present. The impact, when it comes, will land against a relatively still mind.

A vivid thought landing in a still mind hits harder.

The ancient sites — the Ember Cradle, the Verdant Crescent's waking valley, the Isle's fault line, the Glittering Marshes' shards — are responding because they are all, at some level, parts of the same sleeping mind. They feel the approaching thought the way a dreamer feels the approach of a sound in the waking world — not heard yet, but already changing the dream.

Elder Tess's continental-connection theory is correct. She does not know why it is correct, but the reasoning that led her there is sound.

**What happens at impact (the GM's choice — not the setting's answer):**
The document does not prescribe the outcome. Three options, each internally consistent:

1. **Vivid landing:** The thought embeds intact. The continent gains a fourth working core — one of enormous power and total unpredictability. What does the Dreamer's current dream think about? What localized law of reality does it instantiate?

2. **Shatter:** The thought fragments on the existing cores' resonance field. Shards across the Frostwell Tundra and adjacent regions. Devastation. The most survivors in absolute terms, the most immediate suffering.

3. **Near miss:** The thought passes close enough to resonate but does not land. The resonance disrupts the existing cores — Lumencrest's Heart dims to nothing, the Endless Eye's sanctuary contracts, the Ember Cradle cracks open. The Dreamer twitches hard. Widespread prophetic episodes. Then quiet. Then the question of what comes next.

## THE SOMNARCHS

An ancient order, currently based at the Endless Eye's periphery. Small — never more than a few dozen at full strength. They know more about the Dreamer than anyone else alive, and what they know has made them deeply, permanently afraid.

**What they know:**
- The world is the Dreamer's body and the Dreamer is the world's mind
- The Hollowstars are thoughts, not weapons or gifts
- The cycle is indifferent, not designed
- Waking the Dreamer is possible and is the only way to end the cycle
- They do not know what waking the Dreamer would do

**What they do:**
Subtle maintenance. Rituals performed near the cores — chanting in a language assembled from fragments of the pre-sleep world, crystal arrays arranged to mirror neural patterns, careful monitoring of shard exposure in populations near the cores. They are not trying to prevent the Hollostar impact. They are trying to ensure the mind stays asleep through it.

**Their core fear:** Not death. Not the impact. The Somnarchs fear that mortals, faced with the truth about what they are living on, will try to wake the Dreamer on purpose. They have seen what happens to people who glimpse the Dreamer's mindscape. They believe waking it would end everything, not transform it.

**Their current problem:** The approaching Hollostar's resonance is making the cores easier to find. People who have never heard of the Dreamer are starting to dream its dreams. The Somnarchs' quiet maintenance is no longer sufficient. They need help — specifically, they need people who can be trusted with the truth and who will use it to protect rather than exploit.

They have never successfully found such people. They will try again.

## THE SHARD-EXPOSED — WHAT PROLONGED CONTACT DOES

Shard exposure in small doses: magical sensitivity, occasional vivid dreams, mild prophetic flashes. Harmless.

Prolonged or intense exposure: The person begins to resonate with the fragment's original dream-thought. They start to *think like the Dreamer thinks* — which sounds profound and is, in practice, deeply destabilizing. Human minds are not built for the scale.

**Stages:**
1. **Inspiration:** Sudden creative or intellectual insights. Solving problems they couldn't before. Feeling that everything is connected. (Players may seek this out.)
2. **Sensitivity:** Sleep becomes vivid, sometimes unbearable. They pick up emotional resonance from people and places. Crowded cities become overwhelming.
3. **Bleeding:** Their thoughts begin to affect their immediate environment subtly — dreams of rain make damp patches, thoughts of warmth make candles flicker. They are not doing this consciously.
4. **Dissolution:** The boundary between their individual consciousness and the shard's dream-logic begins to dissolve. They speak in fragments of the Dreamer's language. They are not in pain. They are in a state of vast, impersonal peace that is effectively the end of who they were.

There is no known reversal past stage 2. Stage 3 is treatable with removal from shard exposure and time. The Somnarchs know a ritual that can help with early stage 4 — they have used it twice and consider it an act of mercy each time.

## WHAT THE FACTIONS DON'T KNOW (AND WHAT THEY'VE GUESSED)

| Faction | What They Know | What They've Guessed Wrong |
|---------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Starfall Seekers | Hollowstars have measurable properties, three types of landing, pattern of falls is non-random | They believe the Hollowstars are a natural astronomical phenomenon. Elira Swiftpaw's unpublished hypothesis — that the pattern suggests intentionality — is correct but she cannot explain why and has not published it. Nobody in the network has a framework for the Dreamer. |
| Lumencrest Council | The Heart is a power source that is fading | They believe it can be replaced with another Hollostar. They don't know that the Heart is a *thought* and that replacing it requires another compatible thought to land. |
| Somnarchs | Nearly the full truth — but publicly dismissed as fringe mystics with an unprovable belief system | They believe waking the Dreamer means annihilation. This may be wrong. Their public face presents this as belief/claim, not demonstrated knowledge — which is why nobody takes them seriously enough to investigate. |
| Green Covenant | The forest waking is connected to the Hollostar | They don't know the forest is responding to the Dreamer's shift in sleep state. They think it's ecological, not neurological. |
| Galapa (Isle) | The Sleeper is waking | They are the closest to the truth about the Isle. They don't know it's true of the entire world. |
| Elder Tess | Continental connection is real | She doesn't know why. Her instinct is correct. Her explanation would be wrong in detail and right in shape. |
| Mad arcanists (Lumencrest undercity) | Glimpses of the mindscape. Something vast below. | They believe they can *communicate* with the Dreamer. They cannot. What they're receiving is dream-bleed, not message. |

## THE HIDDEN THIRD CORE

Two cores are known: Lumencrest's Heart (motion/invention), the Endless Eye (sanctuary/growth).

The third core exists. Its location is for the GM to place, or to leave as a mystery that drives a campaign arc. Some options:

- Beneath the Glass Cathedral in the Glass Flats (the one the expedition leader wouldn't describe)
- In the polar void referenced by the Greywall Dwarves' sealed records
- Under the deepest point of the Glittering Marshes — not the shards, but something below the shards
- Beneath the Thornwood's northern ruins, which the Steelwatch patrol wouldn't describe in their classified report

What the third core *thinks about* — what dream it instantiates — is also for the GM. It should feel like a discovery, not a reveal. The players should be able to guess it from context before they confirm it.

## FINAL NOTES FOR THE GM

The setting's central moral question — one no faction has articulated cleanly — is:

**Is it right to mine the mind of something that cannot consent, even if that something does not know you exist?**

Lumencrest has been doing this for three centuries. The Starfall Seekers have built their entire knowledge base on it. The Glittering Marshes have been enriched by it. The Verdant Crescent's forest grew rich on the Dreamer's dream-nutrients without knowing it.

The players will eventually learn what they are living on. What they do with that knowledge is the campaign.

There is no right answer in the setting. The Dreamer is not a victim in any way it experiences. But the relationship between mortal civilization and the sleeping mind they are built on is, at its root, one of extraction without awareness. Whether that matters — whether it should matter — is the question underneath every other question in this world.

The wound remains unanswered. Keep it that way.
