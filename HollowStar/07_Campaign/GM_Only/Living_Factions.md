# LIVING FACTIONS — GM Only

> *The clocks tell you what happens. This document tells you who these people are on a Wednesday. Use it when the party goes somewhere unexpected, when they ignore a faction entirely, when they want to have a drink with someone powerful. The world doesn't pause.*

---

# COUNCIL OF LUMENS / LUMENCREST
## *Priority 1 — Party likely encounters early*

---

### THE TEXTURE OF LUMENCREST RIGHT NOW

Walking into Lumencrest today, you notice three things before anyone says a word:

The Radiant Core blazes. Council buildings, wealthy estates, the university — fully lit, warm, exactly as advertised. It looks like the city is fine.

The Lamplight Ward flickers. Three streets are dark. Not broken — officially decommissioned. Residents use candles and lanterns now, which they haven't needed in living memory. There's a particular bitterness to people who had something and lost it versus people who never had it at all.

The Manufactories are quiet two hours early. The power cutoff at 6pm means forges go cold before workers finish. You see people leaving mid-task, covering work they'll resume tomorrow, if the rationing schedule holds.

Nobody on the Council admits this is a crisis. Everyone on the street knows it is.

---

### THE CONSERVATORS — DAY TO DAY

**This week:** Three closed-door sessions reviewing the Heart's latest readings. The data showing accelerating decline is being passed between four people who have agreed not to put it in writing. One of those four — a senior clerk named Daveth Crale — took the readings home last night. He hasn't decided what to do yet but he didn't leave them in the Vault.

**Internal tension:** The Conservators are not unified. The old guard (Councillor Presta Ironfall, 71) genuinely believes managed decline is the right path — she has done the math and concluded there is no alternative. The younger faction (led by Councillor Bram Tessler, 48) is beginning to suspect the old guard knows something they aren't sharing. Tessler has started asking questions Ironfall answers too smoothly.

**What they're afraid of:** That when the truth comes out — not if, when — the city panics. Lumencrest has no history of panic. Nobody knows what that looks like here, and Ironfall has nightmares about finding out. Her second fear, smaller and more personal: that she's wrong about managed decline being the only path, and she sealed that answer away too early.

**If the party ignores them:** Daveth Crale eventually gives the accelerating decline data to an Illuminist contact. The Conservators discover the leak, Crale disappears into a quiet administrative reassignment, and the data surfaces anyway — but six weeks later than it could have, and without the context Crale would have provided.

**If the party engages:** Crale is the door. He's not looking for heroes. He's looking for someone he can hand a problem to and stop carrying it himself. He will not approach the party directly — he'll be somewhere public, doing something ordinary, and if the party is paying attention they'll notice a man who looks like he hasn't slept in two weeks.

---

### THE ILLUMINISTS — DAY TO DAY

**This week:** Researcher Yenna Ashford and her colleague Pell Vane have been in the restricted Vault section for four days on the technicality that expires in three more. They're not sleeping much. The contraction reading — the Heart intensifying at the center while fading at the edges — doesn't fit any model they have. Yenna has written three different explanations and rejected all of them. Pell thinks she's overthinking it. Pell is wrong.

**Internal tension:** The Illuminists are united on *what* they want (more access, more research) but fracturing on *how far to push*. Faction leader Councillor Orin Marsh wants to force a full Council confrontation over Vault access — he thinks the political moment is right. His deputy, Scholar-Delegate Tira Wend, thinks Marsh is about to get them all fired and wants to work quietly for another six months first. They had a genuine argument about this yesterday in a corridor. People heard.

**What they're afraid of:** That they're studying the wrong problem. The shard research, the replacement theory, the expedition to the Endless Eye — all of it assumes the Heart is a resource that can be swapped out. Yenna's contraction data is the first thing that suggests it might be something fundamentally different. If they're wrong about the basic nature of what they're dealing with, all their solutions are wrong too.

**If the party ignores them:** The Conservators shut down Vault access in three months. Yenna's data gets sealed. The contraction continues. Nobody outside the Vault knows what it means until the Heart does something visible and undeniable.

**If the party engages:** Yenna is the most valuable contact in Lumencrest for a party that knows about the Dreamer. She doesn't know what she's found — she just knows her data is *wrong* in a way that means something. A party that can give her framework will get a brilliant researcher who will chase the truth wherever it goes, including places that terrify her.

---

### THE EXPANSIONISTS — DAY TO DAY

**This week:** Councillor Maren Vex is drafting the second ship's authorization papers before the first ship returns. She is moving fast because she knows the full Council will object when they find out about the first ship, and she wants two ships' worth of investment committed before the argument happens. Her aide has been working eighteen-hour days. The aide is starting to ask questions Vex deflects.

**Internal tension:** The Expansionists have a goal everyone agrees on (find a new power source) but a growing disagreement about methods. The older military families want a hard play — formal expedition, back it with force if needed. The merchant-prince wing (led by Lord Fenwick Dall) wants leverage and trade before force — he thinks the Isle and the Merchant Circle can both be negotiated into compliance if Lumencrest stops leading with demands. Vex is navigating between them, currently siding with the military families because they have more votes.

**What they're afraid of:** That the Heart fails before they find a replacement. Vex runs the math privately: if the decline continues and nothing is found, Lumencrest loses power in four years. Not dimming — *gone*. The city's economy, its military advantage, its identity all depend on the light staying on. She has contingency plans. She doesn't like any of them. She sleeps four hours a night.

**If the party ignores them:** *The Meridian Survey* returns, Vex presents the data, the second ship launches. The Isle situation escalates on the Lumencrest end regardless of what the party does on the Isle end. At some point these two pressures collide.

**If the party engages:** Vex is not a villain. She's a person making desperate choices under real time pressure. She will work *with* a party that can offer an alternative to the Isle — something, anything, that gives her a card to play. She will work *against* them if they interfere without offering anything. She respects results over ideology.

# STARFALL SEEKERS
## *Priority 2*

---

### THE TEXTURE OF THE SEEKERS RIGHT NOW

The Seekers are not an organization with a headquarters and a hierarchy. They're a network — scholars who share findings, treasure hunters who share route information, zealots who share prophecies. They disagree about almost everything except that the approaching Hollostar matters enormously.

The Frostwell expedition has made them temporarily more coherent than usual. Expedition Leader Tam Coldridge is the closest thing to a central authority the Seekers have ever had, and only because she's the one with the instruments and the data.

---

### TAM COLDRIDGE — DAY TO DAY

**This week:** Coldridge is three days from finalizing the preliminary report that will go out to every institution on the continent. She has been staring at one section of it for two days. The resonance data — the Hollostar responding to ground-level anomalies — is real, reproducible, and completely inexplicable under current theory. She can publish it accurately (and look like she doesn't understand her own findings) or she can soften it (and lose the detail that might matter most). She hasn't decided.

**Internal tension:** The expedition has three factions. Coldridge and the serious researchers want rigorous publication and controlled follow-up. The treasure hunters (six of them, nominally on staff) want to know where the impact zone is so they can stake claims. The zealot contingent (two people, extremely loud) believe the approaching Hollostar is a divine event and are making everyone else uncomfortable. Coldridge manages this through force of personality and the fact that she controls instrument access.

**What she's afraid of:** Being right about the resonance data and having no framework to explain it. Coldridge is a rigorous empiricist — she has spent her career building credibility, and publishing findings she can't explain feels like handing enemies ammunition. Her deeper fear, the one she doesn't say out loud: the approaching Hollostar is bigger and more coherent than anything in the records, it's responding to the ground in a way nothing should, and she doesn't know what that means at impact.

**If the party ignores her:** The preliminary report publishes, the resonance data is softened to a footnote, everyone focuses on the trajectory and impact zone numbers. The most important data point disappears into a minor observation that future scholars will spend years re-finding.

**If the party engages:** Coldridge needs people who can go *into* the ancient sites she can only observe from outside. She'll trade information — freely, accurately, more than most people get from the Seekers — for reliable reports from inside the Ember Cradle, the waking valley, the Glittering Marshes. She is not sentimental. She will tell the party uncomfortable things without softening them. She is one of the best contacts available if the party wants to understand what's coming.

---

### THE SEEKER NETWORK — DAY TO DAY

**This week across the network:**
- A Seeker crew in the Glass Flats found a shard cluster and is arguing about whether to sell it or report it. They'll sell it. They always sell it.
- A scholar in Starfall Basin published a theory that the Hollostar's coherence suggests it's a "directed thought" — not aimed by anyone, but self-organizing toward something. She's being mocked by colleagues. She is not wrong.
- Two Seekers in Lumencrest are in a bidding war with a Veilmarkets agent over a piece of the original Heart core samples. The Veilmarkets agent will win.

**What the network is afraid of:** Being scooped. Every Seeker believes they're the one who will crack the Hollostar mystery. This makes them simultaneously the best-informed people on the continent and structurally unable to coordinate when coordination matters.

**If the party ignores the Seekers generally:** The preliminary report causes a cascade of institutional panic with no central coordination. Every powerful faction acts in its own interest simultaneously. The window for organized response closes.

**If the party engages the network broadly:** They gain access to scattered pieces of real information — some accurate, some wrong, some accurate-but-misinterpreted. The challenge is synthesis. The Seekers have the data. They don't have the framework. The party, if they know about the Dreamer, has the framework but not all the data. This is the natural relationship.

# THE GREEN COVENANT
## *Priority 3*

---

### THE TEXTURE OF THE COVENANT RIGHT NOW

The Green Covenant's public face is composed and authoritative. Internally, it is the most stressed it has been in Maren Stillwater's twelve-year tenure.

Three things are pulling at the Covenant simultaneously: the ancient waking that nobody outside the inner circle understands yet, the Lumencrest timber pressure that Maren knows is worse than her public position acknowledges, and the Warden of the Reach doing something unauthorized that Maren can't prove yet but can smell.

Maren is good at carrying weight without showing it. She is carrying a lot right now.

---

### MAREN STILLWATER — DAY TO DAY

**This week:** An emergency private session with the Five Wardens, ostensibly about the Lumencrest timber situation. Maren has told the inner circle about the ancient waking's acceleration. She has not told them about the 2,000-year records and what they say — *settlements that ceased to exist, simply incorporated* — because she is still deciding whether that information helps or paralyzes. She is also quietly trying to determine if Warden Dusk has been outside the Verdant Crescent recently without authorization. The travel records are ambiguous.

**Internal tension:** The Covenant's Five Wardens are unified on mission but fracturing on method. Warden Syl of the Edge is patient and political — she wants to absorb the edge settlement problem slowly. Warden Chit of Memory is an absolutist — the 23 families with Lumencrest ties have broken faith and should be cut. Warden Dusk of the Reach is the wildcard nobody has read correctly yet. The other two Wardens are watching the first three and making no enemies.

**What she's afraid of:** Two things at once, pulling in opposite directions. First: the ancient waking does something visible before she understands it, and the Covenant has to respond to a crisis it wasn't prepared for. Second: she understands it, and the answer is that there's nothing she can do — that the forest is going to do what it's going to do and the Covenant's role is to witness, not prevent. Maren finds the second fear harder to sit with than the first.

**If the party ignores the Covenant:** Warden Dusk's unauthorized alliance produces a Lumencrest team inside the Verdant Crescent. The Covenant fractures internally. The ancient waking accelerates without anyone monitoring it who has the full picture. The forest starts doing things that frighten people who live near it.

**If the party engages:** Maren will not trust the party immediately — the Covenant doesn't trust anyone immediately. But she is actively looking for people who can go into the ancient waking site and come back with accurate information. She has sent two teams already. The first came back unsettled and unable to explain what they experienced. The second didn't come back. She hasn't reported the second team missing yet.

---

### WARDEN DUSK — DAY TO DAY

**This week:** Dusk has had three meetings with a Lumencrest anti-Expansionist contact in the past month, always outside the Verdant Crescent, always framed as "informal intelligence exchange." The contact is Councillor Mira Fenn — an Illuminist who has been feeding Dusk information about the Expansionists' operations in exchange for forest intelligence. Dusk believes this is a fair trade. It is not — Fenn is sharing information she's authorized to share; Dusk is sharing information Maren is not.

Dusk is not a traitor. They genuinely believe the Expansionists are the larger threat and that Maren is too slow. They are probably right about the Expansionists being dangerous. They are definitely wrong that the ends justify the method.

**If the party encounters Dusk:** They meet someone who seems like exactly the kind of Covenant member the party wants — pragmatic, well-informed, willing to act. The problem only becomes visible when the party realizes Dusk's "pragmatism" is going to blow up the institution they're operating inside.

# VOSS — THE TIDE KING
## *Priority 4*

---

### THE TEXTURE OF VOSS'S OPERATION RIGHT NOW

Voss doesn't have an office. He has three ships, a permanent room at the Salt Quarter's most expensive inn, and a network of people across every port in his waters who know that certain information goes to certain contacts who pass it upward. The system works because Voss built it to work without him present — he is the apex, not the center.

This week he is at sea, personally, on the quieter of his two working ships. He takes sea trips when he needs to think. He has several things to think about.

---

### VOSS — DAY TO DAY

**This week:** On the water, between Brinehaven and the outer Kept Islands, reviewing the compiled Grey Sails overlap report his people assembled. Three ships. Eleven cargo manifests cross-referenced against his protection records. The math is clear: the Grey Sails have hit his interests four times, not three. The extra incident was a small one — barely worth noting alone. With the others, it establishes a pattern.

He is also thinking about Saltmere. Not angrily — Voss doesn't do anger in the way other powerful people do. He is thinking about what Saltmere's defection reveals about his own operation: that he has let the expansion move faster than the consolidation, and some merchants don't yet believe his protection is real because they haven't seen it tested. That will be corrected.

**Internal tension:** Voss's operation has a structural tension he has managed for fifteen years but never resolved — the people who work for him in the enforcement side and the people who work for him in the trade side have fundamentally different views of what he's building. The enforcement side (led by his First Deputy, a Ribbet named Sable Crin) want to move harder and faster, establish clear dominance before the Silver Belt gets complicated. The trade side (led by his factor in Brinehaven, a human woman named Dorra Mast) think the whole operation lives or dies on merchants trusting Voss more than they fear him, and hard moves destroy that trust.

Voss agrees with both of them. This is not a comfortable position to be in.

**What he's afraid of:** Not the Grey Sails specifically, and not Saltmere specifically. What Voss fears is the convergence — a moment when the Grey Sails situation and the Saltmere situation and the Hollostar panic all land at once, when the Sea stops being predictable and the protection arrangement he's built becomes impossible to honor. He has spent fifteen years making himself indispensable. What happens to indispensable things when the world breaks?

**If the party ignores him:** The Grey Sails start losing ships. Saltmere's trade advantage quietly erodes. Voss's Silver Belt protection cut quietly expands to 50% of merchant traffic. None of this is visible in any single moment — it's weather, not event.

**If the party engages:** Voss will work with the party if the party can do something useful he can't do himself. He is almost impossible to con — he has seen every con, usually from the inside. He is straightforward about what he wants and what he'll pay. He is genuinely fair in disputes and will honor agreements to the letter. He does not do favors without return, but he is not unkind about it.

The best way into Voss's orbit is through Dorra Mast — she is easier to reach, she is the one who can vouch for the party, and she is quietly looking for people who can solve problems that Sable Crin's enforcement methods can't.

# THE FOUR HOUSES / SILVERBELT
## *Priority 5 — Later sessions*

---

### THE TEXTURE OF THE FOUR HOUSES RIGHT NOW

The Council of Banners hasn't met in full session in three months. This is unprecedented. The four Houses are not at war — they are in the particular political state that precedes war, where everyone is maneuvering aggressively but nobody wants to be the one who fired first.

The Caldren succession crisis is the visible problem. The real problem is that the crisis has revealed how little the Houses actually trust each other, and that trust was already thin.

---

### HOUSE BY HOUSE — THIS WEEK

**House Vethara (Admiral-Lord Cassius):** Cassius has received three private intelligence reports this week. One confirms that his Caldren heir candidate (a distant cousin named Petra Caldren, 22, currently deeply in Vethara financial debt) will accept the arrangement. One reveals that House Mourne has been meeting with House Aldric without telling him. One is from his daughter, Captain Rynn Saltblood, who is not reporting something she should be. Cassius is dealing with these in reverse order of importance, which means he is dealing with them in the wrong order.

**House Mourne (Lady Serafine, 55):** Serafine is the most patient person in this conflict, which is not a compliment — her patience sometimes looks like inaction when action is needed. This week she is finalizing the terms of the Mourne-Aldric understanding. It is not a formal alliance (those require Council ratification). It is an "arrangement of mutual interest" which everyone will recognize as an alliance and nobody can technically object to.

**House Aldric (Lord Brennan, 44):** Brennan is the wild card because he genuinely likes people in a way the other House leaders don't. He has real friendships with Coral community leaders, with merchants in his territory, with two members of the Mourne family. He is entering the Mourne arrangement partly because Serafine asked him and partly because he thinks it's the right move, and partly because he thinks Cassius Vethara's plan for Caldren is bad for the region. He might be wrong about this last part. He is probably right.

**House Caldren (Acting Steward Owin, 39):** Owin is barely holding on. He knows what Vethara is doing with Petra. He knows that accepting the arrangement means House Caldren survives but stops being independent. He knows that refusing means the succession crisis drags on until the other Houses force a resolution that's worse. He has not slept a full night in six weeks. He is hoping someone will come up with a third option that he hasn't been able to find.

**What all four Houses are afraid of:** The Grey Sails problem getting big enough that outside intervention — from Lumencrest, from Voss — gives either of those powers leverage over Silverbelt waters. None of the Houses would say this out loud. It shapes every decision they make.

**If the party ignores the Houses:** The Vethara-controlled Caldren heir gets installed. The cold split formalizes. The Grey Sails expand into the gap. Voss quietly picks up the protection contracts that House naval patrols can no longer reliably cover.

**If the party engages:** Owin Caldren is the easiest entry point — he needs help and has nothing left to lose by asking for it. Captain Rynn Saltblood is the most interesting long-term contact — she knows things nobody else knows and is carrying them alone. Lord Brennan Aldric is the most naturally sympathetic House leader and will be genuinely helpful to a party he decides to trust.

# QUICK-REFERENCE: FEAR MATRIX

*When you need to know instantly what a faction leader is most afraid of — what makes them flinch, what they won't admit, what pressure actually moves them.*

| Leader | Public Fear | Real Fear | What Actually Moves Them |
|--------|------------|-----------|-------------------------|
| Councillor Ironfall (Conservators) | City panic | Being wrong too late to fix it | Evidence that managed decline isn't the only path |
| Councillor Marsh (Illuminists) | Political defeat | Research being permanently sealed | Access — give them access to anything |
| Councillor Vex (Expansionists) | Power failure in 4 years | Running out of time entirely | A credible alternative to the Isle |
| Tam Coldridge (Seekers) | Publishing wrong data | Understanding nothing | Data she can't explain — it compels her |
| Maren Stillwater (Covenant) | External threat to forest | The forest acting without her | Information about the ancient waking |
| Warden Dusk | Being too slow | Maren being right about patience | Being caught — after that, the truth |
| Voss | Operational disruption | The world breaking his system | Useful people who keep their word |
| Cassius Vethara | Losing Tidewall | His daughter | His daughter, specifically |
| Owin Caldren | House dissolution | There is no third option | Anyone who offers a third option |
