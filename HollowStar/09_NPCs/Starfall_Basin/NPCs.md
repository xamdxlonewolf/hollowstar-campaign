## MERCHANT CIRCLE COUNCIL (5)

### Senior Merchant Voss Tanakar
**Role:** Senior merchant seat, de facto Council leader  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 54  
**Appearance:** Heavy-set, well-dressed, rings on every finger, warm smile that doesn't always reach his eyes  
**Personality:** Pragmatist, charming, calculating — genuinely wants Endless Eye to survive but has made morally gray calls to get here  
**Goals:** Stabilize Council, solve lake crisis through careful research, keep Lumencrest out  
**Flaw:** Keeps too many secrets (thinks managing information = managing people)  
**Quote:** *"Every problem has a price. I prefer to find it before someone else does."*  
**Hook:** Employs party for sensitive tasks he can't trust to Circle Guards

---

### Elder Suu
**Role:** Senior Ribbet council seat, Traditionalist faction leader  
**Ancestry:** Ribbet, age 89  
**Appearance:** Small even for Ribbet, markings faded with age but still complex, moves slowly but deliberately  
**Personality:** Ancient, wise, deeply spiritual — not against knowledge but against urgency  
**Goals:** Protect The Deeps from exploitation, perform spiritual healing of star, preserve Ribbet culture through transition  
**Belief:** "The star is a living being. We commune with it. We do not study it."  
**Secret:** Has been communing with The Deeps for 40 years. Hears something now that wasn't there before.  
**Quote:** *"The lake has time. It is we who are impatient."*  
**Hook:** Hires party for ritual component gathering, desert spirit quests

---

### Merchant Brek
**Role:** Ribbet merchant seat, Profiteer faction  
**Ancestry:** Ribbet, age 31  
**Appearance:** Unusually well-dressed for Ribbet (surface fashion), bioluminescent markings deliberately dim (hides emotions)  
**Personality:** Cynical, opportunistic, not entirely heartless — believes civilization dies anyway, might as well profit  
**Goals:** Monetize Deeps access, build personal wealth before collapse  
**Secret:** Running black market Hollostar water sales; in communication with Lumencrest Expansionists  
**Weakness:** Actually loves the lake; the profiteering is partly denial  
**Quote:** *"Idealists will watch it sink holding their principles. I'll watch it from somewhere comfortable."*  
**Hook:** Hires party for smuggling, or party investigates his operations

### Voice Kessa

**Ancestry:** Ribbet, age 44  
**Appearance:** Mid-sized, markings active and expressive, always has data scrolls rolled under one arm  
**Personality:** Frustrated scientist-politician — wants action but must navigate tradition  
**Goals:** Expand authorized research, get real data public, save the lake through understanding  
**Conflict:** Respects Elder Suu deeply; disagrees with her approach  
**Quote:** *"Reverence and research aren't enemies. But right now, only one of them can save us."*

## FAIRY COMMUNITY (2)

### Wingspeaker Aelindra
**Role:** Fairy council seat (Traditionalist faction leader), voice of the surface Fairy community  
**Ancestry:** Fairy, age 156  
**Appearance:** Wingspan unusually broad for her height — deep amber-gold, with black veining that spreads when she's agitated or moved. Pale silver hair worn loose. Dresses in layered silks that move with her wings. The overall impression is of someone designed to be looked at, who has learned to use that to her advantage.  
**Personality:** Spiritual, principled, and genuinely warm — until she isn't. Aelindra is the kind of person who makes you feel like the most important person in the room when she's listening to you, and like an irrelevant footnote when she's decided you're not worth her time. She has very clear categories for which you are.  
**Background:** Born in the Endless Eye's Fairy community, trained as a Guiding Light denomination priest before transitioning to community leadership sixty years ago. She has watched the lake her entire life. She knows what it looked like when it was healthy. The knowledge sits in her differently than it sits in the people who are only working from data.  
**Goals:** Prevent the Hollostar from being treated as a resource rather than a relationship. Buy time for what she believes is the correct approach — deep spiritual engagement with the star's distress — to be tried seriously before the Pragmatists' research agenda becomes irreversible. Protect the Fairy community's position on the council as pressure from both Ribbet factions grows.  
**Flaw:** Has difficulty distinguishing between spiritual conviction and political stubbornness. When challenged, she moves to principle — which is sometimes the right call and sometimes the call that ends conversations that needed to keep going.  
**Secret:** She has been in private contact with Path of Spirits practitioners from the Verdant Crescent who believe the simultaneous waking of ancient sites is connected to Hollostar distress across the continent. She hasn't brought this to the council because it would sound like she's claiming the lake's decline is cosmically ordained — which is not what she believes, but she knows how it would land.  
**What she actually fears:** Not the lake failing. She has made peace with the possibility of loss. What she fears is that the Pragmatists are right that there's something actionable to be done, and that her position is causing the delay that makes the difference between saving it and not.  
**Quote:** *"You don't fix a relationship by dissecting it. You fix it by being present, honest, and patient enough to listen for the answer."*  
**Hook:** Will ask the party to undertake a specific ritual preparation — gathering components from three sites connected to the Endless Eye's founding — that she believes is necessary before any research can proceed safely. The task is real. So is her reason for needing people who aren't formally associated with the council.

### Thornwing
**Role:** Fairy council seat (Profiteer faction, loosely), pragmatic dissenter  
**Ancestry:** Fairy, age 78  
**Appearance:** Compact even for a Fairy, wings a deep blue-grey that he keeps folded tight in council sessions — a deliberate choice that makes him look smaller and less threatening than he is. Ink-stained fingers (he drafts his own contracts). Dresses like someone who can afford better and has decided not to bother.  
**Personality:** Blunt, impatient with ceremony, and genuinely funny in the way of someone who has decided honesty is more efficient than diplomacy. He is frequently the least comfortable person in a council session to be around and frequently the most correct. Neither quality has made him popular.  
**Background:** Third-generation Endless Eye merchant family. His grandmother helped negotiate the trade compact that gave Fairy merchants formal standing in the lake's surface commerce. He grew up with more money than most Fairy families in the region and has spent his adult life using it to understand how the city actually works underneath its official story.  
**Goals:** Force the council to confront the reality that the lake is declining and no current plan addresses it adequately. Establish a relationship with Lumencrest that gives the Endless Eye actual leverage rather than making it a dependency. He is not trying to sell the lake — he is trying to avoid the situation where Lumencrest takes it because the council spent too long arguing about how to ask nicely.  
**Flaw:** Believes he's the only person in the room who is being honest. Sometimes he's right. Sometimes this belief makes him dismiss legitimate concerns from people who are being equally honest and reaching different conclusions.  
**Secret:** He discovered three months ago that Merchant Brek is running unauthorized Hollostar water sales to outside buyers. He hasn't reported it. He is using it as leverage to keep Brek cooperative on a specific vote he needs. He knows this is exactly the kind of thing he criticizes the council for. He has decided it's justified. He is not entirely sure he's right about that.  
**His honest assessment:** He thinks the Traditionalists are wrong but not foolish — the spiritual relationship with the lake is real and matters. He thinks the Pragmatists are right but moving too slowly. He thinks the Profiteers (including himself, in the council's framing) are misunderstood — he doesn't want to exploit the lake, he wants the council to stop pretending they can manage this situation without Lumencrest-level resources, and work out terms rather than waiting to have terms imposed.  
**Quote:** *"I'm not the villain here. I'm the person pointing at the fire while everyone argues about whether it's hot."*  
**Hook:** Has a specific piece of intelligence about Lumencrest's actual intentions toward the Endless Eye — obtained through channels he won't fully explain — and needs someone to verify it independently before he decides what to do with it. He'll pay for verification. He'll pay more for discretion.

## SALTMERE (3)

### Grand Auctioneer Sable Marchant
**Role:** Runs The Block — Saltmere's famous auction house — and through it, effectively runs Saltmere itself  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 49  
**Appearance:** Always dressed for a performance she has not announced. The particular stillness of someone who has learned that presence is more useful than movement. Silver rings on both hands that she adjusts when she's thinking, which is always.  
**Personality:** Sharp, neutral, and in possession of more information about what people want than anyone in the Basin would be comfortable acknowledging she has. The neutrality is genuine — she does not take sides in Saltmere's political maneuvering. She does not need to. She sells access to information, and access to access, and everyone needs what she has.  
**Goals:** Expand Saltmere's commercial position while the Endless Eye's governance is distracted; remain the one figure in the Basin that all factions will deal with; identify whether the water situation is an opportunity or an existential threat to Saltmere's lake-adjacent economy  
**Her read on the situation:** Saltmere's prosperity depends on the lake. She has known about the water level decline for longer than the Merchant Circle has classified it — her network covers the basin floor workers who measure depth markers. She has not shared this. She is deciding what to sell it for.  
**Quote:** *"The Block sells everything. Everything has a price. The question is always whether the seller knows what they're selling."*  
**Hook:** The most dangerous neutral party in the Basin; she has the water data; what she wants in exchange for it is not obvious and not small

### Factor Brennan Ashport
**Role:** Saltmere's commercial representative in the Endless Eye — officially a trade liaison, functionally an intelligence operative  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 38  
**Appearance:** Forgettable by design — medium height, neutral presentation, the practiced ordinariness of a professional who has spent years ensuring he does not get remembered.  
**Personality:** Competent, methodical, and increasingly uncomfortable with the distance between what he was told his job would be and what it has become. He was recruited as a trade factor. He has been running intelligence operations for four years. He has not told anyone at home about the distinction.  
**Goals:** Complete his current assignment (identify the extent of the Merchant Circle's classified information); begin planning his exit; not become the person who is held accountable when this situation ends  
**His current situation:** He is the Lumencrest intelligence contact that Voss Tanakar suspects exists in the Endless Eye. He is not, actually, working for Lumencrest — he is working for Sable Marchant. The confusion is deliberate. He arranged it.  
**Quote:** *"I represent Saltmere's commercial interests. Everything I do serves that purpose. I am very good at my job."*  
**Hook:** The party can identify him as the supposed Lumencrest contact — which would be both right and wrong in ways that matter; his actual loyalty to Saltmere over Lumencrest makes him a potential asset if approached correctly

### The Dockmaster of Saltmere — Hura
**Role:** Runs Saltmere's harbor and lake access infrastructure; the city's operational backbone  
**Ancestry:** Ribbet, age 52  
**Appearance:** Unusually large for Ribbet, with the deliberate movements of someone who has learned to occupy space carefully in a world built for larger people. Markings complex and active — reads as perpetually alert.  
**Personality:** Efficient, direct, and possessed of the particular perspective of a Ribbet who chose to work in Saltmere's surface economy rather than the underwater community. She is aware this makes her unusual. She is not particularly interested in discussing it.  
**Goals:** Keep Saltmere's harbor running as the basin's commercial traffic becomes increasingly strange; maintain the unofficial communication channel she has with Ribbet elders at the Endless Eye; figure out what the change in lake current near Saltmere's southern dock means  
**The current change:** Two weeks ago, the water near Saltmere's southern dock started running differently — a subtle shift in direction and temperature that the human dock workers haven't noticed and that she has been tracking every day since. It is consistent with something changing at the lake's center. She has sent a message to a Ribbet elder at the Endless Eye. She has not received a reply.  
**Quote:** *"Saltmere thinks it runs on trade. Trade runs on water. I run on water. This is the actual chain of command."*  
**Hook:** The lake current change is an early signal of the Hollostar situation manifesting outside the Endless Eye; her Ribbet network connects the two cities; she will share what she knows with people who can explain what it means

## KATARI TRADE ROUTES (3)

### Dust-Captain Serin Varr
**Role:** Most respected active Katari trade captain on the Basin routes; has been running the desert roads for thirty years  
**Ancestry:** Katari, age 54  
**Appearance:** Desert-road worn in the specific way of someone who has made the same crossings enough times that the landscape has left marks. Tattoo documentation covering arms and shoulders — every significant route, every notable trade, a career written on skin.  
**Personality:** Unhurried, observant, and in possession of the Katari oral tradition's deep map of what the Basin looked like before and after the lake formed. Treats pattern recognition as a craft. Finds the current situation interesting in the way that people who have seen a lot of things find genuinely novel situations interesting.  
**Goals:** Complete the current trade season without incident; figure out what the unusual dust patterns northeast of the Endless Eye mean before they become a route hazard; get a specific piece of information to Dust-Chief Orah on the Merchant Circle without going through official channels  
**His observation:** For the past six weeks, the dust behavior in the desert northeast of the lake has been subtly wrong — not storm patterns, not wind patterns. Something is changing in the ground temperature in a section of the desert that has been stable for as long as his family's oral records go back. His grandfather mapped this route. His grandfather's route notes do not describe what he is seeing.  
**Quote:** *"The desert changes slowly. Slowly is not the same as never. Something is changing."*  
**Hook:** The northeastern desert anomaly connects to something the party may be investigating; his access to Dust-Chief Orah outside official channels is politically valuable; his oral map knowledge is irreplaceable

### Keeper Lissa of the Waystation
**Role:** Runs the most important waystation on the central basin route — three days from the Endless Eye, two days from Saltmere  
**Ancestry:** Katari-Human mixed, age 41  
**Appearance:** Her mother was Katari, her father was from the Heartlands, and she has built her identity around the waystation rather than either heritage. The waystation is both her home and her product.  
**Personality:** Hospitable in the professional sense — she runs a waystation because she is genuinely good at making people feel welcome and genuinely good at managing what they do and say in her establishment. She knows more about what is moving through the Basin than most people realize.  
**Goals:** Keep the waystation neutral and profitable; collect the information that passes through it without anyone noticing she is collecting it; pass urgent information to Dust-Captain Varr when it warrants it  
**What's passing through:** In the last month, an unusual number of non-Katari travelers have been using the desert routes rather than the more comfortable lake road. They are not explaining why. She has her own theory — the lake road has become watched in ways these travelers want to avoid. She has not shared this theory with anyone yet.  
**Quote:** *"Everyone who passes through here has somewhere to go and something to carry. I make both easier. That's all."*  
**Hook:** The waystation as intelligence hub; the unusual desert route traffic is a signal worth reading; she has pieced together more of the Basin's current situation than any single faction has

### The Cartographer — Messen
**Role:** Semi-retired Katari route-mapper; lives in the Endless Eye; the oral tradition's living archive for Basin geography  
**Ancestry:** Katari, age 71  
**Appearance:** Moving carefully now — the joints from decades of desert work make themselves known. Fingers still precise. Has more maps in his head than on paper, by design.  
**Personality:** Direct, occasionally brusque, genuinely interested in people who ask good questions. Has spent fifty years learning what the Basin looked like at every point in the past century. Is aware that this knowledge is more valuable now than it has ever been and is uncertain what to do about that.  
**Goals:** Document what he knows before he can't; identify the right person to continue the oral tradition; answer the question that has been bothering him for two years  
**The question:** His maps include records from before the lake level drop. He has been comparing the current level to historical records and has calculated something that the Merchant Circle has classified and that he arrived at independently. His number matches Voice Kessa's data. He has told nobody. He is not sure who to tell.  
**Quote:** *"The lake was here before us. It will be here after. The question is what 'here' means."*  
**Hook:** Independent confirmation of the lake decline data; his maps contain historical information about the Basin that no official record preserves; the right person to continue his tradition might be in the party

## DESERT WANDERERS (2)

### The Ironbell Merchant — Carev Dust
**Role:** Independent trader who operates the route between the Endless Eye and the Desert of Glass; one of very few people who regularly crosses the full Basin desert  
**Ancestry:** Infernis, age 33  
**Appearance:** The heat adaptation of Infernis combined with desert road experience produces someone who appears comfortable in conditions that are actively dangerous for most. Small horns, dark complexion, carries more water than anyone who looks at him expects given his apparent comfort.  
**Personality:** Cheerful in a way that suggests genuine enjoyment of a life that most people would find difficult. Collects unusual objects and unusual information with equal enthusiasm and without distinguishing between them.  
**Goals:** Complete the current route circuit; sell the specific item he acquired at the Desert of Glass end that he does not fully understand; find out what is happening at the lake that is making the Endless Eye people nervous in ways they are not explaining  
**The item:** A small glass object, clearly volcanic in origin but with an internal structure that does not match any obsidian variety he knows. He acquired it from a Drakona who had found it near the Skyspine. He has shown it to three people. All three had different reactions. None of them explained why.  
**Quote:** *"Strange things happen at the edges of the world. The edges are my favorite part."*  
**Hook:** The glass object is a thread to the Desert of Glass situation; his cross-basin route knowledge is logistically essential; he is reliably present in the Endless Eye once every six weeks

### The Listener — Ore
**Role:** Desert hermit, former Merchant Circle researcher, voluntary exile who knows things she has chosen not to share  
**Ancestry:** Elf, age 203  
**Appearance:** Two decades of desert living have changed her in ways that mark her as something other than the academic she was. She has stopped maintaining the particular elvish composure that she grew up with. She looks like the desert looks — weathered, patient, and not especially concerned with what others make of her.  
**Personality:** Not unfriendly — just operating on a different timeline than everyone around her. Left the Merchant Circle's research apparatus twenty years ago after a disagreement she has not described to anyone. Has been in the desert since. Has not run out of things to think about.  
**Goals:** Answer the question she has been sitting with for twenty years; determine whether the current situation is what she has been waiting to see; decide whether to return to the Endless Eye  
**The twenty-year question:** She left the Circle because she concluded something about the Hollostar at the lake's bottom that the Circle was not prepared to hear. She has spent twenty years determining whether she was right. She is now fairly certain she was. The implications are significant.  
**What she will tell:** She will not go back to the Circle. She will tell the party what she concluded if they find her and if she decides they can do something useful with it. Getting to her location requires either Katari guidance or significant desert navigation skill.  
**Quote:** *"I left because I was right and they weren't ready. I stayed because the desert is patient and so am I."*  
**Hook:** The deepest available knowledge about what the Hollostar at the lake's bottom actually is; twenty years of independent research with no institutional filter; finding her is itself an adventure

## STARFALL SEEKERS IN THE BASIN (2)

### Senior Researcher Pell Ashwick
**Role:** Holds the Scholar seat on the Merchant Circle; the Seekers' most official and most frustrated presence in the Basin  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 41  
**Appearance:** The particular exhaustion of someone who has been professionally correct about something important for three years and watched the people with authority to act on it decline to do so.  
**Personality:** Brilliant, impatient, bad at concealing either quality. Has made three formal research proposals to the Circle. All three were tabled. Has begun conducting informal research that the Circle has not officially sanctioned and that he is technically not supposed to be doing.  
**Goals:** Get meaningful Deeps access before the decline reaches a point where access becomes irrelevant; publish something before Lumencrest beats him to the conclusion; stop being right about things that nobody will let him prove  
**His unauthorized research:** Using data from licensed Deeps access permits — looking at patterns in what researchers have been allowed to see versus what the Circle has restricted — he has assembled a picture of the Hollostar's condition that is more accurate than anything the Circle has officially released. He has not published it. He is waiting for one more data point.  
**Quote:** *"Reverence and research are not enemies. Right now, only one of them can save the lake. I am the only person on this council who will say that out loud."*  
**Hook:** His unofficial research data is the most complete available picture of the Deeps situation; he needs one more thing to publish; the party can be the path that gets him that thing or the path that gets it somewhere more useful

---

### Field Researcher Yenna Coldbrook
**Role:** Junior Seeker based at the Endless Eye office; handles the practical work Ashwick is too senior to do himself  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 26  
**Appearance:** Perpetually prepared for fieldwork — practical clothing, equipment always organized, the look of someone who learned early that the interesting things happen at the edge of wherever you're supposed to be.  
**Personality:** Enthusiastic without being naive, methodical without being rigid. Has been with the Seekers for four years and has developed the organization's habit of treating every piece of information as potentially the piece that changes the picture.  
**Goals:** Complete the current assigned research; figure out what is in the restricted section of the Circle's permit database that Ashwick keeps referencing; survive her first trip to the outer desert without incident  
**Her discovery:** Three days ago she found evidence in public trade records that someone has been conducting unauthorized Deeps access — the water quantities don't add up and the discrepancy pattern points to a specific permit holder. She has not yet told Ashwick because she is verifying it. When she does tell him, it will change his timeline significantly.  
**Quote:** *"Every mystery has a data trail. You just have to be willing to follow it past where you expected it to go."*  
**Hook:** Her discovery about the unauthorized access ties directly to Brek's black market operation; she can be an ally or an unwitting catalyst depending on who she tells first

## ADDITIONAL FAIRY COMMUNITY (2)

### Shimmerweave Doss
**Role:** The Fairy community's most respected independent artisan; creates living-light installations that blend bioluminescent cultivation with traditional craft  
**Ancestry:** Fairy, age 88  
**Appearance:** Wings that have developed unusual properties over decades of working with bioluminescent organisms — faint patterns in them that glow slightly at night, not from any single biological source but from accumulated exposure. His workshop in the aerial district glows from the outside.  
**Personality:** Quietly obsessive about craft, warm in the specific way of someone who has found their purpose and is not anxious about anything else. Notices things other people miss because he has spent decades paying very close attention to living light.  
**Goals:** Complete his current commission (a light installation for the Merchant Circle Hall that will take six months); document the changes he has been observing in the bioluminescent organism behavior near the lake's surface; not be drawn into the Council's politics  
**What he's observing:** The bioluminescent organisms near the lake surface have been changing their light patterns for eighteen months. He has documented this with more precision than any official researcher because he works with these organisms daily. The pattern change is not random. It correlates with something he cannot identify from the surface. He suspects Elder Suu knows what it correlates with.  
**Quote:** *"Light is honest. It shows exactly what it is. I have been trying to learn that for eighty years."*  
**Hook:** The bioluminescent pattern data is a surface-observable signal of the Hollostar situation; his Elder Suu connection is warm and personal; his workshop is the most interesting room in the aerial district

### Marketwing Bree
**Role:** Runs the Fairy community's largest surface market stall — the main commercial interface between aerial Fairy culture and the rest of the Endless Eye  
**Ancestry:** Fairy, age 34  
**Appearance:** Wings that change color with mood faster than she can suppress — she has given up trying and now dresses to complement whatever they are doing. Moves through the market with the efficiency of someone who has been doing it since childhood.  
**Personality:** Talkative, socially adept, and running a secondary information network that she has never formally acknowledged as such. She knows what is being sold, bought, and whispered about in the Endless Eye's surface commercial district better than the Merchant Circle does. She updates this knowledge continuously.  
**Goals:** Keep the market running profitably; maintain her information network without anyone organizing it into something official that would change how people talk to her; figure out what is making several of her regular Ribbet suppliers nervous in ways they are not explaining  
**The nervous suppliers:** Three Ribbet merchants who regularly supply her market have been arriving late, short on stock, and unwilling to explain why for the past month. The pattern suggests they are being redirected to something. She does not know what. She is asking carefully.  
**Quote:** *"People talk at markets. I listen at markets. These are the same activity from different angles."*  
**Hook:** The Ribbet supplier nervousness connects to the Deeps situation; her market information network is the fastest way to find anyone or anything in the Endless Eye; she trades information for information and is always in the market
