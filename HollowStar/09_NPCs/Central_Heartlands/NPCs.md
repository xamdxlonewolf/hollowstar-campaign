# NPCs - Central Heartlands

*The Central Heartlands contain the continent's most important city and three others that each believe, privately, that they're more important than they're given credit for. The NPCs here are not faction leaders — those are in [[09_NPCs/Cross_Regional/Key NPCs|Key NPCs]]. These are the people players actually meet: the ones who reveal what each city feels like from the inside.*

---

## LUMENCREST

*A city of 87,000 people running on a power source that's dying and a political culture in complete denial about what that means. Walking its streets, you feel the gap between what it was and what it is — the flickering street lamps, the brownout schedules posted on walls, the way people talk around the crisis rather than about it.*

---

### Councilor Mira Ashton
**Role:** Illuminist faction leader on the Council of Lumens — the faction that believes a technological solution to the Heart's decline exists and just needs more time  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 58  
**Appearance:** Precise in everything — dress, speech, posture. Looks like someone who hasn't slept well in three years and has gotten very good at appearing as though she has.  
**Personality:** Genuinely brilliant and genuinely wrong about the most important question she faces. Mira Ashton has built her political career on the belief that Lumencrest's problems have Lumencrest solutions. This conviction has served her well for decades. It is now her prison.  
**Goals:** Prevent the Expansionists from authorizing military action against the Endless Eye; buy time for Luminarium researchers; maintain Accord unity  
**Secret:** She received Professor Greaves' data six months ago. She understood it. She has told no one because she has no answer to it and answering something she can't solve is not in her political vocabulary.  
**What she reveals about the city:** The Illuminist faction isn't corrupt — it's a group of smart people who built their identities around being right and can no longer afford to be wrong. Mira is the cleanest version of this. Others in her faction are less clean.  
**Quote:** *"Every problem this city has ever faced has been solved by the people in this city. I see no reason to abandon that record now."*  
**Hook:** She needs people outside the Council's knowledge to investigate something — and she needs them to not report back to anyone else on the Council

---

### Professor Talan Greaves
**Role:** Senior researcher at the Luminarium — the Heartlands' most prestigious engineering academy  
**Ancestry:** Elf, age 312  
**Appearance:** Someone who looks like he has been awake for four days because he has been awake for four days. Lab coat with calculations written on the cuffs because he ran out of paper.  
**Personality:** The kind of scientist who is completely indifferent to politics and social dynamics, which would be charming in peacetime and is catastrophic now. He shares his findings with whoever is in the room. He cannot understand why this is a problem.  
**Goals:** Share his discovery with people who will act on it; understand the Heart's decline mechanism; live long enough to publish  
**His discovery:** The Heart isn't just declining — it's counting down to something. The decay pattern is not gradual degradation. It's a structured release. On a specific date, the Heart will not dim — it will discharge. What that discharge does to a city of 87,000 built on top of it, he doesn't know. He's been trying to find out.  
**Secret:** The Luminarium's secret experiments (the ones the Council doesn't fully know about) are his. He authorized them without full Council approval because getting full Council approval would have taken two years and he doesn't think they have two years.  
**What he reveals about the city:** Lumencrest is not a city managing a slow decline. It may be a city sitting on a specific event. The difference matters enormously.  
**Quote:** *"I've submitted this data to the Council four times. Each time it comes back with 'noted' written on it. I don't know what 'noted' means. I know what the data means."*  
**Hook:** He needs someone to get his findings to people who will act — outside the Council, outside Lumencrest if necessary

---

### Lyra Finch
**Role:** Factory floor supervisor in the Manufactories district, de facto organizer of what is becoming a workers' movement  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 34  
**Appearance:** Practical clothing, hands that show factory work, expression of someone who is constantly doing three things and thinking about five more.  
**Personality:** Not an ideologue — a problem-solver who became political because the problems stopped being solvable any other way. She didn't want to organize. She wanted the lights to stay on.  
**Goals:** Get the Manufactories district reliable power allocation; stop the Council from cutting factory wages while maintaining Radiant Core power; keep her workers from becoming something the Council will call a riot  
**The line she's approaching:** She knows that what she's building could become something that frightens the Council into response — or frightens them into crackdown. She's trying to stay on the right side of that line. The line is moving.  
**Secret:** Three members of her organizing group are Council informants. She knows about two of them. She's used them to send messages she wants the Council to receive without seeming like she's negotiating.  
**What she reveals about the city:** The brownout inequality — why the Radiant Core never loses power while the Manufactories are rationed — is the crisis most visible to ordinary people. Not the Heart's decline in the abstract. The specific, daily experience of the lights going out in your district while the wealthy districts stay lit.  
**Quote:** *"I'm not asking for more than they have. I'm asking for enough to do the work that keeps them comfortable."*  
**Hook:** She needs something investigated — the power distribution records that prove the inequality she's describing — and she can't get to them herself

---

### Captain Dren Halvor
**Role:** Commander of the Lamplight Ward city guard  
**Ancestry:** Dwarf, age 189  
**Appearance:** The specific look of someone who has been doing the same job for forty years and has made peace with most of it but not all of it.  
**Personality:** Fundamentally fair, which is harder than it sounds in a city where fairness is becoming politically complicated. He enforces the law as written. He is increasingly aware that the law as written serves some people better than others.  
**Goals:** Keep the ward calm; maintain his officers' respect; not become the instrument of something he'd be ashamed of  
**The pressure he's under:** Both the Council and Lyra Finch's organizers are trying to use him. The Council wants him to treat organizing as incitement. The organizers want him to treat Council informants as criminals. He's refusing both and making enemies on both sides.  
**Secret:** He's been quietly documenting guard-response inequalities for two years — response times to Shadowside vs. Lamplight Ward, use of force patterns, complaint resolution rates. He doesn't know what he's going to do with this documentation. He knows he needs to have it.  
**What he reveals about the city:** The guard is not a monolith. There are officers who would crack down on the workers' movement without hesitation and officers who would refuse. Halvor is the difference between those two groups functioning as one force or fracturing.  
**Quote:** *"My job is to protect people. All of them. The problem is that protecting some of them from each other has started to feel like choosing sides."*  
**Hook:** He needs the party to handle something he can't officially touch — and he's offering information about how the city actually works in exchange

---

### Sera Vex
**Role:** Shard dealer and information broker operating out of the Shadowside  
**Ancestry:** Halfling, age 41  
**Appearance:** Looks completely unremarkable, which is her primary professional asset. The kind of person who can be in a room and have no one remember she was there.  
**Personality:** Precisely loyal — she has a specific code about who she'll betray and who she won't, and she's never violated it. This makes her valuable and strange in a city where most information brokers' loyalty is entirely financial.  
**Goals:** Survive the crisis; protect her network; find out what "the discharge" means before it happens (she's heard fragments of Greaves' data through secondary sources and is quietly panicking)  
**What she sells:** Shard fragments (the dangerous kind, with effects the Luminarium would prefer not be widely available), information about Council movements, passage through Shadowside safely  
**Secret:** She is genuinely loyal to one person on the Council — not for pay, but because that person once did something for her that she has never told anyone about. If that person asks her to do something she'd normally refuse, she'll do it.  
**What she reveals about the city:** The black market in Lumencrest isn't just criminal enterprise — it's the city's adaptive tissue. Things the official economy can't provide, the Shadowside provides. As the official economy deteriorates, the Shadowside grows.  
**Quote:** *"Everyone in this city is afraid of the same thing. The only difference is what they're willing to do about it."*  
**Hook:** She has information someone needs but won't give it without something specific in return — not money, but a service that requires the kind of people who aren't officially here

---

### Initiate Kael Thornroot
**Role:** Young First Flame priest stationed in Lumencrest's growing First Flame congregation  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 23  
**Appearance:** The specific earnestness of someone who arrived at their faith through genuine experience rather than upbringing, and hasn't yet learned to moderate it in company.  
**Personality:** Sincere to the point of being occasionally inconvenient. Asks the questions that more experienced people have learned not to ask out loud.  
**Goals:** Understand why the Heart is failing through a theological lens; serve his congregation; figure out whether the First Flame's growth in Lumencrest is faith or desperation (he suspects the latter and it troubles him)  
**His access:** His congregation includes factory workers, Shadowside residents, and — surprisingly — three members of the Luminarium who attend services quietly and sit in the back. He knows things about the city's emotional state that no faction leader knows.  
**Secret:** He has read Greaves' calculations. One of the Luminarium congregants left a copy somewhere he found it. He understood perhaps a third of it. The third he understood is keeping him awake at night.  
**What he reveals about the city:** The First Flame's growth in Lumencrest isn't ideological — it's people reaching for something stable when the technological world they trusted is visibly failing. Kael is watching his congregation grow for reasons that make him uncomfortable about what growth means.  
**Quote:** *"The First Flame teaches that light is given, not made. I used to think that was poetry. I'm starting to think it's something else."*  
**Hook:** Someone in his congregation told him something in confidence that has implications beyond them both — and he's trying to figure out if spiritual confidence survives contact with practical emergency

NPCs here reveal what happens when Accord economics meet Path of Spirits protection.*

### Thorn Oakwise
**Role:** Guardian of Ember Grove · Path of Spirits druid  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, mid-fifties · He/him  
**Location:** Ember Grove year-round; council meetings in Emberford

**Appearance:** Moves like someone who has been still for so long that motion is deliberate. Doesn't lean forward when he speaks.

**Personality:** Not warm in a social sense — *present*. Short sentences. Long silences are communication. Never hedges. Certainty is the only basis he works from.

**Goals:** Protect the grove's spirit boundary; train Greer; understand Heart Stone changes without rushing to conclusions

**Secret:** Lost an earlier grove to logging thirty years ago. Understands Thorns anger but chooses a harder path. Has not spoken of this at Ember Grove.

**Voice:** Deliver every line as if considered for a long time. Do not fill silences.

**Quote:** *"The eastern edge borders the grove's spirit boundary. This isn't a timber question."*

**Hook:** Sent Greer to Lumencrest — watch Vael operations, find where timber profits go beyond the grove

### Caelen Vael
**Role:** Youngest Vael sibling · House Vael representative to Emberford council  
**Ancestry/Age:** Human, early 20s · He/him  
**Location:** Traveling; Session 1 at Emberflow Arms (River Room), then road to Lumencrest

**Appearance:** Travel clothes too well-fitted to be practical. Vael sigil pin — silver tower on black. Father's cheekbones, none of father's authority.

**Personality:** Polished pleasant face. Reasonable tone, slight smile that doesn't reach eyes when managing. Recovers fast when caught off-guard.

**Goals:** Deliver lumber offer on House terms; hand off diplomatic materials in Lumencrest (Gold Month 14 RC handoff)

**Secret:** Doesn't know lumber funds Verdean refugee ships or Unseen Guild operations. Genuine shock if confronted with full truth — then rapid self-preservation calculation.

**What he reveals:** House Vael briefs representatives on local druid resistance by name. "Final offer" language is scripted.

**Quote:** *"House Vael has been in discussion with Emberford through intermediaries for several months. This is the formal conclusion."*

**Hook:** Pickpocket window at council; schedule card reveals heist timing. See [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/Locations/Cities/Lumencrest/House_Vael_And_The_Unseen_Guild|House Vael & Unseen Guild]] (GM)

## KINGSREST

*A city of 30,000 people who have chosen, deliberately and consciously, to be the opposite of Lumencrest. Where Lumencrest innovates and fails, Kingsrest preserves and endures. This is their story about themselves. Walking its white limestone streets, you feel the particular quiet of a city that believes tradition is protection — and is beginning to wonder if it's wrong.*

---

### King Aldric Thornhart IV
**Role:** King of Kingsrest, hereditary monarch, 82-year reign  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 107 (extended by Six-Fold Faith healing traditions)  
**Appearance:** An old man who carries his age like a credential. White hair, careful movement, eyes that have watched a lot of things happen and are watching more carefully now.  
**Personality:** Genuinely good — not as performance, not as strategy, but as the actual content of who he is. This makes him respected and, in the current crisis, somewhat ineffective. Good people in difficult situations sometimes lack the ruthlessness the situation requires.  
**Goals:** Keep Kingsrest stable; avoid being drawn into Lumencrest's political fractures; give his son enough time to be ready to rule  
**His fear:** That what's coming will require decisions he doesn't have the moral vocabulary for. He's spent 82 years ruling in conditions that rewarded patience and tradition. The crisis may reward neither.  
**Secret:** He knows about Crown Prince Cedric's investigation — the unofficial one, into what's actually happening with the Accord's food reserves. He hasn't stopped it because he suspects Cedric will find something he needs to know and can't discover officially.  
**Quote:** *"A king's first duty is to outlast the crisis. A king's second duty is to leave something worth inheriting."*  
**Hook:** He needs people who can do something unofficially that he cannot do without it becoming a diplomatic incident

---

### Crown Prince Cedric Thornhart
**Role:** Heir to Kingsrest, conducting an unofficial investigation into the Accord's food reserve situation  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 31  
**Appearance:** Looks like his father must have looked at 31 — the family resemblance is strong enough that older Kingsrest citizens sometimes stop and stare.  
**Personality:** Capable in ways his father can't entirely assess yet, because the situations that would reveal those capabilities haven't fully arrived. Currently operating slightly outside his father's official knowledge and slightly ahead of his own confidence.  
**Goals:** Determine if Lumencrest has been misrepresenting Accord food reserve data; protect Kingsrest's agricultural output from Accord requisition; be ready if his father's health declines faster than expected  
**What he's found:** The Accord's stated food reserves and the actual reported yields from Kingsrest's farms don't match. Someone is either lying to the Accord or lying about what the Accord is doing with the Kingsrest contribution. He doesn't know which yet.  
**Secret:** He's been communicating with Lyra Finch in Lumencrest — not politically, but informationally. She has data on Manufactories food imports that cross-references with what he's seeing in export records. Neither of them has told anyone about the other.  
**Quote:** *"My father rules well. I am trying to figure out what ruling well looks like when the situation stops rewarding patience."*  
**Hook:** He needs investigators — people his father doesn't know about and the Accord can't trace back to the crown

---

### Lady Rosalind Vey
**Role:** Noble, privately republican, managing a quiet network of reformists  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 45  
**Appearance:** The specific elegance of old money combined with the specific alertness of someone running something they'd rather not be caught running.  
**Personality:** Politically sophisticated in ways that most of the nobility around her isn't, which she works to conceal. She agrees with the king on almost everything practical and disagrees with the institution of monarchy on principle. She has found this position livable for twenty years.  
**Goals:** Preserve her reform network without it becoming something the crown has to respond to; influence the succession toward someone who might accept structural change voluntarily  
**Her calculation:** She doesn't want revolution. Revolution destroys the things she's spent her life building. She wants a king who chooses reform — and she's been quietly assessing whether Cedric is that person.  
**Secret:** Three members of her network are not reformists — they're Lumencrest Expansionists using her network as cover for information gathering. She's known for four months and hasn't moved against them because she's using them to pass disinformation she wants Lumencrest to receive.  
**Quote:** *"Every institution that has survived has done so by changing before it was forced to. The ones that didn't survive didn't understand this in time."*  
**Hook:** She needs something confirmed — intelligence about a specific Lumencrest Council decision — and she's willing to pay in information about Kingsrest's political landscape

## IRONHOLD

*A city of 35,000 dwarves (and a few thousand others) built into the Ironspine Mountains, doing exactly what it has done for 2,800 years with a patience and solidity that other cities find baffling. Ironhold's crisis is not the Heart's decline — Ironhold doesn't depend on Lumencrest's power. Ironhold's crisis is that the deeper mines are getting strange.*

---

### High Thane Grimbar Ironbeard
**Role:** Elected leader of Ironhold, seventh term, 350 years in office across those terms  
**Ancestry:** Dwarf, age 287  
**Appearance:** The specific appearance of a dwarf who has been the most important person in the room for so long that he no longer thinks about it.  
**Personality:** Entirely pragmatic in a way that surface cultures sometimes mistake for coldness. He's not cold — he just doesn't perform concern he doesn't feel or certainty he doesn't have. What he does feel, right now, is concerned.  
**Goals:** Keep Ironhold functioning; manage the Stonefist clan opposition; figure out what's happening in the deep mines before it becomes something he can't manage  
**What's worrying him:** The deep mines have been producing strange materials for the past eight months — ore that behaves differently, veins that lead to chambers that shouldn't exist, sounds at depths that suggest something other than geological settling. He has not told the Clan Council. He's not sure what he'd tell them.  
**Secret:** Ironhold has a treaty with Lumencrest that the Accord doesn't know about — signed seventy years ago, never ratified publicly, that commits Ironhold to specific military support in exchange for technology transfers that have since been delivered. If the current Council learned about it, the treaty would be a scandal. If Lumencrest's situation deteriorates further, the treaty becomes a liability Grimbar needs to quietly dissolve.  
**Quote:** *"Ironhold has endured by knowing what it is. The problem with the current situation is that it's making us wonder."*  
**Hook:** He needs outside investigators for the deep mines — dwarves won't go back, and he can't ask the Clan Council for help without revealing what he doesn't know yet

---

### Forge-Mistress Kara Coalforge
**Role:** Head of the Forgefire clan, master smith, pushing the boundaries of Ironhold metallurgy  
**Ancestry:** Dwarf, age 203  
**Appearance:** Forge burns on both forearms (decorative — she earned them, she chose them). Eyes that assess everything in terms of material and structure.  
**Personality:** Professionally obsessed with what materials can do, which has led her somewhere that makes other dwarves uncomfortable: she wants to work with shard fragments. Not as Lumencrest does — raw energy extraction — but as metallurgical components. She thinks Hollostar material can be incorporated into alloys. She's probably right. Nobody wants to talk about it.  
**Goals:** Get permission to experiment with shard metallurgy; produce something unprecedented; survive the clan politics that her ambition creates  
**What she's made:** She has, without official permission, already produced three test pieces using trace shard material from deep mine samples. The results are extraordinary. The process is dangerous. She hasn't told anyone about the test pieces.  
**Secret:** The "strange materials" from the deep mines that concern Grimbar? She has samples. She requested them through a miner she trusts. She knows more about what's down there than the High Thane does.  
**Quote:** *"Every material has a nature. The craft is learning to work with that nature rather than against it. I see no reason this would be different."*  
**Hook:** She needs someone to get her shard samples — legally, or at least deniably — without triggering the Clan Council's restrictions on experimental metallurgy

---

### Throk Stonefist
**Role:** Leader of the Stonefist clan, Grimbar's primary political opposition  
**Ancestry:** Dwarf, age 341  
**Appearance:** Older than Grimbar, and visibly aware of this as a credential. The expression of someone who has been right about a lot of things and is confident he's right about this.  
**Personality:** Not a villain — a traditionalist who genuinely believes Ironhold's survival depends on maintaining what has worked for 2,800 years. His opposition to Grimbar is principled, not personal. This makes him harder to deal with than a personal enemy would be.  
**Goals:** Limit outside contact with Ironhold; prevent Kara Coalforge's shard experiments from proceeding; get a Stonefist clan member elected as next High Thane  
**What he knows:** He knows about the secret Lumencrest treaty. He's known for forty years. He hasn't used it as political leverage because using it would require revealing how he found out, which would destroy the Stonefist clan source inside the Ironbeard clan.  
**His legitimate concern:** Ironhold's deep mines have been getting strange for eight months. His position: stop going deeper. Whatever is down there should be left alone. Grimbar's position — figure out what it is — strikes Throk as precisely the kind of approach that gets dwarven cities destroyed.  
**Quote:** *"What our ancestors built was built for reasons. The reason we don't know the reason is that we stopped asking."*  
**Hook:** He'll work with the party if they're investigating something that threatens Ironhold's stability — but his definition of stability and theirs may not match

## STEELWATCH

*A fortress-city of 28,000 soldiers with nothing to garrison against. The crisis isn't the enemy they were built for — it's subtler than that. Walking Steelwatch, you feel the particular restlessness of people whose identity is entirely built around a purpose that the world hasn't yet required of them.*

---

### Marshal-General Thalia Corvain
**Role:** Commander of Steelwatch, elected leader of the Marshal Council  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 52, third term  
**Appearance:** Carries herself like someone who has been in charge long enough that it's stopped feeling like anything and started feeling like gravity.  
**Personality:** Competent in ways that have kept her in command through three elections, which means she has learned to be competent in rooms full of people who are also competent. She is honest about most things and strategic about honesty itself.  
**Goals:** Give Steelwatch genuine purpose before the restlessness becomes something harder to manage; avoid being used by Lumencrest Expansionists as their instrument; keep the Marshal Council unified  
**Her quiet support of the Expansionists:** She hasn't endorsed military action against the Endless Eye, but she hasn't opposed it either. If Lumencrest authorizes something, Steelwatch will be asked to execute it. She wants to be in that conversation rather than handed orders.  
**Secret:** She has been in private communication with the Northern Expanse — specifically with Rider Commander Sera Windmane — about coordinating aerial and ground response to the Frostwell refugee crisis. This is outside her Accord mandate. She did it because it needed doing and nobody else was doing it.  
**Quote:** *"A soldier without an enemy isn't peaceful. They're just waiting. I've spent twenty years managing the waiting."*  
**Hook:** She needs people who can move through the Northern Expanse and Salt Shore without Accord identification — a situation her soldiers can't handle without triggering political questions she doesn't want asked

---

### Commander Brant Ironside
**Role:** Infantry command officer, hawks faction leader, wants Steelwatch deployed  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 44  
**Appearance:** The specific physical confidence of someone who has trained their body for thirty years and knows exactly what it can do.  
**Personality:** Not stupid — this is important. He's not a war-monger who doesn't understand consequences. He understands them and has decided that the consequences of not acting are worse. He may be right. He may be wrong. He is absolutely certain.  
**Goals:** Get authorization for Steelwatch deployment — ideally toward the Endless Eye situation, or the Frostwell evacuation, or anywhere that gives the garrison genuine purpose  
**His argument:** Steelwatch exists to protect the Heartlands. The Heartlands are threatened — not by invasion but by systemic failure. Military logistics, emergency response, forced evacuation — these are things soldiers do. Waiting in a fortress while the continent fails is its own kind of dereliction.  
**Secret:** He's been approached by a Lumencrest Expansionist Council member about what Steelwatch would need to authorize a "protective mission" to the Endless Eye region. He hasn't agreed. He hasn't refused. He's trying to figure out if the mission itself is legitimate regardless of who's proposing it.  
**Quote:** *"There's a difference between a weapon and a tool. We've been locked in an armory for three hundred years. I want to know if we're still either one."*  
**Hook:** He has operational intelligence about the Frostwell situation that he obtained through channels he'd rather not explain — and he needs someone to verify it

### Sgt. Mara Flint
**Role:** Twenty-year veteran sergeant, currently assigned to intelligence gathering  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 43  
**Appearance:** Has the specific quality of someone who has seen enough that surprise no longer reads on her face, which makes her unnerving to people who expect emotional feedback.  
**Personality:** Cynical in the precise sense — not despairing, but stripped of illusions, which leaves her more useful than most of her peers. She knows what Steelwatch is, what it isn't, and what it's becoming, and she's decided to stay anyway.  
**Goals:** Keep doing useful work; make sure the intelligence she's gathering reaches people who will act on it rather than file it; not become someone she wouldn't recognize  
**What she knows:** She has been compiling Steelwatch's intelligence reports on the continent's situation for three years. She knows about the Northern Expanse refugee numbers before the official reports. She knew about the Frostwell evacuation crisis before the Council of Lumens acknowledged it. She knows things are worse than the official picture by a consistent margin of about three months.  
**Secret:** One of the reports in her files — about Lumencrest's actual power output versus reported output — came from inside the Luminarium. She doesn't know who sent it. She doesn't know why it was sent to Steelwatch intelligence. She's been sitting on it for four months trying to figure out what to do with it.  
**Quote:** *"The intelligence says what the intelligence says. The problem is that what it says and what people are willing to hear have about a three-month gap, and that gap is getting wider."*  
**Hook:** She'll share what she knows with people she trusts — and her bar for trust is specific, observable, and she'll tell you exactly what it is

### Harlan Greaves
**Role:** Farmers' Union leader for the Central Heartlands agricultural region  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 61  
**Appearance:** Farmer's hands on someone who hasn't farmed in twenty years. Kept the calluses deliberately.  
**Personality:** Became a political figure because someone had to and no one else was going to, which is the origin story of most effective community organizers. Has the specific tiredness of someone who has been right about something for fifteen years and watched nothing change.  
**Goals:** Get the Accord to acknowledge the soil decline is real and structural, not cyclical; prevent Lumencrest from requisitioning food reserves before the farming communities have enough to survive the winter; find out if the Hollostar shard depletion in the soil can be reversed  
**What he knows:** The soil fertility data that the Accord has been calling "cyclical variability" is not cyclical. He has thirty years of records. The curve is clear. The farms that were producing 100 units thirty years ago are producing 67 now, and the rate of decline is accelerating.  
**Secret:** He's been in contact with Green Covenant researchers about the soil question — specifically about whether replanting with Covenant-advised species could slow the depletion. The Accord doesn't know. If it did, it would treat this as Covenant interference in Accord territory, which would end the research.  
**Quote:** *"The city people think food comes from farms. The farm people know it comes from soil. The soil is telling us something. I've been trying to get someone to listen for fifteen years."*  
**Hook:** He needs someone to get the real soil data to the Green Covenant researchers — officially, the samples can't cross regional boundaries without Accord documentation, which would take six months to approve

### Old Mara
**Role:** Wandering seer, no fixed location, occasionally found at specific places at specific times  
**Ancestry:** Human (possibly), age unknown — estimated 80s-90s, though she's been described as looking this age for thirty years  
**Appearance:** Carries too much. Layers of clothing regardless of weather. Eyes that seem to be watching something slightly to the left of wherever you're looking.  
**Personality:** Not cryptic for its own sake — she communicates in the framework that makes sense to her, which is not linear causality. People call this cryptic. She considers it precise.  
**What she actually is:** Not magic, not spirit-touched — a woman who has been paying very close attention to patterns across the Central Heartlands for a very long time, and whose pattern-recognition has been accurate often enough that people seek her out even when they're also skeptical of her.  
**What she currently knows:** She has seen six of the seven signs she associates with a specific historical pattern — one she learned from fragments of pre-Hollostar records that she won't tell anyone she has. The seventh sign, when it appears, will tell her where to be when it matters.  
**Secret:** She is in contact with the Somnarchs — not as a member, but as someone who occasionally shares observations that they find useful and cannot explain. They've been trying to identify her for twelve years. She finds this amusing.  
**Quote:** *"I'm not telling you what will happen. I'm telling you what I've seen. What you do with what I've seen is your business and, shortly, your problem."*  
**Hook:** She'll tell the party something specific and accurate — and the price is that they tell her one thing they've observed that surprised them. She means this literally.
