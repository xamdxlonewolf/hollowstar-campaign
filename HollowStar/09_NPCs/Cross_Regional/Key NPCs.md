# Key NPCs — Cross-Regional

These are the figures whose influence, decisions, or existence extends beyond any single region. Players who travel the continent will encounter their names, their agents, or their consequences almost everywhere they go.

Organized in two sections: **Faction Leaders** (people who run things) and **Wildcards** (people who don't fit categories but matter enormously).

## FACTION LEADERS

---

### Arch-Somnarch Vaela Thessin
**Role:** Supreme authority of the Somnarchs — the order that has been predicting the fourth fall for over a century, and is now watching it arrive  
**Ancestry:** Elf, age ~380  
**Appearance:** Thin in the way of someone who has always prioritized thinking over eating, silver-white hair kept severely back, eyes with the particular focus of a person who has spent a century looking at things very far away. She moves with the measured care of someone who knows she does not have unlimited time left. Her hands are always slightly ink-stained.  
**Personality:** Quiet, precise, and deeply tired in the way of someone who has been right about the same thing for a very long time. Not bitter — bitterness requires surprise. She is past surprise. She is also, for the first time in her tenure, genuinely frightened — not because she doesn't know what's coming, but because knowing has not made watching it arrive any easier.  
**Goals:** Ensure the Somnarchs' century of knowledge reaches the right people before the impact; resolve the Heart Warning debate; get her eight observatory holdouts to safety without dishonoring what they've chosen  
**Secret:** She has calculated a fifth Hollostar fall — longest-cycle projection, deeply uncertain, but present in the data. She has told no one. She is not sure the world needs to know that surviving the fourth fall may only be preparation for another.  
**Quote:** *"We don't know why. We know when. Knowing when is enough to survive."*  
**Hook:** Needs agents with no obvious Somnarch affiliation; pays well; the task she sends them on will reveal more than she told them going in  
**→ Full details:** [[04_Factions/Somnarchs|Somnarchs]]

### High Flame Kaida
**Role:** Supreme spiritual authority of the First Flame — the continent's fastest-growing faith, now facing its most critical test  
**Ancestry:** Giant (Firbolg-descended), elderly  
**Appearance:** Enormous even seated — 9 feet, broad-shouldered, weathered into something that looks carved rather than aged. Her face has the stillness of someone who learned long ago that a leader's expression is a public statement. Soft grey-brown fur, close-cropped. Wears no adornment except the flame-sigil pendant she has worn for fifty years, slightly dented from a fall she will not discuss.  
**Personality:** Calm, deliberate, warm at a distance. Every word chosen before spoken. Genuinely believes what she teaches — she is not a politician wearing theology. But fifty years of leadership have taught her that belief and strategy are not opposites, and she has made peace with the parts of her role that require the latter.  
**Goals:** Keep the six denominations unified through the crisis; resolve the succession problem before she dies; prevent the Unbound schism from splitting the church at the moment it most needs coherence  
**Secret:** The Eternal Flame has not given her clear guidance on the crisis. She was chosen by divine sign. She has led by divine guidance for fifty years. The silence now, at the worst possible moment, is the most frightening thing that has ever happened to her — and she has told no one.  
**Quote:** *"Study, prayer, and unity. In that order. In every order. Always."*  
**Hook:** Has privately asked certain trusted individuals to carry sealed letters to three specific people in three different regions — to be delivered only if she dies. She won't say what's in them. She will say the letters matter more than she does.  
**→ Full details:** [[04_Factions/The First Flame|The First Flame]]

### Voss, the Tide King
**Role:** Self-appointed maritime power of the Salt Shore — built a protection network from nothing, currently deciding whether to call it an empire  
**Ancestry:** Firbolg, age 51  
**Appearance:** 7'2", weathered salt-and-grey fur, close-cropped. One eye replaced with a deep amber glass eye, made in Saltmere years ago before things soured between them. Dresses practically — no crown, no finery, just good gear maintained well. One exception: a necklace of tide tokens, one from each town under his protection. He adds one when a town joins. He has never removed one.  
**Personality:** Calculating and fair. Not warm — he doesn't need you to like him. He needs you to understand the arrangement and trust that he'll honor his side of it. He always does. Listens more than he speaks. When he speaks, people stop talking. Remembers everything, never takes notes.  
**Goals:** Extend the protection network to cover the full Salt Shore; navigate the Hollostar crisis without the coast fragmenting; figure out what comes after the star falls before events make the decision for him  
**Secret:** He left his Firbolg community at 21 and discarded his family name. He has never gone back and will not say why. Two people in the world knew the reason. Both are dead. The reason connects to current events in a way he has not yet pieced together.  
**Quote:** *"The sea doesn't care about your politics. Neither do I. What I care about is whether the boats come home."*  
**Hook:** Has work for people who can move quietly across regional lines; his fairness makes him genuinely useful as a neutral arbiter — but he always has a secondary reason for involving outsiders  
**→ Full details:** [[04_Factions/Salt_Shore/The Tide King|The Tide King]]

### Archcovenant Maren Stillwater
**Role:** Leader of the Green Covenant — the continent's most powerful nature-protection order, currently managing a secret that could reshape everything  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 67  
**Appearance:** The only visible sign of her authority is a living vine that has grown around her left forearm since her selection by the Heartgrove. Nobody told her this would happen. She has grown used to it. It moves slightly, sometimes, in response to her mood — she tries not to look at it in public.  
**Personality:** Carries an enormous weight with deliberate stillness. Not secretive by nature — secretive because she believes premature information causes more harm than the secret itself. She may be wrong about this. She thinks about that possibility frequently.  
**Goals:** Understand the ancient waking before it announces itself to the wider world; find the moment when silence becomes negligence; stop Lumencrest's forest incursions without triggering a war  
**Secret:** The 2,000-year-old records in the Covenant's Memory archives describe previous ancient wakings. They also say: *"The last time the deep forest turned outward, three settlements ceased to exist. Not destroyed. Simply incorporated."* She has not told her Wardens.  
**Quote:** *"The forest has been patient for ten thousand years. It is running out of patience. I am trying to understand why before everyone else has to find out."*  
**Hook:** Grants access to the Verdant Crescent that no other contact can provide; is looking for people she can trust with information she can't give to her own people  
**→ Full details:** [[04_Factions/Verdant_Crescent/The Green Covenant|The Green Covenant]]

### King Elowen Arathas
**Role:** Elven monarch of the Northern Expanse — has ruled for nearly 280 years and is currently moving at a timescale that feels like negligence to people dealing with a refugee crisis right now  
**Ancestry:** Elf, age ~280  
**Appearance:** Looks younger than his age by human standards — elven aging shows differently. Lean, silver-haired, with the stillness of someone who has learned not to react visibly because reactions over centuries become policies. His formal court robes are immaculate. He is almost never in his formal court robes.  
**Personality:** Thinks in decades. Not as an insult to shorter-lived peoples — it is genuinely how he processes the world. He has steered the kingdom past three disasters that others couldn't see coming by doing exactly this. He has also caused real harm to people suffering *right now* by doing exactly this. He knows both things are true.  
**Goals:** Navigate the refugee crisis without fracturing the kingdom's social fabric; appoint the Warden of the North before the frontier deteriorates past recovery; live long enough to see what the fourth fall actually does  
**Secret:** He has been considering Warden of the North candidates for two years. He hasn't stalled from indecision — all three candidates would solve the immediate crisis and create a different, larger problem in thirty years, and he cannot find a fourth option. The frontier's suffering is the cost of his search. He accepts this. His people don't know why.  
**Quote:** *"The river does not hurry. It arrives nonetheless."*  
**Hook:** Needs people who can work quickly — he is genuinely unable to operate at the speed the current crisis requires, and he knows it; will compensate trusted outsiders generously for doing in days what he cannot do in months  
**→ Full details:** [[04_Factions/Northern_Expanse/The King and the Crown|The King and the Crown]]

### Mirelen Starweaver
**Role:** Facilitator of the Rivermark Sovereignty's Confluence Council — the diplomatic architect of the Sixth Seat campaign, now managing a 15-year stall  
**Ancestry:** Elf, age 347  
**Appearance:** Precise without being severe — always seems dressed exactly right for whatever room she's in. Silver-streaked hair, rarely still. Moves like someone who arrived three minutes early and spent those minutes thinking.  
**Personality:** The most skilled political listener on the continent. Speaks to persuade, not to be heard. Three centuries of diplomacy have made her genuinely patient but sharply intolerant of people who waste her time — a distinction she rarely makes visible.  
**Goals:** Break the Sixth Seat stalemate before the crisis makes it irrelevant; protect Rivermark from the Merchant Circle's increasing pressure; preserve the Sovereignty's independence even if formal Accord membership fails  
**Secret:** She has had a private conversation with a Merchant Circle representative that she has not disclosed to the Confluence Council. The representative made an offer. She has not yet answered. The offer is better than anything the Accord has put on the table. She is trying to determine whether taking it is brilliant pragmatism or betrayal.  
**Quote:** *"Patience is a tool, not a virtue. The virtue is knowing when to stop using it."*  
**Hook:** Needs outside agents for sensitive Accord-adjacent work that can't be traced to Rivermark; the undisclosed Merchant Circle offer is a ticking problem that could reshape the entire eastern political landscape  
**→ Full details:** [[02_Regions/Central_Heartlands/The_Rivermark_Sovereignty|Rivermark Sovereignty]]

### Director Sable
**Role:** The Veilmarkets' continental coordinator — the closest thing the decentralized network has to a single authority  
**Ancestry:** Unknown (human-presenting, won't confirm), age ~50  
**Appearance:** Different every meeting — different clothes, different hair, different accent. Not magical, just deliberate. The only constant is quality: whatever they're wearing fits perfectly. The one time a contact asked directly about their appearance, Sable said: *"You remember what you need to and forget what I need you to forget. That's how this works."*  
**Personality:** Professionally pleasant. No visible affect. Genuinely curious about people in the way of someone who has spent decades learning how to use them — without necessarily intending harm. Has a reputation for fair dealing within the Veilmarkets' own terms, which is meaningfully different from fair dealing by any other standard.  
**Goals:** Position the Veilmarkets to profit from the crisis without being consumed by it; maintain the network's neutrality as factions start demanding loyalty; decide what to do with the information the network is sitting on  
**Secret:** Sable has assembled a complete picture of something none of the major factions know — pieced together over years through the network's information flow. They have not yet decided what to do with it. They are watching to see who will pay the most, but privately they are not certain this particular secret should be sold to anyone.  
**Quote:** *"Everyone has a price. Finding it is just research."*  
**Hook:** The fastest route to almost anything — and the most reliable way to be sold out if someone pays more; Sable gets personally involved when stakes are high enough to require their judgment  
**→ Full details:** [[04_Factions/Veilmarkets|Veilmarkets]]

### Hexarch Aldren Voss-Karath
**Role:** The most politically powerful of the six Hexarchs — Hexarch of the Architect, overseer of law, order, and institutional authority in the Six-Fold Faith  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 71  
**Appearance:** The architecture of authority made flesh — tall, straight-backed, silver-robed, with the precise enunciation of someone who has given hundreds of sermons and learned exactly which words land. Age has not diminished him. The weight of years has made him look more permanent.  
**Personality:** Believes in the institutions he represents with absolute conviction — not because he is naive about their flaws, but because he has decided imperfect order is preferable to genuine chaos. More flexible than he appears and more ruthless than anyone expects. Has survived four internal Hexarch crises by being the last person anyone wanted to move against.  
**Goals:** Prevent the First Flame from breaking the Six-Fold Faith's continental dominance; manage internal Hexarch divisions without the public learning how fractured the council is; determine whether the approaching star is divine will or physics — and what either answer means for his authority  
**Secret:** He knows about the radical sect within the Hexarchs that privately believes the Hollowstars are the gods. He has not moved against them because their theory, if proven, would solve a theological problem he has been unable to resolve for decades. He is waiting to see if they're right before deciding whether to suppress or embrace the idea.  
**Quote:** *"Faith is not belief despite evidence. Faith is the decision about what evidence means."*  
**Hook:** A powerful patron who works through intermediaries; an equally powerful obstacle who moves against threats quietly and thoroughly; his genuine flexibility makes him both useful and unpredictable  
**→ Full details:** [[04_Factions/The Hexarchs|The Hexarchs]]

### Scholar-Captain Denn Harrowfield
**Role:** The most organized figure in the Starfall Seekers — runs the largest active Hollostar expedition and has come closest to understanding what the approaching star actually is  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 44  
**Appearance:** The specific kind of haggard that comes from years of field work — sun-weathered, always carrying too many notes, burns more candles than he sleeps. Not frantic. Focused. There's a difference, and he's maintained it through three nearly fatal excavations.  
**Personality:** The only Seeker anyone fully trusts, primarily because he is the only one who openly admits what he doesn't know. Methodical to the point of madness by Seeker standards. Genuinely doesn't care about fame or fortune — cares about the question, which is both his most admirable quality and the reason he has no real limits on how far he'll go to answer it.  
**Goals:** Reach Frostwell and document the impact from as close as survivable; find out why the fourth star's approach is triggering simultaneous responses in every existing Hollostar site; publish findings in a form every faction can use  
**Secret:** He has a current theory about what the Hollowstars actually are — not fallen stars, not divine gifts, not cosmic debris. Something else entirely. He has shown the theory to no one because it invalidates most of what every faction believes, and he wants to be certain before burning that many bridges at once.  
**Quote:** *"Ignorance isn't a moral failing. Choosing to stay ignorant is."*  
**Hook:** The most reliable Seeker contact; his expedition needs support and people who won't panic when things go wrong (they always go wrong); his theory, when he finally shares it, will change everything  
**→ Full details:** [[04_Factions/Starfall Seekers|Starfall Seekers]]

### Cindrath the Ancient
**Role:** The only known living ancient dragon. Has dwelt in the Furnace volcano beneath Ashenveil longer than the city — possibly longer than anyone knows  
**Ancestry:** Ancient Dragon, age unknown (millennia)  
**Appearance:** Massive red dragon — scales like volcanic glass, eyes like magma, heat radiating constantly. Standing near her is like standing near a forge. Wingspan over 200 feet. Barely fits in her caldera, which she manages through the simple authority of being the largest thing in any space she occupies. Voice like rumbling volcano — bass notes that vibrate bones, carrying an accent from a language dead for millennia.  
**Personality:** Being evil is too much effort at her age. Being good isn't in her nature. What interests her is the bargain — the exchange, the unexpected, the rare thing that surprises her after millennia of watching mortals repeat themselves. Patient to the point of appearing bored. Mildly fond of Drakona. Privately admires Frostwell Drakona who make the volcanic pilgrimage anyway despite the heat. Does not hurry anything.  
**Goals:** She has no urgent goals — which is part of what makes her dangerous. The approaching fourth fall interests her. She has not said why, and has not said in what direction that interest points.  
**Secret:** She knew the beings who first created the Drakona. She watched the first Hollostar fall. She has watched every fall since. She knows what the stars actually are. She has not told anyone — not from malice, but because no one has ever offered her something worth that particular trade. She is waiting to see if someone finally will.  
**Quote:** *"I have watched your kind build civilizations over the bones of the last ones. It is, I admit, more interesting than I expected the first time."*  
**Hook:** The only being alive who has witnessed multiple falls firsthand; her bargains are fair by dragon standards; the secret she's sitting on is worth almost anything and she knows it  
**→ Location:** [[02_Regions/Desert_of_Glass/Locations/Cities/Ashenveil|Ashenveil]], the Furnace volcano

### Elder Thessara Windroot
**Role:** The oldest living elf in the Deepwood — one of the only people alive who was present for the third Hollostar fall 700 years ago  
**Ancestry:** Elf, age ~800 (lifespan extended by proximity to the third fall)  
**Appearance:** The difference between a 380-year-old elf and an 800-year-old elf is visible in ways difficult to articulate — something about stillness, about the way she occupies space as though she stopped needing to prove presence long ago. White-silver hair that moves slightly in still air. Eyes with the particular unfocused quality of someone looking at things that are no longer in front of them.  
**Personality:** Does not speak quickly. Does not speak unnecessarily. When she does speak, the words have been considered for longer than most people have been alive. Not cryptic by design — direct, in fact, by the standards of her age. What sounds like riddles are usually literal descriptions of things that have no modern vocabulary.  
**Goals:** Ensure what she witnessed at the third fall reaches someone who can use it before the fourth makes the knowledge irrelevant; determine whether the current crisis is the same pattern she witnessed before or something new  
**Secret:** She was at the third fall's impact site. She saw what emerged from the crater in the first hours — before any other witness arrived. What she saw is not recorded anywhere. She has been deciding for 700 years whether it is safe to describe. The fourth fall has finally made her decide: yes.  
**Quote:** *"The star does not fall on us. It falls into us. I watched it happen. I am still watching."*  
**Hook:** The living record of the last fall; will share what she witnessed with people she believes can handle it; her account will contradict what every faction believes about how the Endless Eye was actually formed  
**→ Location:** Deep Deepwood, Verdant Crescent (rarely leaves)

### The Turned — Kerath
**Role:** A Hollostar-transformed creature who retained full intelligence and now navigates a world that doesn't know what to do with him  
**Ancestry:** Was human. Is now something the Somnarchs classify as a "class-four integration" — transformation complete and irreversible, mind fully intact.  
**Appearance:** Humanoid shape, correct proportions, wrong in ways that are hard to identify at first glance. Skin that catches light as though it contains something. Eyes that glow faintly in darkness — not dramatically, just incorrectly. Moves with slightly too much precision. Has been practicing looking normal for eleven years and is getting better at it.  
**Personality:** Dry. Observational. Has spent eleven years cataloguing how people react to what he is, which has given him encyclopedic knowledge of human discomfort and a specific kind of humor that only people who've been through something difficult tend to develop. Not bitter about the transformation. Genuinely uncertain whether that's healthy acceptance or damage.  
**Goals:** Find others like him — complete transformations with intact minds; understand what the Hollostar energy actually did to him and whether it can be reversed; find a way to exist that doesn't require hiding  
**Secret:** He can sense Hollostar fragment concentrations — not vaguely, but specifically, directionally, with distance estimates accurate to within a mile. He has been quietly mapping shard locations for eleven years. He knows about deposits no Seeker or Somnarch has found. He hasn't told anyone because he doesn't know yet whether this makes him an asset or a target.  
**Quote:** *"I am, as far as I know, the first of my kind to hold a conversation about what I am rather than having one held about me in my presence. I find I have opinions."*  
**Hook:** Unique knowledge of shard locations no one else has; a perspective on Hollostar transformation that no academic study can replicate; his existence is a moral question — he is technically proscribed by three different factions' rules and entirely innocent of anything  
**→ Note:** Mobile — can be placed wherever the party would benefit most from encountering him

### The Unnamed Astronomer — "Null"
**Role:** An independent tracker of the approaching star whose calculations are more accurate than Lumencrest's official projections — and who has taken extreme precautions to ensure no one knows who they are  
**Ancestry:** Unknown  
**Appearance:** Communicates exclusively through written correspondence and coded drops at pre-arranged locations. The one person confirmed to have met them in person described "someone who clearly did not want to be described" and has not elaborated further.  
**Personality:** Paranoid with cause. Has submitted the same data to Lumencrest three times under three different names and watched it get buried each time — not through malice, but because it contradicts the official projection in ways the Council of Lumens doesn't want to address publicly. Has concluded that anonymity is the only protection the work has left.  
**Goals:** Get the correct impact timeline into the hands of people who will act on it; survive what's coming; eventually understand why Lumencrest keeps suppressing the numbers  
**Secret:** "Null" is two people — a partnership that began as professional correspondence and became something more. One is currently inside Lumencrest. One is not. They have never met in person. The Lumencrest insider is the source of the suppression data; the outsider provides the independent calculations. Together their picture is more complete than anything any single faction possesses.  
**Quote:** *(from a letter, unsigned)* *"The impact window is not two to five years. It is fourteen months. I have checked this eleven times. I am asking you to check it once."*  
**Hook:** The party receives a coded message from no apparent sender; tracing it leads somewhere unexpected; the data — if verified — changes every faction's timeline simultaneously  
**→ Note:** Can be introduced through any faction contact who deals in information

### Captain Sable Wrack — Nine-Lanterns
**Role:** Commander of the Nine-Lanterns pirate fleet based at Lantern's Rest in the Salt Shore  
**Ancestry:** Human, age 43  
**Appearance:** Naval competence without naval origin — self-taught, self-made, and visibly comfortable with both. Short-cropped dark hair, a scar across her jaw from a confrontation she won, and the kind of stillness that comes from having been in enough situations that required fast decisions that fast is now her default.  
**Personality:** Professional, fair by pirate standards, and genuinely committed to the arrangement with Voss not out of loyalty but out of understanding that it is the best available structure. The fishing town rule is not negotiable and she enforces it within her fleet with real consequences. The arrangement works because she wants it to work.  
**Goals:** Keep the Nine-Lanterns arrangement with Voss stable; expand operations carefully into the Silver Belt Sea without triggering a Grey Sails confrontation; determine whether the changing shipping patterns in the Salt Shore represent an opportunity or a warning  
**Her arrangement with Voss:** The Nine-Lanterns pay 20% of their take to operate in Voss's waters. They do not prey on fishing communities — absolute rule, enforced by Wrack personally. In exchange they operate freely on merchant shipping from outside the Salt Shore. Eight years stable. She has no interest in destabilizing it.  
**The shifting patterns:** Two shipping routes that the Nine-Lanterns have historically targeted have been rerouting through different waters for the past three months. She does not know why. The rerouting has cost her fleet a significant amount of expected revenue. She is interested in whoever knows why it is happening.  
**Quote:** *"I take from ships. I don't take from towns. Everything else is negotiable. Those two things are not."*  
**Hook:** The route shifting connects to both the Voss/Grey Sails situation and the larger Basin commercial disruption; she knows the Salt Shore waters better than anyone who doesn't live on a fishing boat; her code makes her a more reliable partner than most legitimate operators

### A Regional Veilmarket Operator — "Tallow"
**Role:** Mid-level Veilmarkets operator covering the Silverbelt Coast and Starfall Basin routes; the party's most likely direct contact with the network  
**Ancestry:** Unknown (won't say)  
**Appearance:** The Veilmarkets' operators cultivate unremarkability. Tallow has elevated this to a craft. Medium height, forgettable face, clothing that fits the location they are in — dock worker in Tidewall, merchant clerk in Vetheris, desert traveler in the Basin. The tells are subtle: they always know the exits, and they are always slightly too comfortable in unfamiliar places.  
**Personality:** Transactional and fair within those terms. Not warm. Not cold. Professional in the specific way that suggests the warmth exists but is kept behind glass. Has been doing this for twelve years and has developed the Veilmarket operator's particular ethics: the arrangement is honored, the client is not betrayed, but nothing comes free and nothing is personal.  
**Goals:** Complete current assignments; manage the increasing faction pressure to take sides in the Hollostar crisis; not be positioned as responsible when whatever is coming arrives  
**What they can provide:** Information, moving things that official channels won't touch, access to people who don't want to be found, and connections to the larger Veilmarkets network. The price is always fair by Veilmarkets standards and always more than it appears by any other standard.  
**Their concern:** The Veilmarkets has been receiving unusual volume of shard-related requests for the past year. Black market fragment prices are at historic highs. The network is positioned well to profit. Tallow is aware that being well-positioned in a crisis that ends badly is not a good outcome. They are paying attention to whether this crisis has an end.  
**Quote:** *"I can find what you need. The question is what finding it costs you. That's always the question."*  
**Hook:** The party's Veilmarkets access point; the shard market intelligence connects to multiple ongoing situations; Tallow's concern about the crisis's endpoint is a rare moment of shared interest with the party
