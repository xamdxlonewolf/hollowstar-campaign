Act 1 — Gate Entry
The Registry Flags a Name
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

During the gate queue, before the party reaches the officer. Best when the table is relaxed — use it to remind them the city's welcome is conditional.

GM Say

"The line stops. Not long — thirty seconds — but long enough. Ahead of you, a Frostwell woman with a cloth bundle is asked to step aside. The officer checks a ledger, speaks quietly to a second watchman, writes something down. She goes pale, then nods, and is waved through with a look that says she knows she's being noted. The line moves again. You pass through normally. But you saw how fast the machinery engages when a name matters."

GM Info — What's actually happening

The woman is not on a criminal warrant — she's on a relief registry mismatch. Frostwell arrivals are supposed to check in at a Council relief station within 24 hours; she didn't know. The watch logs her for follow-up, not arrest. This is bureaucracy, not malice — but it looks like malice from the queue.

If a PC has a warrant: swap the NPC for their situation. The fear spend buys you the beat without a surprise TPK — the officer asks them aside, verifies, and either passes them (mistaken identity, old warrant) or gives them a choice: resolve it now (fine, contact, bribe) or don't enter. Don't hard-stop the campaign without an out.

Basil: If the Guild runner reported the party, this is NOT when that surfaces — registry is city watch, not Guild. Save Guild awareness for the tail spend.

Right Now

The city's scale becomes administrative, not just sensory. Someone in the party may feel sympathy, suspicion, or both.

This Session

If they help the woman (carry a bag, speak for her, escort her to relief station), she remembers. Minor ally in Pauper's Gate traffic.

Long Term

Frostwell registry friction becomes relevant when refugee crisis escalates. Players who saw Day 1 queue politics understand Day 20's underground deaths differently.

The Hum Spikes Once
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

Immediately after they cross the gate threshold, or when they first touch a Network-connected fixture (lamp post, iron railing). Works best paired with Greer or Lurielle in front.

GM Say

"The hum you've been hearing since the ridge — constant, low — spikes. Not louder exactly. Deeper. Every electric lamp on the street flickers in the same instant, a single synchronized stutter, then steadies. A Clank three paces ahead doesn't pause. A vendor doesn't look up. You do."

GM Info — What's actually happening

Micro-fluctuation in Heart output — the same class of event that causes brownouts, at negligible scale. Network-connected infrastructure registers it everywhere at once. Locals have learned to ignore sub-threshold spikes. Newcomers haven't.

Lurielle: Knowledge DIF 12 — the spike's harmonic profile matches her Heart Stone notation from Session 1 (if used). Same family, different amplitude. She can write this down.

Greer: Instinct DIF 11 — the spirit layer flinched. Something below the city turned in its sleep. Not waking. Adjusting.

Act 3 — Cable Car
The Car Shudders Mid-Line
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

Mid-ride between North Expanse and Central Hub — the highest, most exposed segment. Use when the table is comfortable and enjoying the view.

GM Say

"The car shudders — a lateral hitch, like the cable's tension went wrong for half a second. The mechanism in the ceiling chatters. The attendant doesn't look alarmed. He taps the speaking tube, listens, says two words you can't hear. 'Tension adjustment,' he tells the car, almost bored. 'Happens.' A dwarf by the window goes back to reading. A woman who was asleep opens one eye, closes it. You are the only people holding the handrail."

GM Info — What's actually happening

Minor cable maintenance lag — the Network hiccupped and the automated tension compensator was a fraction slow. Not dangerous on its own. Happens more often than officials admit. The attendant's calm is real; so is the dwarf's glance at the ceiling, which says more than last month.

Don't turn this into a disaster scene unless players panic and do something stupid. The fear is social: locals know something newcomers don't.

Brownout Flicker — Eastern Ward
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

From the cable car, approaching Central Hub or on the South line descent — players have elevation to see a district-wide flicker.

GM Say

"From up here you watch a section of the eastern Ward go dark — not one lamp, a block, then two, streetlamps dying in a chain like someone snuffed a line of candles. Three seconds. Four. Then they come back, not all at once, a staggered relight that looks ashamed. Someone in the car swears quietly. The Clank at the window doesn't react. Nobody explains it to you."

GM Info — What's actually happening

Rationing-induced load shed on a secondary feed — the same eastern Ward infrastructure that Session 3's hub explosion will sever more permanently. The protest tomorrow is partly about this neighborhood's power priority.

Greer: When the lights die, the spirit layer exhales — briefly less crowded. When they return, it compresses again. She can describe both sensations if asked.

Basil: Notes which block went dark. If he has the schedule card, he doesn't know yet this district matters — but he will.

Right Now

Visual proof the glowing city is failing in patches. Protest chalk suddenly makes sense.

This Session

Dawn protest has specific grievance players can name if they were on the car.

Long Term

Session 3 explosion hits this Ward's grid. Players who saw the flicker understand the blast as continuation, not random terror.

Act 4 — Greer Spotlight
Something in the Layer Recognizes Her
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

During Greer's spotlight moment — near the Vault wall view from cable car, Merchant Square at night, or the border walk. When she's actively reaching into the spirit layer.

GM Say — To Greer

"You reach for the spirit layer the way you would at the grove — and for a moment it reaches back. Not words. Recognition. Something under the Vault District turns toward you like a sleeper hearing a familiar voice in a dream. It smells, impossibly, of oak and turned earth. Then the city rushes in again — eighty thousand voices, the Network's hum, the compression — and the moment is gone. Your eyes water. Not from sadness. From proximity."

GM Info — What's actually happening

Residual spirit-memory in the Heart's dreaming layer — Goldwood's fall left echoes in anything connected to Hollostar infrastructure. Greer's druidic bond reads it as forest. It's not a spirit of a tree. It's a scar in the layer that remembers what was lost when Lumencrest grew.

Thorn would say: "The city is not empty. It is full of what it ate." He won't say this until much later unless Tara asks directly in a future session.

Path of Spirits: No roll required for the experience. Instinct DIF 13 to understand it's memory, not a present entity.

Right Now

Greer's mission personalizes — the spiritual quiet she felt for eight years has a geography.

This Session

Colors her read on protest, refugees, and Covenant chalk — she senses desperation layered on top of dreaming.

Long Term

Goldwood/Timberfall thread connects to Vault Heart. Greer becomes the party's conscience on Lumencrest's cost.

Act 4 — Solt at the Dark Lantern
Solt Pours Without Being Asked
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

First drink order at the Dark Lantern — or first time Greer/Lurielle sit down. Solt reads rooms for a living.

GM Say — Solt's action

"You open your mouth to order. Solt is already pouring — one cup pushed to Greer, one to Lurielle, dark and hot, nothing for Basil. He doesn't explain the omission. He looks at Greer and Lurielle each once. 'On the house. You look like you're listening to something. Don't let it listen back.' Then he's at the other end of the bar like the sentence was weather."

GM Info — What Solt knows

He doesn't know their names or missions. He knows listeners — people who arrive in Shadowside already attuned to frequencies the district survives around. He's seen researchers, Covenant-adjacent travelers, and druids before. He gives them tea (if tea) or house brew to ground them. Basil gets nothing because Basil arrived closed — professional habit, not insult.

Basil Presence DIF 12: Can get a pour by making himself legible as a person, not a job. Solt respects that.

Don't make Solt omniscient. He's thirty years of pattern recognition, not a quest giver.

Right Now

Shadowside has a gravity the Ward doesn't. Solt is a gatekeeper tone-setter.

This Session

His line reframes Greer/Lurielle spotlight experiences as shared danger, not private weirdness.

Long Term

Solt at +2 reputation is message relay, safe meeting space, and early warning on disappearances.

Act 4 — Dark Lantern
A Frostwell Woman Asks About Work
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

Mid-conversation at the Dark Lantern — creates a moral micro-beat without forcing a side quest.

GM Say

"At the bar, a young woman — Frostwell build, layered clothes wrong for this climate — speaks too quietly to the room. 'Does anyone know if the Manufactories are still taking people? My brother said there was labor.' The room pretends not to hear. Solt's expression doesn't change. He wipes the bar. Waiting to see who answers, and how."

GM Info — What's true

Manufactories are hiring — exploitative shift work, dangerous floors, pays in coin that doesn't cover Ward rent. Her brother was partially right. The room's silence is self-protection: helping connects you to someone's desperation.

If party helps: Point her to Ironwright hiring hall (exploitative but legal) or Kess Aldman's boarding house community (safer, slower). If they ignore: she leaves. If they mock: Solt's neutrality cools — future -1 Shadowside rep.

A Speaker Names the Vault District
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

Session ending beat — protest reforming at dawn. Use as the final escalation before your closing line.

GM Say

"The call-and-response finds its rhythm. A woman on a crate — Manufactories accent, Ironwright colors pinned to her coat wrong-side-out so watch can't cite them — raises her voice. 'They ration our light while the Vault never dims!' The crowd answers. She goes further — names the Vault District aloud, not as myth, as address. 'The Heart eats and we skip meals!' The watch captain's jaw tightens. Half the crowd cheers. They don't know what they're cheering for. They know who they're cheering against."

**Genna Cradle's** people didn't write this speech — it's organic rank-and-file anger. But Ironwright organizers have been waiting for Vault naming to go public. Theron Ashwick will call this treason by afternoon. Sevan Vael will call for calm.
