Location Reference — City Hub

Lumencrest

~87,000 people on a dying Hollostar grid. Interactive city map · District detail: Lamplight Ward · Shadowside

🌆
Arriving in Lumencrest
First sight · Pauper's Gate · Copper Gate · Inside the walls
▾
GM: Say this on the Kingroad — first sight at night

"The first thing you notice is the sky. It's not dawn — you know that, you've been watching the stars. But the horizon ahead of you glows. Not the orange of fire. Not the pink of sunrise. Something whiter, steadier, colder. A dome of pale light sitting on the edge of the world where a city should be. It takes another hour before the shapes appear inside that glow — towers, walls, the dark geometry of buildings against the brightness behind them. And then you begin to hear it. A low sound, like a chord played so deep you feel it more than hear it. Constant. Steady. The sound of the city."

GM: Say this at Pauper's Gate (north — Kingroad arrival)

"The northern gate is busy in the way of a place that has always been busy — a functional gatehouse, a queue that moves, and immediately inside on the left, a taproom that has the look of somewhere that has been the first building travelers pass for three hundred years. Old stone gate, iron and electric light in the gatehouse bright as midday. A cable runs overhead toward the city center. Vendors cluster just inside the gate with prices that assume you don't know better. Near the interior wall, a handful of people sit with the look of not knowing where they're going next. They are quiet. Frostwell refugees, if you know what that means. You probably don't yet."

GM: Say this at Copper Gate (south — Lamplight Ward entrance)

"The southern gate opens into the polished face of the city — four inns within sight, currency exchange booths with bright signage, and a licensed information service called the Welcome Gate with a map board and guides for hire. This is where Lumencrest wants you to arrive. The street lamps start immediately. The buildings are four stories of stone with commercial ground floors. It smells like bread from somewhere south and something sharp and metallic you can't place. If you came through the north gate first, the difference is obvious. If this is your first gate, you don't know there's another face yet. You will."

GM: Say this when they walk through any gate into the city

"The street is lit. Not by torches, not by lanterns — the light comes from glass globes on iron posts every twenty feet, steady and white-gold, casting no flicker. Above you, a cable runs between towers, and something moves along it — an enclosed box the size of a room, gliding through the air, carrying passengers who look out the windows without particular concern. The road is wide enough for six carts side by side and it is full. Coal-smoke from somewhere west. Bread from somewhere south. That metallic scent again — faint, warm, in the walls and under your feet. A Clank walks past in plain grey robes. Nobody looks at it twice. It is the middle of the night. It does not look like the middle of the night."

Entry procedure — what guards actually do
  • No papers required. Gates are open. City watch manages flow, not investigation.
  • Clanks are directed aside for implant scan: SERVING, FREE, or FLAGGED. FLAGGED means detention.
  • Weapons: Personal arms fine. Obvious military-scale equipment gets stopped.
  • Pauper's Gate: Names checked against warrant registry. Goods assessed for tariffs. Honest, vague, or creative answers all pass — unless you're actually wanted.
  • Watch questions: Where from, where staying, business in the city. Recorded. Rarely followed up.
Pauper's Gate Taproom — Old Pen's
First building inside north gate · Always open

The Underground's most public-facing anchor. Everyone who enters via Pauper's Gate passes it. City watch drinks here after shift. Old Pen has maintained that arrangement for twenty years by being useful to both sides without compromising either. Full detail on Shadowside page.

Welcome Gate — Tomm's service
Copper Gate · Licensed tour & orientation

2 silver marks for a guided introduction to public geography. 5 silver for a full day. Tomm is the best guide on staff — he'll tell you in the first ten minutes which money exchange to use and which to avoid, costing himself the referral fee. Full detail on Lamplight page.

City Facts
Population · crisis · what everyone knows · what nobody says
▾
At a glance
  • Population: ~87,000 city proper + ~15,000 transient workers and visitors
  • Founded: ~1,200 years ago (0 LE — Lumencrest Era calendar starts here)
  • Unique: Only city with Hollostar-powered electricity — the Lumen Network
  • Government: Council of Lumens — 32 seats, three factions, gridlocked three years
  • Crisis: The Heart is dimming. ~2.5% annual power loss. Six brownouts in the past year.
  • Clanks: ~3,000 registered (indentured service). ~400 free. Scanned at gates.
What everyone in Lumencrest knows (first week)
  • Brownouts are getting worse. Official line: "routine maintenance." Unofficial: the Network is under strain nobody wants to name.
  • Council is gridlocked. Illuminists (technical fix), Conservators (stability), Expansionists (find another Hollostar, military if needed).
  • Clanks are political. Indentured service vs. slavery — Ironwright Collective made it a bargaining chip.
  • Manufactories is angry. Power rationing hit factory floors hardest. The word "strike" replaced "demonstration."
  • Halfling Quarter feeds you. Quarter bread and beer reach every district. Don't make the Quarter unhappy.
  • The Vault District is there. The wall, the guards, the Heart beneath. Brownouts are making people look at it.
GM only — what nobody quite says out loud

The official Heart timeline isn't what researchers believe. Disappearances in Shadowside are happening — carefully, to people whose absence won't be reported. Something connected to the Green Covenant passed through recently asking about Network distribution. The Frostwell is worse than refugees are saying — the ones who arrive left before things became impossible.

Campaign clock — Day 1

Tonight the lights are on. Tomorrow mid-afternoon a distribution hub on Lamplight Ward's eastern edge explodes. Three injured. A section goes dark. Protest becomes panic. Tonight, walk through a city that still looks like it's working.

Party home district
💡 Lamplight Ward — East-Center · ~20,000
Commercial heart for the middle class. Merchant Square, Guildhall Quarter, Entertainment Quarter, Copper Gate welcome zone. Three cable car stops. The surface city at its most convincing — and where the cracks show first.
→ Accord Arms, Long Table, Tomm, Henris Callow, food vendors, gossip
 North arrival
🌑 Shadowside — North · ~15,000
Unlit, dense, self-sufficient. Pauper's Gate, Night Market, Dark Lantern, Maze Quarter. No cable car. City watch patrols minimally. The city's true underbelly above ground.
→ Old Pen, Solt, Mama Voss, Night Market, Frostwell refugees, gossip
Radiant Core
Center · ~8,000 · Full power · Central Hub station

Seat of power. Council, estates, Accord Chamber dome visible for miles. Guards permanent. You need a reason to be here. Founder's Rest inn (5–15 gold/night) — only Core lodging.

Halfling Quarter
Southeast · ~15,000 · Mostly reliable · 1 cable stop

Warmest part of the city. Common Green, breweries, rooftop gardens. Pipwick's Rooms (Bessa Pipwick, 7 silver, breakfast included). Everyone knows the Quarter feeds Lumencrest.

The Manufactories
West · ~25,000 · Rationed power · 2 worker-hub stations

Industrial west. Great Press factory, chimney stacks, shift rotations day and night. Ironwright Collective territory. Lampblack inn 5–8 silver. Workers angry about rationing.

Academic Quarter
East-Center · ~5,000 · Full power · Scholar's Gate station

Luminarium campus, Archives dome, Innovation Row. Four Constants café (best conversations, open almost 24h). Scholar's Rest 1.5 gold/night.

Vault District
Center-sub · Restricted · No public cable access

Heart's entrance. High walls, Vault Guard pairs, auto-turrets on city wall. Hollostar Dust enforcement jurisdiction. Unauthorized access extremely illegal.

The Underground
Beneath all districts · Tunnels, sewers, hidden economy

Connects to Shadowside surface more than anywhere else. Dry Chambers, Quiet Market, tunnel routes. Shadowside page covers above-ground entry points.

Cable Car Guide
Lines · fares · first ride · orientation from above
▾
GM: First cable car ride

"The station is a covered platform eight feet above the street. You pay the attendant — two copper marks per person, any distance — and wait until the car slides in from above. Dark wood and glass, fifteen feet long, hanging from the cable. Inside it smells like warm metal and other people. Worn leather seats. Clean windows. The attendant speaks two words into a speaking tube and the car moves — smoothly, no lurch, a steady pull until you're above the rooftops at a fast walking pace. Below: the whole city. Above: cable and sky. The hum is sharper up here. Nobody in the car is impressed. One woman is asleep. A dwarf reads. A Clank stares out the window. This is how they commute."

Four lines from Central Hub (Radiant Core)
  • North Line: Central Hub → North Expanse Station (Lamplight north / near Pauper's Gate) → North Factory Hub (Manufactories/Shadowside border)
  • East Line: Central Hub → Scholar's Gate (Academic Quarter) → River Station (East Gate)
  • South Line: Central Hub → Merchant Square → South Gate Station (Copper Gate)
  • West Line: Central Hub → Halfling Quarter Station → Mill Quarter (Manufactories south)
Orientation tip

Players will unconsciously use the cable car bell to orient — lines are the fastest way to understand where you are. From the car: Accord Chamber dome, Vault wall from above, Manufactories smoke, Halfling Quarter yeast smell even at cable height.

Marks · where to sleep · eat · gray areas

Currency
  • Units: Copper marks, silver marks, gold marks
  • Exchange: 10 copper = 1 silver · 10 silver = 1 gold
  • Fair exchange: Merchant Square, two licensed Merchant Guild booths
  • Premium trap: Copper Gate exchange booths — worse rates for first-timers. Tomm warns against these.

Legal · Gray · Illegal
  • Legal: Halfling Quarter commerce. Night Market southern/central stretches (specific goods vary). Most Shadowside above-ground trade. The Pit (technically illegal, functionally tolerated).
  • Gray: Hollow Pipe, Velvetongue, Thornbloom. Night Market substance section. Unlicensed medicine (Vael at the Stitch; Night Pharmacy back counter). Information brokering.
  • Illegal: Red Cradle, Fade, Hollostar Dust. Unauthorized Vault access. Unlicensed weapons trade. Slavery — Clank indenture is the live political fight.
  • Extremely illegal (Vault Guard): Hollostar Dust — serious, consistent enforcement.

Cable car

True. Eastern Lamplight lamps unreliable for weeks. Eastwick Cooperative is stockpiling quietly.
Merchant Square
"Factory supply shop on the east side closed two months ago. FOR LEASE sign still up. Nobody's biting."
True. Manufacturing slowdown reducing Ward inventory. Aldous Wren has been to Council twice with no commitment.
Pauper's Gate vendor
"More northerners every week. Quiet ones. They don't haggle — they just look lost and pay whatever you ask."
True. Frostwell refugees arriving in ones and twos. Conditions at source worse than they'll say.
Long Table dinner
"The Illuminists still talk like the researchers will pull a miracle. The people who actually maintain the Network stopped saying that six months ago."
True. Craft Guild professionals know practical Network condition better than Council rhetoric.
Night Market trader
"Someone's buying tunnel components again. Quiet Market stuff. Haven't seen that volume since before the last rationing push."
Partially true. Radical wing activity — Wren/Ash supply chain. Joss Wren moves components through Night Market.
Copper Gate innkeeper
"If you're staying more than a night, change your coin at Merchant Square. The booths here count on you not knowing that."
True. Henris Callow and Tomm both steer visitors to fair exchange — professional honesty as strategy.
Academic Quarter whisper
"Evening Broadsheet's been trying to verify the Heart timeline for weeks. The researchers won't go on record. That usually means they're scared."
True. Official timeline disputed in Academic Quarter. Petra Vane's classified finding unreported.
