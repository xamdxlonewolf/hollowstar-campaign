📢 Act 1 — Morning Protest & Eastwick
Morning — Border Protest
The Protest Crowd Surges Early
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

During the mid-morning protest assembly, before speakers have the crowd's full attention. Use when players are observing or lightly involved — not if they're deep in a Brinne scene (save Brinne fear for that).

GM Say — Read aloud

"Someone at the front shoves someone back. It's not a fight — it's a mistake that becomes momentum. Fifty people push forward before the organizers can shout hold. City watch stiffens, batons visible but not raised. Chalk dust kicks up from the pavement where yesterday's slogans were scrubbed. The crowd is one surge away from becoming something the watch can't pretend is a demonstration."

Roll — Calm or redirect the surge
Stat: Presence or Strength
DIF: 13
Failure

Surge breaks the line. Three arrests, minor injuries. Watch remembers the party if they were visible. Protest organizers blame outside agitators — partially true (Fear spend was agitation).

Partial

Surge stalls at the line. One arrest. Tension high; speakers regain control after 5 minutes. Players marked as capable by organizers.

Success

They help organizers push the surge back. Speaker thanks them by name. +1 civic trust with legitimate protest wing — useful when Council blames protestors tonight.

GM Info — What's actually happening

The surge is not Green Covenant orchestration. It's organic frustration + one agent provocateur (unaffiliated petty criminal paid 2 silver to shove). The radical wing wants distance from today's blast — Wren's cell planned for mid-afternoon precisely to avoid protest linkage. This Fear spend foreshadows how easily the city will conflate the two.

Right Now

Watch on edge. Any player near the front is sweaty and visible. Good tension before the real blast.

This Session

After the hub explosion, NPCs will say "the protest crowd surged this morning" as proof of escalation — players who calmed the surge can counter that narrative.

Long Term

Legitimate protest organizers become a faction contact if treated fairly. They publicly condemn the blast — players who know them get early access to that statement.

Morning — Eastwick Cooperative
Brinne Closes the Cooperative Door
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

When players ask Brinne about the stockpile, the larder, or emergency preparations — especially if they failed a trust roll or asked with entitlement.

GM Say — Brinne's line

"Brinne's hand is on the basement door. She doesn't raise her voice. 'You're not the first strangers asking about our stores today. I'm not rude because I'm busy — I'm careful because I have to be. You can help with the candles or you can leave.' The lock turns. The volunteers pretend not to notice how tense the room just got."

GM Info — Who asked before them

A man in Merchant Guild livery (not Guild officially — borrowed coat) asked about "community storage capacity" an hour ago. He was taking notes. Brinne didn't like him. He is Shard Division-adjacent intelligence gathering capacity data — not connected to the bomb. Players who press Instinct DIF 13 notice she glanced toward Wire Road when she said strangers — east, toward the hub, not the border.

To recover trust: Help load candles without asking questions for 10 minutes. Presence DIF 11 apology with specific offer. Success reopens the conversation; she mentions the Guild-coat man if they earned it.

Right Now

Players feel the Cooperative's vulnerability. Brinne is an ally they have to earn — not a vending machine for prepper intel.

This Session

If they earn trust later, her pop-up triage after the blast is available. If not, they find another path to help injured.

Long Term

Brinne becomes a major mutual-aid contact for Act One. Early mistrust costs access to Eastwick's underground supply network.

⚡ Act 2 — Pre-Blast
Pre-Blast — Two Blocks from Hub
Ash on the Rooftop
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

Mid-afternoon, as players move through Eastwick toward the hub or Entertainment Quarter. Best if they're not expecting it — reward players who look up (Instinct DIF 11 notices automatically without Fear).

GM Say

"Two blocks ahead, slate roof — someone is lying flat against the ridge line, watching the hub access door. Young. Dark hair. Not protest gear — plain clothes, traveler's boots. He sees you see him. He doesn't wave. He rolls off the far side of the roof and is gone before you can shout."

Optional — Immediate pursuit (before blast)
Agility DIF 13 to reach the alley
Failure

Empty alley. Boot scrape on wet stone. The hum still holds — you have 90 seconds before the blast. Pursuit cost positioning.

Partial

Glimpse hood from stairwell. Can't close distance. Then the crack — blast interrupts chase.

Success

See his face for one second. Scared, not triumphant. He mouths something — not to you, to himself — then the hub detonates. You'll remember the face when you see it again.

GM Info — Ash

Wren's second. Observer, not bomber. He was supposed to confirm the maintenance window was clear — it wasn't. His early departure is guilt, not professionalism. If players connect pre-blast Ash to post-blast alley glimpse, they've built the investigation thread without a name.

Right Now

Foreshadowing. Unease. Someone knew the hub mattered before it broke.

This Session

Post-blast pursuit has a face. Ash glimpse fear links the scenes.

Long Term

Ash is the reachable radical. Players who ID him early can approach him in Act One Beat 6 — confrontation, negotiation, or leverage.

Pre-Blast — Network Texture
The Hum Stops for One Heartbeat
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

30–90 seconds before the explosion. Only Lurielle and Greer are guaranteed to feel it; other Resonant characters too. Use as final warning — then blast.

GM Say — Lurielle version

"The hum under the city — the sound that's always there — cuts out. Not fades. Stops. One heartbeat of absolute silence in the Network layer. Your bioluminescence flickers without permission. Then the hum returns, wrong pitch, like a voice cleared its throat. Three seconds later: the crack."

GM Say — Greer version

"The spirit web in a city is thin — you've felt that since you arrived. For one breath it goes thinner. Not dead — withdrawn. Like something technical just flinched before being struck. Your skin prickles. Then the hub breaks and the web screams."

GM Info — What's happening

The detonator handshake with the hub's coupling layer creates a micro-interrupt — a pre-failure pulse. Lurielle's Endless Eye heritage recognizes the frequency. Knowledge DIF 13: she can note it matches her reclassified journal data. This is Hollostar-adjacent resonance in civic infrastructure — Tier 1 secret texture, not explanation.

Right Now

Players who felt it have 1–3 seconds of dread before the blast. Powerful timing.

This Session

Lurielle can connect hum-stop to resonance spike during blast — same frequency signature.

Long Term

Proof the bombers used Network-aware tools, not crude fire. Investigation points to technical actors — eventually Tern Ashe / Green Covenant radical wing.

💥 Act 3 — The Explosion
Blast — Immediate Aftermath
Secondary Collapse
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

5–15 seconds after the initial detonation, while players are orienting. Anyone within 30 feet of the hub facade.

GM Say

"The hub building coughs smoke — and the conduit housing on the street side groans. Bolts shear. A sheet of brick-facing and cable-tray drops toward the alley where you are standing. Not the whole building. Just enough to kill someone who doesn't move."

Roll — Avoid collapse
Stat: Agility or Dexterity
DIF: 13
Failure

2d6 physical damage. Buried partially — Strength DIF 12 ally or watch to extract. Witness route blocked by rubble.

Partial

1d6 damage. Clear but shaken. Boot print evidence under rubble — Finesse DIF 14 to recover before watch arrives.

Success

Clear. You see the coupling damage pattern before the watch tapes the scene — directional, intentional.

Right Now

Physical danger in a situation scene. Forces movement decision — in or out of rubble zone.

This Session

Blocked alley pushes pursuit route toward Entertainment Quarter rooftops — Ash's escape path.

Long Term

Injuries here make Ora Finch path urgent. Carr remembers if they were in cordon zone injured vs helping.

Blast — Resonance
Lurielle's Resonance Spike
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

At detonation if Lurielle is within 200 feet. Can combine with hum-stop for devastating personal continuity. Her arm glows blue-white visible in the sudden dark.

GM Say

"Lurielle — the blast doesn't just hit your ears. It hits your blood. Bioluminescence erupts along your forearms, visible to everyone in the dark street. You taste copper. The frequency is in your journal — the one you copied before they reclassified the paper — and it is here, in a street explosion, humming in your bones for three seconds before it fades. People are staring. The dark makes you impossible to ignore."

Roll — Process or hide the spike
Knowledge DIF 13 identify frequency
Presence DIF 12 play it off
Failure

Crowd panic around her — "Network witch" shouted once. Watch sweep harder when it arrives. +1 Fear attention on Lurielle.

Partial

She notes the frequency match; one witness saw. Carr logs "resonant individual" if swept.

Success

Full identification — coupling-layer handshake, same family as Endless Eye pre-class data. She has words for the investigation. Crowd distracted by injuries.

Right Now

Lurielle is exposed as unusual. Roleplay opportunity — embarrassment, terror, scientific excitement fighting each other.

This Session

Technical witness credibility with Carr or Brinne if she shares coupling theory calmly.

Long Term

Vault Guard / Research Board may hear about resonant witness. Lurielle as living sensor becomes plot asset and liability.

Blast — Forensics
Greer Reads the Blast Pattern
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

When Greer reaches the hub site or surveys from adjacent street. Fear spend grants clarity even on failed roll — the information is traumatic, not optional.

GM Say

"Greer — you've seen fire in forests. Controlled burn. Wildfire. Spite-fire. This is none of those. The damage speaks a vocabulary you don't like: couplings first, housing second, fuel never. Someone shaped this explosion the way a logger chooses which tree falls so the cabin isn't hit. They knew this building. They chose what died."

Roll — Instinct to articulate
Instinct DIF 14
Failure

She feels it in her gut but can't explain. "Wrong. Calculated." Enough for party; not enough for watch.

Partial

Secondary hub, not main — deliberate limiter. Someone wanted Eastwick dark, not city dead.

Success

Full read: architecture knowledge, feed-map intent, non-standard incendiary. She can brief Carr or Brinne in terms they'll act on. Investigation seed planted.

GM Info — Green Covenant context

Greer may suspect ecological radicalism. Instinct DIF 12: the blast has no Green Covenant spiritual signature — no offering, no grove-pattern. This is political infrastructure sabotage wearing green possible blame. Thorn would say: watch who benefits from the wound.

Right Now

Greer becomes party's moral compass on investigation — not random terror.

This Session

Counters Theron statement's "protest escalation" narrative if she speaks publicly.

Long Term

When Green Covenant mainstream is blamed, Greer's read distinguishes radical wing from Thorns philosophy — crucial for her character arc.

Blast — Protest Wave
Panic Stampede from the Protest
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

When players are between protest and hub, or in Eastwick streets as the crowd flees east. Three blocks of screaming humanity.

GM Say

"The protest becomes a river — hundreds of people running from the sound, not toward it. Some think the watch fired. Some think the hub was the start of something worse. You're in the channel where the street narrows. Bodies. Heat. Someone goes down."

Roll — Survive and act
Agility or Strength DIF 12 stay upright
Failure

Knocked down. 1d6 trample. Watch later finds you in pile — swept automatically. Child Jex lost in crowd (side quest hook).

Partial

Stay up. See someone down — Presence DIF 11 to halt one wave; save a stranger OR pursue hub.

Success

Pull one person clear (elderly Marta Henwick — witness!). She owes them a true sentence about the access door.

Right Now

Chaos forces priority: people vs evidence vs safety.

This Session

Marta Henwick witness unlocked on success. Jex lost on failure — find child for Lida/Brinne goodwill.

Long Term

Players who save strangers in stampede build Eastwick reputation before faction cards arrive.

Blast — Medical Pressure
Toma Vess Worsens
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

If players delay at scene >5 minutes without stabilizing Toma, or if they chose pursuit first. Clock pressure — not punishment for wrong choice.

GM Say

"The man who wasn't getting up — Toma — his breathing changes. Shallow. The leg wound darkens. Pella Morr screams for anyone who knows medicine. You have maybe four minutes before four becomes none."

Roll — Emergency stabilization
Knowledge DIF 13 or Instinct DIF 14
Assist: 1 Hope +1d6
Failure

Toma dies in the street. Ora Finch later confirms he could have lived. Guilt hook. Watch notes death count: 1.

Partial

Living but critical — must reach Brinne corner or Mending Room in 8 minutes. Sprint scene.

Success

Stable for transport. Brinne/Ora path opens. Toma survives canon — may testify later (weak voice, specific memory of door opening).

GM Info — Toma

Maintenance adjacency worker — not inside hub, hit by shrapnel. If he lives and players befriend him: he heard the access panel scrape 40 seconds before blast. Complements Marta Henwick's visual. Doesn't know Ash's name.

Right Now

Forces heal path NOW. Other forks wait.

This Session

Alive Toma = investigation asset. Dead Toma = moral weight, Ash's guilt mirrored on players if they ignored him.

Long Term

Toma at Ora's = Halfling Quarter connection. His testimony contradicts "protest mob" theories.

Blast — Power Cascade
Darkness Spreads Block by Block
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

30–60 seconds after initial blackout. Emphasize the feed map was chosen — not all-or-nothing failure.

GM Say

"The dark doesn't stop where the hub stopped. It walks — block by block, east through Eastwick, a swallowing of streetlamps like someone blowing out candles in order. Two blocks south, the Copperstring's emergency exit light dies mid-flash. Three blocks west, the protest zone stays lit — you can see the boundary where the city's priority map says who matters. Someone built this failure to look like an accident and function like a message."

GM Info — Feed map

Secondary hub failure cascades along intentional coupling paths. Knowledge DIF 13: repair crews will later prioritize commercial Entertainment Quarter feeds over Eastwick residential (Brinne's Day 9 complaint). The bombers knew this too — maximum civic insult.

Right Now

Visual horror. Radiant Core glow on horizon unchanged — class politics made literal.

This Session

Brinne's candles become plot-critical. Lida's oven heat draws a line. Copperstring patrons spill into dark streets.

Long Term

Speakers' Corner Day 3 — Bram Kettlemarch: "when power goes out in one part of the city, other parts don't notice." Players lived this.

Player Fork — Watch Response
Cordon Closes on the Party
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

15–30 minutes post-blast. Players still in hub radius when Sergeant Pel Carr's sweep begins. Especially if Lurielle glowed, Basil was seen near access panel, or they ran from protest stampede into cordon.

GM Say — Carr's approach

"Tape and boots. A sergeant with ink-stain fingers and the expression of a man who expected a protest shift and got an architecture fire. 'You. All of you. Inside the tape. Names, now. Where you were when it cracked. This is form-filling, not hanging — but I need answers before my captain invents them for you.'"

Social navigation
Presence DIF 13 credible account
Knowledge DIF 12 technical witness
Deception DIF 15 Basil cover
Failure

Detained 2 hours ward station. Miss pursuit. Logged "persons of interest." Released without charge — for now.

Partial

20-minute street questioning. Exhausted. Carr neutral. Can still help injured if Toma not dead yet — barely.

Success

"Cooperative civilian" note. Carr shares unofficial count: three injured, watch has no suspect. Offers contact if they remember anything odd before the sound.

GM Info — Investigation foil

Carr's file goes to a captain who deprioritizes by Day 5. Carr personally keeps Marta Henwick-type witnesses off the record if they refuse — he doesn't know they exist unless players bring them. Advantage if players helped injured: Carr heard about carriers on relay.

Right Now

Official attention vs investigation time. Meaningful fork tension.

This Session

Evening: they hear investigation has zero leads — they may BE the leads Carr lacks.

Long Term

Carr as recurring watch contact — rare honest officer. Or bureaucratic obstacle if they burned him.

Evening — Political Layer
Theron's Statement Finds the Party
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

Evening in Eastwick — dark streets, candlelight. Broadsheet crier OR neighbor with printed sheet. Use when players are recovering, not while triaging.

GM Say — Crier + crowd reaction

"A crier at Wire Road: 'Council statement — Expansionist response —' and a man two doors down shouts: 'Told you the protest would bring this.' A woman shouts back: 'The hub's three blocks from the border — you think a march blows couplings?' The street holds its breath. Someone looks at you because you're strangers and strangers get blamed when the city is scared."

Roll — De-escalate or counter
Presence DIF 13 calm street
Knowledge DIF 12 cite forensics
Failure

Shoving match. Watch patrol diverts. Brinne's goodwill tested if players escalated.

Partial

Argument stalls. Theron's framing spreads anyway. One neighbor sides with players quietly.

Success

Greer/Lurielle technical counter lands — "couplings, not crowds." Small crowd shift. Lida hears; gossip network favors party.

Theron Ashwick — full statement text

"Today's attack on Lumencrest's Lumen infrastructure was not an accident. It was an act of anti-civilization terrorism — a deliberate strike against the Network that sustains eighty thousand lives… I call on the Council to convene in emergency session and to authorize the protective measures this city has too long deferred."

GM Info — Theron's play

He's not lying about deliberate attack. He's channeling fear into military vote. Day 3 Council: he speaks to Vael twice — moving him toward expansion, not investigation. Players in gallery Session 4 will see this live.

Right Now

Politics enters the neighborhood where the lights are out. Personal stakes.

This Session

Plateau: Emergency Council announced — Theron's win partially achieved before session ends.

Long Term

Expansionist vote thread. Players may oppose Theron without knowing full Heart timeline yet.
