GM Warning — This Is Not a Combat Session

The hub explosion is a situation encounter. City watch, panic, injured civilians, and a darkness that spreads block by block. Combat is possible if players pursue aggressively or resist a sweep — default mode is chaos management and observation. Wren and Ash are not at the site. Save named antagonist confrontation for later.

💡

If you're behind: compress the morning protest to 20 minutes — one Brinne beat, one chalk read, move to mid-afternoon. The blast and player fork are non-negotiable. The Theron statement can be a handout if you're out of time.

Act 1 — Morning Protest & Eastwick
Lamplight / Shadowside border · Brinne · Lida's line · chalk remnants
~60 min ▾
GM Say — Opening narration (Day 2 morning)

"Gold Month, eighth day — your second morning in Lumencrest. Yesterday's protest at the Lamplight Ward / Shadowside border left marks: chalk on brick, mostly scrubbed now, but not entirely. City workers were here at dawn with brushes and solvent. You can still read fragments if you know what you're looking for. WE PAID FOR THIS LIGHT. OUR GRID TOO. A third line is gone entirely — scraped clean — but the ghost of it remains in the stone's lighter patch."

"The border itself is a street width of contested pavement — Shadowside's Maze Quarter bleeding into Lamplight's commercial east. Morning traffic moves around where the demonstration will reform. City watch is already positioning: not armored, not relaxed. Twelve officers visible from where you stand, with more in the side streets. They've done this before. Yesterday was tense. Today is supposed to be louder."

GM Info — Where were the players last session?

Session 2 ended wherever they settled — Wren House, Accord Arms, Copper Gate, wandering Eastwick. Default opening: they're in or near Eastwick having breakfast, or walking the Wire Road toward the border out of curiosity. Ask each player one sentence: where are you this morning and what drew you to this part of the Ward? Whatever they say is true. If they're nowhere near the border, the explosion still reaches them — the hum stops, the lights die, the sound carries.

— Eastwick — Brinne & the Cooperative —
GM Say — Brinne Halloway at the Cooperative hall

"Four blocks east of the border, in Eastwick — the residential neighborhood on the Ward's eastern edge — the Eastwick Cooperative's meeting hall is already busy. Not protest-busy. Organizing-busy. A human woman in practical grey, forties, carrying a clipboard and a ring of keys that sounds like work: Brinne Halloway, chair of the Cooperative. She's directing two volunteers loading crates — candles, lamp oil, spare wicks — into a handcart. She does not make speeches. She makes lists."

Brinne Halloway — if players approach

"If you're here to watch the border, eat first. If you're here to help, tell me what you can carry. If you're here to ask questions —" she looks up, assesses them in one pass "— ask something specific. I don't have time for vague."

She will not explain the stockpile unless they earn it (Presence DIF 12 genuine offer of help, or Instinct DIF 13 reading that she expects the lights to fail). If they help load the cart: she tells them the Cooperative has been preparing for 'the next phase' for six months. She does not say explosion. She says unreliability.
Brinne — social rolls
  • Offer real help (loading, running messages): no roll — she gives them a task and watches how they do it.
  • Presence DIF 12 — convince her they're serious, not tourists. Success: she mentions the shared larder and that Eastwick has been on reduced feed for two weeks. Partial: task assignment, no intel. Fail: polite dismissal — "Border's that way."
  • Instinct DIF 13 — read her preparation level. Success: this woman expects the grid to fail today, not eventually. Partial: she's scared and hiding it well. Fail: just a busy community organizer.
— Lida's Bake — the morning line —
GM Say — Lida's Bake, Wire Road corner

"Lida's Bake has been open since 3am. The ovens are electric — Network-fed — and the line at 8am goes around the corner anyway, because the rolls are that good and the coffee is the strongest in the Ward. Lida Birch moves behind the counter with the unhurried precision of someone who has fed this neighborhood at dawn for twenty-three years. Flour on her apron. A pot of coffee that never quite empties. The line is half regulars, half people who heard there's organizing at the Cooperative and want to be fed before they find out what that means."

Lida Birch — to anyone in line

"Two rolls and coffee is three copper. If you're going to the border, eat first. Loud people with empty stomachs make bad decisions."

She knows Brinne's schedule, the protest organizers by face, and which city watch sergeant is actually fair. She will share gossip freely (no roll) to anyone who buys coffee and doesn't perform entitlement.
😨 Fear Spends — Morning
  • The protest crowd surges early — before organizers are ready, fifty people push past the watch line. Chalk dust in the air. Someone throws something. Not a riot — a shove that becomes a wave.
  • Brinne closes the Cooperative door — mid-conversation, she locks the larder and says: "You're not the first strangers asking about our stores today." Someone else has been asking questions.
Morning fears are pressure tools. One at most before the blast. Save economy for panic and sweep scenes.
— The border protest reforms —
GM Say — Mid-morning, protest assembles

"By mid-morning the border fills. Larger than yesterday — someone counted two hundred, someone else says three. Organized: hand-painted signs, a woman with a list calling names for the speaker rotation, a man keeping water buckets filled. The demands are the same as yesterday, louder: end Shadowside rationing, publish the feed map, stop treating the eastern Ward like a secondary circuit. City watch holds the line. Nobody has drawn steel. The mood is angry and disciplined — the dangerous combination."

Protest speaker (unnamed organizer — not radical wing)

"We paid for this light! Our taxes, our labor, our grid — and when the Network stutters, whose homes go dark first? Not the Vault District. Not the Radiant Core. Ours. We are not asking for charity. We are asking for the contract the city already signed with us!"

The crowd responds. Greer feels the spiritual texture of collective frustration — not evil, not possessed, just human pressure made audible. Lurielle notices two Network junction boxes on the corner humming at slightly different pitches. Basil notices pickpockets working the crowd's edges and a watch sergeant who sees them and looks away — someone's being paid to look elsewhere.
Optional morning investigation — border scene
  • Knowledge DIF 12 (Lurielle likely): read the chalk remnants — protest slogans plus one line scrubbed extra hard: THE HUBS ARE NOT EQUAL. Someone knew technical vocabulary.
  • Instinct DIF 11 (Greer): the crowd's anger has no Green Covenant spiritual signature — this is civic, not druidic radicalism.
  • Finesse DIF 13 (Basil): tail the pickpocket crew — they fold into a side street and meet a man who takes a paper, doesn't pay, leaves. Guild-adjacent logistics, not today's bomber.
📢

Let the morning breathe 30–45 minutes if the table is engaged. If they're restless, time-skip to mid-afternoon with: "The protest holds. You eat. You walk. The hum under the city stays steady — until it doesn't."

Act 2 — Pre-Blast · Mid-Afternoon
The hum · Ash on a rooftop · positioning players

GM Say — Mid-afternoon transition

"Mid-afternoon. The protest is still running — voices hoarse now, watch rotation changed, sun past its peak. Three blocks east of the crowd, where Eastwick's residential streets meet the Network infrastructure, the world still sounds like Lumencrest: the cable-hum under everything, tram bells, the Copperstring's sound check bleeding from the Entertainment Quarter. Normal. Except to anyone who's learned to listen — the hum has a texture today. Slightly strained. Like a voice holding a note too long."

GM Info — Position the party

Ask: Where are you when mid-afternoon hits? Common answers: still at the border, at Lida's, walking Wire Road toward the hub, in the Copperstring, helping Brinne deliver candles toward Eastwick's eastern blocks. The hub is three blocks from the protest — close enough that the crowd hears the blast. Players at the border are in the panic wave. Players in Eastwick are in the dark when the lights die. Players at the hub are in the danger zone.

😨 Fear Spends — Pre-Blast
  • Ash on the rooftop — two blocks from the hub, a young man on a slate roof, watching the access door. He sees the party look up. He leaves. Fast. No chase yet — just the image.
  • The hum stops for one heartbeat — citywide, imperceptible to most. Lurielle and Greer feel it. One second of absolute Network silence. Then it returns. Wrong pitch.
Hub location — GM map (verbal)
  • Protest: Lamplight / Shadowside border, western edge of today's action.
  • Hub: Eastwick neighborhood, eastern Lamplight Ward — a brick utility structure, secondary feed node, NOT the main distribution spine.
  • Feeds: Eastwick residential grid + partial Entertainment Quarter (Copperstring block).
  • Distance: Hub ↔ protest ≈ 3 blocks. Hub ↔ Copperstring ≈ 2 blocks. Hub ↔ Brinne's Cooperative ≈ 4 blocks.

Act 3 — The Explosion
Targeted detonation · darkness spreads · three injured · panic
~45 min ▾
GM Say — The explosion (read aloud, then stop)

"It is not a fireball. It is a crack — sharp, architectural, the sound of something technical being severed. The hub building on the eastern block buckles inward. No mushroom cloud. Oily smoke and the smell of scorched conduit. The Lumen Network's hum — the sound you've stopped noticing because it's always there — stops in a ring around you. Streetlamps die. Shop signs go black. The Copperstring's sound check cuts mid-note, two blocks away, like someone closed a fist around the music."

"Three blocks west, the protest crowd screams. Not one sound — a hundred decisions at once. Some people run toward the blast. Some run away. City watch breaks formation. Someone is shouting for medics. Someone else is shouting about terrorism. The light is gone in Eastwick. Afternoon becomes dusk becomes a darkness that has weight."

Injuries — canon headcount

Three injured in the immediate hub area. (1) Toma Vess — maintenance adjacency worker, serious: shrapnel, leg, unconscious. (2) Pella Morr — street vendor, moderate: burns, mobile, shock. (3) Child (name: Jex Aldeth, 12) — minor: cut glass, crying, looking for their cousin who was at the hub fence. Do not kill civilians for shock value. The serious case is real but stable if helped within 10 minutes.

😨 Fear Spends — Blast & Immediate Aftermath
  • Secondary collapse — conduit housing falls toward the street. Dexterity DIF 13 or take 2d6 damage. Blocks a witness route.
  • Lurielle's resonance spike — bioluminescence flares visible in the dark; she tastes copper; the blast frequency matches pre-classification data in her notes.
  • Greer reads the blast pattern — Instinct DIF 14 or Fear-forced clarity: this was shaped to kill infrastructure, not people. Someone knew the hub's architecture.
  • Panic stampede from the protest — crowd surges into Eastwick streets. Strength/Agility DIF 12 to stay upright. Someone trampled if ignored.
  • Toma Vess worsens — the serious case starts bleeding faster. Help now or lose them.
  • Darkness spreads block by block — the failure cascades slower than expected, like a deliberate drain. Eastwick fully dark; Copperstring flickers; border still lit. Someone chose this feed map.
Blast scene: 2–3 Fear spends max. Each should change a decision, not just deal damage.
— Forensics in the chaos —
What perceptive players can learn (rolls or auto if adjacent)
  • Knowledge DIF 14 (Lurielle): non-standard incendiary — the burn pattern is directional, aimed at junction couplings, not fuel storage. Custom work.
  • Instinct DIF 13 (Greer): the hub was chosen for secondary status — maximum neighborhood disruption, minimum citywide cascade. Calculated.
  • Finesse DIF 14 (Basil): fresh scrape marks on the hub's access panel. Tool marks not city maintenance standard. Partial boot print on the ledge — small feet, running stance, departed toward the Entertainment Quarter.
  • Anyone at scene, Instinct DIF 12: one witness — elderly woman (Marta Henwick) who saw "a young man leave the door" but will not tell watch (distrusts uniforms). Will tell someone who helps the injured.
No Investigation

Strong Read
Architecture knowledge + witness + tool marks → trail toward Entertainment Quarter / rooftop observer. Ash glimpse connects if they saw him pre-blast. Investigation thread opens Session 4.
😨 Fear Spend — Witness Window
  • Ash glimpsed in the alley — leaving the blast radius, hood half-down, looking back once. Not the bomber — the watcher. Chase DCs apply.

If Toma reaches her before sundown, she stabilizes him fully. She asks no politics first — only symptoms. Second question: "Was anyone trying to help when you arrived?" If the party brought him: +1 Halfling Quarter goodwill, Brinne hears their names by evening.

Act 5 — Evening Fallout
Watch investigation stalls · Theron statement · Wren & Ash offscreen
~30 min ▾
GM Say — Dusk in Eastwick

"Evening, and Eastwick is still dark. Candles in windows — Brinne's stockpile, Lida's oven still warm from stored heat, neighbors sharing lamp oil. The Copperstring is closed mid-set. Someone two districts over is posting about the music that stopped. City watch has taped the hub site and posted two guards who don't know what they're guarding. The investigation, according to everyone who would know, has nothing."

GM Info — Watch investigation (background)

23 interviews by end of day. Zero productive leads in the official file. Witnesses who distrust watch (Marta Henwick type) stay silent. Protest organizers publicly condemn the blast — they lose moral high ground if they're blamed. The Vault Guard noted the event in operations log; they did not deploy. This is city watch jurisdiction, and city watch is overwhelmed.

Lida Birch — evening at the bakery

"Line's still long. Dark makes people hungry. Brinne sent word — if you're the ones who carried Toma, your bread's free tonight. Don't make a thing of it."

Gossip hub: Theron's statement already circulating on printed broadsheets. Someone says Expansionists will use this. Someone else says Green Covenant. Lida doesn't speculate — she listens.
— Theron Ashwick's public statement —
😨 Fear Spend — Political Aftershock
  • Theron's statement finds the party — broadsheet + crier at Wire Road. Expansionist framing lands hard. A neighbor blames protestors. Fight or de-escalate.
Broadsheets — Theron Ashwick (read or hand out)

"Today's attack on Lumencrest's Lumen infrastructure was not an accident. It was an act of anti-civilization terrorism — a deliberate strike against the Network that sustains eighty thousand lives. Those who believe that disruption of the common good is a legitimate form of political speech have now shown us the cost of that belief. The Expansionist faction has long argued that our city requires the capacity to defend its essential systems. Today's events demonstrate why. I call on the Council to convene in emergency session and to authorize the protective measures this city has too long deferred." — Theron Ashwick, Senior Councilor, Radiant Core

He's not wrong that the blast was deliberate. He is using it to advance the military vote. Sevan Vael will call for calm tomorrow. Mira Ashton will argue for investigation over expansion. The party doesn't need to meet these people yet — but the city is arguing around them while they eat cold bread in the dark.
