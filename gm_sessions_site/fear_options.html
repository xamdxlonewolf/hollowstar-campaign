Act 1 — Opening Scene
The Crows All Scatter at Once
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

Use this while players are doing their morning routines, before the council meeting. Best triggered during Greer's scene — it's her grove, she reads animals, she will notice this immediately.

GM Say — Read this aloud

"Between one breath and the next, the crows are gone. All of them. Not startled into flight — just gone, like something turned off a switch. The canopy where they were is empty and the silence they left behind has a specific quality. Greer — you've never heard the grove go this quiet this fast. Not from a predator. Not from anything you can name."

GM Info — What's actually happening

A shard fragment — being transported through the grove's eastern boundary by an Unseen Guild courier — crossed the spirit web threshold briefly. The animals felt it before the web could register it. By the time anyone investigates, the courier is gone. This is foreshadowing the shard bear encounter on the road, and a hint that shard material is moving through the region systematically, not accidentally.

Thorn feels it too. He looks at the eastern treeline for exactly three seconds, then looks at Greer, then looks away. He will say nothing about this unless directly asked, and even then: "Something crossed. It's gone now."

Right Now

Tension. Greer is unsettled before the council meeting. The grove feels like it's holding its breath. Good mood-setter for a difficult morning.

This Session

If they take the grove path and encounter the shard bear, Greer may connect this moment to that one. Let her make the link herself — don't point it out.

Long Term

Seeds the Unseen Guild's shard transport routes as a thread. In Lumencrest, when they eventually learn about the Guild's supply chain, Greer may remember this morning.

Act 1 — Opening Scene
The Heart Stone Pulses Once
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

Best used during Greer's morning offering at the Heart Stone. Can also work if Lurielle is near it taking notes. Not both in the same scene — pick one.

GM Say — Greer version

"The stone is warm the way it always is — and then it isn't. For two seconds it's hot enough to hurt, pressing back against your hand like a heartbeat from something very far below. Then it settles. Back to warm. Back to quiet. You've touched this stone ten thousand times. It has never done that."

GM Say — Lurielle version (alternative)

"Your quill stops moving. Something changed — in the air, in your skin, in the faint bioluminescence in your forearm that you've mostly learned to ignore. The Stone's warmth spiked and receded in the time it took to notice. Thorn, across the clearing, looks at it for a long moment. He does not look surprised."

GM Info — What's actually happening

The Heart Stone is connected to the Dreamer's dreaming mind — it is a threshold point, a thin place where the Spirit Realm and Mortal Realm almost touch. The approaching Hollostar, visible in daylight, is close enough now that its resonance is reaching existing threshold points. The Stone isn't reacting to a local event. It's reacting to something cosmic that's getting closer.

Thorn knows this, roughly. He's been feeling the Stone's changes for eight years. The pulse today is the most dramatic one yet. He will tell Greer, if asked directly after the council meeting: "It's been listening to something for years. Today it heard a response." That's as specific as he'll get.

Lurielle's reaction is the more scientifically interesting one — her Hollowstar-adjacent heritage means she doesn't just feel the pulse, she briefly resonates with it. If she makes a Knowledge roll DIF 13, she can note in her journal that the frequency of the pulse matched something she read in a pre-classification research paper at the Endless Eye. She doesn't know what that means yet.

Right Now

Thorn walks toward Emberford slightly earlier than planned. Something has shifted in his assessment of the morning's urgency.

This Session

If Lurielle made the Knowledge roll, she has a notation in her journal that will become significant when she reaches Lumencrest's archives.

Long Term

This is the first tangible evidence that the Endless Eye's decline, the approaching star, and the grove's spiritual changes are connected. Tier 1 secret groundwork — see The Truth document.

Act 1 — Opening Scene
Lurielle's Bioluminescence Flickers Involuntarily
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

While Lurielle is near the Heart Stone taking notes. Works best as a standalone moment — use this OR the Heart Stone pulse above, not both in the same scene. This version is more personal and slightly embarrassing for Lurielle.

GM Say

"Lurielle — your arm. The bioluminescence under your skin that you've spent most of your life learning to control flickers once, blue-white, visible even in morning light. Then gone. You clap your hand over it, but it's too late — Greer saw it, if Greer was watching. You don't know why it happened. You make a note in your journal: 'proximity to Stone threshold — involuntary resonance — query: frequency match with pre-reclassification data?' Then you close the journal quickly."

GM Info — What's actually happening

Lurielle's Faerie-Ribbet hybrid heritage means she carries traces of Hollostar resonance in her biology — the Endless Eye's influence is literally in her genetics, passed down from 700 years of her ancestors living near an active core. The Heart Stone is a different thought, a different frequency, but close enough to trigger a response. She's not in danger. She is, however, a living Hollostar-resonance sensor, which will become increasingly relevant.

If Greer noticed: She can ask. Lurielle will deflect the first time. On a Presence DIF 12 or genuine warmth from Greer, Lurielle will admit: "It's happened before. Near large concentrations of shard material. Never near something like this. I don't have a good explanation."

Thorn, if he saw it: He walks over, looks at her arm for a moment, and says: "The stone noticed you." Then he walks away. This will bother Lurielle for the rest of the session.

Right Now

Lurielle is slightly rattled and slightly embarrassed. Greer and Lurielle have an unexpected connection moment if Greer asks about it.

This Session

On the grove path, when the shard bear's fragment glows blue-white, Lurielle will recognize the frequency. This gives her the Knowledge roll connection ahead of time.

Long Term

Lurielle is a living resonance instrument. The party will eventually realize this is useful. So will people who shouldn't know about it.

🏛️ Council Meeting — Beat 2: Caelen Dismisses the Rumors
Council Meeting — Beat 2
Thorn Says "No"
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

After Caelen dismisses the Green Covenant rumors as "fringe agitation." Thorn has been sitting silently. He doesn't raise his hand. He just speaks.

GM Say — Thorn's delivery

"Thorn doesn't move. Doesn't lean forward. He says one word into the pause after Caelen finishes: 'No.' Then he goes quiet. Caelen looks at him. Thorn doesn't look back. The room waits for more. Nothing comes."

GM Info — What Thorn means and what Caelen does

Thorn is not disputing Caelen's assessment of threat level. He is disputing the underlying premise — that the Green Covenant's actions are "fringe" rather than a legitimate response to legitimate provocation. In his understanding, the Thorns are wrong in method and right in cause. He won't explain this in the council hall.

Caelen's response reveals something: he looks at Thorn for exactly one second, then smiles slightly and says: "I appreciate the guardian's perspective. The Lumencrest Council remains confident in its intelligence assessment." He has been briefed on Thorn. He was ready for this. That readiness is itself information.

Marta's reaction: She writes something down. If players observe this, a Instinct DIF 12 tells them she's writing the word "No" with a question mark next to it. She trusts Thorn's read of situations more than she trusts Lumencrest's intelligence briefings.

Partial

Success

They see Marta's note AND they notice that one council member — Fen Crane's ally — didn't look at Thorn at all after he spoke. Deliberate avoidance.

Right Now

The room is slightly uncomfortable. Caelen reasserts control quickly — too quickly. The meeting moves on but the moment sits.

This Session

After the meeting, if Greer speaks to Thorn, she can ask what he meant. His answer: "The attacks happened. The reports are accurate. He knows they're accurate."

Long Term

Caelen was briefed on Thorn by name. House Vael has intel on Ember Grove specifically. This becomes relevant when players investigate Vael's operations in Lumencrest.

Council Meeting — Beat 2
A Council Member Visibly Relaxes
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

After Caelen's dismissal of the Covenant rumors lands well — particularly if Thorn's "No" doesn't get traction or is smoothly moved past. One of Fen Crane's economic allies physically relaxes in their chair.

GM Say

"Down the table from you, one of the council members — a man in his forties, someone you've seen at the mill — lets out a breath he's been holding. His shoulders drop about an inch. He picks up his water cup. The tension he was carrying about the Covenant question just... left. Caelen's answer was the answer he needed to hear."

GM Info — Who this is and what it means

This is Aldric Shorn, a council member whose family owns the supply contracts for the western logging crews. If the Covenant rumors had escalated into a real concern, his contracts would be suspended. Caelen's dismissal protects his income. He is not corrupt — he's economically self-interested, which is different. He will vote for the lumber offer.

Why this is a Fear spend: Greer reads the room and realizes the economic vote for the offer is stronger than she thought. Before this moment, she might have assumed the Covenant rumors would give people pause. After this moment, she understands: the economic case is winning the room regardless of the safety concerns. The offer has more support than Marta is showing.

Council Meeting — Beat 4
Caelen Addresses Thorn by Name
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

After Thorn's one-sentence statement — "The eastern edge borders the grove's spirit boundary. This isn't a timber question." — instead of pivoting to Marta, Caelen addresses Thorn directly by name.

GM Say — Caelen's line, delivered pleasantly

"Caelen turns to face Thorn directly. He smiles — not unkindly. 'Guardian Oakwise, I believe you'll find our survey documentation satisfies the concerns your tradition would raise. We've taken considerable care to map the boundary as described by your community. I'd be happy to share the survey with you personally before the week is out.' He holds the smile. He was ready for Thorn specifically. That readiness is visible to anyone paying attention."

GM Info — Why this is significant

Caelen was briefed on Thorn Oakwise before coming to Emberford. House Vael's intelligence on the grove is specific enough to include the guardian by name, his likely objection, and a prepared response. This means Vael has been watching Ember Grove specifically — it's not a routine lumber acquisition. The grove's spirit boundary is relevant to their actual goals, not just inconvenient to work around.

Thorn's reaction: He looks at Caelen for a long moment. Then he says: "The survey was prepared by your surveyors." And goes back to silence. He means: the survey was designed to find what House Vael needed it to find. Caelen knows Thorn means this. He smiles anyway.

What players should catch: Caelen knows Thorn's name. He knows the specific tradition-based objection Thorn would raise. He has a prepared counter. This level of prep is disproportionate to a routine timber deal.

This is Sera Millbrook, 50s, textiles. Not corrupt — genuinely calculating economic benefit for the town. She's not dismissing Thorn; she's saying the financial case is stronger than the spiritual case in the current room.

The dynamic this creates: Marta is now managing three visible yes-votes (Fen Crane, Aldric Shorn if you used that fear spend, and now Sera Millbrook) against Thorn's quiet no and whatever the players might say. She knows this. The "one week to consider" offer is a genuine attempt to buy time for a better argument to emerge — not indecision, pragmatism.

If Greer speaks now (Beat 6): She's speaking into an uphill room. Marta will listen carefully. The economic faction will be respectful but unconvinced unless Greer says something they haven't heard before — naming Goldwood specifically, or describing what the spirit boundary actually protects and why losing it matters practically as well as spiritually.

Right Now

The room's lean is now unmistakable. Greer has a harder argument to make. Basil notes the economic appetite and files it — this town can be moved by money, which is relevant to his own read of the situation.

This Session

Long Term

Council Meeting — Pickpocket Window
Caelen's Guard Drifts Closer to the Satchel
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

As people begin to stand and mill after the meeting closes. Use this right before Basil might act — it creates a decision point between rolling at disadvantage now or waiting for a cleaner window that might not come.

GM Say — To the whole table, casually

"As people start rising and moving around, Caelen's guard — who's been standing near the door, easy to not think about — takes two steps toward the side table where the satchel is. Not alert, not aggressive. Just... repositioning. Like muscle memory."

GM Info — Mechanic and decision point

If Basil acts now with the guard nearby: Finesse DIF 14 at disadvantage (roll an extra Fear die, keep the highest two dice total). The guard isn't watching Basil — but peripheral awareness is closer than Basil would like.

If Basil waits: Caelen finishes with Fen Crane and retrieves the satchel himself in roughly 60 seconds. The window closes. No pickpocket opportunity without a significant distraction first.

Creating a distraction: Any party member can attempt to draw the guard's attention (Presence DIF 12 to get him to look away for 10 seconds — enough for a clean attempt at normal DIF 14).

The Full Pickpocket Decision Tree
Act now (guard close): Finesse DIF 14, disadvantage
Distract guard first: Presence DIF 12, then Finesse DIF 14 normal
Wait it out: Window closes. No attempt possible.
Failure (any path)

Guard notices. Steps between Basil and bag. No scene. No accusation. Just a flat look and a quiet positioning. Caelen sees none of this — Basil escapes without escalation, but empty-handed.

Partial

Gets the letter. Caelen pats his bag a minute later and frowns. He doesn't make a scene — he's calculating. He'll be more careful and more observant of Basil going forward.

Full Success

Clean lift. Full intelligence — letter and schedule card. Caelen notices nothing.

Right Now

Basil has to decide immediately. The pressure of the decision is the point — make it feel urgent.

This Session

On the road, if Basil failed, Caelen's guard is slightly more watchful than he should be for a routine departure. Could matter on the road route.

Long Term

A full success here means Basil has the Radiant Core courier timing before arriving in Lumencrest. He doesn't need to surveil from scratch. This is a significant head start.

Caelen is doing a real-time assessment of how much local resistance he'll need to manage in Lumencrest. If Basil successfully pickpocketed the satchel, that card will be inside it — one word. The word is: either "manageable", "escalate", or "withdraw" depending on what Greer says.

This is Tara's moment — let her answer fully in character before continuing. Whatever she says is real. The council hears it. Marta writes it down. Caelen files it.

Council Meeting — Exit
Caelen's Exit Look at Basil
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

As Caelen leaves the council hall. Use this regardless of whether the pickpocket succeeded or failed — it works either way. Best delivered as the last beat of the council meeting before people disperse.

GM Say

"Caelen is at the door. His guard is half a step ahead of him. And then he stops — just for a moment — and looks directly at Basil. No expression. No accusation. Just a look, the kind that says something has been noticed without specifying what. Then he's gone. His footsteps don't change pace. He didn't slow down for it. It lasted less than a second."

GM Info — What Caelen noticed and what he'll do

If the pickpocket succeeded: Caelen felt nothing. But his guard, who was watching the room professionally, noticed Basil's positioning was slightly wrong for a casual observer. He's reported it to Caelen in a whisper on the way out. Caelen noticed Basil already — the look is his way of acknowledging that he's filed this, nothing more. He won't act on it in Emberford.

If the pickpocket failed: Same scenario, but the guard reported the approach more clearly. Caelen suspects a lift attempt. He'll check his bag before leaving town. When he finds the letter missing — if it's missing — he'll send word to Lumencrest before arriving himself.

If the pickpocket didn't happen at all: Caelen simply noticed Basil as "the one who wasn't from here and wasn't affiliated with the grove." He's a rogue-shaped person in a room full of farmers and druids. Caelen is noting him for the same reason he notes everything — professional habit.

In Lumencrest: Caelen will mention Basil's name to a contact if questioned about Ember Grove. Not as a threat — as information. He doesn't know Basil's family name. He knows a face. Whether that matters depends on how visible Basil is in Lumencrest.

Right Now

Basil has been clocked. The party knows it. Creates useful tension before the road.

This Session

If they take the road route and Caelen is on it, he will not interact with them — but his guard will watch the party's direction when the roads diverge.

Long Term

Caelen is going to Lumencrest. If Basil operates openly there, there's a chance Caelen puts the face to the name. Depends on how careful Basil is and how much heat gets generated.

A Fourth Scout Steps from the Hedgerow
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

Mid-combat, when the three scouts are engaged and the party looks like they have it under control. The fourth was waiting — crossbow ready, positioned behind a hedgerow to the party's flank.

GM Say

"From the hedgerow to your left — movement. A fourth figure steps out, and unlike the others he hasn't been running. He's been waiting. The crossbow in his hands is loaded and leveled. 'That's enough of that,' he says, like someone who's said it before."

Fourth Scout — Crossbow
Was waiting · First shot at advantage · Will retreat if the three go down
HP: 10
DIF: 12
Crossbow: 1d10+1 (first shot advantage)
Reload: Can't fire consecutive rounds without moving

Tactic: He fires once, then uses his position to threaten rather than actually fire again — he doesn't want to reload in the open. If the three main scouts go down, he backs into the hedgerow and disappears. He's the most dangerous and also the most cowardly.

GM Info — Who he is and what it means

He's the senior of the four — the others were muscle he hired locally. He's the one with Guild connections. If he escapes, he will report the party's composition, abilities, and direction to his Coppertown contact. If he's captured, he knows only that he picks up work from a contact at the Coppertown waystation who pays in clean coin and asks no questions. He cannot lead them to the Guild. He can confirm it exists.

Right Now

The fight gets harder and more interesting. A ranged threat forces different tactics.

This Session

If he escapes, the runner and the fourth scout both report — the Guild gets a cleaner picture of the party.

Long Term

Captured: one piece of Guild intelligence acquired. Escaped: Guild awareness of the party accelerates by 1 stage.

One Scout Grabs Lurielle's Satchel
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

When one of the scouts realizes they're losing the fight. Instead of pressing the attack, he makes a grab for the most obvious valuables — Lurielle's research satchel is visible and within reach.

GM Say

"The scout nearest Lurielle doesn't attack. He grabs — one hand closing around the strap of her satchel, pulling hard, and he's already backing toward the tree line with it. He doesn't care about the fight anymore. He's going to run."

GM Info — What's in the satchel and why it matters

Lurielle's road journal and research notes — including her notation about the Heart Stone resonance (if that Fear spend was used), her records from the Endless Eye, and critically: her memory of which documents were reclassified before she left. This is not replaceable. If the satchel is lost, she loses her primary research thread going into Lumencrest. She can reconstruct some of it from memory, but not all.

Mechanical choice: The party has to split attention between the ongoing fight and stopping the grab. Whoever pursues the scout must leave the main combat — meaning one fewer fighter for at least one round. This costs a Fear spend to create but creates genuine consequence drama.

Success

Run him down cleanly. Satchel recovered. He surrenders rather than fight — he's not paid enough for this.

Right Now

Split attention, genuine stakes, great dramatic moment.

This Session

Long Term

If captured: this scout has Guild-adjacent knowledge. If the satchel was taken and recovered from him, he's now seen her reclassification notation. Does he even know what he was looking at? Probably not. But someone further up the chain might.

One Scout Recognizes Basil's Fighting Style
😨 1 Fear
What triggers this

When Basil does something particularly competent in the fight — a clean disarm, a precise hit, a feint that reads as trained. One of the scouts notices before he goes down.

GM Say — The scout's line, slightly surprised

"The scout you're fighting takes a step back. He's still in the fight but something shifted in his face. 'You fight like someone who was taught to fight.' He says it like a fact, not an insult. 'Not self-taught. Taught.' He's looking at your hands, not your eyes."
