GM Reference & Script — Session 1

Ember Grove — Where It Begins

Gold Month, 700 AR. A lumber negotiation that smells wrong, a fully scripted council meeting, and the road to Lumencrest.

🌿 Greer — Faun Druid (Tara) ✨ Lurielle — Faerie/Ribbet Wizard 🗡️ Basil — Rogue
⏱ 3–4 hours
📅 Gold Month 8, 700 AR
📍 Emberford → Road to Lumencrest
🗺️
Session Overview & Flow Read First ▾
~40 min
Act 1 — Ember Grove at Dawn. Morning routines. Introduce all three characters. Thorn summons Greer to the council. Goal: ground the party before things get complicated.
~60 min
Act 2 — The Council Meeting. Fully scripted. House Vael lumber offer, Green Covenant rumors, pickpocket window. Goal: plant every thread that pulls them to Lumencrest.
~30 min
Act 3 — Planning. Party digests info. Basil reveals the heist (or hints). All three realize they're going the same direction. Goal: let players drive the Lumencrest decision themselves.
~45 min
Act 4 — The Road. One encounter — thugs (road) or shard bear (grove path). First real dice moment. Goal: memorable first combat that shows the Daggerheart system.
~10 min
Session End — The Plateau. First view of Lumencrest at night. End on wonder and the faint flicker. Goal: leave them desperate for Session 2.
💡

If you're behind: cut Act 3 planning short — Basil just announces they're all going to Lumencrest and moves on. The plateau ending is non-negotiable. That image is the session's entire emotional payoff.

Act 1 — Ember Grove at Dawn
Opening scene · Character intros · Thorn sends them to the meeting
~40 min ▾
GM Say — Opening narration

"Gold Month in the Central Heartlands. Early morning, the kind where you can still see your breath. The Ember Grove is five hundred acres of ancient oak, ash, and beech — some of these trees were old when your grandparents' grandparents were born. The air smells like turned earth and the sweet-sharp edge of autumn. The leaves are changing: amber, rust, gold. Some say the Grove holds that color year-round, even in spring. If you've lived here long enough, you believe it. The Heart Stone at the grove's center is warm to the touch — has been for two years now, though Thorn hasn't said what he thinks it means."

GM Info — Ask Tara first

Before describing anything else, turn to Tara: "Greer — you've been here twenty-five years. It's a normal morning before a council meeting. What does your routine look like? What do you do first when you wake up?" Let her answer. Whatever she says is true. Her details become the grove's details. Don't correct or redirect — just add to it.

Sensory details to layer in during Greer's scene
  • Crows: Louder than usual this week. Six or seven where there'd usually be two. Greer would notice this — it's a signal. Don't explain what it means yet.
  • Heart Stone: Warm when she does her morning offering. Not hotter than usual. Just… warm. Present. Like it's paying attention.
  • The spirit web: Quiet in a specific way. Not absent — watchful. Like the forest is holding its breath. She's felt this for eight years and it still sets something off in the back of her instincts.
  • Gold Month feel: Harvest season smell. The Emberfork river is low and brown, running fast from last week's rains. Somewhere in Emberford, Bess is already baking.
😨 Fear Spends — Act 1 Opening
  • The crows all scatter at once — between one breath and the next, all gone. Something crossed the eastern spirit boundary. No visible threat. Just the absence where the birds were. Thorn notices from across the grove and says nothing.
  • The Heart Stone pulses once — warm becomes briefly hot under Greer's hand, then settles. It has never done that before in her presence. Thorn looks at it for a long moment, then turns and walks toward Emberford.
  • Lurielle's bioluminescence flickers involuntarily — she's been near the Stone taking notes. The Faerie-Ribbet heritage responds to something in its energy. She claps her hand over her arm, embarrassed. She makes a note before she covers it.
Use one at most during Act 1. These are mood tools, not threats. Save your Fear economy for the council meeting.
GM Say — Introducing Lurielle

"Lurielle — you arrived at the Ember Grove about two weeks ago, after a long road from the Endless Eye through Highpass and Greenhollow. Thorn Oakwise said almost nothing when you turned up. He gave you a dry place to sleep and twelve words total, all of them precise. You've been spending your mornings sitting near the Heart Stone with your journal, trying to describe what you're feeling in academic language and finding that it keeps failing you. The trees here feel aware in a way no book prepared you for. It's morning. What are you doing right now?"

GM Say — Introducing Basil

"Basil — you're not a forest person. You've been here three days, which is two days longer than you planned. You told Greer and Lurielle you're passing through on the way to Lumencrest — a vague kind of truth. You've spent two mornings watching the road from Emberford and this morning a traveler mentioned that a representative from a Lumencrest noble house arrived yesterday for today's council meeting. You don't know which house yet. That information made this detour worth it. What are you doing right now?"

🌿

Give each player one small moment before Thorn appears. About 5 minutes each. No dice needed — this is pure character establishment. Then Thorn is the natural transition to the council.

🌿 Thorn Oakwise — Calling Greer to the Meeting
Voice: Still. Not cold, not warm — present. Short sentences. Let silence sit after each one. Do not fill the pause. He never rushes and never repeats himself.

"Greer."

He waits for her to turn. Not impatiently — he just waits. Then:

"The council meeting is this morning. Emberford asked for druid presence. You'll come."

A pause. He glances at Basil and Lurielle without any particular expression — assessing, not dismissing. Then, as an afterthought:

"If your companions want to hear, the hall is open."

He's already walking toward Emberford before the sentence is finished. He doesn't check if they follow. He assumed they would.

Act 2 — The Council Meeting (Full Script)
Fully scripted · NPC introductions · Dialogue options · Fear windows
~60 min ▾
GM Say — Arriving at the Council Hall

"The Emberford council hall is a long timber building on the east square — practical, not grand. You've passed it a hundred times. Inside: a horseshoe of a dozen chairs, the ceiling low enough that the room feels full even with only fifteen people in it. Morning light comes in sideways from two high windows. It smells of old wood and the particular staleness of a room used for serious conversations. Several Emberford elders are already seated. Thorn takes a chair at the far end without being invited and without ceremony — he has been attending these meetings for thirty years. And near the middle of the horseshoe, someone who wasn't here yesterday: a young man, early twenties, human, in travel clothes that fit too well to be practical. A small pin at his collar — a sigil. Two-toned. A name you might recognize or might not yet."

GM Info — What the sigil is

The Vael house sigil: a silver tower on black. Anyone who has had dealings with Lumencrest nobility or merchant politics would recognize it (Greer — Instinct DIF 12 from Covenant briefings; Lurielle — Knowledge DIF 11 from Endless Eye merchant intelligence; Basil — automatic, he came here for exactly this reason). Don't make them roll unless they specifically examine it. It's there if they look.

GM Say — Introducing Elder Marta Dawnbrook

"At the head of the horseshoe, Elder Marta Dawnbrook — you've all seen her around Emberford. Sixty-something, iron-grey hair pulled back, the kind of face that has made ten thousand practical decisions and shows it. She's reviewing notes on a piece of paper and doesn't look up immediately when you come in. When she does, she nods at Thorn and finds seats with her eyes for the newcomers. Not welcoming exactly — acknowledging. There's a difference."

— Meeting Beat 1: Routine Business —
Elder Marta Dawnbrook — Opening the meeting

"Right. Let's get through this. We have the bridge repair estimate — again — which I'll summarize as 'more than we have and less than we need.' The ford toll has been up four percent since Bloommonth which helps. Harvest reports from the east farms are in — Aldric?"

An older man at the far end — Aldric from the Grove, the community-facing druid — gives a short positive summary. Good yield. Grove herbs doing well. The mood in the room is routine, slightly tired. This is a town that has had these meetings for forty years.
Elder Marta — raising the Green Covenant rumors

"Before we get to the main business — Bren, you had something."

A farmer at the back, Bren Ashfield — weathered, 50s, the kind of man who doesn't speak at councils unless something is bothering him enough that he can't sleep — stands up.
Bren Ashfield — the worried farmer

"Three men from the western logging crew came back last week without their equipment. Wouldn't say what happened. Just said they weren't going back. And I heard from my cousin in Greenwatch that there was a fire at a surveying camp two weeks ago on the Coppertown road — equipment, not people, but. There's talk of Green Covenant involvement. I want to know if the council knows anything."

He sits down. The room is a little quieter. Elder Marta looks around the table. Several people look at Thorn. Thorn doesn't react.
GM Info — Player moment before Caelen speaks

This is the first player window. Greer may react to the Green Covenant mention — she has complicated feelings about the Thorns and will know these incidents are likely real. Lurielle may be noting everything. Basil is watching Caelen, who has not reacted yet. Give them 30 seconds of table reaction before Caelen speaks. Don't rush past this moment.

— Meeting Beat 2: Caelen Dismisses the Rumors —
GM Say — Introducing Caelen Vael (first impression)

"The young man in the good travel clothes leans forward slightly. Up close he's carefully put-together — not just the clothes, the posture, the way he holds his hands still on the table. He's been taught to present well, and he does. He has his father's cheekbones and none of his father's authority, though he's working on it. He speaks before being invited to, but not rudely — as if it's simply natural that he would speak next."

Caelen Vael — dismissing the Covenant rumors

"If I may — these incidents come up every season near active logging operations. Fringe elements make noise. The Council of Lumens tracks Green Covenant activity closely and has seen no evidence of coordinated action in this region. If there were a real and present threat, I assure you I would not be here negotiating. I would be home."

Polite smile. Certain without being aggressive about it. He has said some version of this before — it sounds prepared.
Elder Marta — moving things along

"Noted. We'll take the precaution of informing the watchmen on the Coppertown road. Master Vael — shall we move to your proposal."

😨 Fear — Caelen Dismisses the Rumors
  • Thorn speaks — one word: "No." Then silence. Caelen looks at him. Thorn doesn't explain. The room gets slightly uncomfortable. Marta moves on before it has to be addressed.
  • A second council member visibly relaxes after Caelen's dismissal — one of Fen Crane's allies. Greer reads the room and realizes the vote on the lumber offer is closer than she thought. This is not good news.
— Meeting Beat 3: The Lumber Offer —
Caelen Vael — presenting the offer (stand up, pull out document)

"House Vael is seeking a timber rights agreement for the eastern managed woodland — not the grove, I want to be precise about that — the designated logging land adjacent to your eastern boundary. Fifty acres per year, managed rotation, reforestation clause included."

He places a sealed document on the table in front of Marta. It's well-produced — heavy paper, wax seal, everything formal.

"The price offered is eighteen gold per acre annually. That's — I believe — significantly above the current regional rate. We would begin operations after Frostmonth, which gives your council time to review the terms. Eastern edge only. This is House Vael's final offer."

That last sentence — "final offer" — lands with a small but deliberate weight. He knew it would. He's been told to say it exactly like that.
GM Info — The number is real

18 gold per acre per year is genuinely generous — the going rate in the region is 10–12 gold. This is Emberford's bridge repair, plus the south road, plus a year of buffer. Marta knows it. Fen Crane definitely knows it. This is why it's dangerous.

Fen Crane — the mill owner, supporting the offer (from his seat)

"That kind of money solves the bridge problem without the Accord subsidy we've been waiting on for fourteen months. I'm just saying it out loud."

Nobody tells him to be quiet. A few people nod slightly. The economic reality of Emberford is in this room.
— Meeting Beat 4: Thorn Speaks —
🌿 Thorn Oakwise — his one contribution to the meeting
Voice: Quieter than usual. He doesn't lean forward. He speaks like he's reporting a fact that everyone in the room would already know if they were paying attention.

"The eastern edge borders the grove's spirit boundary."

A pause long enough to feel deliberate.

"This isn't a timber question."

He says nothing else. He doesn't look at Caelen. He looks at Marta. Then he looks at Greer. Then he looks at the table. The sentence is complete. He's done.
Caelen Vael — responding to Thorn, perfectly smooth

"I have great respect for the grove's cultural significance to this community. The eastern boundary clause is specifically designed to avoid any encroachment on protected land. Our surveyors have mapped the spirit boundary as described by your druidic community and the designated area falls outside it entirely."

He's prepared for Thorn. He knows exactly who Thorn is. That "your druidic community" has a slight quality — not dismissive, but categorizing. Filing. It's subtle and probably only Greer catches it.
😨 Fear — After Thorn Speaks
  • Caelen addresses Thorn by name — "Guardian Oakwise, I believe you'll find the survey satisfies your concerns." Thorn looks at him for a long moment. It's the first time Caelen has looked slightly less certain. He was briefed on Thorn specifically. That briefing is visible.
  • A third council member speaks up in favor — not just Fen Crane now. The room's lean is visible. Marta is controlling her expression carefully. Greer reads this as worse than she expected.
— Meeting Beat 5: Questions — Player Window —
GM Info — Player question window (important)

After Thorn speaks and Caelen responds, Elder Marta opens the floor briefly: "The council will consider questions from those present before we move to deliberation." This is the players' formal chance to speak. They are guests, not members — they can ask questions, not vote. Let them use this. Each character has things to probe.

Questions players might ask — Caelen's scripted responses
If asked: "What does Lumencrest need the lumber for?"

"Infrastructure. The city is always growing — new residential districts, public works, the Accord infrastructure committee has several projects pending. Timber is a constant requirement."

Vague. True as far as it goes. Changes subject if pushed further. He genuinely doesn't know about the ships.
If asked: "What happened at Timberfall?"

"Timberfall is a different situation entirely. Different agreements, different oversight, different time period. I'd encourage the council not to draw comparisons that aren't supported by the specifics."

A tell. He's been briefed to deflect this exact comparison. The smoothness of the deflection is itself information.
If asked: "Why the eastern edge specifically?"

"Our surveyors identified it as the highest yield area in the designated zone that could be managed sustainably within a reforestation rotation. It's a practical decision, not a symbolic one."

This is a lie of omission. The eastern edge is higher yield AND it's the closest approach to the grove's spirit boundary — the Unseen Guild wants to test how close they can get.
If asked: "Why is this a 'final offer' at the first meeting?"

"House Vael has been in discussion with Emberford through intermediaries for several months. This is not a first meeting — this is the formal conclusion of that process. The terms are generous and the timeline is fair. We have other options if this community isn't interested."

That last sentence is mild pressure. He says it pleasantly. It lands anyway.
If asked about the Green Covenant (again, directly)

"I've addressed this. The Council of Lumens monitors Green Covenant activity. There is no credible threat in this region."

Shorter this time. Slightly less patient. He doesn't like this topic.
— Meeting Beat 6: Greer's Moment —
GM Info — Prompt Greer specifically

After the question window, look directly at Tara: "Greer — you've watched this exact conversation happen before, in a different town, fifteen years before Goldwood fell. Thorn is looking at you. Does Greer say anything?" Give her space to respond. Whatever she says, Marta listens. Marta is looking for a reason to say no that the economic faction will accept. Greer may be that reason — or may plant that seed for later.

Possible Greer moves and how the room reacts
  • She names Goldwood directly: Caelen deflects smoothly. But two council members who've never heard the specifics are now thinking about it. Marta writes something down.
  • She describes what the spirit boundary actually means: This is the most credible thing she can say in this room — she's the druid, this is her expertise. Even Fen Crane goes quiet. Caelen pivots to "our surveys respected the boundary," which sounds thinner now.
  • She asks to see the survey: Caelen offers to provide it within the week. This is a delay tactic. The offer expires in a week. He knows she knows this.
  • She says nothing: Also valid. Thorn nods once, almost imperceptibly, as if that was the correct choice and he's confirming it. There are battles not fought in the council hall.
— Meeting Beat 7: Council Defers —
Elder Marta Dawnbrook — closing the meeting

"The council will take the proposal under consideration. One week, as offered, to review the documentation. Master Vael, we'll send word to your accommodation by the end of Goldmonth."

A pause. She looks at Caelen with the expression of someone who has spent forty years reading rooms.

"We appreciate House Vael's interest in Emberford's development. We take our obligations to all our neighbors seriously — including the grove."

That last sentence is for Thorn. He doesn't acknowledge it but he heard it. Marta is telling him she hasn't decided yet, but she hasn't forgotten what Goldwood was either. The meeting is over. People begin to stand.
⚠️ The Pickpocket Window — Right Now

As people stand and mill, Caelen moves to retrieve his traveling satchel from the small side table where he left it. He sets it down to shake Fen Crane's hand — Fen approached him immediately, wanting to talk privately. The satchel is unattended for roughly 30 seconds. Basil will see this. Finesse vs. DIF 14.

Failure
Caelen's guard — who was standing near the door, easy to overlook — steps in front of Basil. Not aggressive. Just present. "Help you with something?" No letter. But now Caelen knows someone tried.
Partial
Gets the letter. Caelen pats his bag a minute later and frowns slightly. He doesn't make a scene. He thinks about it on the road. He'll be more careful — and more suspicious of the party.
Full Success
Clean lift. The letter AND a folded schedule card. Caelen notices nothing. Basil has the heist timing and the Guild evidence. Everything he came for.
What's in the letter (full success only — read to Basil's player privately)

Two items. First: a fragment of correspondence on Vael house paper referencing lumber shipments routed to Verdean shipyards — ships for "refugee transit operations, Phase 2." Signed with the Vael seal and a second mark: an eye with no pupil. 🔍 Unseen Guild

Second: a small folded schedule card — Caelen's personal itinerary for Lumencrest. The relevant entry reads: "RC handoff — Goldmonth 14, mid-afternoon. Courtyard entrance. Confirm with V." RC = Radiant Core. This is a courier handoff appointment — the moment Caelen transfers the House Vael diplomatic pouch to a contact inside the Core. It names neither the contact nor the pouch's contents, but it gives Basil exactly what he needs: a date, a district, and a window. The heist is not a chaotic grab — it's a precision intercept of the pouch during or just after that handoff, in plain sight, no noise. This is the kind of job Sage could never pull off. That's the point.

😨 Fear Spends — Council Meeting (use 1–2 total)
  • Before the pickpocket moment: Caelen's guard drifts slightly closer to the satchel, as if on instinct. Basil must roll at disadvantage OR wait and risk the window closing as Caelen retrieves the bag.
  • During the questions window: A council member asks Greer directly whether she thinks the grove is threatened — and Caelen is watching her answer with very careful attention. He's assessing how much local resistance he'll need to manage back in Lumencrest.
  • Caelen's exit: As he leaves, he pauses at the door and looks directly at Basil for just a moment — no expression, just a look. Then he's gone. He noticed something. Maybe just Basil's posture. Maybe something else.

Reference ▾
Thorn Oakwise
Guardian of Ember Grove · Human · Mid-fifties · He/him
🎭 Deliver every line as if it has been considered for a long time before being spoken. Short. Definitive. He never hedges or qualifies. Long silences are part of his communication — do not fill them. Think: someone so completely present that small talk would feel like an insult to the moment.

Thorn has lived in the Ember Grove for decades. He was chosen by the previous guardian after two years of observation. He still applies that same unhurried attention to everything and everyone. He began Greer's formal druidic training after another long period of watching — not because he doubted her, but because certainty was the only basis he works from. He does not do things quickly.

He is not warm in a social sense. He is present — fully there in every conversation, which can feel like warmth if you're used to people being elsewhere in their heads. The Grove is his community. The trees are his constituency. He sent Greer to Lumencrest because he cannot go himself and because she is ready, though he will not say this until pressed, and even then only once.

GM only: Thorn lost an earlier grove — before he came to Ember Grove — to logging thirty years ago. He understands the anger that creates Thorns members. He is choosing something harder than that anger. He has not spoken of this to anyone at Ember Grove and will not.
🌿 Thorn — After the meeting, alone with Greer
Quieter than usual. Standing at the grove's edge, not looking at her. Like he's telling her something the trees already know.

"The eastern edge offer. Timberfall heard the same terms, fifteen years before Goldwood fell. The price kept going up. The protections kept getting renegotiated."

He looks at her.

"Watch the people around the Vael name. Not what they want from the grove. Where what they take goes next."

Done. He turns back to the grove. The conversation is over.
🌿 Thorn — If asked "Why aren't you going to Lumencrest?"
The closest he gets to something like regret. Still flat delivery. This one costs him something small.

"The grove needs a guardian. I'm the guardian."

Long pause. Then:

"You've been ready for longer than you think, Greer."

That's the most he will say about this. Do not make him say more. The silence after it is the point.
🌿 Thorn — If asked about the Heart Stone
Still thinking. This is the one topic where he pauses longer than usual.

"It's been warm for two years. Something in the spirit layer is paying attention to this place."

If pushed for more:

"When I know what, I'll tell you."

He means this completely literally. He is not withholding. He genuinely isn't done thinking yet.
Caelen Vael
Youngest Vael sibling · House Representative · Human · Early 20s · He/him
🎭 Polished but not cruel. He's been trained to be the pleasant face in rooms. Reasonable tone, slight smile that never fully reaches his eyes when he's managing a situation. He agrees to disagree without ever seeming cornered. When caught off-guard, the pleasantness slips for just a second — and he notices that you noticed, and recovers.

The one who gets sent on errands. Capable enough to represent House Vael without being trusted with decisions that actually matter. He knows this about his position and he's not bitter about it yet. He was told the terms, told not to accept less, and handed a bag with a letter he hasn't read carefully enough to understand.

GM only: He doesn't know what the lumber is really for. He doesn't know about the ships or the refugees. He was handed a proposal and a letter to deliver to a Lumencrest contact. If the party ever confronted him with the full truth of what he's facilitating, his reaction would be genuine shock — and then a very rapid calculation about what it means for his family and himself.
Elder Marta Dawnbrook
Emberford Council Head · Human · Early 60s · She/her
🎭 Practical. Has made ten thousand hard decisions and looks it. Doesn't waste words. Genuinely conflicted about the Vael offer and will show it to anyone who engages her honestly after the meeting — she doesn't pretend otherwise to people she respects.

Not corrupt. She knows the Vael offer is the kind of money that solves real problems for real people in this town. She also knows what happened to Timberfall. She is hoping someone gives her a reason to say no that the economic faction will accept. The players, if they engage her after the meeting, may be that reason.

If approached after the meeting, privately: "The bridge needs fixing. The south road is half mud. I know what happened to Timberfall. I also know what happens to a town that can't maintain its infrastructure. Someone has to live in both of those realities at once and right now that's me."

Daggerheart Combat — First Session Reference
Read before either encounter · Core loop · Pacing notes
GM Primer ▾
⚔️ The Core Loop
Players act first→ Hope die + Fear die + stat→ Check which die won→ Spend Fear for enemy actions→ Repeat
No Turn Order

No initiative. Players act, then you spend Fear to have enemies act. It's a rhythm, not a queue. Anyone acts when it makes narrative sense.

Two Dice Always

Every roll: Hope die + Fear die + stat mod. Even on success, check which die was higher. Hope higher → player gains Hope. Fear higher → you gain Fear.

Attack vs. DIF

Players roll to hit. Total ≥ enemy DIF = hit, roll damage. Weak enemies: 12–13. Tough: 14–16. Boss: 17+.

Enemy Actions

Spend a Fear token → enemy acts. Announce it, describe it, deal damage. No roll for enemies — their attacks land unless a player does something active.

Taking Damage

Player rolls Armor dice, subtracts from damage. Remainder hits HP. HP = 0 → Death Move roll. Fail = down, not dead.

Hope Spends

Players spend Hope to: reroll one die, add +1d6 damage, or assist an ally (+1d6 to their roll for 1 Hope). Encourage assists — they create the best moments.

First-session GM notes
  • Describe before rolling. Always ask "what are you doing?" first. Description shapes the stat — flanking = Finesse, direct charge = Strength, reading the enemy = Instinct.
  • Make enemy actions feel like story. Not "8 damage." Say: "the scout's elbow catches you across the jaw — 8 damage, and you're pushed back toward the hedgerow."
  • Use partial successes. A 7–11 range is a hit with a complication. The spell lands but Lurielle is off-balance. The stab works but Basil is now visible. These create the best moments.
  • Spend Fear steadily. Hoarding it makes fights flat. Spend it for enemy actions, environmental escalations, and complicating moments. Your Fear economy IS the fight's heartbeat.

Road Route — Hired Thugs
Social or combat · Quick · Seeds Guild awareness of the party
Travel A ▾
GM Say — Setting the encounter

"Early afternoon, a few hours out of Emberford. The road runs through mixed farmland and scattered copse. Three travelers are sitting on a fallen log ahead — big, unhurried, watching you come down the road in a way that has the specific quality of people who've decided something about you before you arrive. One stands up. 'Nice road,' he says. 'Toll's two silver each to use it.'"

GM Info — What's happening

Guild-adjacent scouts testing the road for easy marks, loosely watching traffic near Caelen Vael. Not very dangerous. Will back down at real resistance. If they survive and get back to their Coppertown contact, they'll report "a rogue, a wizard, and a druid." This is how the Guild becomes aware of the party for Session 2.

Road Scouts (×3)
Opportunistic · Will retreat if 2 go down · One is always the runner
HP: 14 each
Armor: 1d6
DIF: 12
Attack: 1d8+1
Fear value: 1 each

Retreat trigger: Two of three down, OR any single visually dramatic action (Lurielle's bioluminescence flaring, Greer shifting, Basil producing a Silverhand seal as a bluff).

Social option: Presence vs. DIF 13. Success: they let the party pass. Partial: coin from two of them. Failure: annoyed, attack with +1d4 to first attack rolls.

😨 Fear Spends — Road Thugs
  • Fourth scout from the hedgerow — crossbow, 10 HP, DIF 12. Was waiting the whole time.
  • One grabs Lurielle's satchel — her research notes and road journal. Retreat toward tree line. Split attention or lose the bag.

"Late evening. The road crests a ridge — and the view opens up. You've been in forest and farmland for hours. And now, spread below you, is Lumencrest. Eighty thousand people.
