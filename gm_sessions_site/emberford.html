Arriving in Emberford
What to say · First impressions · Town layout
▾
GM: Say this when players first see Emberford from the road

"The road crests a gentle rise and Emberford opens up below you — a proper town, not a village, though it still has the feel of a place that grew up from one. A stone bridge carries the road over the Emberfork river, wide and brown this time of year, running fast from last week's rains. The town clusters on both banks. The older half sits on the east side — a dozen timber-frame buildings, the council hall, the square. The newer half crept across the river over the last generation, following the lumber trade. You can see the mill from here, its waterwheel turning slowly. Smoke from cookfires. Smell of bread and river mud and horses. A normal Gold Month morning."

Town facts — for if players ask
  • Population: Roughly 4,000 — mostly human, significant Faun presence near the grove, a handful of Drakona families running the smithy and the mill.
  • Economy: River crossing (trade toll), timber processing, farming. The mill provides a third of the town's income. Lumber buyers from Lumencrest visit seasonally — Gold Month especially.
  • Governance: Elder council of seven. Marta Dawnbrook chairs. Decisions are slow and argued about for months. Most residents consider this appropriate.
  • The ford itself: Shallow stone crossing just upstream of the bridge, used by farmers with carts. Free to use. The bridge costs 2 copper per cart, 1 copper per horse, foot traffic free. Elder Marta's family built the bridge three generations ago. This is mentioned more than it should be.
  • Six-Fold Faith: The Hearthkeeper temple on the east square is the main place of worship. Small but well-kept. The priest is a human man named Aldous Penn, 50s, broad hands, says very little and means all of it.
GM: Say this when players cross the bridge into town

"The bridge stones are worn smooth — thousands of boots, hundreds of cart wheels. On the far side, the east square opens up. The council hall on the left, the Hearthkeeper temple on the right, and between them The Emberflow Arms, the town's inn and tavern. A board outside has the rates chalked up. There's a dog sleeping in the inn doorway. It opens one eye when you approach, decides you're not interesting, and goes back to sleep."

Town layout — what's where
  • East Square: Council hall, Hearthkeeper temple, The Emberflow Arms (inn/tavern), Mira's General Goods. The social and civic heart of town.
  • East Bank (older): Older homes, the healer's cottage (Edda Marsh), Copperpin Tailor, the Stoneback Smithy.
  • The Bridge: Stone, three arches, 60 feet across. Guard post on the west end — one bored town watchman, Gorran, who checks carts for stolen timber.
  • West Bank (newer): The mill, lumber yard, newer housing, the tradesman's warehouse where Lumencrest buyers stay when the inn is full.
  • Grove Road: Leaves the east square heading north. One mile to the grove's edge. Greer knows every stone of it.

The Emberflow Arms
Rooms · Food & Drink · Staff · Atmosphere
Inn & Tavern ▾
GM: Describing the inn interior when they enter

"Inside the Emberflow Arms it's low-ceilinged and warm, with the particular smell of a tavern room that has been serving people for eighty years — woodsmoke, old beer, and something savory from the kitchen. The bar runs along the back wall. A rack of local clay mugs. Three tables occupied for morning, men with work-hardened hands wrapped around something hot. Behind the bar, a woman in her forties is restocking shelves without looking up. She says, without turning around: 'Sitting or staying?'"

Bess Hollowell
Innkeeper · Human · 47 · She/her · Third generation owner
🎭 Voice: Efficient, direct. Warm once she's decided you're all right. Not warm before that. Answers questions in the fewest words that get the job done. Has a very good memory for who paid and who didn't.

Bess inherited the inn from her mother who inherited it from her grandmother. She's never left Emberford for more than a week, has no desire to, and thinks travelers who romanticize the road are people who've never had to sleep in a ditch. She reads people extremely well. She knows about most of what happens in Emberford before anyone tells her, because people talk in taverns.

She already has an opinion about Caelen Vael. He was polite, tipped well, and she doesn't trust him. She won't say why — just that he smelled like someone getting paid to be nice. She'll share this if someone buys her a drink and asks the right question.

GM only: Bess has been quietly hosting Green Covenant-adjacent travelers for fifteen years — giving them rooms, not asking questions, taking payment in coin with no names recorded. She is not a member. She grew up watching her father deal with Lumencrest timber buyers and she made a choice about which side of that she wanted to be on.

The Emberfork Mill
Lumber Mill · West Bank · Industrial · Generates income & political friction

The mill processes timber from the managed woodlands west of town. Operated by the Crane family — three brothers who inherited it and agree on almost nothing except that the mill should keep running. The eldest, Fen Crane, is the one at the Lumencrest buyers' meetings. He was very interested in the Vael offer and said so at the council. He's not evil. He's financially pressured and thinks the Grove druids are overreacting.

🎭 Fen Crane: Human male, 50, big hands, permanently behind on his accounts. Genuinely decent to his workers. Genuinely wrong about what the Vael offer means. Will argue about it if you give him the chance. He has a point about the town's finances. He is missing the larger picture.

Town Gossip & Overheard Talk
What people are saying · Some true, some not · Sources listed
▾
How to use this

Roll or choose a piece of gossip to drop into any tavern scene, shop visit, or conversation with a townsfolk. The "Truth" line is for you only — don't share it unless the players investigate.

Bess, inn
"That Lumencrest boy who was here — polite as you like, but I've seen polite before. Asked me what the grove druids were like. Specific questions. Like he was checking something off a list."
True. Caelen was briefed to assess local druid resistance before making the lumber offer.
Mira, shop
"They say the Heart Stone's been glowing again at night. Third time this moon. Old Benn Thatch swears he saw it from the road at midnight, warm orange like a coal. Not that he should've been on the road at midnight."
Partially true. The Stone does pulse occasionally. Benn was definitely on the road at midnight — he was meeting someone.
At the bar, farmer
"Greenwatch sent a runner three days ago — something happened on the western forest road. Three men from a logging crew came back without their equipment. Wouldn't say what happened. Just said they weren't going back."
True. A Thorns cell discouraged a surveying crew from mapping an approach route near the grove's western boundary. Nobody was hurt. It was intended as a warning.
Theo, tailor
"I've made riding clothes for Lumencrest buyers before. That young man's jacket was different — had the Vael seal worked into the lining, not just pinned on. Family crest, not house colors. That's not a business representative. That's family."
True and perceptive. Theo has noticed what others missed.
Gorran, bridge guard
"You hear about Timberfall? Used to have three inns — now they've got one. And that one's half empty. Mill closed two seasons after they sold the logging rights. Funny how that works."
True. Timberfall is the cautionary tale everyone references but nobody officially connects to the current Vael offer.
Fen Crane, mill
"The druids say no to everything. Always have. But the bridge needs repairing, the south road is half-mud, and the Accord subsidy hasn't come through in fourteen months. Someone's going to have to make a hard choice eventually."
Also true. The economic pressure Fen describes is real. He's being used, but he's not wrong about the pressure.
Edda, healer
"Something's been wrong with the animals near the eastern woods. Brought me three birds this week — not sick exactly, more like confused. Flying in circles, bumping into things. Deer haven't come to the north pasture in two weeks. I told Thorn. He said he knew."
True. Shard fragment contamination from the Unseen Guild's transport route is affecting local wildlife.
Bess, late evening
"There was a woman through here last month. Quiet type. Paid in new coin. Didn't eat with the common room, had her meals sent up. Left before dawn. She had the look of someone doing a job, not traveling."
This was a Thorns courier. Bess doesn't know what she was carrying. She has a guess and will share it with the right person after one drink.

Ember Grove — The Forest
What it looks and feels like · The Heart Stone · Grove community
▾
GM: Say this when players first approach the grove's edge

"A mile north of Emberford the road simply stops being a road — it becomes a path, then a suggestion, then something you feel underfoot rather than see. The trees begin gradually: younger oaks and ash at the edge, getting older and larger as you go further in, until you're standing next to something that was a sapling when the Accord was young. The canopy is high enough that there's real sky between you and the leaves, but it still feels enclosed — not claustrophobic, more like being inside something that chose to have you here. The light changes. Out on the road it's ordinary Gold Month morning light. In here it's amber-filtered and slow, the kind that makes you want to lower your voice without knowing why."

Grove sensory details — pick and use as needed
  • Sound: Crows are the dominant bird — louder than they should be this week. Under that: the particular deep quiet of old forest, the kind where you can hear your own heartbeat. No wind at ground level even when the canopy moves.
  • Smell: Turned earth, fallen leaves beginning to decay, the sharp sweetness of late autumn. Something faintly mineral near the Heart Stone — like warm stone after rain.
  • Feel: The spirit web is tangible here to anyone sensitive (Greer immediately, Lurielle with a Knowledge roll DIF 12). It has the texture of something old that has been in one place for a very long time. Not threatening. Very present.
  • The boundary: There's no fence, no marker, no sign. But you know when you've crossed it. The air changes temperature slightly. Animals that were moving stop and watch. Greer has crossed this boundary ten thousand times and still feels it every time.
GM: Describing the Heart Stone

"The Heart Stone stands at the grove's center — a standing stone, roughly shoulder height, covered in carvings that no scholar has ever successfully read. They're not letters in any known language. They're something else — patterns that almost resolve into meaning if you don't look directly at them. The stone is warm. Not hot — warm, like it's been sitting in sunlight, except it's standing in deep shade. If you put your hand on it, you can feel a very faint vibration. Deep. Like a heartbeat at a distance."

Grove community — who lives here
  • Thorn Oakwise — Guardian. His shelter is a low structure of living wood and stone in the grove's northern quarter. He has lived in it for thirty years. It looks like it grew there.
  • Greer's family home — The family that came from Rivermark twenty-five years ago. Simple, permanent, part of the grove's daily rhythm. Greer's parents are there; let Tara define the specifics.
  • Wren — A young Faun, early twenties, just beginning serious druidic study. Nervous around new people. Has a remarkable ability with birds. Has been tracking the crow behavior for weeks and is quietly alarmed. She'll talk to Greer about it if asked.
  • Aldric — Human man, 40s, a practitioner rather than a guardian. Runs the grove's seasonal ceremonies for Emberford residents. Friendly, social, the face of the grove for most townspeople. He doesn't have Thorn's depth but he has warmth Thorn lacks.
What the grove is like in Gold Month specifically
  • The seasonal ceremony was three weeks ago — the Harvest Acknowledgment, where Emberford farmers bring first-press cider and dried grain to the grove's edge. The druids return a small bundle of blessed herbs for each family. It's been happening for eighty years. Attendance is down slightly this year. Greer noticed.
  • The eastern boundary has felt different for two months. Greer can't describe it precisely — less like the forest holding its breath and more like the forest has turned slightly to look at something in that direction. Thorn has been spending more time at the eastern edge in the mornings.
  • The Heart Stone has pulsed three times in the past moon cycle — each time at night, visible from the road as a faint orange glow. The duration gets slightly longer each time. Thorn is not alarmed in a way he shares. He is studying this the way he studies everything: without rushing.
