Pauper's Gate & Running Shadowside
North arrival · day/night rhythm · watch presence
▾
GM: Pauper's Gate — walking through

"The northern gate is the busiest in the city — end of the Kingroad, Frostwell refugees in ones and twos that add up. City watch, not Vault Guard. They check names against a warrant registry and assess goods for tariffs. They ask where you're from, where you're staying, what's your business. They write it down. The line moves faster than you expected. On the left immediately inside: a taproom that's been the first building travelers pass for three hundred years. Overhead, the cable car's northern terminus — North Expanse Station — with the line running south toward the center. Vendors catch you at your most disoriented. Their prices are bad. A handful of people sit against the interior wall with the look of not knowing where they're going next."

GM: Entering Shadowside proper from the gate

"The street lamps ended somewhere behind you. Here the buildings lean toward each other overhead, blocking light into semi-covered passages. Oil lamps and candles in windows — islands of amber with real shadow between. It smells like coal smoke, cooking fat, and fifteen thousand people living close without electric heat. During the day it's dense and functional — carts crawl, traders work from window sills and folding tables. After dark the Night Market arrives on the main thoroughfare as if it was always there and the daytime market was the temporary thing. People move through dark with the ease of those who read a street by smell. You slow down. They don't."

District facts
  • Population: ~15,000 · North · No Lumen Network power — never had it
  • Cable car: No service. North Expanse Station is on Lamplight Ward's northern edge, not inside Shadowside.
  • Watch: Two officers on main thoroughfare, rotation past Pauper's Gate. Maze Quarter effectively unpatrolled.
  • Brownouts: Non-event here. Candles keep burning. Dry satisfaction among long-term residents — nobody says it to outsiders.
  • Hollow Road border: South into Lamplight — street lamps end at an identifiable line. Feels like crossing a border.
Frostwell refugees at Pauper's Gate
  • Arriving for months in ones and twos — quiet, often don't know where they're going next
  • The ones who left chose to leave before things became impossible — which tells you about conditions at source
  • Salvation Hearth (eastern edge) provides meals and emergency beds — no documentation required
  • Some referred onward to Halfling Quarter lodging (Bessa Pipwick) through priest networks
  • Gate vendors overcharge them. Mama Voss charges everyone the same — which is its own statement
GM only — disappearances

Not many. Careful. People whose absence won't be reported. Communities communicate through Solt's careful mentions, Night Market gaps, who isn't where they should be. Old Pen saw movement. Solt sees people passing through. Part of the radical-wing investigation layer.

"Immediately inside Pauper's Gate on the left. Low ceiling, old stone, the smell of a place that has been a tavern longer than anyone living has been alive. Behind the bar: a woman short and wide and white-haired, moving at her own pace because she's learned everything gets done at her pace. Old Pen
Proprietor, Pauper's Gate Taproom · Human · appears late 60s · Nobody knows her first name
🎭 Voice: Moves slowly on purpose. Complete neutrality in expression — you'll know she's decided something and she won't say what. Few words. Doesn't rush for anyone. Prices gate-traffic inquiries at 3 silver whether the buyer is Underground or trusted watch.

Solt
Proprietor, Dark Lantern · Ribbet · ~70 · Behind the bar every day
🎭 Voice: Economy of motion — forty years behind a bar. Has opinions about everything, keeps them all. Professional courtesy to city watch and fugitives within twenty minutes of each other. Doesn't sell information — provides context to established regulars as hospitality. Tea orders get a slight nod of respect.

Thirty years maintaining absolute neutrality. Served Vault Guard and Free Hand couriers same hour, told neither. More than anyone knows what moves through Shadowside — distinction between selling (gets you targeted) and hospitality context (safe).

GM only: Hasn't taken a day away in eleven years. Quietly hostile to anyone who might destabilize Underground arrangements — visible in who gets less attention. Saw edge of Ash's Pauper's Gate meeting; mentioned carefully to one regular who asked about nervous newcomers.

Mama Voss
Cart operator · Human · 58 · Night Market thoroughfare center
🎭 Voice: Brusque during service — long line, things to cook. Warm when not mid-rush. Dry flat humor when genuinely amused. Coal-smoke on hands and forearms. Apron more repair than original fabric.

Twenty-three years same stretch — notices who's new, nervous, meeting who they claim not to know. Doesn't sell information; mentions observations like weather remarks if she likes you. Best resource for "which part of the market, which kind of person, which time of night."

GM only: Not anyone's actual mother — name preceded her ~15 years, she adopted it. Feeds poorest regulars extra flatbread without acknowledging it. Noticed Frostwell refugees buying only the 1-copper item — tracks who's in real trouble.

Night Market
Layout · sections · rules · Quiet Market contact
▾
Operations
  • Location: Main north-south thoroughfare, Pauper's Gate to Lamplight border — ~400 feet
  • Hours: Traders appear ~1 hour after dark; full operation ~2 hours after sunset until 2–3 hours before dawn
  • Organization: None by authority — habit of regular traders claiming same patches for years
  • Children present: Families live on this street — kids run errands at night. Not exclusively adult.
SectionWhat's sold
Southern (near Lamplight)Salvage, second-hand clothing/tools, food, candles/lamp oil/coal — cheapest in city
CentralUndocumented imports, gray herbs/alchemical prep, live animals, unlicensed minor arcane, off-books locksmiths
Northern / Maze mouthInformation sold like goods, high-value Quiet Market overflow, Joss Wren contact point
Unspoken rules
  • No violence on thoroughfare — traders police collectively
  • No city watch — locals pass word ahead; market doesn't scatter, just stops being obviously what it is
  • Reputation is the only recourse for bad information sellers
Quiet Market — Joss Wren contact
Mobile high-end market · Vetting via northern thoroughfare spot

Shard fragments, restricted alchemicals, significant information, quality stolen goods. Joss Wren (human, 35) near Maze Quarter eastern wall on arrangement nights — 1–2×/week, irregular. Vets: not watch, something worth bringing. First access with escort. Two wrong calls in three years — both caught early.

Maze Quarter navigation
  • Without guide: DC 13 Instinct every 10 minutes or go wrong way without knowing
  • With guide: automatic. Week daily experience: no rolls.
  • The Arch: Covered passage — neutral dispute meeting point
  • Forgotten Well: Center — working well predating city pumps. Critical during blackouts.
