Running Lamplight Ward
Look · reality · day/night · Copper Gate
▾
GM: First walk through Merchant Square

"You hear the fountain before you see it — electric, always on, the sound of water over crowd noise. Merchant Square opens ahead: a plaza built for a thousand people, usually at half that. The electric fountain at the center never turns off; the city would no more shut it down than the cable car. Four-story stone buildings, window boxes maintained with competitive pride. Amber lamps every twenty feet — except on the eastern stretch, where three in a row have been dark for a week and nobody's fixed them yet. Overhead, the cable car line runs the district's spine. It looks like a wealthy city at the height of its confidence. Look closer and the FOR LEASE sign on the east-side supply shop has been there two months."

District facts
  • Population: ~20,000 · East-center · Three cable car stops
  • Power: Reliable historically — eastern lamps failing, repair queue backed up
  • Character: Commercial and social center for everyone who isn't rich or poor
  • Day: Purposeful — work, shopping, Guild business
  • Night: Entertainment Quarter peaks ~3am. Night Pharmacy never closes. Brownouts at 2am hit a crowd, not empty streets.
  • Politics: Illuminist demographic base — slowly revising faith in the technical solution
Key areas
  • Merchant Square — Fountain, Guildhall Quarter south, street food north edge dawn–midnight
  • Guildhall Quarter — Merchant Guild hall (largest), Craft Guild, Artificers' Guild (electric sign)
  • Entertainment Quarter — Theatre, Copperstring, Volta's, Night Pharmacy — east of cable line
  • Eastwick — Residential east of Square, river side. Wren House, Lida's Bake, Eastwick Cooperative
  • Copper Gate — South gate welcome zone. Four inns, exchange booths, Welcome Gate tours
  • Wire Road — Halvern & Stoke smiths, three blocks east of Square

GM: Entering the Accord Arms

"North side of Merchant Square, facing the fountain. The best-positioned inn in the Ward — four floors, professional staff who have seen everything a major city's best inn sees and respond with equable competence. The common room runs until 4am. Guild members, visiting merchants, Council clerks on the city's account, Academic Quarter researchers who prefer noise to quiet — they all pass through. The conversations at the common tables are less guarded than they should be."

The Accord Arms
Inn & Tavern · Merchant Square north · 24h common room

Forty rooms. Best beds in the Ward — real mattresses, laundered linens, scheduled maintenance. Premium rooms have private bathrooms and hot water (worth it after travel or time underground). Passive intelligence-gathering hub. Staff know who's staying; won't say.

Traveller's Mark Inn
Copper Gate · Most reputable of four gate inns · 20 rooms

Clean, professional, 1 gold mark per night. Clientele is entirely arriving strangers — Henris has fifteen years assessing what people need within two minutes and providing it without making the assessment visible.

Henris Callow
Proprietor, Traveller's Mark · Human · 52 · Copper Gate
🎭 Voice: Immediately reassuring — unthreatening, professionally welcoming, competent without showiness. Warm conviction that new arrivals deserve honest information. Tells you which exchange to avoid and which guide to trust within the first conversation.

Fifteen years of south-gate traffic — who arrives, from where, apparent vs. actual purpose. Accurate picture of unusual traffic that often predates official awareness. Does not report complicated guests to watch; quietly ensures they know Ward geography.

GM only: Noticed increased Frostwell-region arrivals three weeks before the Council acknowledged it. Has a private list of "arrivals whose story doesn't match their luggage" he doesn't share with anyone yet.
GM: The Long Table at dinner

"A street off Merchant Square's east side. Through the front window: one long communal table seating thirty. Dinner only — breakfast and lunch run in private booths. Tonight the slow-roasted meat has been cooking since midday. Council clerks, Guild merchants, Craft Guild seniors, and a visiting professor share a table they didn't choose to share. Within one evening you'll hear the Guild's brownout position, what the Council said last week that wasn't in the official summary, and the social dynamics of who trusts whom."

The Long Table
Political tavern · Off Merchant Square east · Communal dinner

Ward's professional class eating together by design. Best Halfling Quarter ales in the Ward. Copperkettle Amber on tap. Hop gin available. Wine via Green Hearth contact.

Halfling Quarter mention — Pipwick's Rooms

Not in Lamplight Ward but one cable stop south: Bessa Pipwick runs seven rooms above a bakery on Common Green's south side. 7 silver/night, exceptional breakfast included. Treats every guest as an expected cousin. The warmest lodging in the city if the party wants community over polish.

Bessa Pipwick
Proprietor, Pipwick's Rooms · Halfling · 62 · Halfling Quarter (brief)
🎭 Voice: Round, warm, perpetual motion between bakery and rooms. Flour-marked hands. Genuine welcome and simultaneous accurate read of people — warmth is real, naivety is not.

Thirty years of guests, Common Green social texture, community mood at everyday level. Endorsement travels through Quarter naturally. Distinct from Bessa Kettlemarch at Copper Kettle — both Halfling, both Bessa, fifteen years of visitor confusion.

GM only: Two current guests are Frostwell families referred quietly by Salvation Hearth priests. She's housing them at reduced rate without announcing it.
Tessiel Wane
Proprietor, Night Pharmacy · Gnome · appears 60s · Entertainment Quarter
🎭 Voice: Economical speech — efficiency not unfriendliness. Charges real prices so people ask real questions. Not warm but fair; respects honest approach. Magnifying lenses on a cord — up for close work, down on forehead otherwise.

Forty years tracking what moves through Lumencrest chemically. Noticed Hollostar Dust quality change six months before price spike. Three theories on supply disruption — one correct, none shared. Hasn't left the building more than a few hours at a stretch in four years.

GM only: Trades information for information, not money. Most valuable player intel: Shard Division operations, Dust tunnel route, arcane activators, Vault Guard enforcement strategy. Watch tolerates back counter because she's genuinely useful to them at 2am.
Brinne Halloway
Chair, Eastwick Cooperative · Human · 46 · Eastwick meeting hall
🎭 Voice: Quietly fierce. Doesn't make speeches — organizes. Always carrying something. Practical colors, slightly worn. Direct about what the Cooperative needs; not interested in crisis talk without action.

Fifty-year mutual aid org expanding into emergency stockpile — food, medical, candles, lamp oil. Most accurate residential emergency preparedness picture in the city. In contact with Halfling Quarter network; aware of Tommas Wickham, hasn't met.

GM only: Has been in contact with Genna Cradle twice in past month. Cooperative larder locked — contents represent significant collective investment. Less optimistic about Ward trajectory than most residents.

Guildhall Quarter · Four stories · Chair: Aldous Wren

Licenses merchants, mediates disputes, Council influence. Public rooms open for commercial business. Upper floor requires membership or escort — door assessor Merric (human, 30s, impeccably dressed).
